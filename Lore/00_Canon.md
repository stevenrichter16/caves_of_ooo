## Preamble

The tepui-thread is **a coherent lore strand** developed across IDEAS.md and several design docs, anchored at a real-world referent (the **Sarisariñama** tepui in Venezuela) and the cultures that live near it. It contains a cosmology, a geography, a partial timeline, a set of factions and Spirits, a system of social practices, and a magical / metaphysical substrate. It does **not yet** contain a plot, a fully resolved cosmological history, or a complete cast of named characters — those are Phase 1+ work.

The thread inherits from CoO's existing universe (the Choir / Palimpsest / Pale Curation / Saccharine Concord factions were already there; the Quest system, House Drama, combat infrastructure, etc. all predate this thread). What the tepui-thread *adds* is a unified cosmological frame — the **Felled Tree** mythology — within which those existing factions now sit, plus a major new geographic feature (the central tepui) and many new systems that flow from it (preservation, light-economy, mineral relics, etc.).

**Cultural-responsibility note (live, applies throughout).** This world borrows structurally from real Indigenous traditions (Pemon, Ye'kwana, Quechua/Andean) and from several historical cultural practices (catacomb burial, salt-curing, sky burial, hospitality codes). The running ledger in IDEAS.md tracks each borrow. The principle is: **inherit the structural shape, do not lift names, credit at ship time.** This file does not re-state every credit — see the IDEAS.md ledger and the per-design-doc credit sections — but the principle is canonical.

## Layer 1 — Cosmology

### What is canonically established

**The Felling.** Once there was a great **Tree** — *Wazaka* in the Pemon cosmology that inspired the design, *the Tree of Life* in CoO's framing. The Tree was **felled** by some agent in some past event. From the Felling came the world: rivers, species, the flood-aftermath that became the present biosphere. **The world is the wound.** *(Structural borrow from Pemon Mount Roraima cosmology, structural-only — no Pemon names used in-game.)*

**The Root of the World.** The Tree's stump still stands, petrified into sandstone. This is the **central tepui** — visible from much of the world map, a flat-topped mountain rising from the surrounding biomes. It is the canonical final-boss site.

**The First Root.** Beneath the Root of the World, the Tree's *first root* still sleeps. It is alive. It dreams. Whether it could wake, what would happen if it did, whether the player should try to wake it or prevent it from waking — *(open; this is one of the central Phase 1 questions, see below).*

**The Tree's substrate.** Every tepui in the world is part of the Tree's geology repeated. Sinkholes are where the root-system breathes. Catacomb villages cultivate the substrate. The Choir consumes from it. The Palimpsest archives memory of it. The world is *grown-through* by what the Tree was.

**Time after the Felling.** The world is in a post-cataclysmic state. The Felling was not recent — generations have passed — but the *aftermath* is still active: Urqu is *what survives* of the Felling event (or what was *born* from the violence, or what was *unbound* by the loss of the Tree's containing presence). The Felling is **historical-feeling but cosmologically-still-unfolding.**

**The three endings.** The player's main-quest arc resolves into one of three possible endings, tracked by the existing `KnowsThreeEndings` player property. Later canon commits them as **Consume**, **Preserve**, and **Renewal**. The Phase-0 faction-readings of the Root suggested the rough shape:
- **Consume the Root** (Choir, partially Concord, partially Driving Bloom)
- **Preserve the Root** (Palimpsest, Inkbound, Pale Curation in their own way)
- **Some third path** *(open — could be wake the First Root, could be re-enact the Felling consciously, could be something else entirely)*

### What is open

- **Who or what felled the Tree.** Pemon mythology says Makunaima the trickster; we do not yet have CoO's equivalent. *(Phase 1.)*
- **Why the Tree was felled.** Accident, malice, necessity, sacrifice? *(Phase 1.)*
- **What the First Root *is*.** Literal sleeping organism? Metaphor for something else? A vessel for divinity? *(Phase 1.)*
- **What Urqu is, exactly.** Born from the Felling? Pre-existing and unbound by it? An anti-Tree that the Tree's life held back? *(Phase 1.)*
- **The third ending.** *(Phase 1.)*
- **The cosmology of death.** Memory Consumption + encasement + Re-Membering + Preservation + Stone Burial give us multiple competing answers to "what is a person after they die." The cosmological *truth* of the matter — if there is one — is open. *(Possibly intentionally left ambiguous; possibly resolved in Phase 1.)*
- **The cosmology of consciousness.** The Preserved are *not specified* to be conscious. Some appear to move. *(Intentionally ambiguous in lore; mechanically resolved for the player only.)*

## Layer 2 — Geography

### What is canonically established

**The central tepui — the Root of the World.** A vast flat-topped sandstone mountain at the world's center. Visible from much of the world map. The petrified stump of the Tree. Surrounded by surface biomes.

**Tepuis as a class.** The central tepui is the largest, but **smaller tepuis sprout across the world map** — each with its own ecology, its own sinkholes, its own potential settlements. Tepuis are the *defining geographic feature* of this world.

**Elevation zones (per the Sarisariñama bestiary mapping):**
- **Lowlands** (400-600m equivalent) — surface villages, savanna, blackwater streams, forest edges
- **Foothills** (450-1100m equivalent) — lower tepui slopes, waterfall spray zones, cascade habitat
- **Sima Interior** (1100-1375m equivalent) — inside sinkholes; Drowned-Sima / Choir-Cathedral hybrid zones
- **Summit** (1300m+ equivalent) — tepui top, bromeliad scrub, the most-endemic species

**Sinkholes — seven archetypes** (Sinkholes IDEAS entry):
- **Drowned Sima** — water-filled, amphibian-rich, Gin Frog home
- **Time-Locked Forest** — endemic species, Inquiry hotspot
- **Stranded Settlement** — catacomb-village host
- **Choir Cathedral** — Rot Choir territory
- **Boneyard** — predator-haunted bone pit
- **Sealed Library** — Palimpsest sub-archive
- **Door** — wormhole-archetype, rare, connects to other zones
- **Pure Catacomb** — (added in the Catacombs entry) — sinkhole whose floor IS the catacomb

**The Bog biome.** Bog-Taken preservation is canonical (per The Preserved entry); a bog biome exists somewhere. Bodies fall in over centuries; bog-bodies become visible at depth. Bog-adjacent catacomb villages have specific relationships with the geography. *(Bog biome's specific world-map placement is open.)*

**Wasteland.** Tent-Right hospitality culture exists in a wasteland biome. Salt-flats geology, harsh sun, sparse water. Salt-trade economy. *(Wasteland's specific world-map placement is open.)*

**Catacomb networks.** Sinkholes connect to underground catacomb networks. Catacomb villages occupy some of these. The networks are mostly unmapped; Surface-Walker guides and catacomb-villager Tongues are the only reliable navigators. Some catacomb networks connect zones that the surface map doesn't.

**Mineral geography.** Different minerals come from different regions (per Stones with Stake). *Specifically:*
- **Tepuibone** comes from the Root of the World itself; rarest stone
- **Pale-Salt** comes from wasteland salt-flats
- **Glow-Quartz, Memory-Marble, Mute-Stone** are mined locally in various tepui zones
- **Sari-Pyrite** clusters in Urqu-active zones (subtle indicator)
- **Bower-Starstone** — *(open which region)*
- **Choir-Iron, Black-Gall, Spore-Block** — *(open which region; could be anywhere)*

**Many-Trunk.** A single-organism forest exists somewhere (a tepui valley, per the IDEAS entry). *(Specific location open.)*

### What is open

- **The number of major regions/continents.** Are we talking about one continent, an archipelago, an unbounded world map? *(Phase 2.)*
- **The names of major regions.** No region has a canonical name yet. *(Phase 2.)*
- **Where the major surface settlements are.** *(Phase 2.)*
- **The wasteland's relationship to the tepui geography.** Adjacent? Distant? On the other side of the Root? *(Phase 2.)*
- **The bog biome's location.** *(Phase 2.)*
- **The world's edge.** Does the world have an edge? An ocean? An impassable mountain range? *(Phase 2.)*
- **Travel scale.** How long does it take to cross from a wasteland edge to the central tepui? *(Phase 2.)*

## Layer 3 — History (mostly open)

### What is canonically established

**The Felling happened.** Generations ago. Cosmologically formative. *Specific date / era open.*

**Faction emergences.** The factions arose *after* the Felling — they are responses to the post-Felling world. *Specific orders and ages open, but probably:*
- **Rot Choir** emerged first, growing from the post-Felling substrate
- **Palimpsest** emerged as a reaction — catalogers of what the Choir was forgetting
- **Pale Curation** emerged from a Palimpsest schism — preservation became more important than cataloging
- **Saccharine Concord** emerged when trade became possible across post-Felling zones
- **Catacomb-villages** emerged as people descended into sinkholes to escape something (the Felling itself? a later war? a famine?)
- **Inkbound** emerged from a Palimpsest-adjacent literary tradition and is later committed as a Palimpsest sub-sect

*(All of the above is the Phase-0 sketch; later history and faction docs settle the current canon.)*

**The Sealed Library catacombs.** Some Palimpsest sub-archives were hidden in sinkholes "to outlast a now-forgotten persecution." This implies a historical persecution event — the Palimpsest knows it happened but has lost some of the details. Later history commits the Lost Persecution frame.

**The Driving Bloom heresy.** The Driving Bloom is a Choir breakaway sect that rejected the Choir's dissolution-into-substrate doctrine in favor of direct mind-hijack. This is a *recent* schism in cosmological time — the Choir still actively suppresses it. *(Phase 3: how recent, what triggered it.)*

**The Imminent Archive (Catcher) schism.** A more recent Pale Curation splinter. Phase 3 dates this.

### What is open

- **Almost all of this.** Phase 3 is largely unwritten. The above are the strongest implications from existing canon.
- **Pre-Felling civilization.** Did one exist? Multiple? What artifacts remain? *(The Felling-Counter artifact implies pre-Felling technology existed at some level.)*
- **The Antikythera-mechanism-like Felling-Counter.** It is a *recovered* pre-cataclysm device. So pre-Felling civilization had this technology. *(Phase 3: what else they had, what they were like.)*

## Layer 4 — Factions

### Established factions

**Rot Choir.** Mycelial collective intelligence. Spore-borne. Patient. Consumes (memory, body, world) by external digestion. Theology: *dissolution into substrate is salvation.* Practices Memory Consumption + encasement on willing subjects. Internal politics include the Driving Bloom heresy (suppressed) and quiet integration of certain catacomb villages. *Disposition: ambivalent toward the player; aggressive only when their consumption is interrupted.*

**Palimpsest.** Scribal preservationist faction. Archives memory in written and corpse form (Ink-Bathed scribes). Practices the Re-Membering ritual. Internal politics include the Pale Curation schism (older) and theological disputes about which memories deserve preservation. *Disposition: friendly to literate players; pays for archival work; gravest crimes are memory-destruction.*

**Pale Curation.** Preservation-first faction. Pale-Salt as primary mineral. Mass preservation chambers of Salt-Cured bodies, indexed under their last words. Internal politics dominated by the Imminent Archive (Catcher) schism. Tension with Palimpsest over preservation-vs-cataloging priorities. *Disposition: rigid; quietly horrified by the Choir; uses carnivorous-plant enzymes for poorly-filed-specimen disposal (paradoxically).*

**Saccharine Concord.** Mercantile, cynical, neutral. Trades everything. "Merchants of the possible." Sells protection charms (sometimes useful, often fake). Runs caravans across zones, including inter-village trade with catacomb settlements. *Disposition: profit-driven; willing to sell to anyone; will quietly mislabel goods.*

**Bower-Folk.** Aestheticist culture/faction. Build elaborate decorated installations; value beauty over utility; collectors of colors. Resin-Cast preservation specialists. *Disposition: largely peaceful; may attempt non-consensual aesthetic preservation on players whose death-pose is "perfect" or whose preserved appearance is unusually beautiful.*

**Inkbound.** Later committed as a Palimpsest sub-sect, not a separate faction. The skin-page Ink-Bathed scribe tradition is theirs. They believe Urqu is *an unread letter*.

**Driving Bloom.** Hostile fungal heresy. Choir breakaway. Cordyceps-style mind-hijack. Suppressed by the Choir. Player encounters as a corrupting infection. *Disposition: hostile to all; player must seek Choir help to cure infection (forced alliance dynamic).*

**Catacomb-villagers.** Civilized humanoid catacomb-dwelling people. Falmer-visual basis. Generations-isolated; species uniform but cultures wildly varied. Five archetypal village types (per catacomb_village_design.md): Patch-Bright (Concord-aligned trader), Wall-of-Names (Palimpsest-aligned memory tender), Spore-Wedded (Choir-integrated), The Quiet (hostile isolated), The Wardward (warden-specialty). *Disposition: by default passive toward the player; village-specific factional relationships.*

**Tent-Right wasteland culture.** Hospitality-first wasteland people. Three-day inviolable guest-protection oath. Bedouin-inspired structural borrow (no Arabic vocabulary lifted). Pale-Salt trade as primary export. *Disposition: by default friendly to anyone who claims hospitality; oath-bound to defend guests; uniquely willing to help Forced-Preserved players un-preserve.*

**Imminent Archive (Catchers).** Pale Curation splinter sect. Preserve the wounded while still alive. Operate from disused Salt-Cure chambers in regional Pale Curation outposts. Mainline Pale Curation quietly disavows but cannot openly condemn the underlying logic. *Disposition: triggered by low player HP; not flagged as combat hostility; the most ethically-complex adversary in the game.*

### Implied but not committed

- **A surface village faction.** Surface villages exist (Tent-Right is one; the Catacomb-villagers entry mentions surface-adjacent villages). Whether they constitute a *unified* faction or are simply unaffiliated settlements is *(open)*.
- **The Branchwork (Physarum slime-mold intelligence).** Not a faction in the social-organizational sense, but a distributed non-Choir intelligence. *Disposition: consultative; rewards alliance with route-knowledge.*
- **Houses, Blocs, Sects.** The Blocs and Houses systems from earlier IDEAS entries are partially fleshed out but not directly tied to the tepui-thread yet. Treat as broader-system backlog unless a later phase adopts a specific piece.

### What is open

- **Inkbound status.** Later settled as Palimpsest sub-sect.
- **Houses and Blocs integration.** *(Phase 4.)*
- **Faction leadership.** No major faction has named leaders yet. *(Phase 4 / Phase 7.)*
- **Inter-faction relationship matrix.** Implied throughout but never formalized. *(Phase 4.)*
- **Faction-quest spine.** Each faction needs a major story arc. *(Phase 4 + Phase 6.)*

## Layer 5 — Spirits and cosmic actors

### Established

**The three Spirits.** Drive-personifications, free of faction allegiance, manifesting in response to behavioral signals (diag-substrate-style observation):
- **Inquiry** (`?` in pale green) — watches investigative acts; rewards examination, dialogue probing, knowledge accumulation
- **Bloodlust** (`!` in arterial red) — watches combat acts; rewards killing, overkill, refused pacification
- **Apatheia** (`-` in pale gray) — watches *acts of refusal*; rewards stillness, declined violence, walking-away

The Spirits are **mechanically rivals.** High affinity with one suppresses the others. Pacts at full affinity offer permanent class-locks.

**Urqu.** Named hostile cosmic actor, *third entity class* (distinct from Spirits and factions). Sourced from Ye'kwana legend of an evil spirit on Sarisariñama caves making the *sari... sari...* sound. **Cosmologically opposed to the Root.** Urqu wants the Root unmade. Manifests as *consequence not trigger* — appears during catastrophic events. Most players experience Urqu only as the long shadow of an active period.

**The First Root.** Cosmologically central; the sleeping organism beneath the Root of the World. **Not currently treated as an actor** — it sleeps, it dreams, but it does not act. *(Whether it could/does act is open and central to Phase 1.)*

**The Branchwork.** Non-Choir distributed cognition. Slime-mold-architecture intelligence. Consultative not consumptive. Real biological grounding (*Physarum polycephalum*). Rival to the Choir in distributed-cognition niche.

**The Choir as collective entity.** Beyond being a faction, the Choir is *also* a kind of distributed intelligence — closer to the Branchwork's mode than to a conventional social faction. The Choir thinks, plans, remembers. Its "members" are extensions of its mind.

### What is open

- **Whether more Spirits exist.** Later canon commits Inquiry, Bloodlust, and Apatheia as the core three; other candidates remain backlog only.
- **The First Root's agency.** *(Phase 1 / Phase 5.)*
- **Urqu's relationship to other dimensions of evil.** Is there *more* than Urqu? Multiple hostile cosmic actors? *(Phase 5.)*
- **Named lesser entities.** Are there small spirits attached to specific tepuis, bogs, sinkholes? *(Phase 5.)*

## Layer 8 — Magic and metaphysics

### Established systems

**Memory magic.** Multiple interrelated mechanics:
- **Memory Consumption + encasement** (Choir): body wrapped, memory extracted via mycelial cortex contact
- **Re-Membering** (Palimpsest): gathering scattered memory to re-instantiate a person; structural opposite of Choir consumption
- **Held Breath / shape-knowing** (Choir-aligned mystic skill): holding the unspoken shape of someone's hidden self as leverage
- **Memory eating** in the broader Memory Consumption as Social Verb entry (paying the Choir to consume a specific memory from someone else)

**Preservation magic.** Hold-against-time mechanic. Five methods. The Imminent Archive's pre-mortem preservation as horror-application.

**Spirit affinity magic.** Behavioral-input → cosmic-response. Pacts at full affinity unlock class-locking permanent abilities.

**Mineral magic.** Stones with Stake — passive / infusion / architectural categories. Cross-faction politics around mineral preferences.

**Bioluminescent ecology.** Catacomb light economy as functional infrastructure with metaphysical undertones (light vs. dark, Urqu's opposition).

**Choir-Touched, Bloomed, Choir-Iron-warded** — status effect systems.

**Carnivorous-plant enzymes.** Counter-system to preservation; specific applications across factions.

### What is open

- **Whether there's a unified "magic system" or whether each of the above is its own discrete substrate.** *(Phase 9.)*
- **Player skill trees** — the existing combat / skills system needs integration with the cosmological framework. *(Phase 9.)*
- **Spell-casting structure.** Some factions imply spellwork (Inkbound dew-ink texts that act on the world; Choir spore-craft). Others don't. *(Phase 9.)*
- **Soul / consciousness mechanics.** Re-Membering implies souls (something persists; can be gathered). Preservation implies consciousness can be *held in flesh*. The Choir implies consciousness can be *transferred to substrate*. These need reconciliation. *(Phase 1 / Phase 9.)*
