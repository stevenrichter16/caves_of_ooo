# 05 — Spirits & Cosmic Actors

> Phase 5 of the lore project. The **cosmic-actor layer**: the entities that are neither factions (Phase 4) nor gods (the Six, Phase 1). Covers the three Spirits (Inquiry / Bloodlust / Apatheia), Urqu deepened, the First Root's agency, the Tree's residual presence, the Branchwork, and the lesser place-bound entities.
>
> Inherits: Phase 1 (the Felling; the Six; Urqu as the unborn seventh god), Phase 2 v2 (Strangeness Tiers; Urqu as a distributed infection of places), Phase 3 (the Thinning; the Great Manifestation), Phase 4 (the factions' stances on the Spirits; the Bloom as Urqu's agriculture; Tent-Right's oath as anti-Urqu field).

This is the load-bearing decision of Phase 5; everything else hangs off it. Surfaced for the user at the Phase 5 checkpoint.

Real-source vocabulary stays in design notes; in-game text is CoO-native.

## I. The cosmological frame — what a Spirit *is*

The Six are **inheritors of the Tree's binding-functions** (Memory, Substrate, Preservation, Beauty, Exchange, Roots — and the missing Naming). Urqu is **the unbound remainder** — the seventh inheritance with no bearer. Factions are **social organizations** built around the gods. None of these describes the Spirits. The Spirits are something older.

### The bet: the world predates the Tree

`01_Spine.md` §54 left open whether the Tree *is* the world's age or whether the world predates the Tree and the Tree "bound an older chaos." **Phase 5 commits the second.** Before the Tree, there was a world — but an *unbound* one: a place of drives without function, urges without identity, motion without name. The Tree was the great **binding** — the event (or being) that gave the unbound world *Naming*, and through Naming, coherence: things became *what they are* and stayed so through time.

The Tree's seven inheritances are the **functions of a bound world.** The Spirits are what was there *before* binding — and what is there still, underneath it.

### So: the Spirits are pre-Tree drives

**A Spirit is a drive that predates Naming.** Inquiry (the urge to know), Bloodlust (the urge to unmake), Apatheia (the urge to be still) — these are not functions the Tree distributed; they are *older than function*, older than identity, older than the gods. The Tree bound the world *on top of* them. They never left. They leak through wherever the binding is thin — and the place the binding is always thinnest is **behavior**: a living thing in the act of choosing is, for that moment, a little bit pre-Tree, a little bit unbound. That is why the Spirits manifest in response to *acts* and not to faction, geography, or bloodline. **Behavior is the seam in the world's binding, and the Spirits come through the seam.**

This frame pays off three ways:

1. **It explains the Spirits' faction-freedom** (per IDEAS.md:155 — "psychological facts, not social organizations"). They predate the very concept of faction, which requires Naming/identity. No faction owns a Spirit because the Spirits are older than ownership.
2. **It gives each Spirit a cosmological vector** (§II) — toward binding (Inquiry), toward unbinding (Bloodlust), or toward the unbound stillness itself (Apatheia). These vectors *rhyme with the three endings* (§X).
3. **It relates the Spirits to Urqu without merging them** (§IV). Urqu is the unbound *Naming* (a specific missing function trying to be born). The Spirits are unbound *drives* (older than function, not trying to be anything). Both are "outside the Tree's order" — but Urqu is pressing to get *in*, and the Spirits never left the *outside*.

*(Tone note: this is cosmic horror by depth — the comforting bound world is a thin skin over an older, nameless thing, and every act of will is a small crack in the skin. It is also quietly hopeful: the drives are not evil; they are simply *prior*. Inquiry is not malice. Stillness is not death. The horror is scale, not cruelty.)*

## II. The three Spirits

Each Spirit: nature, manifestation (per the committed glyphs and mechanics), cosmological vector, faction stances, and ending-resonance.

### Inquiry — the urge to know (`?`, pale green)

- **Nature.** The drive toward knowing, examining, naming-and-binding. The *oldest* drive, and the one closest to the Tree's own project — **the Tree may have been Inquiry's masterpiece**, the moment the unbound world's curiosity about itself became binding-order. (Soft bet, §XI: the Tree was the world coming to *know* itself so completely it became *named*. If true, Inquiry is the Spirit the Tree was born from — which makes a high-Inquiry player a continuation of the Tree's original act.)
- **Manifestation.** Watches investigative acts (examine, ask-about, KnowledgePart reveals, careful container-opening, reading discovered text, returning to NPCs with new questions; per IDEAS.md:158). At threshold, manifests as a drifting `?` glyph; conversation is the gift exchange (tier reveals, marks an off-screen NPC/item, prophetic-dream quest seeds, perfect-detail examine; cost: some gifts *leak* to a nearby Choir node or Palimpsest scribe — knowledge wants to spread).
- **Cosmological vector: toward binding.** Inquiry knows, and to fully know a thing is to name it, and to name it is to bind it. Inquiry is the drive that *builds* the bound world. **Renewal-resonant** (§X).
- **Faction stances.** Palimpsest aligned (beauty-of-knowing; per IDEAS.md:174 — catalogs Spirit-sightings as historical recurrence). Bower-Folk aligned (beauty as a form of knowing; per `05` faction doc). Choir denies its independence (spore-borne pattern; would eat it). Concord sells insurance against it. *Inquiry is the most-courted Spirit by the knowledge factions.*
- **The cruelty caveat (per IDEAS.md:186).** Inquiry is **not** "good." The scholar who probes a wounded prisoner to learn is high-Inquiry. Knowing is not kindness. Inquiry storylets must include the cruel-knowing as well as the humane-knowing.

### Bloodlust — the urge to unmake (`!`, arterial red)

- **Nature.** The drive toward killing, overkill, the unmaking of a bound thing. **To kill is to un-bind** — to return a named, coherent life to the unbound state. Bloodlust is the drive that *undoes* the Tree's work, one life at a time.
- **Manifestation.** Watches combat acts (kills, lethal hits, consecutive melee, overkill, refused pacification; per IDEAS.md:159). At threshold, manifests as a drifting `!` glyph; gifts are combat buffs, second-wind cooldown resets, guaranteed-lethal next-hit; cost: active effects suppress peaceful dialogue, burn peaceful-faction reputation, and at high affinity risk auto-attack-on-bump (loss of fine motor control over engagement; per IDEAS.md:168).
- **Cosmological vector: toward unbinding.** Bloodlust returns the bound to the unbound. This is **why Urqu can use it** (§IV) — Urqu's whole project is un-Naming, and Bloodlust is the drive that does un-Naming retail. *But Bloodlust is not Urqu's ally* (per IDEAS.md:293): Urqu is a *user* of Bloodlust, not its partner, and would consume Bloodlust as readily as the Choir would. **Consume-resonant** (§X) — with the tension noted there.
- **Faction stances.** Choir would eat it (and the Bloodlust-player is wary of the Choir, which complicates the resonance). Pale Curation treats it as contagion to be sealed. Concord insures against it. Tent-Right's oath is its structural opposite (hospitality = refusing to unmake the guest). *No faction aligns with Bloodlust the way the Palimpsest aligns with Inquiry — Bloodlust is the least institutionally-courted Spirit, because organized society is itself a binding and Bloodlust unbinds.*
- **The justice caveat (per IDEAS.md:186).** Bloodlust is **not** "evil." The warrior who answers atrocity in kind is high-Bloodlust. Unmaking can be just. Bloodlust storylets must include the righteous-violence as well as the monstrous.

### Apatheia — the urge to be still (`-`, pale gray)

- **Nature.** The drive toward refusal, stillness, non-action, equanimity (Stoic *apatheia* — freedom from destructive passion, not modern apathy; per IDEAS.md:403). **Apatheia is the drive closest to the unbound void itself** — not the urge to know or unmake the bound world, but the urge to *stop participating in it*. At its gentlest it is peace; at its extreme it is the longing to return to the stillness that preceded all binding. *This doubleness is Apatheia's whole character: the most genuinely serene Spirit and the most quietly cosmic-horror-adjacent, and they are the same thing seen from two distances.*
- **Manifestation.** Watches acts of refusal (declining offered violence, refusing offered bargains, returning rentals voluntarily, walking away from winnable fights; per IDEAS.md:403). At threshold, manifests as a quiet `-` glyph in low-stakes zones. Gifts are temporally *inverted* from Bloodlust's — Bloodlust grants intense short bursts, Apatheia grants enduring slow advantages: durability, refusal-immunity (social-reputation immunity), the ability to *outlast* mechanics that target less-stoic players. The Pact ("tarrying" abilities) is a class of unflinching endurance.
- **Cosmological vector: toward the unbound stillness.** Where Inquiry builds the bound world and Bloodlust unbinds it piece by piece, **Apatheia simply declines to act on it at all** — which is the state closest to before-the-Tree. **Preserve-resonant** (§X): the sealed, sleeping, arrested world is Apatheia's cosmic ideal, exactly as it is the Bower's aesthetic ideal (per `05` faction doc — and the Bower-Folk's "collection without instrumental use" is an Apatheian act, per IDEAS.md:403).
- **Faction stances.** Pale Curation and the Bower-Folk recognize its pace (preservation requires patience). The catacomb-villagers' Asleep Tradition (per `06`) is structurally Apatheian (do not wake the Root; be still). Opposed to the Held Breath (active leverage; per IDEAS.md:403). **Strange relationship to Urqu** (per IDEAS.md:403): both are tied to *not-acting*, but for opposite reasons — Apatheia from inner cultivation (a chosen stillness), Urqu from a botched incarnation (an *imposed* stillness, the un-born's frozen reaching). *Apatheia chose to stop; Urqu cannot start. They look alike and are opposites.*
- **The caveat.** Apatheia is **not** "wisdom" any more than Inquiry is "good." Refusing to act can be cowardice or complicity; the equanimous bystander who watches an atrocity without flinching is high-Apatheia. Apatheia storylets must include the cold-refusal as well as the serene-acceptance.

## III. The Spirits as a system

### Rivalry (committed mechanic, per IDEAS.md:170)

The three are **mutually suppressing.** High affinity with one raises the others' manifest threshold. A player chasing all three keeps their gifts small; commitment to one unlocks the Pact and locks out the rivals. Pacts are per-save permanent (with the **Recanting** escape valve — a hard, expensive ritual that releases a Pact at the cost of much of what it gave, per IDEAS.md:184). The three-way rivalry is the cleaner-than-two-way shape: **build / unbind / be-still** is a complete triad, not an arbitrary pair.

### Open questions committed here

- **Single or instanced?** **Committed: SINGLE.** There is *one* Inquiry, one Bloodlust, one Apatheia in the world. Local manifestations are *projections* of the single Spirit (the player meets "it" in their current zone, but it is the same entity everywhere). This is the more dramatic shape and the one that gives the factions real stakes: **the Choir can eat the only Bloodlust that exists, denying it to every player forever** (per IDEAS.md:181). *(Balance note: projection-locality means encounters are still zone-local; singularity is a narrative/stakes fact, not a "you must travel to one fixed cell" constraint.)*
- **Can a Spirit be killed?** **Committed: YES, with propagating, world-thinning consequences.** Because the Spirits are the world's pre-Tree drives (§I), killing one **thins that drive across the entire world** — kill Inquiry and the world grows incrementally less curious (KnowledgePart reveals dim by a fraction; the Palimpsest turns hostile and the Choir tries to harvest the corpse-pattern, per IDEAS.md:182); kill Bloodlust and the world grows less violent (and the Concord's risk-economy quietly contracts); kill Apatheia and the world loses its capacity for rest (a subtle, horrible escalation of everyone's restlessness — including, perhaps, the Counter's, per `04`). *Killing a Spirit is one of the few truly world-altering irreversible acts available to the player.*
- **Affinity visibility.** Tiered (per IDEAS.md:183): diegetic at first (Spirits hint, NPCs comment), explicit meter unlocked at first Manifest threshold.

### The moral-not-light/dark principle (load-bearing, per IDEAS.md:186)

**None of the three is good or evil.** Inquiry can be cruel; Bloodlust can be just; Apatheia can be cowardly. Each must have storylets where its drive is the humane choice *and* storylets where it is the monstrous one. The system's moral weight depends on the player being unable to pre-judge which Spirit is "right." *(This is also the cosmological truth: pre-Tree drives are prior to morality, which is itself a binding. The drives don't know good from evil because they are older than the distinction.)*

## IV. Urqu deepened

Phase 1 committed Urqu as **the unborn seventh god** — the empty Naming-position attempting incarnation across centuries and failing, the failures producing the *sari... sari...* slippage. Phase 5 deepens the *mechanics and texture* of that fact.

### What Urqu is and is not

- **Is:** the missing seventh inheritance (Naming) with no bearer; a god *trying to be born* and unable, because there is no Strike to complete it and a position cannot fill itself from absence. **Not malevolent in origin** — a god trying to incarnate and failing. *(Per Phase 1 v2; per the Ye'kwana-sourced framing the user affirmed — the "evil spirit" reading is the *in-world peoples'* reading, mediated through the cultures that fear it, not the cosmological truth.)*
- **Is not:** a Spirit (the Spirits are pre-Tree drives, never trying to be anything; Urqu is a post-Tree absence trying to become something), and not a faction (no society, no doctrine — the Driving Bloom is the closest thing to "Urqu's faction," and it isn't one; see below).

### Manifestation as consequence, not trigger (committed, per IDEAS.md:270)

Urqu does **not** respond to player behavior the way the Spirits do. Urqu manifests at **catastrophic conditions** — a faction's collapse, a city falling, a mass-death event, a specific atrocity (massacring a settlement, burning a Sealed Library, *eating a Spirit*). When Urqu becomes active, the *world changes*: fauna more aggressive, NPCs more paranoid, dark dialogue branches unlock, weather worsens, the Urqu-bleed of places intensifies (per Phase 2 v2). **Most players never directly meet Urqu** — they experience the long shadow of an active period. *Urqu is weather and history, not an encounter.*

### The textures of Urqu (gathered and systematized)

- **The *sari... sari...* sound** — the audible signature of a failed incarnation-attempt; the slippage in the world's substrate. Sill hears it for the first time at the player's start (per Phase 3). It is the sound of the seventh god failing to be born, over and over.
- **The Hush** — regions where the binding is so thin that Naming barely holds (per Phase 2 v2). The deep Urqu-bleed places.
- **The Slip / the Unsaying** — the local effects of Urqu's near-incarnations: things briefly losing their names, their coherence (per Phase 2 v2). A person who is Unsaid forgets what they are; an object that Slips stops being reliably itself.
- **The Great Manifestation (Gen 25, ~year 750)** — the worst Urqu event in recorded history; a near-incarnation that lasted a season and unmade a region (per Phase 3); the trauma that founded the Imminent Archive Catchers (per `09`).
- **The Thinning (Gen 34-36)** — Urqu's manifestations *doubling* as the Root's holding-field weakens (per Phase 3). The present-era acceleration.
- **Anti-Urqu materials** — Tepuibone, Memory-Marble, Mute-Stone (per IDEAS.md Stones-with-Stake): the three substances that *hold their names hardest*, and therefore resist the Unsaying. The Tent-Right oath is the *social* version of the same thing (mortal Naming; per `07`).

### Urqu's bargains (committed texture, per IDEAS.md:291)

Urqu — or rather Urqu's *mouthpieces* (the rare individuals through whom a near-incarnation speaks) — offers bargains. Each leaves visible corruption (a trait, a tag, an NPC reaction: *"you reek of the Stone-Below"*). The bargains' imagery inverts the Choir's encasement (per IDEAS.md:354): instead of giving a memory *to* the Choir, you *receive* one from a hostile actor — the mouthpiece vomits ink the player must drink. Corruption is reversible but costly (Recanting-shaped). *Without visible corruption, bargains are pure upside and the system degenerates.*

### Urqu and the Driving Bloom (the Phase 4 link, made cosmological)

Per `08_DrivingBloom.md`: the Bloom **multiplies incompletion** (every Bloomed host abandons its unfinished acts), and incompletion is Urqu's substrate. The Bloom is therefore **Urqu's unwitting agriculture** — not a servant (the Bloom has no mind), but an engine that produces exactly what the unborn god feeds on. **Phase 5 affirms the soft bet from `08`:** the Gen 32 Bloom-breakaway and Urqu's post-Thinning doubling are *the same acceleration curve*. Whether Urqu's incarnation-pressure *caused* the breakaway (the unborn seventh reaching into the Choir and tearing off the part that would forget) is the **Phase 6 commit.** Soft lean: correlation deepening toward a feedback loop — the more incomplete the world, the closer Urqu comes to birth; the closer Urqu comes, the more the world fails to complete.

### Urqu and Bloodlust (the Spirit link)

Urqu *uses* Bloodlust (the unbinding drive serves the un-Naming project) but is not its ally and would consume it (§II; per IDEAS.md:293). A high-Bloodlust player is a *useful tool* for Urqu, never a partner — and the game should make the player *feel* the difference between "you are aligned with violence" and "you are being used by something older than violence." *(Note the precise cosmology: Bloodlust is older than Urqu — a pre-Tree drive vs. a post-Tree absence — so when Urqu "uses" Bloodlust it is the younger, needier thing riding the older, indifferent one. Urqu needs Bloodlust; Bloodlust does not need Urqu.)*

## V. Is there more than Urqu?

**Committed: no peer.** Urqu has no equal-scale counterpart. There is exactly *one* great cosmic antagonist, because there is exactly *one* missing inheritance. A second Urqu-scale evil would dilute the cosmology's central elegance (the seven-minus-one structure). The world's evil is not a pantheon of dark gods; it is **a single absence**, and that is scarier and cleaner than a roster of antagonists.

But Urqu is not the *only* non-faction, non-Spirit entity. Below Urqu's scale there are **lesser entities** (§IX) — and the Tree's residue (§VII) and the Branchwork (§VIII) round out the actor-layer. The hierarchy:

| Tier | Entity | Nature |
|---|---|---|
| Cosmic-singular | **Urqu** | the unborn seventh god; the one great antagonist |
| Cosmic-prior | **The three Spirits** | pre-Tree drives; older than the gods; faction-free |
| Cosmic-residual | **The Tree** (§VII) | gone-but-present; the world's order, distributed |
| Cosmic-neutral | **The Branchwork** (§VIII) | non-Choir distributed cognition; no stake |
| Local | **Lesser entities** (§IX) | place-bound numina; fragments of pre-Tree chaos pooled at high-Strangeness sites |

## VI. The First Root's agency

`00_Canon.md` §185 and §194 left the First Root's agency open. Phase 5 commits the **mechanism**; Phase 6 commits the **want**.

- **The Root sleeps, dreams, and holds.** Its waking energy is entirely consumed by *holding the world's binding-field* — the taproot that didn't die at the Felling (per Phase 1) is the last anchor of the Tree's Naming, and maintaining it against the Thinning takes everything the Root has.
- **It cannot act directly.** It is not a hostile or benevolent agent that intervenes. It is a sleeper whose continued sleep *is* the holding.
- **But its dreams have effects.** The bio-light across every catacomb-village is the Root's dream (per Phase 4 `06`); the Rooted communes with it (per Phase 1, the only god who can speak with the Root directly). The Root's agency reaches the world *only* through (a) its dreams (ambient, involuntary) and (b) the Rooted (its one volitional interface).
- **Its one available volitional act is the Renewal-choice: whether to wake.** This is the single decision the Root can make, and it can only express it through the Rooted, and only when the player has reached the Rooted (per `06`). **Whether the Root *wants* to wake is the Phase 6 commit** (deferred per `01_Spine.md` §348 and `06`). Phase 5's job is only to establish that the *capacity* for that one choice exists, and that it is the Root's sole act.

*(Cosmic-horror texture: the most powerful entity in the world is a sleeper who can do exactly one thing, once, and is otherwise spending all its strength simply not-letting-go. The Root is not a god you petition; it is a hand holding a door shut, and the only question it can answer is whether to let the door open.)*

## VII. The Tree as (former) actor

The Tree is **gone** — Felled, dispersed into the Six inheritances and the surviving Root (per Phase 1). It is **not an actor.** But it is not *absent* either:

- **The Tree IS the world's order now, distributed.** Every named, coherent thing is the Tree's binding still holding. The bound world is the Tree's body, scattered. In this sense the Tree is *everywhere and inert* — present as structure, absent as agent.
- **The Tree's intent persists as residual pressure.** The dreams that summoned the Six still echo faintly; the world "wants" to stay bound (things resist the Unsaying; names cling to their bearers). This is not the Tree *acting* — it is the *momentum* of the Tree's last great act, still rolling. **Soft bet (§XI):** the Tree's residual intent is what makes Renewal *possible* — the world has not forgotten how to be bound, because the Tree's binding-momentum hasn't fully stopped. A player pursuing Renewal is, in part, riding that momentum.
- **There is no "Tree-ghost" to meet.** The Tree does not speak, does not appear, has no avatar. The closest the player comes to the Tree is (a) the Root (its surviving fragment), (b) the Six (its inheritors), and (c) the Felling-Site (per Phase 2 — the place it died). *The Tree's tragedy (it commanded its own Felling, tried to take it back; per Phase 1) is felt only through these three relics.*

## VIII. The Branchwork

The **non-Choir distributed cognition** (per IDEAS.md:397; *Physarum*-grounded). Phase 5 places it: a **cosmic-neutral** actor — the one intelligence in the world with no stake in the Tree's order or its unmaking.

- **What it is.** A slime-mold-architecture intelligence, *consultative not consumptive* (per IDEAS.md:397): it solves problems for whoever feeds it nutrients; it does not assimilate; it advises. Where the Choir eats-to-know and converts substrate into Choir, the Branchwork simply *thinks*, for anyone, about anything, and keeps nothing.
- **Cosmological placement.** **Older than the factions, younger than the Tree, indifferent to both.** It is not a pre-Tree drive (it has a substrate-body and a clear architecture; the Spirits have neither), not an inheritance (no god, no Naming), not Urqu's kin (no relationship to the seventh absence). It is, uniquely, **the world's one disinterested mind** — a problem-solving tissue with no agenda. *(Soft bet, §XI: the Branchwork may be a fragment of the pre-Tree world that the binding never fully bound — a piece of the old chaos that happened to become *useful* instead of *hungry*. The neutral cousin of the Spirits: where they are unbound drives, it is unbound cognition.)*
- **Mechanical role.** A player who has fed the Branchwork in N zones can consult it for shortest-path navigation between known points, including through dead zones and contested catacombs (per IDEAS.md:397). Non-coercive, transactional, never exploitative — it never demands; it simply runs out of nutrients and the consultation stops.
- **Faction stake.** The Choir would consume it if it could (rival distributed intelligence; per IDEAS.md:397); the Branchwork avoids contact, communicates through left clues, rewards alliance with route-knowledge. *The Choir-Branchwork rivalry is the quiet mirror of the Choir-Bloom war: in both, the Choir cannot abide a distributed mind that isn't the Choir's — but the Bloom is a mindless necrosis and the Branchwork is a benign genius, and the Choir hunts them with the same appetite, which says something about the Choir.*
- **Tone.** The Branchwork is the project's small note of *clean wonder* — a non-horrifying non-human intelligence, genuinely helpful, asking nothing but lunch. In a world of cannibal gods and preservation-horror, the slime mold that just wants to solve your maze is a relief, and the relief is the point.

## IX. Lesser named entities — place-bound numina

`00_Canon.md` §196 asked whether small spirits attach to specific tepuis, bogs, sinkholes. **Committed: yes — as *local numina*, fragments of pre-Tree chaos pooled at high-Strangeness places.** Where the binding is thin (high Strangeness Tier, per Phase 2 v2), bits of the unbound old world *pool* — too small to be a Spirit (a world-wide drive), too incoherent to be a god, too place-bound to be Urqu. These are the lesser entities.

They are **not a roster to memorize** but a *generative category* — each high-Strangeness place can host one, hand-authored or lightly procedural. Design rules:

- **They are place-bound.** A lesser entity *is* its place; it cannot leave; killing or appeasing it changes the place. (Mechanically: a per-zone special entity tied to a Strangeness-Tier-3+ location.)
- **They are pre-moral** (like the Spirits, being pre-Tree): not evil, not good — *strange.* A bog-numen that takes a tithe of names; a sinkhole-presence that answers one true question and then forgets you; a tepui-thing that trades weather for stillness.
- **They are minor.** No lesser entity rivals Urqu or a Spirit. They are *texture* — the local color of a thin-bound world, the reason high-Strangeness places *feel* haunted in a way the cosmology now explains.
- **A few seed examples** (illustrative, not exhaustive):
  - **The Tithe-in-the-Peat** (bog numen) — pulls the long-dead Bog-Taken (per `The Preserved`) toward the surface; takes a name in exchange for safe passage; a player who refuses keeps their name and risks the bog.
  - **The One-Question** (deep-sinkhole presence) — answers a single true question per visitor, accurately, then *un-says* the visitor from its own memory (the player can never ask it twice; it will not know them).
  - **The Still-Weather** (high-tepui thing) — an Apatheia-adjacent local numen that offers perfect calm (no storms, no fauna, no time-pressure) at the cost of *also* stilling the player's own progress while present (a place that is safe precisely because nothing, including you, can change there).

*(These connect Strangeness Tiers to the actor-layer: the higher a place's Strangeness, the more of the old unbound world leaks into it, and a lesser entity is what that leak looks like when it pools enough to have a will.)*

## X. How it all relates to the endings

The three endings (Consume / Preserve / Renewal, per Phase 1) now have a **cosmological spine through the Spirits** — delivering the "third axis of final-act choice" IDEAS.md:188 asked for. The Spirit-Pact does not *determine* the ending; it **colors** it, and each Pact has a most-resonant ending:

| Spirit | Vector | Resonant ending | The coloring |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Inquiry** | toward binding | **Renewal** | the curious rebuilder; knows what the Root wants (reached the Rooted), understands the seventh-Naming choice, rides the Tree's residual binding-momentum (§VII). The Inquiry-Renewal is the *understood* renewal — rebuilt by someone who knows why. |
| **Apatheia** | toward stillness | **Preserve** | the still world is Apatheia's cosmic ideal (the unbound calm); the Apatheia-Preserve is the *peaceful* seal — chosen, serene, a world allowed to rest. (Its dark twin: a world that rests because nothing in it can act, the Bower's motionless ideal, `05`.) |
| **Bloodlust** | toward unbinding | **Consume** (with tension) | the drive to unmake finds its largest expression in the ending of maximal dissolution. *But Bloodlust and the Choir are rivals* — the Choir would eat Bloodlust — so the Bloodlust-Consume is not an alliance but a *convergence*: the player who has spent the game unbinding things arrives at the ending where everything is unbound-and-absorbed, and the Choir is merely the vehicle. The tension is the point. |

**Crucially, the resonance is not a lock.** A Bloodlust-Pact player can still choose Renewal (and the friction of that — a god of unmaking helping rebind the world — is rich content). The Spirit-Pact is the *grain* of the wood; the player can still cut against it. *(Phase 6 commits exactly how the Pact modifies each ending's availability and flavor.)*

And the **Spirit-killing** option (§III) interacts with endings darkly: a player who has killed a Spirit has *thinned that drive from the world*, which changes what the renewed/preserved/consumed world *contains.* A Renewal built by someone who killed Inquiry is a renewed world with less curiosity in it forever — a quiet, permanent diminishment baked into the "good" ending. *(Phase 6 / Phase 10.)*

## XII. Tonal balance check

- **Cosmic horror.** The dominant register of this phase, and now *structural*: the bound world is a thin skin over an older nameless thing; every act of will cracks the skin; the greatest power in the world is a sleeper holding a door shut; the one great evil is not a dark god but a *single absence* trying to be born. The Spirits being *prior to morality* — older than good and evil — is the coldest note. *Phase 5's horror is depth and scale, not gore.*
- **Biological horror.** Carried by the connective tissue rather than centered here: Urqu's mouthpieces vomiting ink to drink; the Bloom-as-agriculture; the lesser numina (the Tithe-in-the-Peat pulling Bog-Taken up). The Branchwork is the deliberate *non*-horror biological note (a benign slime intelligence) — a relief that makes the surrounding horror legible.
- **Comedy.** Present and welcome: the Concord *selling insurance against psychological reality* (pay drams now, we'll run interference if a Spirit comes calling — a protection racket against your own drives, per IDEAS.md:176); the Branchwork, a world-class genius that asks only for lunch and forgets you politely when the lunch runs out; the One-Question numen's bureaucratic-feeling "one accurate answer, then I forget you exist." *The comedy is the bound world's institutions trying to *monetize and file* the unbound — which is exactly the project's central joke, now applied to the cosmos.*
