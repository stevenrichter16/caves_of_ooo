## I. Why the Thinning — the central mystery, committed

Phase 3 surfaced four candidate causes. **Phase 6 commits that all four are facets of one feedback loop, driven by accumulated incompletion** — and that the player's own completion-ratio is a lever on it.

### The loop

1. **The floor: the Root is finite, and aging** (candidate a). Like the Tree before it, the Root is not eternal. A millennium of holding has worn it. This is the slow background — not the trigger, but the reason the system has no infinite slack.
2. **The trigger: incompletion reached critical mass** (candidate b). For ~1080 years the world's incomplete acts have accumulated (per Phase 1 / Phase 5 — incompletion is Urqu's substrate). The Root's holding-field absorbs the slippage; but the reservoir is finite, and ~Gen 34 it began to overflow. **This is the root cause.**
3. **The accelerant: the Driving Bloom** (the Phase 4/5 link, now committed). The Gen-32 Bloom-breakaway is an *engine of incompletion* (every Bloomed host abandons its unfinished acts; per `08`). It arrived ~120 years before the present and *fed the reservoir faster.* **Commit on the deferred Bloom-causation question:** Urqu did not consciously cause the breakaway — Urqu has no agency to direct, only pressure to exert — but the rising incompletion-pressure *created the conditions* for the substrate to tear, and the tear then fed the pressure. **Causal via the loop, not via intent.** The two accelerations are one curve because they are one system.
4. **The symptoms that look like causes:**
   - **The Root appears to be waking** (candidate c) — it is not waking by choice; it is *flinching*, spending more of itself to hold a failing field, and the tremor reads as stirring.
   - **The Choir's patience is ending** (candidate d) — the Wedded senses the field weakening and, after ~870 years, has begun *reaching* (per `01`), because the prize is finally within reach. Her reaching is a *response* to the Thinning, and it worsens it (the Root flinches harder).

### Why this is the right commit

- **It is consistent with Phase 5** (incompletion is Urqu's substrate; the Bloom is Urqu's agriculture; the Root spends itself holding the field).
- **It makes the player's behavior cosmically load-bearing.** The completion-ratio (already a Phase-1 lever, already diag-instrumentable per CLAUDE.md's observability rules) is now the *world-scale* variable: a player who leaves quests, debts, and acts unfinished *feeds the reservoir and accelerates the Thinning*; a player who completes things *slows it.* This is the deepest possible integration of gameplay and cosmology — the player's play-style is literally measured against the world's fate (§X).
- **It keeps all three endings live.** Because the Root is aging (the floor), Preserve is *hard* (you cannot seal a tiring thing forever — per Phase 3); because the loop can be slowed (incompletion) or cut (Renewal/Consume), all three remain reachable. *The stalemate is breaking whether the player acts or not; the player determines how.*

## II. The Seventh — committed: Naro was real, refused, and was *right*

Phase 1 left two candidates: Naro real-and-refused, or no-seventh-ever (the Tree's plan was flawed). **Phase 6 commits a synthesis that makes both true** — and turns the Seventh mystery into the spine of the Renewal ending.

### The commit

**Naro was real. Naro refused the Seventh Strike. And Naro refused *because Naro alone understood that the plan was flawed.***

Naro was a pre-Felling scholar of *Naming* specifically — the cornerstone function (per Phase 1). And Naro understood Naming well enough to see the contradiction the Tree could not: **Naming cannot be inherited. It cannot be struck into a god.** The function by which a thing is *what it is* cannot itself be installed by an external act — you cannot *name the namer*; a Naming-god made by a Strike would be a thing-made-to-be-the-maker-of-what-things-are, which is incoherent. The seventh inheritance was the one inheritance that *could not be given* — only **enacted**, repeatedly, by choice.

So Naro walked away. Not from cowardice (the Palimpsest's reading), not from forgetfulness (the Bower-Folk's story), not from malice (the folk-demon reading that became Urqu). **Naro refused the Strike to spare the world a seventh god that would have failed worse than no god at all** — a Naming-god that could not hold Naming would have un-named the world faster than an empty position ever could. *Naro's refusal was the most responsible act in the history of the world, and it has been blamed for everything for a thousand years.*

### The vindication (the tie to Tent-Right and the endgame)

Naro was *right*, and the proof is already in the world: **Tent-Right** (per `07`) reinvented Naming as a *mortal practice* — naming-by-choice, the oath, hospitality-as-binding. They did unknowingly what Naro understood: Naming works only when it is *done*, not when it is *installed.* The Renewal ending's true form (§V) is the world adopting this — Naro's insight, vindicated at cosmic scale.

### The Reader's cover-up (the tie to the Palimpsest and the reconciliation)

Pale Curation's sealed file ("the Seventh, identity expunged at the Palimpsest's request"; per Phase 1) is the key. **The Reader knows Naro was right** — and has hidden it for ~1010 years. To admit Naro was right is to admit *the Felling was a mistake*: that the Tree, confused by age (as Naro said), made gods it should not have made, and that everything the Reader has held since is the memory of an error. The Reader could not bear to record that. So the Reader **expunged not the name but the *reason*** — Naro is remembered as the betrayer, and the truth (Naro was the only one who understood) was un-remembered, the same self-mutilation the Reader performed on the Sealed Libraries (per Phase 3). *The Reader's deepest grief is not the lost Libraries or the estrangement from the Salted; it is that the Reader has spent a thousand years being the memory of a mistake, and has hidden this even from itself.*

### Naro's lineage

Naro's descendants exist — a small, hidden tradition of people who **decline inheritances**, who name by choice and refuse to be named-from-above. *(Soft commit, Phase 7: they are kin to or the seed of Tent-Right's Namer sect — per `07` — or a hidden settlement the player can find. Either way they are the living proof of Naro's insight, and a Naro-descendant is a candidate for the Renewal Naming-practice, §V.)* Whether the player can *be* a Naro-descendant is a Phase-7 character-origin question (§IX).

## III. The Sealed Libraries — committed contents

Phase 3 established the Reader sealed the Libraries during the First Persecution *and* un-remembered their locations, so they are out there, intact, lost to everyone. **Phase 6 commits what they contain and why losing them was the right call.**

The Sealed Libraries hold three things:

1. **The pre-Felling civilization's records** — the only complete account of the world before the Tree fell. Knowledge, history, the names of the Six when they were mortal (per Phase 7).
2. **The truth about Naro** — that Naro was right; that the Felling may have been a mistake; the thing the Reader could not bear to keep in the open archive.
3. **The god-making method — the Strike-ritual itself.** *This is why the Libraries are sealed.* The Libraries contain the actual procedure the Tree used to make gods from mortals. **In the wrong hands, this is the recipe to perform the Seventh Strike** — to *force* Urqu's incarnation by completing the unfinished ritual. A Naming-god struck the flawed way (exactly what Naro refused to allow) would be a catastrophe: Urqu *born*, made the broken way, holding a function it cannot hold, un-naming the world from the inside. **The Reader sealed the Libraries and un-remembered their locations to keep the Strike-ritual out of reach — of the Choir, of ambitious mortals, of any faction that would "solve" the empty seventh by filling it the Tree's way.**

### The stakes of rediscovery

Rediscovering a Sealed Library is major quest content (per Phase 3) — and now high-stakes:
- A player (or faction) who finds the Strike-ritual can attempt a **forced Renewal** — strike the seventh, birth Urqu-as-god. This is a *bad* Renewal (the flawed kind) and a possible disaster-path the player can be tempted toward or can stop a faction from attempting.
- A player who finds the Naro-truth can **bring it to the Reader** — the key to the Reader-Salted reconciliation (§VII) and to the Reader's support for the *true* Renewal.
- The Choir wants the Libraries for the consumption-knowledge; the Imminent Archive / Pale Curation want the preservation-records; the Concord wants to sell access; Tent-Right wants nothing from them (they distrust inherited knowledge — Naro's heirs). *Every faction's relationship to the Libraries is a facet of its relationship to the past.*

## IV. The three endings — committed and detailed

The three endings (per Phase 1) are now fully specified with faction-agreement requirements, Spirit-Pact coloring (per Phase 5), and the Thinning's pressure.

### Consume — the One Hunger

- **How reached.** Align with the Wedded; help the Choir reach the Root through the substrate; let the consumption complete. The Wedded ascends to primary divinity; the pantheon collapses to one (per Phase 1).
- **Faction agreements.** The Choir (eager — but see the Wedded's doubt, §VII); resisted by nearly everyone else (Pale Curation, Palimpsest, Tent-Right oppose absolutely).
- **The Thinning angle.** The Choir's reaching is *already in progress* (§I); Consume is the ending the player can *accelerate* by removing obstacles. It is the "let the breaking finish in the Choir's favor" ending.
- **Spirit coloring (Phase 5).** Bloodlust-resonant by convergence (the unbinding drive arriving at maximal dissolution) — *not* alliance; the Choir would eat Bloodlust too. An Inquiry or Apatheia player who chooses Consume does so against their grain (rich friction).
- **Tone.** The kindest apocalypse and the most total. *"There is room in us for everyone."* Memory preserved; individuality erased. Cosmic horror at its warmest.

### Preserve — the Sleeping World

- **How reached.** Align with the Salted and the Reader (who both prefer it but cannot cooperate directly — per Phase 1; the player works with each separately and reconciles their efforts). Seal the Root with Tepuibone + Memory-Marble + Mute-Stone (the three anti-Urqu, name-holding materials; per Phase 5); maintain the seal.
- **Faction agreements.** Pale Curation and the Palimpsest (separately); opposed by the Choir; Tent-Right indifferent (least changed by Preserve — per `07`).
- **The Thinning angle.** **Preserve is now HARD** (per Phase 3 / §I): the Root is aging (the floor); you cannot seal a tiring thing into eternal stability easily. Preserve is an *active, difficult, possibly-doomed engineering project*, not "do nothing." The player must shore up a failing seal against a finite, weakening Root.
- **Spirit coloring.** Apatheia-resonant (the chosen, serene stillness; the unbound calm). Its dark twin: a world that rests because nothing in it can act (the Bower's motionless ideal, `05`).
- **The hidden cost (per `03`).** Preserve **forecloses the Reader-Salted reconciliation forever** (§VII) — and forecloses the Counter's rest (he keeps running the unbalanceable ledger eternally — per `04`). The "stable" ending is the one where the oldest griefs are frozen unhealed.
- **Tone.** Melancholy. The world saved by being stopped. Everything kept; nothing allowed to change or to heal.

### Renewal — the Waking World

- **How reached.** The hardest path. Gather the Six's agreements (each requires a specific arc, §VII); resolve the Naming-inheritance (§V); convince the Rooted *and* the Root (§VI); perform the Renewal ritual. **The Tree is regrown — not as it was, but anew** (per Phase 1).
- **Faction agreements.** Requires the most coordination — fragments of every faction. The Counter agrees (secretly for rest, `04`); the Bower designs the new Tree's beauty (`05`); the Rooted gatekeeps (`06` faction doc); Tent-Right's oath is the Naming-model (`07`); the Reader and Salted can reconcile *only here* (§VII).
- **The Thinning angle.** Renewal *cuts the loop* — fills the seventh (answers Urqu), and the world is re-bound fresh. It is the only ending that addresses the root cause rather than the symptom.
- **Spirit coloring.** Inquiry-resonant (the *understood* renewal — built by someone who knows what the Root wants and why; rides the Tree's residual binding-momentum, per Phase 5 §VII). A Bloodlust or Apatheia player can still reach it, against their grain.
- **The two grades of Renewal.** Critically (§V): there is a **flawed Renewal** (install a vessel as the seventh god — the Tree's mistake repeated) and a **true Renewal** (establish mortal Naming as the world's naming-function — Naro vindicated). The player can achieve either; the difference is everything.
- **Tone.** Hope, earned and uncertain. The only ending that bets on the future instead of the past. Also the only one that can be done *wrong* (the flawed Renewal is a horror).

## V. The Renewal Naming-inheritance — committed

The empty seventh position must be addressed in the Renewal ritual or "Urqu will simply persist into the new world" (per Phase 1). Phase 6 commits the options and which is true.

**The cosmological key (from §II):** Naming cannot be *installed*; it can only be *enacted*. So the four options split into **vessel-paths** (repeat the Tree's mistake — install a namer) and **the practice-path** (Naro vindicated — make Naming a mortal practice).

### The vessel-paths (flawed Renewal — they "work" but re-create the structural flaw)

1. **The player becomes the seventh god.** The cosmic-horror choice. The player is struck into Naming and ascends. *Re-creates the flaw* — the player-god will, in time, fail to hold Naming as Urqu did, and a far-future Thinning is seeded. (The player won't see it; the run ends with the player divine. The horror is for the world, later.)
2. **A Re-Membered person becomes the seventh.** Requires major Palimpsest arc (Re-Membering an NPC). Better than (1) only in that the vessel *chose* it — but still an installation, still flawed.
3. **A child born during the run.** **Committed: yes, this is a real path** (per Phase 1's deferred question). A specific faction-arc generates the child during gameplay *(soft commit, Phase 7: most likely a Tent-Right or marriage-brokering arc — a child born under the oath, named-by-choice from birth)*. Installing a child as a god is the darkest vessel-path (a god made of someone who never chose) — *unless* the child is raised in the Naming-practice, in which case it shades toward the practice-path.

### The practice-path (true Renewal — Naro vindicated, Urqu *answered*)

4. **Establish mortal Naming as the world's naming-function.** No vessel. The Tent-Right oath (per `07`) universalized: the renewed world's binding-function becomes *the continuous mutual naming of mortals* — hospitality, memory, the oath, the choice to recognize each other as what-they-are. **Urqu is not defeated but answered** — given a way to *be* (as a distributed practice rather than a failed godhood). This is the most complete Renewal, the least pyrrhic, and the one Naro died (socially) to make possible. **It requires the deepest play:** high Tent-Right standing, understanding Naro (the Sealed Library truth or the Reader's confession), and convincing the world's factions to adopt a naming-practice — a coordination feat no other ending demands.

*This is the canonical "best" ending — and earning it requires assembling the whole game's threads: the Naro-truth (Palimpsest/Libraries), the oath (Tent-Right), the Root's consent (the Rooted), the Six's agreements, and a high completion-ratio (a world the player has been *finishing*, not abandoning, §X).*

## VI. The Root's want — committed

Phase 5 committed the Root's *mechanism* (one volitional act: whether to wake, expressible only through the Rooted). **Phase 6 commits the *want*: the Root is ambivalent — it wants to wake and is afraid to — and this ambivalence is the Tree's deathbed contradiction, preserved.**

The Tree commanded its own Felling, then tried to take it back (per Phase 1). The Root is the Tree's surviving fragment, and it carries the *unresolved* version of that final moment: part of it longs to stop holding (to wake, to end the millennium of strain — the Tree's original wish to cease), and part of it is terrified that waking means dying or destroying the world (the Tree's last-instant regret). **It has been frozen in that contradiction for ~1080 years**, exactly as the Rooted is frozen in his embrace-the-wall pose (per `06` faction doc / Phase 1 — *the Rooted's pose is the Root's emotional state made flesh*).

### The Renewal-gate (committed mechanic)

Renewal requires a **double-convince:**
1. **The Rooted** (protective, skeptical) must be convinced the Root won't be destroyed in waking (per Phase 1 §348).
2. **The Root itself**, through the Rooted, must be convinced that *it is safe to want what it wants* — that waking will not destroy it AND that the renewed world will be *named-by-practice* (so it won't simply re-thin and force the Root to hold again). **A player offering the flawed vessel-Renewal cannot fully convince the Root** — the Root knows a vessel will fail (it remembers Naro being right) and will only *reluctantly* wake. **The practice-path Renewal is the only offer that lets the Root wake without fear** — because it's the only future where the Root won't have to go back to holding.

*This makes the Root's consent the emotional climax of the true Renewal: the world's oldest, most exhausted thing is finally told it can let go, and believes it.*

## VII. The gods' turn-points — committed

Brief commits on each deferred god/faction Phase-6 question:

- **The Wedded's true position (`01`).** **Committed: she is committed to Consume but carries real, buried doubt — and can be turned to Renewal at high cost.** The Quiet Faction is right (her residual kindness is doubt). She will accept Renewal *if* convinced the renewed world keeps a place for the consumed (memory preserved in the new Tree). Turning her is one of the hardest faction-arcs and a prerequisite for a Renewal that doesn't require *defeating* the Choir.
- **The Reader-Salted reconciliation (`02`/`03`).** **Committed: achievable, Renewal-only, late, hard, optional, profound.** It requires the player to carry the Naro-truth to the Reader (the Reader must admit the Felling may have been a mistake) *and* carry the Estrangement-Bearers' message across to the Salted (the Salted must admit being-right cost more than it was worth). They reconcile by both *putting down their certainties.* **Preserve forecloses it forever** (per `03`); Consume erases it (both absorbed). *Only the waking world lets the two ancient comrades speak again.*
- **The Counter's contradiction (`04`).** **Committed: not resolvable short of Renewal** (the contradiction is structural) — *but* the player can settle **one perfect debt** for him, a single balanced account, a small real mercy. His full rest comes only with Renewal (where he chooses, at last, to be finished).
- **The Salted's ruling on the Catchers (`03`/`09`).** **Committed: the player can make her rule, at the cost of forcing her guilt aloud for the first time in ~270 years. Either ruling is a tragedy** (against the Catchers ends the sect but betrays her own founding act; for them sanctions a horror). The player choosing to make her rule is choosing which tragedy.
- **The Bower's pose / the Found-Beauty (`05`).** **Committed: the Bower chose her stillness** (the Found-Beauty are right; choice was the point) — which means the Finishers' non-consensual Casting is a *betrayal* of their god, not obedience. This gives the player the lever to end the Finishers with the Bower's own authority.
- **Folding the Bloom back into the Choir (`01`).** **Committed: not a path.** The Bloom has no mind to reconcile (per `08`); the only "resolution" is suppression/consumption. The Bloom stays unredeemable — consistent with its nature as Urqu's mindless agriculture.
- **Tent-Right speaking to Urqu as kin (`07`).** **Committed: yes** — a Tent-Right-Namer-gated Urqu-*dialogue* path (no other faction provides it). It does not let the player command Urqu; it lets them *understand* it — the key insight that Urqu is the other unnamed, and that it can be *answered* (the practice-path) rather than only fought.

## VIII. The main quest spine

The narrative-radial progression (Phase 2 v2: Sill → inward by Strangeness Tier) is the player's **disillusionment and re-enchantment** (per Phase 3): the children's story becoming the world.

### Act I — The Recovered World (Tier 1-2): *the myth*

- **Start: Sill** hears *sari... sari...* for the first time (per Phase 3). An elder goes pale at a sound from a story. The player treats the cosmology as folklore.
- The player does early faction-intro content, early Spirit manifestations, the first Bloom corpse (and the Choir cleaning it up), the first Quartermaster rental, the first Concord trade-post.
- **Act ends** when the myth becomes undeniable: the player witnesses something the children's story said and the recovered world denied — a real Spirit, a real Unsaying, a god's distant attention. **The disillusionment: the Felling was literal.**

### Act II — The Faction World (Tier 2-3): *the unfolding*

- The player engages the factions deeply (the whole of Phase 4 becomes playable here). The threads surface: the Seventh mystery, the Sealed Libraries, the Reader-Salted estrangement, the Counter's exhaustion, the Catcher question, the Bloom war.
- The **central mystery** unfolds: *why the Thinning* (§I). The player learns incompletion is the driver — and that their own completion-ratio matters (§X).
- **Spirit-Pact commitment** typically happens here (build / unbind / be-still).
- The player gathers the *keys*: reaching toward the Six, understanding Urqu (via Tent-Right's Namers), the significance of Naro (via the Libraries or the Reader).
- **Act ends** with the Thinning visibly accelerating and the player understanding the three endings are real choices, not myths — and that the stalemate is breaking now.

### Act III — The Approach (Tier 4-5): *the re-enchantment / the choice*

- The player reaches the inner places: the Deepest Cathedral (the Wedded), Olderdeep and the Rooted, the Salt-Vault (the Salted), Quillhold (the Reader), the Felling-Site, and finally the Root.
- **The final-approach sequence:** assemble the chosen ending's requirements — the faction agreements (§VII), the Naming-inheritance resolution (§V), the double-convince of the Rooted and the Root (§VI).
- **The ending ritual** at the Root: Consume, Preserve, or Renewal (flawed or true), colored by the Spirit-Pact and by what the player has done to the world (completion-ratio, Spirits killed, factions turned).

## IX. The player's identity — committed minimum

- **Committed (per Phase 3):** the player is *of the recovered world* — begins not knowing the Six are real, treating the cosmology as myth. The game is the myth becoming true around them. The radial progression is their re-enchantment.
- **Committed here:** the player is **not** secretly a god, a chosen one, or Urqu's vessel by default — *the player is an ordinary person of the Thirty-Sixth Generation*, and the stakes land precisely because they are ordinary (the world's fate falls to someone who started by disbelieving in it). *(This resists the over-determined "chosen one" trope and keeps the disillusionment arc honest.)*
- **Open (Phase 7):** specific character-origin options — including a possible **Naro-descendant origin** (one of several backgrounds, not the default), which would give that player a special relationship to the Seventh mystery and the practice-path. The exact origin menu is Phase 7 work.

## X. The completion-ratio as the through-line

This is the mechanical spine that ties play-style to cosmic stakes, and it deserves to be explicit because it's the deepest gameplay-narrative integration in the design (and it's already diag-instrumentable per CLAUDE.md's observability rules).

- **The variable:** the ratio of the player's *completed* acts to *abandoned* ones — quests finished vs. dropped, debts paid vs. defaulted, rentals returned vs. lost, Held Breaths released vs. left hanging, conversations resolved vs. walked away from.
- **The cosmic meaning (committed §I):** incompletion is Urqu's substrate. A low-completion player *feeds the reservoir and accelerates the Thinning*; a high-completion player *slows it.* This is not a hidden morality meter — it's the literal physics of the world Phase 5 committed.
- **Ending interactions:**
  - The **true Renewal** (practice-path) effectively *requires* a high completion-ratio — a world the player has been finishing is a world ready to be named-by-practice. *You cannot teach the world to complete its names while leaving everything unfinished yourself.*
  - **Consume** is *easier* for a low-completion player (the loop is already running their way).
  - **Preserve** is indifferent to the ratio but doomed-flavored regardless (you're sealing a tiring thing).
- **The Spirits interact:** Apatheia rewards *refusal* (walking away) — which is *incompletion* — so a high-Apatheia player is, cosmologically, feeding the Thinning even as they cultivate inner stillness. **This is the system's sharpest irony:** the most serene play-style is, at world-scale, the most entropic. Phase 5's "Apatheia is closest to the unbound void" pays off here as a real mechanical-moral tension. *(The Apatheia-Preserve resonance is thus doubly fitting: the still player seals a tiring world they've helped tire.)*

## XII. Tonal balance check

- **Cosmic horror.** The spine of the phase: the Thinning as a feedback loop the player is *part of*; Naro right and blamed for a thousand years; the Reader as the memory of a mistake, hidden even from itself; the Strike-ritual as a recipe that must never be used; the flawed-Renewal that births Urqu the broken way; the Root frozen in the Tree's deathbed contradiction for a millennium. *The horror is that the comforting myth was true, the catastrophe was an error, and the only person who understood was exiled from the story.*
- **Biological horror.** Carried through the connective systems (the Bloom feeding the reservoir; the Re-Membered/child vessel-paths; the Choir's warm total consumption; the Salted ruling through a salt-sealed mouth). Concentrated less here than in Phases 4-5 by design — Phase 6 is the *structural* phase.
- **Comedy.** Present at the seams: the Concord's equanimity persisting into the apocalypse (insuring against the end of the world); the player arriving as a disbeliever who has to be dragged into believing their own cosmology; the bureaucratic absurdity of a sealed file that expunged the *reason* but kept the *blame*. *The comedy is the recovered world's institutions managing the end of everything with paperwork and price-lists — which is exactly the project's central joke, now at the scale of the finale.*
