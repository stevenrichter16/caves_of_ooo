## I. The Six — their mortal names

The deepest pattern of the Six (established across the faction docs) is that **each god's relationship to its own mortal name is a map of its function.** Phase 7 commits the names *and* the way the player learns each — because the *how* of learning is itself characterization.

| God | Function | Mortal name | Its fate | How the player learns it |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| The Reader | Memory | **Maeleth** (commonly *Mael*) | **Remembered everywhere.** The god of Memory remembers itself. | Free; public; in every Palimpsest archive. The player knows it early. |
| The Wedded | Substrate | **Selen** | **Forgotten by all** — even the substrate, even the Reader. A thousand years dissolved even the name of a god. | Recoverable from *one source only*: **the Counter**, who knew her before and kept the one account everyone else lost (§ below). |
| The Salted | Preservation | **Othren** | **Filed.** The name is known and on her file; it is her *last words* that are unrecorded ("[unrecorded]"). | Readable at high Pale Curation standing — on her file, of course. |
| The Bower | Beauty | **Ylaes** | **Never written.** She found written names inelegant; the name exists only as spoken sound. *(The CP437 spelling "Ylaes" is an approximation; the true sound is only ever heard.)* | The player **hears** it spoken in Posy; it never appears as readable text in the game. |
| The Counter | Exchange | **Tovreth** (commonly *Tov*) | **The most-written, least-read name in the world** — on every contract he ever signed; people sign with him without reading it. | A late reveal that *the player has seen it a hundred times* (on every contract) without ever reading it. It was in front of them all along. |
| The Rooted | Roots | **Dohren** | **Buried deepest.** Spoken only in the deepest catacomb-village rite; written only at the very bottom of the oldest plaque-wall (the founding-niche, near the floor — per `catacomb_village_design.md`). | Learnable through deep catacomb-village content — the name at the root of the wall, as he is the root of the world. |

### The Counter keeps the Wedded's name (committed; resolves `01` + `04` open questions)

The Wedded's name (**Selen**) is gone from everyone — except **the Counter (Tovreth)**, who knew her when they were both mortal and the Tree still stood (per `04` §IX soft-recommendation; per `01`'s "the only one of the Six who might still speak of the Wedded as a person"). **Phase 7 commits: the Counter is the sole keeper of Selen's name.** This pays off three threads at once:
- **`04`'s three-minutes-a-year scene** — what the Counter says in his rare non-transacting moment is *her name*, the one account he never let go.
- **The Wedded's turn-point (`01` / Phase 6 §VII)** — turning the Wedded to Renewal may require the player to *carry her own forgotten name back to her*, spoken aloud by the one who remembered. The god who cannot remember herself, reminded of who she was, by the friend who kept the account. *This is a candidate for the most affecting single beat in the game.*
- **The cosmology, made personal** — the god of Memory (Maeleth) lost the Wedded's name; the god of Exchange (Tovreth) kept it. *Memory forgot; the ledger remembered.* Exactly inverted, exactly right.

*(Open, Phase 8/level-design: whether learning Selen's name has a mechanical effect — e.g., it is a "true name" usable in the Renewal ritual or in the anti-Urqu naming-practice. Soft lean: yes; a recovered true name is a small act of the practice-path Naming, §IV / Phase 6 §V.)*

### The pre-Felling specialties (committed soft seeds, per the faction docs)

- **Maeleth** — a transcriber of oral histories; already spent her mortal life recording what others said (per `02`). Ascension *intensified* what she was.
- **Tovreth** — a keeper-of-accounts; already tracked who-owed-what (per `04`). Likewise intensified.
- **Selen** — a woman in her thirties, *kind and quiet* (per `01`). (Specialty open; her defining trait is the kindness that survives, attenuated, in the Choir.)
- **Othren** — a scholar, Maeleth's colleague-and-almost-certainly-more (per `03`); the schism was theological-then-personal.
- **Ylaes** — a young artist (per `05`).
- **Dohren** — open; the most withdrawn even pre-Felling (soft seed: someone already drawn to depth, to roots, to what-lies-beneath).

## II. The Seventh — Naro

Committed in Phase 6: **Naro was real, refused the Seventh Strike, and was right** (Naming cannot be installed, only enacted). Phase 7 gives Naro a character and a living lineage.

- **Who Naro was.** A pre-Felling scholar of *Naming* specifically — the only one of the seven chosen who studied the cornerstone function deeply enough to understand it. Phonically, **Naro's name is the odd one out** (ends in *-o*, where the Six end in *-en / -eth / -a / -aes / -ren*) — a small, deliberate mark that the seventh was always outside the pattern.
- **What Naro did.** Understood that a Naming-god made by a Strike would fail worse than no god (you cannot name the namer); refused the Strike to spare the world; walked away. *The most responsible act in the history of the world, blamed for everything for a thousand years.*
- **What the world thinks Naro was.** The Palimpsest's betrayer; the Bower-Folk's sleeper-who-forgot-the-day; the folk-demon that, mythologized, fed the *idea* of Urqu. None of these is the truth, and the Reader (Maeleth) has hidden the truth for ~1010 years (Phase 6 §II).
- **Naro's lineage — the Declined.** Naro's descendants survive as a small tradition (working name: **the Declined**) — people who *refuse inheritances*, who name by choice and reject being named-from-above. They are kin to (or the seed of) Tent-Right's **Namers** (per `07`). They keep no titles, take no thrones, decline grandeur even in their own names.
- **The named living descendant: *Saedis*.** A quiet keeper of the Declined tradition, findable late-game (soft location: a hidden settlement, or among the Namers in the Beating). **Saedis carries the family memory that Naro was right** — the oral counter-history to the Palimpsest's official record. Saedis is the player's human door into the true-Renewal practice-path: the descendant of the one who understood Naming, teaching the player that Naming is something you *do.* *(Saedis does not want to be important; helping the player is itself a small act of the practice — naming the player as worthy of the truth.)*

## III. Named NPCs by faction

The anchor cast — quest-givers and arc-anchors. One to three per faction.

### Palimpsest

- **Archive-Master Telleth** (Quillhold) — senior institutional authority; gatekeeper to the deep archives and the Reader's channeled voice; assigns the major Palimpsest arc (the Sealed Libraries, the Naro-truth).
- **Field Scribe Iren** — the player's *first* Palimpsest contact; mobile, young, earnest; the conventional early quest-giver (delivery-of-records, transcription jobs) who later becomes the player's tie to the Body-Reader / Inkbound content.
- *(The Reader, Maeleth, speaks through any scribe — so any Palimpsest NPC can, at a major beat, suddenly be the god.)*

### Rot Choir

- **The Elder of the Quiet** — a Spore-Wedded villager (per `06`) who chose integration and now speaks *for* the Quiet Faction (`01`): the substrate-node that has heard doubt in the Wedded's residual voice. The player's path to the Choir's internal heresy and to the question of turning the Wedded. *Has a pre-integration name the player can learn — **Vesh** — which the Elder still uses, unusually, because the Quiet Faction values the names the substrate would otherwise dissolve.*

### Pale Curation

- **Curator Hesh** — reads the Salted's file aloud at the annual ceremony; the most procedure-bound officer in the world; the player's interface to the Salted's chamber and the file-update ritual. Bureaucratic-comedy anchor (per `03`).
- **Sorter Vael** — pursues the Sorters' grail: the seventh's-body (per `03`). Vael has spent decades on a body that may never have existed; the player's tie to the seventh's-body quest and its Renewal-Naming implications.

### Saccharine Concord

- **Factor Brenn** — the player's first trade-post contact; sells the half-working Catcher/Urqu charms (per `04`); the conventional early Concord quest-giver (deliveries, debt-collection).
- **The Counter (Tovreth)** — met personally at Tally, meeting-fee gated; the most human-presenting of the Six; the keeper of Selen's name (§I); the contradiction-revelation arc (`04`).

### Bower-Folk

- **Seer Liss** — reads the Bower's gestures in Posy and relays her wishes (per `05`); the player's interface to Ylaes and the behavior-mediated dialogue.
- **Caster Oel** — a **Found-Beauty** leader who refuses compelled casting (per `05`); the player's ally against the Finishers, and the voice arguing the Bower *chose* her pose (Phase 6 §VII commits Oel is right).

### Imminent Archive (Catchers) — committed Phase-7 details

- **Sister Wenil** — the gentle one; survived a Thinning-era Urqu manifestation in which she watched a child un-named (`09`). **Committed: if the player kills her, her last words are an apology — *to the player, for failing to save them.*** The player carries it afterward.
- **Brother Kavin** — the missionary; relentless cheerful evangelism about being preserved alive (`09`); the comedy-of-sincere-horror anchor.
- **Filer Pais** — the clinical one; dictates the player's own preservation in progress. **Committed: Pais's trauma is *filed and never spoken of* — the most Pale-Curation answer** (`09`). The blank where a reason should be is the characterization.

### Tent-Right

- **Namer Oreth** — lead of the emerging Namers (`07`); the one who has gone furthest toward the truth (the oath is mortal Naming; Tent-Right and Urqu are the two unnamed). The player's guide to the Urqu-as-kin dialogue path and the practice-path Renewal.
- **Tent-Mother Saren** — Oath-Keeper authority of Wellmeet; enforces the three-day oath as law; the player's interface to Tent-Right hospitality-sanctuary and standing.

### Catacomb-villagers

- **Light-Mother Riane** — Patch-Tender / First Elder of a hub village (the name honors `catacomb_village_design.md`'s own example lineage — Riane, granddaughter of Riane the Patch-Tender, line 92). The player's interface to villager faction-politics, the Rooted's bio-light theology, and the route toward Olderdeep and Dohren's name.
- *(Most villager NPCs are the role-archetypes of the design file; Riane is the named anchor.)*

### The Declined / cosmic

- **Saedis** (§II) — Naro's living descendant; the true-Renewal practice-path's human teacher.

## IV. The child-arc — committed specifics

Phase 6 §V committed the **child-born-during-the-run** as a real Renewal-Naming vessel-path, soft-recommending a Tent-Right / marriage arc. Phase 7 commits the specifics:

- **The arc is Tent-Right-gated.** The player, at high Tent-Right standing, can broker (or be party to) a **bond formalized under the oath** — a child conceived and born *under the three-day bond's protection*, named-by-choice from birth in the Namer tradition (per `07` Namers, the Declined practice).
- **Why this child is special.** A child **named by choice from its first breath, under the oath, by people who decline inheritance** is the *least-flawed possible vessel* — because it was never *installed* into a name; it was *enacted* into one, the practice-path way (Phase 6 §V). **This is the vessel-path that shades into the practice-path:** if the player raises (or commits) this child as the Renewal Naming-inheritance, it is the one vessel-choice that doesn't simply repeat the Tree's mistake — because the child is already a *practitioner* of Naming, not a *recipient* of it.
- **The arc's shape (committed structure; specifics to level-design).** A multi-stage Tent-Right relationship arc: standing → brokering/joining a bond → the bond's protection tested (a guest-crisis the player resolves) → the birth, under the oath. The child has no name until born, and naming it is a player-participatory beat (the player helps *enact* the name — a small live instance of the practice-path).
- **The grim alternative the arc guards against.** If a player pursues the child purely as a *tool* for the ritual (not raised in the practice), it collapses back into the darkest vessel-path (a god made of someone who never chose; Phase 6 §V). *The arc's moral weight is whether the player treats the child as a person or a key.*

*(Open, Phase 8/level-design: pacing — the arc must be startable early enough to complete before the finale; whether there is one canonical bond-partner NPC or a choice.)*

## V. The player character-origin menu — committed

Per Phase 6 §IX: the player is an **ordinary person of the recovered world** (not a chosen one), beginning in disbelief, undergoing the myth-becoming-true arc. Phase 7 commits the **origin menu** — backgrounds that color *where you start, what you start disbelieving, and which thread you have a head-start on.* None makes the player special-by-destiny; each is a different ordinary life.

Committed origins (five; a sixth is the special case):

1. **River-Child of Sill** — the default / recommended start (per Phase 3). Grew up where the Tree is a children's story and the river-god gets the offerings. The purest disillusionment arc; no head-start, maximum re-enchantment.
2. **Concord Ledger-Hand** — apprenticed to a Factor; numerate, well-traveled, cynical. Head-start on the Concord and on trade/economy systems; begins *most* disbelieving (commerce has no use for cosmology).
3. **Failed Scribe** — washed out of a Palimpsest field-station; literate, knows the official histories (including the Palimpsest's lie about Naro, which the player will later learn was a lie). Head-start on the Palimpsest and on the Seventh thread; the most painful re-enchantment (your education was partly propaganda).
4. **Wasteland Guest** — grew up under the oath in a Tent-Right settlement; knows hospitality as law, distrusts inherited authority. Head-start on Tent-Right, the Namers, and the practice-path; *least* surprised when the cosmology turns out real (the oath always felt larger than survival).
5. **Catacomb Apostate** — left a catacomb-village for the surface (an apostate, per `catacomb_village_design.md`); knows bio-light, the Sealed Dim, the Root-Traditions as lived faith, not myth. Head-start on the villagers, the Rooted, and Urqu-defense; the *least* disbelieving (you grew up in the dark the surface forgot).

6. **The special case — Child of the Declined** (Naro-descendant origin). **Committed as one optional background, not the default** (Phase 6 §IX). The player is a descendant of Naro, raised in the decline-inheritance tradition without knowing its cosmic weight. Head-start: a special relationship to the Seventh mystery and the practice-path; Saedis is *kin.* **Guardrail (Phase 6 §IX honored):** this origin must not turn the player into a chosen-one — the Declined *decline* destiny; being Naro's descendant grants *understanding*, not *power* or *fate.* The arc is "you carry the truth your family kept, and the choice is still entirely yours." *(Soft flag: this origin should be slightly hidden / unlockable, so it reads as a discovery rather than a power-pick.)*

## VIII. Tonal balance check

- **Cosmic horror.** The name-fates: a god (Selen) whose name a thousand years dissolved, kept alive only in the ledger of an exhausted friend; the god of Memory (Maeleth) who *forgot* her; Naro right and erased; the Bower's name that can be heard but never written (a name you cannot hold). *The horror is intimacy lost to time at divine scale — and the small mercies that survive it (Tovreth kept Selen's name).*
- **Biological horror.** Carried by the cast's embodiment: Othren ruling through a salt-sealed mouth; the Elder of the Quiet speaking *as* substrate; Filer Pais dictating a living preservation; the child-arc's vessel-question (a person made into a god). The bodies the gods became are characterized here through who still speaks for them.
- **Comedy.** The cast's comic anchors are explicit: Curator Hesh's procedure-bound file-reading; Factor Brenn's half-working charms; Brother Kavin's cheerful evangelism; the reveal that the Counter's name (Tovreth) was on every contract the player ever signed and they never once read it. *The comedy is, again, the bound world's small officialdoms persisting around enormous grief — now with faces and names.*
