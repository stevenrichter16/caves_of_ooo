# 08 — Material Culture

> Phase 8 of the lore project. The **stuff of the world** — currency, writing-surfaces, light, preservatives, stone, food, dress, dwelling, craft, art — synthesized across all factions and grounded in the now-complete cosmology.
>
> Inherits all prior phases. Builds *directly on top of* two existing material-culture treatments and does **not** duplicate them:
> - **`catacomb_village_design.md`** — canonical for *all* catacomb-villager material culture (architecture, food, textile, light, craft, commerce). This doc points to it.
> - **IDEAS.md** — canonical for the cross-cutting material systems already designed: the Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy (light-as-currency), Stones with Stake (the mineral palette), The Preserved (the five preservation methods), the currency set (Drams / Ink / Lumen-hours / Stones / Reputation / Spirit-affinity, per `00_Canon.md` Layer 6).

## I. The material grammar — stuff *is* theology here

CoO's central cosmological fact (the gods *are* their functions; Phase 1) has a material consequence: **every faction's material culture is its god's function expressed in objects.** You can read a faction's theology off its stuff before anyone speaks. This is the organizing principle of the whole phase, and it is a *design affordance*: a player learns to identify a place, a person, and a politics by material fingerprint alone (the mineral-palette idea from IDEAS.md, generalized to all material culture).

| God / function | Material expression | The fingerprint |
|---|---|---|
| Memory (Maeleth / Palimpsest) | everything is a **record-surface** | ink, vellum, Memory-Marble, marginalia on every object |
| Substrate (Selen / Choir) | nothing is *made* — everything is **grown / integrated** | no manufactured goods; encased things; substrate-craft |
| Preservation (Othren / Pale Curation) | everything is **held against decay** | salt, seals, labels, gloves, dust-aversion, files |
| Beauty (Ylaes / Bower-Folk) | everything is **arranged & colored** | pigment, display, the most elaborate dress in the world |
| Exchange (Tovreth / Concord) | everything is **portable, weighed, priced** | standardized weights, contracts, caravan-kit, worn wealth |
| Roots (Dohren / catacomb-villagers) | everything is **grown in the dark & inscribed** | bio-light, mycelial-fiber, plaque-walls (see design file) |
| (the unnamed / Tent-Right) | everything is **shared, woven, portable** | the tent, the well, the guest-cloth, the shared vessel |

And the two that *unmake* material culture:
- **Urqu** leaves the **Unsaid** — objects that have Slipped, lost their reliable identity (a tool that is no longer certainly a tool).
- **The Driving Bloom** leaves **the unfinished** — a Bloomed workshop where every task was abandoned mid-act, fruiting bodies erupting from the work (§VII).

*(This grammar is also why the recovered-world surface villages feel different from everywhere else: their material culture expresses* no god *— they make ordinary things for ordinary reasons, and that ordinariness is exactly the "the cosmology is just a story" frame the player starts in. §VII.)*

## II. Currencies — finalized, with cross-faction trust

The currency set (`00_Canon.md` Layer 6) is canonical. Phase 8 adds **what each faction trusts** — because in a fractured world, money is faction-relative:

| Currency | Who trusts it | Who doesn't | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Drams** | the Concord, surface villages, most of the world | catacomb-villagers (barter-first), the Choir (no economy), Tent-Right (oath-first) | the closest thing to universal; the Concord underwrites it |
| **Ink** | the Quartermaster, scribes, preservers | (non-circulating — destroyed on use) | mid-tier; shares its iron-gall source with preservation-ink (per IDEAS.md) — *the rental currency and the embalming fluid are the same substance*, a quiet horror |
| **Lumen-hours** | catacomb economies, the Concord (as broker) | surface-dwellers (meaningless above) | hyper-local; the Concord runs the futures market (per IDEAS.md) |
| **Stones** | everyone, for ritual/long-term value | (no one *spends* them lightly — they're per-life scale) | the only store of value that crosses every faction; doesn't decay |
| **Salt** | Tent-Right, Pale Curation, preservers, the wasteland | (over-abundant near salt-flats; precious far from them) | the wasteland's reliable trade-good (per `07`); doubles as preservative and seasoning — *you can eat your money or embalm with it* |
| **Reputation** | every faction (per-faction) | (non-tradable) | slow, sticky |
| **Spirit-affinity** | the Spirits | (personal, non-tradable) | per Phase 5 |
| **The honored debt** | the Concord internally | — | the Concord's *true* currency of trust (per `04`); a Counter-signed contract outweighs any sum |

**Cross-faction exchange is friction, and friction is content.** A surface good revalues the moment it enters a catacomb (priced against light); drams are dead weight in the deep wasteland (where salt and the oath matter); the Choir has no economy at all (you cannot *buy* anything from the Choir — you can only give yourself). *The player feeling money change meaning as they cross the world is the intended texture.*

## III. Writing & memory materials

The material base of the Palimpsest, the Inkbound, and (inverted) Pale Curation:

- **Ink** — iron-gall-based (per IDEAS.md). Ordinary ink writes; *concentrated* ink preserves (Ink-Bath); the rental-currency Ink is the same family. **Black-Gall** (per Stones with Stake) is the sacred ink-mineral.
- **Vellum & skin-page** — prepared animal vellum for ordinary records; **Ink-Bathed human skin** for the Inkbound's most sacred manuscripts (per `02` / `10` — the scribe who becomes the text). The enzyme-prepared parchment trade runs through the catacomb-villagers (per design file).
- **Memory-Marble** — the sacred writing-surface (per Stones with Stake); holds what the Choir wants to eat; structural material for Re-Membering. The Palimpsest's deep-archive walls and the most important plaques are Memory-Marble.
- **Dew-ink** — self-illuminating bioluminescent ink (catacomb-villager craft, per design file); the Inkbound prize it; self-illuminating manuscripts are readable in the dark.
- **Plaques** — the catacomb-villagers' record-medium (design file); the deepest carry Dohren's name (per `07`).
- **The Voynich-pattern Unread Codex** (per IDEAS.md) — the manuscript no faction can read, wavelength-locked to dim catacomb light; the material artifact of "something the world doesn't understand about itself."

*Horror/comedy note: the Palimpsest writes on everything, including people; Pale Curation files everything, including people; the difference between an archive and a morgue is, here, a matter of emphasis.*

## IV. Light

Catacomb light-as-currency is fully designed (IDEAS.md Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy; `catacomb_village_design.md`). **Phase 8 adds only the surface contrast:**

- **Surface light is free, ambient, and taken for granted** — sun, fire, oil. Surface-dwellers do not *think* about light; it is not an economy, not a craft, not a faith. **This is itself a faction-fingerprint:** a surface-dweller wastes light extravagantly; a catacomb-villager flinches at the waste. The clash of light-attitudes is a reliable culture-gap beat (a surface guest leaving a lantern burning in a catacomb village commits an unintended obscenity).
- **The cosmological layer (per Phase 4 `06`):** catacomb bio-light is the Rooted's dream. So a catacomb-villager tending the patch is tending the god's dream; a surface-dweller striking flint is doing nothing of the kind. *Surface light is mundane; deep light is theology. The same photons, different worlds.*

## V. Preservation materials

The five methods are designed (The Preserved, IDEAS.md). **Phase 8 adds the who-uses-what material map** (consolidating the scattered cross-links):

| Material | Method | Primary faction | Material texture |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pale-Salt** | Salt-Cure | Pale Curation, Tent-Right, wasteland | desiccated, white-crusted, eyes salt-sealed |
| **Ink (concentrated)** | Ink-Bath | Palimpsest / Inkbound | ink-saturated skin, readable as page |
| **Honey** | Honey-Seal | catacomb-villagers, Bower-Folk | golden-translucent, sweet, *edible* (the larder horror) |
| **Peat / bog** | Bog-Take | natural (Sumphold, bog-country) | tanned, dark, leathery; the Bog-Taken |
| **Resin** (Drosera-mucilage candidate) | Resin-Cast | Bower-Folk | clear-sealed, shining, the installation-bodies |

*The material choice is a theological statement* (per IDEAS.md method-as-investment): salt for memory-as-index (Pale Curation), ink for memory-as-text (Inkbound), honey for beauty-and-pragmatism (villagers), resin for beauty-as-arrest (Bower-Folk). **You can tell what a culture believes about death by what it keeps its dead in.**

## VI. Stones & minerals

Fully designed (Stones with Stake, IDEAS.md). **Phase 8 adds the consolidated cosmological weighting** — the stones now sort cleanly against the Phase 1-6 cosmology:

- **Tepuibone** — sandstone from the Root itself; the heaviest stone; **anti-Urqu** (it *remembers what the Tree was*). The First-Root-Chip is the unique final-act fragment.
- **Memory-Marble / Mute-Stone / Tepuibone** — the three name-holding, anti-Unsaying materials (per Phase 5): they resist Urqu *because they hold their names hardest.* The material correlate of the Tent-Right oath.
- **Choir-Iron / Pale-Salt / Spore-Block** — the anti-fungal defensive palette (anti-Choir, anti-Bloom).
- **Bower-Lapis** — the aesthetic stone (Bower-Folk); Pale Curation refuses to file it (*too aesthetic*).
- **Sari-Pyrite** — resonates with Urqu; the dark-bargain stone.
- **Glow-Quartz** — bio-light amplifier; the catacomb-villager aspiration.

**The fingerprint, sharpest here:** a chamber's wall-mineral reveals its faction *before dialogue* (per IDEAS.md) — Memory-Marble = Palimpsest; Pale-Salt = Pale Curation/preservers; Spore-Block + Choir-Iron = anti-Choir defense; Bower-Lapis = Bower-Folk; Glow-Quartz = a prosperous catacomb-village.

## VII. Per-faction material culture (the "(open)" factions, filled)

Compact profiles — dress, food, dwelling, craft, art — for the factions `00_Canon.md` left open. (Catacomb-villagers: see `catacomb_village_design.md`.)

### Surface recovered-world villages (Sill and kin) — *the material baseline*

- **Dwelling:** timber and river-stone; thatch; the village built around a well or a river-landing. Mundane, sturdy, un-theological.
- **Food:** river-fish, grain, root vegetables, the occasional traded delicacy. Bread is bread, not a wonder (contrast the catacombs, where surface bread is a marvel).
- **Dress:** practical homespun; wool and linen; undyed or plant-dyed in muted colors.
- **Craft & art:** functional pottery, woodcarving, river-offerings (carved tokens left for the river-god). The Tree is a children's story carved into a few old lintels no one reads anymore.
- **The point:** this is the **material frame of disbelief.** Sill's stuff expresses *no god* — it is the most ordinary material culture in the world, and that ordinariness is precisely the "the cosmology is just folklore" starting state. As the player travels inward (rising Strangeness Tier), the material culture grows stranger and more theological, *materially enacting the re-enchantment arc* (Phase 6 §VIII).

### Palimpsest — *memory made total*

- **Dwelling:** Quillhold and the archive-stations; dim, dry, Memory-Marble-walled; shelving as architecture; the deepest chambers belong to the Silent Order.
- **Dress:** ink-stained robes; many scribes wear *their own marginalia* — notes inked onto cuffs and hems, a walking palimpsest. Body-Readers wear gloves stained permanently dark.
- **Food:** austere, scholarly, communal-refectory; food is fuel, not pleasure (the opposite of the Bower-Folk).
- **Craft & art:** the manuscript *is* the art. Illumination, marginalia, the beautiful hand. The Inkbound's skin-page is the sacred extreme.
- **Cosmological tell:** everything can be written on, so everything *is* — even the dead, even themselves.

### Pale Curation — *held against decay*

- **Dwelling:** the Salt-Vault and regional chambers; salt-white, climate-controlled-by-mineral, labelled, indexed. Cold, clean, quiet.
- **Dress:** archival — pale, dust-averse, gloved; high collars; the dress of people who handle the irreplaceable. Officers wear their rank as filing-marks.
- **Food:** salt-preserved, long-shelf-life, joyless-by-design (decay is the enemy; fresh food *decays*, so it is faintly distrusted).
- **Craft & art:** the *perfect file*; the seamless salt-cure; the body kept so well it looks asleep. Their art is invisibility-of-craft — the best preservation shows no hand.
- **Cosmological tell:** the label is sacred; the thing un-labelled is the thing un-saved (a quiet rhyme with the Unsaying — *to Pale Curation, the unfiled and the Unsaid are the same dread*).

### Saccharine Concord — *portable, weighed, priced*

- **Dwelling:** Tally and the trade-posts; the caravan is the truer "home"; standardized, modular, packable. A Concord post looks the same in every region (brand consistency as cosmology).
- **Dress:** practical traveling-wear with **wealth worn portably** — sealed credit-tokens, signet rings, the means to transact carried on the body. A Concord Broker dresses to be robbed-proof and trade-ready at once.
- **Food:** the most *varied* in the world (they move everything); Concord caravans carry trade-food from everywhere; a Factor's table is a map of the world's regions. Pale-Salt keeps the inventory fresh.
- **Craft & art:** the **contract** is the Concord's masterwork — and the standardized weight, the honest scale (and the dishonest one). Their "art" is the elegant deal. *They commission art from the Bower-Folk and resell it; they make none.*
- **Cosmological tell:** everything has a price tag, including things that shouldn't (the centuries-long argument with the Bower, `04`/`05`).

### Bower-Folk — *arranged & colored*

- **Dwelling:** Posy and the installations; every surface composed; the dwelling *is* the artwork (the Bower arranges the village through gestures, `05`).
- **Dress:** the most elaborate in the world — pigment, Bower-Lapis, rare-spectrum bio-light woven in; a Bower-Folk artisan dresses to be *seen*, and judges others by their eye. They make the colors everyone else trades for.
- **Food:** plated, arranged, eaten as much by sight as taste; a Bower-Folk meal is a composition (and may be inedible-but-beautiful, which they consider a fair trade).
- **Craft & art:** *all of it.* The Bower-Folk are the world's source of pigment, arrangement, display, and the Resin-Cast (beauty-as-arrest, the horror). Their Cleaners resurface the dead with enzyme-baths.
- **Cosmological tell:** the worthless-but-striking outranks the precious-but-plain (the aesthetic-reputation system, `05`); status is not buyable, which maddens the Concord.

### Tent-Right — *shared, woven, portable*

- **Dwelling:** the **tent** — portable, woven, oriented around the threshold (the guest crosses it; the oath begins there). The well and the salt-work are the fixed points; everything else moves. Wellmeet and the First Tent are the anchors (`07`).
- **Dress:** practical wasteland-wear — woven, layered against sun and grit, undyed wool and the salt-bleached pale of the flats. **The guest-cloth** is the one ceremonial garment: laid over a guest's shoulders, it materially *enacts* the three-day protection (a guest under the cloth is named-protected). *(Structural-Bedouin inspiration, per `07`; no real-culture vocabulary or specific garment-iconography lifted — the guest-cloth is a CoO-native object.)*
- **Food:** wasteland fare — salt-cured meat, hardbread, the shared vessel (eating from one dish binds host and guest); water is the truest wealth and is *given*, never sold, to a guest.
- **Craft & art:** weaving (the tent, the cloth), salt-work, and the **oral-recitation art** — the Namers' growing body of recitation about the oath. They make almost nothing permanent; their art is *the act of welcome*, repeated.
- **Cosmological tell:** the most important objects (the well, the guest-cloth, the shared vessel) are all *given, not sold* — the material inverse of the Concord; the practice-path Naming made of cloth and water (`07` / Phase 6).

### Rot Choir — *grown, not made*

- **Dwelling:** the Deepest Cathedral and the Spore-Wedded villages; *grown* architecture — mycelial vaults, substrate-walls, the encased dead as structure. Nothing is built; everything is cultivated or integrated.
- **Dress:** the Choir-integrated wear what they wore when taken, slowly overgrown; the deeper the integration, the more the body *is* the garment (substrate-bloom across skin). The Choir makes no textile — it makes *more Choir.*
- **Food:** the Choir does not eat in the human sense; it *includes.* Spore-Wedded villagers eat fungal cultivation that is itself Choir-adjacent (a meal that is faintly the god).
- **Craft & art:** substrate-craft — the encasement, the integration, the slow beautiful horror of a person becoming a wall. Choir-Touched skin's faint mycelial pattern is, to the Bower-Folk, beautiful (`05`).
- **Cosmological tell:** the Choir is the one faction with **no manufactured material culture** — its grammar (§I) is growth, so it produces no *objects*, only *outcomes.* The absence of stuff is the fingerprint.

### Imminent Archive (Catchers) — *parasitic material culture*

- They make nothing of their own; they use **Pale Curation's materials wrongly** (the thrown preservative-pot, the confiscated "filing supplies," per IDEAS.md). Their one signature object is the **catch-pot** — a Pale-Salt or resin charge made to be thrown at the living. *A faction defined materially by stolen tools used early.*

### Driving Bloom — *anti-material; the unfinished*

- The Bloom produces no material culture (it has no mind, §I; per `08`). What it *leaves* is the inverse of culture: **the unfinished** — a Bloomed workshop where every task was dropped mid-act, tools fallen, work abandoned, fruiting bodies erupting from the half-done. *The eeriest material site in the world is a place where everything was almost finished and then driven away from. (A direct material echo of incompletion-as-Urqu's-substrate, Phase 5/6.)*

## VIII. Cuisine (cross-cut)

Food is one of the project's best tone-carriers — comedy and horror at the same table:

- **The class of marvel:** surface-grown bread, fruit, cheese, dairy — wonders in the deep (a traveler bringing fresh bread to a catacomb-village makes a major social offering, per design file).
- **The class of staple:** lantern-beetle larva paste (catacomb protein), salt-cured meat (wasteland), river-fish and grain (surface), fungal cultivation (Spore-Wedded).
- **The horror at the table:** the **honey-jar larder** (per IDEAS.md) — jars of food beside jars of Honey-Sealed kin, *labelled with names*, kept "in case." The single most disturbing food-beat in the world, and it is *quiet.*
- **The comedy at the table:** the Concord Factor's absurdly cosmopolitan spread in the middle of nowhere; the Bower-Folk meal that is gorgeous and barely edible; the Pale Curation officer's joyless distrust of anything fresh enough to rot; the surface guest horrifying a catacomb-village by *wasting light* at dinner.
- **The sacred meal:** Tent-Right's shared vessel (eating from one dish binds host and guest under the oath) — the practice-path Naming, edible.

## IX. Dress as faction-signaling (cross-cut)

A consolidated read-table — *how the player identifies allegiance at a glance* (the §I grammar applied to bodies):

| See this | Read this |
|---|---|
| ink-stained robes, marginalia on the cuffs | Palimpsest scribe |
| pale, gloved, high-collared, dust-averse | Pale Curation officer |
| portable wealth, signet, travel-ready | Concord broker |
| elaborate pigment, Bower-Lapis, woven bio-light | Bower-Folk artisan |
| woven layers, salt-pale, a guest-cloth carried | Tent-Right |
| garments slowly overgrown with substrate | Choir-integrated |
| pale skin, near-white eyes, mycelial-fiber, beetle-jar | catacomb-villager (per design file) |
| a stolen Pale-Salt pot, ready to throw | a Catcher (run) |

*Dress-signaling is a gameplay affordance (threat/ally assessment before dialogue) and a worldbuilding one (the world is legible by sight).*

## X. Art (cross-cut)

In a post-Felling world, art is **how each faction answers loss** — and the answers are the gods' functions again:

- **Palimpsest:** art is *recording* — the beautiful hand, the illumination, the manuscript. Art against forgetting.
- **Bower-Folk:** art is *arrangement* — the installation, the Resin-Cast. Art as the central act of living (the only faction for whom art *is* the politics, `05`).
- **Pale Curation:** art is *the invisible perfect preservation* — craft that hides itself.
- **Concord:** art is *the elegant deal*; they commission and resell beauty but make none.
- **Tent-Right:** art is *the act of welcome* and the Namers' recitation — performed, not kept.
- **catacomb-villagers:** art is *slime-paint signage, the plaque-wall, the bio-light composition* (design file) — art that is also infrastructure.
- **Choir:** art is *integration itself* — the slow composition of a person into the substrate (the horror that is, to the Choir, a kindness).
- **Surface villages:** art is *the river-offering and the old carved lintel* — small, mundane, half-forgotten. The recovered world barely makes art about a cosmology it no longer believes — until the player makes them believe again.

*The through-line: every faction's art is its function coping with the Felling. Art in CoO is grief, organized by theology.*

## XII. Tonal balance & cultural sources

- **Cosmic horror.** The grammar itself — *stuff is theology* — means the world's objects are quietly haunted: the rental-Ink that is also embalming fluid; Pale Curation's dread of the unfiled rhyming with the Unsaying; the Choir's absence-of-objects; the Bloom's workshop-of-the-unfinished.
- **Biological horror.** Concentrated at the table and the body: the honey-jar larder; Ink-Bathed skin-pages; the Choir-integrated wearing their overgrown clothes; the catch-pot.
- **Comedy.** The material register carries a lot of the project's humor: the Concord's brand-consistent trade-posts and cosmopolitan spreads; the Bower-Folk's beautiful inedible meals; Pale Curation's joyless distrust of fresh food; the surface guest committing light-obscenity at a catacomb dinner. *The comedy is, again, the texture of ordinary life persisting — with full seriousness — through cosmic catastrophe.*
- **Cultural sources.** No new real-culture borrowing in Phase 8. All structural inspirations are inherited and already credited in their source docs: catacomb material culture (`catacomb_village_design.md` §XIII — multiple cave-dwelling/catacomb-burial traditions, structural-only); preservation methods (The Preserved — Toraja *ma'nene*, Zoroastrian/Tibetan exposure-burial as structural inspiration for Stone Burial, credited and caveated); Tent-Right (Bedouin-structural, no Arabic vocabulary, per `07`); stones (real mineralogy as property-source, CoO-native names, per Stones with Stake). The guest-cloth, the catch-pot, dew-ink, the plaque-wall, etc. are all CoO-native objects.
