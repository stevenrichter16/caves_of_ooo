## I. Founding

The Imminent Archive emerged in **Generation 27 (~year 810)** — within recorded, almost-living memory by the player's era (per Phase 3). It was a *direct consequence of the Great Manifestation* (Gen 25, ~year 750), the worst Urqu event in recorded history (per Phase 3): a near-incarnation of the unborn seventh god that lasted a season and unmade an entire region. Tens of thousands died — and, critically, **died un-preserved**, in conditions where Pale Curation could not reach them in time.

The Catcher founders — a cell of Pale Curation Filers and one Curator who survived the Great Manifestation's edge — drew a conclusion the mainline found monstrous: **if we had preserved sooner, we would not have lost them. By the time death comes, we have already waited too long. We must preserve before death.**

They reached for theological cover and found it in *the Salted's own act.* **The Salted Salt-Cured herself while still alive and conscious** (per `03_PaleCuration.md`, ~Gen 16). If the founder of preservation preserved herself *alive* — if pre-mortem preservation is what *she* chose — then pre-mortem preservation is *sanctioned by the founding act itself.*

The mainline rejected the conclusion. The mainline could not reject the premise. **The Salted did preserve herself alive.** This is the unresolvable knot at the center of the Catcher question, and it is why, ~270 years later, the Catchers still exist.

## II. Doctrine

### The official line

Phrased as a Catcher would phrase it — *gently, because they believe they are kind:*

> *By the time someone is dead, you have already waited too long. The body has begun decomposing in the seconds before you arrived. The self has begun escaping the flesh. The salt sets faster on a still-warm subject; the ink penetrates better through breathing pores; the honey seals around the last exhalation. To preserve at the moment of crossing — to catch them while the body still holds what it was — is the most complete preservation possible. It is not killing. It is preventing loss. The Salted preserved herself while she still lived. We do for others what she did for herself. We are the only true heirs of her act.*

Three doctrinal positions:

1. **Death is loss; preservation should precede it.** The mainline preserves the dead. The Catchers preserve the *about-to-die.* The difference, in their view, is the difference between preserving a person and preserving a corpse — *they preserve the person, while the person is still there.*
2. **The Salted's self-preservation is the founding sanction.** Their entire legitimacy rests on it. *She did it to herself; we extend it to others.* (The mainline's counter: *she did it to herself — consent; you do it to the unwilling — violation.* The Catchers' reply: *she could not have consented either, in the way you mean; she was a god by then; what she did was beyond consent; we honor the act, not the legal niceties.*)
3. **Pale Curation made a sorting error two centuries ago and must never repeat it.** The Great Manifestation's mass un-preserved death proved (to the Catchers) that the mainline's *triage* — choosing what to preserve — fails catastrophically under pressure. The solution is to *abolish triage*: preserve everyone, preserve early, never sort. **This is the deepest Catcher conviction and the most chilling — they are driven not by cruelty but by a horror of repeating a loss.**

### The unspoken core

What no Catcher will say aloud, and what the player can discover late: **the Catchers are afraid.** The Great Manifestation broke something in the founding generation. They watched tens of thousands unmade by the unborn god's failed birth — watched *naming itself fail*, watched people stop being themselves, watched the world's worst proof that *everything can be lost.* The Catchers' pre-mortem preservation is a **trauma response at faction scale** — a desperate attempt to ensure that *nothing is ever lost again*, pursued past the point of ethics into the point of horror. *They are not villains. They are the most frightened people in the world, and their fear wears the face of gentleness.*

## III. Structure

The Catchers have **no hierarchy** — they cannot, because the mainline expelled them, and expelled sects cannot hold official structure. They are organized as **cells**: small groups (often a single Catcher plus a few aides) operating from **disused Salt-Cure chambers in Pale Curation regional outposts** (per Phase 2 v2; Marrowstye's cell is the canonical example).

The cells coordinate loosely by **parasitizing the Pale Curation supply chain** — they use Pale Curation's preservation materials (salt, ink, honey), Pale Curation's chambers, Pale Curation's logistics. The mainline tolerates this parasitism *because it cannot fully evict the Catchers without the Salted's ruling*, which she has never given (§V). So the Catchers persist in the institutional shadow — using their parent's body, like a preservation that never fully set.

**Authority within a cell** is by *conviction and seniority of practice.* The most committed, most experienced Catcher leads. There is no Catcher-wide leadership. Each cell is theologically autonomous and slightly different (some gentle, some missionary, some clinical — see §VI).

**Crime, within Catcher doctrine:** failing to preserve a savable subject. Allowing someone to fully die when they could have been caught. *(Note the inversion: the mainline's crime is "preserving the unwilling"; the Catcher's crime is "failing to preserve the dying." The two factions criminalize each other's central act.)*

## V. The Salted's silence — the faction's whole existence

Everything about the Catchers reduces to one fact: **the Salted has never ruled on them, and her silence is why they exist.**

Per `03_PaleCuration.md` and later canon: **her silence is not indecision; it is guilt.**

- She knows the Catchers are her theological children. Her self-preservation created them.
- She cannot **disown** them: they are doing, to others, a version of what she did to herself. To condemn them is to condemn the act that defines her god-form.
- She cannot **endorse** them: what they do to the unwilling is monstrous, and she — rigid, theologically certain, but not cruel — knows it.
- So she is **silent.** For ~270 years (since the Catchers emerged), the annual file-update ceremony has not produced a ruling on the Imminent Archive. The mainline waits. The Catchers take the silence as permission. The silence holds.

**The structural parallel to the Reader.** The Salted's silence on the Catchers and the Reader's silence on the Palimpsest's parallel question (the Catchers preserve before final-memory can be archived, which the Palimpsest doctrinally opposes — yet the Reader has also never ruled) is *the closest thing to coordination the two estranged gods have managed in a thousand years* (per `02` and `03`). **Both ancient comrades are refusing to settle a matter that involves both their factions.** They are not speaking. They are not cooperating. And yet, on this one matter, *they are synchronized in their refusal* — as if grief had taught them the same silence. A player who notices this is noticing something profound about the two gods: even estranged, they move together.

**What the player can do with the silence.** Quest-design candidates:
- The player can attempt to *make the Salted rule.* This would require forcing her to confront her guilt — possibly the hardest single conversation in the Pale Curation arc. If she ruled *against* the Catchers, the sect would lose its only theological cover and the mainline could finally evict them. If she ruled *for* them, the Catchers would become a sanctioned Pale Curation practice — a horror.
- The player can leave the silence intact (the default; the world's stable, terrible compromise).
- The player can *expose the silence's nature* (the guilt) to the mainline, changing how the faction understands its own founder.

## VI. Named Catchers

The Catchers are *individuals*, encountered by name (per IDEAS.md — the world-log identifies them). Three named Catchers, each a distinct theological flavor of the same fear:

### Sister Wenil — the gentle one

- The canonical first Catcher the player encounters (per IDEAS.md examples). Operates from the Marrowstye cell (Phase 2 v2).
- **Gentle and sad.** She preserves the way a hospice worker tends the dying — with tenderness, apology, conviction that she is helping. Her world-log dialogue is soft: *"Hold still. You are too valuable to lose."* / *"The salt sets best while you are still warm."* / *"Don't run. The honey hasn't started yet."*
- **Her trauma:** she survived the edge of a *recent* Urqu manifestation (not the Great Manifestation — a smaller, Thinning-era one) in which she watched a child un-named. She has been a Catcher since. *She believes, sincerely, that catching the dying is the only mercy left in a world that unmakes people.*
- **The player's relationship with her can develop across a run.** If the player escapes her preservation and survives, she will remember. If they meet again, she is *apologetic* but undeterred. If the player kills her, the mainline quietly approves — but Sister Wenil, dying, will say something the player carries afterward. Strongest version: her last words are an apology to the player for failing to save them.

### Brother Kavin — the missionary

- Operates from a regional cell; the exact place is a writing / level-design detail. **Enthusiastic and missionary.** Where Wenil is gentle, Kavin is *evangelical* — he believes pre-mortem preservation is *good news* and wants to share it. His world-log dialogue is bright, eager, almost joyful: *"You're hurt! Let me help! This won't take long and you'll thank me forever!"*
- **The most disturbing Catcher** precisely because he is the most cheerful. He does not understand why people flee. He genuinely cannot comprehend that being preserved alive is not a gift.
- He ships his preservations to *regional* archives (per the Fully Preserved Player variable-distance model) — he believes the displacement is part of the gift (*"you'll wake somewhere safe!"*).

### Filer Pais — the clinical one

- Operates from near the Salt-Vault itself (the most dangerous Catcher to encounter — closest to the central archive). **Clinical and documentary.** Pais narrates the player's preservation as if dictating to a scribe: *"Subject ambulatory at 30% capacity. Beginning salt application. Note resistance — common, not significant. Subject attempting flight. Filing as Salt-Cured, central archive, priority."*
- **Pais always ships to the central archive** (per the Fully Preserved Player variable-distance model) — Pais considers *every* preservation worthy of central filing. Meeting Pais means accepting central-archive-displacement risk: the player who reaches 100% Being-Preserved with Pais wakes up at the central archive, the farthest possible displacement.
- Pais is the *least* emotional Catcher and the *most* committed — Pais has no trauma-narrative the player can find, only a chillingly complete conviction. Strongest version: Pais's trauma is so deep it has been filed away, indexed, and never spoken of — the most Pale-Curation answer imaginable.

## VII. Inter-faction relationships

### Pale Curation (mainline) — parent and prosecutor

Covered in `03`. The mainline officially condemns and excommunicates the Catchers but cannot fully evict them (the Salted's silence). The relationship is *parasitic on the mainline's body* — the Catchers use mainline chambers, materials, logistics. The mainline tolerates this with gritted teeth, waiting for a ruling that never comes.

### Rot Choir — most-mutual hatred

Per `01_RotChoir.md`: the Catchers are the Choir's **most loathed mortal faction.** Pre-mortem preservation is the *opposite* of the Choir's offer (encasement *after* willing death). A Catcher cell in Choir territory would be eradicated; a Catcher in mixed territory is suffered only because the Choir cannot extend openly. **The Catchers, for their part, consider the Choir the ultimate proof of why they exist** — the Choir *consumes* the dead; the Catchers *save* the dying from ever reaching the Choir. The two factions are perfect inverse-enemies.

### Palimpsest — doctrinally opposed

Per `02`: the Palimpsest opposes pre-mortem preservation (a living person's memory isn't final yet; archiving requires death). But the Reader has not ruled either (the silence-synchrony with the Salted). Palimpsest scribes complain about Catchers, avoid Catcher-preservations, refuse to Re-Member a Catcher subject — institutional cold-shoulder.

### Saccharine Concord — cynical service

The Concord sells **Catcher-detection charms** (per IDEAS.md — "warns of nearby preservers," works half the time, premium-priced). The Concord trades with the Catchers (preservation supplies) as it trades with everyone. The Counter finds the Catchers *commercially uninteresting and morally none-of-his-business* — the most Concord stance possible.

### Tent-Right — the one place Catchers cannot operate

Per IDEAS.md and `07_TentRight.md`: **Catchers cannot operate in Tent-Right settlements.** The three-day hospitality oath supersedes all preservation work; a Catcher who threw a pot at a guest would be killed for breaking hospitality, and they know it. **The wasteland is the one safe place for a wounded player** — Tent-Right will defend them, including against Catchers. *This makes the Beating a refuge in a specific, mechanical way.*

### Catacomb-villagers — village-by-village

Some catacomb villages contain Catcher cells the village no longer endorses but cannot remove without breaking their Pale Curation contract (per `catacomb_village_design.md` and Phase 2 v2). Other villages — especially Stone Burial villages — find Catchers abhorrent. Spore-Wedded (Choir-aligned) villages would eradicate them.

### Bower-Folk — eerie kinship

The one faction the Catchers have something like *rapport* with. Both preserve the living-or-just-dead against their will (the Bower-Folk Resin-Cast players whose death-pose is "perfect"; the Catchers preserve the dying). **The Bower-Folk and the Catchers occasionally cooperate** — a Catcher who catches an aesthetically-significant subject may ship them to the Bower-Folk for Resin-Cast finalization. *This is one of the most disturbing inter-faction alliances in the world.* (The Bower would approve; the Salted would be horrified if she knew; perhaps she does know, and it is part of why she cannot bring herself to rule.)

### Driving Bloom — opportunistic enemy

Half-preserved bodies (the Catchers' fresh in-progress work) are uniquely valuable Bloom hosts (per `03` and IDEAS.md). The Bloom *follows* Catcher cells, harvesting their half-preserved subjects. The Catchers loathe the Bloom — a Bloomed half-preservation is the *opposite* of preservation, a body lost in the very act of being saved.

## VIII. Internal variation (sects-of-a-sect)

The Catchers have no formal sub-sects (they barely have formal structure), but the *cells* vary by theological temperament:

- **The Gentle cells** (Wenil-type) — preserve as mercy; apologetic; tender.
- **The Missionary cells** (Kavin-type) — preserve as good-news; evangelical; cheerful.
- **The Clinical cells** (Pais-type) — preserve as procedure; documentary; un-emotional; ship to central.
- **The Penitent cells** — Catchers who have begun to *doubt* the doctrine, who continue the practice out of inertia or fear of admitting a thousand savings were wrong. The seed of a Catcher-reform or Catcher-collapse arc. A player who finds a Penitent cell finds the sect's own internal heresy — the possibility that the Catchers might *stop.*

## IX. Player-facing surface (faction-tier; mechanics in IDEAS.md)

- **First encounter:** low-HP, a thrown pot, a gentle voice by name. Per IDEAS.md.
- **Investigation:** the player can investigate Catcher cells (Marrowstye's is the canonical first), learn the founding story, the Great Manifestation trauma, the Salted-silence.
- **The ethically-clean path:** killing a Catcher earns quiet mainline Pale Curation approval (a hidden secret-flagged reputation bonus, per IDEAS.md). But the Catcher dies *speaking* — by name, gently — and the player carries it.
- **The Salted-silence arc:** the player can attempt to make the Salted rule (per §V). The hardest Pale Curation conversation. Forces her guilt into the open.
- **The Penitent arc:** the player can find a Penitent cell and *help the Catchers stop* — a redemption arc for the sect, possibly the only way to end the Catcher threat without violence.
- **The Catcher-join path (dark):** per the Imminent Archive IDEAS entry — the player can *join* the Imminent Archive, gaining the throwing-pot mechanic as their own ability (forcibly preserve hostile NPCs into Preserved Predecessors the player can return to). Visibly marked in the player's file; other factions react with horror or fascination. **The darkest player-class option in CoO** — the player becomes the gentle, frightened thing that catches the dying.

## X. Later settlements and writing notes

- **Whether the Salted can be made to rule, and what it costs her.** Quest-design note: yes is the strongest path, but only at the cost of forcing her to name her guilt aloud for the first time in 270 years. Either ruling breaks something.
- **Filer Pais's trauma.** Writing note: filed and never spoken of.
- **Sister Wenil's last words.** Writing note: an apology to the player for failing to save them.
- **Whether the Penitent cells can reform the whole sect.** Quest-design note. A non-violent end to the Catcher threat is one of the most satisfying possible resolutions; whether it is achievable in a single run remains a design choice.
- **What happens to the Catchers in each of the three endings.** Per Phase 1 v2: Renewal dissolves them (no more emergency to preserve against). Consume makes them irrelevant (the Choir's new Tree consumes the preservation question). Preserve *entrenches* them (the world endures broken; the Great-Manifestation-fear persists; the Catchers persist with it). *Notably, Preserve — the ending Pale Curation mainline wants — is the ending in which Pale Curation's own heretical children survive longest.*

## XI. Tonal balance check

- **Cosmic horror.** The Catchers are the project's purest fusion of horror and tenderness. Being preserved *while alive*, by someone who loves you, who apologizes, who is *not wrong* about the loss they fear. The Salted's guilt-silence. The Great Manifestation trauma at faction scale.
- **Biological horror.** The thrown preservatives; the Being-Preserved accumulation; the half-preserved-as-Bloom-host; the player waking up alive-and-encased in a distant archive.
- **Comedy.** Specific and dark: the Concord's half-working Catcher-detection charms; Brother Kavin's *relentless cheerful evangelism* about being preserved alive; Filer Pais's clinical dictation of the player's own preservation in progress. *The comedy is the framing-mismatch — the Catcher's sincere kindness against the player's sincere terror — which is also the horror. They are the same beat, told twice.*
