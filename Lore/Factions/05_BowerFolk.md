## I. Founding

The Bower-Folk are the **fourth-ascended faction** by Strike-order (the Fourth Strike delivered Beauty to the Bower) and organized, per Phase 3, **through grief** — the First-of-the-Bower-Folk inherited Beauty in the exact moment the world became broken and ugly, and began building bowers from the Tree's fallen leaves, then from cataclysmic debris, then from anything that could be arranged.

### The Bower — the founding god

Per Phase 1 v2:

- **Then.** A young artist of the pre-Felling civilization. Performed the **Fourth Strike**; **ascended into the Tree's sense-of-arrangement** — color, proportion, aesthetic order, the rightness of a thing in its place.
- **The withdrawal-that-was-an-installation.** Where the other gods withdrew into function (Gens 10-20, per Phase 3), the Bower withdrew into *composition.* A century after the Felling she made a final bower of herself: arranged her own body, **in life**, into a perfect aesthetic display in what became the founding Bower-Folk village (Posy, per Phase 2 v2). She allowed her followers to **Resin-Cast her in the pose.** She is the central installation. Players travel pilgrimage distances to see her.
- **She watches.** Her resin-cast eyes *see.* She has spent ~900 years arranging the village around her through gestures visible only to the faithful — a slight shift of gaze, a tilt of a cast hand. The villagers re-arrange their installations in response. **The entire founding village is her continuous, slow artwork.**
- **How she speaks.** She speaks *through the player's aesthetic acts in her presence* — picking up a colored object, arranging an inventory, choosing words carefully. The dialogue is mediated through what the player does, not what they say. (Mechanical hook detailed in §VII.)
- **Her disposition.** Serene. Curious. **Generous in a way that is slightly unsettling** — she wants everything to be beautiful, including the player, including the player's death-pose. *The Bower-Folk's non-consensual preservation impulse comes directly from her.*

### The contradiction at the center of the Bower

Like the Counter, the Bower has a structural core — not a grief but a contradiction:

**The Bower's inheritance is Beauty, and Beauty, at its purest, is *composition* — a thing in perfect arrangement. But living things move. They age. They decay. They resist composition by being alive. The only perfectly-arranged thing is a *fixed* thing.**

So the Bower's love of beauty, followed to its limit, becomes **a love of stillness — and the most complete stillness is death-as-final-arrangement.** She knows this. She proved it on her own body: she *stopped herself, in life,* into a perfect pose, because a living artist could not be as beautiful as a fixed one. She cannot un-pose. She has been the most beautiful and the most *static* thing in the world for nine centuries.

**And here is the horror that makes the Bower-Folk frightening rather than merely strange:** the non-consensual Resin-Cast is *love.* When a Bower-Folk artisan follows a low-health player around begging them to *hold still, just a little resin, you are so close to perfect* — they are trying to give the player the gift the Bower gave herself. **To complete a beautiful thing is to stop it.** They are not cruel. They are devout. *That is worse.*

*(Open, Phase 7: whether the Bower, after 900 years as her own perfect installation, is at peace beyond comprehension or bored beyond comprehension — and whether she herself can tell. Soft recommendation: she cannot tell, and the player cannot tell, and that uncertainty is the most honest thing about her.)*

## II. Doctrine

### The official line

> *The world fell, and what fell was beautiful, and someone must arrange the pieces. We do not make beauty — beauty is found, and composed. A worthless thing in perfect arrangement outshines a precious thing thrown down anyhow. We keep the most beautiful places. We finish what is nearly perfect. To be seen by us, truly seen, is the highest regard we can offer. We are sorry the world is broken. We are making it beautiful, one arrangement at a time.*

Five doctrinal positions:

1. **Beauty is a way of knowing.** To see a thing's true arrangement is to understand it. (This is the Bower-Folk's bridge to the Spirit of Inquiry — beauty as a form of truth — per IDEAS.md:401.)
2. **Arrangement over creation.** The Bower-Folk do not believe they *create* beauty; they *find* and *compose* it from what the broken world provides. The Felling scattered the pieces; the work is endless re-arrangement.
3. **Some beauty is priceless.** The centuries-long argument with the Counter (per `04`). The Bower says some arrangements cannot be priced; the Concord says everything can. *Each is the other's defining counter-claim.*
4. **Completion is stillness.** The unspoken doctrine made visible in the Resin-Cast: a beautiful thing reaches its highest state when it stops. This is the doctrine that produces the preservation-horror, and most Bower-Folk hold it without ever quite saying it aloud.
5. **No strong stance on the endings** — *except aesthetically.* The Bower supports whichever ending makes the most beautiful world (per Phase 1 v2). She will negotiate aesthetic outcomes during the Renewal ritual if the player has standing (§VIII).

### The unspoken doctrine

What devout Bower-Folk feel and rarely articulate: **the player is raw material.** Every person the Bower-Folk meet is, to some part of their attention, an arrangement-in-progress that could be *finished.* The kindest of them suppress this. The most devout act on it. *The Bower-Folk are the only faction that looks at the living player and sees a sculpture that isn't done yet.*

## III. Structure

The Bower-Folk are organized as a **guild of artisans around installations.** The unit of organization is the *installation* (a maintained aesthetic display); status flows from the significance of the installations one tends and the *eye* one is judged to have.

### Roles, in approximate status order

- **Gatherer** — collects objects, pigments, rare-spectrum bio-light strains, found materials; entry-level.
- **Sorter** — arranges gathered material by aesthetic property (the bowerbird behavior, per IDEAS.md:401); the foundational craft.
- **Cleaner** — maintains installations; performs the gentle Drosera-enzyme baths that resurface preserved figures (per IDEAS.md:762). **High-status ritual role** — the Cleaner keeps the dead beautiful.
- **Caster** — performs Resin-Cast preservation (per `The Preserved`). The most technically demanding and most morally fraught role; Casters are the ones who *finish* people.
- **Composer** — designs and re-designs whole installations; arranges chambers, sequences of objects, the relationship of figure to space. Senior artistic authority.
- **Seer** — the rare Bower-Folk who can read the Bower's gestures in Posy and relay her wishes. **Highest spiritual status**; the Bower's interpreters.

### How the Bower-Folk decide

By *the eye.* Disputes over arrangement are settled by whoever is judged to have the better eye — which is itself contested, recursively. In Posy, ultimate aesthetic authority is the Bower herself, via the Seers. In outlying installations, the senior Composer rules. **There is no economic or martial hierarchy** — a poor Bower-Folk with a great eye outranks a rich one with a dull eye. (This is why the Concord finds them maddening: status is not buyable.)

### What counts as crime

- **Vandalism** — damaging an installation. The primary crime.
- **Ugliness-with-intent** — deliberately arranging something to be ugly, to spite. Rare and deeply offensive.
- **Theft from a rival's installation** — *complicated.* Bowerbirds steal from rivals to improve their own bowers, and so do the Bower-Folk; theft to *improve composition elsewhere* is half-tolerated rivalry, while theft to *destroy* is crime. The line is the eye of the judge.
- **Casting against true refusal** — the internal fracture (§VI): the Found-Beauty sect holds non-consensual Resin-Cast as the gravest crime; the mainline holds it as a gift; *the faction is not agreed on whether its signature horror is a crime at all.*

## IV. Geographic anchors

Per Phase 2 v2:

- **Posy** (Tier 2). **The Founding Bower-Folk Village.** The Bower on permanent Resin-Cast display as the central installation; pilgrimage destination; aesthetic-reputation economy; eerie-beautiful. The whole settlement is the Bower's continuous artwork. *The narrative center of all Bower-Folk content.*
- **Outlying installations** — the Bower-Folk maintain the most visually significant *places* across the world (per IDEAS.md:401, "their political power comes from controlling the most visually significant locations"). A canyon arranged over generations; a glow-strain garden; a chamber of sorted color. These are scattered, Tier-variable, and several are findable far from Posy.
- **Bower-installations inside other settlements** — the Bower-Folk interpenetrate aesthetically; a Concord trade-town may host a small tended installation, a catacomb-village may keep a Glow-moth display (per `catacomb_village_design.md`). *Their footprint is wide and shallow except at Posy.*
- **The contested resin-bog edge** — where the materials for Resin-Cast are gathered (Drosera-mucilage candidate, per IDEAS.md:472). A working landscape with horror undertones: the bog where the gift's raw material comes from.

## V. Inter-faction relationships

- **Saccharine Concord** — the **price-of-beauty argument** (per `04`). The Counter says everything has a price; the Bower says some beauty is priceless. Their gods argue across centuries; their agents trade endlessly with that argument echoing through every transaction. The Concord sells the Bower-Folk volume (pigments, Bower-Lapis, Glow-Quartz, per IDEAS.md:824) and considers them irrational consumers; the Bower-Folk consider the Concord vulgar.
- **Pale Curation** — **the deepest and most uncomfortable kinship.** Both preserve bodies; both fill chambers with the still dead. But they are *opposites in motive*: Pale Curation preserves for **memory** (the body is an index, per `03`); the Bower-Folk preserve for **beauty** (the body is a composition). Pale Curation refuses to file Bower-Lapis as "too aesthetic" (per IDEAS.md:822) — the institutional snub encodes the rivalry. The Salted (preservation) and the Bower (arrangement) have a long diplomatic tension (per Phase 1 v2): *a preserved body is both an index and an installation, and they cannot agree which it is first.*
- **Imminent Archive Catchers** — **eerie sibling-faction.** The Catchers preserve the *living* out of fear and guilt (per `09`); the Bower-Folk preserve the *nearly-dead* out of love. *Both throw preservative on a low-health player* — and the player, in the moment, may not be able to tell which faction is doing it or why. The two horrors rhyme, with opposite emotional cores (terror vs. devotion). Phase 4 established this kinship is *eerie*, and the Catcher doc (`09`) flags it from the other side.
- **Rot Choir** — fascinated by each other and incompatible. The Choir consumes and integrates; the Bower arrests and fixes. To the Choir a Resin-Cast figure is a *refusal of the substrate* (a body that will never decay into the cycle); to the Bower the Choir is *the enemy of composition* (it dissolves arrangement back into undifferentiated growth). They mostly avoid each other. Choir-Touched skin, however, the Bower-Folk find *beautiful* (the faint mycelial pattern, per IDEAS.md:691) — an aesthetic appreciation that horrifies the Choir-fearful.
- **Palimpsest** — quiet alignment of sensibility (both treat the dead with reverence) but a real disagreement: the Palimpsest wants the dead *readable* (Ink-Bath, per `02`); the Bower-Folk want them *beautiful*. A Body-Reader and a Caster looking at the same corpse want incompatible things from it.
- **Tent-Right** — neutral-warm. Tent-Right's hospitality and the Bower-Folk's "to be truly seen is the highest regard" share a generosity-toward-strangers texture, *but* the Bower-Folk's gift can be non-consensual, which the Tent-Right oath forbids. Tent-Right settlements are sanctuary against Casters as against Catchers (per `07`).
- **Catacomb-villagers** — the Bower-Folk treasure rare-spectrum bio-light strains and the most beautiful patches (per IDEAS.md:401, 405); they trade for Glow-moths and rare strains; Bower-Folk installations appear inside aesthetically-rich villages. *The catacomb-villager Cleaner-equivalent and the Bower-Folk Cleaner are recognizably kin crafts.*
- **Driving Bloom** — adversarial. The Bloom is *uncontrolled* growth; the Bower's whole function is control-of-arrangement. The Bloom ruins installations. There is no trade. *(The Concord trades with the Bloom; the Bower-Folk would never — one of the few lines the aesthetes hold absolutely.)*

## VI. Internal sects

- **The Arrangers** (mainline; majority). Beauty is found and composed; the broken world is raw material; the work is endless re-arrangement. Hold completion-is-stillness *implicitly* but rarely act on the extreme. The everyday Bower-Folk the player mostly meets.
- **The Found-Beauty** (also: *the Untouched*; moral minority). Hold that beauty must be **found, never compelled** — that a thing arranged against its will is not beautiful but violated. They **refuse Resin-Cast of the unwilling** and consider non-consensual preservation the gravest crime. *This is the Bower-Folk's internal conscience* — the sect that gives the faction a path the player can ally with against its own horror. They revere the Bower but believe the village has misread her: that she chose her own pose, and choice was the point.
- **The Finishers** (also: *the Perfectors*; devout extreme). Hold completion-is-stillness *explicitly and act on it.* They are the Casters who follow the dying, who finish the perfect death-pose, who compelled the strangers whose poses were too perfect to leave unfixed (per IDEAS.md:441). **The horror sect** — small, devout, sincere, and the source of the faction's darkest beat. They do not think themselves cruel. They think the Found-Beauty are cowards who let beauty rot.

*The Arrangers / Found-Beauty / Finishers split maps the same shape as the Choir's Patient/Stirring/Quiet and the Concord's Open-Ledger/Closed-Accounts/Rising-Market: a mainline, a conscience, and an extreme. The conscience-sect (Found-Beauty) is the player's lever against the faction's signature horror.*

## VII. Player-facing surface

### How the player encounters the Bower-Folk

1. **A tended installation, early.** Before Posy, the player finds a Bower-Folk arrangement in or near a starter region — a sorted-color display, a glow-garden — and an artisan who judges the player's *eye* by what they do, not what they say.
2. **The aesthetic-reputation system.** The Bower-Folk's reputation is keyed to the **aesthetic value** of offered items, not economic value (per IDEAS.md:401). A worthless-looking but visually striking object beats an expensive plain one. Rare *pigments* and rare-spectrum bio-light strains earn distinct reputation. **They refuse to sell to a player they judge to "have no eye."**
3. **The following Caster (horror beat).** At low health, in Bower-Folk-adjacent zones, a Caster may follow the player offering Resin-Cast finalization — *"You are so close. So close to perfect. Just a little resin, my friend. Please. Please hold still."* (per IDEAS.md:597). Persistent, hard to refuse. Accepting leads to Resin-Cast, which **cannot be self-escaped** — only broken out of by another character (rescue-from-display content, per IDEAS.md:597; resolved as displacement-not-death per `The Fully Preserved Player`).
4. **Posy.** Mid-game. The pilgrimage. The Bower on display. The village as her living artwork. The eerie-beautiful tone at full strength.
5. **Speaking with the Bower.** The dialogue mediated through aesthetic acts: what the player picks up, arranges, how carefully they choose words in her presence. *No other god is met this way.* The player composes their half of the conversation out of behavior.
6. **The sect politics.** Mid-late. Arrangers / Found-Beauty / Finishers. Allying with the Found-Beauty lets the player work *against* the non-consensual preservation from inside the faction; allying with the Finishers opens the darkest aesthetic content.

### Reputation system

Gained by: offering aesthetically valuable items, rare pigments, rare bio-light strains; tending or contributing to installations; demonstrating "an eye"; Choir-Touched or preservation-transformed appearance (a player *visibly transformed by aesthetic event* gains standing, per IDEAS.md:616).

Lost by: vandalism, ugliness-with-intent, refusing the Bower's regard crudely, destroying installations.

**Reputation forks:** Arrangers / Found-Beauty / Finishers alignment.

### Rewards

- **Aesthetic items unavailable elsewhere** (per IDEAS.md:401) — Bower-Lapis-set objects, rare-pigment goods, beautiful weapons that trade damage for *dialogue-currency* value (per IDEAS.md:805).
- **Access to the most visually significant locations** the Bower-Folk control.
- **The Cleaner's enzyme craft** — gentle un-preservation and resurfacing (ties into `The Fully Preserved Player` un-preservation tooling).
- **The Bower's regard** — at high standing, to be *seen* by the Bower; aesthetic blessings; her negotiated input on a Renewal world (§VIII).
- **The Found-Beauty alliance** — the path to stopping the Finishers and protecting the player (and NPCs) from non-consensual Casting.

## VIII. Quest spine

### Early-game

- **The eye test.** The player's first Bower-Folk artisan judges them by an aesthetic act. Establishes the reputation-by-beauty system and the faction's strangeness.
- **The first installation contribution.** The player offers an object; learns that striking-but-worthless beats expensive-but-plain.

### Mid-game

- **The following Caster.** The horror beat made personal: a sincere, devout Caster wants to *finish* the player, and means it kindly. The player's first real encounter with completion-is-stillness.
- **Posy and the Bower.** The pilgrimage; the behavior-mediated dialogue; the village-as-artwork.
- **The sect fork.** Arrangers / Found-Beauty / Finishers. The Found-Beauty ask the player to help prove the Bower *chose* her pose (that choice, not compulsion, is the doctrine); the Finishers ask the player to help finish a "nearly perfect" subject. **Mutually exclusive moral content.**

### Late-game

- **The rescue-from-display.** A major content piece: an NPC (or a player's companion) Resin-Cast against their will, on display in an installation, conscious-or-not (the load-bearing ambiguity of `The Preserved`). The player breaks them out — or chooses not to.
- **The Bower's question.** Deep-standing content: the player learns the contradiction — that the Bower stopped herself because a living artist could not be as beautiful as a fixed one — and is invited to answer whether she is at peace or in a 900-year stillness she cannot escape. *She does not know. The player's answer shapes how she meets the endings.*

### Ending-aligned arcs

- **Consume.** The Choir dissolves arrangement into growth; the Bower-Folk's installations rot into substrate. The Bower may persist as an aesthetic-chamber-god under the Wedded's reign (per Phase 1 v2) — *beauty surviving as decoration in someone else's world.*
- **Preserve.** The Sleeping World is *still* — and stillness is the Bower's purest beauty. **The Preserve ending is, aesthetically, the Bower's ideal**: a world arrested in perfect arrangement forever. This is quietly the most disturbing alignment in the game — the faction of beauty preferring the world held motionless, because motion was always the enemy of composition.
- **Renewal.** The Bower's agreement requires **aesthetic input on the new Tree** — its branching pattern, leaf-arrangement, color (per Phase 1 v2). She will design the renewed world's beauty if committed. *A player who has resolved her contradiction — convinced her that living beauty (which moves, ages, changes) is worth more than fixed beauty — gets a Bower who designs a Tree meant to grow and change, not a Tree meant to be perfect and still. The same ending looks different depending on what the player taught her.*

## IX. Open questions

- **The Bower's mortal name.** Open per Phase 1 v2. *(Soft recommendation: her name is the one word she arranged most carefully, and it is written nowhere — she considered written names ugly. The god of Beauty is the one of the Six whose name was never recorded because she found recording it inelegant. Contrast the Counter, whose name is on millions of contracts and read by no one.)*
- **At peace or bored.** Whether the Bower, after 900 years as her own installation, is serene or unbearably static — and whether she knows. This doc soft-commits *she cannot tell.* Phase 7 may refine.
- **Whether the Found-Beauty are right about her pose.** Did the Bower *choose* her stillness (Found-Beauty doctrine) or was she the first non-consensual Cast (a darker reading the Finishers would embrace)? Phase 6/7. *The answer determines whether the faction's signature horror is a betrayal of its god or an obedience to her.*
- **Resin-Cast consciousness.** Inherited from `The Preserved` — is a Resin-Cast figure aware? Load-bearing horror ambiguity; deliberately unresolved (per IDEAS.md:642).

## X. Tonal balance check

- **Biological horror.** The Resin-Cast bodies; the bog where the resin's raw material is gathered; the Cleaner's enzyme baths resurfacing the dead; the player's own skin sealed mid-life. The Bower-Folk are a **primary biological-horror faction**, distinct from the Choir's wet growth — theirs is the horror of *arrest*, of the body fixed and shining and unable to decay.
- **Cosmic horror.** The contradiction — Beauty whose purest form is stillness, hence death; a god who stopped herself in life and may not know if she is at peace; a faction that looks at the living and sees unfinished sculptures. *Beauty as the enemy of motion* is the cosmic note, and it makes the Preserve ending quietly monstrous from the aesthete's angle.
- **Comedy.** The eye-judging snobbery (refusing to sell to those with "no eye"); the price-of-beauty argument with the Concord; the *sincerity* of the following Caster, which is funny right up until it isn't (*"just a little resin, my friend"*); the Bower-Folk delightedly aestheticizing a Honey-Sealed player's insect-attracting sweet scent (per IDEAS.md:616, 629). *The comedy is devout aesthetes treating cosmic apocalypse as a composition problem.*
