# 04.03 — Pale Curation

> **Authority:** Phase-history faction document. `Lore/10_Bible.md` is the current canon authority; this file is current only where it aligns with the Bible or is explicitly marked canonical/current.
>
> Phase 4.03 of the lore project. Third per-faction design doc. **Salted-founded; the preservation infrastructure of the world; parent of the Imminent Archive Catchers** (`Lore/Factions/09_ImminentArchive.md`).
>
> Where the Palimpsest holds memory in *record* and the Choir dissolves it into *substrate*, Pale Curation holds memory in *the preserved body itself.* The vessel is the message. This is the precise doctrinal inverse of the Palimpsest — and the reason the Salted and the Reader, once comrades, have not spoken in ~1010 years.

## I. Founding

Pale Curation is the only major faction that **did not exist in the Cataclysm Years.** It was founded in the **Pale Curation Schism, Generation 3, ~year 70** (per Phase 3), when the Salted broke from the Palimpsest over the doctrine of what memory's vessel is worth.

### The Salted — the founding god

Per Phase 1 v2:

- **Then.** A scholar, like the Reader. In the pre-Felling civilization the two were *colleagues, and almost certainly more* — the Palimpsest's archive is uncharacteristically vague about the relationship, and the vagueness is the Reader's editorial choice (the Reader controls the archive; the Reader has reasons). Both were summoned by the Tree's dreams; both gathered at the Felling-Site; the Salted took the **Third Strike** and **ascended into Preservation** — the Tree's instinct to *hold-against-decay.*
- **The first decades.** The Salted did not found a faction immediately. She worked *with* the Reader, inside the Palimpsest, for ~70 years. The two led the Palimpsest jointly — the Reader holding Memory-as-record, the Salted holding Preservation-of-vessel — *as one faction.* By every account those decades were the closest either god came to *partnership.* They were rebuilding the world's memory together, in two complementary modes, in love (the archive will not say "in love"; everyone who reads between the lines knows).
- **The Schism (~year 70).** The argument was theological and it became personal. Reduced: **the Reader held that memory is held through record — bodies are vessels, the vessel is unimportant. The Salted held that memory is fragile and record is fragile; bodies endure; preserve the body and you preserve the memory by extension; the vessel is the substrate of the memory.** Neither could yield. The Salted walked away — with perhaps a third of the Palimpsest's membership, those who agreed — and built the first preservation chamber, the future Salt-Vault (per Phase 2 v2). **They have not spoken since.** ~1010 years.
- **The self-preservation (~Gen 16, ~year 480).** The defining act of her god-form. She had herself **Salt-Cured while still alive and conscious.** She did not die first. She lay down in the deepest chamber of the Salt-Vault, and her people salted her, and she *continued* — preserved, conscious, file-able. She has lain there ever since. *(This act is the theological seed the Imminent Archive Catchers would later misread — see `Lore/Factions/09_ImminentArchive.md` and §V below. The Salted preserved herself **while alive**; the Catchers concluded that pre-mortem preservation is sanctioned.)*
- **Now.** She lies in the deepest Salt-Vault chamber, **Salt-Cured, perfectly conscious, perfectly file-able.** Her eyes are salt-sealed. Officers visit her chamber once a year for the **file-update ceremony** — they read her current file aloud and append the year's single line. For ~600 years the line has, with rare exceptions, read: **"Status: continuing."** She can speak. Her voice cracks with salt-grit; the words come slowly through a salt-sealed mouth. *She does not speak often. When she does, the Pale Curation hierarchy treats it as scripture.*
- **Her disposition.** **Rigid. Theologically certain. Heartbroken.** She has been right (in her own frame) for a thousand years and it has cost her everything that mattered to her before she was right. *The certainty and the heartbreak are the same thing, hardened together like salt around a wound.*

### The Salt-Vault's founding

The first preservation chamber (~year 70) was a salt-vault in a lesser tepui near a wasteland-edge salt source (the salt geology that would become the Pale-Salt economy). The Salted chose salt because *salt was abundant in the post-Felling raw-sun zones and salt preserves* — the choice was practical before it was sacred. Over centuries the Salt-Vault grew into the central Pale Curation archive (Phase 2 v2, Tier 3), a vast complex of indexed preservation chambers built down into and around the tepui's sinkhole.

## II. Doctrine

### The official line

Phrased as Pale Curation would phrase it:

> *Memory is fragile. Record is fragile — ink corrodes (the Palimpsest knows this and pretends not to), inscriptions erode, witnesses misremember, copies drift. But the body endures. A body preserved is a memory preserved entire — not the abstracted, copied, lossy version the Palimpsest archives, but the thing itself, held against time. We do not interpret the dead. We do not paraphrase them. We hold them. When the next great cosmological event comes — and it is coming; the Thinning is its herald — the world will not lose what it lost at the Felling, because we will have preserved the vessels that matter.*

Six doctrinal positions:

1. **The vessel is the substrate of memory.** A body is not a container memory can be poured out of; the body *is* the memory, in its truest form. To preserve the body is to preserve the person *entire*, not a copy. This is the precise inverse of the Palimpsest's "bodies are vessels."
2. **Record is lossy; preservation is total.** Every act of recording loses something (the Palimpsest abstracts, copies, paraphrases; each step drifts). Preservation loses nothing — the preserved body is *exactly* what it was. *(The Catchers take this further: even death is a loss; preserve before death and lose nothing at all. The mainline rejects this, but the logic is the mainline's own.)*
3. **The Felling's execution failed because the right bodies were not preserved.** The Tree's plan was sound; the catastrophe came partly because, in the chaos, *no one preserved the cosmologically-important.* If the bodies of the Six's mortal-selves had been preserved, if the *witnesses* had been preserved — *if the seventh's body had been preserved* — the inheritance might have been completable later. **This is Pale Curation's deepest theological hope: somewhere, preserved by accident or design, the seventh's body might still exist** (the Drowned Ledger? the Beating ruins?), and if found and preserved properly, *the Naming-inheritance could still be claimed posthumously.* This hope ties Pale Curation directly to the Seventh question and the Renewal ending's Naming-inheritance mechanic.
4. **Preservation is the highest sacred act.** To preserve a body is to defy the Felling's lesson (that everything is lost) and the Choir's project (that everything should be dissolved). It is a moral act, the highest one.
5. **The dead must be correctly filed.** A preserved body that is mis-indexed, mis-positioned, or un-catalogued is *almost as bad as a decayed one* — because a body whose identity is lost is a memory that cannot be retrieved. Filing is sacred administration. (This is where Pale Curation becomes genuinely, bleakly comic — see §III.)
6. **Preserve, do not wake.** Pale Curation prefers the Preserve ending absolutely. The Root should sleep, sealed, forever; the world should continue, held against time, indefinitely. Waking the Root (Renewal) risks another Felling; consuming it (Consume) is the Choir's abomination. *Pale Curation's entire theology is "hold what is; change nothing; preserve."*

### The unofficial sub-positions

- **The Mainline filers** hold all six. Most members.
- **The Salt-Pure.** Hold that *Salt-Curing is the only true preservation* — the Salted Salt-Cured herself, and her choice is the definitive endorsement. The other four methods (Ink-Bath, Honey-Seal, Bog-Take, Resin-Cast) are *tolerated* but lesser. Conservative; dominant in the Salt-Vault itself.
- **The Method-Pluralists.** Hold that all five preservation methods are valid; the Salted chose salt for *practical* reasons (it was abundant), not as a doctrinal exclusion. They point to the Bower's Resin-Cast and the Palimpsest-adjacent Ink-Bath as legitimate. More cosmopolitan; dominant in regional chambers far from the Salt-Vault.
- **The Sorters.** A small, obsessive sub-group of Curators who study the Felling itself, trying to identify *whose bodies were the cosmologically-important ones that were lost.* They cross-reference Palimpsest archives (the rare warm Palimpsest-Pale-Curation cooperation), Beating ruins, Drowned Ledger excavations. Their grail is *finding the seventh's body.* Most of Pale Curation considers them eccentric; the Sorters consider themselves the only ones doing the work that matters.
- **The Catchers (Imminent Archive).** Expelled. Their own doc (`09`). Mentioned here because they are *technically* a Pale Curation sub-position that the mainline excommunicated — pre-mortem preservation. See §V.

## III. Structure — the filing bureaucracy

Pale Curation is a **bureaucratic hierarchy** organized around *filing.* Like the Palimpsest, it has individual mortal members with named roles; unlike the Palimpsest, its god does not speak through its members (the Salted speaks only from her chamber, once a year, in person). **Pale Curation is the most *institutionally* structured faction** — it runs on procedure, index, and protocol.

### Roles, in approximate seniority

- **Aide** — apprentice. Learns preservation chemistry, filing protocol, indexing systems. Several years of training.
- **Filer** — performs preservation work. Salt-cures, ink-baths, honey-seals, resin-casts. The hands of the faction. *Most of the player's Pale Curation encounters are with Filers.*
- **Indexer** — catalogs and positions preserved bodies. Maintains the index systems. The body's *file* is the Indexer's responsibility; misfiling is the Indexer's crime.
- **Curator** — decides *what* to preserve. Triages incoming bodies; allocates preservation resources; makes the sorting decisions that the faction's whole theology turns on. Senior, weighty role.
- **Archive-Keeper** — runs a specific chamber. Administrative head of a regional archive.
- **Officer** — the faction's administration; inter-chamber coordination; external relations (with the Concord, Tent-Right, Palimpsest).
- **The Salted** — consulted annually via file-update ceremony; speaks rarely; her words are scripture.

### The filing system

Pale Curation's preserved bodies are **indexed under their last words.** A Salt-Cured body's file leads with the final sentence the person spoke (or, if unknown, "[unrecorded]" — a category the faction considers a small tragedy). The chambers are organized so that bodies can be *retrieved by what they last said* — a cataloging system that is simultaneously beautiful (every preserved person remembered by their final utterance), horrible (the mass chambers of indexed corpses), and bleakly comic (Indexers arguing over the correct transcription of a last word; the cross-reference systems; the protocol disputes).

**The Salted's own file** leads with — *nothing.* Her last words before self-preservation were never recorded; she preserved herself mid-sentence, or in silence, and the file's lead-line is "[the Salted; founding; unrecorded]." The annual ceremony appends "Status: continuing." For ~600 years. *That her own file lacks a last-word is one of the faction's quiet griefs — the founder of the index cannot be found by the index's own method.*

### What counts as authority

Institutional rank governs entirely. There is no channeled-divine-voice (unlike the Palimpsest). The Salted's annual utterances are the only divine input, and they are *rare and brief.* This makes Pale Curation **the most self-governing of the god-founded factions** — the Salted's near-total silence means the mortal hierarchy runs the faction on procedure, and has for ~600 years.

The Salted's silence is *itself* a structural fact the faction has organized around. **Major decisions the Salted has never ruled on are simply unresolved** — the faction operates by precedent and protocol in the absence of divine direction. The largest such unresolved matter is the Catchers (§V).

### What counts as crime

- **Allowing decay.** Failing to maintain a preserved body; letting a specimen degrade. The primary crime.
- **Misfiling.** Mis-indexing or mis-positioning a body so it cannot be retrieved. Treated nearly as seriously as allowing decay.
- **Destroying a preserved body.** The worst crime (preservation reversed is memory annihilated).
- **Preserving incorrectly.** Botching the chemistry; producing a poorly-preserved body. A craft-failure that is also a moral one.
- **Pre-mortem preservation of the unwilling** (the Catcher heresy). *Technically* a crime by mainline doctrine — and yet *the Salted has never ruled on it*, so the faction cannot fully prosecute it. The deepest institutional fault-line.
- **Enzyme-disposal of a valid specimen.** Pale Curation uses carnivorous-plant enzymes (per IDEAS.md) to dispose of *poorly-filed or decayed* specimens — but enzyme-disposing a *valid* specimen is destruction. The line between valid-disposal and crime is policed obsessively.

## IV. Geographic anchors

Per Phase 2 v2:

- **The Salt-Vault** (Tier 3). The central archive. The Salted lies in its deepest chamber. The mass preservation chambers — *tens of thousands of Salt-Cured bodies, indexed under their last words, in indexed silence* — are here. The annual file-update ceremony happens here. **The single most cosmic-horror-by-scale location in the game** (per The Preserved IDEAS entry). Adjacent: the Palimpsest's Salt-Vault scribe-station (the Estrangement-Bearers; the Reader-Salted proximity).
- **Marrowstye** (Tier 2). A regional preservation chamber doing *mundane* work (the dead of nearby villages, salt-cured for fees). **The unwanted Catcher cell operates from a disused wing.** The player's likely first encounter with both Pale Curation mainline and the Catcher problem.
- **Regional chambers** — every Tier-2 and Tier-3 place has a Pale Curation chamber of some scale. Marrowstye-style mundane preservation for fees is the faction's bread-and-butter economy.
- **The Beating salt-mining sites.** Pale-Salt comes from the wasteland (per Phase 2 v2; Stones with Stake). Pale Curation has a **salt-supply partnership with Tent-Right** — the wasteland mines the salt, Pale Curation buys it, the Concord transports it. *Pale Curation's entire material basis depends on this wasteland relationship.*
- **The Sorter expeditions** — to the Drowned Ledger (joint with the Palimpsest, reading pre-Felling bog-preserved humans) and the Beating ruins (looking for cosmologically-important bodies). The Sorters' grail — the seventh's body — would be found, if anywhere, in one of these.
- **Mixed-presence places** — Thresk (per Phase 2 v2; Pale Curation holds the upper quarter), and others. Pale Curation is present at low strength in many places, running mundane local preservation.

## V. Inter-faction relationships

### Palimpsest — estranged siblings; tactical alliance; the central wound

Covered from the Palimpsest side in `02_Palimpsest.md`. From Pale Curation's side:

- **The Schism cost the Salted everything.** She was right (in her frame); being right cost her the Reader. The faction she founded is, in a sense, *a monument to a theological position she would have traded for the relationship if she could have stayed and been wrong.* (She would not say this. She has said almost nothing in 600 years. But the Sorters who study the founding suspect it, and the Estrangement-Bearers next door pray it.)
- **The tactical alliance is real.** Pale Curation and Palimpsest cooperate constantly against the Choir, against memory-and-body destruction, against the Bloom. The Salt-Vault scribe-station exists *because both factions wanted the cooperation* — and, secretly, because later members of both factions hoped proximity might force the gods to speak. It never has.
- **The Reader-Salted estrangement** is the deepest character arc in the game (per Phase 1 v2). From Pale Curation's side: the Salted's annual utterances have, on rare occasions, contained sentences scholars suspect were *meant for the Reader* — but the Salted has never asked anyone to deliver them, and Pale Curation protocol does not permit transcribing the Salted's words for external delivery without her explicit instruction, which she has never given. *The words are spoken into the chamber and stay there.*

### Rot Choir — adversarial

Preservation directly opposes consumption. Salt-Cured bodies resist mycelial dissolution (salt is fungus-toxic). The two factions are *structurally opposed* — every body Pale Curation preserves is a body the Choir cannot consume.

- **The Choir's un-preservation chambers exist because of Pale Curation's success** (per IDEAS.md). The Choir developed enzyme-and-deliquescent techniques specifically to break down Salt-Cured bodies. *This is the closest the détente comes to active conflict on the Pale Curation front.*
- **Pale Curation's enzyme-disposal paradox** (using carnivorous-plant enzymes to dispose of poorly-filed specimens) is *noticed and quietly approved by the Choir* (dissolution is dissolution, even when Pale Curation does it). This makes Pale Curation deeply uncomfortable — *the act they use for hygiene is the act their enemy reveres.*

### Imminent Archive Catchers — their own expelled splinter

The deepest internal matter. Covered fully in `09_ImminentArchive.md`. The essential fact for Pale Curation: **the Catchers are the Salted's theological children, and she will not disown them, and she will not endorse them, and her silence is the faction's open wound.**

- The Catchers' founding logic (~Gen 27) is *the Salted's own act extended*: she preserved herself while alive; therefore pre-mortem preservation is sanctioned. **The mainline rejects the conclusion but cannot deny the premise** — the Salted *did* preserve herself alive.
- **Why the Salted will not rule.** Later canon keeps this answer: *her silence is not indecision; it is guilt.* She knows the Catchers are her reflection. She knows her self-preservation created them. She cannot disown them (they are doing what she did); she cannot endorse them (what they do to the unwilling is monstrous). So she is silent — and her silence is a wound she shares, in shape, with the Reader's silence. **The two ancient comrades both refusing to settle a matter involving both their factions is the closest thing to coordination they have managed in a thousand years.** *They are, even estranged, still synchronized in their grief.*

### Saccharine Concord — major partner

The Concord is Pale Curation's logistics. Salt transport from the Beating; body transport between regions; preservation-supply distribution; the gray-market trade in preserved bodies (which Pale Curation officially condemns and unofficially cannot stop). The Counter is *Pale Curation's most important external relationship* — without Concord logistics, the salt-supply chain collapses.

### Bower-Folk — uneasy overlap

Both preserve. Pale Curation preserves for *memory-retention*; Bower-Folk preserve for *beauty.* They overlap on Resin-Cast (the Bower-Folk's specialty; a method Pale Curation's Method-Pluralists accept and the Salt-Pure distrust). They overlap on *which bodies deserve preservation* — and disagree (Pale Curation: the cosmologically-important; Bower-Folk: the beautiful). **The overlap produces a long-standing, low-grade territorial tension** — occasionally both factions want to preserve the same body for different reasons. The Bower (the god) and the Salted (the god) have a centuries-old diplomatic coolness over exactly this.

### Catacomb-villagers — village-by-village

- Villages that practice **preservation** (Salt-Cured, Honey-Sealed) are natural Pale Curation contacts; some buy preservation expertise.
- Villages that practice **Stone Burial** (giving the body to subterranean fauna; per IDEAS.md) are *theologically opposed* to Pale Curation — Stone Burial is, in Pale Curation's view, *deliberate decay*, almost as bad as Choir consumption. Pale Curation finds Stone-Burial villages distressing and avoids them.
- The **Honey-jar larder villages** (per IDEAS.md The Preserved) are a special horror to Pale Curation — Honey-Sealed kin kept near food is *preservation without proper filing*, which Pale Curation considers a category of disrespect to the preserved.

### Tent-Right wasteland culture — salt-supply partnership

The material basis of Pale Curation's existence. Tent-Right mines Pale-Salt; Pale Curation buys it; the relationship is centuries-old and stable. **Pale Curation officers who deal with Tent-Right learn the three-day oath and respect it scrupulously** — the salt-supply depends on it. Tent-Right, for its part, finds Pale Curation's obsession with the dead *strange but harmless* and values the steady salt-trade.

### Driving Bloom — enemy

Half-preserved bodies (in the first week of salt-cure, before the salt fully penetrates) are *uniquely valuable Bloom hosts* (per IDEAS.md). The Bloom targets fresh Pale Curation preservation work specifically. Pale Curation's preservation chambers maintain Bloom-watch protocols on all in-progress preservations. *A Bloomed half-preserved body is one of the most dangerous things in a Pale Curation chamber.*

## VII. Player-facing surface

### How the player encounters Pale Curation

1. **Marrowstye** (Tier 2). The player's likely first contact — a mundane regional preservation chamber, and the unwanted Catcher cell in the disused wing. The player can take body-delivery contracts, observe mundane preservation, and discover the Catcher problem.
2. **Body-delivery contracts.** Early-mid: Pale Curation pays drams for the player to deliver bodies for preservation — found dead, fallen travelers, the unclaimed. *The player learns the faction's economy is built on this mundane service.*
3. **The filing systems.** The player who spends time in a Pale Curation chamber encounters the *index* — bodies catalogued under their last words. A genuinely affecting and disturbing piece of environmental storytelling.
4. **The Salt-Vault.** Mid-late. The player reaches the central archive; sees the mass preservation chambers; understands the scale. **A designed set-piece encounter** (per The Preserved IDEAS entry — not procedural).
5. **Meeting the Salted.** At Trusted standing, the player is invited to a file-review ceremony in the Salted's chamber. Her salt-sealed eyes; her cracking voice; the annual line "Status: continuing." She speaks to the player slowly. *This is the player's audience with the third god.*
6. **The Sorter subplot.** The player can join the Sorters' work — the search for cosmologically-important lost bodies, the obsessive study of the Felling, the grail-quest for the seventh's body. *This subplot ties directly into the Seventh question and the Renewal ending.*
7. **The Catcher entanglement.** Per IDEAS.md and `09`. The player will encounter Catchers (most threateningly at low HP). The player can investigate the Catcher cells, choose to oppose or aid them, and become entangled in the mainline-vs-Catcher politics.
8. **The Reader-Salted reconciliation (Pale Curation side).** The player who pursues the reconciliation arc (from the Palimpsest side, per `02`) must *also* build trust with the Salted here — possibly by bringing her a preserved body of personal significance, possibly by completing Sorter work, possibly by being the first to *ask her directly* about the Reader in a thousand years.

### Reputation system

Gained by:
- Delivering bodies for preservation
- Correct handling of bodies (the player who damages a body in transit loses reputation)
- Reporting decay or misfiling
- Opposing the Choir (preventing consumption of a preservable body)
- Completing Sorter work
- Supporting the mainline against the Catchers (or, on a Catcher-aligned fork, the reverse)

Lost by:
- Destroying or damaging preserved bodies
- Enzyme-disposing valid specimens
- Aiding the Choir's consumption
- (Mainline fork) Aiding the Catchers
- Practicing or endorsing Stone Burial

**Reputation forks:** Mainline / Salt-Pure / Method-Pluralist / Sorter / (and the Catcher fork, which is `09`'s content and is *anti*-mainline).

### Rewards

- **Pale-Salt** (per Stones with Stake) at internal rates
- **Preservation services** — the player can have *their own dead* (companions, found bodies) preserved by method, with all the downstream consequences (per The Preserved IDEAS entry — preserved companions can be Re-Membered cheaply, carried as funeral cargo, etc.)
- **Preservation chemistry** — the player can learn to preserve bodies themselves (Tinker-adjacent skill)
- **Salt-Cured durability infusions** — items/gear that resist decay, Bloom, and Choir-consumption
- **Sorter archive access** — lore about the Felling and the Six's mortal-selves unavailable to other paths
- **The Salted's counsel** — at the highest reputation, ongoing dialogue with the Salted (her slow, salt-cracked voice; her thousand-year perspective; her guarded grief)

## VIII. Quest spine

### Early-game arc

- **Marrowstye preservation contract.** The player delivers a body; observes mundane preservation; sees the Catcher cell.
- **The Catcher cell discovery.** The player learns Marrowstye's unwanted-Catcher-cell problem; the village wants it gone but the Pale Curation supply contract complicates removal (per Phase 2 v2). First exposure to Catcher politics.
- **The index.** The player encounters the last-words filing system. A small body-delivery quest where the player must *learn the dead person's last words* (from a witness, a companion, a found note) so the body can be correctly filed. *Affecting introduction to the faction's soul.*

### Mid-game arc

- **The Salt-Vault.** The player reaches the central archive. The mass chambers. The scale-horror set-piece.
- **Meeting the Salted.** File-review ceremony. The third god. Her certainty and her heartbreak.
- **The Sorter subplot.** The player joins the Sorters; participates in the Drowned Ledger or Beating expeditions; learns the grail-quest for the seventh's body. *Ties to the Seventh question.*
- **The Catcher politics deepen.** The player learns the Catchers are the Salted's theological children; learns the Salted has never ruled; begins to understand the silence-as-guilt. *(Maybe the player can ask the Salted directly about the Catchers — and receive only silence, the same silence she gives everyone, the silence that is its own answer.)*

### Late-game arc

- **The Reader-Salted reconciliation (Pale Curation side).** The player builds trust with the Salted. The first messenger between the two gods in a thousand years (the Palimpsest side has the Estrangement-Bearers; the Pale Curation side has *no one* — the Salted has no equivalent sub-sect because she ordered none; *the player is the first to even attempt it from her side*).
- **The seventh's-body quest.** If the Sorters' grail is pursued to its end, the player may find — or definitively fail to find — a body they believe could serve as the seventh vessel. Later canon frames any such use as a **flawed vessel-path** Renewal, not the true answer Naro understood.

### Ending-aligned arcs

- **Preserve path.** Pale Curation's preferred outcome, *passionately.* The player aids in sealing the First Root. The Salted's agreement is among the easiest of the Six for this path — she wants it more than anything. **The cost:** the Salted's agreement is *parallel-not-joint* with the Reader's (per Phase 1 v2), which means the Preserve path *forecloses* the Reader-Salted reconciliation (reconciliation is a Renewal-path requirement; on Preserve, they cooperate in parallel, never reconciling). *The player who chooses Preserve is choosing to leave the two gods estranged forever.* This is one of the heaviest hidden costs of the "safest" ending.
- **Consume path.** Pale Curation opposes absolutely. The player is the faction's enemy. The Salted may speak — a rare second utterance in a year — to plead, in her salt-cracked voice, that the player not let the Choir eat the world's last preserved memory.
- **Renewal path.** The Salted's agreement requires *the player committing to preserve the existing post-Felling cultural memory alongside Renewal* — the new world must *remember what was*, not overwrite it (per Phase 1 v2). She will not agree to a Renewal that erases the thousand years of preservation. **And the Renewal path is the only one on which the Reader-Salted reconciliation is possible** — because Renewal requires their *joint* agreement, which requires the player to have brought them, at last, to speak.

## IX. Later settlements and writing notes

- **The Salted's mortal name.** Later canon names her **Othren**. Her name is filed; her last words remain "[unrecorded]."
- **Why exactly the Salted will not rule on the Catchers.** Settled as guilt: they are her reflection. Whether a player can force a ruling, and what it costs her, remains a quest-design choice.
- **Whether the seventh's body exists and can be found.** Keep this genuinely uncertain until very late. If the player uses a found/preserved vessel for Renewal, it belongs to the flawed vessel-path, not the true practice-path.
- **The contents of the Salted's rare non-"continuing" file-lines.** Over ~600 years, the annual ceremony has *occasionally* recorded something other than "Status: continuing." What those lines say is open. *(Soft recommendation: the player can read the full file at high reputation; the rare deviating lines, read in sequence, tell a fragmentary thousand-year story of the Salted's grief — and at least one of them is, unmistakably, about the Reader.)*
- **Whether Stone Burial and Pale Curation can ever reconcile.** Open. Probably not; the doctrinal opposition is total.

## X. Tonal balance check

- **Biological horror.** The mass preservation chambers (tens of thousands of indexed corpses); the Salted Salt-Cured-and-conscious; the half-preserved-as-Bloom-hosts; the enzyme-disposal of poorly-filed specimens. *Preservation is body-horror held still.*
- **Cosmic horror.** The Salted's thousand-year conscious preservation, file updated annually with "Status: continuing," is one of the project's quietest and deepest cosmic-horror beats. The scale of the Salt-Vault. The seventh's-body hope (a faction that has spent centuries hoping a specific corpse exists). *The certainty-and-heartbreak hardened together like salt around a wound.*
- **Comedy.** Pale Curation is the project's **bureaucratic-comedy anchor faction.** The filing protocols. The Indexers arguing over the correct transcription of a last word. The annual file-update ceremony's relentless "Status: continuing." The "[unrecorded]" category as a small institutional tragedy. The cross-reference disputes. *The comedy is the bureaucracy taking the cosmic horror with deadly procedural seriousness — which is exactly how a real institution would.*
