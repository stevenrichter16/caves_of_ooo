# 04.10 — The Inkbound (formalization)

> Phase 4.10 of the lore project. **A decision record, not a faction treatment.** This doc exists to *commit* the open question the Palimpsest doc (`02` §"Inkbound (open)") deferred to Phase 4: **are the Inkbound a separate faction or a Palimpsest sub-sect?** It closes Phase 4.
>
> **Committed: the Inkbound are a Palimpsest sub-sect.** There is no separate Inkbound institution, no separate founding god, no separate doctrine. Their content is canonical *inside* the Palimpsest's structure. This doc records the decision, the rationale, and the cross-references — and exists primarily so a future reader looking for "the Inkbound faction" finds the redirect rather than assuming an unwritten doc is missing.

---

## I. The decision

Per the recommendation committed in `02_Palimpsest.md` §196 (and restated at §305):

> **The Inkbound are not a separate faction. They are the Palimpsest's Body-Readers and Silent Order, viewed together, taken to their sacred extreme.**

The two candidate framings the Palimpsest doc laid out:

1. **Sub-sect of Palimpsest** — the Inkbound *are* the Body-Readers (`02` §54, §70) plus the Silent Order (`02` §55), the deepest expression of the body-as-archive practice. No separate doc, no separate god. **← COMMITTED.**
2. **Separate splinter faction** — a Gen 7-9 schism producing a distinct institution, partial-Naming-heirs tracing to a sub-First who performed a symbolic strike. *Rejected.*

**Why the sub-sect framing wins** (per `02` §196, affirmed here):

- **Faction-count discipline.** Phase 4 already carries nine faction surfaces (Choir, Palimpsest, Pale Curation, Concord, Bower-Folk, Catacomb-villagers, Tent-Right, Imminent Archive, Driving Bloom). A tenth distinct institution dilutes each one's player-facing distinctiveness. The Inkbound's content is *richer as an intensity within the Palimpsest* than as a thin standalone faction.
- **Doctrinal honesty.** The Inkbound practice (the skin-page Ink-Bathed scribe; the scribe who in death *becomes the manuscript*, per IDEAS.md) is *literally* an extreme version of Palimpsest body-reading, not a different project. The Body-Readers extract-then-archive (`02` §54); the Inkbound make the body the permanent archive. That is a *deepening of the same act*, not a schism from it.
- **It makes existing IDEAS.md Inkbound content canonical with zero new structure** — the skin-page scribes, the most-sacred Ink-Bathed material (per IDEAS.md:478), all live inside the Palimpsest's already-written doc.
- **The "partial Naming-heirs" idea survives the fold** (`02` §196). The Body-Readers + Silent Order, *as a sub-sect*, can carry the Naming-adjacent partial-inheritor framing without needing a separate founding-First. They are the Palimpsest's hands reaching toward Naming through the body — which is thematically apt, since Naming is the missing seventh inheritance and the Inkbound are obsessed with making the dead *legible*, i.e., *named-in-ink-forever*.

## II. What the Inkbound *are* (inside Palimpsest)

For the actual content, **read `02_Palimpsest.md`** — specifically:

- **§54 — The Body-Readers.** Read Ink-Bathed corpses; blur the doctrinal line with the Choir (extract memory vs. preserve-in-archive); defend the practice as *extract-then-archive: the body is a stage, the archive the destination.* Recover memories no other Palimpsest practice can.
- **§55 — The Silent Order.** Scribes under vows of silence who speak only through writing; live in the deepest Quillhold archives; *unusually good Body-Readers — the silence helps them hear what Ink-Bathed bodies say.*
- **§70 — Body-Reader role.** Trained over decades; eccentric; quietly powerful within the institution.
- **§224, §279 — Body-Reading content.** The player can witness a Body-Reading (an Ink-Bathed corpse opened, the inked skin read aloud, the Body-Reader's voice perhaps becoming the Reader's), and can apprentice into the practice (player-class-tier ability).
- **§312 — biological-horror surface.** Ink-saturated skin opened like book-pages; the corpse's voice issuing as the Body-Reader speaks.

**The Inkbound = the Body-Readers + the Silent Order at their sacred limit.** The term "Inkbound" names the *vocation taken to its end*: a scribe so committed to the body-as-archive that they arrange, in life, to *become* an Ink-Bathed manuscript at death — the skin-page scribe of IDEAS.md. The most sacred Inkbound material is an Ink-Bathed scribe's own body (per IDEAS.md:478).

### The one thing this doc adds to the Palimpsest treatment

A clarifying distinction the fold-in makes explicit:

- **A Body-Reader** *reads* Ink-Bathed bodies (a practice).
- **The Silent Order** *are the purest Body-Readers* (a vocation).
- **The Inkbound** *are those who will themselves become the read* (a vow). An Inkbound scribe is a Body-Reader or Silent Order member who has taken the **skin-page vow** — committing their own death-body to Ink-Bath, to be read by future scribes, to *become a permanent named text.* It is the Palimpsest's most extreme answer to the Reader's grief (memory must not be lost): *make yourself unloseable by making yourself a book.*

This is a vow within the sect, not a new institution. An Inkbound is a Silent-Order-or-Body-Reader who has signed their own body into the archive.

## III. Cross-references

- **Palimpsest (`02`)** — parent faction; the Inkbound's entire structural and doctrinal home. The Reader (Memory, distributed across archives) reads *through* Body-Readers when Ink-Bathed bodies are read (`02` §22).
- **Rot Choir (`01`)** — the doctrinal tension the Body-Readers live on: extracting a body's memory *resembles* Choir consumption (`02` §54). The Inkbound's defense (archive-as-destination, not dissolution) is the line that keeps the practice Palimpsest and not Choir.
- **Pale Curation (`03`)** — preservation kinship and rivalry: Pale Curation preserves the body as an *index*; the Inkbound make the body a *text*. Both are "the dead made legible," different media. (Compare the Bower-Folk's "body as composition," `05` — three factions, three readings of the same corpse.)
- **The Preserved / Ink-Bath (IDEAS.md)** — the Ink-Bath preservation method is the Inkbound's sacrament; Ink-Bathed bodies are most useful as Palimpsest reading-material (per IDEAS.md:463); skin-page Ink-Bathed scribes are the most sacred Inkbound material (per IDEAS.md:478).
- **Carnivorous-Plant Enzymes (IDEAS.md:779)** — Drosera-enzyme softening of a sacred Ink-Bathed corpse is the *gravest crime when misused* — an Inkbound-relevant atrocity.
- **Stones with Stake (IDEAS.md)** — Memory-Marble (sacred writing surface) and Black-Gall (sacred ink material) are the Inkbound's material palette, shared with the broader Palimpsest.

## IV. Open questions (deferred to later phases)

- **The skin-page vow as player content.** Can the player take the Inkbound vow — commit their own (eventual) death-body to Ink-Bath and the archive? A Lineage-tier end-of-run choice (per IDEAS.md Lineage cross-links: method-specific descendant-readability). Phase 6/7.
- **Whether an Inkbound's read-aloud voice is the Reader's.** `02` §279 leaves the voice-shift ambiguous (the Body-Reader's voice "may or may not be the Reader's"). For the Inkbound specifically — a scribe reading *another Inkbound's* skin-page — does the Reader speak doubly? Phase 7 (god-voice authoring).
- **Naming-adjacency payoff.** If the Inkbound are the Palimpsest's partial-Naming-heirs (§I), does that matter at the Renewal Naming-inheritance choice (per Phase 1 v2 / Spine §350)? Could an Ink-Bathed, fully-read, perfectly-named body be a *candidate vessel* for the seventh Naming? Phase 6 — potentially a significant late-game thread. *(Soft flag: a "Re-Membered person becomes the seventh god" path already exists per Spine §352; an Inkbound skin-page scribe is the most completely-named dead in the world, which makes them a natural candidate for that path's most potent variant.)*

## V. Tonal balance check

(Inherited from `02_Palimpsest.md` §312-314; the Inkbound do not change the Palimpsest's tonal profile, they intensify it.)

- **Biological horror.** Concentrated here: ink-saturated skin opened like book-pages; a person who arranged in life to become a corpse-manuscript; the read-aloud where the dead speak through the living scribe.
- **Cosmic horror.** The Reader's distributed attention present in the read; the Inkbound's vow as the most extreme answer to memory-loss (*make yourself a book so you cannot be forgotten*) — and the unbearable question of whether it works, whether anything of the scribe is *in* the text or whether it is only ink.
- **Comedy.** Quiet, inherited (`02` §314): the Silent Order's stubborn refusal to speak through anything but ink. *The Inkbound themselves are not comic — they are the Palimpsest's solemn extreme — but the Silent Order's eccentric purity carries the faint thread of it.*
