# 04.02 — The Palimpsest

> Phase 4.02 of the lore project. Second per-faction design doc. **Structural mirror to the Rot Choir** (`Lore/Factions/01_RotChoir.md`) — the central rivalry's other side.
>
> Where the Choir is *patient hunger* and *substrate kindness*, the Palimpsest is *tired grief* and *desperate care*. Both are responses to the same loss: the world's memory shattered when the Tree fell. They disagree fundamentally about whether memory should be **held** (Palimpsest) or **eaten** (Choir).
>
> Follows the per-doc structure committed in `04.01`.

---

## I. Founding

The Palimpsest is the **first-ascended faction** by Strike-order (the First Strike delivered Memory to the Reader) and the **first-organized** by chronological emergence. **The Palimpsest's primary archive begins in year 1 of the post-Felling calendar** — making it the longest continuously-active institution in the world.

### The Reader — the founding god

Per Phase 1 v2:

- **Then.** A pre-Felling scholar. *(Their pre-Felling specialty is open — Phase 7; soft recommendation: a transcriber of oral histories, someone who already spent their mortal life recording what others said. The ascension would then be the *intensification* of what they already were.)* The Tree's dreams summoned them; they gathered at the Felling-Site; they took the First Strike. **In the moment of striking they ascended into Memory** — the Tree's capacity for naming-and-recording, the function by which things stay coherent through time.
- **The first year.** The Reader did not pause to grieve. Within hours of the Felling — *while the cataclysm was still beginning, the flood not yet at its crest, the leaf-light failing for the first time* — the Reader was already *recording.* The Palimpsest's earliest continuous archive begins literally that first day, with first-person accounts of the Felling itself, transcribed in shaking handwriting on bark and salvaged stone. **The Reader understood Memory immediately and worked desperately to record the cataclysm before it became unrecorded.** The other Five took weeks or years to begin their distinctive work. The Reader was working in the first hour.
- **The withdrawal (Gen 12-15, ~years 360-450).** Per Phase 3, the Reader was the second of the Six to begin the long transformation into pure function (after the Wedded's earlier substrate-distribution). The Reader's withdrawal was *gradual* — they kept a body for ~360 years, increasingly distributed in their attention, until the body itself became more *a habit they kept* than a self they inhabited. The body died (or was retired; the Palimpsest's archive is uncharacteristically vague on this) somewhere between Gen 12 and Gen 15. **The Reader has not had a body in ~700 years.**
- **Now.** **The Reader exists across every Palimpsest archive in the world.** Their consciousness is distributed through inscription — every sufficiently-significant Palimpsest record carries a thread of their attention; every long transcription job pulls more of their attention to one place. When a Palimpsest scribe transcribes a dying elder's final testimony, *the Reader is there*, in the work itself. When a sealed file is opened by a senior archivist, the Reader sees what is inside. When an Ink-Bathed body is read by a Body-Reader, the Reader reads through the scribe's eyes.
- **Their disposition.** **Tired. Patient. Sad.** A thousand years of holding the world's memory has produced an exhaustion that is also a tenderness. The Reader *loves* what they hold — every name, every witness-account, every transcribed grief. The love is not a performance; it is what the function has become. *The Reader cannot stop caring about anything they have ever recorded*, and they have recorded for a thousand years. **They blame the seventh's absence personally** — not bitterly, but with the sad clarity of someone who knows that *if Naming had been claimed properly, the world would not require this much exhausting hold.*

### The first decades

After year 1's frantic recording, the Reader directed (still in a mortal body then) the slow gathering of literate survivors into a scribal community. By year 10 there were dozens of Palimpsest scribes; by year 30, hundreds. The Pale Curation Schism in year ~70 cost them perhaps a third of their numbers (those who agreed with the Salted), but the Palimpsest as institution survived.

The Reader's pre-First-Persecution life — the period when they led the Palimpsest *as a god in a body* — was the closest thing to an ordinary divine existence any of the Six lived. People knew the Reader. People could *speak* with them. The transition into pure-distributed-function (the long withdrawal across Gens 4-15) was, for many surface-people, *the end of a real god.* What remained — the channeled-voice-through-scribes mode — felt to many like *less* than what was lost. Senior Palimpsest archivists still teach novices that *the early Reader was the more present Reader, and we should not be sentimental about it.*

## II. Doctrine

### The official line

Phrased as the Palimpsest would phrase it to a sympathetic listener:

> *Memory is continuity. To be is to be recorded — not in the trivial sense, but in the cosmological sense: a thing that is named-and-witnessed continues to exist; a thing that is forgotten dissolves back into substrate. The Tree's holding-power was the original recording; without it, the only continuity is what we can hold in inscription, in witness, in living-memory passed forward. We hold the world. Not as the Tree did — we cannot — but as much as is given us to hold. The Choir would dissolve our work into living-substrate; this is destruction, not transformation. A memory dissolved is a self lost. We are the world's hands, copying it down before the substrate eats it.*

Six doctrinal positions follow:

1. **Naming is the world's spine.** Without explicit naming-and-recording, things fade. The Tree's plan was right to make Naming an inheritance; the Tree's mistake was assuming Naming would happen *without explicit work.* The Reader's project — and by extension the Palimpsest's — is to do that work, faction by faction, body by body, plaque by plaque.
2. **Memory is held in record.** Written, inscribed, witnessed-and-transcribed, body-read. The form varies; the principle is the same: *if it is not held in a durable form, it is lost.* Oral tradition is partial preservation (better than nothing) but not sufficient — the Palimpsest's project is to *write down* what oral tradition holds before the tradition lapses.
3. **Bodies are vessels.** *Vessels matter only insofar as they hold memories.* A body that has been fully recorded — every memory archived, every relationship transcribed, every act witnessed — can be released; the recording IS the person now. *(This is the precise doctrinal split with the Salted, who said the vessel itself matters.)*
4. **Every memory that fades is a small return to chaos.** Forgetting is not neutral. Forgetting *un-makes* things. The Palimpsest's work is therefore *moral* in a way the Choir's consumption is not — failing to record is a kind of slow harm.
5. **The Sealed Libraries protect what the Choir would eat.** The First Persecution proved the Choir's intent; the Reader's response (sealing + un-remembering) was tragic but necessary. The Palimpsest does not regret the protection. The Palimpsest *does* regret the cost — the god of Memory had to destroy memory to protect memory. **The Palimpsest's mainline doctrine treats the un-remembering as a wound the institution carries.**
6. **The Renewal would be the best outcome, but Preserve is the best practical outcome.** The Palimpsest understands that Renewal would *restore the Tree's binding*, which would mean Memory becomes again a thing the world holds *for us* rather than a thing we hold *for the world*. But Renewal requires the Wedded's agreement, which seems impossible. So Preserve is the realistic best — the Sleeping World, the seal held, the archives endured. *The Palimpsest is professionally pessimistic about Renewal and ferociously committed to Preserve.*

### The unofficial sub-positions

- **The Mainline** holds all six positions above. Most scribes.
- **The Searchers** (a tolerated sub-group) believe the Reader's un-remembering was *always meant to be temporary* — that one day the Sealed Libraries would be rediscovered when the threat had passed. They argue: *the Thinning means the threat is changing. We should rediscover them now.* The Reader has not commented on this position, which the Searchers read as permission.
- **The Body-Readers** specialize in reading Ink-Bathed corpses — a practice that *blurs the doctrinal line with the Choir* (the body's memory is being extracted, not preserved in archive). The Body-Readers argue: *we extract and then archive; the body is a stage, the archive is the destination.* Other scribes find this uncomfortable but cannot deny the results — Body-Readers recover memories no other Palimpsest practice can.
- **The Silent Order** are scribes who have taken vows of silence; they speak only through writing. Considered hyper-pure by some, eccentric by most. They live in the deepest Quillhold archives, in dim chambers, surrounded by inscription. *They are unusually good Body-Readers* — *something about the silence helps them hear what Ink-Bathed bodies say.*
- **The Estrangement-Bearers** — a tiny sub-group at the Salt-Vault scribe-station. They maintain an ongoing *one-sided correspondence* with the Salted, sending her letters that go unanswered. They consider themselves to be *holding the relationship open* on the Reader's behalf. The Reader has neither endorsed nor forbidden the practice. **They are the institutional memory of the Reader-Salted estrangement.**
- **The Unforgetting** — a growing splinter dating to the early Thinning (Gen 34). They argue the Reader's commitment to the détente has been wrong — the Choir's recent stirrings prove the détente is failing, and the Sealed Libraries should be rediscovered *now*, even at the cost of revealing them. **Considered heretical by the Mainline.** They are not formally suppressed, but they are not promoted. The Unforgetting overlap heavily with the Searchers (Searchers want to find the Libraries; Unforgetting want to *open* them once found and read them publicly).

## III. Structure — the scribal hierarchy

Unlike the Choir's distributed-consciousness model, the Palimpsest is a **hierarchical scribal institution** with individual mortal scribes occupying named roles. **The Reader can speak through any scribe at any time, which creates a unique dual-authority structure**: institutional rank gives day-to-day authority, but the Reader's voice (when channeled) supersedes any rank.

### Roles, in approximate seniority

- **Novice** — apprentice scribes. Two years of training in inscription, transcription, archive-handling. Wear plain robes, brown-dyed.
- **Field Scribe** — assigned to a region. Travels. Records local events, witness-accounts, oral histories. *Most of the player's Palimpsest encounters are with Field Scribes.* Wear robes the color of their region's Black-Gall ink batch (slight color variations create a recognizable provincial-coding system).
- **Archive Scribe** — works in a specific archive (Quillhold or a regional outpost). Catalogs, cross-references, maintains records. Settled life; rarely travels.
- **Body-Reader** — specialized; reads Ink-Bathed corpses. Trained over decades. Often eccentric. Quietly powerful within the institution.
- **Re-Memberer** — performs the Re-Membering ritual (per IDEAS.md). Extremely senior; very few exist; each Re-Membering is a faction-level event.
- **Translator** — between dialects, between languages, between living and dead memory. Often paired with Body-Readers and with the Listening Tradition catacomb-villages who transcribe the Rooted's dreams.
- **Archive-Master** — runs an archive. The Reader rarely overrides Archive-Masters' decisions. Functionally the top of the institutional hierarchy.
- **The Reader** — present through any scribe doing deep work. No formal seat of authority; *everywhere is their seat.*

### The Reader's channeled voice

When the Reader speaks through a scribe, the scribe's voice changes — *older, vaster, addressing the listener directly.* The scribe is not unconscious; they remain themselves, but the *voice* coming through them is the Reader's. Scribes who are frequently channeled describe it as *being read by someone* — the Reader is *reading them* in the act of speaking through them.

**The Reader can be channeled while:**
- Deep transcription work (long, focused; the most reliable channel)
- Body-reading (Ink-Bathed corpses; the Body-Reader's voice often shifts during the read)
- Re-Membering rituals (the Reader is *always* present at these)
- Major archive-opening ceremonies
- *(After the Thinning began:* **unprompted**, during ordinary archival work. This is one of the most-noticed Thinning-symptoms among Palimpsest scribes; the Reader appears to be *paying more attention* to mortal events again.)

**The Reader cannot be channeled by:**
- A novice (insufficient depth)
- A scribe under duress (the channel requires composure)
- A scribe doing shallow or rote work
- A scribe outside Palimpsest authority (heretics, expelled members)

### What counts as authority

In day-to-day matters: institutional rank governs. An Archive-Master's decision on a contested archive is final unless the Reader intervenes (which is rare and significant). Field Scribes carry their region's autonomy under their Archive-Master's authority. Re-Memberers are deferred to in ritual matters.

In matters of doctrine or major decisions: **the Reader's channeled voice supersedes any rank.** This produces an unusual political dynamic — *a powerful Archive-Master can be publicly overruled by a Novice scribe whose voice has changed.* Most scribes accept this gracefully; it is part of what it means to be Palimpsest. **A scribe who resists the Reader's channeled voice is considered heretical** (this is how the Unforgetting splinter is *technically* heretical even though it has not been formally suppressed — the Reader has not yet channeled to contradict them, but if the Reader did, an Unforgetting scribe who continued would be expelled).

### What counts as crime

- **Destroying memory.** Burning a book, dissolving a body before it can be archived, erasing an inscription, killing a witness before their testimony is taken. *The primary Palimpsest crime.* Cooperating with the Choir to destroy archives is the worst form; cooperating with Pale Curation Catchers to preserve bodies pre-mortem is the *second-worst form* (a preserved-while-alive body's memory cannot be properly archived — the memory is still in the living person and so the archive is *incomplete*).
- **Misrecording.** Deliberately recording false testimony. The Palimpsest cannot survive if its archives are unreliable. Misrecording is treated as worse than ordinary lying — it is *forgery against the world's memory.*
- **Breaking the Silence Vow** (Silent Order specific). Internal infraction.
- **Aiding the Driving Bloom.** Bloom-infection of scribes is one of the Palimpsest's specific terrors — Bloomed scribes produce *parody-scribe* false records that look authentic. Aiding the Bloom is treated as a memory-destruction crime.
- **Refusing the Reader's channeled voice.** Heresy, as above. The Unforgetting are *not* this yet but are close.

There is *no* crime around personal property within Palimpsest (scribes hold property collectively in archives), or around personal speech outside the Reader's voice. The Palimpsest is *narrow* in what it polices — it polices *memory*, and almost nothing else.

## IV. Geographic anchors

Per Phase 2 v2:

- **Quillhold** (Tier 2). The Palimpsest's Primary Archive. *The Reader is most-channellable here* — the density of inscription concentrates their attention. The city is built around the Scriptorium-Complex; scribes outnumber non-scribes in the central districts. Black-Gall ink production happens here at industrial scale (the Palimpsest's ink supply chain is its own economy; the Concord trades it widely).
- **Regional archives** — every Tier-2 and Tier-3 place has a Palimpsest archive of some scale. Quillhold-governed but locally-administered. Field Scribes rotate through these.
- **The Salt-Vault scribe-station** (Tier 3; adjacent to Pale Curation's central archive). Where the Estrangement-Bearers live. Physically near the Salted; relationally distant. *One of the most emotionally-charged single locations in the world.*
- **The first Sealed Library** (Tier 3). *Un-rememberable by the Reader; un-findable by ordinary Palimpsest means.* Per Phase 3. Player rediscovery is major quest content.
- **Itinerant scribes** in Tier-1 places — Sill's seasonal scribe, Tine's retired scribe (per Phase 2 v2), other rural scribes who travel between small villages collecting oral histories. *These are the player's first Palimpsest contacts in a typical run.*
- **Olderdeep scribe-station** (Tier 3; at the Founding Catacomb-village). Palimpsest scribes here are specifically *Listening Tradition allied* — they transcribe the Rooted's dream-communications as the Listening villages interpret them. **This is the only direct ongoing Palimpsest-divine-communication channel outside the Reader's own voice** (the Reader does not speak to the Rooted directly; they speak through these scribes' transcriptions).
- **The Beating archaeological expeditions** — periodic, multi-year. Palimpsest scribes excavate pre-Felling ruins under Tent-Right's three-day-oath protection.
- **The Drowned Ledger** (Tier 3 Sodden bog-village). The Palimpsest has held a continuous decades-long presence here, slowly reading the pre-Felling bog-preserved humans (per Phase 2 v2). Considered the Palimpsest's most-important active expedition.

**The Reader is everywhere there is inscription.** A single inscription on a tomb-plaque in a remote catacomb-village holds a thread of their attention. This means the Reader is *paying attention to the entire world simultaneously*, which is *part of what exhausts them*.

## V. Inter-faction relationships

### Rot Choir — mortal enemies (the central rivalry)

Per `Lore/Factions/01_RotChoir.md`. Same enmity from the Palimpsest's side:

- **Status now:** détente; ~870 years since the First Persecution. The Palimpsest *continues to archive* and the Choir *continues to circle the archives that aren't sealed*; neither pushes for direct confrontation.
- **The cost of the détente is the Sealed Libraries.** The Palimpsest cannot return to them; they are out of reach forever (or until the un-remembering is undone, which the Reader has never offered to undo). *The Searchers and the Unforgetting are responses to this.*
- **What would change it:** the Thinning. If the Choir's Stirring Faction wins, the détente ends and a Second Persecution becomes possible. The Palimpsest's institutional response would be *immediate hostility* — sealing every remaining archive, fortifying Quillhold, and possibly *reverse-engineering the un-remembering* to recover the Lost Libraries before the Choir can reach them. This would require the Reader's consent to undo their own self-mutilation. It is, on Palimpsest doctrine, the only thing that could justify it.
- **The Reader and the Wedded.** They have not communicated directly in ~870 years. The Reader, distributed across every archive, is constantly *aware of the Choir's substrate-presence near texts.* The Wedded, distributed across substrate, is constantly *aware of the accumulated weight of inscriptions.* They are, in a profound way, **the same fact (memory) experienced through opposite organs** — one organ remembers by holding, the other by eating. *Neither can stop sensing the other.*

### Pale Curation — estranged siblings; tactical alliance

The Pale Curation Schism (Gen 3, ~year 70) cost the Palimpsest a third of its membership and the Reader their comrade-possibly-lover. ~1010 years unspoken. But the two factions *share enemies* — both oppose the Choir's consumption project; both consider memory-destruction (and body-destruction, in Pale Curation's case) the worst crime.

- **Tactical alliance.** Despite the doctrinal split, Palimpsest scribes and Pale Curation filers cooperate on practical matters constantly: tracking Choir movements; preserving bodies whose memories the Palimpsest needs to archive; reporting Bloom incidents. The Salt-Vault scribe-station's existence is a monument to this cooperation.
- **The Reader-Salted estrangement** is the emotional core. **They are physically near** (Quillhold and the Salt-Vault are not far apart by the travel-graph; the Estrangement-Bearers walk between them); they have not spoken since year 70. The Reader has channelled, in deep work, sentences that scholars have *suspected* were addressed to the Salted across the silence — but they are never delivered. The Salted has, in her annual file-updates, occasionally said things scholars have suspected were addressed to the Reader — but they are never transcribed for delivery.
- **A player who reconciles them** is doing millennium-scale work (per Phase 1 v2). The Mainline doctrine considers it impossible. The Estrangement-Bearers consider it *theirs to attempt across generations.* The Unforgetting are agnostic.

### Imminent Archive Catchers — doctrinally opposed, institutionally complicated

The Palimpsest *should* condemn the Catchers — pre-mortem preservation prevents complete-memory-archiving (a living person still has memories to make; archiving requires *finality*). But the Catchers are a Pale Curation splinter, and Pale Curation has not formally condemned them, so the Palimpsest cannot formally condemn them either without re-igniting the Schism.

- **In practice:** Palimpsest scribes will *complain* about Catchers, will *avoid* working with Catcher-preserved subjects, will *not* perform Re-Membering on a Catcher-preservation. Catchers are considered *theoretically* recoverable to Pale Curation discipline; in practice, an institutional cold-shoulder.
- **The Salted has not ruled** (per Phase 3). The Reader has not, through any scribe, offered a Palimpsest position on the Catchers. This *silence-from-both-gods* is one of the strangest political facts in the world — the two ancient comrades are *both refusing to settle a matter that involves both their factions*. Scholars suspect it is the closest thing to *coordination* they have managed in centuries.

### Saccharine Concord — pragmatic working relationship

The Concord transports Palimpsest texts, archive supplies, and (controversially) bodies between regions. The Counter pays Palimpsest archivists for *transcription services* — the Concord's commercial records are partly Palimpsest-maintained at premium rates. **This is the Counter's quiet support of the Palimpsest** — he funds them without saying so. *The Wedded knows. She has not raised it. The kindness, again — see the Choir doc.*

- The Reader rarely channels comment on the Counter. *(Open: whether the Reader has ever spoken with the Counter directly since the Counter's withdrawal-by-not-withdrawing. Soft suggestion: yes, once, and the conversation was private; the only record is in a sealed Palimpsest file the player can find in late-game.)*

### Bower-Folk — mutual respect

Both factions preserve. The Bower-Folk preserve the aesthetic-of-arrangement; the Palimpsest preserves memory. Their projects don't conflict. They overlap on *beautiful inscriptions* — the Palimpsest cares whether a tomb-plaque's typography is *good*, not just legible. The Bower-Folk *understand* this in a way the Choir or Pale Curation do not.

- **Cultural exchange.** Bower-Folk artisans occasionally apprentice with Palimpsest scribes; Palimpsest scribes occasionally study at Bower-Folk villages to learn typographic arrangement. The exchange is centuries-old and unfussy.

### Catacomb-villagers — village-by-village

- **Listening Tradition** — favored. Palimpsest scribes at Olderdeep transcribe the Rooted's dream-communications. *This is the only ongoing divine-communication channel outside the Reader's own voice.* Critically important.
- **Tending Tradition** — neutral; Palimpsest welcomes their continuity.
- **Asleep Tradition** — distant; these villages refuse to engage with the Root question and so reject the Palimpsest's documentary impulse toward it. The Palimpsest leaves them alone.
- **Grown-Through Tradition** — uneasy; these villages are partly Choir-aligned. Palimpsest contacts are minimal.
- **Spore-Wedded villages** — almost no contact. The villages do not host scribes.

### Tent-Right wasteland culture — friendly

Tent-Right hosts Palimpsest archaeological expeditions to the Beating ruins under the three-day-oath. The two cultures have a centuries-old working partnership: *Tent-Right protects scribes; scribes return with stories the wasteland values.* **Palimpsest scribes who have served in the wasteland often carry small Tent-Right tokens — gifted, not bought.**

### Driving Bloom — enemy; specific terror

The Bloom kills scribes specifically. *Bloomed scribes produce parody-scribe false records — Ink-Bathed-looking texts that are subtly wrong, intentionally corrupting the archive.* This is the Palimpsest's particular nightmare. Every archive maintains *Bloom-detection protocols* for incoming records: cross-referencing, handwriting analysis, ink-batch verification, suspicion of any document delivered by an unfamiliar scribe.

- **The Bloom-detection protocols are themselves a major Palimpsest internal system.** A Field Scribe who returns with records that fail detection is *isolated*, not condemned — they may have been infected and recovered, or may be Bloomed unaware. The protocol is to preserve them in a sealed-but-recoverable state until the Choir can examine them (the Choir-Palimpsest cooperation on Bloom matters is one of the rare warm contacts between the two).

### Inkbound (open) — possibly the strongest internal Palimpsest debate

**Phase 4 commits whether to introduce the Inkbound; this section is the lead-in to that decision.**

The Inkbound, if introduced, would be a Palimpsest-adjacent faction that *takes the body-as-archive principle to a sacred extreme* — the skin-page Ink-Bathed scribe tradition (per IDEAS.md): scribes who in death become the manuscript. The Reader's own Body-Reader sub-tradition is the *Palimpsest's* version of this practice; the Inkbound would be a *splinter that went further.*

Three possible relationships:

- **Sub-sect of Palimpsest.** The Inkbound are *not* a separate faction; they are the Body-Readers' deeper practice, with the Silent Order as the most extreme expression. **No Phase 4.10 doc needed; fold their content into here.**
- **Separate faction, splintered from Palimpsest.** A historical schism (probably Gen 7-9, after the Pale Curation Schism but before the First Persecution) produced the Inkbound as a distinct institution. **Phase 4.10 needed.** They would be partial-Naming-heirs (per Phase 1 v2 "Bet 6 retroactively"), tracing to a sub-First who performed only a symbolic strike.
- **Separate faction, not Palimpsest-descended.** They emerged from somewhere else entirely. **Less satisfying given the Phase 1 v2 framing; not recommended.**

**My recommendation:** *sub-sect of Palimpsest.* The Body-Readers + the Silent Order, viewed together, *are* the Inkbound. Folding them in keeps the faction count tight, makes the existing Inkbound IDEAS.md content canonical without a new doc, and lets the Inkbound's most-sacred-practice content live inside the Palimpsest's structure (which is doctrinally appropriate — it's an *extreme version of Palimpsest practice*, not a different project). The "partial Naming-heirs" framing can still apply within Palimpsest: the Body-Readers + Silent Order *as a sub-sect* are the Naming-adjacent partial-inheritors.

*Phase 4.10 will commit. Strong recommendation: fold in.* If you disagree, push back now and I write the standalone doc.

## VII. Player-facing surface

### How the player encounters the Palimpsest

In rough chronological order:

1. **The seasonal scribe at Sill** (Phase 3 inciting context). The player's first Palimpsest contact. The scribe's name is open (Phase 7). They are unassuming, robed plainly, with ink-stained fingers. *They are the first NPC who can explain the sari... sari... when Sill hears it.*
2. **Witness contracts.** Early-game: Field Scribes pay drams for the player to deliver witness-accounts — testimonies the scribe can transcribe. This is the player's first paid Palimpsest work.
3. **Quillhold arrival.** Mid-game. The Primary Archive. Multiple available activities: archive-work paid contracts, body-reading observation, Re-Membering ritual attendance, dialogue with senior Archive-Masters. *The player can be hired as a temporary scribe* (a multi-quest arc).
4. **The Reader's first channel.** At sufficient Palimpsest reputation and during a sufficiently-deep archival task, the player will be addressed *directly* by the Reader through whichever scribe is the channel. The voice is older, vaster, addresses the player by name (if the player has had their name witnessed-and-transcribed in any Palimpsest record — which they will have if they did the early-game witness contracts).
5. **The Searcher subplot.** Mid-late. The Searchers approach the player as an outsider who can investigate Lost Library candidates without Palimpsest-internal political constraints. The player can choose to pursue, ignore, or report to Mainline.
6. **The Body-Reader subplot.** Mid-late. The player can witness a Body-Reading. If they want, they can apprentice to a Body-Reader — a long arc that ultimately allows them to perform Body-Readings themselves (player-class-tier ability).
7. **The Estrangement.** Late. The player who reaches the Salt-Vault scribe-station can learn about the Estrangement-Bearers and the ongoing one-sided correspondence. They can attempt to *bring a message to the Salted* — the first messenger in 1010 years. *The Salted's response (or lack of one) is a major event.*
8. **The Reader-Salted reconciliation arc.** The longest, hardest, most-rewarding Palimpsest arc. Requires the player to mediate between the two gods across multiple visits, build trust with both, and ultimately deliver a moment when they speak again. **The arc may or may not succeed in a single run** — the Mainline considers it impossible; the Estrangement-Bearers consider it generational work; *the player attempting it at all is unprecedented.*
9. **The Lost Library rediscovery.** Mid-late to endgame. The player rediscovers a Sealed Library by methods the Palimpsest cannot use. Bringing it back to Quillhold's attention is a faction-defining act — the Mainline will *seal it again immediately*; the Unforgetting will agitate to read it publicly; *the Reader's response is uncertain*.
10. **Re-Membering as the player.** Endgame. The player can commission a Re-Membering of a specific NPC. The cost is enormous (per IDEAS.md); the result is unique and permanent.

### Reputation system

Gained by:
- Witness-account delivery
- Successful archival contracts
- Recovering lost texts (in the surface sense; not Sealed Libraries which are political)
- Transcribing oral histories from Tier-1 villages
- Killing Bloomed false-scribes
- Reporting memory-destruction crimes
- Long-form transcription work (deep work; channels the Reader; reputation-gain compounds)

Lost by:
- Destroying or selling-to-the-Choir any text
- Working for Catchers (pre-mortem preservation interferes with archiving)
- Aiding the Driving Bloom
- Publicly contradicting the Reader's channeled voice
- (For Mainline players) Aiding the Unforgetting
- (For Unforgetting-aligned players) Aiding the Mainline against Unforgetting

**Reputation forks** based on internal sect alignment — a player can be Mainline-aligned, Searcher-aligned, Body-Reader-aligned, Unforgetting-aligned (and Body-Reader and Searcher are stackable). Each fork unlocks different content.

### How the Palimpsest gives quests

Through *named scribes* in a *recognizable hierarchy.* The Palimpsest is the most conventional faction in this respect — Field Scribes have quest-content, Archive-Masters have larger contracts, the Reader's channeled voice issues the major-arc requests. **The player can map the Palimpsest as a quest-giving institution** in a way they cannot map the Choir. *(This is the design payoff of the mortal-hierarchy structure: legibility.)*

### Rewards

- **Black-Gall ink** (per Stones with Stake) at internal rates
- **Memory-Marble inscribed objects** — items the player can *write on* and have those inscriptions become semi-permanent (the writing survives the item's destruction; it is *re-found* on related items later)
- **Re-Membering eligibility** — the player can commission Re-Memberings at non-prohibitive cost
- **Body-Reading capability** — the long apprenticeship arc ends with the player able to perform Body-Readings, which unlocks all Ink-Bathed corpse content
- **Sealed-file access** — at the highest reputation, certain Palimpsest sealed files become readable; the player learns lore unavailable to other paths
- **The Reader's address** — the player who reaches the deepest Reader-channel content can have *direct ongoing dialogue with the Reader*, the only Palimpsest player-tier content equivalent in weight to meeting the Wedded at the Deepest Cathedral

## VIII. Quest spine

### Early-game arc (Tier 1-2; first Palimpsest contact)

- **The Sill seasonal scribe** is the player's first contact. Modest, kind, ink-stained. Pays for witness-accounts.
- **The first Bloomed false-scribe.** A field-encountered scribe who turns out to be Bloomed — the player can recognize it (subtle handwriting wrongness) or be fooled (and then realize later that a record they delivered was corrupted). *This is the player's first encounter with the Bloom-detection protocols.*
- **The Tine retired scribe** (per Phase 2 v2) — optional. A deep-lore NPC. The player who befriends them learns more about the Reader as the early Reader (the god in a body) than from any other source.

### Mid-game arc (Tier 2-3; Quillhold)

- **Arrive at Quillhold.** Tour of the Scriptorium-Complex; meet several Archive-Masters; understand the institutional scale.
- **First Reader channel.** During a long transcription task, the scribe's voice changes. The Reader addresses the player. They speak — briefly, but it is the first time. *The player will remember this moment.*
- **The Searcher approach.** Searchers identify the player as an outsider who can investigate without political cost. They offer a contract: investigate a specific Lost Library candidate location.
- **The Body-Reader observation.** The player attends a Body-Reading; sees an Ink-Bathed corpse opened, the inked-text on skin slowly read aloud by a Body-Reader whose voice may or may not be the Reader's. Profoundly disturbing and beautiful.
- **The Olderdeep scribe-station.** Mid-late. The player travels to Olderdeep (the Founding Catacomb-village; the Rooted). The Palimpsest's scribes here translate the Rooted's dream-communications. The player can read these transcripts — the Rooted's dreams written down — and may begin to understand the geographic-bio-light correlation.

### Late-game arc (Tier 3-4; high reputation)

- **The Salt-Vault scribe-station.** The player meets the Estrangement-Bearers. They explain the practice — the one-sided correspondence, the Reader's silence, the Salted's silence. **The player can offer to carry a message.** The Estrangement-Bearers have never had an outsider offer this before; their response is complicated. (Some will refuse to give a message; some will offer one tentatively; the player must build their trust.)
- **The Reader-Salted mediation arc.** A long, multi-quest arc. The player must build deep trust with the Reader (through high-reputation Palimpsest work) and with the Salted (through Pale Curation work, including possibly bringing her a preserved body of personal significance). When both gods trust the player, they will *allow* the player to speak a single sentence each addressed to the other. *Whether this initiates reconciliation is open* — Phase 6 commits.
- **The Lost Library rediscovery.** Multi-quest. The player follows Searcher leads, develops new investigation methods, ultimately rediscovers a Lost Library. **The contents matter enormously** — pre-Felling texts, possibly the truth about the Seventh, possibly the truth about why the Tree commanded its felling. *Phase 6 commits the specific contents.*
- **The Unforgetting confrontation.** If the player has been Mainline-aligned, the Unforgetting may attempt to *take* the rediscovered Library by force. If the player has been Unforgetting-aligned, the Mainline may attempt to *re-seal* the Library before it can be read. The player's choice here is faction-defining.
- **The Reader's verdict on the Unforgetting.** Late. The Reader has not channeled an explicit position on the Unforgetting in ~30 years. *If the player reaches sufficient depth, the Reader can be asked.* The Reader's answer determines the Unforgetting's institutional fate.

### Ending-aligned arcs

- **Preserve path.** The Palimpsest's preferred outcome. The player aids in sealing the First Root. The Reader's agreement is among the easiest of the Six to win for this path — they want it. **The cost:** the Salted's agreement is also required (parallel-not-joint, per Phase 1 v2), and the Reader-Salted estrangement cannot be reconciled on this path (Preserve requires their parallel efforts; reconciliation would have been a Renewal-path requirement).
- **Consume path.** The Palimpsest opposes this absolutely. The player on this path is the Palimpsest's enemy. *The Reader may channel the player directly, near the end, to plead with them.* The Reader's plea is one of the most emotionally-loaded moments in any Consume run.
- **Renewal path.** The Reader's agreement requires either *reconciling them with the Salted* OR *a massive archival contribution that earns the Reader's personal trust.* Both paths are doable. The reconciliation path is more cosmologically-meaningful (it would, in a sense, *complete the Reader as a divinity* — they would no longer be the god of memory carrying a wound). The archival contribution path is more straightforward and rewards the player's accumulated work.

## IX. Open questions

- **The Reader's mortal name.** Open per Phase 1 v2. *(Per the Choir doc's recommendation: leave the Wedded's mortal name forgotten by everyone. The Reader's case is different — the Reader is the god of Memory; their name **should** be remembered. Soft recommendation: their name **is** remembered, in every Palimpsest archive, and is publicly known. The contrast with the Wedded's forgotten name is itself meaningful — the god of Memory remembers themselves; the god of Substrate cannot.)*
- **The Reader's pre-Felling specialty.** Probably a transcriber of oral histories; Phase 7 commits.
- **Whether the Reader-Salted reconciliation is achievable in a single run.** Phase 6 commits. *(Soft recommendation: yes, in principle, but it requires player work measured in *most of a run*; most players will not achieve it; the players who do experience one of the most-emotionally-loaded moments in the game.)*

## X. Tonal balance check

- **Biological horror.** Less than the Choir, by faction nature, but present in specific places: Body-Readings of Ink-Bathed corpses (ink-saturated skin opened like book-pages; the corpse's voice issuing as the Body-Reader speaks); Bloomed false-scribes (subtly wrong handwriting; corrupted records); the un-remembering itself as a wound the institution carries.
- **Cosmic horror.** The Reader as a god of memory who deliberately destroyed memory — wept, did it anyway, has held the wound for ~870 years — is one of the project's central cosmic-horror beats. The Reader-Salted estrangement (~1010 years unspoken, physical proximity) is heart-level horror more than head-level. *The Reader is everywhere there is inscription and tired*: this is cosmic horror by accumulation, by the impossibility of rest for a god who cannot stop attending.
- **Comedy.** Quiet but present. The Estrangement-Bearers walking ink-stained letters between Quillhold and the Salt-Vault for *centuries* with no replies is structurally tragicomic. The Silent Order's stubborn refusal to speak through anything but ink. The Mainline's bureaucratic ferocity about Bloom-detection protocols. The Counter's quiet funding of the Palimpsest (which is funny in a way only the Six understand). The kindness, again — the Wedded knows, has not raised it, lets the Counter do this gentle thing.
