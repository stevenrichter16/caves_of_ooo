## I. Founding — the Rooted

The catacomb-villagers are the people of the **Sixth Strike** (the inheritance of *Roots*; per Phase 1 v2 / Spine §97-99). Their founder did not lead them so much as *become their ground.*

Per Phase 1 v2 (the committed Rooted pose):

- **Then.** The First-of-the-Catacomb-Villagers performed the Sixth Strike and **ascended into the Tree's connection to depth** — to underground, to roots, to what-lies-beneath.
- **Now.** The Rooted is **a humanoid laying back, holding himself up by his core, shins flush to the ground, arms wide as if to embrace the wall that separates his chamber from the Root.** He has held the pose ~1,080 years. A **fungal plume erupts from his torso** — and *that plume is the founding patch*, the first of all village hearths. The founding village (**Olderdeep**, per Phase 2 v2) is built around him.
- **His dreams.** The Rooted dreams, and **his dreams manifest as bio-light across every catacomb-village in the world.** The glow the villagers live by is, in the deepest theological reading, the Rooted dreaming. *(This is the user-committed image from Phase 1.)*
- **His disposition.** The most withdrawn of the Six (per Spine §301). He communicates rarely, through dreams that reach his villages and propagate slowly. **He is the only one of the Six who can speak with the Root directly** (per Spine §348) — which makes him the load-bearing NPC for the Renewal ending's central question.

The villager *theologies* about him are already authored — the Listening / Tending / Asleep / Grown-Through Traditions in `catacomb_village_design.md` §V are four different readings of exactly this god. **That section is the canonical statement of villager cosmology; this doc only supplies the fact those Traditions are reading.**

## III. What this doc adds (new relative to the design file)

Three things the design file predates and the Phase 1-3 cosmology now supplies:

### 1. The Rooted-link is now explicit

The design file's four Root-Traditions referred to "the First Root" somewhat abstractly. Phase 1 v2 commits the **specific** founder-god, his **pose**, and the **dreams-as-bio-light** mechanic. The retroactive readings:

- **The Listening Tradition** believes the Root can hear them — and is *correct that the Rooted can*, since the Rooted lies with his arms spread toward the Root-wall, listening, for over a millennium.
- **The Tending Tradition** believes the village is the Root's gardener — and the founding patch *is the Rooted's own torso-plume*, so tending the patch is, literally, tending the god.
- **The Asleep Tradition** (do not wake the Root) and the **Grown-Through Tradition** (the felling is complete) are now readable as two answers to the Renewal question the Rooted himself embodies.
- **The bio-light the villagers live by is the Rooted's dream** — which gives the Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy a cosmological floor it didn't have in the design file.

### 2. The Thinning reaches the villages

Per Phase 3, the Thinning (Gens 34-36) is the Root's holding-field weakening and Urqu doubling. **The catacomb-villagers are the world's best Urqu-defenders** (Ward-songs, the Sealed Dim, Sari-Snake ward-fluid; design file §V). The Thinning is therefore *felt first and most acutely underground.* Sealed Dims grow longer and more frequent; the dreams (bio-light) flicker in ways the Plaque-Tenders record as omen. **The villages are the early-warning system for the endgame** — a player attentive to villager omen-records learns the Thinning is accelerating before the surface factions admit it.

### 3. The villagers and the endings

- **Consume.** The Choir consumes the Root; the Rooted *fades* (per Phase 1 v2). The bio-light — his dream — would go out across every village at once. **The Spore-Wedded archetype welcomes this; every other village experiences it as the death of their god and their light.** This is among the most affecting Consume-path consequences.
- **Preserve.** The Root is sealed; the Rooted holds his pose, holds the field, *forever.* The villages continue as they are. The Asleep Tradition's ideal.
- **Renewal.** **The Rooted is the gatekeeper.** His agreement requires the player to convince him the Root will not be destroyed in waking (per Phase 1 v2 / Spine §348). Because he is the only one who can speak with the Root directly, **a player who has reached the Rooted in Olderdeep knows what the Root itself wants** — the single most load-bearing piece of information for the Renewal choice. Later canon settles the true practice-path: the Root wakes without fear only when the world no longer asks it to hold alone.

## IV. Cross-references into the rest of Phase 4

- **Rot Choir (`01`)** — the Spore-Wedded archetype is Choir-integrated; Choir tendrils growing into a non-Choir village is an emergency event (design file §VII "Feared"). The Choir's substrate-kindness reads very differently to a villager whose god *is* the substrate.
- **Palimpsest (`02`)** — the Wall-of-Names archetype hosts a Palimpsest sub-station; villager plaque-tending and Palimpsest re-membering are kindred crafts; Re-Membering hosting is a major village service (design file §VIII).
- **Pale Curation (`03`)** — village preservation traditions (Salt-Cure, Honey-Seal) intersect Pale Curation supply contracts; the unwanted-Catcher-cell quest hook (Patch-Bright archetype) is the village-side of the `09` faction.
- **Saccharine Concord (`04`)** — the Patch-Bright archetype is Concord-aligned; the Concord runs the longest surface-to-catacomb supply lines and the largest Glow-Quartz mine (per `04`, IDEAS.md:801). Light-economy trade is the villager-Concord spine.
- **Bower-Folk (`05`)** — they treasure the most beautiful patches and rare-spectrum strains; Bower-Folk installations and Glow-moth displays appear inside aesthetically-rich villages; the villager Cleaner and Bower-Folk Cleaner are kin crafts.
- **Imminent Archive (`09`)** — Catcher cells embedded in villages the village can no longer endorse but cannot evict (design file §IX, Patch-Bright; `09` Penitent-cell content).
- **Tent-Right (`07`)** — adjacent guest-protection logic; villager cave-quiet sign language overlaps Tent-Right surface-sign (design file §VI). Two different cultures, same hospitality instinct.

## V. Implementation and writing notes

The design file's open questions (§XI: number of named villages, world-map distribution, inter-village travel time, apprenticeship-as-player-class, dialect-as-mechanic, the Catcher-cell pattern, procedural cultural drift) all stand as implementation or writing tasks. **Additive notes:**

- **Can the player reach the Rooted, and what is that scene?** Yes as a committed late-game route; the exact authored encounter remains writing work. It is the Renewal-gate scene and potentially the most visually arresting god-encounter in the game.
- **Do villages know the bio-light is the Rooted's dream, or only the Listening Tradition?** Recommendation: only the Listening and Tending Traditions believe it literally; to most villagers it is metaphor they half-believe until the Thinning makes the light flicker and the metaphor starts looking like fact.
- **What do the Plaque-Tenders' omen-records show during the Thinning?** Concrete content hook: the villages as early-warning system.

## VI. Tonal balance check

- **Biological horror.** Already deep in the design file (sleeping in your own future tomb; the Honey-jar larder; un-preservation chambers). The Rooted-link adds *the god is a body with a fungus growing out of it, and you live in his glow.*
- **Cosmic horror.** The bio-light is a sleeping god's dream; if he fades, every village goes dark at once; the people who defend the world best against Urqu are the people closest to the god who is failing to hold the field. The Thinning is felt underground first.
- **Comedy.** Carried by the design file's customs (the bright-mouthed surface-dweller offending everyone by talking at normal volume; the dewstep greeting that horrifies first-time visitors; villages arguing for generations over whether cave-rats are pets or pests). The catacomb-villagers are the project's **deepest culture-comedy** anchor — comedy from a fully-realized society's customs, not from absurd events.
