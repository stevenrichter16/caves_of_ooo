# 03 — History: the thousand years between the Felling and the player

> **Authority:** Phase-history document. `Lore/10_Bible.md` is the current canon authority; this file is current only where it aligns with the Bible or is explicitly marked canonical/current.
>
> Phase 3 of the lore project. **Commits the post-Felling timeline** — pre-Felling civilization, the Felling's place in history, the Lost Persecution, faction-emergence dates, the major events across ~1080 years, and **the defining situation at the player's arrival.**
>
> Builds on Phase 1 (the Spine) and Phase 2 v2 (the place-network). History is told *as the factions remember it* — and the factions disagree, and some of them have deliberately forgotten. There is no neutral chronicle. The Palimpsest's archive is the closest thing to one, and the Palimpsest censored its own memory once (see §IV).

---

## The three creative bets, surfaced for redirect

**Bet 1 — Pre-Felling was the Tree's civilization, and it was *singular*.** There was one world-spanning civilization, grown *into and around* the living Tree. Not nations — *one people, one Tree, one continuity*, because the Tree's holding-power made everything coherent with everything. The Felling did not end "a civilization"; it ended *coherence itself.* The post-Felling factions are not successor-states; they are *the six broken pieces of the one thing.*

**Bet 2 — The Lost Persecution was the Choir's First Persecution, and the Palimpsest's defense was self-mutilation.** In Generation 5-6 the Wedded (god of Substrate) attempted to consume the world's written memory wholesale before it could harden into permanent record. The Reader (god of Memory) saved the most vital archives by sealing them in sinkholes — *and then deliberately un-remembered where.* A remembered location is a vulnerable location. **The Palimpsest censored its own memory to protect its memory.** This is why the Sealed Libraries' locations are lost *even to the Palimpsest itself*, and why the Reader and the Wedded are mortal enemies in concrete, not abstract, terms.

**Bet 3 — The player arrives at the Thinning: the generation the millennium-long stalemate breaks.** For ~800 years the post-Felling world was a frozen standoff — the Root sleeping, the factions entrenched, the Six gods withdrawn, the question of *what to do about the Root* deferred indefinitely. Over the last two generations the First Root's holding-field has measurably weakened. Urqu manifests more, and worse. The Hush-bleed spreads into places stable for centuries. **The Six gods are paying attention to the surface again for the first time in eight hundred years.** The player arrives at the hinge: the deferred question can no longer be deferred. *Why* the Thinning is happening is the central plot mystery (Phase 6 commits).

If any of these three bets don't match your reading, stop me here.

## I. Pre-Felling — the Tree's civilization

There is almost no reliable record. The Felling dispersed memory; ~1080 years have passed; the Palimpsest's oldest continuous archive begins *during* the cataclysm, not before it. What is known about pre-Felling comes from three thin sources:

1. **The Reader's own memory.** The Reader was a pre-Felling scholar; the Reader *remembers being there.* But the Reader is a thousand-year-distributed consciousness who has been holding the entire world's memory since; their recall of their own mortal life is *fragmentary and unreliable in a specifically tragic way* — they remember everyone else's memories better than their own.
2. **The Drowned Ledger's preserved.** The pre-Felling humans accidentally bog-preserved in the Sodden (per Phase 2 v2). Physically intact. *Cannot speak* (Bog-Taken; per The Preserved IDEAS entry, consciousness ambiguous). The Palimpsest has spent centuries trying to *read* them — body-reading, dream-leakage, Re-Membering attempts. Each yields fragments.
3. **The Beating's ruins.** Pre-Felling structures in the wasteland, not buried by the cataclysm flood (the canopy was thinnest there; less flood-mud). Archaeological, not testimonial. They show *what was built* but not *what it meant.*

### What the fragments agree on

- **One civilization, world-wide, Tree-integrated.** Cities grown into living wood. Light was leaf-light. Technology was *grown alongside* the Tree's slow consciousness — devices like the Felling-Counter (per IDEAS.md, the Antikythera-analog) were *cultivated*, not manufactured. The Unread Codex (per IDEAS.md, the Voynich-analog) is a pre-Felling document no post-Felling reader can decode *because its language assumed the Tree's Naming-function was active* — it is written in a grammar that requires the seventh inheritance to parse.
- **No war in the record.** This is the eeriest fragment. The pre-Felling fragments show no evidence of organized inter-human violence. *Because the Tree held everyone coherent with everyone*, conflict in the modern sense may not have been structurally possible. (Factions dispute this: the Choir says it means pre-Felling humans were *not free*; the Palimpsest says it means they were *not broken*; the player can find evidence for both.)
- **The Tree was *known* to be sentient and *was* spoken with.** Pre-Felling humans communed with the Tree. The dreams the Tree used to summon the Six were not unusual — the Tree dreamed *to everyone*, always. The Felling-summons was unusual only in *what it asked.*

### The deliberately-unknown

- **How long pre-Felling lasted.** Unknowable. The Tree's age is the world's age (Phase 1, open). Pre-Felling "history" may have been hundreds of thousands of years of changeless coherence, or may have had its own ages. *No faction claims to know; the Reader will not say, possibly cannot.*
- **Whether pre-Felling had any concept of the seventh-that-would-fail.** Open (Phase 6). If the seventh (Naro, or no-one) was a known pre-Felling figure, the Beating ruins or the Drowned Ledger may hold the answer.

## II. The Felling (Generation 0 — year 0)

Per Phase 1 (the Spine) — the full account is there. Placed in history here:

- **The Felling is the zero-point of all post-Felling reckoning.** Every faction dates its calendar from it. Generation counts run from the Felling. "Year 0" is the year of the act.
- **It happened at the Felling-Site** (Phase 2 v2 — Tier-5, at the Root of the World's surface base). The six bare-ground positions and the empty seventh slippage-point are still there, ~1080 years later.
- **The Six were ascended in the act** (Phase 1 v2, Bet 6). The first generation of every faction was *personally led by its founding god*, who was at that point a recently-transmuted, traumatized, partly-comprehending new divinity.

## III. The Cataclysm Years (Generations 1-2, years ~1-60)

### What happened

- **The flood.** The Tree's blood-flood rose slowly, unstoppably, for years. It crested around Generation 1's midpoint, then receded over decades. It drained from the future-Spread and future-Veins; it never fully drained from the future-Sodden (which became the bog country).
- **The light-death.** Leaf-light failed within the first year. The world went from the Tree's warm even illumination to raw sun. *The first generation's defining trauma was not the flood — it was the light.* Pre-Felling humans had never seen unfiltered sun. Mass blindness, mass migration toward shade, mass descent into the new sinkholes (where the leaf-light persisted as fungal bio-light). **The first catacomb-villagers were light-refugees**, following the only familiar light into the dark.
- **The Six found their lineages.** Each newly-ascended god gathered the people who would become their faction. This was *not* organized — it was six traumatized divinities and scattered survivors finding each other in cataclysm. The factions' founding myths romanticize this; the reality was desperate.
- **The sinkholes opened.** Where the Tree's roots had been densest, the earth collapsed. The sinkhole network (Phase 2 v2's catacomb-tunnel edges) dates from here.

### Faction foundings (committed dates)

| Faction | Founded | By | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Palimpsest** | Year ~1 | The Reader | Founded *first* and *fastest* — the Reader understood Memory immediately and began recording the cataclysm *as it happened.* The oldest continuous archive begins year 1. |
| **Catacomb-villagers** | Years ~2-15 | The Rooted | Not a single founding — many family-groups descended over the first decade-plus, following bio-light. The Rooted's own descent and rooting-into-the-patch happened ~year 8. |
| **Saccharine Concord** | Year ~5 | The Counter | The Counter recognized within five years that survival required trade across the breaking world. First inter-region caravan: year 5. |
| **Rot Choir** | Years ~1-40 | The Wedded | Slow. The Wedded allowed her own encasement (the first knowing encasement) ~year 3; the substrate-network took decades to become a coherent collective. The Choir "existed" from year 3 but was not *the Choir* until ~year 40. |
| **Bower-Folk** | Year ~20 | The Bower | Founded through grief, later than the others — beauty in a broken world took a generation to become a *practice* rather than a wound. First bower: ~year 20. |
| **Pale Curation** | Year ~45 | The Salted | *Did not exist yet.* The Salted was, in the Cataclysm Years, still with the Palimpsest — see §IV. Pale Curation's founding is a Schism-Century event. |

## IV. The Schism Centuries (Generations 3-9, years ~60-270)

### The Pale Curation Schism (Generation 3, ~year 70)

The Salted and the Reader were, in the Cataclysm Years, *together* — comrades, almost certainly lovers (the Palimpsest's archive is uncharacteristically vague here; the Reader, who controls the archive, has reasons). They led the Palimpsest jointly: the Reader holding Memory, the Salted holding Preservation, *working as one faction.*

The split was theological and it became personal. The argument, reduced: **the Reader held that memory is held through *record* — bodies are vessels, the vessel is unimportant. The Salted held that memory is fragile and record is fragile; *bodies endure*; preserve the body and you preserve the memory by extension; the vessel is the substrate of the memory.**

They could not agree. The Salted walked away in ~year 70 with those who agreed with her, and founded **Pale Curation.** She built the first preservation chamber — the future Salt-Vault (Phase 2 v2). She Salt-Cured herself centuries later, but the *vault* dates from year 70.

**The Reader and the Salted have not spoken since.** ~1010 years of estrangement. The Salt-Vault's Palimpsest scribe-station (Phase 2 v2 — they are physical neighbors) was established *deliberately by later Palimpsest scribes* who hoped proximity might force reconciliation. It never has. **This is CoO's deepest single character arc; a player who reconciles them is doing millennium-scale work** (per Phase 1 v2).

### The Lost Persecution — the Choir's First Persecution (Generations 5-6, years ~150-210)

**Bet 2, in full.**

By Generation 5 the Wedded's Choir was a coherent collective. The Wedded reached a conclusion the other gods found monstrous: **written memory, hardening into permanent record, was *trapping* the world's memory in dead form. Memory should be *living* — held in substrate, in the Choir, able to grow and change and be re-tasted. The Palimpsest's archives were not preservation; they were *embalming.*** The Wedded resolved to consume the archives — all of them — and free the memory into the living substrate.

This was the **First Persecution.** For roughly two generations, the Choir's substrate aggressively expanded toward every Palimpsest archive, consuming scribes, dissolving books, absorbing inscribed stone. It was not war in the conventional sense — the Choir does not *fight*, it *grows* — but it was an extinction-pressure on written memory.

The Reader's defense was twofold and the second part is the tragedy:

1. **Sealing.** The most vital archives were hidden in sinkholes the Choir's mycelium could not quickly reach — the **Sealed Libraries** (Phase 2 v2, Sealed-Library-archetype sinkholes). Tepuibone, Memory-Marble, and Choir-Iron (per Stones with Stake) were used to wall them; the Choir-Iron specifically because it is fungus-toxic.
2. **Self-mutilation.** A *remembered* location is a *vulnerable* location — the Choir could read the locations from any captured scribe's mind, from any inscription, from the Reader's own distributed consciousness. So the Reader **deliberately un-remembered where the Sealed Libraries were.** The Reader, the god of Memory, *cut the memory of the Sealed Libraries out of the world's memory* — including out of the Reader's own self. It is the only time the god of Memory has ever *destroyed* memory. The Reader did it weeping (the Palimpsest's account; the Reader will confirm it if a player reaches that conversation).

**Consequences that drive present gameplay:**

- The Sealed Libraries' locations are lost *to everyone, including the Palimpsest, including the Reader.* The Choir never reached them; the Palimpsest can never return to them. They are *out there*, intact, holding pre-Felling texts (including possibly truths about the Seventh — Phase 6) — and *no one knows where.* **Rediscovering Sealed Libraries is major quest content.** The Palimpsest cannot guide the player there; *the Palimpsest unmade its own ability to.*
- The Reader-Wedded enmity is now concrete: the Wedded tried to eat the world's memory; the Reader had to *mutilate the world's memory* to stop her. They have not communicated in ~870 years. Each continuously senses the other (the Wedded feels the archives' weight; the Reader studies the Choir through the Palimpsest). They are the oldest, deepest hatred among the Six.
- The First Persecution **established the détente.** After Generation 6 the Choir stopped the campaign — not from defeat (the Choir is not defeated, it is *patient*) but because the Wedded recognized that *forcing* it would only produce more sealing, more mutilation, less memory. The Choir resolved to *wait.* **The post-Felling stalemate begins here.** The Choir has been waiting ~870 years for the world to come to it. (This is why the Thinning matters — see §VIII — the waiting may be ending.)

## V. The Settling Centuries (Generations 10-20, years ~300-600)

The defining process: **the Six gods withdrew from public leadership and began their long transformations.**

For the first ~9 generations the founding gods personally led their factions. Mortals knew them, saw them, were governed by them. Beginning ~Generation 10, each god began the inward turn — *becoming their function* rather than *leading their faction.*

- The **Reader** dispersed fully into the archives (lost their last body ~Generation 12-15).
- The **Wedded** dissolved her last individual selfhood into the collective (~Generation 14).
- The **Salted** Salt-Cured herself (~Generation 16) — *the first willing pre-mortem-style preservation, which is also the theological seed the Imminent Archive Catchers would later misread.*
- The **Bower** Resin-Cast herself at the founding village (~Generation 13) and became the central installation.
- The **Counter** did *not* withdraw — he kept working, and *that refusal to withdraw is his transformation*: a god who would not stop, worn across centuries into the exhausted commercial divinity he now is.
- The **Rooted** rooted into the patch (~Generation 11) and began the long dreaming.

**The mortal consequence:** by Generation 20 the factions are run by *mortal institutions* — scribe-hierarchies, filer-bureaucracies, Choir substrate-elders, caravan-guilds, village councils — *in the gods' absence.* The gods became *legend within their own factions.* Most faction-members for the next ~500 years would never encounter their god and would half-believe the god had died or was a founding-myth. **This is why the player meeting one of the Six is a major event — to the factions themselves, it is nearly unprecedented.**

Other Settling-era events:

- **Catacomb-village traditions split** into the four cosmological doctrines (Listening / Tending / Asleep / Grown-Through, per `catacomb_village_design.md`) — Generation 14-18, as villages multiplied and isolated.
- **Tent-Right hospitality codes formalized** (~Generation 12) — the wasteland-survivors, never god-led (Tent-Right is not First-descended, per Phase 1), invented their own ethics; the three-day oath became binding law at Wellmeet's founding.
- **The Spread's local gods emerged** — in the gods' withdrawal, the recovered surface civilization (Phase 2 v2's Tier-1 places) generated *small, local, post-Felling deities* — river-gods, harvest-gods, household-gods — to fill the cosmological vacancy. These are *not* the Six and *not* Spirits; they are *folk-divinity*, possibly real, possibly not (the lore does not adjudicate; per the CoO ambiguity principle). Most Tier-1 villagers serve these and treat the Six as myth.

## VI. The Long Quiet (Generations 21-30, years ~600-900)

The most stable era. The détente held. The Six were gone-into-function. The factions ran as institutions. The world *recovered* — Tier-1 civilization (Phase 2 v2) is largely a product of the Long Quiet's stability.

Two events break the quiet:

- **The Great Manifestation (Generation 25, ~year 750).** The single worst Urqu event in recorded history. *(Per Phase 1 — Urqu manifestations are failed incarnations of the unborn seventh god; they scale with the world's accumulated incomplete-acts.)* By Generation 25, ~750 years of accumulated incompletion across the recovering world reached a threshold. The Great Manifestation was a near-incarnation that lasted a *season* and unmade an entire Tier-2-equivalent region (now a Hush-bled scar place — possibly **the Unsaying**, Phase 2 v2). Tens of thousands died or were un-named. *It is the reference-catastrophe; every faction's Urqu-doctrine dates its seriousness from the Great Manifestation.*
- **The Imminent Archive Catchers emerge (Generation 27, ~year 810).** A direct consequence of the Great Manifestation: so many died *un-preserved* that a Pale Curation splinter concluded preservation must happen *before* death (per IDEAS.md Imminent Archive). Mainline Pale Curation rejected it; the Catchers persisted; the Salted (Salt-Cured, conscious, consulted annually) has *never publicly ruled on the Catchers* — her silence is itself a Pale Curation political fault-line.

## VII. The Modern Centuries (Generations 31-35, years ~900-1080)

Recent history — the player's elders and great-elders lived this.

- **The Driving Bloom heresy (Generation 32, ~year 960).** A Choir substrate-elder argued that the Wedded's *patience* (waiting ~870 years since the First Persecution) was failure — the world would never come to the Choir; the Choir must *take* it; consumption-with-dissolution was too slow; *control* was necessary. The Wedded — the collective — rejected this absolutely (it is the *opposite* of dissolution; it is the Choir becoming what it consumes rather than freeing it). The elder broke away. The breakaway substrate-lineage became the **Driving Bloom** (per IDEAS.md). The Choir has been actively suppressing it for ~120 years — the **Choir-Bloom internal war**, invisible to most of the surface, fought in the substrate.
- **Catacomb-network integration (Generation 33-34).** Inter-village travel through the catacomb tunnels became routine for the first time; the Tongue-translation economy matured; the catacomb-villager network became *politically* coherent (envoys to Olderdeep, per Phase 2 v2).
- **Surface technological recovery (Generation 34-35).** Tier-1 civilization reached its current form. Concord trade-infrastructure reached its current reach (the caravan network, Tally as hub).

## VIII. The Thinning — the player's era (Generation 36, year ~1080)

**Bet 3, in full. This is the inciting situation of the entire game.**

For ~800 years the post-Felling world was a frozen standoff:
- The Root slept; its holding-field kept the world coherent-enough.
- The Choir waited (since the First Persecution).
- The Palimpsest archived and could not return to its Sealed Libraries.
- The Pale Curation preserved.
- The gods were gone-into-function, legend within their own factions.
- The deferred question — ***what do we do about the Root?*** — was deferred, indefinitely, by everyone.

**Over the last two generations, the First Root's holding-field has measurably weakened.** The symptoms, in order of how the player encounters them:

1. **Urqu manifests more, and worse.** Not yet another Great Manifestation, but the *frequency* has roughly doubled within living memory. The *sari... sari...* is heard in Tier-1 places that never heard it before. Slip (Phase 2 v2) is a *new* phenomenon — a Tier-1/2 town that began going-Hush within the last generation.
2. **The Hush-bleed spreads.** Places stable for centuries are developing Urqu-bleed. Routes that were safe forget themselves. The Last Counter outpost (Phase 2 v2) has been *pulled back twice* in two generations — the Concord's guaranteed-delivery line is retreating.
3. **The Six gods are paying attention to the surface again.** This is the part the factions find most alarming. For ~800 years the gods were gone-into-function and effectively unreachable. In the last generation: the Reader has begun *speaking unprompted* through scribes who were not doing deep archival work. The Rooted's dream-bio-light has been *agitated* — global patterns shifting weekly instead of over years (per Phase 1 v2 — his nightmares dim the world's catacomb-light). The Counter has started *refusing certain contracts* for the first time in centuries. **The gods do not withdraw from function unless something is changing. They are not withdrawing. Something is changing.**

**Why the Thinning is happening is the central plot mystery.** Phase 6 commits. Candidate causes, surfaced now:
- **The Root is dying of age** — as the Tree did. The same senility the Tree feared. If true, the Preserve ending is doomed (you cannot seal a dying thing into eternal stability) and the choice narrows to Consume or Renewal.
- **Accumulated incompletion reached critical mass** — ~1080 years of the world's incomplete-acts (per Phase 1, Urqu feeds on incompletion) have finally overwhelmed the Root's holding-field. If true, the player's *own* completion-ratio is not just a local lever but a *world-scale* one.
- **The Root is waking on its own** — the Thinning is the side-effect of the Root *stirring*. If true, the question is not "do we wake it" but "it is waking; what do we do as it does."
- **The Choir's patience is ending** — the Wedded, after ~870 years, has begun *reaching for the Root through the substrate*, and the Thinning is the Root flinching. If true, the Consume ending is already in progress and the player is racing it.

**The player arrives at the hinge.** The deferred question is now urgent for the first time in a millennium. Every faction is mobilizing. The Preserve ending is no longer "do nothing" — *the seal is failing; preserving the Root now is an active, difficult, possibly-doomed project.* The three endings have stakes because **the stalemate is breaking whether the player acts or not.** The player's role is to determine *how* it breaks.

## IX. The player's starting context (recommended)

- **Start at Sill** (Phase 2 v2, Tier-1 river village). The player begins in the *most-recovered* part of the world — the part where the Tree is a children's story and the river-god gets the offerings.
- **The inciting local event:** Sill hears the *sari... sari...* for the first time in its history. An elder who has never left the valley recognizes a sound from a story their great-grandparent told. *The Thinning has reached even here.* This is the player's first contact with the cosmological frame — not exposition, but a sound in the dark and an old person going pale.
- **The player's identity is open** (Phase 6 / Phase 7). What is committed: the player is *of the recovered world* — they begin not knowing the Six are real, not knowing the Felling was literal, treating the cosmology as myth — *and the game is partly the process of the myth becoming true around them.* The radial-narrative progression (Phase 2 v2, Strangeness Tiers) is *the player's disillusionment and re-enchantment*: the further they travel toward the Root, the more the children's story turns out to be the world.

## XI. Tonal balance check

- **Biological horror:** the First Persecution (the Choir *growing* toward the archives, dissolving scribes — extinction-by-fungus); the Salted Salt-Curing herself; the gods' transformations dated and made concrete.
- **Cosmic horror:** the Reader *un-remembering* — the god of Memory deliberately destroying memory, weeping, the only time it ever happened — is among the strongest single beats in the entire project. The Thinning (the stalemate breaking, the gods stirring after 800 years, *something is changing and even the gods are alarmed*) is dread at civilizational scale. The pre-Felling "no war in the record" fragment is quietly horrifying both ways you read it.
- **Comedy:** preserved structurally — the Concord's guaranteed-delivery line *retreating twice in two generations* is bleakly funny; the Salted's annual file-update ("Status: continuing") now has 1010 years of entries; faction-politics-as-bureaucracy persists through every era. The comedy is sharper because the history is heavy.
