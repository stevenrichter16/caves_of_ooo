# House Thresker of Low Vennen

*A worked example of the House Drama template, generated from scratch. Tests whether the schema accommodates a meaningfully different dramatic texture than House Ordren.*

## The House

House Thresker holds the seed-keeper's craft in Low Vennen, a valley settlement on the downstream edge of a broad agricultural basin. The basin was farmland before the cataclysm and is farmland again. The difference — the thing the Threshers know and their neighbors don't — is that the plants currently growing across the basin are overwrite-generation varieties, descended from whatever seed made it through the first chaotic decades. The original varieties, the ones that had been bred and crossed and selected across centuries of pre-cataclysm agricultural work, would have been lost if not for the Threshers.

The craft is not archival. It is *living*. A Thresker seed vault is not a sterile locker; it is a rotating planting system. Each old variety must be grown out every few years — the exact interval depends on the variety — or its viability drops. Some of the Thresker varieties require regrowth every second year. Some can sleep for a decade. The House's full rotation plan covers roughly four hundred varieties across a seven-year cycle, and keeping it running is a full-generation commitment that requires the active labor of at least three competent practitioners in any given planting season.

Threshers are trusted but held at a slight distance. The common understanding is that they do something half-metaphysical with plants and they are not entirely of this century. Individual Threshers develop, with training, what the House calls *the green sense* — an ability to assess seed viability, variety identity, and regrowth readiness by touch. Strong practitioners can distinguish varieties at a glance. The ability is taught, but only some members of the lineage develop it fully. It is the marker of a real Thresker.

## The Founding

The House descends from **Oril Thresker**, who is the FoundationalDead of this drama and is important enough to introduce separately. Oril was not the first Thresker — the lineage goes back eight generations — but she was the practitioner who reorganized the rotation system into its current form after a decade of near-collapse in her middle years. She was the House in living memory. She died fifteen years ago, age seventy-two, and the family has spent the fifteen years since laboring in her shadow.

The House's founding deed, as it appears in `{ancestor_deed}` slots, concerns Oril's ancestor Mava Thresker, who during the second generation after the cataclysm walked the basin alone for six years gathering any seed she could find from abandoned farmsteads, storing them in clay pots in her mother's root cellar. Roughly sixty varieties in the modern rotation descend directly from Mava's walk. This story is true and is told frequently. Oril is the figure who added discipline and scale. Mava is the figure who added the myth.

## The Six

### Oril Thresker *(deceased, d. fifteen years ago, age 72)*

Matriarch, restorer of the rotation system, the most respected seed-keeper of her century. Every Thresker now alive was trained by her directly or by someone she trained. Her reputation is total.

What the family does not know — what Oril never told any of them — is that in her early fifties, during the rotation system's near-collapse, she accepted help from a scholar-cult that had approached her with a proposal. In exchange for access to specific pre-cataclysm varieties for "study," they would provide her with resources, information, and a guarantee of protection for the House during a period when it was politically vulnerable. Oril told herself it was an acceptable trade. The varieties she gave them — seeds, not living plants — would not be missed because the House's own stocks were full. She stopped the arrangement after seven years, when she understood better what the scholars were using the seeds for. But she did not end it formally, and she did not tell her children, and she did not stop tending one particular seed they had returned to her altered. That seed is still in the House's vault.

She was not a villain. She was a practitioner who made one compromise that she could not make undone, and it is now the structural problem of the House.

### Halden Thresker *(deceased, d. twenty-four years ago, age 31)*

Oril's son. Gifted, serious, the obvious heir. Discovered his mother's ongoing tending of the altered seed when he was twenty-nine and confronted her privately. The argument was unresolved. Two years later he fell ill with what the region calls *the wasting* — a slow, unexplained decline that is a real disease in the basin — and died within a year.

The wasting is real. Halden may have simply caught it. The scholar-cult, however, possesses techniques that can induce a condition nearly indistinguishable from wasting, and Halden was actively asking the wrong questions in his final healthy year. Whether his illness was cause or coincidence is genuinely uncertain, and the uncertainty is what has allowed his survivors to live with the question.

Oril tended him through his death and never spoke of the arrangement again in his presence.

### Wenna Thresker *(living, age 52)*

Oril's daughter, Halden's younger sister, current head of the House. Competent, respected, not visibly broken in the way Ordren's Sotheb is broken. The green sense came to her strongly. The rotation runs under her hand. From outside the House she looks like a restoration of Oril.

What Wenna is, in fact, is the current maintainer of her mother's arrangement. She found out about it five years after Oril's death, while organizing the vault. The altered seed was still there. A scholar-cult courier arrived at the appropriate interval and was surprised to find Wenna in charge rather than a still-living Oril. Wenna — having spent the previous five years quietly suspecting that her brother's death had been too convenient, and having now been handed hard evidence that the arrangement her mother kept had continued past Halden's objections — chose, in that moment, to continue it.

Her reasoning was strategic. Ending the arrangement abruptly would provoke the cult to act against the House; continuing it bought time to understand what she was dealing with and to protect her nephew from following his father. She has been running that strategy for twenty-two years. In that time she has not told anyone, has not confronted the cult, has not investigated her brother's death further, and has not made a meaningful plan to end the arrangement. What began as tactics has calcified into a way of living. She is a woman who has convinced herself she is holding a line while actually maintaining a breach.

She is not Sotheb. She is lucid, functional, and wrong in a different way.

### Corv Thresker *(deceased, d. eight years ago, age 26)*

Halden's elder son. Drowned in a flooded rotation field during a freak storm. The death is genuinely accidental — this is important — and it is the one thing in the drama that has no hidden cause. The cult had nothing to do with it. The weather did it.

Wenna has always wondered. She has never known. She is the only person in a position to suspect cult involvement, and her suspicion has fed her rationalization: if the cult can reach the next generation, she cannot stop the arrangement. The accidental death has, through no one's intent, done more to enable her paralysis than any deliberate cult action could have.

### Tarn Thresker *(living, age 28)*

Halden's younger son, Corv's brother. Was twenty when Corv died. Has spent the years since quietly investigating the death, in the spare hours of a working practitioner's life. He does not suspect his aunt. He does not suspect the scholar-cult, whose existence he has never been taught to see. He suspects a different faction entirely — a Choir-collaborator movement active in the basin eight years ago, whose records he has been trying to access for years. He is wrong. He is the sort of wrong that could take a decade to unwind.

He has not inherited his father's designated varieties — a portfolio of roughly forty seed lines that should have transferred to him at age eighteen. Wenna has held them as "shared custodianship" for ten years. Tarn knows this is irregular. He has not pushed because he loves his aunt and because the House's politics are not his first concern. His first concern is his brother. His second is his niece. The inheritance dispute sits at a distant third, but it is visible to any outside observer and it is the reason his professional standing in the wider seed-keeper network is lower than his talent would otherwise warrant.

His green sense is strong. Not quite his grandmother's. Better than his aunt's.

### Sable Thresker *(living, age 11)*

Corv's daughter. Her mother — a Thresker by marriage, not blood — left the valley after Corv died and has not returned. Sable was raised by Wenna and Tarn jointly, more by Tarn in practice. Her green sense is already the strongest in the living House, possibly the strongest since Oril.

When Sable was four, Wenna — on an impulse she has never fully explained to herself — gave the child a single seed in a small clay pot and told her it was a special seed that needed to be watched over. Sable has watched over it for seven years. She plants it every three years, as the seed's internal cycle requires, in a small private plot behind the house. She harvests the daughter-seed and keeps the original method intact. She does not know the seed is altered. She does not know the seed is the physical record of her great-grandmother's compromise. She knows it is hers, and she takes the charge as seriously as an eleven-year-old can take anything.

She is the functional custodian of the Hidden Evidence. Every player path that touches the seed touches Sable.

## The Root Conflict

The adversary is a small, ideologically intense Palimpsest-adjacent faction calling themselves **the Redactors**. They are not mainstream Palimpsest scholars and are regarded with discomfort even by scholars who share their broad alignment. Their specific discovery, made roughly sixty years ago by their founder: certain pre-cataclysm materials possess what they call *signature density*, a metaphysical property that lets them serve as anchors for Palimpsest annotations. An annotation stabilized against a dense-signature substrate becomes dramatically more durable — it can survive being overwritten in turn. It becomes, in effect, permanent.

Living pre-cataclysm seed is the densest-signature material the Redactors have ever identified. Extraction, however, is destructive. The seed, once its signature is drawn off, is sterile at best and decomposed at worst. Each extraction produces a small number of stabilized annotations, which Redactor scholars then use for their own work — forged pedigrees, permanent marks of authorship on contested histories, the kind of Palimpsest Annotation the lore bible describes, made durable.

The **NamedAntagonist** is **Inrian, the Third Reader**, the Redactor who manages basin-region extractions and who inherited the Thresker arrangement from his own predecessor. He knew Oril briefly. He has corresponded with Wenna for twenty-two years. He is sixty-one, precise, personally uncorrupt, and fully committed to his faction's project. He considers the arrangement with House Thresker one of his lineage's significant achievements and would be genuinely sad — not angry, sad — if it ended. This emotional signature is deliberately different from an Ordren-style adversary: Inrian is not cruel. He is a true believer, and he treats Wenna with respect.

The **SilencedHelper** is **Lowen of the Quiet Mouth**, a former junior Redactor who broke with the faction four years ago after attempting to expose the Thresker arrangement internally. The Redactors did not kill them or vault them. They performed a Palimpsest rite that edited Lowen's voice out of the palimpsest specifically with respect to Redactor matters. Lowen can speak normally about anything else. The moment they try to name the faction, describe its work, or identify its members, their voice goes. They have been wandering the basin for three years trying to warn someone through gesture, symbol, writing — and writing degrades rapidly in their hand, too, fading from the page within days. They are alive, free, in no obvious distress, and completely unable to deliver the message they are carrying.

The **secret truth**: Oril Thresker entered into an extraction arrangement with the Redactors during a period of vulnerability; Wenna has continued it for twenty-two years out of rationalized caution; Halden discovered it and may or may not have been killed for discovering it; and the current Thresker House is, without knowing it unanimously, a supplier of substrate density for durable Palimpsest annotations across the basin.

The **disclosure stakes**: if this becomes common knowledge, the Thresker name is ruined in the seed-keeper network; the Redactors lose their most important source and will likely be absorbed or destroyed by more mainstream Palimpsest factions; and a specific set of durable annotations currently circulating in the region will be recognized as forged.

## Pressure Point 1: The Wasting

*Archetype: Wound. Temporal mode: Past. Dominant alignment: Rot Choir.*

The question: did Halden Thresker die of natural disease, or was he killed for what he discovered? The truth is unknowable through ordinary investigation because both causes produce indistinguishable symptoms. A Choir substrate reading on his remains can distinguish the two; so can a sufficiently advanced Palimpsest Echo Reading on objects he handled in his final year. No other approach can.

Focus NPCs: Halden (the LostDead), Wenna (DiminishedHead, who has been living on the ambiguity). The pressure point activates when the player learns about Halden's death through ordinary conversation — Tarn will mention his father unprompted within the first hours of acquaintance.

### Paths

**The Substrate Path (Choir, Investigate).** Retrieve any remaining physical trace of Halden — a bone fragment from the family cemetery, a lock of hair preserved in a memento — and bring it into Choir territory for reading. The Choir can distinguish annotation-sickness from natural wasting at tissue level. Fast and definitive. Costs: minor Trust with the House (disturbing the grave without explanation), Ideology 2 (requires alliance with the Choir). If the player has high Mycelial Communion, this can be done at the graveside without removal.

**The Forensic Path (Home, Investigate).** Work through the medical and geographic record of wasting cases in the basin across the year Halden died. Was there a cluster? An anomalous single case? The data exists in village record-keepers' ledgers but has never been cross-referenced. The work takes in-game weeks. Outcome is probabilistic, not certain: you leave with an informed judgment, not proof. Costs: Time 3.

**The Echo Path (Palimpsest, Investigate).** Handle objects Halden handled in his final healthy year — his tools, the argument-letter he drafted to his mother and never sent, the bedframe he died in. Echo Reading in sequence reconstructs his suspicion, his mother's denials, and his encounters with an unnamed scholar in the final months. Definitive on cause-of-death; deposits substantial Halden-memory into the player's self. Costs: SelfEdit 3.

**The Confrontation Path (Avenge).** Skip the investigation. Approach Inrian and demand an answer. Inrian will, after a brief assessment, tell the truth — he believes his arrangements should be legible to those who inquire seriously. He will confirm that Halden was treated. He will express regret. He will not apologize. The player now knows but has no proof that would hold outside the conversation, and Inrian now knows the player knows. Costs: Time 1, and a ticking clock begins on the rest of the drama.

**The Burial Path (Accept).** Tell Tarn that his father probably died of wasting, without further investigation. Help him perform the appropriate grief-rites for a parent lost to natural disease. This is a genuine option. Tarn does not need to be right about his father's death to live a meaningful life; he needs to grieve, and he has not been able to. Cost: LineagePurity 1 (the truth remains unrecognized within the House), Trust-gain with Tarn significant.

**The Edit Path (Palimpsest, trap).** Use Palimpsest Annotation to fabricate a clear natural-wasting history for Halden — medical testimony, observed symptoms, a village-record entry. Every Thresker who inspects the record will find it conclusive. The question goes quiet. Sable grows up without her father's death hanging over anyone. Costs: SelfEdit 1, LineagePurity 4. This path contributes to the Corrupted end-state.

## Pressure Point 2: The Silent Walker

*Archetype: Prevented Help. Temporal mode: Suspended. Dominant alignment: Palimpsest.*

Lowen has been trying to warn someone — anyone — in the basin for three years. They cannot say the word *Redactor*, cannot describe the arrangement, cannot name Inrian or Oril or Wenna. They can say *there is something in the seed vault that should not be*. But the moment any of their sentences verges on specificity, the voice leaves. They have been variously treated as mad, drunk, or cursed, and most people give them food and send them on.

Focus NPCs: Wenna (the DiminishedHead who would act if she heard the truth from a credible outside source), Lowen (the SilencedHelper). The pressure point activates through rumor — traveling merchants, other villagers, a reputation of a silent walker. Tarn has heard the rumors and ignored them.

### Paths

**The Restoration Path (Palimpsest, Retrieve).** Identify Lowen's condition as Redactor silencing. Restore their voice through a counter-annotation rite — possible for a Palimpsest-attuned player at mid-tier, requires seeking out a specific non-Redactor Palimpsest scholar who knows the counter-rite. Lowen can then speak openly and will travel to the House voluntarily to warn Wenna. This is the cleanest resolution and forces the most favorable cascade. Costs: Time 2, Trust with mainstream Palimpsest factions (good direction), Ideology 1.

**The Translation Path (Redirect).** Do not restore Lowen's voice; instead, work with them to develop a communication system that bypasses the silencing. Written symbols that don't fade if inscribed on the right materials. Gestural grammar. Work-arounds. The information gets through in fragments. Slower and lossier than restoration, but does not require Palimpsest alignment. Costs: Time 3.

**The Substitution Path (Substitute).** Another former Redactor does not exist. Lowen is the only defector in living memory. Therefore *construct* a substitute: find someone — ideally a village authority, a scholar, an outside witness — willing to carry the message once the player has verified it independently through Pressure Point 1. The House receives warning from a source other than Lowen. Lowen remains silent, remains wandering. Costs: Trust investment with whoever the substitute is.

**The Voice Path (Replace, SelfEdit).** Use Echo Reading on Lowen's silenced vocal cords directly, temporarily borrowing their voice into your own mouth for a period of minutes. You speak their warning to Wenna as if it were yours. You receive, as the cost, the full Redactor-silenced memory-load Lowen has been carrying — every detail they could not say, all at once. This is usable exactly once and cannot be repeated. Costs: SelfEdit 4. Contributes to the Corrupted end-state pool under certain combinations (if used *without* restoring Lowen afterward).

**The Abandon Path (Abandon).** Do nothing with the rumors. Lowen continues wandering. Eventually a wasting season or a winter will end them. The House is never warned from the outside, which means any resolution of the broader drama must come from inside — Pressure Point 1 or 3 doing the work alone. Not inherently wrong; it is the correct path for a player who has decided the Thresker family should reach its own answers. Cost: Time 0, a minor ambient reputation hit if Lowen's eventual death becomes attributed to the player's indifference.

## Pressure Point 3: The Withheld Inheritance

*Archetype: Succession Hinge. Temporal mode: Imminent. Dominant alignment: Home.*

Tarn should have inherited Halden's varieties ten years ago. He has not. Wenna has held them as "shared custodianship" for ten years past the traditional transition age. The other six seed-keeper Houses in the broader region have noticed. Tarn has not pushed. Sable is too young to ask. Wenna knows exactly why she has done what she has done, and she knows that she cannot keep doing it indefinitely. She is fifty-two. The House needs a middle-generation inheritor who can absorb the varieties before she is gone.

Focus NPCs: Wenna, Tarn, with Sable as ambient consideration. Activates when the player is present in the House for any extended period and begins to map the vault; within a handful of days of attentive observation any reasonably-attuned player will notice that Tarn's workstations are set up for thirty-year-old seed lines but the vault keys he holds cover only his own generational portfolio.

### Paths

**The Public Confrontation Path (Catalyze).** Raise the inheritance question in front of other members of the seed-keeper network — at a seasonal meeting, during a visit from another House, in front of a village council. Wenna cannot refuse without public explanation; she will either transfer the varieties or be forced to make her real reasoning legible. This path is aggressive and effective. Costs: Trust with Wenna (significant and lasting), but forces movement. If Wenna confesses the arrangement under the pressure, the drama's cascade begins in earnest.

**The Confidant Path (Counsel).** Speak with Wenna privately, as someone who has begun to understand what she is managing. The goal is not to force the transfer but to give her the first outside interlocutor she has had in twenty-two years. She will not crack immediately; she has defended this position alone for a long time and is practiced. Two or three well-structured conversations, spaced over in-game weeks, with the right Dialogue Beats available, can move her to confess to Tarn. The transfer then happens voluntarily. This is the strongest traditional resolution. Costs: Time 3, requires high Trust with Wenna at the start.

**The Split-Custody Path (Bypass, Home).** Propose a technical arrangement: Tarn takes nominal inheritance of the varieties while Wenna retains practical custody for a defined remaining period. This is legally and traditionally allowable. It satisfies the outside observers and restores Tarn's professional standing. It does not resolve the underlying problem — the varieties remain under Wenna's hand, and the Redactor arrangement continues. Costs: LineagePurity 2. A clever move that changes appearances without changing substance.

**The Leapfrog Path (Transform).** Transfer the varieties not to Tarn but, on paper, to Sable as designated future inheritor with Tarn as steward-regent until her majority. This is unprecedented and genuinely protective — the Redactors do not track children, and an eleven-year-old heir is not the sort of target Halden was. It also means the varieties must survive another decade under Wenna's hand before they truly transfer. The House bets on time. Costs: Time 7 (long-term), and requires Wenna's active cooperation, which requires some portion of the Counsel path first.

**The Emigration Path (Escape).** Persuade Tarn to leave Low Vennen and apprentice himself to one of the other six regional Houses. His varieties are formally ceded. The House's middle generation ends. When Wenna dies, Sable inherits too young. The rotation fails within two cycles. This is the Extinct-adjacent path and should be available because it is the correct choice for a player who has concluded that the Threshers cannot be saved and their members should be saved individually. Costs: Trust with Wenna, Trust with Sable (she will not understand). The varieties are distributed among the other Houses and the cultural knowledge partially survives.

**The Usurpation Path (Catalyze, hostile).** Help Tarn seize the inheritance through formal grievance proceedings — a regional-network tribunal, an appeal to senior seed-keepers. Wenna loses the custodianship not by confession but by judgment. She may not be excluded from the House but she is excluded from leadership. Tarn becomes head. The arrangement with the Redactors breaks abruptly, which was Wenna's original fear; the player must then manage the consequences through Pressure Point 1's escalation paths. Costs: Violence 1 (not literal; this is political violence within the seed-keeper network), Trust with Wenna (broken).

## Pressure Point 4: The Iron Pear

*Archetype: Hidden Evidence. Temporal mode: Latent. Dominant alignment: Variable.*

The altered seed has a name. Oril called it the Iron Pear, after a pre-cataclysm fruit variety whose genome it partly carried. It is not that variety. It is a variety Oril and the Redactors co-produced, which carries — encoded in the way Redactor annotations encode, metabolically legible to sufficiently-attuned observers — a stabilized record of Oril's own rationalization for the arrangement. Not a memory. A *justification*. Planted in the open and allowed to set seed, the Iron Pear would release a regional bleed-through: every adult in the basin, over weeks, would begin to feel a quiet intuition that arrangements like Oril's are the sort of compromise mature practitioners must sometimes make. A soft contamination of public ethical judgment, anchored in a genuine plant, spread through pollination.

Sable has been tending the Iron Pear for seven years in a small plot she considers her own. The plot is behind the house, partly screened by the greenhouse, roughly three paces square. Sable plants the seed every three years, harvests the daughter-seed, and stores it in a tin box that she keeps under her bed. She believes her great-grandmother chose her for the task. In a certain sense, Wenna did.

Focus NPCs: Sable (the actual custodian), Oril (originator of the seed's condition). Activation: a Palimpsest-attuned player will feel the anomaly on any walk through the property; a Choir-attuned player will notice the substrate tension around the small plot; a player without either may notice Sable's unusual privacy around her plot and investigate socially.

### Paths

**The Copy Path (Preserve).** Record the Iron Pear's annotations without disturbing the seed itself. Requires high Palimpsest alignment (Echo Reading on the seed in hand, extended session). The player now carries a private record of Oril's compromise. The chain of annual plantings continues. Sable is not disturbed. Costs: SelfEdit 3.

**The Sterilization Path (Destroy).** Take the seed, destroy it by fire or deep burial. The chain ends. Sable will notice within days. She may or may not confess the loss to Wenna; she may or may not recover her confidence in her gift. The annotations Oril stabilized over decades lose their anchor and slowly degrade — over years, the forged annotations circulating in the basin begin to feel softer, less certain, and eventually fade. Costs: Trust with Sable (severe), LineagePurity 1.

**The Scholarly Disclosure Path (Disclose).** Take the seed to a mainstream Palimpsest scholar and reveal what it is. The scholar will preserve and study it; the Redactor project's method becomes legible to Palimpsest scholarship at large; the Redactors lose their methodological secret within a year. Sable loses the seed but the knowledge survives. Costs: Trust with Sable (lesser), the Redactors will identify the player as the leak source within months.

**The Substrate Exchange Path (Exchange).** Offer the seed to the Rot Choir. The Choir will compost it. Oril's annotations are absorbed into substrate memory — still recoverable through Mycelial Communion but no longer annotations and no longer able to anchor Palimpsest durability. The forged annotations across the basin dissolve within weeks, much faster than the Sterilization Path produces. Sable feels the loss and, under certain circumstances, may be drawn toward Choir practice herself. Costs: Trust with Wenna (she will know what the Choir did and will recognize the signature), contributes to TransformedB if paired with other Choir-aligned paths.

**The Trade Path (Exchange, adversary).** Return the seed to the Redactors. Inrian will be grateful and will offer the player proportionate reward — a durable personal annotation of their choosing, equivalent to a permanent minor ability. The arrangement with the House is essentially completed on the player's side; Wenna's compromise is externalized to the player. Costs: Ideology 5. The Corrupted end-state's strongest single-path contributor.

**The Open Ground Path (Unlock).** Plant the Iron Pear in the open — in a field, in the village commons, anywhere it can set seed and pollinate freely. The annotations release into the regional bleed-through over the following weeks. Ethical judgment across the basin softens toward arrangements-like-Oril's. Wenna is vindicated by a landscape that agrees with her. Other seed-keeper Houses begin, quietly, to consider their own compromises. This is the largest-scale single action available in the drama and is explicitly world-modifying. Costs: SelfEdit 2 (the planting player feels Oril's justification most intimately), and the basin is permanently annotated thereafter. Contributes to Corrupted if performed by the player directly; can contribute to TransformedA if performed *after* Disclosure to mainstream Palimpsest scholars who oversee the release as a documented event.

## Crossovers

Eight edges, instantiating the canonical patterns from the template.

**Actor-Doubling.** `(Wasting, Entered) → (Silent Walker, UnlocksEntry)`. Any investigation of Halden's death surfaces the faction responsible, and any investigation of the Redactors surfaces Lowen as the visible casualty of their silencing work.

**Actor-Doubling, reverse.** `(Silent Walker, Entered) → (Wasting, ExposesTruth)`. If the player restores or translates Lowen first, the cause of Halden's death is named in Lowen's testimony without further investigation.

**Truth-Propagation.** `(Wasting, PartiallyResolved) → (Iron Pear, ExposesTruth)`. Once the player understands the Redactor method, the Iron Pear is legible for what it is; before then, it reads as a family seed with a slight metaphysical halo.

**Stabilization.** `(Silent Walker, Restoration Path Complete) → (Withheld Inheritance, ReducesCost)`. Wenna who has heard outside credible testimony is dramatically more movable than Wenna defending alone. Counsel-path conversations become tractable. Confrontation-path consequences soften.

**Amplification (multi-target).** `(Iron Pear, Open Ground Unlock) → (Wasting, ForcesResolution Edit) & (Withheld Inheritance, ForcesResolution Split-Custody)`. Planting the seed changes the ethical landscape enough that the drama's other pressure points resolve in their most compromised forms whether or not the player chose those paths directly. A soft corruption, landscape-wide.

**Decay.** `(Withheld Inheritance, Ignored) → (HouseDrama, ForcesResolution Extinct)`. If Tarn's inheritance is never addressed, Wenna dies before transferring, Sable is eleven and not yet the green-sense adult she will eventually become, and the rotation fails within two cycles. The House extinguishes.

**Antagonist-Reveal.** `(Wasting, Confrontation Path) → (Iron Pear, ClosesPath: Trade)`. Once Inrian has been confronted by the player, the Trade Path closes — Inrian will not accept the seed back from a party who has challenged him on the Wasting, because accepting would confirm what he told them. Pride as mechanical constraint.

**Mutual-Exclusion.** `(Wasting, Edit Path) XOR (Iron Pear, Disclose Path)`. The Edit Path suppresses the very evidence the Disclose Path would give to outside scholars. If Edit is taken first, Disclose's payoff collapses; if Disclose is taken first, Edit is no longer needed and its annotation-footprint would be detected by the scholars reviewing the seed. Only one is effectively available.

## Composite End-States

Five, as required.

**The Restored House.** Wasting investigated and truth-accepted by the family; Silent Walker restored or translated; Withheld Inheritance transferred via Confidant Path; Iron Pear sterilized or disclosed under Thresker witness. Wenna retires in her sixties with dignity; Tarn becomes head; Sable inherits in her adulthood; the arrangement is ended, acknowledged, grieved. Path signature centers on Home and mixed-cosmic-but-honest paths. Tag: *Redeemer of House Thresker*. Kin-oath available with Tarn.

**The Palimpsest-Adjacent House.** Iron Pear Disclosed to mainstream Palimpsest; Silent Walker Restored and installed in Low Vennen as a cultural adjunct; Wasting resolved via Echo Path; inheritance transferred via Confidant Path. The House opens its vault to scholarship. It becomes, within a generation, the regional center of legitimate seed-study. Its craft continues and is materially expanded. Tag: *Sponsor of the Second Thresker*. Access to a new scholar-network.

**The Choir Anomaly.** Iron Pear Exchanged to Choir; Wasting resolved via Substrate Path; Sable drawn toward Choir practice over years; at some point Tarn or Wenna accepts the mycelial node. The seed-keeper practice becomes a seed-and-substrate practice — living vaults expanded into Choir-symbiotic cultivation, the varieties grown in mycelially-integrated plots, each seed's record held in both biological and substrate form. Other seed-keeper Houses do not recognize it. It works. Tag: *Broke the Furrow*, permanent reputation consequence with the regional seed-keeper network.

**The Ended Lineage.** Tarn Escapes; or the Inheritance is Ignored past Wenna's death; or the Usurpation Path escalates into Redactor retaliation that kills Tarn. The Thresker name ends as a seed-keeper House within a generation. Sable, if she survives, may join another House as a rank-and-file practitioner rather than a lineage heir. Mava's walk ends at the end of its eighth generation. Tag: *Witness to the Last Thresker*.

**The Corrupted House.** Wasting resolved via Edit Path and Iron Pear planted in Open Ground; or Iron Pear traded back to Redactors; or Voice Path taken on Lowen without subsequent restoration, binding Lowen's silenced memory permanently into the player while Lowen themselves continues to wander. The House thrives on a quieted question. Wenna dies in her seventies, respected. Tarn inherits smoothly. The arrangement continues, or its ethical cost becomes locally unfeelable, or its only witness is in the player's head. Tag: *Editor of House Thresker*. Visible to sufficiently-attuned NPCs thereafter.

## Validation Check

Walking through the template's Step 8 validators against this drama:

*Every archetype present exactly once.* Yes — Wound (Wasting), Prevented Help (Silent Walker), Succession Hinge (Inheritance), Hidden Evidence (Iron Pear).

*Every end-state class present exactly once.* Yes.

*Corrupted reachable without completing other end-states' signatures.* Yes — the Trade Path alone on Iron Pear, or the Edit Path alone on Wasting, each contributes meaningfully to Corrupted without requiring path commitments elsewhere.

*Winnable by single-alignment player.* Checked for each alignment. A pure-Choir player can reach Restored via Substrate Path (wasting), Translation Path (walker), Confidant Path (inheritance), Exchange Path (seed) — producing in fact TransformedB rather than Restored, which is correct for a single-alignment commitment. A pure-Palimpsest player reaches TransformedA naturally. A pure-Home player reaches Restored via Forensic, Translation, Confidant, Sterilization. All three single-alignment paths produce coherent endings.

*Coverage rule per pressure point.* Each has Home-aligned path, Palimpsest path, Choir path, Ideology≥3 path, and Corrupted-contributing path. Checked against the path inventories above.

*No dead-ends.* Each pressure point has zero-cost entry triggers: Wasting through Tarn's unprompted mention, Silent Walker through ambient rumor, Inheritance through observation of vault organization, Iron Pear through physical proximity to the plot.

The schema holds.

## What This Validates About the Template

Differences from Ordren that the template absorbed without strain:

*FoundationalDead as compromised origin rather than pure loss.* Oril is not Naerra. Naerra is the standard the living head fails to match; Oril is the standard the living head is secretly continuing. Both fit the FoundationalDead role; the template does not constrain which.

*DiminishedHead as strategic-compromised rather than grief-fractured.* Wenna is not Sotheb. Sotheb cannot do the job his mother did; Wenna can do the job and is actively doing the wrong version of it. The role accommodates both.

*RisingInheritor multiplied across generations.* Tarn-and-Sable is a two-heir configuration with different ages and different relationships to the drama. The template allowed the RisingInheritor role to be bound to two NPCs with different dramatic weight, and the pressure points routed cleanly around both.

*Cosmic alignment inverted.* Ordren tilts toward Palimpsest because its adversary is Palimpsest-adversarial. Thresker tilts away from Palimpsest because its adversary *is* Palimpsest-adjacent, which pushes its sympathies toward the Choir. Same schema; opposite cosmic gravity. The dominant-alignment field per pressure point carried the asymmetry without additional machinery.

*Hidden Evidence as living artifact with child custodian.* A seed tended by an eleven-year-old is a structurally different object than a box of papers in an attic. The Hidden Evidence archetype described the object at sufficient abstraction to cover both. The Unlock path — planting in the open — produced worldbuilding-scale consequences rather than family-scale consequences, without any schema change.

*SilencedHelper as free-but-speechless rather than captured.* Lowen wandering the basin is a different kind of rescue operation than Ahreth held in a vault. Both instantiate SilencedHelper. The verbs (Retrieve, Substitute, Redirect, Replace, Abandon) covered both situations without reinterpretation.

*NamedAntagonist as true believer rather than functionary.* Inrian considers his work respectable and treats his counterparties with dignity. Dhelvar considered his work necessary and treated his counterparties as obstacles. Both fit the antagonist slot; the emotional texture is different without the schema caring.

One adjustment the process suggested to the template itself: the **FoundationalDead role should admit an explicit variant flag** for compromised-versus-idealized founders, because the drama's texture differs sharply between the two and generator logic downstream (Dialogue Beats referencing the founder, Memorial Acts on their shrine) probably needs to know which kind of reverence to produce. A small schema extension, not a structural revision.

The template survives the second instance. It can be treated as load-bearing for further House-drama authoring, with confidence that the shape will hold across culturally-distinct Houses, inverted cosmic alignments, and varied antagonist-and-helper textures.
