# Sarisariñama Drosera Design Notes — tepui carnivorous plants

> Companion to `sarisarinama_herpetofauna_findings.md` and `sarisarinama_bestiary_design.md`. Integrates the **Drosera** (sundew) carnivorous plant genus into the **Caves of Ooo** tepui-thread as flora content — habitat mapping, mechanical behaviour, attacks/skills, faction stances, and lore significance. Per the running **Cultural Sources & Credits** ledger principle, every borrow is documented; real species are cited.

---

## Why Drosera fit the tepui-thread

Sundews aren't a stretch — **they are real tepui flora.** The biological grounding is exact, not metaphorical:

1. **Drosera is genuinely native to South American tepuis.** Several species are endemic or near-endemic to tepui geology — most directly relevant: ***Drosera meristocaulis*** sits on the **Brazil-Venezuela border**, the same region as Sarisariñama itself. The other species the user identified (*D. magnifica*, *D. graomogolensis*, *D. spiralis*, *D. peruensis*, *D. graminifolia*) are South American highland species sharing the tepui-adjacent biogeography.
2. **They thrive in nutrient-poor sandstone substrate.** Tepui summits are sandstone — *exactly* the soil chemistry Drosera evolved to exploit (insufficient nitrogen and phosphorus drives the carnivory). Drosera communities are *characteristic* of tepui summit ecology, not exotic to it.
3. **Their mucilage glistens.** The sticky droplets on Drosera tentacles catch and refract ambient light — and several species' mucilage exhibits **faint biological fluorescence under UV**. This is real biology, not embellishment, and slots directly into the **Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy** entry as a fourth light-category alongside fungi, beetles, and slime.
4. **They digest externally and slowly.** Tentacle-bending is one of the fastest plant motions known but still measured in *minutes to hours.* The waiting-and-then-slow-pull-in shape is **Apatheia-aligned** in mechanical terms — sundews are the patient predator made vegetative.
5. **They are "still being described."** *Drosera magnifica* was formally named in **2015** from Facebook photos taken on a Brazilian mountain. The "world isn't fully cataloged" thread is *still active* for Drosera in real botany — perfect Palimpsest / Inkbound material.

The species the user identified become CoO content with specific positions in the tepui-thread topology established by the existing IDEAS.md entries.

## Habitat mapping — where Drosera live in CoO

Per the elevation topology established in the bestiary file:

| CoO zone | Drosera presence | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Surface lowlands (400-600m) | **Sparse.** A few forest-shade species (*D. graomogolensis*-analog) tolerate shaded bank cells. | Lowland populations are commodity-tier; Concord trades them as ornamentals. |
| Foothill slopes (450-1100m) | **Moderate.** Cascade-margin and stream-bank populations exploit waterfall-spray nutrient-poor wet zones. | Forest-edge populations frequently companion Cascade-Father (rocket frog) habitat. |
| Inside Sima Mayor (1100-1375m) | **Specialized.** Light-filtered sinkhole zones with leaf-litter and bromeliad cells host *small pygmy-style* species (*D. meristocaulis*-analog). | The single most lore-rich Drosera habitat in CoO — see below. |
| Tepui summit (1300m+) | **Dense.** This is **prime Drosera country.** Summit bromeliad fields (*Brocchinia* per the bestiary file) host extensive sundew communities — every Brocchinia tank-cell typically has neighboring Drosera. | Summit Drosera fields are biome-defining. |
| Catacomb-village patch boundaries | **Cultivated.** Catacomb-villagers explicitly farm certain Drosera species at their fungal-patch edges as **Driving Bloom defense** (see below). | One of the load-bearing cultivation links. |
| Dead-zone edges | **Opportunistic.** Drosera colonize the boundary between live light-economy zones and dead zones, feeding on insect populations that flee dying patches. | Their presence at a dead-zone perimeter is itself an indicator that the dead zone is recently active (insects still fleeing). |

## Per-species entries

### *Drosera magnifica* — the Greatdew

**Real species.** Among the three largest sundew species in the world (~123cm tall in real specimens), endemic to southeastern Brazil. **Critically endangered.** Formally described in 2015 from Facebook photographs taken on Pico do Padre Ângelo — one of the most recently-discovered large carnivorous plants on Earth.

**CoO in-game name.** *Greatdew*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Tepui summit cells specifically. Rare; one or two adult specimens per summit zone. Always solitary or in small groups — the size that fully-mature Greatdews reach is a function of *age*, not population density.

**Mechanical behaviour.**
- **Multi-cell entity.** A mature Greatdew occupies a **3-cell vertical column** (root-cell, leaf-rosette-cell, flower-stalk-cell). Each cell behaves differently.
- **Rosette cell** — the dangerous one. Bumping it triggers full sticky-tentacle contact: player is **immobilized for 4 turns** while digestive enzymes deal slow damage (~5% max HP per turn). Strength check or specific items required to break free; **Apatheia affinity passes through without triggering** (the plant responds to motion + struggle, not still presence).
- **Flower-stalk cell** — harmless and *valuable*. Mature Greatdew flowers are large, pale pink, and **emit faint sweet attractant scent** that can be harvested as a ritual ingredient.
- **Root cell** — interactive only when uprooting (a multi-turn activity that destroys the plant).

**Attacks / skills.**
- **Tentacle-Embrace** (rosette cell): immobilize + DOT.
- **Sweet-Scent** (flower stalk): no damage; passive aura that draws insects and small fauna toward the cell, including in-game bait for combat encounters.
- **Patient Hunger** (passive): a Greatdew that has caught prey recently glows slightly brighter at night — visible from adjacent zones, recognizable to trackers.

**Lore significance.** **Real-world critically-endangered status carried into CoO.** Greatdews in CoO are **rare and protected** by the Palimpsest and Pale Curation specifically — both factions consider their loss irreversible. **Killing or uprooting a Greatdew incurs massive faction-reputation penalties across both factions.** The Concord (cynically) sells "Greatdew protection charms" to other adventurers, leveraging the rarity. The Bower-Folk consider mature Greatdews living masterpieces and may quest the player to *find* an unrecorded Greatdew without disturbing it.

**Cross-links:** **Palimpsest / Pale Curation** (protected status), **Bower-Folk** (aesthetic regard; quest-style "find but don't disturb"), **Saccharine Concord** (cynical commodification of the rarity), **Spirit of Apatheia** (patient-predator exemplar).

### *Drosera graomogolensis* — the Wine-Leaf Sundew

**Real species.** Brazilian (Minas Gerais), highland species, distinctive **wine-red leaves** that catch and reflect light vividly. One of the most popular ornamental sundews in real horticulture.

**CoO in-game name.** *Wine-Leaf Sundew*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Lower slopes of tepuis and some lowland forest-edge cells. Less specialized than summit species — the most likely Drosera the player encounters early.

**Mechanical behaviour.**
- **Single-cell entity.** Standard tentacle-bump immobilize for 2 turns + small DOT.
- **Color-shift indicator.** Wine-Leaf intensity correlates with recent feeding — deep saturated red means recently fed and dangerous; pale pink-tinged means hungry and slow to react. **Players who learn to read the color avoid the dangerous ones.**

**Attacks / skills.** Standard tentacle immobilize. The species' real interest is its color-as-information mechanic.

**Lore significance.** **Most common Drosera in commerce.** The Saccharine Concord runs **Wine-Leaf nurseries** — they're the gateway carnivorous-plant for surface gardeners and Bower-Folk apprentices. **Wine-Leaf cultivation is the entry tier of the Drosera economy.** The leaves themselves are aesthetic objects (the wine color stains glass-vial drinking cups beautifully) and are traded by the dozen. Catacomb-villagers also cultivate them as **decorative perimeter** at fungal patches, with the dual purpose of pest interception and visual display.

**Cross-links:** **Saccharine Concord** (entry-tier commerce species), **Bower-Folk** (aesthetic apprenticeship), **Catacomb-villagers** (decorative perimeter cultivation), **Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy** (their wine-red glows under specific bio-light spectra).

### *Drosera spiralis* — the Spiral-Bloom

**Real species.** Highland species with **distinctive long upright thread-like leaves** arranged in a spiral around a central stem. **Large pink flowers.** Distinct ecology — likes wetter, mossier microhabitats than other Drosera.

**CoO in-game name.** *Spiral-Bloom*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Wet summit cells, mossy crevices, cloud-forest cells in the upper slopes. Pairs with Cave-Singer frog habitat (per the bestiary file).

**Mechanical behaviour.**
- **Vertical multi-cell entity** (1-cell base, 3-cell tall thread cluster). The thread cluster occupies the player's *vertical space* — bumping any of the three cells triggers contact.
- **Slow-spiral attack**: when triggered, the threads twist around the player **over 3 turns** (visible animation). Each turn the player is more fully wrapped; escape difficulty increases per turn.
- **Pink flower at the apex** is a **harvestable ingredient** for love-potions / aesthetic dyes / Bower-Folk materials.

**Attacks / skills.**
- **Spiral-Wrap** (3-turn escalating immobilization, harder to break later).
- **Pink-Flower Attractant** (passive aura).

**Lore significance.** **Cloud-forest signature flora.** A summit zone's Spiral-Bloom population indicates the zone's moisture profile — Spiral-rich zones are damp, Spiral-poor zones are arid. The pink flowers are **prized by lovers and Bower-Folk equally** — they're considered romantic in surface culture.

**Cross-links:** **Cave-Singer** habitat (paired species), **Bower-Folk**, **Tank-Brocchinia** (often grow adjacent in the same wet summit cells).

### *Drosera peruensis* — the Hair-Stem Sundew

**Real species.** Peruvian species, recognizable by **specific hairy stem structures** beneath the leaf rosette. The hair fringe is part of the species' identification.

**CoO in-game name.** *Hair-Stem Sundew*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Western tepui slopes (the Peruvian-direction equivalent in CoO geography). Less common than the Brazilian-region species; gives the western-CoO biome distinctive flora.

**Mechanical behaviour.**
- **Standard tentacle immobilize** plus a **secondary stem-hair contact effect**: stepping near a Hair-Stem (one cell range from rosette) leaves player covered in tiny hooked hairs that **cause periodic slow movement** for 10 turns even after leaving the area. The plant doesn't immobilize you — it *marks* you, and the marks slow you down.
- **The hairs are also sticky** in a different way from the rosette mucilage — they catch *passively dropped items* (a player walking through who drops an item finds it stuck, requiring extraction).

**Attacks / skills.**
- **Stem-Hair Mark** (lingering slow effect).
- **Item-Catch** (drops near the plant get stuck).

**Lore significance.** **Western-tepui regional fauna marker.** A village whose surrounding terrain has Hair-Stem populations is geographically western — gives the world's west a distinctive flora signature. Surface explorers use Hair-Stem encounters to confirm they've crossed certain longitude thresholds.

**Cross-links:** geographic identifier flora.

### *Drosera meristocaulis* — the Pebble-Sundew

**Real species.** **Small, pygmy-style species, found on the Brazil-Venezuela border** — *the same region as Sarisariñama itself.* This makes it the **most directly Sarisariñama-relevant Drosera in the user's list.** Resembles the unrelated Australian pygmy sundew complex through convergent evolution; ground-level rosettes that look like dewy pebbles.

**CoO in-game name.** *Pebble-Sundew*. (Or, since it's so directly tied to Sarisariñama and the existing tepui-thread, alternatively *Sima-Pebble* — naming it for the Sima Mayor habitat where it would naturally fit.)

**Habitat in CoO.** **Inside Sima Mayor and equivalent sinkhole-floor zones** — the leaf-litter floor where Pricklebrow geckos live. Small enough to be *unnoticed* until stepped on. Forms dense ground colonies in light-filtered sinkhole zones.

**Mechanical behaviour.**
- **Floor-cell entity** that the player will not visually distinguish from leaf litter without effort. Walking through Pebble-Sundew cells is the **single most common Drosera encounter** because the species is so easily missed.
- **Density-mechanic.** A single Pebble-Sundew is a minor irritant (1-turn slow). A dense colony (3+ Pebble-Sundews in adjacent cells) **stacks** — walking across a colony incurs cumulative slow + DOT for each crossed cell.
- **Bioluminescent dew variant.** Some Pebble-Sundew colonies have particularly luminous mucilage. **Catacomb-villagers prize these for in-village bio-light** — a well-tended Pebble field can light a small chamber dimly.

**Attacks / skills.**
- **Cumulative Field-Slow** (stacks per cell crossed).
- **Dew-Light** (cultivated colonies as faint passive illumination — adds a 5th light-source category to the Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy).

**Lore significance.** **The Sarisariñama-region Drosera.** The most Sima-Mayor-canonical species in the user's list. Catacomb-villagers' relationship with Pebble-Sundews is intimate — many catacomb villages have a Pebble-Sundew chamber as part of their light infrastructure, and walking across a tended Pebble-field is a quasi-ritual act (the gentle "stickiness" is a rite of arrival, like a doormat that recognizes you).

**Cross-links:** **Catacomb-villagers** (cultivated for dim chamber light + ritual welcoming function), **Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy** (4th/5th light category — cultivated dew-light), **Sima Pricklebrow** (shared microhabitat — they live in the same sinkhole-floor cells; Pricklebrows feed on Drosera-trapped insects), **Sinkholes Drowned-Sima archetype** (Pebble-Sundews are part of what makes a Drowned-Sima distinctive vs. a Boneyard or Door archetype).

### *Drosera graminifolia* — the Grass-Sundew

**Real species.** **Grass-leaved species** related to *D. spiralis* — long thin leaves resembling grass blades. Visually camouflaged among actual grasses.

**CoO in-game name.** *Grass-Sundew*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Summit grasslands and savanna-edge cells. Hidden among real grass cells.

**Mechanical behaviour.**
- **Concealment mechanic.** Grass-Sundew cells are **visually identical to standard grass cells** at low Inquiry affinity. Players with high Inquiry affinity see the dew-glints and can avoid them; players without it may walk through entire fields without realizing.
- Standard immobilize on contact, but **the player doesn't know which grass cell triggered it** — adds a real exploration tension to grassland zones.

**Attacks / skills.** Concealment-as-attack. The fact that you don't know a Grass-Sundew is a Grass-Sundew is the threat.

**Lore significance.** **Grassland-zone hazard.** Tepui summit grasslands become non-trivial because of Grass-Sundews. A player who learns to recognize them (via repeated Inquiry-yielding encounters) gains progressively safer grassland traversal. Surface guides are paid for their ability to identify the patches.

**Cross-links:** **Spirit of Inquiry** (recognition skill yields with affinity).

## Cross-cutting mechanical hooks

Beyond per-species behaviour, several mechanics apply across the genus:

### Mucilage as harvestable resource

Every Drosera produces **mucilage** — sticky digestive enzyme droplets. Harvested mucilage becomes:
- **Trap-glue** for player-set traps (alternative to slime-paint from the Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy).
- **Adhesive crafting reagent** (combined with cordage for sticky bandages).
- **Edible** when neutralized (carnivorous-plant tea is a real surface-culture food in CoO; tastes faintly of trapped flies, which is its own narrative joke).

Per-species mucilage profiles vary: Greatdew mucilage is the most potent and rarest; Wine-Leaf is the most commodity-scaled (Concord nurseries produce volume); Pebble-Sundew mucilage is the most luminous (catacomb-villagers harvest it for dim-light cultivation).

### Drosera as Driving Bloom defense (load-bearing)

This is the single most lore-consequential integration with existing IDEAS.md content.

The **Driving Bloom** entry establishes that the parasitic-fungal Choir heretical breakaway hijacks individual minds and infests fungal patches. **Drosera intercept airborne Driving Bloom spores** — the sticky tentacles catch them in the air, the digestive enzymes neutralize them, the plants are unaffected. **A Drosera-cultivated perimeter around a fungal patch is the most reliable Driving Bloom defense in the world.**

This transforms Drosera from "interesting flora" to **strategic infrastructure** for catacomb-villagers:
- Light-economy patches are surrounded by cultivated Drosera rings.
- The Drosera require their own water and mineral inputs (which the village provides), and they kill insects that would otherwise damage the fungi.
- A village whose Drosera ring fails or is destroyed is *immediately vulnerable* to a Driving Bloom incursion.
- **Driving Bloom-aligned enemies actively attack Drosera rings** as a tactical preliminary — the villagers must defend the plants to defend their light.

This gives Drosera a load-bearing role in the catacomb world's defensive ecology.

### Spirit of Apatheia tie-in (load-bearing)

Drosera are **the patient predator made vegetative** — they wait, they wait, they wait, then they slowly digest. This is the Apatheia mechanical model in plant form.

- **High Apatheia affinity passes through Drosera fields without triggering** them. The plants respond to motion + struggle. A still-and-patient player walks through the field as if it weren't there.
- **High Bloodlust affinity escalates Drosera response.** Struggling triggers more tentacles; the plants "smell" rapid movement and respond aggressively.
- **A meditation-station mechanic.** Specific large Greatdews can host an Apatheia-aligned meditation activity — sitting still beside one for N turns yields Apatheia affinity ticks. The plant doesn't trigger if the player has *committed* to stillness.
- **Drosera-tending is itself an Apatheia practice.** Cultivating sundews requires patience (slow-growing, finicky, must not be moved). Surface gardeners who tend Drosera generations-deep are often Apatheia-aligned.

### Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy — a fourth/fifth light category

The Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy entry establishes three light-source archetypes: glowing fungi, lantern-beetles, luminous slime. **Drosera dew adds a fourth: cultivated dew-light.**

Properties:
- **Faint** — much dimmer than fungi or beetles.
- **Cool color** — the bluish-violet of UV-fluorescing mucilage, distinct from the warm yellows of fungi and the greens of slime.
- **Living** — the dew is produced as long as the plant is healthy and well-fed.
- **Insect-attracting** — the same dew that produces light *also* draws insects and lantern-beetle larvae, which is sometimes useful (food for the beetle colony) and sometimes a problem (lures Driving-Bloom-vector insects).

Dew-light is an **acquired taste** — most catacomb-villagers prefer the brighter fungi/beetles. Some elder villages keep dew-light chambers for **ritual purposes**: the dim cool light is associated with reflection, meditation, and tending the dead.

## Faction stances

Mirroring the established pattern across all major factions:

- **Rot Choir.** Surprised respect. Drosera achieve external digestion through botanical means — *kindred work* by a different evolutionary path. The Choir does not attack Drosera and may cultivate certain species in its cathedrals as **non-fungal allies** against the Driving Bloom (their shared enemy). This is one of the rare Choir-aligned ecologies that *does not* require eventual integration; the Choir and Drosera coexist as recognized peers.
- **Driving Bloom.** Hostile. Drosera intercept spores. The Bloom actively destroys cultivated rings.
- **Palimpsest.** Fascinated. *Drosera magnifica* was named in 2015 from photographs in real life — the Palimpsest treats Drosera as an active research frontier and pays for any newly-encountered species or unrecorded population. Their best botanical scribes specialize in the genus.
- **Pale Curation.** Concerned. Drosera digest organic matter, **including paper**. Pale Curation explicitly *excludes* Drosera from preservation chambers and considers wild Drosera populations near archives a structural threat. A Curation officer may quest the player to remove a Drosera ring encroaching on a sealed archive.
- **Saccharine Concord.** Markets the genus heavily. Wine-Leaf nurseries are mid-tier inventory; Greatdew "rarity charms" are high-tier curio; mucilage is bulk commodity. The Concord controls perhaps 60% of inter-village Drosera trade. They also sell **misinformation** about which species are which — players must be wary of mislabeled stock.
- **Inkbound (if introduced).** Particularly interested in **dew-ink** — Drosera mucilage neutralized and concentrated produces ink that **glows faintly when fresh**. Self-illuminating texts, written in dew-ink, can be read in true darkness for several hours after writing before the bioluminescence fades. The Inkbound's most precious manuscripts use this technique.
- **Bower-Folk.** Adore. The wine-red leaves, the spiral-thread foliage, the pink flowers, the glistening dew, the slow tentacle-curling toward a captured insect — every visual element of Drosera meets Bower-Folk aesthetic standards. **Bower-Folk villages frequently feature Drosera installations** as central aesthetic objects, displayed under specific bio-light spectra to maximize the dew-glint effect.
- **Catacomb-villagers.** Pragmatic + ritual. **Pebble-Sundew chambers** are common village infrastructure; **cultivated Drosera rings** protect light-economy patches from Driving Bloom; **dew-light chambers** serve ritual functions. A village's Drosera-tending tradition is one of its cultural-identity markers — tendings differ from village to village, and a visiting traveler can read the village's personality from the Drosera arrangement.
- **Tent-Right (wasteland) culture.** No Drosera (the genus needs moisture they don't have). The Tent-Right relationship with Drosera is mythological — they've heard stories of plants-that-eat-flies but most have never seen one. **A traveler arriving at a Tent-Right settlement carrying live Drosera** is a remarkable event, sometimes interpreted as sacred.
- **Spirit of Apatheia.** Aligned. Tending Drosera is an Apatheian practice. Patient passers-through pass safely. Meditation-stations near large Greatdews are real Apatheia-affinity yielders.
- **Spirit of Inquiry.** Aligned. Drosera reward observation — recognition of Grass-Sundew concealment, reading Wine-Leaf color-as-feeding-state, identifying Pebble-Sundew dew-glints. Inquiry-aligned players develop progressive Drosera-recognition over a run.
- **Spirit of Bloodlust.** Punished. Aggressive movement triggers stronger Drosera response. High-Bloodlust players literally cannot pass through Drosera fields safely.
- **Urqu.** Indifferent in normal periods. During Urqu's manifest periods, certain Drosera populations *crash* (Urqu's extinguishing influence affects them as it affects bio-light). A Drosera-rich zone going suddenly Drosera-poor is one of the more subtle Urqu-manifestation indicators.

### Sarisariñama Drosera (tepui carnivorous flora)

- **Biological sources (real species, biological-only — no cultural attribution required, standard real-species treatment):**
  - ***Drosera magnifica*** (Rivadavia & Gonella, 2015) — described from photographs taken at Pico do Padre Ângelo, Brazil. Critically endangered.
  - ***Drosera graomogolensis*** (T.Silva, 1997) — Minas Gerais, Brazil.
  - ***Drosera spiralis*** (T.R.Santos, 1986) — Brazilian highland.
  - ***Drosera peruensis*** (T.R.Santos, 1990s) — Peru.
  - ***Drosera meristocaulis*** (Maguire & Wurdack, 1957) — Brazil-Venezuela border (Cerro de la Neblina region; **directly Sarisariñama-adjacent**).
  - ***Drosera graminifolia*** (St.Hil., 1824) — Brazilian highland; close relative of *D. spiralis*.
- **Geographical source:** the **tepui** geology of the Guiana Highlands — already extensively credited in the existing Sinkholes / Root of the World / Sarisariñama Bestiary entries. Drosera communities are a *characteristic* tepui flora and slot directly into the established geography.
- **Cultural sources:** the species themselves don't carry specific cultural baggage I (the design author) am aware of. However, **Cerro de la Neblina** (where *D. meristocaulis* lives) is on the border of **Yanomami** territory, and the broader region overlaps with **Ye'kwana** territory already credited in the Sarisariñama Bestiary. Any in-game lore that draws on specific Indigenous knowledge, uses, or cosmology around tepui carnivorous plants must be researched in primary ethnography before authoring.
- **What was used:** the species themselves (cite by binomial in shipped credits); the carnivorous-tentacle-immobilize behavioural pattern; the slow-digestion patience trope; the bioluminescent mucilage characteristic; the "still being described" research-frontier framing (mirroring real *D. magnifica* 2015 description).
- **Stance:** **biological-only references.** Cite real species names in shipped credits per the Gin Frogs / *Stefania ginesi* treatment. The Wanadi-divine-association recommendation from the Bestiary file does NOT apply here — these species don't carry that level of sacred cultural weight in the literature I have access to. *However* — see Yanomami / Ye'kwana note above; specific cultural use of these plants must be researched before authoring lore that touches it.
- **Follow-up required:**
  1. Coordinate with the existing Sarisariñama Bestiary cultural-credit treatment — the same cultural region is the source.
  2. If specific Drosera-related ethnobotanical knowledge from Yanomami / Ye'kwana / Pemon traditions is referenced in design, that knowledge must be sourced from primary ethnography and credited at ship time.
  3. Real-world *D. magnifica* is **critically endangered** — the in-game lore should reflect this seriously rather than lifting the rarity casually. Conservation framing in shipped credits is appropriate.
