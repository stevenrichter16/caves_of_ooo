> Design-doc-tier deep dive on the **Catacomb-villagers** IDEAS.md entry. Develops inhabitants, non-villager fauna, behaviors, customs, architecture, cosmology, dialect, commerce, and inter-village variation in the depth needed to ship village content to the player.

## What this document inherits

The Catacomb-villagers IDEAS entry establishes:
- **Visual basis:** Falmer (Skyrim) — pale, near-eyeless, tall-stooped, long-fingered, long-eared, chitin-armored. **Behavior intentionally inverted** — passive toward the player by default, civilized, runs settlements not hunting grounds.
- **Apex-predator concern resolved:** dead-zone eyeless apex predators are a *separate species*; villagers often hunt them defensively.
- **Settlements centered on mature fungal patches** (the village's hearth — physically and politically).
- **Niches and tomb-chambers repurposed as homes** ("we sleep where our grandmothers' names are inscribed" — communion, not desecration).
- **Lantern-beetle colonies, slime-paint signage, Drosera rings, Spore-Block walls** — combined cultivation + architectural defense.
- **Trade with the surface:** they have what surface-dwellers don't (rare fungal strains, breeding stock, slime-paint, deep-cave craft, deep-cave knowledge); they want what they can't grow (textiles, metal, surface food, news).
- **The species is uniform; cultures are wildly varied.** Generations of isolated drift produce per-village dialect, cosmology, faction-relationship.

This document develops each of those threads in depth, plus several new ones.

# I. Population and demographics

## Village size distribution

| Tier | Population | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tiny** | 8-20 | Most common | Single fungal patch; one extended family; often a Sect-style breakaway from a larger village a few generations ago |
| **Small** | 20-60 | Common | Two or three patches; multiple family lines; the typical "stranded settlement" the player encounters in the Sinkholes entry's Drowned Sima archetype |
| **Mid** | 60-200 | Uncommon | Multiple patches, specialized roles emerge, internal politics begin to matter; usually located in a sinkhole large enough to host them |
| **Large** | 200-600 | Rare | Major catacomb-village hub; multiple sub-chambers, distinct quarters, internal markets; usually centered on an exceptionally productive Glow-Quartz vein or a foundational ancient fungal patch |
| **Hub** | 600+ | Very rare | Sinkhole-civilizations; several across the world map; player can spend major game-time in one |

Population density per cell is high — chambers carved deep into walls maximize use of limited horizontal floor. A village of 60 may occupy a single sinkhole-floor chamber and three wall-stories of niche-housing.

## Lifespan and physiology

- **Average lifespan:** comparable to or slightly longer than surface humans. Adapted-to-low-light vision and reduced UV exposure may slow some skin-aging processes; reduced muscle mass from confined living may shorten others. Net: ~70-90 years average for healthy individuals in stable villages.
- **Childhood:** children stay close to the village's central fungal patch (the hearth — both warmest and most-defended). They are pale even by adult standards; their eyes are *nearly white*, and pigmentation darkens slowly over the first decade as they're exposed to bio-light.
- **Adulthood:** marked by the **first plaque-inscription** (see Customs below) — the moment a young adult first inscribes their own name on a wall niche, claiming a future tomb-position. Typical age: 14-18 in different village traditions.
- **Eldership:** marked when an individual stops working at the fungal patch and begins *tending plaques full-time* — usually 50-65 in different traditions.
- **Death:** villagers die at home when possible — in their own niche, with their own plaque already inscribed. The death-watching tradition (sitting with the dying for hours or days) is near-universal.

## Genetic drift across villages

Centuries of isolation produce visible variation:
- Some villages are **taller, even by villager standards** (a founder effect)
- Some are **smaller and more compact** (adaptation to particularly cramped catacombs)
- **Eye morphology varies** by village — some have functional vestigial eyes (sensitive to specific bioluminescent wavelengths), some are fully eyeless and rely on echolocation-clicks (a real human-developed skill IRL — see *flash sonar*)
- **Skin pigmentation drift** — some villages are nearly-white-pale (the deepest catacombs), some are slate-grey, some have faint bluish tints from spore exposure
- **Hair** is often present but pale-to-white; some villages crop it short for hygiene, some grow it long as social marker

The variation is *bestiary-tier* — players can recognize which village an individual is from by a single glance, much as you'd recognize a dog breed.

# II. Social structure and roles

## Role specialization

Catacomb villages are **role-defined societies.** Each individual has a specialty learned through long apprenticeship. The roles are stable across most villages, with name variations.

| Role | Primary responsibility | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Patch-Tender** (also: *Light-Mother*, *Hearth-Keeper*) | Cultivates the central fungal patch; manages pruning, watering with bone-meal, Choir-spore inoculation if the village allows it | Highest practical status; the patch IS the village |
| **Beetle-Master** | Breeds lantern-beetle colonies; maintains beetle-jars; supplies mobile light to other villagers | High status; closely allied to Patch-Tender |
| **Plaque-Tender** (also: *Wall-Reader*, *Inscriber*) | Maintains tomb-plaques across generations — re-inscribes faded ones, adds new ones, knows the catacomb's full history | Highest *spiritual* status; plaque-tenders are usually elders |
| **Warden** | Hunts dead-zone apex predators; defends fungal patches from Driving Bloom incursions; guards village perimeter | High status; physically demanding role; warriors of the village |
| **Drosera-Keeper** | Cultivates the Drosera ring around fungal patches; harvests mucilage and digestive enzymes; tends Pebble-Sundew chambers | Mid status; specialized work; pairs with Beetle-Master in food chain (Drosera catches insects that beetles also need) |
| **Sigil-Stitcher** (also: *Slime-Painter*) | Mixes slime-paint, paints signage and ritual symbols; maintains glow-paint over generations | Mid-to-high status; aesthetic-and-ritual work |
| **Bone-Carver** | Carves new niches into walls; reshapes rooms; restores collapsed sections; works with both new bone (animal) and ancestral bone (controversial) | High specialized status; physically demanding |
| **Slime-Milker** | Maintains slime-pools; harvests luminous slime for paint, tools, dew-ink | Mid status; messy work; often tied to medicinal practice |
| **Surface-Walker** (also: *Sun-Bearer*, *Trader*) | The handful of villagers who can tolerate surface light without distress; conducts all surface trade | Variable status — high in trade-village cultures, low or feared in isolated cultures |
| **Death-Watcher** | Sits with the dying; performs end-of-life rituals; coordinates the village's funeral and plaque-inscription | High spiritual status; usually elder; often dual-roled with Plaque-Tender |
| **Honey-Keeper** (where applicable) | Maintains the village's honey-jar larder; the role is *ambiguous* in villages that practice Honey-Sealed preservation of kin (per The Preserved entry) | Variable status; in some villages, the most-respected; in others, secretly feared |
| **Tongue** | The village's translator — speaks both the village dialect and a "trade tongue" intelligible to surface-dwellers; usually a Surface-Walker's apprentice | Mid status; functionally essential for any trade |

## Authority and decision-making

Most catacomb-villages are **consensus-led**, not chief-led. Decisions affecting the patch (the village's hearth) require the agreement of the senior Patch-Tender, the senior Plaque-Tender, and at least one Warden. Smaller decisions are made by individual role-holders within their domain.

A few villages have **single elder authority** — a *First Elder* or *Patch-Mother* who has final say. These are usually small or beset villages where consensus is too slow.

A very few villages have **rotating leadership** — the Patch-Tender, Plaque-Tender, and Warden senior cycle through a "speaking" role on a multi-month cadence. These are the most stable and the most cautious villages; the player rarely gets a quick answer from them.

## Family structure and tomb-inheritance

Family lines are organized around **shared tomb-rows.** A grandparent, parent, and grown child may all sleep in adjacent niches on the same wall row — the family's *living and dead are interleaved by generation, not separated by death.* Most villages consider this the default and right way to arrange residence.

Children typically inherit the niche-position of a same-sex grandparent who has died; the connection is explicit and inscribed on the plaque ("Riane, daughter of Jas, granddaughter of Riane the Patch-Tender, holds her grandmother's place"). The repetition of names across generations is common; in some villages, *every individual carries a name borne previously by an ancestor*.

Marriage / pair-bonding: most villages practice formal pair-bonds, often arranged in adolescence and finalized in young adulthood. Bonded pairs typically share a niche-row and merge family lines for the inheritance purpose. A few villages practice more fluid arrangements (multi-person households, periodically-renewed bonds).

Inter-village marriage is **structurally rare** — but when it happens, it's important. The marrying-in spouse carries their birth-village's dialect and customs into the new village permanently. Some villages refuse all inter-village marriage; some welcome it as a way to refresh the gene pool. The player can sometimes broker an inter-village marriage as a major faction-quest event.

# III. Daily life, behaviors, customs

## The day cycle (invented from nothing)

Catacombs have no day or night. Villagers invent a cycle from the village's bio-light infrastructure:
- **"Bright"** (rough surface-equivalent: morning + midday) — the Patch-Tender increases water flow to the fungal patch; the patch glows brighter; villagers begin work
- **"Half-Bright"** (surface-equivalent: afternoon) — patch glow steady; main work continues
- **"Dim-Down"** (surface-equivalent: evening) — Patch-Tender reduces flow; patch dims; communal eating; storytelling
- **"Dark-Watch"** (surface-equivalent: night) — patch glow minimal; sleeping; one Warden walks the perimeter

The cycle is enforced by ritual rather than light alone — every household keeps a lantern-beetle jar that is opened/closed in sync with the patch.

A villager raised in this cycle, taken to the surface, experiences profound disorientation. The surface day-cycle is *too long, too bright, too irregular* by comparison.

## Greeting and dialogue customs

- **Stranger entering a village** is met first by a Warden — courteous, direct, asks the visitor's purpose. The Warden is the village's representative for first contact and may refuse entry.
- **Visitor accepted into the patch's light** crosses a threshold — usually a tended Pebble-Sundew field at the chamber entrance. The faint sticky welcome of the Drosera underfoot **is part of the formal greeting** — the village is *recognizing* the visitor by feel of their step. Surface-dwellers find this disturbing the first time; villagers find the surface custom of a handshake equally so.
- **Greeting between villagers** is silent — a slight bow at the neck, eye-contact (or near-eye-contact, for fully-eyeless villagers, a head-tilt that orients sense-organs). Silence-as-greeting is a deep cultural value.
- **Loud speech** is reserved for emergencies. Normal village speech is at near-whisper volume; the catacombs amplify sound and silence is a courtesy.
- **Direct contradiction** is rude. Villagers disagree by *adding context*: "another way to see this is..." A surface-dweller's blunt "no" is shocking.

## Eating customs

- **Food is communal.** A village eats together at the Dim-Down hour, in a chamber adjacent to the patch.
- **The patch itself is never eaten.** Mushrooms grown deliberately for food are a separate cultivation.
- **Lantern-beetle larvae** are a staple protein — bred specifically for food, grown to size in fattening jars, eaten roasted or paste-mashed.
- **Cave-fish** from stream-cells are common where streams exist.
- **Surface-foods** are *delicacies* — bread, cheese, fruit are objects of wonder. A traveler bringing fresh bread to a remote catacomb-village is making a major social offering.
- **Honey** is reserved — most villages have very little, and what they have is allocated for preservation use first, food use second. (The Honey-jar larder horror beat from The Preserved entry sits at the unsettling intersection of these two uses.)
- **Eating in silence** is the norm; speaking during eating is reserved for storytelling at meal's end.

## Sleep arrangements

- Villagers sleep in their own niches — *the same niches that will hold them after death.* The arrangement is not morbid in their cosmology; it's *home.*
- Niches are lined with mycelial-fiber textile (woven from cultivated fungal threads); some villages add slime-painted patterns inside the niche as personal-aesthetic.
- **Children too young for their own niche** sleep in a parent's niche or a shared "small-niche" alcove.
- **Dying individuals** are moved from any temporary location *back to their own niche* for the death-watch.

## Conflict resolution

- **Minor disputes** go to a village-elder for adjudication; the elder consults the relevant role-holders and decides.
- **Major disputes** go to **Three Voices** — three respected villagers (often Patch-Tender + Plaque-Tender + Warden) who hear the case and decide unanimously. A non-unanimous decision means *no decision* — the dispute is unresolved and the village must adapt around it.
- **Crime against the patch** (sabotage, theft of breeding-stock beetles, harming a Drosera-ring) is the most serious internal crime; punishment is exile or, very rarely, refusal-to-inscribe (the perpetrator's name is never added to a plaque, *ensuring they cannot be Re-Membered* — a uniquely catacomb-village punishment that has horror weight when a player learns of it).
- **Crime against an ancestor** (defacing a plaque, disturbing a niche) is ranked equal to crime against a living villager.
- **Surface-dweller crimes against the village** are typically resolved by *exile rather than violence* — the village drives the perpetrator out, refuses future trade, sends word to neighboring villages (slow but effective).

## Birth, growth, marriage, death rituals

| Stage | Ritual |
|---|---|
| **Birth** | The newborn is laid briefly on the patch's outermost mycelium *to be recognized by the village substrate.* The Patch-Tender pronounces the child accepted; if the child does not survive to acceptance, no plaque is inscribed |
| **Naming** | At a few days old, the child is named — typically taking a same-sex grandparent's name. The Plaque-Tender records the name; *the name is not yet inscribed on a tomb-plaque* but is recorded in the village's living-roll |
| **First Walk** | Around 18 months — the child walks unaided across a Pebble-Sundew threshold and is *recognized by the dew*. Mild ceremony |
| **First Light** | Around 5-6 — the child is given their own small lantern-beetle and a tiny jar; they tend the beetle alone for the first time. They are now considered a *light-bearer*, a step into responsibility |
| **First Plaque** | Adolescence — the young adult inscribes their own name on a wall-niche, claiming their future tomb-position. The act marks transition into adulthood. *They will sleep in this niche from now on* |
| **Bonding** | Pair-bond formalization in young adulthood; the bonded individuals merge plaque-rows |
| **Eldership** | When an individual transitions from active work to plaque-tending, the village marks the transition with a quiet meal and a re-inscription of their plaque (adding *Elder*) |
| **Death-watch** | Hours-to-days vigil at the dying individual's niche; family and Death-Watcher present; conversation, food, silence |
| **Inscription** | After death, the Plaque-Tender adds the death-date and any final words to the individual's plaque |
| **Repose** | Body may be left in the niche (most common), Salt-Cured (some villages), Honey-Sealed (some — the controversial larder tradition), Stone-buried (per the Stone Burial IDEAS entry — given to subterranean fauna), or in very rare cases enzyme-dissolved (controversial; usually reserved for criminals) |

# IV. Architecture and infrastructure

## Standard village chamber types

A typical mid-sized village occupies one main sinkhole chamber plus excavated wall-extensions. The chamber types:

1. **The Patch-Chamber.** Center of village. Holds the main fungal patch. The patch is *encircled by Drosera ring* (Driving-Bloom defense), *walled with Spore-Block* if the village can afford the mineral, *floored with Pebble-Sundew* in elder villages. Communal meals happen here. The patch is the village's hearth.
2. **The Wall-Niches.** Sleeping/dwelling/burial-position cells carved into the chamber's perimeter walls. Multi-story (vertical); accessed by carved stair-niches and rope-ladders.
3. **The Beetle-Hatchery.** A separate small chamber where lantern-beetle colonies are bred. Heavy with the Beetle-Master's work. Smells distinctive.
4. **The Slime-Pools.** Where the village gets its luminous slime — small natural pools or cultivated ones; tended by Slime-Milkers.
5. **The Plaque-Wall.** The chamber's main inscribed wall; reads from oldest plaque (deepest, often near floor or in the original founding-niche) to newest (top). A village's *literal history is the wall.*
6. **The Drosera-Greenhouse.** Where Drosera variants are cultivated for ring-replanting and enzyme-harvest. Carefully tended; one of the village's most important food-and-defense assets.
7. **The Threshold.** Pebble-Sundew floor that visitors cross on entry. *The village's first sense-organ.*
8. **The Common Stream-Cell** (where present). Stream-cells provide water and host catacomb fish; tended by the village.
9. **The Larder.** Food storage. Honey-jars (some labeled for food, some for preserved kin, in villages that practice Honey-Sealed kin-preservation — this is the chamber where the labeling distinction matters).
10. **The Outer Chambers.** Excavated sub-chambers for storage, beetle-stock overflow, visitor-quarters, *defense-positions* during Driving-Bloom incursions or Catcher attacks.

## Defensive architecture

- **Spore-Block walls** around the patch (per Stones with Stake)
- **Drosera ring** around the patch (per Drosera design file)
- **Choir-Iron lintels** at chamber entrances (Choir-resistant — per Stones with Stake)
- **Mute-Stone meditation chambers** in elder villages (Apatheian + Urqu-immune — per Stones with Stake)
- **Pebble-Sundew threshold** at all entries (welcoming + alerting)
- **Lantern-beetle alarm jars** at perimeter — when crushed, beetle pheromones alert the colony in distress, summoning Wardens

## Lighting infrastructure

The village's bio-light is **layered**:
- Primary: the central fungal patch (always-on, varying intensity per day-cycle)
- Secondary: **Glow-Quartz veins** in chamber walls (per Stones with Stake — the highest village aspiration; sustained passive lighting)
- Tertiary: Drosera dew-fields (cool blue-violet faint glow — per Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy)
- Mobile: lantern-beetle jars carried by individuals
- Decorative: slime-paint signage and ritual symbols (faint glow even after years)
- **Rare:** Inkbound dew-ink texts (where introduced) — self-illuminating manuscripts

# V. Cosmology and beliefs

## Their reading of the Root and the First Root

Most catacomb-villages believe they are **the Root's closest living neighbors.** The First Root sleeps; their patches are its dreams; their dead are its memory. Several distinct theological positions exist across villages:

- **The Listening Tradition** — believes the Root *can hear* the village, and that the deeper a village's roots go, the more clearly they're heard. Encourages deep-burial, plaque-deepening, mutter-prayer to the substrate.
- **The Tending Tradition** — believes the Root *requires their care* — that the village is the Root's *gardener*, that without villager cultivation the Root would fail. Encourages aggressive Drosera defense, ritual fungal-patch tending, hostility to Choir consumption.
- **The Asleep Tradition** — believes the Root is *asleep and should not be woken.* Quiet, conservative, refuses Choir integration, refuses surface contact. The most isolationist tradition.
- **The Grown-Through Tradition** — believes the Root has *already been grown through*, that the felling was complete and what remains is the new world's substrate. Most accepting of Choir integration; quietly Choir-aligned.

A village's tradition is one of its strongest cultural-identity markers; some traditions are openly hostile to others.

## Stance on Urqu

All villagers know the *sari... sari...* sound. **It is weather to them.** Their cultural responses are far more sophisticated than any surface faction's:
- **Ward-songs** sung at certain manifest periods
- **Recognition signs** carved at chamber entries (specific spore-mark patterns the surface has forgotten)
- **The Sealed Dim** ritual — when Urqu activity intensifies, the village dims its patch deliberately and waits in ritual quiet for the manifest period to pass. Surface adventurers caught in a village during a Sealed Dim must observe the silence or be exiled
- **Sari-Snake bounty** — wardens hunt Sari-Snakes during manifest periods; their venom is harvested for ward-fluid

## Stance on death

- **Death is continuation, not departure.** The dead remain in the village's walls. Their plaques are read regularly. A village without its dead is no village.
- **Re-Membering** (per the IDEAS entry) is regarded with deep ambivalence. Some villages welcome it (particularly Memory-Tender villages); others consider it desecration (the dead chose to be in the wall; bringing them back interrupts the choice). A village's stance on Re-Membering is one of its deepest theological commitments.
- **Stone Burial** (per the Stone Burial IDEAS entry — body given to subterranean fauna) is the *opposing tradition.* Some villages practice both; some practice one and consider the other monstrous.
- **Preservation** (per The Preserved IDEAS entry) is village-by-village; see the Catacomb-villagers IDEAS entry for the cultural-identity-marker discussion.

## Stance on the surface

- **Most villages:** the surface is *strange but real.* Surface-dwellers are people, just unusually adapted (to bright, hot, dry, irregular conditions). Trade is possible; understanding is hard.
- **Hostile-isolated villages:** the surface is *false* — too bright, too irregular, possibly a delusion. Surface-dwellers are not real people. These villages refuse all contact.
- **Trade-aligned villages:** the surface is *valuable* — source of textiles, metal, news. Trade is welcome and well-organized.
- **Apostate villages** (very rare) — the surface is *the future*; the village will eventually ascend and take its place there. Most other villages consider apostates fools or worse.

# VI. Language and dialects

## Language drift in isolation

Each village has a dialect of the broader catacomb-villager language. Drift varies by village age and contact frequency:
- **Recent breakaway villages** (<100 years old) speak a dialect easily understood by their parent village
- **Centuries-isolated villages** may be barely mutually intelligible with their cousins
- **Hub villages** speak a "trade dialect" — simpler, more standardized; used for inter-village commerce

A *Tongue* (translator) per role list above is essential for inter-village trade and surface contact.

## Specific vocabulary

A few examples of distinctive village-vocabulary that translates oddly:

| Village concept | Approximate surface translation | Comment |
|---|---|---|
| **the Tending** | "work", "labor" | But specifically *care for the patch and its dependents* — implies emotional commitment, not just labor |
| **first-warm** | "child" | Refers to the first time a child was warmed by the patch's light |
| **plaque-cold** | "anxious", "uneasy" | Literally: "the plaque feels cold" — the sense that something is wrong with the village's continuity |
| **bright-mouthed** | "loud", "rude" | A villager who speaks at surface-volume |
| **the Sealed Dim** | (untranslatable single word) | The Urqu-period silence ritual |
| **dewstep** | "welcome", "greeting" | The Pebble-Sundew threshold crossing |
| **un-inscribed** | "exiled criminal" | The worst social status |
| **patch-warmed** | "old friend", "trusted" | Someone who has eaten communal meals at this village's patch |
| **Wakeling** | "newborn" | The just-named child |
| **Closing** | "death-watch" | The hours of vigil |

## Cave-quiet sign language

Most villages develop a parallel sign-language for use in *quiet* contexts (Death-Watches, Sealed Dim, hunting expeditions). The signs are functional rather than poetic — yes/no, danger/safe, water/food/shelter — and most villagers know enough surface sign to communicate basics with Tent-Right travelers.

# VII. Non-villager fauna in catacomb settings

These are the creatures that share catacomb-village habitat — some kept, some hunted, some tolerated, some feared. Builds on the Sarisariñama bestiary content where applicable; introduces catacomb-specific species.

## Kept / domesticated

- **Lantern-beetles** (already canonical) — colony-bred for light + larval food. Each household keeps at least one jar.
- **Glow-moths** (new species) — small light-emitting moths kept in mesh-cages; favored for *decorative* light at niche-entries; less practical than beetles but prettier. Bower-Folk treasure them.
- **Cave-fish** — stream-cell food fish; tended where streams are present.
- **Pricklebrow geckos** (per Sarisariñama Bestiary) — communal-nesting perimeter alarms; their swarm-defense behavior makes them cheap living guards.
- **Slime-snail** (new species) — mollusks that produce luminous slime; cultivated in Slime-Pools by Slime-Milkers.
- **Cave-rat** (new species; or *Pebble-Eared Rat*) — small intelligent omnivores; some villages keep them as companions; others consider them pests; *the same village may have an internal disagreement*.
- **Bandfrogs** (per Sarisariñama Bestiary) — kept in glass enclosures by skilled poison-crafters; their secretions are weapon-coating material (and the Bower-Folk-aesthetic-display use noted in the Bestiary).

## Hunted

- **Eyeless apex predators** (per Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy dead-zone fauna) — the canonical villager prey when defending the patch. Wardens specialize in this hunt. Apex-predator pelts and chitin are valuable.
- **Sari-Snakes** (per Sarisariñama Bestiary) — hunted during Urqu manifest periods; venom harvested for ward-fluid.
- **Surface-fauna that wandered in** — occasional Helmwood Frogs (per Bestiary), other accidental fauna; eaten when found.
- **Driving Bloom-infected creatures** — must be killed before they spread; often feared more than apex predators.

## Tolerated / commensal

- **Sima-Stewards** (oilbirds, per Bestiary) — sacred. Their seeds founded the sinkhole. Protected by deep taboo in nearly all villages.
- **Glasspane Frogs** (per Bestiary) — too small to be food, too valuable as Bower-Folk-aligned commodity to harm casually. Tolerated in cool damp chamber-corners.
- **Various small invertebrates** — silverfish-analogs, springtails, small spiders that the villagers' ecology supports without specifically curating.

## Feared

- **Bog-Taken** (per The Preserved IDEAS entry) — bodies that surface from peat in bog-adjacent villages. Treated with caution; the question of whether they're conscious is not wholly resolved.
- **Choir tendrils growing into a non-Choir village** — emergency event; village mobilizes for defense (Choir-Iron + Spore-Block + Drosera + enzyme-spray).
- **Sky-Saris** (per Bestiary) — surface-only threat, but villagers tell stories. Never seen by most.

# VIII. Trade and commerce

## Items available from village merchants

Catacomb villages offer **distinctive trade goods** unavailable from surface villages. Pricing in drams (when trading to surface visitors) or in specific commodities (textiles, metal, food, news) when bartering. Most villages prefer barter; the Concord teaches them dram-pricing.

| Item | Description | Typical price tier | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lantern-beetle colony** (live, breeding stock) | A tended starter colony in a glass jar | Major trade-good | Unique to villages; surface buyers want them for catacomb expeditions |
| **Beetle-larva paste** (food) | Protein-dense, faintly sweet | Mid-tier commodity | Most villages export; preserves well |
| **Slime-paint** (various colors) | Bioluminescent paint in jars | Mid-tier | Per-color pricing; rare-spectrum colors are higher |
| **Bright-fungi seedlings** | Cuttings from the village's patch | Major | Each village's patch has unique strains; rarest strains command high prices |
| **Pebble-Sundew cultivars** | Seedling Drosera in tray | High | Per-cultivar; used by other villages for Drosera-ring expansion |
| **Drosera mucilage** (jars) | Sticky resin for crafting | Mid-tier | Used as trap-glue, adhesive |
| **Drosera enzymes** (vials) | Digestive fluid | High-tier | Multiple uses (per the Carnivorous-Plant Enzymes IDEAS entry); commodity value with major faction politics |
| **Dew-ink** (concentrated bioluminescent ink) | Self-illuminating writing material | High-tier | Inkbound buyers premium; surface scribes also pay |
| **Cave-fish** (dried) | Standard food | Low-mid commodity | |
| **Mycelial-fiber textile** | Soft pale woven cloth | Mid-tier | Comfortable; takes color poorly; village-aesthetic |
| **Bone-craft objects** | Carved tools, ornaments, ritual objects | Variable | NEVER from ancestral bone — sourced from animal kills or trade |
| **Catacomb maps** | Charts of the local network | High-tier | The village's own knowledge; usually only sold to trusted buyers |
| **Pre-treated parchment** (enzyme-prepared vellum) | Substrate for high-tier scribing | High-tier | Inkbound demand specifically |
| **Oral-history transcriptions** | Manuscripts of village dialect / cosmology | High-tier; sometimes refused | Some villages will not sell; it's their soul |
| **Ward-fluid** (Sari-Snake venom processed) | Anti-Urqu protective material | High-tier | Recognized by adventurers as essential for Urqu-period travel |
| **Memento mori** (carved name-tokens of ancestors) | Small ancestor-keepsakes | Variable | Highly culturally weighted; sale is sometimes scandalous |
| **Lichen-paste** | Medicinal antimicrobial topical | Mid-tier | Common in apothecary's stock |
| **Spore-Block fragments** (mined locally if villager-mined) | Mineral defense material | High-tier | Per Stones with Stake — Driving-Bloom defense |
| **Choir-Iron raw** (mined locally if villager-mined) | Mineral preservative | High-tier | Per Stones with Stake |
| **Glow-Quartz fragments** (mined locally) | Bio-light amplifier mineral | Very high-tier | Per Stones with Stake |
| **Mushroom brews / tinctures** | Mild psychoactive or medicinal | Mid-tier | Local-cultivar specific; village aesthetic |

## Services offered

| Service | Provider role | Faction-stake |
|---|---|---|
| **Tomb-plaque inscription** | Plaque-Tender | Highly skilled; a player who commissions an inscription gains permanent record (and potential Re-Membering material) |
| **Light-cultivation consulting** | Patch-Tender | Surface buyers paying for advice on starting a fungal patch |
| **Driving-Bloom defense advisory** | Warden | Crisis-response service; the village dispatches a warden |
| **Sima navigation guides** | Surface-Walker apprentice | Player hires a guide for a sinkhole expedition |
| **Translation** | Tongue | Inter-village or village-to-surface |
| **Honey-Sealing** | Honey-Keeper (where applicable) | Body preservation per The Preserved entry |
| **Salt-Curing** | Specialist; varies by village | Body preservation per The Preserved entry |
| **Bone-Carving** | Bone-Carver | Niche-creation, structural work, custom carved objects |
| **Drosera enzyme-spray treatment** | Drosera-Keeper | Anti-Driving-Bloom field treatment of an infected area |
| **Re-Membering hosting** | Plaque-Tender + Patch-Tender consensus | Major — village hosts a Palimpsest-led ritual on one of their dead; very rare and expensive |
| **Apprenticeship** | Any role-elder | A surface-dweller who wants to learn a village skill must be accepted; the apprenticeship is years long and changes the apprentice |

## What villages refuse to sell

Some things are *never sold* by most villages:
- **Ancestral bone** (an ancestor's actual bone is not commerce; villages that sell ancestor-bone have lost something culturally)
- **The patch's mother-strain** (the foundational fungal strain of the village; some villages will sell daughter-cuttings but never the mother)
- **Plaque-rubbings of named ancestors** (the dead's names are not commodity)
- **Tongue-services for inter-village hostile contact** (a Tongue who translates for an attacker is exiled forever)
- **Sima-Steward parts** (the seed-bringer-bird is sacred; even a feather is taboo)

## What villages buy from surface

| Surface good | Why villages want it |
|---|---|
| **Textiles** (cotton, wool, linen) | Cannot grow plant fibers underground; mycelial-fiber is functional but limited |
| **Metal** (iron, copper, steel) | No ore at depth; metalwork from surface is essential for most tools |
| **Surface-grown food** (grain, fruit, dairy) | Variety; delicacy; nutritional supplement |
| **News** | Information about the wider world |
| **Salt** (large quantities for preservation) | If the village isn't salt-flat-adjacent |
| **Honey** (ditto) | Preservation supply |
| **Books / texts** | Knowledge and material for Plaque-Tender study |
| **Maps of the surface** | For Surface-Walker training |

# IX. Five archetypal village examples

To make the design concrete: five sample village types, each a different combination of the above dimensions.

## 1. **Patch-Bright** — a Light-Trader village (Concord-aligned)

- **Population:** ~120 (mid-sized)
- **Specialty:** Lantern-beetle hatcheries, dew-ink production, surface trade
- **Tradition:** The Tending Tradition (Root requires care; village is its gardener)
- **Faction relationships:** Strongly Concord-aligned (regular caravans), neutral Pale Curation, suspicious of Choir, hostile to Driving Bloom, welcoming of Catchers historically (regretted, now ambivalent — the village hosts a *unwanted* Catcher cell that the village cannot evict without breaking the Pale Curation supply contract).
- **Distinctive: ** twelve breeding hatcheries; the village's lantern-beetle exports supply six other villages; their *Surface-Walkers* travel a regular three-village circuit and bring back textiles in return.
- **Player content:** Trade hub. Easy entry. Will sell almost anything for the right price. Quest hook: the unwanted Catcher cell is causing problems and the village wants the player to remove them without breaking the Pale Curation contract.

## 2. **Wall-of-Names** — a Memory-Tender village (Palimpsest-aligned)

- **Population:** ~80 (small-mid)
- **Specialty:** Plaque-tending; oldest continuously-tended catacomb plaques in the region; hosts a Palimpsest archive sub-station
- **Tradition:** The Listening Tradition (Root hears; deep burial matters)
- **Faction relationships:** Strongly Palimpsest-aligned, hostile to Choir consumption (would never permit encasement of a villager's body), tolerates Pale Curation, will host Re-Membering rituals on rare occasions.
- **Distinctive:** Their plaque-wall reads back 700+ years; the village has continuity records from the founding; visiting Palimpsest scribes spend years here.
- **Player content:** Knowledge-rich. The catacomb-reading mechanic from the Catacombs IDEAS entry is most rewarding here. Quest hook: a 600-year-old plaque has been damaged and the village needs the player to find a specific reference to restore the missing inscription.

## 3. **Spore-Wedded** — a Choir-aligned village

- **Population:** ~200 (mid-large)
- **Specialty:** Choir-aligned fungal cultivation; the village's patch is openly Choir-integrated
- **Tradition:** The Grown-Through Tradition (felling already happened; Choir is the new substrate)
- **Faction relationships:** Choir-aligned (deep), hostile to Pale Curation, hostile to mainstream Palimpsest, accepted Catcher cells, dismissive of Driving Bloom (they consider it heretic and would never tolerate it).
- **Distinctive:** The patch is *visibly* Choir-tendril-integrated; villagers tolerate spore exposure that would terrify outsiders; some elder villagers have accepted Memory Consumption + encasement willingly.
- **Player content:** Politically charged. Player who has high Choir reputation gains entry; player who's Choir-Touched is welcomed; player who has Choir-Iron equipment is treated with suspicion. Quest hook: the village wants the player to retrieve a Choir-significant relic from a hostile-isolated village's archives.

## 4. **The Quiet** — a hostile-isolated village

- **Population:** ~30 (small)
- **Specialty:** Self-sufficiency
- **Tradition:** The Asleep Tradition (Root sleeps; do not wake it)
- **Faction relationships:** Refuses all surface contact. Refuses inter-village trade beyond the immediate parent village. Hostile to Choir, hostile to Pale Curation, hostile to Concord, hostile to Catchers, hostile to *every* faction. The only outsiders accepted are Tongues from their own parent village.
- **Distinctive:** The village's dialect is *barely intelligible* even to other catacomb-villagers; their cosmology is the most strict; their plaque-wall has 400 names but is unreadable to outsiders without translation; their patch is small but ancient.
- **Player content:** Almost impossible early-game access. Major faction reputation work to enter. *The most rewarding catacomb-village content for players who invest the time.* Quest hook: they need an outsider for *something they cannot do themselves*, and that need is the only reason they've allowed contact.

## 5. **The Wardward** — a Warden-specialty village

- **Population:** ~150 (mid)
- **Specialty:** Dead-zone hunting; supplies wardens to other villages; chitin-armor production
- **Tradition:** The Tending Tradition (Root requires care; especially defense)
- **Faction relationships:** Neutral with most factions; deeply hostile to the Driving Bloom; respected by Pale Curation, Concord, Palimpsest as the *sword arm* of catacomb-villager society.
- **Distinctive:** The village's main chamber walls are decorated with mounted apex-predator skulls; the Warden role is the highest social status here; villagers are physically taller and stronger than typical (founder effect of selective in-village marriage favoring warden-bloodlines).
- **Player content:** Combat-rich. The village will train surface adventurers in dead-zone tactics; the player can hire wardens for expeditions; chitinous-armor unique to this village is a high-tier weapon and armor source. Quest hook: a particularly dangerous apex predator has emerged in a far-distant dead zone and the village wants the player to lead a hunt.

# X. Conflict and politics

## Internal village conflicts

- **Tradition conflicts** (Listening vs. Tending vs. Asleep vs. Grown-Through) — usually slow-burning over generations; rarely violent
- **Patch-management disputes** — should the village invite Choir integration to grow brighter strains? should we expand the Drosera ring at the cost of beetle-hatchery space? Often resolved by Three Voices (per the Conflict-resolution section above)
- **Inheritance disputes** — niche-row inheritance can become contested between cousins
- **Outsider-handling disputes** — village splits over how to treat a specific surface visitor

## Inter-village conflicts

- **Patch-strain piracy** — one village steals breeding cuttings from another. Major incident.
- **Inter-village marriage disputes** — a young adult who marries against the village's wishes
- **Shared-sinkhole conflicts** — where two villages share access to a sinkhole resource
- **Catacomb territorial conflicts** — overlapping cultivation zones

## Inter-village alliances

- **Trade pacts** — formalized; long-running
- **Defense pacts** — wardens trained in one village serve in others
- **Marriage alliances** — strategic inter-village bonds
- **Plaque-sharing** (rare) — two villages with shared founding ancestors maintain joint plaque-tending traditions

## Surface-faction politics

Each village has a **standing faction relationship** that the player can shift through diplomacy. The standing is a multi-axis state (Choir / Pale Curation / Concord / Palimpsest / Bower-Folk / Inkbound / Driving Bloom — and possibly Tent-Right, Imminent Archive Catchers as separate axes). Player actions in one village ripple outward through inter-village communication (slow but real).

## Apostasy

Rare but possible: an individual leaves their birth village to live on the surface or in another village permanently. **Apostasy is a deep wound** — the village considers itself diminished. The apostate's plaque is *not destroyed* (that would be a worse crime), but it is *not advanced* either; their name remains as it was at the moment of leaving, frozen.

A surface-dweller who marries into a village (rare but possible) is *adopted*; they receive a niche-position and their children may be *patch-warmed* villagers in their own right.

# XI. Open design questions

- **Number of named villages.** How many specific, distinct villages should ship in v1? Probably 8-12 (one of each archetype plus a few variants, so the player encounters real cultural variety without needing thousands of villages).
- **World-map distribution.** Are catacomb villages located procedurally per-sinkhole or hand-placed? Probably hybrid — handful of named hand-placed hub villages; smaller villages procedurally distributed in catacomb sub-zones.
- **Inter-village travel time.** How long does it take to cross the catacomb network between two villages? This affects all the "ripple" mechanics (player reputation propagating, news flowing, inter-village marriages). Probably variable by sinkhole-graph distance.
- **Apprenticeship as a player class option.** Should the player be able to *become* a catacomb-villager? (Major commitment; major faction-permanent-trait outcome — see the Catacomb-villagers IDEAS entry's "become one of them" open question.) Probably yes for late-game content.
- **Languages and translation as gameplay.** How heavily should dialect-as-barrier be modeled mechanically? Surface-village dialect → catacomb-village dialect → specific village dialects. Inquiry-affinity could partially translate. Tongues become genuine quest assets.
- **The Catcher cell question.** How widespread are unwanted Catcher cells in catacomb villages? The Patch-Bright archetype has one as a quest hook — should this be a recurring pattern? Probably yes, with several villages each having a different relationship with their resident Catcher cell.
- **Procedural cultural drift.** Should procedurally-generated villages have lightly-randomized cultural traits (founding tradition, faction-alignment, role-emphasis) so each instance feels distinct? Probably yes — randomized traits drawn from the systems above, with handcrafted villages getting deeper authoring.

# XIII. Cultural Sources & Credits — additions

When this content is implemented, the IDEAS.md Cultural Sources & Credits ledger should be extended with the following entries:

### Catacomb Village customs and structure

- **Cultural sources (general — multiple historical traditions for STRUCTURAL-INTUITION-ONLY borrowing; lighter-fraught):**
  - **Tibetan / Andean / Egyptian / Roman / European catacomb burial practices** (already credited generally under The Preserved and Catacombs entries) — provide the underlying structural intuition that "the dead and living share infrastructure." Per existing caveats: do not lift specific iconography or vocabulary.
  - **Toraja people of Sulawesi (ma'nene ritual)** — already credited under The Preserved. The Toraja's specific tradition of *living with the mummified dead* is structurally close to catacomb-village niche-and-niche residence patterns. **Strong recommendation: structural-only borrow; do not lift Toraja vocabulary or specific ritual imagery.** Toraja is a living tradition.
  - **Generic anthropological consensus-leadership models** (council systems, "Three Voices" governance, role-specialization in small-scale societies) — Standard ethnography; not culturally restricted.
  - **Cave-adapted human practices** — real cave-dwelling communities exist (e.g., Setenil de las Bodegas in Spain, various Tunisian troglodyte villages, Cappadocia underground cities in Turkey). These are *historical settlements* in caves rather than the catacomb-villager fictional concept. **Light borrow only** for architectural inspiration; named cultures (Cappadocian Christian communities, etc.) deserve credit if specific architectural patterns are referenced.
  - **Real flash sonar / human echolocation** — the practice of blind humans navigating by mouth-clicks. **Real living practice;** Daniel Kish is the most-known practitioner. The catacomb-villager use of clicks for navigation is structurally inspired here; **acknowledge in shipped credits as inspiration source.**
- **Stance:** **CoO-native naming throughout.** No catacomb village in CoO bears a real-world name; no specific real-tradition vocabulary is used in-game. Acknowledge inspirations in shipped credits as a unified note: "structural inspiration drawn from multiple real cave-dwelling, catacomb-burial, and council-led-society traditions; specific cultural traditions credited individually for any specific borrow."
- **Follow-up required:** if specific village customs get authored that closely echo a specific real culture's practice (e.g., a Toraja-style "living-with-the-mummified-kin" custom), that culture must be researched in primary ethnography and credited explicitly at ship time. Same caveat as the broader Preserved-system entries.
