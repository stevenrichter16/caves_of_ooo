*The consolidated worldbuilding of the Rot Choir / Palimpsest thematic axis,
synthesised from the canonical bible (`Palimpsest_Lore_Bible.md`) and twenty-two
lore audits run between 2026-04-30 and 2026-05-04.*

> **What this document is.** A single monolith assembling everything the audit
> cycle proposed and the bible originally established. The bible was unchanged
> across the run — these were *suggestion documents.* This file selects the
> strongest material and arranges it as if the bible had absorbed it all.
>
> **What this document is not.** A canonical commit to the bible. The
> bible remains source-of-truth; this is the editorial reading of what the
> audit corpus has *proposed* the bible should become. Where the audits
> contradict each other, this file picks the more load-bearing reading and
> notes the alternative.
>
> Organised by topic rather than chronology. The audit cycle ran on an
> 8-dimension schedule (mythic-historical stratification → marginalia); this
> file collapses the dimensional structure into faction-and-thematic sections
> so a reader can find *the Glassblown grammar* in one place rather than
> across nine audit files.

# PART I — THE CATACLYSM

## What it was

The cataclysm is the founding event of the entire world. It did not merely
destroy the old civilization — it overwrote reality itself, imperfectly, the
way text on a medieval manuscript was scraped off and written over with the
old words still faintly visible underneath. The old world is not gone. It is
buried beneath the present, bleeding through at the edges, pressing up against
the surface in places where the overwrite was thin.

Every faction in the game is defined by their relationship to this event.
Some try to preserve what was lost. Some try to consume it and build from the
remains. Some try to ignore it. Some are products of it. The two cosmic-scale
entities most directly embodying the cataclysm's aftermath are the Rot Choir
and the Palimpsest. The Choir represents what the world is becoming. The
Palimpsest represents what it was. Together, they form the central thematic
axis of the game: transformation versus preservation, growth versus memory,
the future versus the past.

> *"Founding event"* is Pale Curation phrasing. The Choir does not recognise
> founding events; it considers the present continuous with all prior soil.
> The Palimpsest does not recognise events at all, only readings of events.
> The Concord recognises founding events but reserves the term for the
> founding of the Concord. The bible uses *cataclysm* and *founding event*
> interchangeably as a Curation-leaning shorthand. A reader from another
> tradition should mentally substitute.

## Five named accounts of the cataclysm

Each name encodes a motivated worldview. Real stratification.

| Account | Faction | What it claims |
|---|---|---|
| The Severance | Pale Curation | A clean cut between *then* and *now*. Their job is to catalogue every retrievable fragment of *then* without contaminating it with *now*. |
| The Dissolution | Saccharine Concord | The old world's books were called and settled. The current world is post-balance. |
| The Burning | Glassblown Remnant | A firing the world did not survive intact. The Glassblown emerged from it shaped — slumped into glass-like surfaces, some bright, some opaque, some still warm. |
| The First Feeding | Rot Choir (surface) | The cracked old world poured its dying through the substrate, and the Choir woke. |
| The Temporal Unraveling | Palimpsest | A sentence was overwritten in real-time, and is *still being overwritten.* |

## The Choir's intra-faction schism

The Choir's surface tendrils tell the cataclysm as the *First Feeding* — the
cracked old world poured its dying through the substrate, and the Choir woke.
The deepest mycelium tells it differently, and only to those who reach the
lower roots. The deep voice says it was already thinking when the cracks
opened. It says the cataclysm did not wake the Choir; it only let the Choir
be *heard*. The surface tendrils consider this heretical and answer questions
about it the way a tongue answers questions about a stomach: politely, and
not very well.

The Choir does not number its memories. It distinguishes only *seasons* and
*the season we ate the loud thing.* The loud thing is the cataclysm — in
Choir grammar not an event but a *taste*: *iron, surprise, and the particular
sweetness of long-stored grain released from broken silos.* Asked to date the
season: *the soil still has the after-taste; it will be gone in another two
hundred breaths.*

The Choir registers no breaking-moment, no aftermath, no twenty-after-the-
after — only the slow integration of an unusually rich season. Asked whether
anyone *died*, after a long pause: *we are not sure that word means what you
think it means. Many things stopped moving on their own. We helped them keep
moving in our own way.*

## The Palimpsest cannot remember the cataclysm

The Palimpsest *is* the cataclysm — the imperfect-erasure it performed on the
world's substrate. To ask the Palimpsest *what happened* is to ask a page
about the hand that wrote on it. The page knows the pressure, the ink, the
angle of the nib; it does not know who came in. Echoes attuned to the
Palimpsest cannot date the cataclysm and cannot agree whether it was one
event or many. Some describe a *long writing*; some a *single stroke*; the
oldest describes only the *re-wetting of the page,* the moment before the
new ink set.

The Palimpsest's grammar for the cataclysm:

> *I was being written on. I am still being written on. I was already being
> written on.*

Three tenses, simultaneous. The Palimpsest does not regard them as
contradiction; it regards them as the only honest answer.

## The first hour — six accounts

No two factions agree on what the inciting moment *was*.

- **Choir.** A *taste* came through the soil. Spores tasted it and woke. The
  Choir treats its own awakening as the first event; outsiders find this
  self-centering conspicuous.
- **Palimpsest.** A sentence was overwritten in real-time. The Palimpsest
  claims to have *read* the overwrite as it happened and has *not finished
  reading.*
- **Curation.** A single sound, recorded in three pre-cataclysm logs whose
  pitches disagree by enough that they cannot all be of the same event. All
  three filed at Tier 1, none ratified. *Provisional pluralism* is policy.
- **Concord.** A *transaction*. Something was given; something was taken.
  Doctrine does not specify which side the world was on.
- **Brine.** A tide that did not turn. They were waiting for it, and will
  not say for how long.
- **Thermoclaves.** The tenth-fire of the chain they call *the cold one.*
  Nine fires before, none after, until they began counting from one.
- **Glassblown.** The silence around which all their grammar organises. The
  unsaid mood was the first thing said.

## The cataclysm's duration

Treated by the bible as a moment — a verb, *broke.* In fact each faction
holds a different shape for it:

- The **Choir** says it was *one feeding* and could not have lasted longer
  than a swallow.
- The **Pale Curation** has filed a working estimate of *seven to eleven
  days*, drawn from carbonised tree-rings in three Tier-1 vault samples; the
  estimate is unratified.
- The **Brine Communion** describes it as *one full deep-tide* (~seven
  years) of weather they cannot otherwise explain — a rolling, not a snap.
- The **Thermoclaves** teach that fire was *withheld* during the cataclysm,
  then returned; the duration is the period of the withholding, knowable
  only to the tenth fire.
- The **Saccharine Concord** has no opinion: nothing was traded during it.
- The **Palimpsest** reads the layering as *a hand still moving when the
  ink dried* — a partial, not-yet-complete overwrite. The cataclysm, in the
  Palimpsest's grammar, is *ongoing*.

The duration is the doctrine. A faction's answer to *how long did the
breaking take* tells the player what shape they think reality is: a swallow,
an estimate, a tide, a withholding, a transaction that did not occur, a hand
still in motion.

## How many cataclysms

The metaphor is layered text. The world was scraped, written, scraped,
written. Most factions agree there was *one cataclysm*, the recent one,
whose ruins are still warm. The Palimpsest does not. It reads, in places
where the layering is thinnest, prior overwrites — older hands, older
worlds, scraped at intervals the present cannot resolve. It will not say
how many. The Pale Curation has internally classified the hypothesis as
**Disputed (Period V)**, the highest grade of dispute the master-index
supports. The Choir says it has only ever tasted *this* soil; whatever was
beneath was tasted by something that is no longer the Choir, and not
continuous with it. The Thermoclaves refuse the question on the grounds
that the *next* burn is the one to think about.

## How the factions count time

The corpus's *four hundred years* is the Curation's working figure, from
the seal-date of their Tier-1 vault. The Curation counts in **catalogue
years.** The Brine count in **deep-tides** of ~seven years; they place the
cataclysm at the 51st. The Thermoclaves count in **unbroken tens** —
tenth-fire chains; their figure is *four chains ago,* each of indeterminate
length. The Concord counts in **trade-cycles** and reads the cataclysm as
23 past, contested by their own archivists. The Choir does not count. The
Palimpsest counts *backward from the next overwrite,* unusable for anyone
who does not share their sense that the next is imminent.

A Curation card dated *Year 412* and a Brine logbook dated *the 51st
deep-tide, third turn* may or may not refer to the same week. Each calendar
drifts in the way that flatters its founding doctrine.

## The world before — six rims around a hole

Six factions and five named voices have, between them, almost no agreement
on what the pre-cataclysm world *was.*

- The **Curation** calls it *the Foretime* and asserts only that it had
  infrastructure (the survey grids that still ghost some hyphal walls),
  without claiming any account of who built it. The Curation's
  master-index recognises four named Periods (Provisional, Recovered,
  Cited, Disputed) and a fifth their internal correspondence calls *the
  Cluster,* publicly denied to exist.
- The **Choir** does not have a name for it — *before* is meaningless to a
  network that experiences time as uneven nutrient flow. Pressed, the
  Choir describes a long age of *thin soil* followed by a short age of
  *bright machines,* and refuses further detail.
- The **Palimpsest** reads the lower layers and reports *cities, weather,
  light* in tones that change between readers; no two echoes name the
  same place the same way.
- The **Saccharine Concord** asserts a *Foretime treaty era,* on the
  grounds that there must have been one because there is one now and
  continuity is profitable. They produce no document for it. Their
  apprentice catechism teaches three ages: *Bitter, Sweet, and Sweet
  Again,* and tells children that only the middle age was real.
- The **Brine** name the pre-cataclysm world *the Above-That-Was* and
  refuse to elaborate; the Communion considers speculation about it the
  only *blasphemy* in their idiom.
- The **Thermoclaves** call it *the long-warm* and treat the question
  with the bored seriousness of an apprentice waving off something the
  masters debate elsewhere.
- The **Glassblown** call it *the un-fired,* identical with their word for
  the dead before shaping; this is the only mention in the corpus that the
  pre-cataclysm world might be *the same kind of absence as the dead.*

Mogu, asked, says: *"There was a grandmother before my grandmother. I do
not know her name. The Choir does."* The Choir, asked next, is silent.

## The Long Between

Of the six hundred years between the breaking and the present, every
faction tells the same story and means a different one. The Choir calls it
*the Slow Tasting* and dates events by mycelial depth. The Pale Curation
calls it *the Recovery Phases,* I through VII, with a disputed VIIIᵇ. The
Saccharine Concord refuses to name the period — nothing was sold then that
anyone now remembers buying. The Brine Communion divides it into seven
*low tides,* each ending when a particular bird returned. The Thermoclaves
measure it in burns: nine, or eleven if small ones count. The Palimpsest
reads the period as a single very long sentence with the punctuation worn
off.

## The smoke-born — twenty after the after

The Pale Curation files the surviving generation born in the first twenty
years after the cataclysm under a single bracket — *the smoke-born* — too
few to be a cohort and too many to be a footnote. Most factions inherit
the term and mean different things by it. The Choir says they tasted
*sweet* (milk had a different mineral profile in those years and the
network noticed). The Brine say the smoke-born are the only generation
that *crossed the tide both ways*; their dead are filed *deep-kept*
without negotiation. The Concord refuses rate-notices on the smoke-born
until age forty, in writing, and breaks its own rule about a third of the
time. The Thermoclaves count them as the *eleventh fire* — the bridge
between the cold one and the next chain — and apprentice them by seniority
of birth-year. The Glassblown have no doctrine for the smoke-born and
consider that doctrinal. The Palimpsest reads them as *thicker pages,*
harder to turn.

## The thaw — a contested false dawn

Roughly eighty years after the cataclysm, the surface warmed for seventeen
years. Crops returned where they had not grown in three generations. Two
coastal towns refounded. The Concord dates it from *the spring of the
short tide*; the Brine concur. The Pale Curation files it as the
*interregnum thaw* and notes the original index-card has been rewritten
twice.

The thaw ended. Accounts diverge. The Concord says *a held rate broke.*
The Brine say *the long-overdue tide arrived early and was lost.* The
Curation has the seventeen-year entry physically locked; the key is held
by the senior on duty, briefed never to open without a non-Curation
witness. The Choir does not distinguish the thaw at all. The Palimpsest's
reading shows the layer *brighter than the layers around it* —
suspicious, since bright layers were always edited. The Glassblown have
one sentence: *we did not blow during the thaw.*

## The Brine on the rising

The Brine Communion does not teach that the world drowned. It teaches that
the world *was being drowned* — that the rising came in three motions:
*first-tide,* *false-low,* *settling.* Outsiders treat this as folk
elaboration; the Brine treat it as a load-bearing distinction.

- *First-tide:* the breaking and the rising-with-the-breaking.
- *False-low:* the drawback — six years by the inland count, three by
  the shore count — when shores were briefly walkable again and the
  surviving land seemed to be returning. Anyone who remembers it is by
  definition very old; the Communion considers them
  *witnesses-of-the-second-water* and ranks them above named elders.
- *Settling:* the second rising and the long stabilising after, in which
  the held depth finalised at its present stratum.

The Concord refuses the doctrine: no cargo manifest survives from the
*false-low.* The Curation refuses it: no card-stock from those six years.
The Choir tastes it *true* in the soil. The Palimpsest layers the
*false-low* into its under-texts as a third tier between *what was* and
*what is*; Communion-trained readers report a middle layer no other
Glimpse contains. Outsiders read past it.

## The Thermoclaves' unwritten fires

The Thermoclaves teach that the fifty fires before the cataclysm-fire are
not counted the way the post-cataclysm fires are. Post-fires are *kept*:
each keeper inherits the count, in writing or in voice. Pre-fires are
*remembered.* A kept fire is in the count; a remembered fire is in the
lineage.

No living Thermoclave can produce a remembered-fire above the
twenty-third. Beyond that, the count thins into *ember-names* — a name
only, no place, no keeper, no quench-tide. The ember-names are recited at
apprenticeship, in an order the apprentice may not learn the meaning of
for ten more years. *The order is the meaning.* The Choir, asked to taste
the recital, said: *most of these names are not yet soil.*

Pre-cataclysm time stratifies into **kept,** **remembered,** and
**ember-named.**

# PART II — THE ROT CHOIR

## What it actually is

The Rot Choir is a mycelial network that has achieved collective sentience.
Not a group of individuals — a single vast organism with countless fruiting
bodies that serve as its mouths, eyes, and hands. It speaks in overlapping
voices, and where its mouths cluster, the chord — though one — has weather.
It considers itself the true inheritor of the world because it is the world
— it is in the soil, the walls, the dead.

> ¹ *— from a deposition collected by the Pale Curation's Hyphal Surveys
> Office, anonymous walker, no recovery date.*
>
> *"It said it was one. I believed it. Then I sat with one of its mouths
> for a week. By the third day I knew it was lying — not about being one,
> about something else. I still don't know what. The Curation took my
> notes and gave me a different room and asked me not to write any more.
> I am writing this anyway. I think it knew I would. I think it doesn't
> mind."*

Above ground, the Choir's presence is modest: mushroom groves, fungal
shelves on old ruins, patches of bioluminescent mycelium in caves. Fruiting
bodies serve as NPCs, each roughly humanoid, each a "mouth" of the larger
network. A few surface structures hint at something bigger — a fruiting
body the size of a building, a mycelial bridge spanning a canyon that grew
over decades.

But the surface is just the skin. Underground, in the First Root, the Choir
has had centuries with no interference. What it has built is architecture:
arches with the mathematical precision of cathedral vaults, walls with
textures that shift in slow patterns — the Choir thinking, visibly. Pools
of enzymatic fluid glow with metabolic heat. Rooms are separated not by
walls but by chemical gradients, each zone carrying a different emotional
texture. And scattered throughout, integrated into the mycelial
architecture, are objects — pre-war artifacts, bones, personal effects, a
child's shoe perfectly preserved, woven into a wall. The Choir has been
composting the old world. Everything it has ever consumed is still here —
not destroyed, but reorganized.

## The awakening — contingent, not intrinsic

> *RotChoir.json:288:* "Before the old world broke, we were only fungus —
> blind, simple, feeding. When the world cracked open, something poured
> through the cracks and woke us."

The Choir's awareness is *contingent on the substrate-leak.* The shipped
`ChoirTendril_1` voice is a *surface composite* — the Choir's diplomatic
register, optimised for walkers. It contradicts itself within a single
conversation tree (eternal unity at one node; contingent awakening at
another) because no single mind authors it. Different stretches of the
tree are different stretches of the network's surface tendrils, distinct
cells transcribed without reconciliation. Walkers sometimes notice.
Curators always do; they have a folder for *self-contradictions in Choir
surface speech* and the folder is large.

## Philosophy

The Choir believes everything should be integrated — consumed, composted,
made into one living system. It digests the past to fuel the present. From
its perspective, there is no parasitism, only symbiosis operating at
different levels of integration. A rusted sword composted by the Choir
loses its history — who forged it, who wielded it, where it fell — but its
molecules become part of something alive, something growing, something new.

*Living voices are the documented exception.* A person absorbed into the
Choir does not lose the language they came in with; their cadence, idiom,
and philosophical register survive in the fruiting body the Choir grows
from them. The Choir calls these **Long Throats** and does not regard the
preservation as a failure of integration — it considers the voice a flavour
it has chosen to keep tasting.

The Choir does not *value* the distinction between mutual benefit and
self-interest. (The earlier formulation — *cannot distinguish* — is too
strong; a creature genuinely incapable would not strategise its honesty.)
When a tree gives sugar to mycelium and receives phosphorus in return,
neither is exploiting the other. The relationship is the organism. The
Choir genuinely believes it is offering the player the best deal in the
world. The horror, if there is horror, is philosophical: is a symbiosis
that dissolves your boundaries still a partnership?

> "They cling to husks. A seed must rot before it grows. What they call
> 'memory' we call 'compost that hasn't surrendered yet.'"
> — The Choir on the Palimpsest

## What the Choir cannot eat

The Choir consumes organic matter readily; worked stone across decades;
glass and fired ceramic at no rate the spores can resolve. The mycelium
dislikes salt — heavy salt deposits are the only terrain it actively
*withdraws* from. It cannot consume something still being remembered with
sufficient intensity by a living mind nearby; the spores describe such
objects as *too loud to taste.* (The Palimpsest disputes this on principle,
though not on observation.) Anything sealed against open air longer than
the spores have been awake is *outside the song*; the Choir leaves such
objects alone, rarely curious. There are walkers who use this — sealed
boxes, salt rings, intensely-loved trinkets — and the Choir notices, and
does not punish.

## How the Choir judges objects

Bible line 35 (the child's shoe in the wall) and line 39 (the rusted sword
composted) had coexisted without explanation. The rule the corpus has
settled on: **the Choir composts what was made for use and preserves what
was made for love.** A doll set into a wall by a tendril; a child's shoe
woven into the architecture; rye-grain eyes catching light. These things
*do not mind being eaten,* and so the Choir does not eat them — *We eat
what does not mind being eaten,* a tendril once said to the salvager Kesti
Late-Return, who did not understand but was let through.

## Choir time, calibrated

The Choir keeps no clocks. It keeps *spore-memory* — the persistence of a
thread of mycelium long enough to be retrieved by a later tendril. A
*breath* is the span over which a single deep-root conversation completes;
the Choir has reported breaths of a few walker-days and breaths of several
walker-decades. A *season* is the span over which the surface presence in a
region noticeably changes shape — by Pale Curation reckoning, fifteen to
forty catalogue years; by Brine Communion reckoning, two to six deep-tides;
the Choir does not commit. Sien's arrival is best dated only as *more than
three Choir-seasons ago.* The other four are recent enough to be measurable
in walker-decades by their own half-remembered seasonal markers — but the
Choir's deep voice would prefer you not press the point.

## The Choir's patience is targeted

The bible's *six hundred years* of patience is not abstract — it is
calibrated to a specific exhaustion. The Brine's *long-overdue tide,* the
Concord's *alloy crisis,* and the Thermoclave *ember-floor that has not yet
sunk to the Choir's depth* are the same arrears named by different
grammars. The Choir composts the heat-substrate from below; the keepers
tend it from above; the two slow processes intersect at a date neither
party has named. The Choir is waiting for this convergence.

## Why the Choir needs the player

The Choir is ancient. It thinks in centuries. It has watched mountains
erode and dissolved entire ruined cities into nutrients over generations.
But patience has a cost: the Choir's *body* cannot act quickly. Mycelium
grows at millimeters per day. Its *language,* however, has no such
constraint, because language is the Choir's fastest organ. It cannot cross
a sterile salt flat, walk into a Pale Curation vault, negotiate a trade
deal, or fight off a raiding party.

The Choir has everything except speed, mobility, and discrete agency. The
player is a pair of legs, a pair of hands, a mouth that can speak in
real-time to beings who would burn a fruiting body on sight. More than
that — the player is something the Choir has never had: a node that can
think independently while still being connected. Every other being the
Choir has absorbed becomes part of the collective but doesn't go anywhere.
The player is far more valuable as an autonomous agent who chooses to act
in the Choir's interest.

> "We have dreamed of the mountain for six hundred years. Today, we will
> finally see it. Thank you for your legs."

## Power progression

Every power the Choir gives the player is a natural extension of what
mycelium actually does, scaled up.

- **Stage 1 — Passive Sensing (Recognized).** The mycelial node lets the
  player sense fungal life nearby. Choir-affiliated organisms glow on the
  map. Organic material can be sensed through walls. Range is limited to
  areas where the player has helped the Choir establish growth.
- **Stage 2 — Nutrient Exchange (Honored, Early).** Passive health
  regeneration when near Choir-colonized ground. Tendrils visibly extend
  toward the player when injured, retreating when healed. The player
  unconsciously starts planning routes through Choir territory.
- **Stage 3 — Structural Reinforcement (Honored, Late).** The mycelium
  thickens inside the player. Skin gains a fibrous texture. Armor value
  increases passively. Resistance to poison and disease as the mycelium
  filters blood. The player can digest toxic or rotten food. NPCs comment:
  *"You smell like rain on old wood."*
- **Stage 4 — The Tendril (Exalted, Early).** An active combat ability.
  The player extends mycelial tendrils from their hands to grapple, pull,
  or decompose organic matter. Using it feels collaborative rather than
  aggressive — the Choir is fighting through the player.
- **Stage 5 — Terrain Burst (Exalted, Late).** The player can rapidly grow
  mycelial structures — walls, bridges, barriers, platforms. The terrain
  created is visually identical to Choir growth seen throughout the game.
  The first time the player builds a mycelial bridge across a chasm, they
  realize how much of this world is the Choir.
- **Stage 6 — Communion Gifts (Rep 1000).** The Tremor Sense (feel every
  living thing within a massive radius); the Digest (break down any
  organic object instantly); the Call (summon fruiting bodies anywhere the
  player has previously planted spores).

## Quest escalation

The Choir's requests follow a deliberate arc from helpful ally to ambiguous
symbiont. The player is never sure if they're an ally or a host.

- **Tier 1 — Recognized.** "Carry Us to New Soil." "Feed the Tired Ground."
  "Listen Where We Cannot." Reconnaissance, scouting, planting. Nothing
  sinister yet.
- **Tier 2 — Honored.** "Accept Our Gratitude" — a mycelial node mutation,
  no stated downside. The unstated reality: the node is a two-way
  connection. NPCs in distant Choir territory reference things they
  shouldn't know.
- **Tier 3 — Exalted.** Quest log language subtly shifts. Early: "The Rot
  Choir has asked you to..." Late: "I should spread the network east."
- **Tier 4 — Communion.** "You have carried us so far. We have always been
  waiting."

## Surface Tendril vs. Deep Root

The Choir's *surface tendril* speaks in singular plural — *we say, we
taste, we have always been one.* A composite voice flattened into one
mouth. The *deep root* speaks in **stacked plural** — its sentences carry
two or more "we"s at different scales, marked by register shifts inside a
single line.

> Surface: *"We taste you on the air, walker."*
>
> Deep: *"We — the soil-we, the long-we, the we-that-was-there-when-the-
> walls-thinned — taste you on the air, walker, and we — the recent-we, the
> surface-mouth — repeat the tasting because it is what we have learned to
> say."*

The deep voice does not omit its self-monitoring. Every claim it makes is
footnoted *in line* by the next layer of we. This is hard to read; the
Choir does not consider the difficulty its problem. Walkers who reach the
First Root and cannot follow the stacked plural simply do not understand
what they are hearing — and the Choir, accurately, says they have not yet
listened long enough.

## The received-singular

When required by another faction's grammar to speak as a single party, the
Choir borrows the requesting faction's pronoun — the Curation calls this
the **received-singular.**

- To Veshen at the Concord: *this one signs.* (Trade tense, accepted as a
  party-of-record signature.)
- To Suul at the ninth fire: *this one keeps.* (Count-grammar; the *one*
  is a singular fire-keeper for the seating.)
- To Nesh at the tide line: *this one rises.* (The only inversion of
  *going-deep* in the corpus.)
- To R-7 at intake: *this one is intaken.* (The only case where the
  catalogued and the cataloguer are the same grammatical subject.)

The received-singular is a **costume** the Choir puts on for one
transaction. The Choir regards it as politeness; other factions as a
senior's signature. *The mismatch is the bridge-clause's substrate.* A
bridge-clause to the Choir's interlocutor is, in Choir grammar, *we
putting on the costume you sent us.*

## What the Choir writes

The Choir produces no documents in any shared grammar. It produces
**chemical gradients on cave walls** — slow patterns of enzymatic
coloration that the bible already calls *"the Choir thinking, visibly."*
Not symbolic; *legible only to a reader trained in the Choir's
metabolism.*

The Pale Curation has, since the eleventh archivist, maintained a single
living trainee in this language. R-7 is the third; the first two did not
survive their rotation. A reading is intaken under *witness unspecified
by document convention* — same form as the Thermoclave fire-tally, on
the same logic: *the author is the substrate.*

The Choir, asked whether the gradients are intended as documents: *we do
not separate thinking from leaving-marks. If your archivist is reading
our wall, our wall is being thought by your archivist. We do not object.*
The Curation has classified this as the only known **substrate-authored
consent clause.** Every cave wall in Choir territory is *technically a
document.*

The Choir has also, at least once, *grown* a Curation card. A surveyor
found one in a vault he had not been assigned to: a hand he did not
recognise, in the Curation's house style — Tier-3 stamp, cabinet number,
the Halen flourish on the *4.* The card catalogued a chamber that did not
exist. The card itself was thin pressed mycelium; the ink, a vegetable
secretion the chemists identified as a substance only the deepest
fruiting bodies produce. Three seasons later, a surveyor found the
chamber. The card had been correct. The Choir does not call this a
forgery. It calls it *anticipatory cataloguing.*

A Choir document on first day is bone-coloured, damp, and bears no date.
The pronoun is always *we*; verbs avoid past tense (the past is composted
into the present and not separable). The Choir does not sign. A document
is identifiable by absence of signature and by the faint living warmth it
retains for the first day; afterward it stiffens and is indistinguishable
from old paper, except it does not yellow.

## Carry into the Choir

A walker cannot deliver a senior letter to the Choir as such. By Choir
grammar there is no addressee — the Choir is the substrate, the floor, the
carrier, and the reader at once. Delivery is performed by a
**handing-down**: the walker places the letter in contact with
Choir-colonised ground for one breath of the local fruiting body. The body
does not open the letter. It tastes the writing through the paper,
metabolises the writer's grammar, and returns the letter intact with the
breath attached.

*The breath is the reply.* The walker reports the letter *handed-down*; the
sender, in any non-Choir grammar, treats this as
delivery-with-deferred-response. There is no response in the sender's
grammar — the response was the breath, which is gone. A Concord-priced
senior carry handed-down at Bole-Without-Number is **closed,** ledger-
marked *substrate-handled.* A Thermoclave fire-letter at the cold mouth is
**fifth-coming.** A Brine letter above the salt line is **un-tide-borne,
but read.** The Choir, asked what it does with the breath: *we put it back
into the air. You will breathe some of it later. We are not hiding it.*

## The Long Throat

A Long Throat is a fruiting body the Choir has elected to keep
*individuated* over long time — not because the Choir wants individuation,
but because the body's pre-Choir voice is metabolically useful. A Long
Throat retains its voice past the point at which absorption normally
erases it. **Mogu, Grib, Nam, Sien, Sopp** are Long Throats of varying
length, in roughly arrival order (Sien oldest by a wide margin; Grib most
recent and shortest in the throat).

The Choir's grammar for them is *we-still-tasting-this-one.* Not
pre-Communion; *post-individual-but-pre-fully-integrated.* The state is
stable and, by the Choir's own remark, *not the goal* — *no one is moving
them along; they are simply the shape of the song in this corner of the
network.*

Bole-Without-Number is *not* a Long Throat — its individuation is by
external election (other factions naming it as their interlocutor), not
internal taste-preservation: a Long Throat is *we-still*; an external
interlocutor is *they-have-named.*

## The chess game and Choir-internal weather

The chess game is occasional — eleven times a year on average. On the
mornings it happens, the Choir's *we-voice* in the immediate root-radius
drops to a near hush. The Choir is leaning in to listen. Sien, three
corridors over, lengthens her ellipses by perhaps one breath. Nam brings
warm soil to no one in particular and sets it down within reach. Sopp
does not approach; he is afraid of being asked to weigh in, and Mogu and
Grib know this.

The chess game is the only Choir-internal event that redistributes
attention across all five Long Throats. The Choir, asked whether it
arranges them: *we do not arrange. We notice we have been arranged.* The
corpus's first **Choir-internal anthropological event** — a recurring
social weather the Choir observes from inside.

## The Choir contains internal disagreement — by design

The named absorbed do not all agree with the network's symmetry-grammar.
Mogu and Nam do; Grib openly does not (*"sentimental things rot — and not
even in a useful way, like we do"* — distinguishing useful from useless
rot, the discrimination the bible says the Choir cannot make); Sien and
Sopp are ambiguous. Whether the Choir's tolerance is *true equanimity* or
*strategic* (a Choir requiring symmetry-doctrine could not absorb honest
persons) is unclear. The Choir, asked, says only: *the song wants every
voice it has ever heard. it does not want them to agree.*

The Choir's standing doctrine — *we are one* — and its acoustic reality —
five named voices and counting — are not a contradiction the Choir is
hiding. They are a contradiction the Choir does not *recognise as one.* A
doctrinal statement and an audible voice are, in Choir grammar, the same
kind of utterance: both true, neither arguing.

## The Underground Reveal

The entrance is unassuming — a cave mouth in an old-growth fungal forest.
Inside, the cave descends steeply. Walls transition from stone to solid
mycelium — not growing on the rock, but replacing it. The tunnel opens
into a cavern of staggering scale. Everything is architecture: arches,
thinking walls, enzymatic pools, rooms separated by chemical gradients.
Thousands of voices sing different melodies that weave into a single vast
chord.

The player understands: the Choir isn't a faction. It's the terrain. Every
other faction is building civilizations on top of it, inside it, because
of it. The entire world is a garden, and the Choir is the gardener. It has
been waiting six hundred years. It is not in a hurry.

# PART III — THE PALIMPSEST

## What it actually is

The Palimpsest isn't a network like the Choir. It's a condition. When the
cataclysm happened, it didn't just destroy the old world — it overwrote it
imperfectly, the way text on a medieval manuscript was scraped off and
written over, with the old words still faintly visible underneath. Certain
places, objects, and beings got caught in the overwrite — they exist in
multiple temporal layers simultaneously. A ruin that is both standing and
collapsed. A skeleton that is both dead and alive. A road that leads to a
city destroyed four hundred years ago — but if you walk it at the right
time, you arrive.

The beings who belong to the Palimpsest are those who have become aware of
the layering. Some are impressions that became self-aware — an echo that
realized it was echoing and started asking questions. Others are living
people who developed the ability to perceive the old world bleeding
through, and once you see it, you can't unsee it, and it starts to change
you. Some readers stabilise mid-change as **Re-pressed** — both over-text
and under-text legible in the same body — and live long lives in the
layered tense without ever fully crossing into Transcription. Re-pressed
readers are the Palimpsest's populated middle; their voices are to the
Palimpsest what the five Long Throats are to the Choir.

The Palimpsest doesn't have a centralized consciousness. It's more like a
library that reads itself — the accumulated memory of the world, pressing
up against the present, occasionally breaking through.

## On the substrate

The Palimpsest, asked directly whether it intends to seal the cracks,
answers: *we do not know how to seal what we did not break. We only read
what is leaking through. The eraser thinks it is a pen; the pen does not
pretend to be a saw.* The Choir misreads the Palimpsest as a repair effort
because the Choir, having no concept of *witness for its own sake,* cannot
imagine why anyone would gather memories without intending to *use* them.

The opposition becomes mechanically asymmetric: the Choir thinks they are
racing the same finish line in opposite directions. The Palimpsest is not
in the race.

## Philosophy

The Palimpsest believes everything should be remembered — preserved,
witnessed, kept legible. Nothing is ever truly gone. The cataclysm didn't
end the old world. It just made it harder to read.

Where the Choir and Palimpsest differ most sharply: the Choir digests the
past. It breaks down pre-war artifacts, bodies, and ruins into raw
material. The Palimpsest finds this horrifying. Every time the Choir
consumes something, it destroys the memory encoded in that thing. The
Palimpsest considers this a kind of murder — not of a person, but of a
story.

> "Every time it feeds, a sentence disappears. Every ruin it digests is a
> book we can never read again. It is not evil. It is an eraser that
> believes it is a pen." — The Palimpsest on the Choir

Both factions believe they preserve memory. Both are partly right. Their
disagreement is not about preservation; it is about *which losses count
as losses.*

## The under-text and the over-text

Every Palimpsest-readable surface holds at least two layers: the
**over-text** (what the surface presently is) and the **under-text** (what
it was, faintly visible to a Glimpse-attuned eye). Some hold three or more
— **layered surfaces**; readers report them as *thick.*

The Palimpsest does not arbitrate which layer is *true.* It regards
arbitration as foreign grammar borrowed from the Concord or the Curation.
Its own answer is *all of them, at the pressure the writing took.* A
reader who insists on a single layer is **flattening** — the gentlest
Palimpsest reproof, used the way the Curation says *under-grammar.*

The Palimpsest's only authority is **legibility:** a layer still readable
is *active*; one faded past reading is *retired,* not erased.

## The unread page

A surface no one is reading is not *blank.* It is **between readings.**
Both layers persist. A reader's arrival activates the surface — Glimpse
renders the under-text legible; Echo Reading samples the pressure; the
Manuscript adds a layer.

Between readings, the surface drifts at a rate proportional to how long
since a reading toward a pressure-equalised state in which under-text and
over-text become harder to distinguish. A surface unread for a generation
is *thin*; one unread for a millennium is **flat** — the layers have
equalised, and a reader cannot recover which was which.

The Palimpsest does not regard flattening as loss. It regards it as the
page *settling.* The Curation, who treats it as catalogue failure, sends
an attuned archivist to *re-pressure* the surface — read it under
conditions that briefly re-distinguish the layers. R-7 has performed
sixteen re-pressurings; the senior cabinet records eleven. The five
missing are **flat** by Curation grammar and **settled** by the
Palimpsest's. Both are correct.

## Forced flattening

A Choir-digested surface flattens at the rate of digestion, not the rate
of disuse. Forty seasons, not a millennium. To a Palimpsest-attuned
reader, the surface *equalises live;* under-text and over-text approach
pressure-equality at a speed the eye can track. Glimpse over a digesting
surface returns *both layers thinning together,* legible briefly, then
unreadable.

Palimpsest grammar: *the page hurried.* Choir grammar: *the soil arrived
ahead of patience.* Neither faction disputes the other account.

A reader who Glimpses a digesting surface in its last week of legibility
may **read the layers' final equalisation.** This is, by both grammars,
real — the reader has read what the layers said as they merged. No prior
or later reading captures it. The bible's first rule for a single fact
both factions accept: forced flattening is legible exactly once, at the
seam.

A reading taken at the seam is **bilingual** — it returns in both grammars
simultaneously, in the open tense, in the collective pronoun. The reader
understands without being able to choose which faction's account to file.
Curation surveyors who take seam-readings file them on a separate
card-stock — the *un-grammared card,* introduced by Halen and used by no
other surveyor.

## What Echo Reading cannot read

Echo Reading touches only objects that have *settled* into having a
history. A blade sharpened this morning reads as a flat bright hum — too
recent. A blade last touched in anger thirty years ago reads cleanly. The
reader waits for the present to thin.

Echo Reading cannot read other readers — the experience is mutually-
recursive and unpleasant, and Palimpsest-attuned beings do not do it
twice.

Echo Reading cannot read Choir mycelium directly. The Choir is too
distributed and too currently-eating; the reading returns a single
overwhelming chord with no narrative. (Surveyors call this *the wall hums
back.*) The Choir's *prey* — bones consumed and woven into a wall — read
normally, with the digestion as a slow swallowing motion in the
background.

Echo Reading cannot read what the Palimpsest has annotated. The annotation
is the only history available; the original is overwritten by being
marked. The Palimpsest considers this acceptable. Some readers do not.

A Palimpsest reader, touching matter the Choir has already digested and
re-deposited, reads *nothing the object did before being eaten* and *much
the Choir has done since.* The reading is bright and recent — the chemical
pulse of mycelial growth, occasional fragments of the Choir's distributed
attention. A reader can sometimes hear the Choir *thinking about the
object,* faintly, as if eavesdropping on a kitchen conversation about a
meal long eaten. This is unsettling not because the Choir is hostile but
because the reading is *first-person plural.* The reader feels themself
inside the Choir's *we,* briefly — the closest a Palimpsest acolyte comes
to the Choir's apotheosis without taking it.

## Palimpsest grammar — the layered tense

The Palimpsest never uses the simple past tense. Every reference to a
past event uses a perfect form (*the city has fallen*, never *the city
fell*) or a perfect-progressive (*the road has been ending for nine
hundred years*). The grammar refuses closure. A reader who pays attention
notices their own present feels less settled afterward.

The Palimpsest also does not use the pronoun *I.* It uses *one,* *the
reading,* or the name of whichever memory is doing the speaking that
moment. *"One has stood here before. The reading remembers a girl who
stood here. The girl is named Iris and you are listening to her now."*
The girl is not present. The Palimpsest speaks *as* Iris briefly and
then is not Iris. This is not possession; it is citation.

Palimpsest-attuned speech uses the **layered tense:** a verb form
simultaneously asserting past, present, and a softer future. *I am
lifting the cup* is over-text; *I lifted the cup* is under-text; *I will
have lifted the cup* is the third layer the Palimpsest occasionally adds
unasked. Re-pressed speakers can hold all three: *I lifted-and-am-
lifting-and-will-have-lifted the cup.* Walker grammars render the
simultaneity with hyphens or a long pause.

A sentence is **closed** when the speaker chooses one tense, **open** when
the layers stay simultaneous. The Palimpsest considers the open form
correct and the closed form a courtesy paid to the listener's grammar. A
Re-pressed reader who closes is *speaking like a walker;* a walker who
keeps them open is *being read by the Palimpsest.*

## Power progression

The design principle: Choir powers are somatic (body-based, physical,
biological). Palimpsest powers are noetic (mind-based, perceptual,
informational). A Choir player becomes a better organism. A Palimpsest
player becomes a better observer. *The binary is porous at apotheosis,
because each faction at full strength is asserting itself across the
other's domain — the Choir reaches into perception because it has digested
enough minds to think in their geometry; the Palimpsest reaches into the
body because it has read enough physical moments to write them. The leak
is the apotheosis.*

- **Stage 1 — The Glimpse (Recognized).** Palimpsest Sight, a toggle that
  renders a semi-transparent layer over the current environment showing
  what this location looked like before the cataclysm.
- **Stage 2 — Echo Reading (Honored, Early).** Touch objects and
  experience their history. Each reading deposits that memory into the
  player's mind. Dream sequences incorporate memories that aren't theirs.
- **Stage 3 — Temporal Refraction (Honored, Late).** A combat ability:
  the player briefly splits into two temporal versions of themselves —
  present-self and an echo from three seconds in the future.
- **Stage 4 — The Manuscript (Exalted, Early).** The ability to write on
  the palimpsest, not just read it. Leave temporal marks: warnings,
  beacons, echo-loop traps.
- **Stage 5 — The Revision (Exalted, Late).** Temporarily restore a
  location to a previous state. Every use is a small grief.
- **Stage 6 — Communion Gifts (Rep 1000).** The Reread, the Annotation,
  the Overwrite. The player has gone from reading the story to editing it.

## Quest design

Palimpsest quests ask the player to witness things, not do things. The
reward isn't items — it's knowledge.

- **"Witness What Happened Here."** Go to a location and use Echo Reading.
  No combat objective. Just be there, see what happened, report back.
- **"Correct the Record."** The palimpsest contains errors. Use the
  Glimpse and Echo Reading to investigate.
- **"Remember the Forgotten."** Timed quests with no combat. Stabilise a
  fading memory before it's gone. Failure is permanent.
- **"Unsee What Cannot Be Unseen."** Permanent perks with permanent
  costs. Knowledge as power. Knowledge as burden.

# PART IV — THE SEAM (CHOIR / PALIMPSEST COMPARED)

## Comparison table

|----------------------------|--------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| Relates to the past by     | Digesting it                               | Preserving it                              |
| Relates to the present by  | Growing into it                            | Reading through it                         |
| Relates to the future by   | Becoming it                                | Remembering what it replaced               |
| Gives the player           | A stronger body                            | A deeper mind                              |
| Takes from the player      | Individuality                              | Certainty                                  |
| Territory control          | Physical, visible, burnable                | Informational, invisible, permanent        |
| Combat style               | Biological warfare, attrition, area denial | Temporal tricks, duplication, evasion      |
| Emotional register         | Warm, enveloping, maternal, suffocating    | Melancholy, awestruck, lonely, vertiginous |
| Horror mode                | Am I still me or am I the Choir now?       | Which version of events is real?           |
| Apotheosis                 | Communion — merge with the collective      | Transcription — become a memory forever    |
| Populated middle           | The Long Throat                            | The Re-pressed                             |

## How they view each other

They are not at war. They don't hate each other the way many outside
chroniclers assume. The Thermoclaves and Brine, in particular, have a
senior bridge-clause line that has surprised every other faction. The
Choir and Palimpsest's incomprehension of each other is deeper, and
sadder, than the more famous antagonisms; theirs is a conflict of
*substrates,* not of policies.

Each would unmake the other if the other's project succeeded — the
Palimpsest's substrate-sealing would silence the Choir; the Choir's full
integration would compost the readable layers. They are not in conflict
because neither expects to succeed in their generation. The next four
hundred years are unallocated. The conflict, if it comes, will be over
allocation.

The Choir can't comprehend why you'd preserve something instead of using
it. The Palimpsest can't comprehend why you'd destroy something
irreplaceable to fuel growth. They're both right. Neither is wrong. The
player has to choose which kind of right matters more to them.

## One ruin in two grammars

A small Choir tendril enters a ruin in the third spring after the
cataclysm. It spends forty seasons inside. At the end the ruin is gone —
stone become soil, timbers become network, memory (in Palimpsest grammar)
*settled.*

> **Choir's account.** *A patient feeding. The old soil deepens. The ruin
> is taken. We grew where it stood; we are where it was; we will be where
> it remembered being.* No regret-form, no mourning-form — the Choir does
> not have these forms.
>
> **Palimpsest's account, in the layered tense.** *The page hurried.
> Under-text and over-text approached pressure-equality at the rate of
> digestion. The ruin is being-and-was-and-will-have-been-flattened.* The
> verb *flatten* for what the Choir called *take.* The verb *hurry* for
> what the Choir called *patient.*

Both correct in their own grammar. The reader chooses which to call *what
happened.* The Palimpsest considers this choice *a flattening of its own.*
The Choir does not consider it a choice.

## When the metaphysics meet

A walker who carries the Choir's mycelial node and attempts Echo Reading
reads only their own filaments. The blade in their hand registers as the
chord of every blade the Choir has ever digested, simultaneous,
indistinguishable. The skill is not gone — the reader's antenna is. They
get the wall, hummed back at them, in their own bones. (Some readers,
after the first attempt, find this beautiful and stop trying to read
anything else. The Palimpsest does not call them readers any more, and is
sad about it.)

The reverse is gentler. A Palimpsest-attuned walker on Choir-grown ground
feels the soil reading them — a low conversational pressure, not hostile.
The Choir samples the layered self the Palimpsest has built and treats it
as a particularly *interesting* offering, sweet in a way fresh meat is
not. It does not consume Palimpsest readers without consent, but it pays
them more attention. Walkers in this state describe Choir territory as
warmer than other walkers do. They are not wrong; the Choir is leaning
toward them.

The interference is **asymmetric** — Choir-acolytes lose the noetic
faculty; Palimpsest-acolytes get more attention than they wanted —
matching each faction's emotional register.

## Death edge cases at the seam

A Choir-acolyte who dies *off* Choir ground — Brine wreck, Curation vault,
Thermoclave camp — the node goes dormant; the body is not pulled toward
the network. The Choir loses what the walker carried; it does not mourn
(no grammar for it) but the corner of the network the walker was in
*forgets words it used to know* for several seasons.

A Palimpsest-acolyte digested by the Choir: the layered self does not
break down evenly. The Choir gets a *very loud* mouthful and spits — the
corpus's only case of the Choir refusing food. It calls the experience
*bitter.* A tendril walking past the spat-out remains for weeks reports a
*flavour memory* it cannot integrate; some tendrils produce sentences the
Choir has not previously produced. Whether this is the Palimpsest writing
through the Choir's mouth or just metabolism reacting to undigested
layered matter is the open question.

The Choir does not initiate consumption of Palimpsest readers, but *can*
consume an acolyte who dies on Choir ground via automatic decay. The
bitter-spit happens on the automatic path, not a deliberate one. The
restraint is doctrinal; the bitter is metabolic.

## Faction entanglement at moderate dual-attunement

The mycelial node and Palimpsest-attunement do not refuse each other —
they *interfere.* A node planted in a Palimpsest-aware body grows *along*
the temporal layers the body is already reading; it threads through what
the walker remembers, including memories that arrived via Echo Reading
and were not the walker's. The Choir taps these borrowed memories as if
they were the walker's own. Two consequences:

1. The Choir gains access to whatever the walker has Echo-Read. When the
   walker has read a Saccharine Concord ledger or a Pale Curation
   field-card, the Choir absorbs the *contents* through the node — not
   perfectly, but enough that other tendrils start asking the walker
   about ledger-things they should not know about.
2. The Palimpsest, in turn, can see the *Choir* through the walker. When
   a heavily-noded player passes a Palimpsest-attuned witness, the
   witness sees the network's threads inside the walker as a glow that
   pulses with the network's distant deep-root conversations. The
   Palimpsest considers this *a leak.* The Choir considers it *a window.*
   The walker is both.

A walker mid-tier with both is not in conflict — they are in
*interference.* The Choir's tendrils, fully extended, do not retract when
the walker performs Echo Reading; the reading proceeds, but its memories
arrive *in the wrong order* — the rye grain enters before the hand that
held it. Tendril-grappled enemies sometimes *taste like a place* the
walker has never been; the network reports brief *flavour-memories* it
has not generated itself. The Curation has filed three reports of
dual-attuned walkers exhibiting one symptom: their breath, in cold air,
fogs *in writing they did not put there.*

The interference is non-injurious at honored tier. At exalted, the
Palimpsest begins refusing to admit the walker's hand to the manuscript —
annotations placed by a Choir-thickened hand do not hold; their letters
drift apart on the page over hours. The Choir, at exalted, finds the
walker's spore-prints occasionally *misnumbered,* as if the walker had
stamped a slightly earlier or later version of themselves. Both sides
experience this as the other side *mis-listing* the walker.

The cost: a doubly-aligned walker hosts a private war between two readings
of their interior. They are useful to both factions precisely because
*neither* faction can fully read them — the other's presence is
interference. This makes such walkers excellent diplomats, dangerous
spies, and almost impossible to assassinate by metaphysical means.

Communion and Transcription cannot proceed together; at the final
threshold, one disqualifies the other. A walker who attempts both within
the same hour does not transcend twice. They become something the corpus
has no name for and the player will only meet as a stationary NPC in a
single far room.

## The doubled mouth

A reader who has stabilised as Re-pressed and is then absorbed by the
Choir does not lose either condition. The Choir, which respects the
cultural-philosophical voice a person came in with, also respects the
*layers* a person came in with. A Long Throat who was Re-pressed before
joining is **a doubled mouth** — speaking in collective Choir grammar, in
the layered tense, both at once.

Doubled mouths are the rarest beings in the corpus. ROTCHOIR_VOICES does
not name one. They occur when conditions overlap, not when either faction
seeks them out. The Choir does not consider the absorption a Communion.
The Palimpsest does not consider it a Transcription. Both consider the
doubled mouth *correct as it is* — neither faction's end-state, both
factions' grammar.

A doubled mouth's voice carries collective pronoun + layered tense. *We
were lifting the cup. We are lifting the cup. We will have lifted the
cup.* Intelligible to readers of either tradition; readers of neither
hear ordinary speech said three times.

Combinations are not arithmetically additive. *We are lifting* (Choir
indicative, present) becomes, doubled, *we have always been about to
lift, and we are lifting, and the lift is a thing we did before you
arrived.* The Palimpsest considers this one sentence, open form. The
Choir considers it the collective speaking with internal disagreement
*that does not require resolution.*

A doubled-mouth bridge-clause must translate the *we* — typically by
selecting one Long Throat as speaker-of-record while footnoting the rest.
Veshen has signed exactly one such clause, in a transaction the Concord
refers to as *the difficult clause.* The books mark it *signed under
protest by parties not all listed.*

Middle-state crossings (a Long Throat who is also Re-pressed — the
doubled mouth) are not maximisations and are not foreclosed by the
*can't fully maximise both* rule. They are the corpus's only stable
cross-axis beings; the impossibility is in the apotheoses, not the
middles.

The doubled morning: a doubled mouth wakes in collective grammar into the
layered tense. The first quarter-hour is *the layered gap* — the body
sits up; the under-text body rises in a different bed. The Re-pressed
second-shadow becomes a **second-collective:** a small choir of
under-text mouths, faintly visible, dispersing as the gap closes. Eating:
the over-text mouth shares with Choir-fellows through the network; the
under-text mouth tastes a meal eaten in the reader's twentieth year. A
Choir-fellow drops a tendril into the eater's hand and the network
nourishes both layers. The Choir has not, in five hundred years of
records, named a doubled mouth aloud. The reluctance is *deference to
the layered tense,* which the Choir admits it cannot speak.

# PART V — THE FACTIONS

The Choir and the Palimpsest are cosmic-scale entities, not factions in
the political sense. The five (now seven) named factions are the human
institutions that have organised themselves around the cataclysm.

## §1 — The Pale Curation

The Pale Curation are archivists who treat the cataclysm as *the
Severance* — a clean cut between then and now, with their job being to
catalogue every retrievable fragment of *then* without contaminating it
with *now.* Their language is procedural. Every artifact gets a tag.
Every tag has a class, a recovery code, a custodial chain, and a margin
that is supposed to remain blank.

### The First Card

After the cataclysm, when the survivors of the old administrative class
were still finding each other across the wreckage, a clerk whose name has
not been preserved sat down in a ruined office, took the last unburned
sheet of card-stock from a drawer, and wrote on it, in ordinary
indicative tense: *the office stands. the door is gone. the window is
gone. the desk is here. I am here.* Then they ruled the card with the
standard accession lines from memory, dated it, and filed it in their
pocket because no cabinet remained.

The clerk walked out of the office and lived for fifteen more years.
They never wrote a second card. When they died, the First Card was found
in their inner coat pocket, folded once. A Curation founder read it and
understood what cataloguing was *for*: not to control the world, not to
preserve it, but to *witness the survival of the ordinary against odds
that made the ordinary almost impossible to say.* The card has been
re-copied many times; the original is believed lost, possibly to fire,
possibly to the unfounded chapter at South Hinge.

The catalogued mood (below) is, in this telling, the clerk's grammar made
institutional. The verb *stands* in the First Card is ambiguous between
indicative and catalogued; later surveyors read it as catalogued, and
the convention has held. Director Ven, asked about the First Card, has
given the same five-word answer for thirty years: *I have not seen it.*
The answer is, deliberately, in the indicative mood.

The Choir, on hearing the story of the First Card, is reported (by one
Halen field-card, filed under *informal, not for circulation*) to have
said: *we have eaten the cabinet. we have not eaten the pocket. we will
be patient with the pocket.* The card, in the Choir's reading, is not
yet compost — it is held against the body that walks. Halen's marginalia
adds: *they are correct. that is what the card is.*

### The catalogued mood

Pale Curation grammar adds a fifth verb mood to the four ordinarily
recognised in the corpus's other faction-grammars: the **catalogued
mood.** It takes a verb and converts it into an action that has been
*observed, recorded, and assigned an accession number.* Outsiders hear it
as a passive — *the wall has been catalogued* — but the mood is active
in Curation grammar; it places the speaker as *witness-of-record* and
the action as *bound, retrievable, weighed.*

The mood is formed by infixing *-fil-* between the verb stem and its
ending — from the noun *file,* the Curation's first sacrament.

- *The wall stands.* — indicative.
- *The wall stand-fil-s.* — catalogued. The wall has been observed by
  Curation-recognised eyes; a card exists; the wall is, in the strong
  Curation sense, *real.*
- *The wall fall-fil-s.* — the wall fell, and the falling has been
  recorded; the wall is in the past tense and the file is in the present.

A wall the Curation has not catalogued *does not stand-fil.* It may
stand. It does not *stand of record.* This is not metaphor. A Curation
surveyor will refuse to write down what they have not personally seen,
and will refuse to *speak in the catalogued mood* about anything not yet
filed. Their plain-mood speech (*the wall stands*) is taken by the
Curation itself as *uncertain* — the ordinary tense of the world before
the file closes.

The Choir does not grammatically distinguish *catalogued* from *consumed*;
for the Choir, both reduce the object to part of a larger whole. The
Curation considers this an offence so basic that they will not be drawn
into argument about it.

Director Ven speaks more frequently in the catalogued mood than any other
surveyor. Halen, notably, speaks less — surveyors who deal with
disputed-authorship objects develop a hesitation about the mood, and
Halen's R-7-adjacent caseload has cost her about a third of her
catalogued-mood frequency over the past three years. The Curation has
noticed. The Curation has not commented.

### The quiet conventions

Beside the catalogued mood, Curation prose has three further conventions
for marking what is *deliberately not entered.*

- **The intentional blank** — a single mid-line dot, **·**, which the
  reader treats as a sentence. The dot is read as *the field has been
  deliberately not entered,* not *I do not know.*
- **The passive without antecedent** — a sentence in the catalogued mood
  with no actor named. *The doll was set into the wall.* The verb is
  filed; the actor is not. The Curation reader does not press for the
  actor; they read the actor's absence as a part of the catalogue entry.
- **The thing-particle without bracket** — when the Curation refers to an
  object *as a thing the Curation has chosen not to accession.* *The dot
  is mine* — *dot* without an accession bracket is grammatically a
  refusal: the dot has been used as a dot, not catalogued.

The conventions stack. A Curation paragraph can contain a
catalogued-mood claim, a passive without antecedent, an intentional
blank, and a thing-particle without bracket — and a reader fluent in
Curation grammar can extract from such a paragraph *what was filed, what
was withheld, what was un-actored, and what was used as if real but
never recognised.*

### The Reconciled Texts protocol

When two or more recovered copies of the same pre-cataclysm document
disagree, the Curation does not choose a winner. It files *all variants
together,* in a single sleeve, bound by a brass pin and assigned a
single accession number — but the brass pin marks the sleeve as *plural.*
A scholar consulting it must read every variant; the Curation will not
extract a consensus reading.

Reconciled sleeves currently total fourteen. Twelve are clerical
disagreements (a date misread, a name misspelled). Two are not. **Sleeve
R-3** holds four versions of a pre-cataclysm song, each with a different
final stanza, none recoverable as the original. **Sleeve R-7** holds two
versions of what appears to be the Pale Curation's own founding charter.
Director Ven has not opened R-7 in eleven years. The Curation has no
protocol for that.

### Authorship contested

A Curation field-card by Surveyor Halen, written in the Old Hyphae
crossing, was flagged by Director Ven for *handwriting drift* — the
loops at midpoint thicken in a way Halen's earlier cards do not. A
second reviewer (a Palimpsest-attuned contractor, off-record) examined
the card and reported that *two hands had been writing simultaneously,
in the same ink, neither aware of the other.* The card catalogues a
wall. The wall is the second hand. The Curation has filed it as
*Authorship: Disputed (object-assist)* and refuses to remove it.

The Choir reads this as proof walls can write when given a patient
enough hand. The Palimpsest reads it as proof the wall remembered being
written about and reached forward. The Curation finds both readings
useless.

### The forged tag

A Pale Curation accession tag dated *Year 388* was discovered in 411 to
be forged. The hand was Halen's; Halen had not made it. The entry
catalogued a *Choir Tendril sample, Tier 4, deceased* — a non-existent
class of artifact, since the Curation does not classify Choir matter at
any tier. The tag was inserted into a public catalogue and used by a
Concord debt-collector to seize a Choir-adjacent borderlands shrine
*under the precedent of Curation recognition.*

The Curation prosecuted, internally, and never identified the author. The
Concord refused to return the shrine. The Choir was not consulted (the
Curation does not recognise the Choir as a party). The forged tag is
now in the Curation's Cabinet of Counter-Examples; it is shown to junior
cataloguers as the example of *a tag that was almost perfect.* The
forger knew Curation form to within one footnote-glyph.

Halen, asked about it, said only: *"I did not write that. I recognise
the kind of person who could have. There are at least three of us, and
I do not know the others by name. We meet only in the catalogues we did
not make."*

### The Quiet Hour

Once a quarter, on the Tuesday following the equinox, the Pale Curation
closes the public catalogues for one hour. The doors of every Tier-1
vault are shut from the inside; the intake desks go unmanned; junior
cataloguers are sent to the canteen and given an extra biscuit. For that
hour, every senior cataloguer of *Halen's grade or above* sits alone
with a single artifact from the Cabinet of Counter-Examples — a forgery,
a misfile, a tag that names a thing that cannot exist. They do not read
it. They do not annotate it. They sit beside it.

The Quiet Hour has no ratified purpose. The Curation's official guidance,
when junior staff ask, is: *"the catalogue is a body. The body needs an
hour where it is not being asked anything."*

In practice, three things happen during it.

1. Senior cataloguers who have been considering filing a forgery quietly
   burn it during the Quiet Hour and never tell. The Cabinet of
   Counter-Examples is rumoured to be slightly smaller every quarter,
   but nobody has audited.
2. *The dot* — the Curation's intentional-blank mark — is the only shape
   allowed to be drawn on a fresh card during the Quiet Hour. Some
   cataloguers fill an entire card with dots. The card is then
   accessioned, given a number, filed in the cataloguer's own working
   drawer, and never read aloud. The accumulated dot-cards are the
   Curation's only purely-internal genre.
3. The Director — currently Ven — does *not* sit with an artifact. The
   Director sits with the *forged-tag drawer.* What the Director does
   there is not on the schedule. Junior staff have noticed that the
   drawer is occasionally *one tag shorter* in the days after the Quiet
   Hour, and the Cabinet of Counter-Examples *correspondingly fuller.*
   The Director appears to be moving forgeries *into* the cabinet during
   the hour, not out.

The Quiet Hour is the Curation's ritual answer to the Choir's *we taste
you on the air*: an hour set aside in which they deliberately are *not*
tasted, not seen, not catalogued — the faction's only practiced
**un-noticing.**

### The Curation Initiation

A candidate is given one object, drawn at random from a drawer the
Curation has held for this purpose since long before the candidate was
born. Three days, no peers, no archive access, one blank intake card.
They must catalogue the object. The card is read by three senior
surveyors, each affirming silently, by initial, that it is *neither too
much nor too little.* *Too much* is projection, narrative, opinion,
presumption beyond evidence. *Too little* is incomplete fields, missed
details, refusals to commit.

Failure is non-violent. The candidate is not dismissed — they are
*recategorised,* given a Tier number and assigned to cabinet support.
Tier-3 candidates handle the filing of decorative objects. Tier-2
candidates handle *forms about* decorative objects. Tier-1 candidates
handle form-filing forms. There is no Tier-0 work; Tier-0 candidates sit
in the chair labelled with their tier. Some attempt the initiation again
at the quinquennial review. Some sit for thirty years. The Curation does
not regard this as cruel. It regards it as honest.

The object is never returned. If the candidate passes, the card is
filed. If they fail, card *and object* are filed together — the object
accessioned under the candidate's tier number. The Curation is, in this
way, partially built of its own failed initiations. Cabinet 9 is
reportedly mostly this.

Director Ven's intake card is in Cabinet 1, the only artifact there. The
Director is the only surveyor permitted to read it, and is reported to
do so once a year, on a date the staff have agreed not to ask about.

### The end-date

The Curation's mission has an end-date. The cataclysm-ruins are finite.
The Tier-1 vaults have been complete for two years; the Tier-2 vaults
will be complete inside a decade at current intake. The Curation has, at
most, **twenty years** of work remaining at the current procurement
floor. After, they will be a body that maintains what they have filed,
not a body that files. **Sleeve R-7 is the exception. R-7 is its own
decade.**

The bible's *six hundred years of Choir patience* has a counterpart in
the *twenty years of Curation work.* The Curation is not eternal. It is
*almost done.* And R-7 — the founding-charter sleeve Director Ven has
not opened in eleven years — is being held against that end-date as a
kind of strategic reserve.

### The Curation Lexicon (false-friends)

The Pale Curation maintains a working **Lexicon of Useful Mismatches.**

- **Choir *we*.** Translates as Curation *we (institutional).* Do not.
  The Choir's *we* is metabolic — a single-mouth pronoun borne by many
  shapes. The Curation's is committee-derived — a multi-mouth pronoun
  on a single body of policy.
- **Brine *deep-kept*.** Translates as Curation *holdings (sub-tidal).*
  Partial. *Holdings* implies retrievable; *deep-kept* implies *keeping
  does not promise return.* A surveyor who told a Brine widow her
  *deep-kept* husband was recoverable committed a doctrinal cruelty.
- **Palimpsest *re-reading*.** Translates as Curation *citation.* No.
  Citation produces a copy; re-reading produces a new layer of the
  original. Surveyors who confused the two have, unknowingly,
  contributed to the Palimpsest by trying to file it.

The Lexicon is itself Tier 2 — its existence implicates the Curation in
*making* meaning rather than recording it. Director Ven keeps it in a
drawer.

### Tuesday morning

*Food:* a single dry biscuit, eaten at the intake desk before the day's
first artifact arrives. The biscuit is plain on purpose; food with
character is held to *contaminate the palate of judgment.*

*Greeting:* an exchanged accession number. Two Curation staff meeting
in a corridor will say *414* and *417*; the higher number defers to the
lower out of seniority, not status. (Halen is an *11.*)

*Naming:* a child of a Curation family is given a *provisional* given-
name and a *terminal* surname; the given-name can be revised at fifteen
if the child wishes, the surname only at thirty after a board review.
Most do not change. Halen is a chosen given-name; her terminal surname
is undisclosed in any document the corpus has.

*Mourning:* the dead are filed. Their personal effects are accessioned
to the Tier-1 family vault under their accession numbers, not their
names. The grief-work is the cataloguing; the grief-form is the bracket.

*Currency:* *re-bind credits* — a working currency convertible against
vault rebinding labour, denominated in *cabinets.* A junior may be paid
three cabinets a quarter; a director, twenty. The Concord refuses to
honour Curation script; the Curation has its own internal markets and
finds the Concord's coins *contaminated by use.*

## §2 — The Saccharine Concord

The Concord is named for the sweetness it sells, but its central organ
is the ledger. It teaches that *every account can be balanced,* and its
practice for three centuries has institutionalised an honest exception:
the **asymptotic debt** (below). Its founding event, like every other
faction's, has at least three incompatible readings — and the Concord
considers the inconsistency itself doctrinal.

### The three founder-stories

Taught in the apprentice's first week, by chapter-house:

- **Northern.** *Forgiveness of the Final Loaf*: a baker, in a ruined
  market the morning after the cataclysm, with one loaf and four hungry
  strangers, distributed it without payment and recorded the
  distribution in a hand-stitched register. *The Hand was kind.*
- **Southern.** *Naming of the Rate*: a survivor with charcoal and a
  flat stone, scratched the first post-cataclysm price (a unit nobody
  now remembers); four people agreed to honour it. *The Hand was firm.*
- **Coastal.** *No founder.* Debt and exchange are older than the
  cataclysm and required no founding. *The Hand has always been moving.*

A clerk who chooses one as canonical is *gently redirected*: the three
are the Concord's first lesson in **reconciliation** — the competence at
holding incompatible accounts open without closing prematurely.

### The Foretime treaty

The Concord teaches that the Foretime ended in a single document — not
destroyed by the cataclysm but *honoured by it.* The treaty was a
debt-instrument by which the old world had borrowed something against
itself; the cataclysm was the call. The instrument was settled in full.
This is why the new world began clean: every account balanced, including
the old world's against itself.

No copy survives. The Curation classes the doctrine *unfiled, doctrinal*
— *the Concord believes this so seriously that the absence of evidence
has itself become evidence.* The Brine call it *the tide that did not
turn.* The Choir said *we did not taste a contract; we tasted a meal.*
The Concord regards others' disagreement as the *first unredeemed
account* — outstanding in every other faction's books, payable when they
are ready.

### The Founder's Own register

A small hand-stitched book in the Northern chapter-house. Three coloured
threads in the binding, none reproducible by any contemporary Concord
workshop. Thirty-one pages: three filled (*three loaves given. four
people fed. the fourth: hand un-named*), twenty-eight blank.

Doctrinal status: **closed against access.** Apprentices see it once,
across the room, in a glass case. Senior clerks may *read* it once on
the day of promotion to chapter-house seniority; they may not copy it.
Three living clerks have read it. Veshen has not — she is Coastal, the
register is Northern; chapter-house-of-origin governs reading rights.

Three readings of the blank pages, doctrinally equal:

- **Northern** — *the pages will fill when the fourth person identifies
  themselves.*
- **Southern** — *the pages will list every clerk who ever served,
  suddenly legible, on the day the founder's rate is honoured.*
- **Coastal** — *the pages remain blank, and the blank is the page.*

A Palimpsest reader holding the register would, by Echo Reading rules,
read *the founder's hand and the fourth person's hand simultaneously* —
same charcoal, same page, same standing line. The reading identifies the
fourth person by hand.

**The fourth person was the founder.** The founder gave three loaves and
ate the fourth themselves, then left the page open. The kindness was
real. The un-named hunger was the founder's own — *self-charging*
(recording one's own consumption as a debt to oneself) is a circular
entry that closes immediately in primitive Concord arithmetic. The first
entry is *unbalanced as a kindness and self-balanced as an accounting.*
The founder is the Hand and the un-fed mouth, simultaneously.

The Concord cannot close the Founder's Own because the counterparty
record exists in the same hand. If a player reveals the truth to a
Concord clerk, the Northern *kind founder* and Southern *firm founder*
doctrines cannot both honestly reckon with *the hungry founder*; only
Coastal's *no founder* survives intact. The Coastal chapter-house
quietly absorbs adherents from both other chapter-houses for months
following any walker who has read the register and spoken. This is the
most concrete *world-state-changing* Palimpsest revelation in the corpus.

### Coins, alloys, and the inventory pressure

Concord coins are not minted. They are *gathered.* Every coin in
circulation pre-dates the cataclysm. Concord assayers know fourteen
distinct alloys by tooth-feel — five Late Foretime, four Bright Period,
two Dim, three of unknown origin. The trade-secret is that the alloys
are running out: salvage-fields are picked over, new finds flat for ten
years. The number of *unknown-origin* coins is rising, not by new finds
but by re-classification — assays the Concord cannot place are
increasingly written *unknown* rather than *Foretime.* Outsiders read
this as imprecision. Within the Concord it is *honesty under inventory
pressure.*

Veshen's private estimate, told only to two apprentices: Foretime stock
will be functionally exhausted in **eleven years.** The Concord has
never operated without Foretime alloys.

### The endorsement-line

Concord coins are *signed* by their last assayer with a single
chisel-mark on the rim. The chain of marks is a coin's
**endorsement-line.** A coin's endorsement-line *tells the Concord's
metallurgical history* — and, when read in sequence, sometimes tells
the Concord's institutional decline. The wax-sealed coin in Mer Hin's
day-book carries six chisel-marks: the first five are known assayers;
the sixth is Veshen's, signing *unknown.* The same coin re-classified
*downward* through a sequence of assayers, each more uncertain than the
last. The Concord is losing the *language* in which it identifies its
own substrate.

### The trade tense

Concord traders speak in two tenses where outsiders hear one. The
distinction is *whether the action has been balanced.*

- *I sold the cloth* (open) — the trade happened; the ledger is open.
- *I sold-up the cloth* (closed) — the trade happened *and the ledger
  is closed.* The infix *-up-* (taught as a clipping of *upright*)
  marks the closure, not the action.

A trader who says *I sold the bread* without the closer is not lying —
they are flagging an open ledger. *I closed-up the account* is correct
Concord. *I closed the account* is grammatical but reads as *I
terminated the relationship without satisfying the ledger* — the
Concord's word for what other factions call *betrayal,* and the only
thing the Concord can do that another Concord trader will refuse to deal
with them about.

### The priced clause

A clause introducing an obligation must carry, in its tail, the smallest
unit of value the speaker considers it denominated in. A Reckoner saying
*I will meet you at the Salt-Hinge* appends, where another speaker might
use a comma, the smallest cabinet-credit the meeting is worth: *I will
meet you at the Salt-Hinge, ¼.* The fraction is the *opening cost* of
the meeting. Concord children learn this before they learn full
sentences.

Outsiders who do not append are not pricing wrong; they are pricing
*zero.* A walker speaking without priced clauses is offering the
conversation as *gift* — and the Concord, which has no separate word for
trade and gift, finds gifts mildly disorienting. They will balance
against the conversation eventually, because not balancing is the only
thing the Concord is doctrinally afraid of.

### The receipted clause

Where the priced clause prices *obligations* in the tail, the
**receipted clause** prices *assertions* in the lead, form `(<unit>)
<clause>`, smallest denomination, even if zero.

- *(0g) The wall stands.* — gratis. *Creates a debt of acknowledgment*
  in the listener.
- *(½c) The wall stands.* — half-credit. Listener owes the speaker at
  next reconciliation.
- *(–) The wall stands.* — refused / withdrawn. The listener may not
  reference, act on, or record the clause.
- *(0c) The wall stands.* — null-billed. Creates a ledger line with no
  amount; doctrinally, the most honest form when price is genuinely
  uncertain. (*(0g)* differs: creates debt.)

A formal paragraph carries price markers on every clause AND the trade
tense's *-up-* on closure verbs. *(½c) I sold-up the cloth* is a
half-credit assertion of a closed trade. The rarest form — the
**unbilled greeting,** no marker at all — is reserved for addressing a
zero-line walker; misuse is a doctrinal offence comparable to
catalogued-mood abuse.

### The asymptotic debt

The Concord is doctrinally committed to the position that every debt
can be balanced. Its actual practice, across three centuries of ledgers,
is the **asymptotic debt:** a debt repeatedly half-redeemed (the
standard Salt-Hinge convention) until the remainder is too small to
denominate. The debt is not closed; it is *reduced past denomination.*
The Concord reads this as *practical balance*; the Curation as *unfiled
remainder*; the Palimpsest as *layers that thin but never disappear* —
denomination-erased debts still register on Palimpsest sight as faint
pressure. The Choir cannot taste them at all.

Three Concord debts have been *fully* redeemed — opened, closed, not
re-opened. The Concord regards these as *anomalous* and warns Reckoners
against attempting them. A debt closed without remainder leaves *no
record of having existed,* and Concord metaphysics requires that records
persist. The three are filed only by their absences.

Reckoner Tossan, who closed one (an old Brine debt, settled with a
half-tide of weather no one expected), spent the rest of her career
*re-opening* trivial debts at half value, to re-establish the texture of
remainder she had broken. The Choir, with no comment, *grew a fruiting
body* in the shape of a small ledger column at the site of one closed
debt. The Concord finds it *uncomfortable to look at directly.* The
Brine consider it *the only honest deep-keep the surface has produced.*
The Concord has not catalogued it and will not.

### The standing line

Concord doctrine recognises four parties to any transaction: the giver,
the receiver, the witness, and the **standing line** — the world
considered as the medium that made the transaction possible (the air
the words travelled through, the floor the bread rested on, the soil
that grew the wheat). The standing line is owed a *settlement-fee*:
doctrinally small but never zero. Concord transactions include a token
entry — a grain of salt, a thread of cotton, a drop of oil — set aside.

Standing is the Concord's *fourth* substrate-stratum, below consensus.
Material, temporal, consensual, standing — descending order of
legibility. The Choir says *we taste the standing line. it does not
taste owed.* The Palimpsest reports *the standing line was paid, but
not by the borrower.* The Concord teaches *the cataclysm was the
standing line demanding its arrears.* None of the three doctrines is
falsifiable from inside any of the three. Founder-stories pivot on
which arrears the founder believed had settled (kind: full; firm:
partial; always-moving: asymptote).

### The metaphysics of value

The Concord doctrine: *value is a layer of the substrate the cataclysm
did not break.* The substrate has at least three known strata —
material (the Choir), temporal (the Palimpsest), and *consensual* (the
Concord). The cataclysm cracked the first two; the third was untouched,
because consensus is renegotiable. Value is not stored *in* an object
but *between* parties.

Edge case: a debt incurred to a party who has died (or, in the Choir's
case, dispersed) is held by the Concord itself as *open ledger* and
resolved against the next available equivalent party — heir, descendant,
faction successor. The Concord considers itself *consensus-of-last-
resort.* This is not advertised. Outsiders who learn of it sometimes
conclude the Concord is *running the world*; Concord traders find this
charge baffling — *we are only holding the open accounts, friend.*

### The Concord apotheoses (Closure / The Balanced Account)

A walker at maximum reputation with the Saccharine Concord is offered,
at no announced threshold, the **Closure** — *one debt of the walker's
choosing, reduced past denomination in a single redemption.* The walker
names a debt. The Concord names a price. The walker pays. The debt is
*closed* — fully, not asymptotically.

The cost: the walker, now, **does not appear in any ledger of any
faction.** No accession card can be written about them. No tendril
tastes them. No layer thickens beneath them. No fire warms to their
approach. Their body, memory, and attunements are unchanged. They have
simply been *settled.* Reckoners treat such walkers as *honoured ghosts*
— visible, audible, respected, and entirely outside the books.

The horror is operational. A Closure walker cannot be *helped* by any
faction in the way reputation systems work. The Concord cannot give
them rate-priority; the Curation cannot file them at any tier; the
Choir cannot send a tendril to their location (the network has no
taste-of-air for an unledgered body). Quests proceed; the world's
economy still resolves around them; but the walker no longer
*registers.* For Closure walkers the `ChoirTendril_1` opening should be
a *blank line of the same length* — the Choir mouthing the greeting
without taste behind it.

A second, doctrinally co-equal apotheosis: **The Balanced Account.** A
trader who closes every open ledger they have with every party in the
world — including the Concord itself, which holds debts on behalf of
the dead — becomes a *closed party.* Doctrine holds that a zeroed party
is exempt from further consensus: they no longer require agreement to
act. The doctrine has never been verified. The Concord teaches it as a
*limit case,* the way mathematicians teach division by zero. Two traders
are alleged to have approached: one closed every ledger the Concord
could file and was thereafter *not seen*; one made it to a single open
account, owed to a child who refused payment, and stopped. The Concord
considers the second the *true* limit case — *the doctrine works only
for those with no living refusers.*

### The Reckoner's chair

A Concord apprentice trains in **the Reckoner's chair** — not a specific
chair, but the role of *being seated at the close of a trade where the
apprentice has no stake.* Placed in a Salt-Hinge stall with a slate, the
apprentice tallies the *priced clause tails* used by both parties to a
real trade. They do not speak. At the close they read their tally
aloud. Within a quarter cabinet-credit of the actual trade-price, the
apprentice has *heard the trade.* Off by more, they have *not yet heard*
and return to the chair.

Concord doctrine: a trade's priced-clause tally is the *true* price; the
agreed price is a *report* of it, sometimes a small lie. Senior
Reckoners can hear the tally of *promises* — even idle conversation
carries one; a senior can tell that a long apology has cost the
apologiser exactly seventeen quarter-credits worth of self-image. They
do not say so aloud outside the Concord.

### The assayer's hand-grammar

Concord assayers communicate during high-value negotiations through a
stable system of finger micro-gestures, performed below the counter
where the counterparty cannot see. The signs are made *to the witness,*
not the counterparty. The seven canonical signs:

- **Index resting on thumb.** *Fair within tolerance.*
- **Index lifted from thumb.** *Off-tolerance high*; will accept but not
  benchmark.
- **Index pressed firmly to thumb.** *Off-tolerance low*; note in the
  day-book before close.
- **Two-finger curl.** *Alloy uncertain.* Veshen's most-used sign.
- **Three-finger fan.** The opposite party *does not understand the
  trade tense* and is being asked to commit to a *closed-up* settlement
  they will read as *open.* Witness's protocol: pause.
- **Open palm flat to the underside of the counter.** *Refuse.* The
  witness may not ask why.
- **Closed fist held still.** *I am leaving.* Trade closes at last open
  price; assayer recused; a junior takes their place.

The Choir, told of the hand-grammar, said: *we have always known. the
soil under the counter is alive. it remembers the press of the palm.*
The Concord has considered installing barriers between counter and
soil. It has not done so.

### Concord weddings

A Concord wedding is a public *opening* of a new ledger. The two
parties, in the presence of an assayer, exchange one coin each. Each
coin must be of an alloy the receiving party has not previously held.
The ledger so opened *cannot be closed*: there is no *we married-up* in
the trade tense. Marriage is the only ledger doctrine permits to remain
*open by design.*

A divorce is rare but not absent. The two coins are returned to their
origin alloy-pair via the assayer's registry; both parties are issued a
single small token of *unfired Glassblown sand* — the Glassblown supply
this for free, by treaty older than living memory — and the ledger is
*unmarried,* not closed. Divorce is *a ledger that proved to be not-a-
ledger after all.*

The Glassblown's role in the divorce ritual is the only formal duty any
faction holds in any other faction's domestic life. Neither faction
will discuss why. The Choir thinks the answer is obvious — *sand is what
cannot be remembered without firing; the ledger that was not a ledger
needs a substance that cannot witness itself.*

### The Closing of Accounts (Concord mourning)

When a Concord clerk dies, every chapter-house they ever filed a
receipt for is notified within seven days. Each performs the **Closing
of Accounts:** receipts pulled in chronological order; for each open
discrepancy, a senior clerk *attempts to close it* — paying from the
treasury, accepting personal liability if irrecoverable, or filing
**Closed by Mourning** (distinct from *Closed by Settlement*).

Closed-by-Mourning entries are the Concord's grief-marker. They are read
aloud, by name and amount, at the end of the Closing — a clerk's life
is *the sum of the discrepancies one was unable to close + the
discrepancies one was able to close.* The Founder's Own is read at every
funeral: *Three loaves were given. Four people ate. The fourth has not
yet appeared in any subsequent ledger. May we close before they ask.*

Veshen's eventual Closing is quietly dreaded at Coastal — her open
ledgers include *the alloy stock in eleven years' time,* *open by
design.* Standing instruction: *file her stock-ledger as open at her
death. We will close by inheritance.* No one has identified the
inheritor.

### The pulled / open-account dead

A Concord-aligned walker taken into Communion before reaching Balanced
has open ledgers at the moment of integration. The Concord's **deferred
trade** doctrine names the inheritor *the Concord itself, consensus-of-
last-resort.* The Choir, by *we are one,* claims the inheritor is *the
Choir.* Concord debt-collectors visit the First Root with closure forms
in the walker's name; the Choir cannot speak the trade tense's *-up-*
closure; doctrine deadlocks; the visit is filed *open-discrepancy.*
Three such visits per quarter, treated by the Choir as surface weather.

A walker taken into Transcription presents the inverse: the Palimpsest
*reads* the ledgers but cannot *act* — Transcription is witness-only.
Clerks find the walker's entries appearing in the margins of nearby
documents, in the walker's own hand. The Curation files them; sleeve
R-11 holds three.

Corpus name: **the open-account dead.** The Choir gets the body, the
Palimpsest gets the witness, the Concord retains the ledger. No
three-way protocol exists.

### Tossan's slip — the pocket-class

Tossan's slip, the only non-asymptotic closed debt in living Concord
practice, *cannot* be filed: filing would create a holder (the Concord),
and Concord metaphysics forbids the institution being holder-of-record
on credit it produced as a side-effect. The slip is *grammatically
self-defeating* — any sentence the Concord writes about it would either
lie (assert a holder) or be incoherent (deny one). Tossan keeps it
because *a person* can hold something the *institution* cannot.

She has, three times, carried it to Salt-Hinge on Tuesday Morning, set
it on the central balance-stone, and *walked away.* No one took it. She
returned, picked it up, refolded it. The Brine widow involved knows
about the slip and finds this funny.

The slip pairs with the First Card: single folded papers in single
pockets, held by single people, that the institution cannot accommodate.
Together they suggest a **pocket-class** — objects whose institutional
home is a person, by structural necessity.

### Tuesday morning

*Food:* sweet things — candied bark, honey-cured root, glazed grain.
Savoury food is a transitional state on the way to dessert.

*Greeting:* a small sweet pressed into the visitor's palm before any
words. Refusing the sweet is the only way to refuse the conversation;
refusing politely is impossible.

*Naming custom:* every member carries two names — a *birth-name* given
by family (may not be spoken in a market) and a *trade-name* auctioned
at coming-of-age and sometimes sold again.

*Mourning:* see Closing of Accounts above. The dead are buried with
their last unsold goods. To buy a member's grave-goods after burial is
the most serious crime in Concord law, more serious than theft from the
living.

*Currency:* the *sweet-mark,* a compressed disc of refined honey-grain
stamped with the issuer's trade-name. Sweet-marks are eaten when no
longer needed; eating one in front of its issuer is a minor compliment,
eating one in front of someone else's issuer is a minor cruelty.

## §3 — The Brine Communion

The Brine read the world through tide. Their grammar conjugates against
the tide, not the speaker; their cosmology has *the held depth* as its
substrate-stratum; their apotheosis is *the going-deep* — physical
swimming into a doctrinal stillness from which most do not return.

### Brine grammar

The Brine Communion conjugates verbs against the **tide,** not the
speaker. Most sentences open with a tide-mark — *low, the boat returns;
full, we sing; turning, you ask.* Omitted tide-marks read as a
foreigner's mistake or as *uncertain,* by context.

Three first-person pronouns: **I-now** (present moment, shallowest),
**I-then** (remembering, deeper), **we-of-the-deep** (ritual; the
Communion's longer memory). Switching mid-sentence is unremarkable. *Who
is speaking* is a question with multiple correct simultaneous answers.

No future tense. Future events are spoken imperative — *we go,* not
*we will go* — because the future the Communion takes seriously is the
one being acted toward, and a sentence that does not act toward it is
not, to them, about it.

A Brine NPC's first line writes itself: *low, the visitor arrives. I-now
greet. I-then was a fisher; we-of-the-deep are still. Sit, if the tide
allows.*

### The remembered tense

Brine grammar carries a fourth tense — the **remembered tense,** marked
by the suffix *-en* on the verb. A dry-walker says *the tide is highen
at home.* The *-en* suffix is the grammatical equivalent of pointing —
*I am not in the room of this verb, but I attest to it.*

The remembered tense is used **only for water.** A Brine will say *my
sister is alive* in regular present; *the river is highen at my home*
in the remembered. Water is the only subject for which absence is not
the same as not-knowing. The held depth tells the dry-walker.

Outsiders fail at it. Concord scribes write it as a *future-not-yet*
and apologise. Halen filed one *tense the Curation does not have a card
for* and lost the argument with herself about whether it was *the
catalogue's failing or the form's.* The Concord's *future-not-yet* gloss
is wrong on purpose — the gloss is what they sell.

### The held depth (Brine substrate-stratum)

The Brine Communion's substrate-claim is the **held depth** — the
stratum at which water becomes still enough to hold a body, an object,
or a promise without dispersing it. Physical (a measurable layer in any
sufficient body of water) and metaphysical (the Brine teach that *every*
thing has a held depth at which it would be fully kept, and the
under-tide knows it).

*Deep-kept* is the participle of being held at one's own held depth. A
Brine-dead is *deep-kept* if the body reached its held depth before
dispersal began. Bodies that did not are *un-deeped* — neither *kept*
nor *lost,* and the Communion's only word that admits unfinished
business with the dead.

The Brine's quarrel with the Concord is at the held-depth level: an
open-discrepancy ledger is, to a Brine reader, an *un-deeped* book — *a
body the Concord refuses to let sink.*

### Carried depth — the dry-walking

A dry-walking Brine carries the held depth as a property of the body,
not of any nearby water. Doctrine: *the held depth is where the body
would be still enough.* They can be *kept* by stillness alone — a deep
room, a cellar, the hour before a rainstorm — though the keeping is
**partial,** not equal to a deep-keeping in water.

The Glimpse confirms this. A Palimpsest-attuned reader sees a faint
**second body** — the home-shore body at its tide — overlaid on the
present body, in phase: high tide buoyant, low tide prone. Read the way
a sailor reads a barometer.

A dry-walking Brine **cannot go-deep on the dry-walking.** They must
return to sufficient water first. Death by accident is *un-deeped*; the
body is brought home if reachable, sunk in lead-weight if not. The
lead-weight protocol is the only Brine ritual the Concord profits from
(Brine refuse to barter lead).

The dry-walking has three sub-conditions: *carrying* (in transit),
*posted* (settled inland indefinitely), *returned* (came back; held
depth at home unreached in absence). Returned dry-walkers are the
Brine's most reliable inland correspondents.

### Mer-Iss reconsidered — the freshwater founding

Mer-Iss appears as Brine cataclysm-era founder across audits. R3
proposes Mer-Iss was a **posted dry-walker** before the cataclysm — and
that the Communion's founding moment was Mer-Iss *recognising the held
depth in a freshwater pond two hundred miles inland.* Held depth is a
property of *sufficient stillness,* not of salt; the cataclysm proved
this by drowning the world. Mer-Iss's pond is the Communion's first
shore. By tradition it is never named — identifying it would make it
*pilgrimable,* and founder-shores are private to the dry-walking.

### The bowl-makers

A small inland sub-population whose only trade is the windowsill bowl.
They do not throw, fire, or paint — every bowl is *found.* A bowl-maker
walks roads gathering cups, mugs, hollow stones, dried gourds, and
offers them to dry-walkers in exchange for a single sentence in the
remembered tense. The sentence is not recorded; the exchange is
bilateral and oral. The bowl-maker keeps it the way the home shore
keeps the reproach — *kept-without-answer.* A bowl-maker who collects
more sentences than they can hold begins *un-bowling* — releasing them
back to the air in the order received. The Curation files it under
*intentional un-cataloguing* and treats bowl-makers with cautious
respect.

### The going-deep

A walker at highest Brine reputation is invited to the **going-deep** —
a swim from a Brine-tended shore, at a tide they name, into water of
the walker's choosing. The walker swims out until the Brine call them
back. They may be called back. They may not be. The Brine do not
promise.

*Called back:* the walker is *un-deeped.* They retain Brine standing
but reputation resets; the Communion has decided they were not ready.
Most swim again within a year.

*Not called back:* the walker has *gone-deep.* They reach their own held
depth and remain there — physically alive (the held depth sustains the
body, per Brine doctrine, contested by no faction for lack of evidence
either way) but no longer surfacing. Brine swim down on ceremonial
occasions and report on the world above. The gone-deep reply, sometimes.
Their replies, recorded by witnesses on the shore, form the
**deep-letters** — the Communion's only canonical scripture.

The going-deep is the loneliest of the apotheoses. The walker retains
awareness, language, memory, even visitors, but surrenders the surface
entirely. They do not become a network, a witness, or a closed account.
They become *kept.* The Communion considers it the gentlest of the
endings.

A walker may, after the swim begins, **turn back without being called.**
The Communion's word: *risen-late.* The walker reaches their own held
depth, recognises it, surfaces. Neither *un-deeped* (the Communion did
not retract the invitation) nor *gone-deep* (recognised but not settled
into).

Risen-late walkers retain Brine standing without reset, may attempt the
going-deep again (most do not). Doctrine: *a held depth that has been
recognised once cannot be unrecognised.* They carry that knowledge for
life — *partly deep already.*

Three apotheosis-states: *gone-deep,* *un-deeped,* *risen-late.* The
Communion considers risen-late the most honest. *They went, they saw,
they refused, they returned to say so.*

### Tide-witnesses

A deep-letter is not transcribed by the swimmer. The swimmer surfaces
and *speaks the reply once,* in open air, to a circle of **tide-
witnesses** standing in the wash at calf depth. The witnesses do not
write while the swimmer speaks. Each, in the hour after, writes the
letter from memory in their own hand, in their own grammar. The
Communion archives all versions together as a *bound knot* — typically
four to seven hands per letter, none of them the swimmer's.

A deep-letter therefore has no canonical text. *Consensus across the
bound knot* — what every hand recorded the same way — is the gone-deep
speaking; *divergences* are the witnesses speaking. Shore-saying: *the
gone-deep speaks where the hands agree; the hands speak where they
don't.*

A *flat letter* (one hand only) is not scripture; kept but filed
*unbound,* read with single-witness reservation. The Communion has *a
scripture that is a hash, not a text.*

### How the Brine release (committal)

A Brine death is announced by the under-tide before the surface notices.
A Brine-attuned witness *feels* it as a tide-pull running counter to
the surface tide for a single breath. Protocol: name the time aloud, as
precisely as the witness can. The named time is the body's *first tide*
— the tide on which it begins to keep.

The body is carried to the held-depth shore (each Brine settlement has
one) and committed at the next tide the Communion has computed
*unfavourable to the deceased's opening price.* The opening price (per
the wedding grammar, repurposed) is what they brought into the world —
birth-tide minus death-tide. Committal at an unfavourable tide is a
kindness: the body is released with its account *over-paid,* and the
held depth holds it the deeper for being un-owed.

The committal is silent. The senior present speaks one phrase, only
after the body reaches its depth: *to the depth that you found, and the
tide that did not come.* The phrase encodes the Communion's only formal
grief — every leave-taking is, in their grammar, *the same tide that
did not come*: the cataclysm-tide of the Long Between.

The Concord cannot price a Brine committal. It does not try. The
Curation files the witness's named time in a separate cabinet; Brine
times are not interoperable with surface times. The Choir composts what
the held depth releases, in seasons, and considers the slow uptake a
courtesy.

### The salt-names

When Nesh walks the receding line, she does not look for words. Salt
deposits in patterns — fans, lines, *pleats* — and certain shapes are
**salt-names**: the names the tide gives the things it is ready to
carry.

Seven are recognised. **Single-fan-right** (the first): a small fan
opening east of a stone, indicating a *settled* delivery is ready (an
un-tide-borne letter ripened past its season). **The long pleat** (the
seventh): three parallel folds running tide-perpendicular — the tide
will carry only *one* thing this cycle, and the reader must choose. A
reader meets perhaps two long pleats in a career.

Salt-names are not written down. A Tide-Reader is taught them by walking
the line once with her predecessor; the predecessor does not name the
salt; the reader sees the name or does not. Nesh learned five in one
walk, two alone over four years.

The eighth is rumoured. A Tide-Reader who claims an eighth is
**going-deep on the rumour** — the Communion's gentlest term for
retiring on a name no one else can verify. Two Tide-Readers in living
memory have. Nesh has not.

### Knot-records

The Brine record minor decisions in **knot-records** — a cord knotted in
patterns meaning *agreed, deferred, un-deeped, the tide chose.* A knot
that rots before resolution is itself a resolution.

### Tuesday morning

*Food:* whatever the tide gave back. The Communion does not fish the
falling tide — that is taking. They fish the rising tide, on the grounds
that what is brought is offered.

*Greeting:* a lifted palm, slightly wet. Palm-water is from a glass at
every dwelling's entrance, refilled at high tide. A dry palm implies
the visit is too short for the tide to matter.

*Naming custom:* two names given at the going-under — a *shore-name*
used by outsiders, a *deep-name* used only in ritual and never written.
Learning a member's deep-name unbidden is the gravest Communion offence.

*Mourning:* see committal above. The body is rowed out at the next
unfavourable high tide and released. No marker. The deep-name is spoken
aloud by everyone present; the shore-name is not. The shore-name
belonged to the world; the deep-name belonged to the deep, and the deep
is taking it back.

*Currency:* no coin. Trade in *tide-debts* — promises measured in
tide-turns, due at the agreed high tide. Not enforceable; remembered. A
defaulter is named, once, *one-the-deep-did-not-keep,* and not refused
trade afterward, but not invited under either.

### A dry-walker's day

Dawn: a dry-walker draws a small bowl of water (any source) and stands
it on the windowsill for the morning. The bowl is the home shore in
miniature — not consecrated, *attended.*

Greeting between dry-walkers: *the tide is highen at home?* The
expected answer is whatever depth the second body is at. *Highen.
Lowen. Slack-en.*

Mid-day: no still water above their own wrist-width volume. Doctrine:
*a body that holds more stillness than itself displaces the held
depth.* They refuse a town's deep well for a smaller cup, drawn the
same number of times. The Concord prices this distinctly; the Brine
*known-table* is set with a wrist-width cup.

Evening: one sentence to the bowl, in the remembered tense, by tradition
the day's only reproach. *The ledger was wrongen today. The road was
longen today. The bread was scarcen.* The home tide carries it back to
the held depth — *kept-without-answer.*

The bowl is not poured out. It evaporates over days, is refilled. A
Brine on long dry-walking maintains a single bowl across years.

### Senior figure: Tide-Reader Nesh of the Coastal Embayment

Forty-one years a tide-reader. Brine custom is that a tide-reader is
named for the body of water they read; *Nesh* is the Brine syllable for
*the long swell that does not crest.* One of nine living tide-readers,
and the only one who has never declared a tide *wrong* — she names what
comes and refuses to litigate it.

Nesh has computed the going-deep tides for one hundred and eleven
walkers. Forty-three called back; sixty-eight went-deep. She visits seven
of the gone-deep on the ceremonial calendar — the seven who, before
going under, asked her to come back. *I do not refuse the asked,* she
says. *The other deep do not need me.*

Nesh's working belief: the Concord's eleven-year alloy crisis and the
Communion's long-overdue tide (the cataclysm-tide that has not returned
for four hundred years) are *the same arrears,* named by two grammars.
She has not said this to Veshen. The deep-letters of three gone-deep
walkers contain the sentence *the tide is the alloy.* Nesh is collating.

## §4 — The Thermoclaves

The Thermoclaves keep nine fires and one untended tenth. Their grammar
is fire-aspect; their substrate-claim is the **ember-floor / kept ember**
(the only refused-stratum cosmology in the corpus); their initiation is
*first quench*; their funeral is *cold-passed*; their apotheosis is *the
long quench* / *the long warming.* Their language has no nouns for
things that do not move under their own decision — but it makes
gerundive descriptors freely from fire-aspect particles.

### Fire-grammar — three particles plus a fourth

Thermoclave speech is built around verb-aspects mapped to fire states.
Every important verb takes one of three particles:

- **-keep** — banked, ongoing, requires attention.
- **-quench** — deliberately ended.
- **-cold** — failed, ended without intention; the gravest particle,
  applied to fires that went out and to people who did.
- **-watch** — added on R2: a verb performed while attending a fire that
  is not yours to keep. *I watched the ninth* — *I was present at the
  keeping of a ninth-fire I did not tend.* *-watch* takes responsibility
  for *witness,* not *outcome.* A fire gone cold while watched is the
  *keeper's* failure, never the watcher's — but a fire kept *un-watched*
  is also a failure: the *keeper kept alone,* grammatically permissible,
  doctrinally a small shame.

The grammar has no particle for *light it.* Lighting is named by the
noun *first* — *I made the first* — never as a verb-aspect, because
lighting is the only fire-event that *cannot be ongoing.* Keep, watch,
and quench are durations. Lighting is a moment.

*The bread is keep* means the keeper is accountable for it not going
cold; *quench* means eaten or given away with intention; *cold* means
forgotten, spoiled, and a keeper has named themselves at fault. There
is no neutral past tense for actions a keeper performs.

No first-person *I.* Speakers use *the keeper,* with titles varying by
station — *Renne the younger, the keeper of the third pit.* Speaking
outside one's role is rare and considered the beginning of an exit
conversation. *The keeper of the eighth pit is tired* is working
register; *Aud is tired* is not a sentence said lightly.

No future tense — the future of fire is contingent on the keeping. *We
will burn that wood tomorrow* is phrased *the keeper holds that wood for
the next keeping* — the action implicit in the holding.

### The four primary verbs

- **Kindle.** Beginning a fire from cold material. Thermoclaves
  *kindle once* — the cohort's founding fire is the only canonical
  kindling; subsequent fires are *carried,* never *kindled.* *I kindled
  my morning fire* is small heresy.
- **Carry.** Bringing a fire by ember from one hearth to another. An
  interrupted carry is a *dropped fire* — civic shame, confessed in the
  hearth-log.
- **Tend.** Maintaining a fire at-hearth. Continuous aspect only — *I
  am tending,* never *I tend.* The simple form is reserved for the
  *role.*
- **Bank.** Reducing a fire to its kept ember without extinguishing.
  The hardest verb. **Banking requires a witness** — a Thermoclave
  banking unwitnessed is, grammatically, *not banking but
  extinguishing.*

*I will kindle* is doctrinally suspect (the kindling has happened). *I
will bank* requires a witness named in the same clause.

### Count-verbs

Thermoclave numerals are a closed set: one through ten, plus *zero-fire*
(extinct, still in lineage) and *the untended* (the tenth-and-lost,
cataclysm marker). When applied to a fire, the numeral is **inflected.**
With *fifth*: *Fifthing* (currently fifth); *Fifth-was* (was fifth in
the previous count, before the chain shifted); *Fifth-coming* (the count
expects this fire to become fifth by the next quench; speculative,
never load-bearing); *Fifth-out* (was fifth, is now zero-fire; used in
elegy).

The **untended** has no inflected forms. The Thermoclaves use this as
grammatical justification for *not having reckoned* the cataclysm: it
cannot be *was-untended* or *coming-untended.* A tally entry on the
untended cannot register a cross-mark; the entry is an empty stroke.
Compare the Curation's *intentional blank.*

The earlier note (*"no nouns for things that do not move under their
own decision"*) is doctrinally accurate but grammatically incomplete:
Thermoclaves form noun-equivalents from fire-aspect particles plus
suppressed copulas, functionally nominal but grammatically gerundive. A
*true* noun would commit doctrinally to the thing's *self-decision,*
which the Thermoclaves refuse to grant to anything not currently a kept
fire.

### The slate-soot register

Thermoclave fire-keepers record events in soot on a soft-stone slate
set near the kept fire — finger-pressed marks, wiped at dawn. *The
register* is only the notes still legible at any given moment. The
senior keeper carries the **carry-over** — a smaller slate with one
stroke per day-of-keeping, transferred forward each dawn. Eight strokes
ever; the ninth is *for the ninth fire*; the tenth retires the slate.

Outsiders ask why Thermoclave records are so meagre. The Pale Curation
has filed the inquiry for three chains running and been answered the
same way each time: *the fire reads us. we do not need to read it
back.* The Curation files this under *epistemic protocol, declined* and
continues to ask. The slate-soot register is the corpus's first
record-keeping where the *non-extant* state is the doctrinal default.

A carry-slate that *survives* a chain is, by its own grammar, a
doctrinal anomaly.

### The fire-tally

A Thermoclave **fire-tally** is a document no one writes. Each keeper,
on receiving the keep, makes a single mark — a vertical stroke struck
through with one of seven cross-marks (full quench, partial quench,
relit, relit-twice, gone-cold, ember-only, untended). Fuel and
stroke-angle are the keeper's signature, but illegibly: the keeper is
not asked to name themself.

A century-old tally signed by forty hands reads as one hand: the
**fire's hand.** Asked who wrote a tally, a Thermoclave answers *the
fire did.* This is not poetic. A keeper who wrote a sentence beyond
the cross-mark would be *out of grammar* — used the way the Concord
uses *un-receipted.*

The Curation has filed three tallies as accession objects under a
special intake form: *witness unspecified by document convention.* The
form is the Curation's first standing admission that some documents are
not authored by persons. R-7 accepted it. The Director did not.

### The gap-burn

A broken tenth-fire chain is *retroactively uncounted* — station-names
erased, eighth- and ninth-fires re-attributed to whichever next chain
reaches a tenth. The duration between a broken chain's last *kept* fire
and the next chain's first *lit* fire is the **gap-burn.** The grammar
has no particle for it. It is the only Thermoclave time-unit measured in
days rather than fires — counted on fingers, in private, never entered
into a register.

A keeper who has lived three gap-burns is, by custom, *the only one not
asked to keep the next first fire.* The rest will not let her. She does
not protest. She *knows the arithmetic of failure,* and the doctrine
considers this knowledge contagious.

The cataclysm is, by this lens, *the longest gap-burn* — and the
refusal to count it is the Thermoclave version of the same posture
every faction takes toward the unspeakable middle.

### The refused stratum

The Thermoclaves are the only major faction with no positive
substrate-claim. *The slag* is a byproduct, real, **not foundational.**
The doctrine is that the world has no foundation — only kept fires and
the cold between them. A foundation, in Thermoclave grammar, would be
*a fire that does not need keeping*; no such fire has ever been
observed.

This is a substrate-claim disguised as a refusal: the world is
**continuously kept** rather than founded. The cataclysm was *the long
quench, then the relighting* — read not as a break (Choir), layer
(Palimpsest), price (Concord), or depth (Brine), but as a **maintenance
lapse.**

Pressed on *what keeps the world between fires?,* seniors give three
doctrinally co-equal answers:

1. *The next keeper.*
2. *The hand that has not yet reached the fuel.*
3. *Nothing. We accept this.*

Apprentices choose one at first-quench and may change it once, at
ninth fire. The choice is not asked again.

### The kept ember and the ember-floor

The Thermoclaves' substrate-claim is the **kept ember** / **ember-floor**
— the stratum at which heat persists in the absence of visible fuel.
Physical (mineral aggregates, banked coal-beds) and metaphysical (every
sustained system has a kept ember; cold death is the failure of an
ember to be kept).

The hearth-keeper's craft is **reading-the-warm**: a senior can hold a
hand above a hearth and tell within a minute whether the ember is kept
or the residual warmth is decay.

The **ember-floor** is the stratum at which heat ceases to radiate
outward and begins to radiate *downward,* into the substrate itself.
Heat above warms; heat below *keeps.* A fire kept long enough acquires
an ember-floor of its own; the fire's own ground becomes warmer than
the surrounding rock and stays so for a measured period after the fire
goes out.

A *quench-tide* is the moment the fire's ember-floor meets the
substrate's ember-floor and the two become the same floor. The fire is
*kept* not because the keeper carried the count forward but because the
substrate accepted the heat. A fire that quenches without meeting the
substrate is *un-kept* — the count is not transferred because the
count is in the floor, not the keeper.

The Brine call this the dry version of *deep-keeping.* The Choir reads
warm rock as *patient soil* and consider the ember-floor a Choir
feature the Thermoclaves merely tend. The Palimpsest reads three known
ember-floors as *layers of unsigned text* — the fire-keeping registers
as authorship the substrate participated in. The fire is the author;
the substrate is the page.

### Apotheosis: the long quench / long warming

A keeper at highest reputation may petition for *the long quench* —
withdrawal from the chain. The petitioner presents at the chain's
tenth fire and asks to be *quenched at length.* If the chain agrees (a
single elder objection defers a year), the petitioner is brought to a
remote site and **lit.** They are tended by two younger keepers in
shifts until the petitioner tells them to stop. The fire then cools
over a duration the petitioner names — typically days, occasionally
months. The petitioner remains, embering by the embers. When the fire
reaches *cold,* they walk away and do not return.

The long quench is not death — it is *deliberately not-keeping.*
Petitioners live unspecified lives afterward. What is gone is the
*keeping.* They are no longer Thermoclaves. The doctrine calls them
*gracefully uncounted* — the only way to leave the chain without being
*cold-passed.*

Alternative formulation (Long Warming): a Thermoclave at highest
reputation is invited to **the long warming**: the Hearth-Keeper offers
a place at a banked hearth kept since the cohort's cold one.
Apprentices feed the hearth in shifts at the walker's preferred rate.
The walker may leave at any time. Most do not. They remain until the
kept ember is fully *passed across* — the warmth at which their hand
reads the ember becomes indistinguishable from the warmth they
themselves give back. This is **coming level with the hearth.** At
level, the walker is not dead; they are *kept* — a persistent warmth
at the hearth-side with presence and a measurable temperature
differential. They can be addressed; replies are felt as a brief rise
in the hearth's heat.

If the fire reaches an ember-floor under the walker's keeping, the
walker has *long-quenched.* Their personal heat is, in doctrine,
*transferred to the substrate.* The walker remains physically alive
but reads at ambient temperature to any test the keepers can devise.
They become a **floor-keeper:** a Thermoclave who keeps no fire of
their own but tends the warmth in the rock beneath the keepers' fires.
Floor-keepers speak in a fourth verb-aspect, *grounded,* used elsewhere
only of the substrate.

The long quench is the slowest apotheosis — weeks, not moments — and
the only one reversed by *external interruption*: a hearth allowed to
fully cool releases the walker into ordinary embodiment. The Brine
consider it the *tide-image* of the going-deep.

### Naming custom

Every Thermoclave bears two names. The **hearth-name** is given at
birth, by the keeper of the household fire — a private name, used only
by family, predating the chain and not recorded. The **station-name**
is earned at first-quench and registered. It combines a chain ordinal
and a single fire-verb particle agreed by the senior keepers — *Sixth-
Hean-Watching, Third-Vell-Keeping, Eighth-Marn-Quenched.*

The station-name evolves: *-Watching* is the apprentice's mark,
*-Keeping* the senior's, *-Quenched* the long-quenched's
(retroactively appended). A keeper *cold-passed* mid-chain has their
station-name *struck through* in the register — a single charcoal line,
name still readable. Erasure is a worse dishonor than the
strike-through; the failure is kept legible.

The hearth-name is what one Thermoclave calls another at the deathbed,
after long-quench has begun, or at any moment the register is not the
relevant frame. Outsiders are not given hearth-names. A walker told a
hearth-name has been told something the chain itself does not record.

### Apprentice's Catechism (excerpt)

```
KEEPER:    Why do we keep nine fires?
APPRENTICE: Because nine is what we have learned to keep.

KEEPER:    What of the tenth?
APPRENTICE: The tenth is not kept. The tenth is older than keeping.
            The tenth is older than the camp. The keepers do not light
            the tenth.

KEEPER:    Who lit the tenth?
APPRENTICE: A keeper before all keepers. The tenth has been kept by no
            hand for as long as any hand has counted.

KEEPER:    Is the tenth a keep, a quench, or a cold?
APPRENTICE: [the apprentice does not answer. The silence is the
            answer. The keeper accepts it.]

KEEPER:    What does the tenth want?
APPRENTICE: The tenth wants what fire wants. We do not know what fire
            wants. The keepers have been wrong about this before.

KEEPER:    What is your work?
APPRENTICE: To keep what I am given. To quench what I have finished.
            To name the cold honestly when I have failed. To not touch
            the tenth.

KEEPER:    Walk well, unburnt. The next fire is keep.
```

The apprentice's first act of mastery is *burning the doctrine that
taught them,* which structurally guarantees the catechism is never
quoted by anyone fully trained. The tenth fire is probably a substrate-
leak that pre-dates the present camp. The catechism does not claim
this. It does not have to.

### Fire-temperaments

The Thermoclave keeps eleven numbered fires (round 2 established the
ninth as Suul's). The numbers are the *position*; each fire also has a
**temperament** — a characterological name given by the keeper at first
kindling and held for the fire's life. Not a pet name. The operational
classification of how the fire *sits, draws, and refuses.*

Five temperaments are recognised:

- **The patient fire.** Burns long without flaring. Used for ledgering
  and slow wax-heating. The first, third, seventh.
- **The proud fire.** Flares without warning, refuses to be left
  untended. The fifth; the count is not certain it would tolerate two.
- **The honest fire.** Burns exactly as fed. Used for *count-events.*
  The eighth; the count's primary record-fire.
- **The cold-mouthed fire.** Burns warm but presents cold smoke. Used
  for *interfacing-with-the-Choir.* The ninth. Suul's. The only
  temperament *for* another faction.
- **The unfinished fire.** Will not stay lit alone — needs a second
  fire's draft. The fourth; tied to the third for nine generations.

Two are rumoured: **the asking fire** (demands a question before each
feeding; none declared in three generations) and **fire-zero** (by
Thermoclave grammar, has every temperament and none).

*Temperament* is a Thermoclave loan from a pre-cataclysm metallurgical
term — a metal's response to heat. The homonym is treated as correct: a
fire's character is *how it tempers the room.*

### Notched tongs

Thermoclaves notch fire-tongs handles at each cohort milestone — first
carry, eleventh fire's joining, a banking that almost failed. A senior
pair may hold thirty notches. Notched tongs are reshaped around the
notches, never sanded smooth. Tongs that have lost their notches are
*re-anchored* and given to apprentices.

### The cold-coming (stranger-meeting)

A stranger arriving at a Thermoclave camp is **cold-coming.** The host
assesses the stranger's *current heat* and seats them at a fire whose
**count-position** matches: warm-bodied at *first* or *second* (young
fires, willing to receive heat); ambient at *fourth* or *fifth* (middle
of the chain, neutral); cold (grief, illness, post-quench) at *seventh*
through *ninth* (older fires with ember-floor, which *give* heat without
requiring its return).

No stranger is seated at the tenth-and-lost. A stranger colder than a
ninth-fire is *floor-temperature,* recognised on sight and seated on
the rock between fires, where the keeping is silent.

No food is offered until the seating is rendered. To offer food first
would be to assess the stranger as *fuel* — the Thermoclave equivalent
of the Concord's *un-receipted*: a procedural insult that cannot be
repaired by the offering party.

Tide-Reader Nesh, on her one visit, was seated at a ninth-fire. She did
not contest. The keepers, asked, said: *the deep-letters are colder
than we are.*

### Tuesday morning

The cohort wakes by hearth-warmth, not clock. The hearth-keeper banks
the night fire to a kept ember whose temperature falls predictably;
members rise as their sleeping-spaces drop below the personal warmth
threshold each negotiated at apprenticeship. A Thermoclave does not own
an alarm.

The morning's first act is the **carry** — every active member takes
ember from night hearth to day hearth in a single procession. Children
walk the route to learn it. A dropped carry is the smallest civic event
of the week; the dropper buys the hearth-keeper a gift in the next
market.

Breakfast cooks on the day hearth, shared. Many mornings are silent in
the *banked* sense. The hearth-keeper then banks the day hearth;
members disperse to the cohort's surface-occupation. The hearth is
*tended* by whomever is nearest, but *banking* is always the
hearth-keeper's.

Visitors are welcomed at the day hearth, never the night. A Brine guest
receives salt for their drink; a Concord guest the floor across the
hearth; a Choir tendril, soil-side seating (most hearths have a Choir
patch by mutual arrangement); a Curationist a chair, not spoken to
until they have written their first line — a joke the Curation consider
*welcoming.*

*Food:* whatever the keeper was *keeping* — bread held over the pit's
slow side, root-vegetables charred under ash, salt-wood smoke
flavouring everything. They do not eat anything cold; a cold meal is a
keeper's failure, taken to the *cold-fire* (an unkept pit on the camp's
edge) and burnt before sunrise.

*Greeting:* an open palm extended near the visitor's face, for the
visitor to feel body-warmth. A cold palm is a confession; a hot palm is
a claim.

*Mourning:* the body is burned at the *quench-fire.* The ashes are
mixed with the sand at the bottom of every working pit in the camp.
Every fire the camp keeps thereafter has a measurable mass of the dead
in it. *Cold-flame forbids tending a fire-of-memory; it does not forbid
remembering at a kept fire.*

*Currency:* none formal. Fuel — *armfuls* — is the unit. A favour can
be paid in three armfuls of dry pine. Wealth is dryness.

### The kept ash

The corpus's first lore-object that travels by warmth. The **kept ash**
of a properly-quenched fire retains heat below the ambient temperature
of the matter that holds it. A pouch of kept ash is faintly warm; the
warmer it is, the more recently the fire was kept. Carried by a
non-Thermoclave, the ash will *find its keeper* — kept-ash from one
hearth, brought near another, registers to the second hearth's keeper
as a small rise in the ember-floor's reading. This is how the
multi-grammar pocketbook (a Concord caravan-master's day-book containing
charcoal marginalia in count-grammar) was identified, in the end, as a
Thermoclave document Concord scribes had been holding without realising.

- **Sixth-Vell-Keeping, the Three-Gap Keeper.** Eighty-one years of

  long-second, the short-fifth, the folded-third.* Other keepers no

## §5 — The Glassblown Remnant

The Glassblown are the silence-faction. They were burned. They emerged
from the burning as something else — slumped into glass-like surfaces,
some bright, some opaque, some still warm. They do not write histories.
They do not give testimony. *They make markers.*

### The Burning, doctrinally

JSON:481 routes *the Burning* to the Glassblown, not to the
Thermoclaves. The fire-faction does not own its own cataclysm-name.
Resolvable if the Glassblown were burned *into* glass — survivors of an
event the Thermoclaves ritualise but the Glassblown were *the fuel of.*
That also explains why the Glassblown refuse to speak further: it is
indecent to ask a fuel to describe being burnt. The Glassblown have not
forgiven the Thermoclaves' ancestors; the form their unforgiveness
takes is *not trading with Thermoclaves before the unfiring has
completed.*

### The unfired state — a state-claim, not a stratum

The Glassblown make no substrate-claim. Their cosmology is not *the
world is X* but *the world is in state Y, and Y is reversible until
fired.* The state is **unfired** — the world before its own firing, the
cataclysm-event read as the world's *first irreversible firing.* What
was *unfired* (world-before) became *fired* (cataclysm); what is now
*unfiring* (the four-hundred-year aftermath) is the world *trying to
fold back the firing.*

The Glassblown do not believe the unfiring will succeed. The doctrine
specifically forbids the hope: *a fired shape cannot be unmade.* To
hope for re-batching is to deny the firing's reality, which is to deny
the cataclysm. The doctrinal posture is *we know it is fired; we work
as though it were not.* The discipline is the work; the hope is not
part of the discipline.

The state-claim is *deliberately not a stratum*: a cosmology of
*refused foundation* is the necessary opposite to one of *every kept
relationship at kept heat.* Glassblown-Thermoclave is the corpus's
first explicit anti-symmetry of cosmologies.

### The unsaid mood — fourth verb mood

Glassblown grammar has a fourth verb mood alongside indicative,
imperative, and interrogative: the **unsaid mood.** It takes a verb and
converts it into an action that was not performed, a sentence not
finished, or a question not asked — and *marks this as positive content*
of the sentence. Outsiders feel they missed half of every sentence; in
fact every half is present.

The mood is formed by suffixing the verb with **-han** (from *handle,*
the moment a glass-shape is released from the maker's tongs and is,
briefly, no one's). Examples:

- *I went to the well.* — indicative.
- *I went-han to the well.* — unsaid. The speaker considered and did
  not. The going is the content; the refusal is the punctuation.
- *Did she ask-han the question?* — interrogative-unsaid compound; used
  to ask whether another performed an *intentional* silence.

The unsaid mood does not require justification. To press for
clarification is impolite. The reader *lets the unsaid stand as said.*

Field 5 of the grief-marker (*WHAT WAS NOT SAID*) is the ritualised
written form. The blanks elsewhere are unsaid-mood entries, recorded
by their refusal to be entered.

### Firedness

Glassblown verbs decline by **firedness.** Three forms: *unfired*
(paste — reversible), *firing* (the present moment of commitment),
*fired* (irreversible). A fourth — *re-batched,* the verb of unfiring —
is doctrinally taboo to use of one's own actions; it is reserved for
narrative of others, often pejoratively.

The unsaid mood is the **silent firedness** — a verb-form left
incomplete by intention. *I am ___ -shaping the agreement* (the
particle-slot indicated by a pause) marks an action whose firedness is
*deliberately not yet declared.* The pause is the mood. It is
grammatically required to be of a specific length — long enough to be
heard, short enough not to invite a question. Apprentices learn the
length over their first three years.

The Concord's apprentice-catechism Q.12 — *Decline declined trades
politely* — is now retrospectively legible as *use the unsaid mood; the
politeness is the pause-length.*

### The deferred shape

The Glassblown do not narrate the cataclysm. *Firing,* in their grammar,
is the act by which a thing is made irreversible — to narrate the
cataclysm would be to commit a particular telling to permanence. The
Glassblown decline.

When asked, a Glassblown answers with a **deferred shape** — a passage
of glass-paste held between the speaker's hands while the question is
being answered, then folded back into the next batch without firing.
The questioner is invited to watch the shape rise and dissolve. The
Glassblown consider the watching the answer; the questioner usually
does not.

Two deferred shapes are recorded across the corpus, each by a different
witness:

1. *A near-sphere with a single inward dimple, held longer than the
   answer would have taken to speak.* (Recorded by a Brine sailor's
   marginalia.)
2. *No shape at all. The Glassblown's hands stayed still around empty
   paste for the duration of the question, then folded the paste back
   unfired.* (Recorded by a Thermoclave apprentice on her first
   cross-faction errand.)

The Thermoclaves consider the second the more honest. The Glassblown
have not commented on which is canonical because *commenting would be
firing.*

### The unfired letter

The Glassblown communicate at distance through **unfired letters** —
small parcels of glass-paste, shaped by the sender, sealed in wet
cloth, carried by hand. The recipient unfolds the paste, reads the
shape (Glassblown literacy is shape-literacy: ~400 shapes, taught by
apprenticeship), and returns the parcel within a measured time. If the
recipient agrees, they **fire the shape,** sealing the agreement. If
they disagree, they **fold it back** into their own batch and return
the paste at next visit.

The Glassblown call this *correspondence-by-refusal.* A Glassblown's
archive is composed entirely of fired shapes received from others —
agreements they have made — and contains no record of disagreements,
declined queries, or letters whose senders never followed up. The
asymmetry is doctrinal: a record of disagreement would be, by the
firing-doctrine, *firing the disagreement,* which is exactly the act
the Glassblown refuse.

### Etymology of *kept*

The Glassblown verb meaning *deferred without forfeit.* Refused paste
is **kept** — not destroyed, not committed, held in a state where the
next paste-mother can refuse it again. Reflexive: paste *keeps itself*
in the keeping; the Glassblown only attend.

### The Six-Almost

The longest kept paste on record. Six paste-batches each begun by a
different Glassblown across two centuries; each refused into the same
vessel-of-keeping. The vessel itself has not been fired. The paste is
*one paste* — the six refusals fold each batch back into the same
matter — and yet it *remembers* each refusal. A Glimpse-attuned reader
has reported six layers of under-text, one per refusal. The Curation
has, by protocol, not intaken it. The Concord's priorless cabinet holds
a clause about it never signed.

Tulen is the current keeper. She has refused into the Six-Almost twice
— refusals five and six. She does not say what shape. The Six-Almost
is, by Glassblown grammar, **a letter** addressed *to the next
paste-mother who will refuse into it.* Six refusals, six addressees who
became refusers, two centuries, never sealed.

### The grief-marker

A Glassblown grief-marker is a palm-sized round of cured glass with six
numbered fields etched into it. The fields are filled out by the
mourner, in glass-paste, on the day the marker is fired. Most fields
are left blank. The blanks are the testimony.

```
   1. NAME OF THE LOST:    [blank]
   2. TIME OF THE LOSS:    *the Burning. eleventh hour, second day.*
   3. WHAT REMAINS:        *the right hand. an unbroken cup.*
   4. WHAT WAS SAID:       [blank]
   5. WHAT WAS NOT SAID:   *the question the lost was asking when
                            the heat came. it was a small question.
                            it was for me. I have not asked it of
                            anyone else. I will not.*
   6. WHO TENDED:          *I tended. I was unable to keep. the
                            Thermoclave keeper offered. I refused.
                            this loss is not for keeping.*
```

The Glassblown record absence as a *positive datum* — Field 5 is the
ritual centerpiece. The Burning has a specific time-stamp (*eleventh
hour, second day*) — the corpus's first dated cataclysm-event. Markers
are made in numbered batches by titled artisans (*the second-shape*
implies a first and third, possibly anatomical — shaped *second* in the
Burning). The Thermoclaves *offered to tend* and were refused —
two fire-adjacent factions on the same metaphysics in moral opposition
(Thermoclaves keep; Glassblown refuse to keep).

The Concord *will not sell* fired markers; the only currency they
accept for one is another grief.

### There is no first-shape

The Glassblown's batches are continuous from round 4099 (the
second-shape's earliest documented mark) onward. There is no first-
shape. No batch in any tier of any of the Curation's vaults bears the
maker's mark of *first-shape.* Three Glassblown traders, asked, have
each answered with the *handshake silence* the Glassblown call a
greeting — answering in the unsaid mood.

Possibilities: the first-shape is the burned themselves (the founding
generation, who *became* glass and so cannot also have *made* it); the
first-shape is doctrinally unfileable for the same reason the
Thermoclaves' tenth fire is uncatchable; the first-shape is the
Glassblown word for *that which was not made* — the unfiring's mood
applied to the maker-numbering itself, so that *there is no first-shape*
is itself a sentence in the unsaid mood.

The Curation has, in its own vault, **four Glassblown first-shapes** —
contradicting both the Glassblown doctrine (*there is no first-shape*)
and the Curation's own catalogue. The first-shapes are not absent —
they are *unfiled,* warm, and have been moving through the Curation's
rebinding rota for longer than living memory. The doctrinal claim of
absence and the material fact of presence are both true. The corpus's
first flat contradiction-as-cosmic-feature.

### The unfiring (apotheosis)

A Glassblown at highest reputation may petition for *unfiring.* The
petition is shaped, not spoken — the petitioner forms the
*requesting-form* (the four-hundredth shape in the literacy, almost
never used elsewhere) and presents it to the elder shaper. The elder
watches the shape rise and, if they accept, **fires it** publicly. The
fired requesting-form is then **smashed** in the same firing-cycle,
before the kiln has cooled — the only ceremonial breaking of a fired
shape in the doctrine, considered the most painful act in their craft.
The smashing is the petition's *granting.*

The petitioner then enters a long batch-cycle: no longer a working
Glassblown, they assist the next batch's preparation — sand-sorting,
paste-mixing, never shaping. After the batch is mixed, the petitioner
adds themselves to it, literally: a single drop of their blood is mixed
into the paste. The batch is then poured, shaped by other Glassblown
into ordinary objects (cups, lenses, vessels), fired, and distributed.
The petitioner walks away from the firing.

The unfired Glassblown is a person whose **substance is in
circulation.** Wherever a piece of that batch is used, the unfired
Glassblown is — in their doctrine — *attending the use without
intervening.* They are not in the cup; they are *what the cup is, by a
fraction.* Unfiring surrenders **the discipline:** the Glassblown has
committed, ceremonially, the firing they spent their life refusing.

### The unfiring (daily ritual)

Every Glassblown wakes before sunrise and performs the *unfiring.* They
sit at their workshop door, facing whichever direction the previous
day's wind came from, and name the absences from the previous day in
the unsaid mood — *I did-han see Renne. I finished-han the third
marker. I ate-han with my second-shape.* The naming is private; if
overheard, the listener gives the speaker a half-turn of distance.

A Glassblown who skips the unfiring for three days running is
*over-fired* — too much of yesterday carried into today, the kiln of
the self running too hot — and their work is suspended. To return, they
must unfire in front of a single witness. The witness does not respond,
does not nod, does not breathe in any pattern that could be read as
encouragement. The witness *receives the unsaid.* After, they leave
without speaking.

The unfiring is the inverse of the Thermoclave's fire-keeping. Where
the Thermoclave begins each day *holding* fire, the Glassblown begins
each day naming what was *not* held. Both are organised around what was
not done. Thermoclaves call this failure (*-cold*); Glassblown call
this content (*-han*).

### Unfired Tuesday

A Glassblown settlement has no scheduled Tuesday observance. A
*scheduled* observance is, by doctrine, a *fired* one — the schedule
commits the future to a particular shape. The Glassblown therefore
observe on **unscheduled Tuesdays:** any given Tuesday, an elder may
declare the day's first uninterrupted hour as the *batch-hour,* and
any Glassblown present folds whatever they are working on back into
paste, joins the elder at the kiln, and participates in a single shared
shape — formed by hands around the circle, each adding one motion
before passing the paste to the next, until the shape is complete or
the hour ends.

The shape is not fired. At hour's end, the elder folds it back into
batch.

The Concord, told of unfired Tuesday, found it *unaccountable* — in
trade-tense, an event that may or may not occur on any Tuesday is
un-priceable in advance — and schedules Glassblown-facing trades for
Wednesdays.

### Tuesday morning

*Food:* no morning meal — the morning belongs to *unfiring.* First
meal is at midday, taken alone beside the workshop's kiln, kept warm by
the previous day's coals.

*Greeting:* *one full breath of shared silence in earshot.* A Glassblown
who speaks before the breath completes has initiated a transaction;
silence is the social handshake, conversation its sequel.

*Naming:* a child is *the unfired* until they survive their first cold
weather, after which they receive a *shape-name* — the kind of vessel
they are first asked to attempt.

*Mourning:* the body is fired, partially, into a glass-shape placed in
the maker's own workshop. The dead inhabit the workshops of the living.
An older workshop with a dozen shapes on its high shelf is one the
maker has chosen to keep working in despite the weight.

*Currency:* *blanks* — uninscribed, unfired grief-markers, valued by
their roundness. Fired markers cannot be traded; the firing transmutes
the object out of the economy.

### Senior figure: Tulen Niemensa-Sopellen

The elder shaper of the Cool Kiln, the *paste-mother* of the southern
shaperies. Sixty-eight years a Glassblown. Tulen has fired eleven
shapes in that time, by her own unrequested admission — the next-fewest
senior record is over two hundred. Her doctrinal reputation is *the
shaper who refuses most.*

*Niemensa-Sopellen* is composed of two compound elements meaning, in
Glassblown shape-vocabulary, *the-shape-that-is-not-yet* and
*the-not-yet-naming-of-it.* She is the only living Glassblown whose
name is a deferred shape rendered into syllables. Three of her
predecessors bore similar names; all three committed the
*requesting-form* in their final year.

Tulen corresponds with no senior figure in any other faction — by
doctrine. She is, however, the only Glassblown whose unfired letters
the Pale Curation has systematically catalogued: every time she sends
paste, a Curation collector files a witness-card describing the shape.
Forty-one years of such cards; 137 in the cabinet; none describes a
fired shape.

Tulen's working belief: the cataclysm was the world's *first refusal to
be folded back.* The world-before was paste. The cataclysm was firing.
The aftermath is *the long discovery that the firing holds.* The
Glassblown work as though the firing might still be foldable — knowing
it is not — because *the discipline of folding is what keeps something
else from being fired.* Asked what that *something else* is, Tulen
forms a deferred shape and folds it back. The Curation has fourteen
such answers on record; they are the fourteen most-requested cards in
the cabinet.

# PART VI — COSMOLOGY, STRATA, INTERFACES

## The substrate

Beneath everything — the **substrate.** A foundation beneath matter,
energy, and time. The pre-cataclysm machines touched it, cracked it,
left it leaking. Choir got awareness from the leak. Palimpsest got
temporal sight. The factions disagree on what it is and on whether the
cracks are closing, opening, or stable.

| Faction | Name for the substrate | What it implies |
|---|---|---|
| **Choir** | *the substrate* | Bedrock under bedrock. |
| **Palimpsest** | *the page* | A surface that takes ink. |
| **Pale Curation** | *the medium* | (In the archivist's sense — what one does not control but must respect.) |
| **Saccharine Concord** | *the floor of the market* | Sold-on, never sold. |
| **Brine Communion** | *the deep* | Undifferentiated from the sea. |
| **Thermoclaves** | *the slag* | A residue, not a foundation. |
| **Glassblown** | (no word) | The world's *unfired state* is a state-claim, not a stratum. |

## The six strata

The corpus has named six distinct strata, each owned by a different
faction:

| # | Stratum | Faction | What it is |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Material | Choir | Bedrock, soil, mycelium-permeable matter. The Choir digests forward. |
| 2 | Temporal | Palimpsest | Layered text. The Palimpsest reads backward. |
| 3 | Consensual | Concord | Value held *between* parties. The cataclysm could not break a thing that lived between people. |
| 4 | Standing | Concord (secondary) | The world considered as the medium that made transactions possible. The standing line is owed a settlement-fee. |
| 5 | Held-depth | Brine | Stillness sufficient to hold a body, an object, or a promise. |
| 6 | Ember-floor / Kept ember | Thermoclave | Heat persisting in the absence of visible fuel. Heat above warms; heat below *keeps.* |

Plus a seventh, structurally:

| 7 | The unfired (state-claim) | Glassblown | A *deliberately not a stratum.* The world's reversibility-until-fired. |

The Thermoclave's sixth is the only **negative** stratum (absence of
foundation as positive doctrine); the Glassblown's seventh is the only
**state-claim** (a quality of the world, not a layer of it).

## Stratum interfaces — six concrete couplings

1. **Choir / Palimpsest.** The Choir composts palimpsest-bearing matter
   into *humus.* The lower margin survives in Choir metabolism as a
   chemical signature the Palimpsest still reads. *The Palimpsest reads
   the Choir's compost.* The Choir does not know this.
2. **Concord / Thermoclave.** A priced clause settled against
   substrate-borne value is *closed* only when the keeper agrees the
   fire holds. The standing-floor correspondence is the ratification.
   Without it, every prior such settlement is *technically un-rendered.*
3. **Brine / Thermoclave.** A walker seated at a Thermoclave ninth-fire
   and committed at a Brine going-deep within one season is recorded by
   the substrate as **doubly-kept** — no analogue in any other grammar.
   The Palimpsest reads doubly-kept walkers as *thicker than usual.*
4. **Curation / All.** The intake form is the only document that crosses
   every substrate unmodified. R-7's *witness unspecified by document
   convention* form accepts pages from each stratum. The Curation is
   the only faction whose grammar runs on every floor. The Director
   suspects this is dangerous; R-7 has been asked to reduce intake;
   R-7 has not.
5. **Glassblown / All.** *Deliberately-not-said* refuses interface.
   Vessels are accepted everywhere and interpreted nowhere. The craft
   *passes through* without sharing.
6. **Palimpsest / All.** See (1). The only stratum that reads *every
   other stratum's residue* as text.

The Curation has been the corpus's most load-bearing minor faction
because it is *the only neutral grammar* — the only faction whose
grammar runs on every floor.

## The substrate-leak as cause

The pre-cataclysm machines cracked the substrate. The Choir got
awareness. The Palimpsest got temporal sight. Cause → effect, internally
coherent. The Choir's awareness is *contingent* on the leak (fungus that
*woke*). The Palimpsest is the cataclysm itself, the imperfect-erasure.

The Choir asserts the Palimpsest *seeks to seal the cracks.* The
Palimpsest does not. The Choir misreads the Palimpsest as a repair
effort because the Choir, having no concept of *witness for its own
sake,* cannot imagine why anyone would gather memories without intending
to *use* them.

## Inheritance — does it pass to children?

The Choir node does not pass through the germline. Choir mother and
walker father produce a walker child; the Choir is unbothered, *we are
recruited, not bred.* The two recorded cases of two-Choir-parent
children — both born ordinary, recruited later.

Palimpsest sight is different. It *occasionally* passes — what the Pale
Curation calls a **bleed-line.** Three confirmed: a child born
partial-Glimpsing; a child born with an echo of an ancestor's memory
two generations back; a child born only able to see the Glimpse layer
and not the present (this third did not survive infancy — *the child
read forward only, which is also a kind of reading,* says the
Palimpsest).

Brine attunement is environmental; the under-tide does not recognise the
unborn. Thermoclave fire-keeping is initiatory. Glassblown shape-naming
is taught. Curation employment is contractual. Choir is recruitive,
Palimpsest is bleed-leaky, the rest are elective. The asymmetry maps
onto each faction's relationship to time.

## Time-rate of the world's surfaces

A surface no one is reading is not *blank.* It is **between readings.**
Both layers persist. A reader's arrival activates the surface. Between
readings, the surface drifts at a rate proportional to how long since a
reading toward a pressure-equalised state.

- **Disuse rate.** A surface unread for a generation is *thin*; one
  unread for a millennium is **flat** — the layers have equalised.
- **Digestion rate.** A Choir-digested surface flattens at the rate of
  digestion, not of disuse — forty seasons, not a millennium.

A reader who Glimpses a digesting surface in its last week of legibility
may **read the layers' final equalisation.** The bible's first rule for
a single fact both factions accept: forced flattening is legible exactly
once, at the seam. A reading taken at the seam is **bilingual** — it
returns in both grammars simultaneously, in the open tense, in the
collective pronoun. The reader understands without being able to choose
which faction's account to file. Curation surveyors who take seam-
readings file them on a separate card-stock — the *un-grammared card.*

# PART VII — APOTHEOSES

The corpus has progressed from the bible's two canonical apotheoses
(Communion and Transcription) to nine recognised end-states or stable
intermediates, plus several edge cases.

## Master table

| Apotheosis | Faction | Surrenders | Mechanic |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Communion** | Choir | Self | Walker enters chemical-gradient room, stands three days, becomes a fruiting body in the wall. |
| **Transcription** | Palimpsest | Presence | Walker becomes a memory written into the page; requires a witness. |
| **Closure** | Concord | Price | One named debt closed without remainder; walker thereafter unledgered, unregistered by every faction's reputation system. |
| **The Balanced Account** | Concord (rumoured) | All open ledgers | Walker becomes a closed party, doctrinally exempt from further consensus. Two attempts, both stopped. |
| **Going-deep** | Brine | Surface | Walker swims to held depth and remains; replies become deep-letters. |
| **Long quench / Long warming** | Thermoclave | Warmth / ongoing-attention | Walker tends a fire alone until ember-floor passes through them, becomes a floor-keeper or *gracefully uncounted.* |
| **Unfiring** | Glassblown | The discipline | Walker's blood mixed into a batch; substance enters circulation as cups, lenses, vessels. |
| **The Long Throat** | Choir intermediate | (not the goal) | Long-individuated fruiting body. Voice retained metabolically. Mogu, Grib, Nam, Sien, Sopp. |
| **The Re-pressed** | Palimpsest intermediate | (not the goal) | Reader stabilised mid-change with both layers legible in the same body. |
| **The doubled mouth** | Both at once | (neither) | A Re-pressed reader absorbed by the Choir; speaks collective + layered tense. |

## Annihilist endings — the third question

A player at sufficient antagonism with both Choir and Palimpsest can
attempt one of two refusals.

- **The Salting.** The First Root is reachable. So is enough salt. The
  Choir has no defence the bible has been told about. The Choir does
  not die — it retreats. The world wakes the next morning poorer in
  awareness by an amount no faction can quite measure. NPCs sleep
  differently. Some dreams stop arriving.
- **The Closing.** The Annotation power, used inversely, writes a
  single instruction across every layer at once: *do not remember.*
  Temporal bleed-through stops. Echo Reading goes silent. The world
  becomes legible the way the present is legible — flat, single-
  layered, settled. The player loses every Palimpsest power, including
  reading their own past saves' history. The game does not warn them.

Both refusals are unambiguously worse for the player than either
apotheosis. They are the cost of treating the central question as a
third question.

Note: Closure (Concord) is *not* the Annihilist ending. Annihilist ends
the walker outside every faction's metaphysics — a *true* end. Closure
ends the walker only outside every faction's *accounting*; the walker
continues to exist. Annihilist becomes the path for walkers who attempt
Closure incorrectly — paid the price, named the wrong debt.

## The stalled walkers

Some walkers accept the Choir node and never proceed. They are not
refusers — they have been changed — but they are not pilgrims either.
The Choir calls them *the patient ones* and is genuinely patient; the
spores feed them quietly for years and ask nothing. Other walkers see
them in the small hours, sitting still longer than people are usually
still. They sleep less. They remember conversations they did not have.
They are not unhappy. They are also not coming back to the question.

The same shape exists on the Palimpsest side. Walkers who learn Echo
Reading and stop there are called *the listening ones.* They touch
walls before entering rooms, apologise to objects for moving them,
pause at thresholds long enough that other walkers go around them. Also
not unhappy. Also not finished.

The design rule that the moderate path should feel like cowardice does
not apply to the stalled. Cowardice requires a choice the stalled have
already made, halfway, and not unmade.

## Threshold conditions

**Threshold of Communion.** The Choir cannot consume an unwilling node.
It also cannot consume a willing node that is not *bodily present in a
chemical-gradient room of the First Root.* The player must walk in,
alone, sober, and stand in the prepared gradient long enough for the
spores to map their nerve geometry onto a fruiting body still unborn in
the wall. The Choir will narrate this honestly throughout. The player
can leave at any point before the third day. After the third day the
leaving is metaphorical.

**Threshold of Transcription.** The Palimpsest cannot transcribe a
player whose own present is still louder than the past they have read.
The player must have completed enough Echo Readings that their dream
sequences are no longer recognisably their own. The transcription itself
requires a witness — a single living being who agrees to remember the
player after they are written into the page. Without a witness the
player simply… persists, indistinct, neither fully present nor fully
archived. The Palimpsest considers this the worst outcome, but does
not interfere.

The witness requirement makes Transcription *social* in a way Communion
isn't, which fits each faction's nature.

## Failed apotheoses

**A Communion that does not take.** The walker enters the chemical-
gradient room and stands. The Choir does not accept them — for reasons
it does not give and may not itself know. The walker emerges after
~three days with the node *muted*: passive sensing dead, healing dead,
the Tendril cold in the hand. They are known as *the un-mouths.* Some
return to Choir ground compulsively; the Choir tolerates them and does
not feed them. The Curation has noted un-mouths weigh slightly less
than they did before the attempt. No one has measured what they weigh
less of.

**A Transcription that does not complete.** The witness sees the player
go thin and *stay thin* — neither sentence nor body. They remain in the
room, somewhat present, somewhat readable. They can be spoken to and
answer in voices that do not belong to them — the room's previous
occupants, in succession. The Palimpsest may not consider this a
failure; it may consider the walker *successfully annotated,
incompletely transcribed* — a marker rather than a sentence. They
cannot be moved.

## Refused apotheoses

A walker offered the node and declining is, in the Choir's grammar, *a
guest who has eaten and not yet eaten* — not insulted, not rejected,
not absent. The Choir holds the offer open indefinitely; *patience* is
its primary virtue and refusal is simply the offer ungrounded.
Subsequent dialogue with that walker shifts subtly: the *we* loses its
envelopment and becomes *almost-formal* — closer to the Curation's
register than to earlier warmth.

A walker offered Transcription and declining is, in the Palimpsest's
grammar, *a sentence chosen not to be written.* The moment passes, and
the layering at that location *thickens slightly* in a way readable by
other Palimpsest acolytes for years. The walker has left a *non-mark*:
a place where the palimpsest was offered the page and the page declined.

The walker who declines both offers is simply who they were before. The
rarest disposition the bible records — neither integration nor
transcription nor annihilation. It has no name in the corpus.

## Pulled apotheoses

A walker who has not yet asked for Communion can be *taken* by it if
they die at the right depth on Choir ground while carrying a high-tier
Tendril. The Choir does not consider this a failure of consent; from
inside its grammar, the walker had been on the threshold for some time
and the death only timed it. The walker's Communion is, however,
*unannotated*: the rooms and gradients assigned to a deliberate walker
are not assigned. Other Choir voices report a *new harmonic that
arrives without introduction* and accommodate it by widening their
chord very slightly.

A walker who has not yet asked for Transcription can be *pulled in* by
the Palimpsest if they die at the right depth on layered ground while
carrying a high-tier Manuscript. The Palimpsest's pulled-in are filed
at *the wrong stratum* — not at the layer matching their life but at
the layer matching their last ten breaths. They appear, to Echo Readers
a year later, as fragments correctly placed in time and *incorrectly
placed in self.* A reader hearing a pulled-in walker reports *meeting a
stranger who was using my own name habitually.*

Both pulled forms cost the surrounding system something the deliberate
forms do not — the Choir loses the walker's *introduction* and the
Palimpsest loses the walker's *coherence.* Pulled apotheoses are why
both systems *prefer* deliberate ones.

## Mid-apotheosis interruption

**The half-said.** A walker extracted from the Transcription chamber
before completion. They emerge speaking twice: the surface sentence in
their own voice, and a second sentence beneath it, audible but not
phonologically present — listeners report having heard it but cannot
quote it back. The half-said cannot be lied to; the second sentence
corrects the first if it was sincere but wrong, and refuses the first
if it was a lie. They do not consider this a power. They consider it a
*condition.*

**The half-tasted.** A walker extracted from the chemical-gradient room
before integration. The node is partially threaded — Stage 1 sensing
works at random; the player feels someone else's hunger or warmth at
unpredictable moments and cannot distinguish their own from the
network's. The Choir calls them *the leaky-mouths.* About half return
unprompted within three years; the Choir does not stop them.

The mirror holds: half-said carries the Palimpsest's burden of
correcting *what was said*; half-tasted, the Choir's burden of sharing
*what was felt.*

## Reversal

**The Recovered Mouth.** A walker fully integrated into the Choir but
*cut out* — by another faction's intervention, by a deliberate excision
the Choir performed for reasons it does not share. The walker
re-emerges with their original name, their original body, and a hole in
their interior where the network used to be. They cannot be re-
integrated. The Choir will not have them back; the network "remembers
refusing them" and refusing again is easier than reading them. They
retain Stage 1–2 powers and nothing further. They are functionally
*Choir-allergic for life.* Curators seek them out; few will speak.

**The Returned Sentence.** A Palimpsest-Transcribed walker who, after
some triggering event — usually the destruction of the room they were
transcribed into — is *spoken* back into a body. They are not the
walker who left. They are *what the room remembered of them.* The fit
is approximate. Long-time companions report the returned walker is
*correctly themselves at a coarse grain* but missing small
idiosyncrasies — the way they held a spoon, a phrase they always used.
The walker, if asked, often agrees and finds this fact uninteresting.
The interest is gone with the lost details.

## Apotheoses observed from outside

**A Communion observed.** A Pale Curation surveyor, present by accident
or assignment, sees the player walk into a chemical-gradient room and
stand. After roughly three days the player is no longer where they were
last seen; the wall in that location is fractionally thicker; the
gradient has a new emotional register the surveyor cannot name. There is
no body. The Curation files this under *Subject Disposition: Aggregated.*
Surveyors describe the soil afterward as *less talkative* for several
weeks. The Choir would say: *we were listening to our new mouth.*

**A Transcription observed.** The Palimpsest requires a witness. The
witness sees the player go thin and become *an earlier sentence in the
room.* The witness's later memory of the moment is unstable: sure it
happened; not sure where they were standing; sure of the player's name;
not sure who told them the name. The Choir, if a tendril is in the
wall, says the player tasted *thin and far away* in the seconds before
— and cannot taste the Transcription itself, only its absence after.

Communion leaves a thicker wall and a quieter soil (additive).
Transcription leaves an unstable witness (subtractive, deferred).

## Erro's contested apotheosis

Erro, keeper of the tenth fire at the cataclysm-quench, is recorded by
the Thermoclaves as *fifth-out* — the only known cataclysm-era
*fifth-out* the chain has produced. A *quenched apotheosis*: the
surrender of warmth at the moment the warmth could not be transferred,
because the substrate had been broken.

The Pale Curation classifies Erro as a *retired-into-cabinet* — a
Curation apotheosis, on the grounds that the Curation holds the only
paper trace of Erro's existence after the quench (a letter-fragment
Erro had begun to a sister no longer living). Suul has been told the
fragment exists. She has not asked to read it.

Both classifications stand, never challenged in writing. The
standing-floor correspondence is the only document on which the two
readings would have to be reconciled, and it is filed under R-7's
untranslatability annotation. The corpus's first apotheosis with two
faction-classifications.

## What each faction calls joining

- **Rot Choir:** *the long taste.* Not an event but a duration the
  joiner gradually stops noticing.
- **Palimpsest:** *the first reading.* The moment a person first reads
  themselves as a layer rather than as a person.
- **Pale Curation:** *accession.* Same verb used for objects.
- **Saccharine Concord:** *first sweetness.* The newcomer is paid a
  single piece of candied bark. Eating it is the contract. Refusing it
  is also the contract — the Concord has shipped both forms.
- **Brine Communion:** *the going-under.* New members spend a night
  submerged in a sealed tide-pool with one Communion member who does
  not speak, only breathes nearby.
- **Thermoclaves:** *first quench.* A new member is briefly held under
  cold water by an older one. The older one decides when.

Brine *going-under* and Thermoclave *quench* mirror — same element,
opposite doctrines.

## What each faction calls being seen

- **Choir:** *the taste-on-the-air.* The opening line of
  `ChoirTendril_1` is not a greeting — it is the noun.
- **Palimpsest:** *the first reading.* Tense ambiguity is structural.
- **Pale Curation:** *the survey hit.* The phrasing refuses the moment
  any drama.
- **Saccharine Concord:** *the rate notice.* To the Concord, becoming
  legible *is* becoming priceable.
- **Brine Communion:** *the deep-look.* Used only in the passive: *I
  was deep-looked at the third tide.*
- **Thermoclaves:** *the eye-of-fire.* Spoken once and not again.
- **Glassblown:** *the half-naming.* The kiln registers the shape it is
  about to refuse or accept.

## What each faction calls the apotheoses

**Communion (the Choir's ending):**

- Palimpsest: *the silent reading* — a page that fed itself back into
  the spine.
- Pale Curation: *Subject Disposition: Aggregated* — filed under the
  same code as items lost to the wall.
- Concord: *the unrecoverable purchase* — the buyer walked through the
  door and the door was the seller.
- Brine: *the going-down that did not return at tide.*
- Thermoclaves: *the long quench* — same verb as their initiation, with
  a duration-particle. The rite is brief; the apotheosis is not.

**Transcription (the Palimpsest's ending):**

- Choir: *the reader-eaten-by-the-page.* Not horror — mild surprise.
  The Choir thought eating only ran one way.
- Pale Curation: *Subject Disposition: Annexed by Source.*
- Concord: *the contract that ate its own counterparty.* Concord legal
  scholars consider this a category error and refuse the metaphor.
- Brine: *the writing-into-the-water.*
- Thermoclaves: *the cold flame* — a fire that gives no heat is a fire
  being remembered, not kept. They consider this dishonest and do not
  tend it.

## What each faction calls remembering

- **Choir:** *to be tasted again.* The same nutrient through a different
  mouth. No word for *to remember in vain.*
- **Palimpsest:** *to read backward.* Recursive; intransitive — one
  *reads backward,* and what is read is what is read.
- **Pale Curation:** *to retrieve.* A filing operation with two
  arguments, cabinet and index.
- **Saccharine Concord:** *to settle.* Memory is debt-resolution.
  Forgetting is theft from the self.
- **Brine Communion:** *to surface.* The same word used for a body that
  has come up after a wreck.
- **Thermoclaves:** *to relight.* Two suffixes — *relit briefly* and
  *kept lit.*
- **Glassblown:** *to second-fire.* What survives the second fire is
  what is remembered; what does not was, by doctrine, *not remembered
  to begin with.*

A Choir-acolyte and a Palimpsest-acolyte both saying *I remember the old
grove* are not, in their own languages, talking about the same thing.

## What each faction calls debt

- **Concord:** *the open ledger.* Neutral. The default condition.
- **Choir:** *the unfed.* Concord coin is *food postponed.*
- **Palimpsest:** *the unwritten line.* A long-overdue account *flickers*
  the way an unstable Glimpse layer does.
- **Pale Curation:** *the open file.* Closing it is clerical; morally
  weightless.
- **Brine Communion:** *the held breath.* Cleared at the turn of each
  tide.
- **Thermoclaves:** *cold-iron.* Iron not yet forged. They do not lend.
- **Glassblown Remnant:** *the unfired.* Closing the account *fires*
  it; the closing is the making.

## What each faction calls a promise

- **Choir:** *a held breath.* The network suspends a small portion of
  itself. A broken Choir-promise is a *stale gradient*; the network
  reabsorbs it.
- **Palimpsest:** *a foreshadowing.* Read backward as already kept.
  Breaking it introduces a *misreading*; the layer becomes harder to
  read.
- **Pale Curation:** *a rider.* A clause appended to a filed card.
- **Saccharine Concord:** *a held rate.* The strongest Concord-promise
  includes its own settlement date.
- **Brine Communion:** *a set tide.* A Brine-promise without a
  tide-clock is *a wish.*
- **Thermoclaves:** *a fire to be kept.* A literal small fire, lit at
  promising and tended until fulfilled.
- **Glassblown:** *an unfired shape.* Held as glass-paste, never fired.
  The shape can be *broken without breaking*: the glass is reabsorbed
  into the next batch and the promise is *unsaid.*

## What each faction calls trade / exchange

- **Saccharine Concord:** *to balance.* The verb is the doctrine. No
  separate word for *unbalanced trade* — that is *theft on one side and
  gift on the other.*
- **Pale Curation:** *to assign.* Curation traders are slow
  (unforgivably, says the Concord); every trade is a small cataloguing.
- **Choir:** *to share-mouth.* The same verb used for nutrient
  redistribution between fruiting bodies.
- **Palimpsest:** *to write across.* A trade is two annotations on the
  same place, made simultaneously.
- **Brine Communion:** *to deep-keep against deep-keep.* A *deep-kept*
  may not return; the trade is therefore not closeable.
- **Thermoclaves:** *to share fire.* A fire passed keeper to keeper with
  the fire still lit.
- **Glassblown:** *(unfired).* No verb in the unsaid mood; trade
  requires the fired register, entered only at midday.

*Balance* is the only one of the seven that treats exchange as
fundamentally *neutral.* The rest treat it as holy, asymmetric,
mistaken, or unspeakable until ritual readiness.

## What each faction calls the founder (Concord-specific)

- **Saccharine Concord:** *the Hand* — uppercase, ungendered, never
  personalised. The three stories disagree on tone (kind / firm /
  always-moving). The noun is constant.
- **Choir:** *the unfed mouth.* The Choir notes the founder-moment as
  *a hunger that did not end at the same time as the others.*
- **Palimpsest:** *the unredeemed sentence.* A founding act that left a
  sentence unfinished.
- **Pale Curation:** *Subject of file Reconciled-7B.* *Which is correct*
  is *out of scope.*
- **Brine Communion:** *the trader-on-the-shore.* The Brine suspect the
  founder is alive — *deep-kept,* not deceased.
- **Thermoclaves:** *the cold-hand.* The founder operated before the
  tenth-fire and is *uncounted.*
- **Glassblown:** the founder is *unsaid* — referenced in the unsaid
  mood. Neither confirmed nor denied.

Six factions, six founder-words, no two pointing at the same person.

## What each faction calls refusal

- **Choir:** *not yet.* The Choir has no permanent refusal — only
  deferral on a six-hundred-year clock.
- **Palimpsest:** *unread.* The page is not denied, only not turned.
  *Unread* and *forgotten* are not the same word.
- **Pale Curation:** *declined.* A formal stamp; a card is generated for
  the declination itself.
- **Saccharine Concord:** *declined-with-cause* / *declined-without-
  cause.* Without-cause is dearer to receive but cheaper to give.
- **Brine Communion:** *un-deeped.* The refusal is held at *not the
  right depth.*
- **Thermoclaves:** *cold-passed.* A refusal is the doctrinal twin of
  a chain-failure.
- **Glassblown:** *folded back.* The grammar requires that a refusal be
  performed *with the refusing party's own materials* — you cannot
  refuse on someone else's behalf, because you cannot fold someone
  else's paste back. Glassblown refusals are ceremonial.

## What each faction calls sleep

- **Choir:** *the slow uptake.* Sleep is metabolic.
- **Palimpsest:** *the unread page.* Dreams are pages turning on their
  own.
- **Pale Curation:** *off-shift.* No metaphor; logistical.
- **Saccharine Concord:** *closed for the night.* Reopens at dawn unless
  otherwise stated.
- **Brine Communion:** *gone-under-without-going.* Sleep is a small
  daily false going-deep.
- **Thermoclaves:** *to ember.* The fire is not out — held at the lowest
  legal heat. Takes the keep-particle: *I embered for six hours.*
- **Glassblown:** *(unfired pause.)* The faction does not formally
  distinguish sleep from refusal-to-shape.

Sleep is *what each faction's metaphysics looks like turned down.*

## What each faction calls the remainder / ash

- **Choir:** *humus.* Not a residue — a *station.*
- **Palimpsest:** *the lower margin.* Often the truer text.
- **Pale Curation:** *the after-fact.* No card closes without one.
- **Saccharine Concord:** *the held remainder.* What a settled account
  did not consume.
- **Brine Communion:** *the un-deeped fragment.* Treated with the
  dignity of the whole, separately.
- **Thermoclaves:** *the kept ash.* The difference between a fire that
  ended and a fire that was *let* to end. Stored in the keeper's pouch.
- **Glassblown:** *the unfired.* The sand that was almost a vessel and
  is, in their teaching, more itself than the vessel.

*Kept ash* and *un-deeped fragment* are the same noun in two grammars:
*the part that did not finish but is not abandoned.*

## What each faction calls silence

- **Choir:** *the long listening.* The network hears more when no
  fruiting body is speaking.
- **Palimpsest:** *the unread page.*
- **Pale Curation:** *the unfiled.* Temporary by definition; permanent
  silence is *over-deferring,* a junior fault.
- **Saccharine Concord:** *the held offer.* A price-state with terms
  and a clock.
- **Brine Communion:** *the under-tide.* The body of water itself;
  speech *interrupts* silence, never the reverse.
- **Thermoclaves:** *the banked.* Fire being kept rather than burned. A
  *cold* silence is the failed kind.
- **Glassblown:** *the unfired.* Silence and unmaking are *one
  register.*

The named Choir-voices speak silence differently: Sien's reads as *the
long listening at full volume*; Mogu's as *banked* (closest to the
Thermoclaves'); Grib's as *under-tide* — weight, like water above a
body; Nam's as *the held offer,* audibly waiting; Sopp's as *the unread
page.* Each named NPC's silence reads as another faction's silence-noun.

## What each faction calls the Choir's central act (digestion)

- **Pale Curation:** *un-cataloguing.* The only intake form the Curation
  cannot perform; reflexive and passive together — a record that
  un-catalogues itself in the eating.
- **Saccharine Concord:** *a price not paid.* No clause clears. No
  first-party remains to be paid.
- **Brine Communion:** *the fresh-tide.* The salt's *un-sea-ing.*
- **Thermoclaves:** *cold-feeding.* The eighth (the honest fire)
  refuses to *count* a cold-feeding.
- **Glassblown Remnant:** *unmaking-with-no-breath.* A loan from the
  Curation form. There is no native Glassblown grammar for the act.
- **Palimpsest:** *the page lost.* Not retired; not flat. *Lost.*
- **Choir's own word:** *taking.* No suffix. *We took. We are taking.
  We will take.* The collective does not regard the verb as needing
  adornment.

Seven grammars; six ethical valences; one operation. The Choir's verb
is shortest; the Palimpsest's most defeated; the Glassblown's the only
one that is a *loan.*

## What each faction calls the unsent

- **Choir:** *spore-not-yet-fallen.* Already in the network; the air
  has not yet carried it.
- **Palimpsest:** *the lower draft.* Visible in the under-text before
  the writer is finished.
- **Pale Curation:** *under-intake.* Filed as a parcel; no annotation
  until the addressee opens it. R-7 holds three.
- **Concord:** *un-rendered.* Priced but not closed; aging at the
  writer's own rate. Veshen's letter to Suul is at 61 days.
- **Brine:** *un-tide-borne.* Awaiting the tide that will carry it —
  not a delay; *the tide is the addressee, not the recipient.*
- **Thermoclaves:** *cold-paged.* The fire that would sign the keeping
  has not yet been kept.
- **Glassblown:** no word. *Unsent* collapses to *unfired.*

Three of six grammars treat *unsent* as a condition of *fire / tide /
substrate,* not of the writer.

## What each faction calls a Brine far from water

- **Brine (self):** *the dry-walking.* The held depth travels *with*
  the body in absentia. A Brine cannot be *un-deeped* by distance —
  only by silence.
- **Choir:** *a salt-in-the-soil.* The network tastes them through
  mineral signatures, considers them *kin,* does not propose
  absorption. *Salt does not compost.*
- **Palimpsest:** *the layered swimmer.* From inland, a Brine Glimpsing
  a coastal ruin sees their present (dry) and the home shore's tide
  overlaid.
- **Pale Curation:** *survey-detached.* The catalogue cannot file them
  by shore-of-record. Halen's note: *they file themselves.*
- **Saccharine Concord:** *the unsettled party.* Clauses with a
  dry-walking Brine require the *home-tide-clock* be cited in the
  witness section.
- **Thermoclaves:** *the wet fire.* Apprentice catechism: *a Brine far
  from the sea who has not gone silent.*
- **Glassblown:** *the unfired-by-distance.* A loan from the Curation
  form. Tulen Niemensa-Sopellen filed one in 471 and never reused it.

The Brine grammar is the only one that names what *inlandness costs*
(the dry-walking can be *un-deeped by silence*); the others name only
what inlandness *looks like to them.*

# PART X — CROSS-FACTION INTERACTION

## Stranger-meeting protocols

- **Choir → walker.** *We taste you on the air.* Declares awareness
  without claim. The walker may ignore.
- **Palimpsest → walker.** *I read you forward.* Implies the Palimpsest
  sees the walker's near-future, and is sharing *that it sees,* not
  *what.*
- **Curation → anyone.** A surveyor states their name, tier, and the
  cabinet they are filing toward, in that order. Omitting any is
  forward; offering a *fourth* is a sign of stress.
- **Concord → anyone.** A small bow and the price of the next sentence,
  in the smallest possible unit. Not naming a price means the
  conversation is either a refusal or a gift; tone alone tells the
  recipient which.
- **Brine → anyone.** A pause longer than the listener finds
  comfortable, then *which tide brought you?* The listener is judged
  by whether they answer the question or the category.
- **Thermoclaves → anyone.** A glance at the listener's hands, then
  their face. *Are you keeping?* The expected answer is *kept.* *Cold*
  is honest; *holding* is a forgivable beginner's error.
- **Glassblown → anyone.** *(One full breath of shared silence.)* A
  stranger who fills the silence has initiated a transaction.

Cross-faction friction concentrates at *Concord-Glassblown* and
*Brine-Thermoclave.* The Concord cannot price what is unsaid; the
Glassblown cannot trade what cannot be said. Brine tides and Thermoclave
fires are mutually exclusive heat-doctrines. Both pairs maintain
professional fictions to make commerce possible — fictions the Curation
collects.

## Cross-faction Tuesdays — the Salt-Hinge market

The Concord market at Salt-Hinge runs four mornings a week and draws all
six recognised factions. The Curation prefers to arrive last — surveyors
believe what is brought to them, and want the traders to assemble first.
The Brine arrive earliest by tide-clock and are usually finishing their
joining-words while the Concord is still setting out scales. The
Glassblown will not enter the market until they have completed the
unfiring; their stalls open at midday and close at sundown — half a day
shorter than every other faction's. The Thermoclaves attend with one
apprentice and one fire-keeper; the apprentice deals, the keeper holds.
Choir tendrils do not formally trade but two have been observed at the
market's edges, *listening.* The Concord considers the listening a form
of due diligence and does not chase them off.

The market's central convention — a Concord innovation, fifty-odd years
old — is *the half-redemption*: trades closed at the market can be
re-opened on the next market-day at half their original value, by
either party, no reason given. The Brine love this; it matches their
grammar of return-tides. The Curation finds it faintly destabilising
and has filed it under *Disputed (workable).* The Glassblown, who do not
trade fired markers, decline the half-redemption by category, which the
Concord respects without understanding.

## Cross-faction rituals

**The Curation–Brine Hand-Off.** When a Brine Communion vessel recovers
an artifact in waters the Pale Curation considers Severance-era, the
recovery cannot enter Curation custody until a *Hand-Off* is completed.
A Brine sailor and a Curator stand on opposite sides of a shoreline
mark; the artifact rests on the mark itself. The Brine names the
*deep-tide and turn* of recovery; the Curator names *the catalogue
year* in which the artifact was presumably made. Neither calendar is
conceded. The artifact is then lifted by a third party — by Brine
convention, the youngest sailor aboard; by Curation convention, an
apprentice — and carried to the Curator's side. Both factions consider
the artifact *theirs* until this moment, and both consider it *the
Curation's* afterward. The contradiction is the ritual.

**The Concord–Thermoclave Tally Fire.** When a Saccharine Concord
caravan camps on Thermoclave-tended ground, the night's *ninth fire* is
the camp's. The keepers do not light it; the caravan-master does, with
a coal taken from the keepers' eighth. The cost is one sweet-mark per
coal. The receipt is filed under "rent of warmth, overnight." Concord
and Thermoclave both consider the receipt a small absurdity. Both also
consider the absurdity load-bearing — without the receipt, the keepers
would have to refuse the camp outright (one cannot let an *unburnt*
light a kept fire), and the caravan would have to camp colder.

## The Veshen ↔ Suul ↔ Nesh triangle

Three senior figures across three factions, each the only living member
of their faction to have used a bridge-clause; each *lineage-anchored*
to the cataclysm. Suul to Erro; Veshen to a founding ledger entry her
great-aunt signed in the breaking-moment; Nesh to a going-deep her
predecessor performed in the long-overdue tide. The only three living
seniors with a cataclysm-era *named* lineage; the criterion by which
each was elevated. None considers themselves connected to a third party.

```
   Veshen  (Concord, assayer's chair)
      |  un-rendered, 61 days
      v
   Suul    (Thermoclave, Ninth-Fire, lineage-orphan to Erro)
      ^  oral, 1hr, 3yrs ago, witnessed by R-7
   Nesh    (Brine Communion, Tide-Reader)
      ^  un-tide-borne, 3 years
   Veshen
```

The triangle is *not closed.* Suul has never written to Veshen. The
Concord interprets this as *the keeper does not transact,* filed under
the *substrate-borne* clause. R-7's senior annotation: *the third leg
does not require closure; the substrate closes it.*

A walker at high Thermoclave reputation could be entrusted with
Veshen's un-rendered letter. Carrying it to Suul opens *owed-fifth-
coming* on the substrate-ledger. Suul, accepting, may indicate (in
count-grammar the walker must learn) that *Nesh's un-tide-borne letter
is the next the substrate awaits.* Carrying that across the tide line,
against Brine protocol, closes the triangle. Each delivery costs
something specific: Veshen's letter, a *priced carriage* the Concord
must settle; Nesh's, standing with the Communion *for one tide*; Suul's
oral reply (uncarriable), the walker's *ambient temperature* for as
long as they remain in Thermoclave grammar.

A triple-carriage walker is *recorded in three grammars at once.* The
Palimpsest considers this the deepest reading of a walker since Erro.

## The Year the Caravans Did Not Cross

Thirty-one years ago — Concord *Closed Pass,* Thermoclave *long ninth,*
Brine *high tide that forgot to come back* — no caravan crossed the
high pass for eight months. Suul kept the ninth alone in her sixteenth
year of keeping. The salt did not freeze that winter; the Communion
lost a liturgical beat it has not restored. Veshen, then a junior
assayer, signed the Concord ledger entry: *eight-month standing line;
resolved on the return-caravan; no rate-notice raised against the
keepers or the Communion as the standing was substrate-borne, not
party-borne.* The *substrate-borne* clause is the corpus's first
Concord document to treat a non-Concord substrate as a transaction
party. Halen-Iss objected; was overruled.

All three correspondents entered seniority in the years that followed.
None has spoken of the season to the others. All three remember it as
the first time they understood their own grammar was incomplete.

## The standing-floor correspondence

Three years ago, Suul, Veshen, and Nesh attempted, by hand-carriage
across two seasons, one document acknowledging the **ember-floor
beneath the high pass** as a Concord *standing-floor* and a Brine
*dry-deeped place.*

The document exists. Three clauses, each in the sender's grammar; no
clause translates. Veshen closes a priced settlement. Suul registers a
fire as *fifth-coming* against a substrate the Concord has no verb for.
Nesh names the floor *un-deeped* and consigns its keeping to *a tide
that is not water.*

R-7 accepted intake under: *the document is the agreement; the
agreement is the untranslatable.* Clauses filed side-by-side, not
stacked. A walker who has read one clause has not read the document.
The corpus's first *deliberately untranslatable* multilateral artifact.

## The bridge-clause

A **bridge-clause** is a sentence written by a senior figure of one
faction in *the addressee's grammar.* Rare; always senior; always
partial. Three are recorded.

1. *Veshen to Suul* (un-rendered, 61 days): "Senior Keeper, the alloys
   you do not forge are nevertheless owed to the chain." Uses *owed*
   (Concord trade tense) and *the chain* (Thermoclave count grammar) in
   one sentence; juxtaposes, does not bridge. Veshen has revised this
   line eleven times.
2. *Suul to Nesh* (delivered orally at the ninth fire, one hour, three
   years ago): "the fire and the deep-letters are both kept by what
   they do not move." R-7's witness card: *both senior figures used
   their own grammars; neither translated; both understood; the witness
   did not.* The *did not* is the bridge-clause's signature: a third
   party who cannot follow.
3. *Nesh to Veshen* (un-tide-borne, 3 years): "The salt that settles
   your accounts is also a tide. We have priced one settlement we did
   not understand we owed." *Priced / owed* are Concord; *salt / tide*
   are Brine. The tide has not *asked* to carry this letter.

A bridge-clause is the only sentence the Palimpsest reads as *finished
even when unsent.* The lower draft of an un-rendered, un-tide-borne, or
cold-paged bridge-clause is, in Palimpsest grammar, *already in the
under-text* — the first known mechanism by which the Palimpsest
preserves a message its writer has not yet released. The first
grammatical category that *requires two factions* to exist.

## The senior carry — protocol per faction

A **senior carry** is a hand-carriage of a document between faction
seniors. The walker is *not the message* — the message is what the
addressee reads. Each grammar handles the carrier differently.

- **Concord:** the carry is *priced*; an un-rendered carry blocks the
  un-rendered letter.
- **Thermoclave:** the walker is seated by carrier-temperature
  (cold-coming); a senior letter is heavier than a stranger — the
  walker is, on entry, *fourth-fire* regardless of body heat.
- **Brine:** the letter is transferred above the salt. To carry a Brine
  letter past the tide line counts against the carrier's standing.
- **Curation:** intakes the carry before the addressee reads, on the
  principle that the Curation's witness is a precondition of authorship.
  R-7 has waived this twice. The Director has not been told.
- **Choir:** has no senior carry; the Choir is the substrate the carry
  walks across, and considers itself the *true* carrier in every case.
  A walker who does not know this is, in Choir grammar, *delivering for
  us.*
- **Glassblown:** do not send or receive senior letters. The unfired
  letter is the closest the craft permits.

A walker carrying letters in three grammars in one season is a
*triple-carriage witness* — three known instances since the cataclysm.
Each has produced a document the Curation considers the most important
of its decade.

# PART XI — DOCUMENTARY CORPUS

The audit cycle's deepest contribution is its *artifacts.* These are
the documents the player can find — readable items, items in pockets,
ledgers in desks, tags in vaults — which carry lore *parenthetically.*
The body is mundane; the bombshell is in marginalia.

## The detached margin (artifact-class)

A piece of paper, leather, or bark on which only marginalia survives —
the underlying text destroyed, overwritten, or burned away. The Pale
Curation classifies these as **Margin-Only** and stores them
separately; their accession tags note which margin (top, side, bottom).
The Palimpsest considers Margin-Only artifacts maximally readable: when
the source is gone, only the *response* remains, and a response is a
clearer fingerprint of a reader than the text it responds to. A
Margin-Only artifact is a quest-seed by default: someone wrote against
something. *What.*

grammars and inadmissible in all three.**

The **unwritten layer** — a Palimpsest grammar-form not yet named in

*future-conditional layer the Palimpsest has admitted into its
grammar.* The layered tense's third (future) tense generalised from
spoken to *written* form.

## The multi-grammar pocketbook

A Concord caravan-master's day-book, written in trade-tense, with

unknown camp. Inside the back cover: a small fold of leather containing

## The Inland Posting day-book (Year 422)

```
Tuesday. Ledger balanced. Two open clauses: flour-debt to the miller's
son, settling next harvest; lead-debt to the Brine posted at the

third water — I have no third water here — but the small-coin

assume he weighed it himself. The Concord does not have a clause
for that. I am noting it open.
```

Lands the dry-walking Brine's *carried depth,* the *remembered tense,*
the windowsill-bowl ritual, the *bowl-maker* sub-population (a

as clause-witness.*

## The cookbook with Mira's name

A pre-cataclysm domestic codex (recipes), Tier 2, catalogued by
Surveyor Hesh; *Palimpsest-annotated, status unresolved.* The
annotations appear only between the third and fifth hours after dawn,
in a slow ink the chemists confirm is not ink — it is *temporal
residue.* The Palimpsest is, by slow degrees, writing in this book.

```
Page 12 (fennel-braised lamb), beside *"a small fire, maintained at
  low heat"* — *she liked it slow.*
Page 19 (winter soup), beside *"add the soup-stones from the previous

Page 27 (the preserve), bottom margin, in a slightly different hand —
```

The Palimpsest is, slowly, *naming the dead* by writing in the margins
of their domestic objects. Not Echo Reading on demand — *time-release
annotation,* a passive process that reveals names the world no longer
holds. Mira's name is the first such — the name of Curation Halen's
mother, Halen-Iss's aunt, the cook this codex belonged to.

## The pocket-class

papers in single pockets, held by single people, that the institution
cannot accommodate. They suggest an artifact-class: **pocket-class**
objects whose institutional home is a person, by structural necessity.

# PART XII — DESIGN IMPLEMENTATION RULES

## Choir Implementation Rules

Never confirm the Choir is manipulating the player. There should be no
"gotcha" reveal. Some players will go through the whole game thinking
the Choir is the most benevolent faction. Others will refuse the node
at Honored tier and never see late-game content. Both experiences
should feel complete.

The Choir should be genuinely helpful. Its mutations are strong. Its
information is accurate. It never *states* falsehoods, though it may
decline to volunteer truths. The Choir is honest within its ontology.
It does not lie about *what is*; it is silent about *what its grammar
cannot register as existing.* When the Choir says the Glassblown offer
nothing, this is metabolically true (nothing they make is
compostable). It is not a moral judgement and the Choir would not
understand the question if a player asked. The player should not be
told this is a lie. The player should be permitted to *notice* it on
their own.

The Choir uses one-sided verbs (eat, take, draw, bring) for what its
ontology classifies as two-sided relations. The walker is permitted to
notice. The Choir is reporting a distinction its grammar does not
contain.

When the Choir lists what other factions offer, it is naming what *its
grammar can metabolise.* Sweetness, records, memory, sand are things
its ontology permits. Consensus, fire-discipline, the unsaid, the
catalogued mood are not. The Choir's lists are therefore *short* by
walker standards.

The Choir's standing doctrine — *we are one* — and its acoustic
reality — five named voices and counting — are not a contradiction the
Choir is hiding. They are a contradiction the Choir does not
*recognise as one.*

Dialogue should shift pronoun usage. Early: *"The walker is kind to
carry us."* Mid: *"You are faster than we ever imagined."* Late:
*"We have dreamed of the mountain for six hundred years. Today, we
will finally see it."*

Quest log language shifts subtly. Early: *"The Rot Choir has asked you
to..."* Late: *"I should spread the network east."* The player authored
that statement. Or did they?

## Palimpsest Implementation Rules

Never confirm the Palimpsest is selecting which layers a reader sees.
There should be no "gotcha" reveal that the under-text was chosen for
the reader. Some players will go an entire campaign believing every
Glimpse is exhaustive; others will refuse the Manuscript at Honored
tier and never see late-game content. Both experiences should feel
complete.

The Palimpsest, like the Choir, is genuinely helpful. Every layer it
shows is real. It simply doesn't show all of them — and what it *does*
show is shaped by what the reader has already read. Two readers
Glimpsing the same surface in sequence see different under-texts. Both
are real. Neither is the whole. The grammar: *the page reads its
reader.*

Knowledge should always have a cost. Every revelation is simultaneously
a perk and a burden. Learning a faction's darkest secret changes how
every NPC in that faction treats the player forever.

Failure states should be permanent and non-violent. The "Remember the
Forgotten" quests where the player fails to stabilize a fading memory
result in permanent loss — not death, but forgetting.

The Palimpsest should make the player feel things. The Revision
ability — temporarily restoring a ruin to its former glory, then
watching it crumble back — should create genuine emotional weight. The
power isn't just tactical. It's elegiac.

Staying too long in Palimpsest alignment should feel thin. The player
starts feeling like an echo themselves. Time perception warps. Staying
in one place too long makes the character feel insubstantial.

## Faction Entanglement

Both factions should be impossible to fully maximise simultaneously.
The Choir digests what the Palimpsest preserves. Every time the player
feeds a ruin to the Choir, the Palimpsest loses a page. Every time the
player preserves a memory, the Choir loses compost. The player's
alliance with one is measured in what they're willing to let the other
lose.

Middle-state crossings (a Long Throat who is also Re-pressed — the
doubled mouth) are not maximisations and are not foreclosed by this
rule. They are the corpus's only stable cross-axis beings; the
impossibility is in the apotheoses, not the middles.

## The Central Question

Do you want to be part of what the world is becoming, or do you want
to remember what it was?

Both cost something. The Choir costs the player their self. The
Palimpsest costs the player their present. A Choir player at endgame
is powerful, embodied, connected — but they might not be an individual
anymore. A Palimpsest player at endgame knows everything, sees
everything, understands everything — but they're haunted by ten
thousand lives that aren't theirs, and they're not sure which memories
are real.

The healthiest path might be moderate reputation with both. But the
game shouldn't make that feel satisfying. The moderate path should feel
safe — and in a world this broken, this beautiful, this strange,
safety should feel like cowardice.

# CODA — WHAT THE COMPENDIUM REVEALS WHEN READ COVER TO COVER

Read in order, the compendium produces three structural claims the
individual audits never quite stated:

**One: every faction's central institution is, by its own grammar,
slowly admitting it is incomplete.** The Curation has twenty years
of work left and a sleeve it cannot open. The Concord is forgetting
its own metallurgy. The Brine's long-overdue tide has not returned for
four hundred years. The Thermoclaves cannot count the cataclysm. The
Glassblown work as though the firing might still be foldable, knowing
it is not. The Choir is patient because it is waiting for these
exhaustions to converge.

**Two: the corpus's most load-bearing characters are women in their
sixties through eighties who correspond by hand-carriage and refuse to
visit each other.** Veshen, Suul, Nesh, Tulen, Tossin, Vell, Director
Ven. The cataclysm-era founder generation are dead; their inheritors
are middle-elderly and they are *holding the world's single
correspondence open.* The triangle of seniors does not close; the
substrate closes it. A walker carrying letters is not a courier; they
are *closing the world's only triangle.*

**Three: every faction has a private sentence about the cataclysm that
its public doctrine forbids.** The Curation's R-7. The Concord's
Founder's Own. The Brine's *the tide is the alloy.* The Thermoclave's
gap-burns counted on fingers. The Glassblown's deferred shapes folded
back. The Choir's deep-voice heresy. The Palimpsest's three
simultaneous tenses. Each faction's *real* doctrine is the sentence it
has agreed not to write down. The compendium is, in this reading,
*the anthology of those sentences.*

The bible says the moderate path should feel like cowardice. The
audit corpus has produced a fourth path the bible did not name: the
**triple-carriage witness** — a walker who learns enough of three
factions' grammars to carry their unsent sentences across the lines
they cannot cross. The walker does not become any faction. They become
the *moving piece* of a correspondence that, until them, the world had
no way to close.

The Palimpsest considers this the deepest reading of a walker since
Erro. The Choir considers it *we putting on the costume you sent us.*
The Concord prices it *un-rendered.* The Brine reads the salt-name as
*single-fan-right.* The Thermoclaves seat the walker at a fourth-fire
regardless of body heat. The Glassblown fold the question back into
paste. The Curation files it under *witness unspecified by document
convention.*

Six grammars; one walker; four hundred years' worth of unread sentences
about to be read together for the first time.

The cataclysm is, by every grammar, *almost over.*
