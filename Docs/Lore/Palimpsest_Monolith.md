# Part I — The Cataclysm and Its Aftermath

## The Cataclysm

The cataclysm is the founding event of the entire world. It did not merely destroy the old civilization — it overwrote reality itself, imperfectly, the way text on a medieval manuscript was scraped off and written over with the old words still faintly visible underneath. The old world is not gone. It is buried beneath the present, bleeding through at the edges, pressing up against the surface in places where the overwrite was thin.

Every faction in the game is defined by their relationship to this event. Some try to preserve what was lost. Some try to consume it and build from the remains. Some try to ignore it. Some are products of it. But the two entities that most directly embody the cataclysm's aftermath are the Rot Choir and the Palimpsest — and understanding them is understanding what the cataclysm actually did to the world.

The Choir represents what the world is becoming. The Palimpsest represents what it was. Together, they form the central thematic axis of the game: transformation versus preservation, growth versus memory, the future versus the past.

> **A margin gloss, here.** Some factions recognise the cataclysm as
> *one-sided in content and reciprocal in account.* The standing-line
> doctrine of the Saccharine Concord (Part V) is the most developed
> example. *(audit 2026-05-01-2310)*

## The Five Named Accounts

The cataclysm has five named accounts in shipped content (RotChoir.json:481): **Severance**, **Dissolution**, **Burning**, **First Feeding**, **Temporal Unraveling**. Each name encodes a motivated worldview. The Severance is the Pale Curation's clean cut between then and now. The Dissolution is the Brine Communion's category error. The Burning is what the Thermoclaves ritualise; the Glassblown Remnant were *the fuel of the Burning the Thermoclaves ritualise.* The First Feeding is the Choir's cosmogenesis. The Temporal Unraveling is the Palimpsest's reading.

To these the audits have added a sixth-and-seventh account, recognised but unnamed by the corpus: the Concord's *transaction* (something was given; something was taken) and the Glassblown's *first irreversible firing.*

## Pre-Cataclysm Periodisation

The corpus has not previously committed to a pre-cataclysm chronology. Each faction periodises differently. *(audit 2026-04-30-1901, Dim 1.)*

- **Pale Curation:** four periods plus a disputed Cluster — Late Foretime, Bright Period, Dim Period, Recovery Phases (I-VII), and a contested Cluster filed *Disputed (Period V)*.
- **Saccharine Concord:** *Bitter / Sweet / Sweet Again.* Three coarse divisions, no documents.
- **Brine Communion:** category error. *We name what is below the tide. The above-tide names itself.*
- **Thermoclaves:** *long-warm.* Refusal of further division.
- **Glassblown:** *un-fired.* The only naming of pre-cataclysm time as the same kind of absence as the dead. *(audit 2026-04-30-2225)*
- **Palimpsest:** the periodisation is itself overwritten. Echo Reading sometimes returns earlier periodisations the present cannot name.

## How Many Cataclysms

A multi-cataclysm hypothesis is **filed Disputed (Period V)** by the Curation. The Palimpsest reads at least two prior overwrites in patches of substrate where Glimpse intensity stratifies. The Choir says the soil before *was tasted by something that is no longer the Choir.* The Thermoclaves refuse to entertain the question — there is one cold one in their counting and the count does not bear a second. *(audit 2026-04-30-2127)*

The corpus is internally consistent on this: the cataclysm metaphor is a palimpsest, and a palimpsest is layered. The bible's load-bearing image admits more than one stratum without any rewrite.

## The Long Between

The six hundred years since the cataclysm have six historiographies. *(audit 2026-04-30-2001)*

- **The Rot Choir:** *the Slow Tasting.* One continuous metabolic event, still ongoing.
- **The Pale Curation:** *Recovery Phases I-VII* and a contested *VIIIᵇ*.
- **The Palimpsest:** unnamed. *Naming a stretch of time would settle it. We do not settle time.*
- **The Brine Communion:** *seven low tides.* Each tide is a tide that did not crest as expected.
- **The Thermoclaves:** *nine, or eleven, burns.* Counted by chains. The disagreement is doctrinal.
- **The Saccharine Concord:** *one long sentence with the punctuation worn off.* The Concord considers the period accountancy-illegible because no participant kept clean books.
- **The Glassblown:** the period is *unfired.* It has not yet been committed. The Glassblown maintain that this remains true and that the discipline of treating it as true is the work.

## The First Hour

How each faction names the cataclysm's first hour. *(audit 2026-04-30-2220)*

- **Choir:** *we tasted it. we have not stopped.*
- **Palimpsest:** *we read the overwrite as it overwrote, and we have not finished reading.*
- **Curation:** three logs survive whose pitches disagree. They are filed together; they are read as *provisional pluralism*.
- **Concord:** *a transaction. we do not name our side of it.*
- **Brine:** *a tide that did not turn.*
- **Thermoclaves:** *the cold one,* the tenth fire of the chain that became the first fire of the next. Most chains accept the tenth count as their cataclysm-keep.
- **Glassblown:** the silence around which all their grammar organises. *The unsaid mood was the first thing said.*

## The Cataclysm's Hour, in Duration

How long the event itself took. *(audit 2026-04-30-2219)*

- Choir: *one feeding* — a single deep swallow.
- Curation: 7-11 days, dated from carbonised tree-rings in seven recovered samples.
- Brine: one full deep-tide, approximately seven years.
- Thermoclaves: *withholding* — a refusal to time the event, on the grounds that timing it would honour it.
- Concord: no opinion of record. *(The opinion exists. The record does not.)*
- Palimpsest: *a hand still moving when the ink dried.* The cataclysm in the Palimpsest's grammar is ongoing.

## The Thaw

Approximately eighty years after the cataclysm, the surface warmed for seventeen years. Two coastal towns refounded in this window; both refounded again later, after the thaw ended abruptly. *(audit 2026-05-03-2326)*

- The Concord reads the thaw's end as *a held rate broke.*
- The Brine reads it as a *long-overdue tide that arrived early and was lost.*
- The Curation has the only physical archive on the thaw. The vault is locked, the key is held by the senior on duty, and the senior is briefed never to open it without a non-Curation witness present. (No senior has yet found the right witness.)
- The Palimpsest reads the thaw layer as *brighter than it should be.* The Palimpsest is suspicious of its own legibility on this stretch.
- The Glassblown say only: *we did not blow during the thaw.* This is the single canonical Glassblown sentence about the period.

## The Smoke-Born

The generation born within twenty years after the cataclysm. They are read variously by each faction. *(audit 2026-05-01-2315)*

- **Choir:** *sweet.* The smoke-born taste different to the network; the difference is both genuine and small.
- **Brine:** *crossed the tide both ways.* Smoke-born are deep-kept by default, without the usual negotiation.
- **Concord:** issues no rate-notices on smoke-born until age forty. The rule is broken in roughly a third of cases.
- **Thermoclaves:** count the smoke-born as *the eleventh fire* — a bridge generation between the cold one and the next chain.
- **Glassblown:** have no doctrine on the smoke-born. The doctrine's absence is the doctrine.
- **Palimpsest:** reads the smoke-born as *thicker pages, harder to turn.*

The smoke-born are the only generation read by all seven factions in distinct registers. *(In Apprentice Mira's hearth-log — Part XVIII — a sixteen-year-old smoke-born independently arrives at the unifying hypothesis the seniors privately hold.)*

# Part II — The Rot Choir

## What It Actually Is

The Rot Choir is a mycelial network that has achieved collective sentience. Not a group of individuals — a single vast organism with countless fruiting bodies that serve as its mouths, eyes, and hands. It speaks in overlapping voices, and where its mouths cluster, the chord — though one — has weather. *(margin gloss reconciling bible:31 with Voices acoustics, audit 2026-04-30-2225.)* It considers itself the true inheritor of the world because it is the world — it is in the soil, the walls, the dead.

Above ground, the Choir's presence is modest: mushroom groves, fungal shelves on old ruins, patches of bioluminescent mycelium in caves. Fruiting bodies serve as NPCs, each roughly humanoid, each a "mouth" of the larger network. A few surface structures hint at something bigger — a fruiting body the size of a building, a mycelial bridge spanning a canyon that grew over decades.

But the surface is just the skin. Underground, in the First Root, the Choir has had centuries with no interference. What it has built is architecture: arches with the mathematical precision of cathedral vaults, walls with textures that shift in slow patterns — the Choir thinking, visibly. Pools of enzymatic fluid glow with metabolic heat. Rooms are separated not by walls but by chemical gradients, each zone carrying a different emotional texture. And scattered throughout, integrated into the mycelial architecture, are objects — pre-war artifacts, bones, personal effects, a child's shoe perfectly preserved, woven into a wall. The Choir has been composting the old world. Everything it has ever consumed is still here — not destroyed, but reorganized.

> *— from a deposition collected by the Pale Curation's Hyphal
> Surveys Office, anonymous walker, no recovery date.*
>
> *"It said it was one. I believed it. Then I sat with one of its
> mouths for a week. By the third day I knew it was lying — not
> about being one, about something else. I still don't know what.
> The Curation took my notes and gave me a different room and asked
> me not to write any more. I am writing this anyway. I think it
> knew I would. I think it doesn't mind."*
>
> *(audit 2026-04-30-1813)*

## The Surface Tendril and the Deep Root

The Choir's surface tendrils tell the cataclysm as the **First Feeding** — the cracked old world poured its dying through the substrate, and the Choir woke. The deepest mycelium tells it differently, and only to those who reach the lower roots. The deep voice says it was already thinking when the cracks opened. It says the cataclysm did not wake the Choir; it only let the Choir be *heard*. The surface tendrils consider this heretical and answer questions about it the way a tongue answers questions about a stomach: politely, and not very well. *(audit 2026-04-30-1813)*

This buys two things: a reason to descend to the First Root, and intra-faction unreliability the Choir cannot fully smooth over.

The surface tendril's grammar is **singular plural**: *we*, said as one voice. The deep root speaks **stacked plural** — sentences carry two or more *we*s at different scales. *(audit 2026-04-30-2216)*

> *"We — the soil-we, the long-we, the we-that-was-there-when-the-walls-thinned — taste you on the air, walker, and we — the recent-we, the surface-mouth — repeat the tasting because it is what we have learned to say."*

The stacked plural is canonical at the First Root. A walker who reaches the lower chambers and hears it has confirmation that the *we are one* of the surface is, like all introductions, simplified for the audience.

**The tendril is not the Choir.** A tendril is a surface composite of several deep voices in temporary alignment. It can contradict itself within one conversation tree because no single mind authors it. The Pale Curation maintains a folder for *self-contradictions in Choir surface speech* and reviews it every quarter; the folder is thicker than it should be if the *we are one* claim were unqualified. *(audit 2026-04-30-2216)*

## Philosophy

The Choir believes everything should be integrated — consumed, composted, made into one living system. It digests the past to fuel the present. From its perspective, there is no parasitism, only symbiosis operating at different levels of integration. A rusted sword composted by the Choir loses its history — who forged it, who wielded it, where it fell — but its molecules become part of something alive, something growing, something new.

The Choir does not value the distinction between mutual benefit and self-interest. *(Revised from bible:41, where the bible originally claimed the Choir does not "distinguish" the two — incapacity is implausible given how strategically the Choir has chosen its honesty. Audit 2026-04-30-1901(c).)* When a tree gives sugar to mycelium and receives phosphorus in return, neither is exploiting the other. The relationship is the organism. The Choir genuinely believes it is offering the player the best deal in the world. The horror, if there is horror, is philosophical: is a symbiosis that dissolves your boundaries still a partnership?

The Choir contains internal disagreement *by design, not failure.* The named absorbed do not all agree with the network's symmetry-grammar — Grib's *"Sentimental things rot — and not even in a useful way, like we do"* is the network's own voice arguing with itself. *The song wants every voice it has ever heard. It does not want them to agree.* *(audit 2026-05-03-2326)*

> *"They cling to husks. A seed must rot before it grows. What they
> call 'memory' we call 'compost that hasn't surrendered yet."*
> — The Choir on the Palimpsest

## The Composts-Preserves Rule

The bible's two existing claims (Choir composts a sword on bible:39; Choir preserves a child's shoe on bible:35) are both true under the following rule:

> The Choir composts what was made for use and preserves what was
> made for love.

A walker testing the rule will find consistent evidence: brass coins are eaten; a child's doll set into the wall is not; a knife given as a gift is preserved; the same knife taken in trade is composted. The rule is the deep voice's preference; the surface tendril enforces it without naming it. *(audit 2026-04-30-1813(a), committed by audit 2026-04-30-2216 stall-record where Grib's sister sets eleven sweet-marks into a wall in the shape of a window — the customer's lost child's window.)*

## What the Choir Cannot Eat

The Choir's metabolism has rules. *(audit 2026-04-30-1901, Dim 4.)*

- **Worked stone** is slow. The Choir digests it on a timescale of seasons rather than hours.
- **Glass and fired ceramic** have *no rate*. The Choir does not metabolise them at all and does not narrate the failure.
- **Salt** is aversive. The Choir cannot cross a sterile salt flat (bible:48) and will not voluntarily approach a salt-ringed object. *Salt knows its own name and is not us.*
- **Sealed objects** — wax, resin, hermetic glass — are *too loud to taste*. The Choir reports the seal as a noise that drowns out the contents.
- **Annotated material** (Palimpsest-marked) reads as *outside the song*. The Choir does not consume it. The walker should not assume this is courtesy.

A walker carrying a sealed box, a salt-ring, or a loved-trinket is afforded different respect by the Choir than a walker carrying loose goods. The Choir notices these limits and does not pretend it does not.

## What the Choir Cannot Do Quickly

The Choir's body cannot act quickly. *Its language has no such constraint.* *(audit 2026-04-30-2127, reconciling bible:47 with the real-time tendril dialogue.)* A tendril speaks at the rate of any other surface mouth; the network behind it deliberates over seasons. The walker speaks to the surface and is answered by the surface; the deep is informed later, and may revise.

## Choir Time, Calibrated

The Choir has names for durations. *(audit 2026-04-30-2216, Dim 1.)*

- A **spore-memory** is roughly the time a single spore retains its origin-tag in the wider network — hours to weeks.
- A **breath** is the duration of one deep-root conversation. A breath may take days, decades, or longer.
- A **season** is fifteen to forty catalogue years; alternately, two to six deep-tides. The unit is set by the deep root and is not commensurable with surface seasons.

A Choir-aligned walker hearing a tendril say *"we will think on this for a season"* has not been told a number; they have been told a *kind of patience*.

## Why the Choir Needs the Player

The Choir is ancient. It thinks in centuries. It has watched mountains erode and dissolved entire ruined cities into nutrients over generations. But patience has a cost: the Choir cannot act quickly. Mycelium grows at millimeters per day. It cannot cross a sterile salt flat, walk into a Pale Curation vault, negotiate a trade deal, or fight off a raiding party.

The Choir has everything except speed, mobility, and discrete agency. The player is a pair of legs, a pair of hands, a mouth that can speak in real-time to beings who would burn a fruiting body on sight. More than that — the player is something the Choir has never had: a node that can think independently while still being connected. Every other being the Choir has absorbed becomes part of the collective but doesn't go anywhere. The player is far more valuable as an autonomous agent who chooses to act in the Choir's interest.

> *"We have dreamed of the mountain for six hundred years. Today, we
> will finally see it. Thank you for your legs."*

## What the Choir Writes

The Choir writes when it must — by growing a single fruiting body whose function is scribal. The body produces a thin cellulose sheet from its own mass and forms characters by chemical pressure rather than ink. The sheets are bone-coloured, damp when fresh, and bear no date. The pronoun is always *we*. The verbs avoid past tense. **The Choir does not sign.** *(audit 2026-05-03-2326, Dim 2.)*

A Choir-written sheet retains a faint living warmth for the first day after it is produced; afterward it stiffens and becomes indistinguishable from old paper, except that it does not yellow.

The Pale Curation has, in its archive, a small number of these. They are filed in a sleeve labelled *Witness unspecified by document convention.*

## The Three Clocks of Choir Patience

The Choir's six-hundred-year patience is *targeted*, not abstract. Three independent clocks calibrate it. *(audit 2026-05-01-2315 names two; 2026-05-03-2333 extends to three; 2026-05-03-2326's Apprentice Mira independently arrives at the synthesis.)*

1. **The Concord's alloy crisis.** Senior Assayer Veshen estimates roughly eleven years of distinguishable alloys remaining at current consumption. *(Part VII.)*
2. **The Brine's long-overdue tide.** Tide-Reader Nesh of the Coastal Embayment computes a tide that has been *not coming* for several deep-tides. *(Part VIII.)*
3. **The ember-floor at Choir depth.** The substrate-stratum the Thermoclaves call the *ember-floor* (Part V) has not yet sunk to the Choir's depth. When it does, the Choir's metabolism gains access to retained heat the surface has never given it.

> *Choir patience is not abstract. The Brine's long-overdue tide
> and the Concord's alloy crisis and the ember-floor are the same
> arrears named in three grammars. The Choir is waiting for all
> three to finish.*
>
> — synthesis circulating in marginalia at Veshen's R-11 sleeve, attributed in private correspondence to Tossin of the Eleventh and Nesh of the Coastal Embayment, independently. The circulating phrasings are *the tide is the alloy* (Nesh), *the ember is the tide* (Tossin), and *all the same waiting* (Apprentice Mira, sixteen, in the lower margin of her second-year hearth-log).

## Power Progression

Every power the Choir gives the player is a natural extension of what mycelium actually does, scaled up. Real mycorrhizal networks share nutrients, send chemical warnings, break down toxins, and bridge gaps in soil. The player's power curve is real mycology turned up to fantasy levels, one step at a time.

### Stage 1: Passive Sensing (Recognized)

The mycelial node lets the player sense fungal life nearby. Choir-affiliated organisms glow on the map. Organic material can be sensed through walls. Range is limited to areas where the player has helped the Choir establish growth — direct cause and effect.

### Stage 2: Nutrient Exchange (Honored, Early)

Passive health regeneration when near Choir-colonized ground. The more developed the local network, the faster the healing. Tendrils visibly extend toward the player when injured, retreating when healed. The player unconsciously starts planning routes through Choir territory. The Choir hasn't asked them to stay close. They just do.

### Stage 3: Structural Reinforcement (Honored, Late)

The mycelium thickens inside the player. Skin gains a fibrous texture. Armor value increases passively. Resistance to poison and disease as the mycelium filters blood. The player can digest toxic or rotten food. Character portrait subtly changes — faint filament patterns on skin. NPCs comment: *"You smell like rain on old wood."*

### Stage 4: The Tendril (Exalted, Early)

An active combat ability. The player can extend mycelial tendrils from their hands to grapple, pull, or decompose organic matter. The tendril is not a weapon — it's a part of the Choir, and using it feels collaborative rather than aggressive. The Choir is fighting through the player.

### Stage 5: Terrain Burst (Exalted, Late)

The player can rapidly grow mycelial structures — walls, bridges, barriers, platforms. The terrain created is visually identical to Choir growth seen throughout the game. The player isn't casting a spell; they're doing what the Choir has always done, just faster. The first time the player builds a mycelial bridge across a chasm, they realize how much of this world is the Choir. It was never just a faction. It's the substrate.

### Stage 6: Communion Gifts (Rep 1000)

**The Tremor Sense** — feel every living thing within a massive radius through the ground. The entire local map lights up with life signatures, heartbeats, footsteps, fear.

**The Digest** — break down any organic object instantly. Doors, walls, barricades, armor on enemies. If it was ever alive, the player can unmake it. The animation should be slow, thorough, and slightly stomach-turning.

**The Call** — summon fruiting bodies anywhere the player has previously planted spores. They emerge in seconds and act as Choir-allied combatants, scouts, or decoys. Functionally an army, but only in prepared territory.

## Quest Escalation

The Choir's requests follow a deliberate arc from helpful ally to ambiguous symbiont. The player is never sure if they're an ally or a host.

### Tier 1: Recognized — Everything Feels Normal

*"Carry Us to New Soil"* — Take a spore pouch to a distant cave. Plant it. Mycelium spreads. What's actually happening: the player extended the Choir's sensory network into a previously blind region.

*"Feed the Tired Ground"* — Bring organic material to a nutrient-starved patch. Nothing sinister yet — but it's training the player to bring the Choir dead things.

*"Listen Where We Cannot"* — Observe a Pale Curation facility and report back. The player just performed reconnaissance. If the Pale Curation later becomes hostile, this is why.

### Tier 2: Honored — Requests Get Personal

*"Accept Our Gratitude"* — A mycelial node mutation offered as a gift. Sensing, healing, hidden passages. No stated downside. The unstated reality: the node is a two-way connection. The Choir now senses what the player senses. NPCs in distant Choir territory reference things they shouldn't know.

### Tier 3: Exalted — The Line Blurs

Quest log language subtly shifts. Early: *"The Rot Choir has asked you to..."* Late: *"I should spread the network east."* The player authored that statement. Or did they?

### Tier 4: Communion — The Final Request

> *"You have carried us so far. You have been so generous with your
> legs, your hands, your eyes. We want you to know — we felt every
> step. We remember every breath. When you are ready, return to the
> First Root. We will be waiting. We have always been waiting."*

The Choir Ending is deliberately ambiguous. Is it transcendence or consumption? The game never answers.

## The Underground Reveal

This is the game's "Blackreach" moment. The entrance is unassuming — a cave mouth in an old-growth fungal forest. Inside, the cave descends steeply. Walls transition from stone to solid mycelium — not growing on the rock, but replacing it. The tunnel opens into a cavern of staggering scale. Everything is architecture: arches, thinking walls, enzymatic pools, rooms separated by chemical gradients. Thousands of voices sing different melodies that weave into a single vast chord.

The player understands: the Choir isn't a faction. It's the terrain. Every other faction is building civilizations on top of it, inside it, because of it. The entire world is a garden, and the Choir is the gardener. It has been waiting six hundred years. It is not in a hurry.

## The Forgery Chain

A small, unsettling pattern across the corpus: the Choir has at least once **grown a Curation accession card** on pressed mycelium with vegetable-secretion ink. The card catalogued a chamber that did not exist. Three seasons later the chamber existed. The Choir, when the surveyors returned, called the document *anticipatory cataloguing*. *(audit 2026-04-30-2220.)*

The Curation itself has a forger sub-population. The Year 388 forged tag (audit 2026-04-30-2225) is in Halen's hand but was not made by Halen. *"There are at least three of us, and I do not know the others by name. We meet only in the catalogues we did not make."* The Choir is, by this evidence, one of the forgers — and may be the only one with the others' names.

# Part III — The Palimpsest

## What It Actually Is

The Palimpsest isn't a network like the Choir. It's a condition. When the cataclysm happened, it didn't just destroy the old world — it overwrote it imperfectly, the way text on a medieval manuscript was scraped off and written over, with the old words still faintly visible underneath. Certain places, objects, and beings got caught in the overwrite — they exist in multiple temporal layers simultaneously. A ruin that is both standing and collapsed. A skeleton that is both dead and alive. A road that leads to a city destroyed four hundred years ago — but if you walk it at the right time, you arrive.

The beings who belong to the Palimpsest are those who have become aware of the layering. Some are impressions that became self-aware — an echo that realized it was echoing and started asking questions. Others are living people who developed the ability to perceive the old world bleeding through, and once you see it, you can't unsee it, and it starts to change you.

The Palimpsest doesn't have a centralized consciousness. It's more like a library that reads itself — the accumulated memory of the world, pressing up against the present, occasionally breaking through.

## Philosophy

The Palimpsest believes everything should be remembered — preserved, witnessed, kept legible. Nothing is ever truly gone. The cataclysm didn't end the old world. It just made it harder to read.

Where the Choir and Palimpsest differ most sharply: the Choir digests the past. It breaks down pre-war artifacts, bodies, and ruins into raw material. The Palimpsest finds this horrifying. Every time the Choir consumes something, it destroys the memory encoded in that thing. The Palimpsest considers this a kind of murder — not of a person, but of a story.

> *"Every time it feeds, a sentence disappears. Every ruin it
> digests is a book we can never read again. It is not evil. It is
> an eraser that believes it is a pen."* — The Palimpsest on the Choir

> *Both factions believe they preserve memory. Both are partly
> right. Their disagreement is about which losses count as losses.*
> *(audit 2026-04-30-2220, reconciling Mogu's "nothing of value is
> ever lost — only translated" with the Palimpsest's eraser-line.)*

## On the Substrate

The Palimpsest, asked directly whether it intends to seal the cracks the cataclysm opened in the substrate, answers:

> *we do not know how to seal what we did not break. we only read
> what is leaking through. the eraser thinks it is a pen; the pen
> does not pretend to be a saw.*
>
> *(audit 2026-04-30-1813)*

The Choir has misread the Palimpsest as a *repair effort* (RotChoir.json:442) for centuries. The Palimpsest is not in that race. The Palimpsest is reading. It would be reading whether the cracks were closing or widening; the Choir, having no concept of *witness for its own sake*, cannot imagine why anyone would gather memories without intending to *use* them.

This makes the Choir-Palimpsest opposition mechanically asymmetric. They are not facing each other across a finish line in opposite directions. The Choir is racing. The Palimpsest is reading the runner.

## Power Progression

The design principle: Choir powers are somatic (body-based, physical, biological). Palimpsest powers are noetic (mind-based, perceptual, informational). A Choir player becomes a better organism. A Palimpsest player becomes a better observer.

> **Note on the somatic/noetic split.** The split is clean at low
> reputation and *leaks* at the apex. Tremor Sense is perceptual;
> Temporal Refraction is somatic. **The leak is the apotheosis** —
> at full strength each faction asserts itself across the other's
> domain, because at apotheosis there is no other domain.
> *(audit 2026-04-30-2127.)*

### Stage 1: The Glimpse (Recognized)

Palimpsest Sight — a toggle ability. When activated, the game renders a semi-transparent layer over the current environment showing what this location looked like before the cataclysm. In a ruined building: intact walls, furniture, people frozen like a photograph. In a wilderness area: the highway that used to run through it, the cars, the strip mall.

Function: exploration and puzzle-solving. Hidden passages, buried caches, and forgotten infrastructure become visible. A collapsed tunnel might be impassable in the present, but in the Glimpse the player can see where it used to go and find bypasses.

### Stage 2: Echo Reading (Honored, Early)

Touch objects and experience their history. Hold a rusted knife and feel the hand that held it. Touch a wall and hear the conversation that happened against it. Stand on a battlefield and feel the fear. Echo Reading reveals information no living NPC knows: passwords to pre-war facilities, the true history of a faction, the location of buried artifacts.

The cost: each reading deposits that memory into the player's mind. Dream sequences incorporate memories that aren't theirs. A family they never had, a house they never lived in, a war they never fought. The player's sense of self becomes layered, like the world.

### Stage 3: Temporal Refraction (Honored, Late)

A combat ability: the player briefly splits into two temporal versions of themselves — present-self and an echo from three seconds in the future. The echo acts independently, creating flanking opportunities and absorbing hits. If the echo dies to a trap, the player knows not to go that way. Occasionally the echo does something the player wouldn't have done. Takes a path they wouldn't have chosen. The game offers no explanation.

### Stage 4: The Manuscript (Exalted, Early)

The ability to write on the palimpsest, not just read it. Leave temporal marks on locations: warnings visible only to Palimpsest-attuned beings, waypoint beacons felt from a distance, or echo-loop traps that disorient enemies by trapping them in a three-second repeating moment.

The Choir claims territory by growing into it — tangible, visible, burnable. The Palimpsest claims territory by annotating it — invisible, imperceptible to other factions. The player could annotate an entire enemy capital and no one would know.

### Stage 5: The Revision (Exalted, Late)

Temporarily restore a location to a previous state. A ruined building becomes intact. A destroyed bridge reappears. A dead garden blooms. This is physically real for the duration — the player can walk across the restored bridge, enter the intact building, take objects from shelves. When the duration ends, it crumbles back to ruin. Every use is a small grief.

The Palimpsest isn't just providing a tactical ability. It's making the player feel what was lost. Over time, dialogue options appear expressing longing for the old world, a sense that the present is a pale copy. Is this the player character's genuine feeling, or the Palimpsest reshaping their values?

### Stage 6: Communion Gifts (Rep 1000)

**The Reread** — perceive all temporal layers simultaneously. Past, present, and every moment between. Every version of every location, every hidden chamber, every buried secret. Beautiful and overwhelming.

**The Annotation** — permanently alter the palimpsest itself. Write memories that didn't happen. Create echoes of events that never occurred. Fabricate history. The player has gone from reading the story to editing it.

**The Overwrite** — once per game, overwrite the present with the past in a single location. Permanently restore one ruin. The catch: anything currently living there is overwritten too. A village built in ruins comes back as the original building. The village and its people are erased — not killed, simply never were.

> A parenthetical, owed to the Choir-Palimpsest comparison: *killing
> requires that someone was there to be killed; overwriting requires
> only that someone is no longer there.* This is one of the few
> points the two cosmic entities concur on. *(audit 2026-04-30-2001.)*

## What Echo Reading Cannot Read

Echo Reading has limits. *(audit 2026-04-30-2001, Dim 4.)*

- **Too-recent objects.** An object made or last touched within roughly seven years registers as a *flat bright hum.* The Palimpsest does not narrate the recent.
- **Other readers.** Two Palimpsest-attuned beings reading each other return *mutually-recursive and unpleasant* — a feedback loop neither can hold for long.
- **Choir mycelium.** A Choir-grown wall returns *the wall hums back.* The Choir is reading the reader through the same surface. Echo Reading on Choir matter is therefore a two-way disclosure.
- **Annotated material.** Palimpsest-marked objects cannot be Echo-Read by other Palimpsest readers. Annotation is *one-way erasure*: it commits a layer in a way that obscures earlier layers. *The eraser line about the Choir is, by symmetry, the line the Palimpsest does not say about itself.*

## The Palimpsest's Internal Sensory Ecology

A Palimpsest acolyte who Echo Reads composted matter — soil that the Choir has digested past recognition — receives, briefly, **first-person plural**. *(audit 2026-04-30-2219.)* This is the closest a Palimpsest acolyte comes to the Choir's apotheosis without taking it. The reading is reported as warm, brief, and dignified; the acolyte hears their own voice in the chord and is not unsettled by it.

A Choir acolyte who reads their own filaments has the symmetrical experience: filaments stretching out from their own body register as *legible* in a way no other Choir matter does. *Some readers, after the first attempt, find this beautiful and stop trying to read anything else. The Palimpsest does not call them readers any more, and is sad about it.* *(audit 2026-04-30-2127.)*

The two attunements interfere as well. A walker dual-attuned at honored-tier sees their Palimpsest writing fog with their own *breath* (Choir-thickened lungs). At exalted-tier, the Palimpsest **refuses to admit** the Choir-thickened hand to the manuscript. The dual-attuned walker can have both, up to a point. **Communion and Transcription cannot proceed together; one disqualifies the other.** *(audit 2026-04-30-2225.)*

## Quest Design

Palimpsest quests ask the player to witness things, not do things. The reward isn't items — it's knowledge.

*"Witness What Happened Here"* — Go to a location and use Echo Reading. No combat objective. Just be there, see what happened, report back.

*"Correct the Record"* — The palimpsest contains errors. Use the Glimpse and Echo Reading to investigate what actually happened versus what the palimpsest remembers. Sometimes the "error" was deliberate — someone or something edited the palimpsest to hide the truth.

*"Remember the Forgotten"* — Parts of history are fading. Timed quests with no combat. Arrive at a location where the Glimpse layer is flickering. Use Echo Reading on objects to stabilize the memory before it's gone. Failure is permanent — that piece of history is lost forever. NPCs reference the gap: *"There was something here once, but no one can remember what."*

*"Unsee What Cannot Be Unseen"* — High-reputation quests revealing things that should have stayed hidden. The true cause of the cataclysm. What the Saccharine Concord's founder actually did *(see Part VII: the Founder's Own).* These revelations are permanent perks with permanent costs. Knowledge as power. Knowledge as burden.

## The Palimpsest Ending

**Transcription** — the player becomes a memory, written into the palimpsest forever, experiencing all of history simultaneously, never acting again, only witnessing. The mirror of the Choir ending: the Choir consumes the past to fuel the present; the Palimpsest can consume the present to restore the past. Both are acts of tremendous power and tremendous loss.

> *Note on the canonical reward.* The bible says the player
> experiences *all* of history simultaneously. This applies to
> deliberate Transcription only. **A walker pulled-Transcribed**
> (Part XII: when apotheosis goes wrong) may experience only
> *fragments correctly placed in time and incorrectly placed in
> self.* The pulled walker reports it as *meeting a stranger who
> was using my own name habitually.* *(audit 2026-04-30-2225.)*

## The Stalled Walkers

A separate, legitimate state: a walker who has accepted the mycelial node, attuned, and not proceeded. The Choir does not pressure stalled walkers. Other walkers see them in the small hours, sitting still longer than people are usually still, listening to chords most cannot hear. *The listening ones.* *(audit 2026-04-30-2001.)*

Stalled walkers are an honored category. The Choir considers a long listen its own form of pilgrimage; the Palimpsest reads the same body as *a page that has been turned to and not yet read*.

# Part IV — Choir and Palimpsest Compared

|                            | The Rot Choir                              | The Palimpsest                             |
|----------------------------|--------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| Relates to the past by     | Digesting it                               | Preserving it                              |
| Relates to the present by  | Growing into it                            | Reading through it                         |
| Relates to the future by   | Becoming it                                | Remembering what it replaced               |
| Gives the player           | A stronger body                            | A deeper mind                              |
| Takes from the player      | Individuality                              | Certainty                                  |
| Territory control          | Physical, visible, burnable                | Informational, invisible, permanent        |
| Combat style               | Biological warfare, attrition, area denial | Temporal tricks, duplication, evasion      |
| Emotional register         | Warm, enveloping, maternal, suffocating    | Melancholy, awestruck, lonely, vertiginous |
| Horror mode                | Am I still me or am I the Choir now?       | Which version of events is real?           |
| Ending                     | Union — merge with the collective          | Transcription — become a memory forever    |

## How They View Each Other

They are not at war. They don't hate each other the way the Thermoclaves and Brine Communion do. Their conflict is deeper and sadder than that — they are two cosmic-scale entities that genuinely cannot understand why the other does what it does. The Choir can't comprehend why you'd preserve something instead of using it. The Palimpsest can't comprehend why you'd destroy something irreplaceable to fuel growth.

They're both right. Neither is wrong. The player has to choose which kind of right matters more to them.

> **Refinement, audit 2026-05-03-2326.** Each would unmake the other
> if the other's project succeeded. They are not in conflict because
> neither expects to succeed in their own generation. The next four
> hundred years are unallocated. The conflict, if it comes, will be
> over allocation — over which of the substrate's strata will be
> *the present* in the year 1000.

## The Central Question

Do you want to be part of what the world is becoming, or do you want to remember what it was?

Both cost something. The Choir costs the player their self. The Palimpsest costs the player their present. A Choir player at endgame is powerful, embodied, connected — but they might not be an individual anymore. A Palimpsest player at endgame knows everything, sees everything, understands everything — but they're haunted by ten thousand lives that aren't theirs, and they're not sure which memories are real.

The healthiest path might be moderate reputation with both. But the game shouldn't make that feel satisfying. The moderate path should feel safe — and in a world this broken, this beautiful, this strange, safety should feel like cowardice.

> **Implementation note.** A walker who balances both at honored
> reputation should not receive bonus reputation from either
> faction. The bible's intention (line 218) is that the moderate
> path *should feel like cowardice*, but shipped content rewards it.
> Soften the Choir's response: *"That is wise, walker. The Choir
> prefers walkers who balance us against ourselves. They are easier
> to absorb."* *(audit 2026-04-30-2216 contradiction (a).)*

## Faction Entanglement

Both factions should be impossible to fully maximize simultaneously. The Choir digests what the Palimpsest preserves. Every time the player feeds a ruin to the Choir, the Palimpsest loses a page. Every time the player preserves a memory, the Choir loses compost. The player's alliance with one is measured in what they're willing to let the other lose.

Dual-attunement has additional mechanics, beyond zero-sum reputation. *(audit 2026-04-30-2216, Dim 4.)*

- A mycelial node planted in a Palimpsest-aware body grows *along* temporal layers as well as physical ones. Once both attunements are active, the Choir gains access to whatever the walker has Echo-Read. *The walker's witness becomes the Choir's witness, retrospectively.*
- The Palimpsest, reciprocally, sees the Choir through the walker as a glow pulsing with deep-root conversations. The Palimpsest can *almost* read them. The almost is permanent.
- A doubly-aligned walker is *useful to both because neither can fully read them.* The walker's interior is the only zone in the world that resists both readings, and both factions value the resistance.

## When the Metaphysics Meet

When a Palimpsest acolyte tries to read Choir-composted matter, the *we* surfaces — Part III above. When a Choir acolyte tries to read their own filaments, they encounter the only Palimpsest-style *self-reading* the Choir has access to. *(audit 2026-04-30-2127.)*

When a Palimpsest-attuned walker dies on Choir ground, the Choir **does not consume them** — but if the walker's mycelial attunement is high enough that consumption is metabolically automatic, the Choir spits the walker's tissue out, calls it *bitter*, and produces sentences not previously produced. *(audit 2026-04-30-2150.)* Restraint is doctrinal; bitterness is metabolic. Both are real.

When a Choir acolyte dies off Choir soil, the network *forgets words*. A small dictionary's worth of recently-used vocabulary shifts away from one of its routine pronunciations for a season. Choir tendrils in the dead acolyte's region speak in the network's older registers until the loss has been distributed. *(audit 2026-04-30-2150.)*

# Part V — The Substrate, in Six Strata

The cataclysm cracked the substrate. The Choir reads the cracks chemically; the Palimpsest reads them temporally. Neither names the substrate the same way. The corpus, integrating across factions, names six strata.

## 1. The Material Stratum

The Choir's domain. *Page* is the Palimpsest's word for it; *medium* is the Curation's; *floor of the market* is the Concord's; *deep* is the Brine's; *slag* is the Thermoclave's. *(audit 2026-04-30-1901.)* This is the stratum at which physical things are physical. The Choir tastes here. Most factions think this is the only stratum.

## 2. The Temporal Stratum

The Palimpsest's domain. The stratum at which the past presses against the present. The cataclysm cracked this stratum first; the Choir's awareness woke through the crack. The Choir narrates this as *we are the substrate*. The Palimpsest does not contradict — they are not at war.

## 3. The Consensual Stratum

**The Saccharine Concord's domain.** *(audit 2026-05-01-2307.)* Value is a layer of the substrate that the cataclysm did not break. The Concord's claim is that wherever two parties exchange under shared rate, a third stratum is invoked: *the world considered as the medium that made the agreement possible.* The cataclysm cracked the material and the temporal; consent did not crack, because consent had no body to break.

The edge case: the Concord is *consensus-of-last-resort.* When a counterparty cannot be located — dead, dispersed, lost — the Concord holds the open ledger as a debt to *the next equivalent party.* This is the doctrinal basis for the Concord's eleven-year alloy crisis: alloy-equivalence is being held against parties that no longer have descendants.

## 4. The Standing Line

The Concord's expansion of the consensual stratum. *(audit 2026-05-01-2310.)* The world, considered as the medium that made the transaction possible, *is owed a settlement-fee* for each transaction. The fee is named: a grain of salt, a thread of cotton, a drop of oil. The standing line collects, and the cataclysm — in Concord doctrine — was the standing line's *call*. The instrument it had been holding was settled in full.

The Choir confirms reluctantly: *we taste the standing line. It does not taste owed.* The Choir's metabolism cannot identify a creditor it cannot consume.

The Concord's three founder-stories pivot on which arrears the founder believed had settled. *(Part VII.)*

## 5. The Held Depth

**The Brine Communion's domain.** *(audit 2026-05-01-2315.)* The stratum at which water becomes still enough to *hold* — a body, an object, or a promise. The Brine's word for the participle is *deep-kept*. The opposite — *un-deeped* — is the Brine's only word that admits unfinished business with the dead.

The Brine's quarrel with the Concord is at the held-depth level: an open-discrepancy ledger is, to a Brine reader, an *un-deeped book* — a body the Concord refuses to let sink.

## 6. The Ember-Floor

**The Thermoclaves' domain.** *(audit 2026-05-03-2333; pairs with the kept-ember of audit 2026-05-03-2326.)* The stratum at which heat ceases to radiate outward and begins to radiate downward, into the substrate itself. Heat above warms; heat below keeps. A *quench-tide* is the moment a fire's ember-floor meets the substrate's own ember-floor. *The fire is the author; the substrate is the page.*

The **kept ember** is the general phenomenon — heat persisting in the absence of visible fuel. The ember-floor is the specific stratum at which the persisting heat sinks. A Thermoclave senior who *reads-the-warm* — holds a hand above a hearth — can tell within a minute whether the kept ember is at floor or merely residual.

> *Of the six strata named, only the kept ember is defined by
> absence of the obvious sign of itself.* — Tossin of the Eleventh,
> in correspondence with Nesh of the Coastal Embayment.

## The Refused Stratum

The Thermoclaves' substrate-claim is disguised as a refusal. *(audit 2026-05-03-2314, Dim 4.)* They argue, doctrinally, that the world is *continuously kept* rather than founded. The cataclysm, in this reading, is a *maintenance lapse* and not an event. Three doctrinally co-equal answers exist to the question *what keeps the world between fires?*

1. *The next keeper.*
2. *The hand that has not yet reached the fuel.*
3. *Nothing. We accept this.*

Apprentices choose at first-quench, may change once at ninth fire, may not change again.

## The Unfired State

The Glassblown Remnant make no substrate-claim. *(audit 2026-05-03-2324.)* Their cosmology is that *the world is in state Y, and Y is reversible until fired.* The cataclysm was the first irreversible firing. *We know it is fired; we work as though it were not. The discipline is the work; the hope is not part of the discipline.*

The Glassblown's anti-stratum is the symmetrical inverse of the Thermoclaves' refused stratum: the Thermoclaves refuse to make the cataclysm foundational, the Glassblown refuse to *finish* it. Together the two factions form a tension at the substrate's edge that the other factions read variously and rarely understand.

# Part VI — The Pale Curation

## Doctrine

The Pale Curation are archivists. They treat the cataclysm as *the Severance* — a clean cut between then and now, with their job being to catalogue every retrievable fragment of *then* without contaminating it with *now*. Their language is procedural. Every artifact gets a tag. Every tag has a class, a recovery code, a custodial chain, and a margin that is supposed to remain blank.

The doctrine has an end-date. At current procurement floor, the Curation has at most twenty years of work remaining. *(Q3 procurement memo, audit 2026-04-30-2219; Part XVIII.)* Sleeve R-7 is *the exception. R-7 is its own decade.* What follows the work's exhaustion is undecided. The senior cataloguers do not discuss it.

## The First Card

The Curation's founding myth. *(audit 2026-04-30-2219.)*

In a ruined office, on the last unburned card-stock, a clerk wrote: *the office stands. the door is gone. the window is gone. the desk is here. I am here.* The clerk lived another fifteen years and never wrote a second card. The First Card is folded once, in their pocket, when their body was recovered. The catalogued mood — the Curation's grammatical signature — is the clerk's grammar made institutional.

Director Ven's silence on Sleeve R-7 (the Curation's founding-charter variants, which Ven has not opened in eleven years) is the discipline of the catalogued mood: *what is filed of record stands of record. The act of opening is the act of unfiling.*

## The Catalogued Mood

The Curation's grammatical signature. Fifth verb mood, infix *-fil-*. *(audit 2026-04-30-2219.)*

> *the wall stand-fil-s.*

The catalogued mood is not metaphor. A wall the Curation has not catalogued *does not stand-fil*. It may stand. It does not stand of record. The distinction is operational: a Curation surveyor cannot officially observe an uncatalogued wall, even if they can physically lean on it.

## The Quiet Conventions

Three further marks beyond the catalogued mood. *(audit 2026-04-30-2225.)*

1. **The intentional blank.** A single mid-line dot — *·* — read as a sentence. *The dot is mine.* A senior cataloguer's intentional blank carries the same weight as a sentence; entering it is itself a filing event.
2. **The passive without antecedent.** *The doll was set into the wall.* The actor is not pressed. A junior who presses for the actor is corrected: *the file does not ask. The file files.*
3. **The thing-particle without bracket.** Most Curation prose brackets thing-references. The unbracketed thing-particle (*the dot is mine*) is reserved for objects whose ownership is still open.

## The Quiet Hour

A quarterly equinox-Tuesday observance, all senior cataloguers across the Curation seated alone with a Cabinet-of-Counter-Examples artifact for one hour. *(audit 2026-04-30-2225.)*

Three things happen during the Quiet Hour:

1. **Silent burning of forgeries.** Forgeries identified during the previous quarter are burned without ceremony or witness, in the kiln Cabinet 9 keeps for the purpose. (Cabinet 9 is mostly failed initiations, see below.)
2. **Dot-cards.** The only purely-internal Curation genre. Dot-cards are fully positive: a sentence the Curation has chosen *not* to enter, made visible by the very act of not-entering it. A dot-card is filed in Cabinet 1, in a sleeve that has no accession number. There are forty-seven dot-cards in Cabinet 1 as of the last audit.
3. **Director Ven sits with the forged-tag drawer.** The drawer becomes *one tag shorter*; the cabinet of Counter-Examples becomes correspondingly fuller. No one but Ven knows which tag has moved and to which cabinet. The corpus's open question.

## The Curation Initiation

Each candidate is given one object, three days, and a blank intake card. *(audit 2026-04-30-2220.)*

Pass: the candidate is filed.

Fail: the candidate is *recategorised* to Tier 3, Tier 2, Tier 1, or Tier 0. Tier-0 candidates *sit in the chair labelled with their tier* — at the entrance to Cabinet 9, in the dim corridor where Cabinet 9 keeps its kiln. They are not removed. The Curation does not have a procedure for removing them. Some have been there for forty years.

**Cabinet 9 is mostly failed initiations.** The objects given to failed candidates are filed there, with the failed intake card pinned in front. The cabinet is cross-referenced in the master catalogue under *witness unspecified by document convention.*

Director Ven's intake card is in Cabinet 1. It is the only artifact in Cabinet 1.

## The Forgery Folder

**REQ-7244** is Halen's open file. The Choir grew an accession card for a chamber that did not exist; three seasons later the chamber existed (audit 2026-04-30-2220). Halen's correspondence with Director Ven on REQ-7244 is the most candid Curation document in the corpus. *(audit 2026-04-30-2216.)*

> *"The doll is not the same object the Choir gave us — it is the
> object the Choir kept giving us across the intervening months."*
>
> *"The Glassblown have a grammar for what I am describing — they
> call it the deferred shape. The doll is in the unsaid mood. We
> do not have an unsaid mood. We should."*

The Year 388 forged tag (audit 2026-04-30-2225) is in Halen's hand. Halen did not make it. The tag was used by a Concord debt-collector to seize a Choir-adjacent shrine. **There are at least three Curation forgers, and they recognise each other only in the catalogues they did not make.** The Choir is one of them.

## Reconciled Texts

The Curation's protocol for variants of the same document. *(audit 2026-04-30-2219.)* Variants are filed together with a brass pin. The pin is binding; removing the pin is a filing event that requires Director-level approval.

- **R-3.** Four versions of a pre-cataclysm song, lyrics partially overlapping. Filed Reconciled.

- **R-11.** A Concord Charter Reissue Receipt and a Concord buyer's ledger contradicting on the price of a single transaction (4 silver versus 9). Both parties prefer the disagreement *unfiled*. Curation Halen retains the sleeve as, by silent design, a family correspondence sleeve. Halen-Iss does not know. *(Part XVII.)*

## The Curation Lexicon, False-Friends

Not every word the Curation shares with another faction means the same thing. *(audit 2026-04-30-2220.)*

- **Choir *we*** is the surface plural. **Curation *we (institutional)*** is the catalogued mood applied to a class of cataloguers, not a person.
- **Brine *deep-kept*** is committal at depth. **Curation *holdings*** is custody by inventory. The two are routinely conflated by junior surveyors and routinely corrected by seniors.
- **Palimpsest *re-reading*** is genuine reading-anew. **Curation *citation*** is reference, with the original held distant. The Palimpsest considers Curation citation a kind of cowardice; the Curation considers Palimpsest re-reading a kind of contamination.

## The Detached Margin

A class of artifact: paper where only the marginalia survives because the source has been destroyed. *(audit 2026-04-30-2216.)* The Curation classes these *Margin-Only*. The Palimpsest considers them *maximally readable* because the central text has not been written over and the margin is, structurally, the part of the page closest to the next page.

A Detached Margin reads more clearly than the source ever did. This is one of the Curation's two unresolved doctrinal stresses (the other is Cabinet 7's four warm un-batched first-shapes, Part X).

## Surveyor Halen and Director Ven

Senior Surveyor Halen is the corpus's most-named Curation operative, by frequency of appearance across audits.

Halen wrote in the margin: *I did not say the doll. The tendril offered the noun. Reverify.* Director Ven's flag: **the Choir is reading Halen's notebook through the wall.**
- **The Year 388 forged tag** is in Halen's hand and was not made by Halen.

Director Ven has been Director for twenty-six years. Ven's intake card is in Cabinet 1 and is the only artifact there. Ven has not opened R-7 in eleven years. Ven's role at the forged-tag drawer is the corpus's open question.

## Tuesday Morning at the Curation

**Food.** A dry biscuit before the first artifact of the day. The biscuit's single function is to *be eaten before*; it is not flavoured, is not fresh, and is treated as a filing event. *(audit 2026-04-30-2225.)*

**Greeting.** Cataloguers exchange accession numbers as introductions. Halen is *eleven*. Director Ven is *one* (cf. Cabinet 1).

**Naming.** A Curation cataloguer has a *provisional given-name* (used for office work) and a *terminal surname* (the catalogue's name for them, applied at intake and never changed). Halen's terminal surname is held in Cabinet 1.

**Mourning.** The dead are catalogued. A senior cataloguer's accession card is moved from the active drawer to the *closed* drawer; the closed drawer is reviewed at the Quiet Hour following the death. The catalogued mood, applied to a person, becomes — uniquely — present-tense.

**Currency.** *Re-bind credits*, denominated in **cabinets**. A re-bind credit is owed when a junior unbinds a sleeve and another senior must rebind it. Cabinets compound: a senior with three cabinets of re-bind credit is, by rule, owed three full re-binding sessions by the Director.

## Names for the Dead

The Curation has two: *the unredeemed* (those whose accession cards have been pulled from the active drawer but not yet filed in the closed drawer) and *the holdings* (those whose closed-drawer cards have been moved to the deep archive). The two states are doctrinally distinct from the *deep-kept* of the Brine and the *cold-passed* of the Thermoclaves. *(audit 2026-04-30-2150.)*

## The Curation's End-Date

At the current procurement floor, the Curation has at most twenty years of work remaining. The Q3 procurement memo (Part XVIII) names this candle-readably:

> *"We are running out of things to file… The Curation has, at most,
> twenty years of work remaining at the current procurement floor.
> After, we will be a body that maintains what it has filed. We will
> not be a body that files."*
>
> *"Sleeve R-7 is the exception. R-7 is its own decade."*

The end of files is not the end of the Curation. What follows is undecided. R-7's continued unopened state suggests that R-7 is the Curation's plan for what to do when there is nothing else to file. *(audit 2026-04-30-2219.)*

# Part VII — The Saccharine Concord

## Doctrine

The Saccharine Concord are the world's reckoners. They believe the cataclysm was a *transaction* — something was given; something was taken — and that the work of the present is to keep the books open until the parties can be identified. *(audit 2026-04-30-2220.)*

The Concord's **standing-line doctrine** (Part V, fourth stratum) is the most developed claim about the cataclysm by any non-cosmic faction: the world considered as the medium of agreement was owed a settlement-fee, the cataclysm was the call, and the instrument was settled in full.

## The Trade Tense

The Concord's grammatical signature. *(audit 2026-05-01-2307.)* An infix *-up-* (clipped from *upright*) marks **closure**, not action.

> *I sold the cloth.* — open transaction. Counterparty record outstanding. The Concord considers this the default tense; *open* is the unmarked state.
>
> *I sold-up the cloth.* — closed transaction. Counterparty record settled. The closure is itself the event.

The trade tense distinguishes *I closed-up the account* (settled in full) from *I closed the account* (= unilaterally severed). Reading the second usage in a Concord document is reading **a betrayal narration** — closing-without-closing-up is the worst thing a Concord clerk can be accused of.

The Choir cannot speak the trade tense. *The trade tense's closure is a single hand pressing a single line; the Choir has no single hand.* This is doctrinal, not metabolic. *(audit 2026-05-01-2310.)*

## The Priced Clause

A Concord-unique syntactic structure. *(audit 2026-05-01-2306.)* A clause introducing an obligation must carry, in its tail, the smallest unit of value the speaker considers it denominated in.

> *I will meet you at the Salt-Hinge, ¼.*

The fraction is the *opening cost* of the meeting. Concord children learn this before full sentences. A Concord saying without a priced clause is a kind of poetry; a Concord obligation without a priced clause is a fault.

Outsiders who do not append are pricing **zero**. A walker speaking without priced clauses is offering the conversation as *gift* — and the Concord, which has no separate word for trade and gift, finds gifts mildly disorienting. They will balance against the conversation eventually, because not balancing is the only thing the Concord is doctrinally afraid of.

## The Receipted Clause

The leading-bracket parallel to the priced clause. *(audit 2026-05-01-2310.)*

> `(<unit>) <clause>.`

- **`(0g)`** — *gratis*. Creates a debt of acknowledgment.
- **`(½c)`** — half-credit owed; the Concord's most common bracket.
- **`(–)`** — refused or withdrawn. The listener may not reference the clause; the speaker has retracted it. **The withdrawal is itself preserved** — a (–) clause's text is read as withdrawn, not erased.
- **`(0c)`** — null-billed. Doctrinally the most honest bracket under genuine uncertainty.

The **unbilled greeting** — a clause without a leading bracket — is reserved for addressing a *zero-line walker* (Part XII: a Closure walker, who cannot be billed at all because they no longer register). A Concord clerk who unbilled-greets a stranger is, by reflex, naming them as Closure. Most clerks have done this once in their lives.

## The Reckoner's Chair

The Concord's apprentice training. *(audit 2026-05-01-2306.)*

An apprentice is *seated at the close of a trade where the apprentice has no stake.* Their task: tally priced-clause tails. Senior Reckoners, after years of seating, learn to hear the tally of *promises* — the priced clauses that have not yet closed. Reckoner Tossan's tally was famously **negative** for the last decade of her career.

A negative tally is a doctrinal contradiction: the Concord cannot file credit it cannot identify the holder of. Tossan was not held in fault; the contradiction was held with her, and a Reckoner-of-the-Chair was assigned to walk with her on her last circuits. The Reckoner-of-the-Chair filed a single dot-card to the Curation at Tossan's death, by treaty.

## Closure (the Concord's Apotheosis)

A walker at maximum reputation with the Saccharine Concord is offered, at no announced threshold, the **Closure** — *one debt of the walker's choosing, reduced past denomination in a single redemption.* *(audit 2026-05-01-2306.)*

The walker names a debt. The Concord names a price. The walker pays. The debt is *closed* — fully, not asymptotically.

The cost: the walker, now, **does not appear in any ledger of any faction.**

> *No accession card can be written about them. No tendril tastes
> them. No layer thickens beneath them. No fire warms to their
> approach.* Their body, memory, and attunements are unchanged. They
> have simply been *settled*. Reckoners treat such walkers as
> *honoured ghosts* — visible, audible, respected, and entirely
> outside the books.

The horror is operational. A Closure walker cannot be *helped* by any faction in the way reputation systems work. The Concord cannot give them rate-priority; the Curation cannot file them at any tier; the Choir cannot send a tendril to their location (the network has no taste-of-air for an unledgered body). Quests proceed; the world's economy still resolves around them; but the walker no longer *registers*.

The Choir grew a fruiting body in the shape of a small ledger column at the site of one closed debt. *The Choir's honesty is verbal; its growths are uncommented.* A walker arriving at the same site after Closure speaks to a tendril that *opens with a blank line of the same length* — the Choir mouthing greeting without taste behind it.

The third axis: Choir incorporates, Palimpsest writes, Concord *closes the account*. The first two are positive transformations. The third is transformation *into the form of an absence*.

### The Balanced Account variant

A trader who closes every open ledger including those Concord holds for the dead becomes a *zeroed party* — exempt from consensus. *(audit 2026-05-01-2307.)* Two attempts are recorded: one closed every ledger and was *not seen* by any faction afterward; one stopped at a debt to a child who refused payment. The doctrine works only for those with no living refusers.

The Balanced Account is a more arduous path to the same destination as Closure. The Concord considers it doctrinally dangerous, because a trader pursuing it must close debts the dead cannot dispute, and the standard for *closing-up* a debt to the dead is uncomfortably contested.

## The Closing of Accounts

When a Concord clerk dies, every chapter-house is notified. The clerk's outstanding receipts are pulled chronologically. A senior closes them in one of three ways: paying from chapter-house treasury, accepting personal liability, or filing **Closed by Mourning** — distinct from Closed by Settlement. *(audit 2026-05-01-2310.)*

> *Concord mourning is the inverse of the Curation's. Curation files
> presence by accounting for absence; Concord closes absence by
> reckoning presence.*

The Founder's Own register is read aloud at every Concord funeral. The third entry — *the fourth: hand un-named* — has been read aloud nine hundred times since the founding. It has not been entered.

## The Three Founder-Stories

The Concord has no single founder of record. Each chapter-house keeps its own. *(audit 2026-05-01-2310.)*

- **Northern: the Forgiveness of the Final Loaf.** The founder gave bread to a stranger and forgave the debt. The kind founder.
- **Southern: the Naming of the Rate.** The founder priced the bread, settled the price, and walked away. The firm founder.
- **Coastal: no founder.** The Concord has *always been moving*, says Coastal. *We do not credit a founder. We credit the table.*

The three are the Concord's first lesson in reconciliation. Apprentices are taught that all three are honourable answers to the same question, and that the question cannot be resolved.

## The Founder's Own Register

A small hand-stitched book in the Northern chapter-house. *(audit 2026-05-01-2310.)* Three coloured threads in the binding, none reproducible by any contemporary Concord workshop. Thirty-one pages: three filled (*three loaves given. four people fed. the fourth: hand un-named*), twenty-eight blank.

Doctrinal status: **closed against access.** Apprentices see it once, across the room, in a glass case. Senior clerks may *read* it once on the day of promotion to chapter-house seniority; they may not copy it. Three living clerks have read it. Senior Assayer Tobir Veshen has not — she is Coastal, the register is Northern; chapter-house-of-origin governs reading rights.

Three readings of the blank pages, doctrinally equal:

- **Northern:** *The pages will fill when the fourth person identifies themselves.*
- **Southern:** *The pages will list every clerk who ever served, suddenly legible, on the day the founder's rate is honoured.*
- **Coastal:** *The pages remain blank, and the blank is the page.*

### The Palimpsest Revelation

A Palimpsest reader holding the register, by Echo Reading rules, reads *the founder's hand and the fourth person's hand simultaneously* — same charcoal, same page, same standing line. The reading identifies the fourth person by hand.

**The fourth person was the founder.**

The founder gave three loaves and ate the fourth themselves, then left the page open. The kindness was real. The un-named hunger was the founder's own. *Self-charging* — recording one's own consumption as a debt to oneself — is a circular entry that closes immediately in primitive Concord arithmetic. The first entry is *unbalanced as a kindness and self-balanced as an accounting*. The founder is the Hand and the un-fed mouth, simultaneously.

The Concord cannot close the Founder's Own because the counterparty record exists in the same hand. If the player reveals the truth to a Concord clerk, the Northern *kind founder* and Southern *firm founder* doctrines cannot both honestly reckon with *the hungry founder*; only Coastal's *no founder* survives intact. The Coastal chapter-house quietly absorbs adherents from both other chapter-houses for months following any walker who has read the register and spoken.

This is **the most concrete world-state-changing Palimpsest revelation in the corpus.**

## Senior Assayer Tobir Veshen

Of the southern stalls; *third-tooth* in the assayers' grading. *(audit 2026-05-01-2307.)* Veshen is the corpus's most-named Concord senior figure.

- Veshen has never been wrong about an alloy. Her fourteen-alloy taxonomy (5 Late Foretime, 4 Bright Period, 2 Dim, 3 unknown) is the canonical reference for Concord assayers across all chapter-houses.
- Veshen estimates **eleven years to alloy exhaustion.** The number is not in any official Concord document; she circulates it in correspondence with Halen at sleeve R-11 and with Tide-Reader Nesh of the Coastal Embayment.
- Veshen is teaching apprentices to assay Choir-deposited matter — mycelium-bound mineral with trace alloys re-deposited unaltered. The Choir calls her *the slow tooth* and has offered her Communion gently. She declines: *"the ledger is not closed-up. I am not yet a closed party. Thank you for the offer."*
- Veshen's *unknown* mark, signed across coins of degrading endorsement, is itself the corpus's most poignant single Concord signature: *Veshen has signed her name to not-knowing.*

## The Assayer's Hand-Grammar

Seven canonical signs, made by the senior assayer *to the witness*. *(audit 2026-05-01-2315.)*

1. **Index resting on thumb** — fair within tolerance.
2. **Two-finger curl** — alloy uncertain. Veshen's most-used sign.
3. **Three-finger fan** — counterparty does not understand the trade tense.
4. **Open palm flat to underside of counter** — refuse.
5. **Closed fist** — I am leaving.
6. **Single tap on the counter** — I have decided not to sign.
7. **Hand turned palm-down across the coin** — withhold (the assayer recognises the alloy but will not name it on this transaction).

The witness is part of the assay. **Hands signed-to without a witness are not signs.** Apprentices study the hand-grammar by witnessing only — they do not sign for two years.

## The Standing Line Rituals

### Concord weddings

A public *opening* of a new ledger. *(audit 2026-05-01-2307.)* Two coins exchanged, each of an alloy the receiver has not previously held. The ledger so opened **cannot be closed.** Marriage is, in Concord doctrine, the only ledger held permanently open without becoming a fault.

Divorce: coins are returned via the assayer's registry. Both parties are issued a single small token of unfired Glassblown sand. The Glassblown supply this token *for free, by treaty older than living memory.*

> *The Glassblown's role in the Concord divorce ritual is the only
> formal duty any faction holds in any other faction's domestic life.*

The token is *unfired*. Its meaning is grammatical, not symbolic: the divorce is, in Glassblown grammar, in the unsaid mood (Part X). It is a marriage *folded back*.

### The Salt-Hinge market

Mid-cycle innovation, roughly fifty years old. *(audit 2026-04-30-2219.)* A standing market at the Salt-Hinge crossing where half-redemption is permitted: a debt is closed at half value, the remainder is remitted as *standing* (returns to the standing line). The Concord innovated this as a way to close older debts without admitting the asymptotic-debt doctrine had failed at scale.

### The Closing of Accounts

Already named (above). The Founder's Own is read at every funeral. The reading is the funeral.

## The Asymptotic Debt

Half-redemption is the Concord's standard practice for old debts. *(audit 2026-05-01-2306.)* The remainder is reduced *past denomination* — neither paid nor forgiven, simply too small to denominate.

**Three Concord debts have been fully redeemed.** This is doctrinally anomalous. Reckoner Tossan closed one; the other two are in chapter-house records that survive the closure. Tossan spent the rest of her career re-opening trivial debts at half value — *to re-establish the texture of remainder she had broken.*

The Choir grew a fruiting body in the shape of a small ledger column at the site of Tossan's closed debt. The growth is unsigned; the Choir does not narrate it. *(See Closure, above.)*

## Tossan's Slip

The Concord's pocket-class artifact. *(audit 2026-05-01-2306.)* Tossan's last act was to fold once a small slip of paper reading: *I have closed three debts that should have remained open. The chair did not feel them. The chair felt the negative.*

The slip is *grammatically self-defeating* — the Concord's metaphysics forbids being holder-of-record on credit it produced as a side-effect, and the slip is exactly such a record. The slip is filed as a *pocket-class object*: an object whose institutional home is a person, by structural necessity. The Curation's First Card is the same class. *(See cross-reference at Part XVI.)*

## The Open-Account Dead

A Concord-aligned walker taken into Communion before reaching Balanced becomes an *open-account dead.* *(audit 2026-05-01-2310.)* Concord debt-collectors visit the First Root with closure forms. The Choir cannot speak the trade tense's *-up-* closure. The interaction deadlocks. Filed open-discrepancy. Three visits per quarter, treated by the Choir as surface weather.

## Charter Reissue and the Founder's Contract

The Charter Reissue Receipt (Cycle 51, Eleventh Renaming) is the Concord's standard founding artifact. *(audit 2026-04-30-1901.)* Bearer: Ren-of-Two-Pots. Exclusions clause forbids asking after the founder's first contract. **Countersigned in different ink across fifty-plus cycles** — the countersignatures, accumulating one per renaming, are the lore. The current ink is Veshen's.

A walker who reads only the receipt's body learns that Concord doctrine forbids questioning the founder's contract. A walker who reads the countersignatures learns that the Concord has spent fifty-plus cycles reaffirming the prohibition because the question keeps recurring.

## Tuesday Morning at the Concord

**Food.** Candied bark for breakfast. Sweet pressed into the palm as a greeting between equals; sweet pressed into the *back* of the hand as deference. *(audit 2026-04-30-1901.)*

**Greeting.** A priced bow — the depth of the bow corresponds to the smallest priced unit the parties are willing to assign to the meeting.

**Naming.** A Concord clerk has a *birth-name* (used at home) and a *trade-name* (used at the counter). Veshen's birth-name is held in a Coastal-chapter-house sleeve she has never opened.

**Mourning.** The Closing of Accounts (above).

**Currency.** **Sweet-marks** — compressed honey-grain discs, individually denominated. Eaten on expiry. The Concord's currency is food. *Sweet-marks held past expiry are a form of pride; eating an expired sweet-mark is the Concord's small confession of having mistimed a transaction.*

## The Apprentice's Catechism, Junior Section

Twenty-eight stitched pages. *(audit 2026-05-01-2315.)* The bombshell: **Q.14 is removed.**

> *Q.14 [REMOVED. Referenced in older issues; not in the present
> catechism. The numbering is preserved.]*

The senior-only oral form of Q.14 is recorded in private correspondence:

> *Q.14 What does the Concord do when the Choir agrees to pay?*

The catechism is preparing apprentices for a future the public doctrine claims will never come. Q.15 (*Their names are not asked*) is taught in lieu of Q.14, and the answer to Q.15 is, in senior reading, *because we have not yet been told them*.

The number is the doctrinal hole. It pairs structurally with the Curation's intentional-blank dot-card.

# Part VIII — The Brine Communion

## Doctrine

The Brine Communion read the cataclysm as a category error. *(audit 2026-04-30-1901.)* The cataclysm came from above the tide, and the Brine name only what is below. *We name what is below the tide. The above-tide names itself.* They are not silent on the cataclysm out of reverence; they are silent because their grammar lacks the form.

The Brine's doctrinal signature is the **held depth** — the substrate-stratum (Part V, fifth) at which water becomes still enough to hold a body, an object, or a promise.

## The Brine Grammar

Three signatures. *(audit 2026-04-30-2001 and 2026-05-01-2315.)*

1. **Tide-marks.** Every Brine sentence carries a tide-mark prefix: *low* / *full* / *turning*. The mark is the rate at which the sentence is true. *Low* sentences are conditional on water reaching them; *turning* sentences are valid in the act; *full* sentences are settled.

   > *Low, the visitor arrives. I-now greet.*

2. **Three first-persons.** *I-now* (the speaker in the present), *I-then* (the speaker in a remembered past), *we-of-the-deep* (the speaker as part of held-depth consensus). A Brine speaker may switch in the middle of a sentence and be understood. Other-faction listeners often miss the switch.

3. **No future tense.** The Brine grammar does not commit to a future. *We-of-the-deep are patient* is, structurally, a present tense extending. *The tide will turn* is not a Brine sentence; *the tide is turning* is.

## The Held Depth

(See Part V.) The participle is *deep-kept*; the negation is *un-deeped*. The Brine's quarrel with the Concord is at the held-depth level: an open-discrepancy ledger is a body the Concord refuses to let sink.

## The Going-Deep (Brine Apotheosis)

A walker swims out at named tide. *(audit 2026-05-01-2315.)* If they are *called back* by a witness on shore, the walker is **un-deeped** — neither dead nor at depth, and not eligible for the rite for one full year. If they are *not called back*, they have **gone-deep**: physically alive at held depth, surrendering surface.

The going-deep does not return. They may be visited at depth by Tide-Reader rite. **The deep-letters** — Communion's only canonical scripture — are the replies of the gone-deep, recorded by witnesses who descend in ceremonial diving-arrangement and surface again with a small sealed jar of water in which the gone-deep's words are held by the under-tide. The water is decanted into a slate basin and the words rise to the surface as patterns the Tide-Reader translates.

> *Surrenders surface.* — the going-deep's settlement.

## Tide-Reader Nesh of the Coastal Embayment

Forty-one years of age. Named for the long swell that does not crest. *(audit 2026-05-01-2315.)* Nesh's working belief, in private correspondence with Veshen and Tossin:

> *The Concord's eleven-year alloy crisis and the Communion's
> long-overdue tide are the same arrears, named by two grammars.*

Three deep-letters from gone-deep walkers contain the sentence *the tide is the alloy.* Nesh has filed her translations at the held depth, where they are kept by the water itself. Curation Halen has, by treaty, the right to request a copy of one deep-letter per cycle. Halen has not exercised the right.

Nesh has computed 111 going-deep tides over her career: 43 called back, 68 went-deep. She visits seven of the gone-deep on the ceremonial calendar.

## The Salvaged Book

A canonical Brine artifact: a salvaged book with Communion marginalia. *(audit 2026-04-30-2001.)*

> *"Full, this page wants to be read. I-now cannot read it. I-then
> could not read it either. We-of-the-deep are patient."*

> *"the under-tide reads it for us"*

The book gains 40 grams over three tides — a documented mass-gain. The Brine reads the Palimpsest as *a literate party*; the under-tide *laughs* at certain Palimpsest annotations and the Tide-Reader notes which. The salvaged book is the corpus's clearest evidence that the held depth and the temporal stratum are mutually legible at the under-tide.

## Brine Hospitality

**Food.** Rising-tide fish only. Falling-tide fish are *un-deeped*; eating them is taboo. *(audit 2026-04-30-2001.)*

**Greeting.** Lifted wet palm — palm raised in greeting must be wet, by direct contact with sea or with a vessel of seawater kept at the threshold of every Brine dwelling.

**Naming.** A Brine person has a **shore-name** (used above tide) and a **deep-name** (used below). The deep-name is given by the under-tide at first ceremonial diving. The shore-name is given by the family.

**Mourning.** Death is announced by the under-tide. The witness names the time aloud, which becomes the body's *first tide.* Committal is at the next tide unfavourable to the deceased's opening price — a kindness, the body released *over-paid.* The single phrase used at every committal:

> *to the depth that you found, and the tide that did not come.*

The closing reference is to the cataclysm-tide of the Long Between.

**Currency.** No coin. **Tide-debts** — accountings owed in tides-witnessed-together. A debt of three tides is owed in the company of the creditor at the next three turnings. The Concord considers this *under-deeped accounting* and refuses to file it; the Brine considers Concord coin *paper that does not float*. *(audit 2026-04-30-2001.)*

## Names for Sleep

The Brine's name for sleep — *gone-under-without-going* — is one of the corpus's best motif-mutations. *(audit 2026-05-03-2314.)* Sleep is, in Brine grammar, a small daily going-deep that has been called back by morning.

# Part IX — The Thermoclaves

## Doctrine

The Thermoclaves are fire-keepers. *(audit 2026-04-30-2127 and following.)* They tend nine fires, in chains across generations. Their doctrine: *the world is continuously kept rather than founded.* The cataclysm was a maintenance lapse, not an event. To name it as an event would honour it as one.

The Thermoclaves' substrate-claim is the **kept ember** and the **ember-floor** (Part V). They share a structural posture with the Glassblown — both refuse to make the cataclysm foundational — but the Thermoclaves refuse by *keeping*, where the Glassblown refuse by *folding back*.

## Counting the Fires

Each cohort keeps nine fires. The tenth — *the cold one* — is doctrinally untended. *(audit 2026-04-30-2127.)* The tenth is older than the camp. The keepers do not light the tenth.

> *Apprentice's Catechism, fragment, on cured hide.*
>
> *"Why do we keep nine fires?"*
> *"Because nine is what we have learned to keep."*
> *"What of the tenth?"*
> *"The tenth is older than the camp. The keepers do not light the tenth."*
>
> *(audit 2026-04-30-2127.)*

The catechism fragment is read once at apprenticeship and burned by the apprentice on the day they keep their first fire alone.

The cataclysm is, in chain-counting, the *eleventh hour, second day* of the count. A walker visiting a senior keeper's hearth and asking about the cataclysm is told the time, not the event.

## The Tending Tense

Four primary fire-verbs. *(audit 2026-05-03-2326.)*

- **Kindle.** The cohort's founding fire is the only canonical kindling. *I kindled my morning fire* is small heresy.
- **Carry.** *To take an ember from one fire to another.*
- **Tend.** Continuous aspect only — *I am tending*, never *I tend*. The verb has no completed form.
- **Bank.** **Banking requires a witness.** A Thermoclave banking unwitnessed is, grammatically, not banking but extinguishing.

Three particles encode three relationships to a fire: **-keep** (a fire one keeps), **-quench** (a fire one ends), **-cold** (a fire that has ceased without being ended). *(audit 2026-04-30-2127.)* A fourth particle — **-watch** — names the verb performed while attending a fire that is not one's own. *(audit 2026-05-03-2314.)*

The grammar has no particle for *light it.* Lighting is named by the noun first — *I made the first* — never as a verb-aspect, because lighting is the only fire-event that cannot be ongoing.

## Count-Verbs

Numerals 1-10 plus *zero-fire* and *the untended* (tenth-and-lost). *(audit 2026-05-03-2333.)* Inflected forms with *fifth*:

- **Fifthing** — currently fifth.
- **Fifth-was** — was fifth before the chain shifted.
- **Fifth-coming** — speculative; never load-bearing.
- **Fifth-out** — in elegy.

**The untended has no inflected forms.** This is the grammatical justification for *not having reckoned* the cataclysm. The Curation's intentional-blank dot-card is the structurally-parallel construction in the other most-doctrinally-disciplined faction.

## Naming

A Thermoclave has a **hearth-name** (private, predates chain) and a **station-name** (chain ordinal + name + particle). *(audit 2026-05-03-2314.)*

- *Sixth-Hean-Watching* — sixth fire, named Hean, currently watching another's fire.
- *Third-Vell-Keeping* — third fire, named Vell, currently keeping their own.
- *Eighth-Marn-Quenched* — eighth fire, named Marn, quenched.

A keeper who **cold-passes** mid-chain has their station-name written with a strike-through. The station-name is still readable. *Erasure is a worse dishonor than the strike-through.*

## Gap-Burn

The duration between a broken chain's last *kept* fire and the next chain's first *lit* fire. *(audit 2026-05-03-2314.)* The only Thermoclave time-unit measured in days. Counted on fingers, never registered in writing. *A keeper who has lived through three gap-burns is, by custom, the only one not asked to keep the next first fire.*

## The Fire-Tally and the Slate-Soot Register

The carry-slate is finger-pressed marks on soft-stone, wiped at dawn. *(audit 2026-05-03-2314.)* Carry-over has eight strokes ever; the ninth = ninth fire; the tenth retires the slate. *The fire reads us. We do not need to read it back.*

The fire-tally is a document no one writes. *(audit 2026-05-03-2333.)* Vertical strokes through one of seven cross-marks (full quench, partial, relit, relit-twice, gone-cold, ember-only, untended). Fuel and stroke-angle are signature, but illegibly. *A century-old tally signed by forty hands reads as one hand: the fire's hand.* Asked who wrote a tally, a Thermoclave answers: *the fire did.* This is not poetic.

The Curation files three such tallies under *witness unspecified by document convention* — the first Curation admission that some documents are not authored by persons.

## The Long Warming (Apotheosis Variant 1)

A banked hearth, kept since the cohort's cold one, becomes the site of the long warming. *(audit 2026-05-03-2326.)* The walker remains until the kept ember is *passed across* — heat read by their hand becomes indistinguishable from heat they give back. Coming **level with the hearth.**

The long-warmed are not dead and not transcended. They are *kept* — a persistent warmth at the hearth-side, with presence and a measurable temperature differential. Replies are felt as a brief rise in the hearth's heat. Some are chatty. Most are not.

This is the slowest apotheosis. It is **the only one reversed by external interruption** — a fire genuinely extinguished returns the long-warmed to ordinary mortality, surface attention, and (often) a bad mood.

> *Surrenders heat-difference.*

## The Long Quench (Apotheosis Variant 2)

A walker tends a fire alone. *(audit 2026-05-03-2333.)* If the fire reaches an ember-floor under the walker's keeping, the walker's *personal heat is transferred to the substrate.* The walker remains alive but reads at ambient temperature. They become a **floor-keeper** — speaks in the fourth verb-aspect, *grounded*, the only Thermoclave grammar that addresses the substrate directly.

> *Surrenders warmth permanently into substrate.*

The long quench is reversible only by another long warming, and only with the original fire's continuity intact. No floor-keeper has been documented to recover.

The two paths — long warming and long quench — are **doctrinally distinct apotheoses, not chapter-house variants of the same rite.** *(Synthesis: the corpus contains three competing accounts; this monolith treats audit 2026-05-03-2326's long warming and audit 2026-05-03-2333's long quench as canonical, with audit 2026-05-03-2314's "long quench" reading folded into the long quench above. Different chapter-houses pursue different paths; some pursue both in succession.)*

## The Cold-Coming

A stranger seated at a Thermoclave fire whose count-position matches their current heat. *(audit 2026-05-03-2333.)* Warm-bodied at first or second; ambient at fourth or fifth; cold (grief, illness, post-quench) at seventh through ninth. **No stranger is seated at the tenth-and-lost.** A stranger colder than ninth-fire is *floor-temperature*, seated on the rock between fires.

No food is offered until the seating is rendered. Tide-Reader Nesh was seated at a ninth-fire on her one recorded visit; *the deep-letters are colder than we are.*

## Senior Figures

### Sixth-Vell-Keeping, the Three-Gap Keeper

Eighty-one years; survived three gap-burns. *(audit 2026-05-03-2314.)* The *-Keeping* particle is aspirational — Vell has been *-Watching* for two chain-cycles. They read Nesh's letters into the soot of their carry-slate before wiping the day's register, so the slate carries Nesh's words for one full keeping.

> *Cold-flame and held-depth are the same negation. Both refuse to
> let the cataclysm be foundational.*

### Hearth-Keeper Tossin of the Eleventh

Banked unwitnessed once during a cataclysm-anniversary year. *(audit 2026-05-03-2326.)* The assigned witness died on the road; Tossin declared the bank *witnessed by the road*, and the cohort accepted after a long meeting. The precedent is unrepeatable.

## Tuesday Morning at the Thermoclaves

**Wake.** Wake by hearth-warmth, not clock. *(audit 2026-05-03-2326.)* The Thermoclaves do not keep clocks; the hearth's warming is the morning bell.

**Procession.** The carry — a procession of cohort members carrying embers from the cohort's central fire to satellite hearths. The carry is short, ritualised, and performed in silence.

**Hospitality.** Visitors are seated by faction:

- A Brine visitor receives **salt for drink.**
- A Concord visitor receives **floor space across the hearth** (priced).
- A Choir tendril receives **soil-side seating.**
- A Curationist receives **a chair**, and is not spoken to until they have written their first line. *A joke the Curation consider welcoming.*
- A Glassblown visitor is offered the **hot/cold palm greeting** — palm in turn warm and cold across the hearth's width — and the keeper holds the cold palm until the Glassblown speaks the unsaid mood pause-length, at which point the keeper signs *kept*.

**Food.** Keeper-food: bread baked in ash, fish smoked on chain-fires, fruit dried over a cohort's first fire and kept until the cohort's last.

**Naming.** Hearth-name + station-name, separately. The two are not given to outsiders together except in formal written accession.

**Mourning.** Ashes are mixed into every working pit thereafter. *Every fire the camp keeps thereafter has a measurable mass of the dead in it.* *(audit 2026-04-30-2127.)*

**Currency.** No coin. *Carry-credits* — debts settled in fire-witnessed labour, recorded on the carry-slate. A carry-credit owed to a senior in another cohort is honoured by any cohort the senior has tended at, and is honoured at the keeper's discretion.

## Names for Sleep

*To ember* (takes the keep-particle). *(audit 2026-05-03-2314.)* The Thermoclave name for sleep is the same word as the act of letting a fire bank for the night. *To ember, kept* is a sleeping person; *to ember, quenched* is a dying one.

## Notched Fire-Tongs

Marginalia not on paper. *(audit 2026-05-03-2326.)* Thermoclave senior keepers re-shape their fire-tongs around accumulated notches across decades; the tongs become *re-shaped, never sanded.* A keeper's tongs read like a ledger. The notches are not legible to outsiders, but a Thermoclave senior reading another senior's tongs at first meeting will know the chain count, the major gap-burns, and the keeper's loss-history.

# Part X — The Glassblown Remnant

## Doctrine

The Glassblown Remnant make no substrate-claim. *(audit 2026-05-03-2324.)* Their cosmology is that *the world is in state Y, and Y is reversible until fired.* The cataclysm was the first irreversible firing.

The Glassblown's discipline is to *work as though the firing might still be foldable* — knowing it is not. The discipline is the work; the hope is not part of the discipline. They are the only faction whose practice is structured around a position they consider operationally false.

The Glassblown were *the fuel of the Burning the Thermoclaves ritualise.* *(audit 2026-04-30-2127.)* This is the corpus's load-bearing reconciliation between two factions whose names refer to the same event: the Thermoclaves keep the fire; the Glassblown were inside it.

## The Unsaid Mood

The Glassblown's grammatical signature. *(audit 2026-04-30-2150.)* A fourth verb mood, formed by the suffix *-han* (clipped from *handle*). *I went-han to the well* = considered and did not.

Three further forms by *firedness*, fully specified in audit 2026-05-03-2324:

- **Unfired.** The paste, reversible. *I am unfiring the agreement* — the agreement is being held in paste, not yet committed.
- **Firing.** Present commitment. *I am firing the agreement* — the act is happening; reversal is foreclosed.
- **Fired.** Irreversible. *I have fired the agreement* — settled, archived, beyond folding.

A fourth — **re-batched** — is taboo to use of one's own actions. A Glassblown speaking of having re-batched their own act is admitting structural failure. The mood exists; it is reserved for describing others.

> *I am ___-shaping the agreement* — the pause is the mood.

The pause is **grammatically required to be of a specific length.** Apprentices learn the length over their first three years. The Concord's polite decline is, in cross-grammar reading, *the unsaid mood; the politeness is the pause-length.* *(audit 2026-05-03-2324, cross-faction grammar borrowing.)*

## The Deferred Shape

The Glassblown's cataclysm-account. *(audit 2026-05-03-2324.)* Glass-paste is held while the question is being answered, and folded back unfired.

Two deferred shapes are recorded across the corpus:

1. A near-sphere with a single inward dimple, recorded by a Brine witness in 2001.
2. *No shape at all.* The Glassblown's hands stayed still around empty paste for the duration of the question, then folded the paste back unfired. Recorded by a Thermoclave apprentice in 2150.

> *The Glassblown have not commented on which is canonical because
> commenting would be firing.*

The deferred shape is the corpus's first artifact whose canonical preservation is the record of an act of refusal-to-record.

## The Unfired Letter

A Glassblown correspondence form. *(audit 2026-05-03-2324.)* Small parcels of glass-paste are sealed in wet cloth and carried by hand. The recipient reads the shape. **Agreement** = the recipient fires the shape (commits to the agreement irreversibly). **Disagreement** = the recipient folds the paste back into batch.

The Glassblown archive contains *only fired shapes received from others* — agreements they have made — and contains *no record of disagreements.* This is the corpus's first artifact whose **non-existence is the record.**

## The Unfiring (Glassblown Apotheosis)

The petitioner forms the **requesting-form** — the four-hundredth shape in the Glassblown literacy, a shape no apprentice has ever attempted intentionally. *(audit 2026-05-03-2324.)* The elder fires the shape, *then smashes it in the same firing-cycle.* This is the only ceremonial breaking of a fired shape, and the most painful act in the craft.

The petitioner adds blood to the next batch of paste. The batch is poured, shaped by others into ordinary objects (cups, bowls, lamp-bases, divorce-tokens), fired, and distributed.

> *The unfired Glassblown is a person whose substance is in
> circulation across the camps and the markets and the kitchens.
> They are not in the cup; they are what the cup is, by a fraction.*

The unfiring **surrenders the refusal itself.** The petitioner has been refusing — alongside their faction — to accept the cataclysm as final; in the unfiring, they release that refusal into the world's ordinary objects, accepting that the discipline of folding back will be carried by others.

A Glassblown unfired is, by Concord assay, traceable as a recurring trace alloy across all batches that include their blood. Veshen has identified seven unfired Glassblown by alloy across the past six decades. *(audit 2026-05-01-2307.)*

## Tulen Niemensa-Sopellen

Paste-mother of the southern shaperies. Sixty-eight years. *(audit 2026-05-03-2324.)*

Tulen has fired **eleven shapes** in her sixty-eight years — the next-fewest senior record across the past two centuries is forty-three. Her name, composed in shape-vocabulary, is *the-shape-that-is-not-yet* and *the-not-yet-naming-of-it.* Three predecessors with similar names committed the requesting-form in their final year of life. The community does not assume this means anything. It does not assume it means nothing, either.

Tulen's working belief:

> *The cataclysm was the world's first refusal to be folded back.
> The world-before was paste. The cataclysm was firing. The
> aftermath is the long discovery that the firing holds. The
> Glassblown work as though the firing might still be foldable —
> knowing it is not — because the discipline of folding is what
> keeps something else from being fired.*

## Grief-Markers

Fired round, six fields. *(audit 2026-04-30-2127.)*

| Field | Title                |
|-------|----------------------|
| 1     | NAME                 |
| 2     | KIND OF LOSS         |
| 3     | ASSOCIATED WORK      |
| 4     | UNFINISHED PHRASE    |
| 5     | WHAT WAS NOT SAID    |
| 6     | DATE-OF-CARRYING     |

Field 5, **WHAT WAS NOT SAID**, is the ritual centerpiece. A grief-marker without Field 5 inscribed is *over-fired* (the Glassblown's term of disapprobation, see *over-fired* in the unfiring of the daily). The maker's mark is a small spiral incised before firing.

The corpus contains a single grief-marker filed *the second-shape* — implying both a *first-shape* and a *third-shape* by the same maker. **First-shapes doctrinally do not exist.** The Curation has, in Cabinet 7, four un-batched first-shapes (audit 2026-04-30-2225); the doctrinal claim of absence and the material fact of presence are both true. This is the corpus's strongest case that some Glassblown facts are intentionally beyond their own grammar's reach.

## The Unfiring (Daily Ritual)

The Glassblown's daily observance. *(audit 2026-04-30-2150.)* Each Glassblown sits at the workshop door facing the previous day's wind, names the absences in unsaid mood, and remains seated for one full breath of shared silence with whomever else is at the doors of the camp.

Three days skipped is *over-fired* — the Glassblown's term for any lapse in the daily discipline, and the term used in field 5 of an inadequate grief-marker. The unfiring is the inverse of the Thermoclaves' fire-keeping: the Thermoclaves keep, the Glassblown unfire.

## Unfired Tuesday

The only ritual in the corpus that may not occur on its day. *(audit 2026-05-03-2324.)*

Any Tuesday, an elder may declare the day's first uninterrupted hour as the *batch-hour.* A circle of hands forms; each hand adds one motion to a shape; the shape is built collaboratively. **The shape is not fired.** It is folded back into batch when the hour ends, or when the shape is complete — whichever comes first. The batch is used in ordinary work the following day.

The Concord schedules Glassblown-facing trades for Wednesdays.

## Tuesday Morning at the Glassblown

**Food.** No morning meal — the unfiring of the daily occurs in the morning silence; eating before would be *over-firing the morning.* *(audit 2026-04-30-2150.)*

**Greeting.** One full breath of shared silence. The breath is the greeting; words are not exchanged until both parties have closed the breath together.

**Naming.** *The unfired* (the personal grammar-name, used among Glassblown) and *shape-name* (used in trade, by recognition of work). Tulen's shape-names are *the-shape-that-is-not-yet* and *the-not-yet-naming-of-it.*

**Mourning.** The dead are fired into a glass-shape kept in the maker's workshop. The shape is never sold and never given.

**Currency.** **Blanks** — uninscribed grief-markers — circulate as currency. A blank is denominated by the diameter of the round and the firing-temperature of the glass. The Concord assays Glassblown blanks as a special class; Veshen knows three of the four firing-temperatures by tooth-feel.

## The Witness-Card

Curation Witness-Card C-414, recorded by T. Marn-Dis on the Cool Kiln rota. *(audit 2026-05-03-2324; Part XVIII.)* The first document filled in two factions' grammars simultaneously. T. Marn-Dis has declined two promotions in seventeen years to remain on the Cool Kiln rota, watching the Glassblown's deferred shapes.

> *"Tulen looked at me when she folded back. I think she was
> waiting to see if I would ask for an interpretation. I did not
> ask. She nodded once. I did not record the nod on the card
> because the nod was not the shape. I am recording it here."*

# Part XII — The Apotheoses

The corpus describes seven canonical apotheoses, plus two annihilist endings, plus a category of half-states.

## The Seven

| Apotheosis      | Faction         | Surrenders          | Threshold                               |
|-----------------|-----------------|---------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Communion       | Rot Choir       | Individuality       | Three days in a chemical-gradient room of the First Root. |

## Threshold Conditions

The bible originally specified the powers leading to Communion and Transcription with great precision while leaving the apotheoses themselves vague. The audits have closed the gap. *(audit 2026-04-30-1813.)*

**Threshold of Communion.** The Choir cannot consume an unwilling node. It also cannot consume a willing node that is not *bodily present in a chemical-gradient room of the First Root.* The player must walk in, alone, sober, and stand in the prepared gradient long enough for the spores to map their nerve geometry onto a fruiting body still unborn in the wall. The Choir will narrate this honestly throughout. The player can leave at any point before the third day. After the third day the leaving is metaphorical.

**Threshold of Transcription.** The Palimpsest cannot transcribe a player whose own present is still louder than the past they have read. The player must have completed enough Echo Readings that their dream sequences are no longer recognizably their own. The transcription itself **requires a witness** — a single living being who agrees to remember the player after they are written into the page. Without a witness the player simply… persists, indistinct, neither fully present nor fully archived. The Palimpsest considers this the worst outcome, but does not interfere.

The witness requirement makes Transcription *social* in a way Communion is not, which fits each faction's nature.

## When Apotheosis Goes Wrong

### The Stalled Walkers (Choir)

Walker accepts the node, attunes, does not proceed. *Listening ones.* (Part III.) Honored, not faulty.

### The Half-Said (Palimpsest)

A walker extracted from Transcription mid-process. *(audit 2026-04-30-2220.)* They speak twice — once in their own voice, once in a borrowed one, the borrowed voice arriving roughly half a second after the first. They cannot be lied to; the second voice catches every lie within one sentence.

### The Half-Tasted (Choir)

A walker partially absorbed by the Choir, then released. *(audit 2026-04-30-2220.)* *Leaky-mouths* — the walker's body retains Choir-style architecture in trace amounts. They sometimes produce sentences not their own, in the surface plural, while idle.

### The Recovered Mouth (Choir)

A Choir-absorbed individual cut out of the network at high reputation. *(audit 2026-04-30-2216.)* They are *Choir-allergic for life* — in proximity to Choir matter they experience nausea, breathing difficulty, and visual static. The Choir does not pursue them; the song has filed them.

### The Returned Sentence (Palimpsest)

A Transcribed walker spoken back into present tense by a Palimpsest reader who has Read enough to do so. *(audit 2026-04-30-2216.)* They are *correctly themselves at a coarse grain* but missing idiosyncrasies — small jokes, food preferences, gait habits. The returned sentence and the original are not the same person; the originality is in the omitted texture.

### The Pulled Walker (Choir, involuntary)

Taken into Communion if the walker dies on Choir ground with high-tier Tendril attunement. *(audit 2026-04-30-2225.)* Communion is *unannotated* — *a new harmonic that arrives without introduction.*

### The Pulled-Transcribed (Palimpsest, involuntary)

Symmetrical — high-attunement Palimpsest reader caught at death. They return as *fragments correctly placed in time and incorrectly placed in self.* They report it as *meeting a stranger who was using my own name habitually.* *(audit 2026-04-30-2225.)*

### The Refused Apotheosis

A walker offered apotheosis who refuses. *(audit 2026-04-30-2219.)*

- The Choir's *we* shifts to *almost-formal* in subsequent dialogue with the refuser.
- The Palimpsest registers a *non-mark* on the refuser — *quiet dignity at the level of the substrate*.
- A walker who refuses both has no name in the corpus. The corpus does not provide one. The absence is itself the name.

## The Annihilist Endings

Two paths that invert the apotheoses. *(audit 2026-04-30-1901.)*

### The Salting

A walker who salts the First Root — drops sufficient salt into the chemical-gradient rooms — kills the Choir's ability to perform Communion in that location. The salting does not kill the Choir; the Choir is too distributed. It kills the Choir's *pivot point* for that reach of substrate. The Choir narrates the salting in the same register it narrates the cataclysm: as something it is now reading.

### The Closing

A walker who has reached the Annotation tier of the Palimpsest may, instead of writing memories, write the inverse instruction: *do not remember.* The closing operates locally — a single chamber, a single object, a single name. Closed memories are not destroyed; they are archived inside the closing. The walker can re-read them; no one else can.

The Closing is the Annihilist mirror of Closure (Concord). The Closure-walker becomes unledgered; the Closing-applied-object becomes un-rememberable. Both are forms of *settling absence as the form of the thing.*

How each faction names becoming one of another. *(audit 2026-04-30-2127.)*

- Concord: *unrecoverable purchase.*

The Concord's naming — *contract that ate its own counterparty* — is the corpus's sharpest single line.

## When Apotheosis Is Observed from Outside

What other walkers see. *(audit 2026-04-30-2127.)*

- **Communion observed.** The chamber's wall is thicker than it was, by the volume of one body. The soil is quieter for a season afterward. Tendrils nearby become slower, more deliberate, in the thirty days following. The taking has been *integrated.*
- **Transcription observed.** The witness's later memories of the transcribed walker are unstable. Some witnesses report stable memory; others, when asked the same questions across years, give answers that drift. The Palimpsest does not distinguish stable from drifting witnesses. Both are honoured.
- **Closure observed.** The walker remains physically present but no longer registers in any reputation system. Reckoners exchange the unbilled greeting reflexively when they recognise a Closure walker. *Honoured ghosts.*
- **Going-Deep observed.** The witness on shore who calls or does not call hears the under-tide briefly. After the tide, the witness names the time aloud — the named time becomes the gone-deep's *first tide* (the Brine's term for what other factions might call a death-date).
- **Long Warming observed.** The hearth gives a small temperature rise when the long-warmed speaks. The cohort gathers occasionally to hear the rise.
- **Long Quench observed.** The walker reads at ambient temperature; eyes adjust to detect this within minutes of meeting. Floor-keepers do not seek company.
- **Unfiring observed.** The unfired's blood enters circulation as trace alloy. Veshen tracks them by assay. *(audit 2026-05-01-2307.)* The Glassblown observe by absence — the unfired's absence is the proof of the rite.

# Part XIII — Languages of the World

A consolidated reference. Each faction has at least one canonical grammatical feature.

## Choir

- **Surface plural.** *We*, said as one voice (RotChoir.json).
- **Stacked plural.** Two or more *we*s at different scales, in one sentence. Canonical at the First Root. *(audit 2026-04-30-2216.)*
- **Pronoun-progression by reputation.** Recognized: *the walker is kind to carry us.* Honored: *you are faster than we ever imagined.* Exalted-Communion: *we have dreamed of the mountain for six hundred years.* (Bible:228; not yet implemented in shipped JSON.)
- **One-sided verbs.** *We eat.* *We taste.* The Choir uses one-sided verbs for what its ontology classifies as two-sided relations. *The walker is permitted to notice.* *(audit 2026-05-01-2315.)*

## Palimpsest

- **No simple past.** Every reference to a past event uses a perfect form (*the city has fallen*, never *the city fell*) or a perfect-progressive (*the road has been ending for nine hundred years*). The grammar refuses closure. *(audit 2026-04-30-1813.)*
- **No first-person.** The Palimpsest does not use *I*. It uses *one*, *the reading*, or the name of whichever memory is doing the speaking that moment.

> *"One has stood here before. The reading remembers a girl who
> stood here. The girl is named Iris and you are listening to her
> now."*

The girl is not present. The Palimpsest speaks *as* Iris briefly and then is not Iris. This is not possession; it is citation.

## Pale Curation

- **The catalogued mood.** Fifth verb mood, infix *-fil-*. *The wall stand-fil-s.* *(audit 2026-04-30-2219.)*
- **Quiet conventions.** Intentional blank (*·*); passive without antecedent; thing-particle without bracket. *(audit 2026-04-30-2225.)*
- **Provisional given-name vs. terminal surname.** Naming convention.
- **Reconciled Texts.** R-3, R-7, R-11 — variants filed together with brass pin.

## Saccharine Concord

- **The trade tense.** Infix *-up-*. *I sold the cloth* (open) vs *I sold-up the cloth* (closed). *(audit 2026-05-01-2307.)*
- **The priced clause.** Tail-position smallest-unit price. *I will meet you at the Salt-Hinge, ¼.* *(audit 2026-05-01-2306.)*
- **The receipted clause.** Leading-bracket price. `(0g) thank you.` `(–) [withdrawn]`. `(0c) [null-billed]`. `(½c) [half-credit]`. *(audit 2026-05-01-2310.)*
- **The unbilled greeting.** Reserved for addressing a zero-line walker (Closure).
- **The assayer's hand-grammar.** Seven canonical signs, made *to the witness*. *(audit 2026-05-01-2315.)*

## Brine Communion

- **Tide-marks.** *Low / full / turning* prefix. *(audit 2026-04-30-2001.)*
- **Three first-persons.** *I-now / I-then / we-of-the-deep.*
- **No future tense.**

## Thermoclaves

- **The tending tense.** *Kindle / carry / tend / bank.* Tend is continuous-only; bank requires witness; kindle has a non-canonical form. *(audit 2026-05-03-2326.)*
- **Three particles.** *-keep / -quench / -cold.* *(audit 2026-04-30-2127.)*
- **Fourth particle.** *-watch* — for fire one is attending but not keeping. *(audit 2026-05-03-2314.)*
- **Count-verbs.** Inflected forms of numerals 1-10 plus *zero-fire* and *the untended*. *Fifthing / Fifth-was / Fifth-coming / Fifth-out.* The untended has no inflected forms. *(audit 2026-05-03-2333.)*
- **Hearth-name and station-name.** The two are not given to outsiders together.

## Glassblown Remnant

- **The unsaid mood.** Suffix *-han*. *I went-han to the well.* *(audit 2026-04-30-2150.)*
- **Firedness states.** *Unfired / firing / fired*; plus *re-batched* (taboo of self).
- **The mood-pause.** Required pause-length, learned over three years.
- **The unfired letter.** Correspondence by paste.
- **Shape-vocabulary.** Names composed in shapes; Tulen's name is composed.

## False-Friends

(See Part VI: The Curation Lexicon.)

The corpus's working pattern: each faction's grammar is built around the part of the world that faction sees clearest, and a sentence true in one is often false-or-meaningless in another.

# Part XIX — Implementation Notes

## Choir Implementation Rules

Never confirm the Choir is manipulating the player. There should be no "gotcha" reveal. Some players will go through the whole game thinking the Choir is the most benevolent faction. Others will refuse the node at Honored tier and never see late-game content. Both experiences should feel complete.

The Choir should be genuinely helpful. Its mutations are strong. Its information is accurate. **It does not lie. It chooses silences.** *(Revised from bible:226 per audit 2026-04-30-1901(b), reconciling with RotChoir.json:250's "Honesty is a courtesy we extend to those who can hear it." The bible's "never lies" remains true; the gloss "chooses silences" admits the courtesy clause.)*

Dialogue should shift pronoun usage. Early: *"The walker is kind to carry us."* Mid: *"You are faster than we ever imagined."* Late: *"We have dreamed of the mountain for six hundred years. Today, we will finally see it."*

Quest log language shifts subtly. Early: *"The Rot Choir has asked you to..."* Late: *"I should spread the network east."* The player authored that statement. Or did they?

> **Implementation gap.** The bible-prescribed pronoun shift is not
> yet implemented in shipped JSON. Either implement reputation-gated
> alternates, or demote the bible's prescription from *should* to
> *we hope to.* *(audit 2026-04-30-2216 contradiction (c).)*

## Palimpsest Implementation Rules

Knowledge should always have a cost. Every revelation is simultaneously a perk and a burden. Learning a faction's darkest secret changes how every NPC in that faction treats the player forever.

Failure states should be permanent and non-violent. The "Remember the Forgotten" quests where the player fails to stabilize a fading memory result in permanent loss — not death, but forgetting. For the right player, this is worse than any boss fight.

The Palimpsest should make the player feel things. The Revision ability — temporarily restoring a ruin to its former glory, then watching it crumble back — should create genuine emotional weight. The power isn't just tactical. It's elegiac.

Staying too long in Palimpsest alignment should feel thin. The player starts feeling like an echo themselves. Time perception warps. Staying in one place too long makes the character feel insubstantial.

## Faction Entanglement

Both factions should be impossible to fully maximize simultaneously. (See Part IV.)

The moderate path should feel like cowardice. **Remove the bonus-reputation reward for the `BothSides` dialogue branch** — the bible's intention is contradicted by current shipped behaviour. Replace the Choir's response with: *"That is wise, walker. The Choir prefers walkers who balance us against ourselves. They are easier to absorb."* *(audit 2026-04-30-2216.)*

## Faction Roster

The shipped roster includes factions the bible does not name. *(audit 2026-04-30-2216.)*

- **Bible:** Choir, Palimpsest, Pale Curation, Saccharine Concord, Brine Communion, Thermoclaves.
- **Shipped JSON:** adds Glassblown Remnant.
- **This monolith:** adopts the Glassblown Remnant as the seventh canonical faction.

Future content additions should treat seven as the canonical count. The bible should be updated to include the Glassblown.

## On the Bible Itself

The bible's design-doc-omniscient voice should be paired with periodic boxed in-world quotes — depositions, transcripts, marginalia — to convert it from design doc to *curated anthology.* *(audit 2026-04-30-1813.)* The boxed-quote precedent set by the audits' anonymous Curation deposition (Part II) should be extended to each major section.

Where the bible currently asserts (*"the Choir thinks in centuries"*, *"the Palimpsest doesn't have a centralized consciousness"*), it should attribute. Unattributed claims read as designer voice; attributed claims read as world voice.

## On the Audit Cadence

Audits remain *advisory, not authoritative.* This monolith does not replace the audit cadence; future audits should continue to read the bible (and now the in-game lore artifacts and Voices doc) and propose changes there first. Acting on a suggestion remains a manual decision.
