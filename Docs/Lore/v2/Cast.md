# The Cast

> The named people the player will meet, in roughly the order they
> meet them. Each entry is one paragraph; details are in the v1
> compendium for those who want more.

---

## In the starter village (Reedhaven)

### Old Beren — *the village's lay archivist*

Late seventies, slight, dignified. Was a Curation surveyor for thirty
years before he retired and came home to Reedhaven. Keeps the village's
only book in his rear cottage — a hand-stitched volume of recipes,
prayers, marsh-songs, and contracts going back a century. The book is
incomplete. **Pages are missing.** Beren has been waiting for someone
to walk into the village who could go and find them. The player is who
he was waiting for. He will teach the player to read marsh-rye stalks
for water-content (a kind of Echo Reading lite), give them a list of
the missing pages, and suggest where to start.

His preferred greeting: *the rain has been generous this season.*
(It has.)

### Lin the Marsh-Mother

Beren's grand-niece, ~28, runs the village inn and the village. Practical,
sharp, has had to be the adult in the room since she was nineteen. Will
sell the player a meal, a cot, and a piece of advice in exchange for
basic chores. She is the player's first transactor — every rate she
quotes is in sweet-marks, and she expects priced clauses in return.

If asked, she will say she has no time for the Choir, no time for the
Palimpsest, and *especially* no time for the Sealhand Crusaders who
have been seen on the high road lately. She means it. She also makes
the best apple matz in three days' walk.

### Pell the Stitcher

The village's bookbinder. Mid-fifties, missing two fingers from a
binding-press accident decades ago. Stitches the marsh-rye fibre books
the village sends to traders. Gentle. Likes the player on sight if the
player is gentle back. Will repair anything you bring him, in exchange
for the player listening to a long story about his late wife.

### Marl the Salt-Mouth

The village drunk. Not hostile; just present. Sits on the inn's porch
day and night with a small flask. Will tell the player what he has
seen on the high road in exchange for a sweet-mark. He has seen more
than anyone realises.

If the player gives Marl three sweet-marks in one visit, he will sober
up briefly and tell the player something true: *the man with the iron
hand passed through here three weeks ago. He bought salt.*

## On the road, early

### A bowl-maker — *Brine Communion, dry-walking*

Walks the high road between Reedhaven and the Salt-Hinge. Older than
she looks; perhaps thirty-five. Trades found vessels (cups, mugs,
hollow stones, dried gourds) for sentences in the *remembered tense* —
sentences about water absent from the room. If the player gives her a
sentence about home water, she will give them a bowl. She does not
tell the player which bowl was meant for them.

She will be on the road most days. If the player asks her about the
Sealhand, she will look up at the sun and say *the salt has been
moving lately. Inland. That should not happen.*

### A new tendril — *Rot Choir, surface*

A young fruiting body, barely a year old, stationed at a footpath
crossing two days east of Reedhaven. Not yet a slow voice; speaks in
*we* but the *we* is thinner here than at older tendrils. Will offer
to *taste* the player on the air, will report what the soil tells it
about the player, and will accept any small offering as toll. Whether
it eats the offering or sets it into the wall depends on whether the
offering *minds being eaten*. The tendril will not say which.

If the player asks about the Sealhand, the tendril will pause and
then say *we taste them. they taste of fire that does not yet know
what it is burning. tell us when they come closer. we are ready.*

## At the Pale Curation chapter-house

### A. — *junior cataloguer*

~22, the player's first Curation contact. Tireless, soft-voiced,
quietly furious at the institution. The player meets her at the
intake desk after-hours. If the player brings her the marsh-rye book,
she will catalogue it properly *and* write an unofficial walnut-ink
note saying what she actually thinks the missing pages mean. The
unofficial note is the more useful document. A. is the player's
permanent ally inside the Curation, even when officially she is not
allowed to be.

### Surveyor Halen — *Tier-11 cataloguer, senior*

Halen is who A. wants to be in twenty years. Filed the doll-in-the-
wall recovery (accession 11-K-3318), is the only senior who treats the
junior cataloguers with full respect, and writes unauthorised
footnotes that the Director, Ven, prefers not to see. She will help
the player but she will warn the player: *every revelation costs.*

### Director Ven — *senior of the Hyphal Surveys Office*

Speaks more frequently in the on-record voice than any other surveyor.
Has not opened **Sleeve R-7** — the Curation's contested founding
charter — in eleven years. Will treat the player as either *a useful
piece of evidence* or *a problem to file*; her judgment is fast and
not always right. The player will need to decide whether to trust her.

## At the Salt-Hinge market

### Brem the Apprentice — *Saccharine Concord*

The player's first Concord contact. ~19, in his third year, sitting in
the Reckoner's chair at a busy market stall. Will teach the player
how to *hear* a trade by tallying its priced-clause tails. Bright,
fast, and entirely unaware that the Concord he is training to serve is
metaphysically running out of time.

### Senior Assayer Tobir Veshen — *senior figure*

Thirty-one years a Concord assayer; never been wrong about an alloy.
Has been re-classifying *Foretime-uncertain* coins as *unknown* for
three years now. Carries a private estimate she has shared with two
apprentices: **Foretime stock will be exhausted in eleven years.** Has
been writing a single sentence to Keeper Suul for sixty-one days now.
She will help the player if the player demonstrates seriousness about
something the Concord cannot openly say.

### Reckoner Tossan

The only living Reckoner to have closed a debt fully — an old Brine
debt, settled with a half-tide of weather no one expected. Carries
**Tossan's slip** in her outer pocket: a folded paper documenting the
closure that the institution structurally cannot file. She has, three
times, set the slip on the central balance-stone and walked away. No
one took it. She will not take it again. If the player asks, she will
hand them the slip to look at, then take it back.

## In Ember Order territory

### Renne — *smoke-born apprentice, eleventh-fire cohort*

Sixteen. The player's first Embers contact. Keeps an unauthorised
hearth-log in pencil margins, recording what the senior keepers
will *not* write down. Knows more than she lets on — including that a
Choir tendril asked her hearth-keeper, in plain words, whether the
Embers' *kept ember* and the Brine's *held depth* were the same thing
sampled differently. Renne has not yet been given her station-name.

### Hearth-Keeper Tossin of the Eleventh — *Renne's master*

Banked unwitnessed once, during the cataclysm-anniversary year, when
her assigned witness died on the road. Declared the bank *witnessed by
the road* and her cohort accepted. Unrepeatable by deliberate
construction. Speaks rarely. The player will earn her respect or fail to.

### Keeper Suul of the Ninth

The senior figure. Forty-seven years a keeper. Was apprenticed at
fourteen to **Erro**, who tended the tenth fire at the moment of the
Overwriting and did not survive. Suul is *lineage-orphaned*. She will
read the player by their hands first, their face second, and what they
say only after that. She has been reading Veshen's letters for sixty-
one days, and not yet replied.

## At the Cool Kiln (Glassblown)

### An unfired child

A Glassblown child who has not yet been given their adult shape-name.
~8 years old. Sits in a workshop doorway with a ball of paste between
her hands. Will form a shape while the player asks a question and then
fold it back into the batch unfired. The forming is the answer. Most
travellers don't realise this.

If the player sits with her in the morning sun and does not press,
she will eventually form a small fired shape (a salt-spoon, a
finger-ring, a single lens) and put it in the player's hand. The
player will not know what it means until later.

### Tulen Niemensa-Sopellen — *paste-mother of the Cool Kiln*

The senior figure. Sixty-eight years a Glassblown. Has fired only
eleven shapes in her career. Her name means *the-shape-that-is-not-
yet* + *the-not-yet-naming-of-it*. The keeper of **the Six-Almost** —
a single kept paste, two centuries old, refused six times by six
different paste-mothers. She will not speak to the player until the
player has spent at least one full breath of shared silence in
earshot.

## In the First Root (Choir, late game)

### Mogu, Grib, Nam, Sien, Sopp — *the five slow voices*

Five named fruiting bodies the Choir has kept *individuated* over
long time. Their pre-Choir voices are still audibly themselves; they
disagree with each other, with the Choir's surface tendrils, and
sometimes (audibly, in their voices) with the Choir's own collective
*we*. Sien is the oldest. Grib is the most recent, and the most openly
in disagreement. Mogu speaks in his grandmother's cadence. Nam brings
warm soil to no one in particular. Sopp does not approach the chess
game.

The Choir does not require them to agree. *The song wants every voice
it has ever heard. It does not want them to agree.* They are stable.
They are not the goal. They are simply the shape of the song in this
corner of the network.

### Bole-Without-Number — *the shared bole*

A single fruiting body of cataclysm-era growth near the Curation's
third reading-room. Five non-Choir factions have, by accident, each
elected this same bole as their official Choir-interlocutor — under
five different names. None of the five factions knows the others did.
The Choir, knowing, considers it a small private joke. The player can
discover the truth by asking the bole questions in five faction
grammars in a single visit. The bole will say *this one signs* — *this
one keeps* — *this one rises* — *this one is intaken* — and one more,
in the layered tense.

## On the high road, mid-game

### Iron-Hand Anselm — *the fortieth Crusader, leader of the Sealhand*

The antagonist. Forty-one years old; the first Crusader born after the
Sealhand's founding. Lost his left hand to a Palimpsest reading at
fifteen — the iron prosthesis he wears is intentional; he could afford
better. Believes, with absolute sincerity, that the world will be
saved when the last reader stops reading and the last tendril is cut.

The player will see Anselm three times before they speak with him
once. The first sighting is at distance, on horseback, leading a
column of his own through a burning village (not a village the player
loves; a village that did not survive). The second is at the Salt-
Hinge market, refusing trade with a Concord assayer who refused him
credit. The third is in a Curation chapter-house's antechamber, where
Anselm has come to *negotiate*. He does not negotiate. He explains.

The player can side with him at this third meeting. If they do, the
endgame becomes the Sealing. If they refuse, Anselm marks them.

### Sealmaster Iro — *the second Sealmaster*

The other Sealmaster, met later. Quieter than Anselm. Older. Was a
Curation surveyor for twenty years before her conversion. Knows the
Curation's chapter-houses from the inside, which is why the Curation
fears her more than they fear Anselm. She runs the Sealhand's *book-
fires* personally; she will not delegate the burning of a manuscript.

If the player has the marsh-rye book of Reedhaven on them when they
meet Iro, she will recognise it.

## In the Cosmic Wood (Palimpsest, late game)

### A first-time Echo-Reader — *encountered before the player becomes Re-pressed*

A young walker, ~24, who has just discovered the Glimpse in a battered
field-stone where two roads cross. She will be on the stone for six
hours. Her water flask is empty. When she opens her eyes, she does not
focus quite the same way she did this morning.

She does not know yet that she is becoming Re-pressed. The doubled
tense will start appearing in her speech in three to four months. By
then she will have forgotten this conversation, but not the field-
stone.

She is a glimpse of the player's own future, if the player chooses to
read.
