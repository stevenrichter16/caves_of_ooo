# Caves of Ooo — Lore (v2 Redesign)

> A simpler retelling of the world. Everything in this directory is a
> *parallel redesign* — none of it modifies the canonical bible, the
> audits, the compendium, or the v1 surface materials. The v1 work
> remains in place at `Docs/Lore/`. This `v2/` directory exists
> alongside it.

---

## What this is

The v1 lore corpus (the bible, 25+ audits, the compendium, the graphs)
is dense at every layer. The v1 simplification work — the primer, the
glossary, the faction one-pagers, the starter NPCs — added a *legible
surface* but did not change the underlying shape. The world is still
seven factions, seven substrate-strata, nine apotheoses, six contested
cataclysm-accounts, and dozens of named seniors writing each other
letters across grammatical lines they cannot translate.

That world is *literary*. It reads like an anthropology of a discipline
that doesn't exist. It is excellent for the obsessive reader. It is
hard for a player to step into.

This directory is the world *redesigned to be as simple as Caves of
Qud's*. Caves of Qud reads densely on its wiki and accessibly in its
opening hour. A player wakes in **Joppa**, meets **Mehmet** the
watervine farmer, and is given a fetch-quest by **Argyve** the
tinkerer. Within thirty minutes they understand: this is the world,
these are the people, this is what I do today. The cosmology — Eaters,
Sultanate, Resheph, the Coven, the Argent Fathers — is *available*
across hundreds of hours of play, but it is never the price of entry.

The v2 redesign aims for that shape. The cosmology becomes one clean
paragraph. The factions become six clear cultures (five from canon
plus one new antagonist). The starter village (**Reedhaven**) and the
starter NPC (**Old Beren**) and the starter quest are defined in full.
A reader spending fifteen minutes in this directory should be able to
play the world, not just discuss it.

## What's in here

- **[World.md](World.md)** — the cosmology, the cataclysm (named **the
  Overwriting**), the two cosmic forces, in ~2500 words. The single
  document a new player needs to read.
- **[Factions.md](Factions.md)** — the six factions, including a new
  antagonist invented for this redesign: **the Sealhand Crusade**.
  Tagline + one section + one starter contact each.
- **[Cast.md](Cast.md)** — the named NPCs the player meets in
  approximate order across a campaign. ~14 figures, one paragraph
  each.
- **[Places.md](Places.md)** — the world map. ~10 named locations,
  starting with Reedhaven. One paragraph each.
- **[Opening.md](Opening.md)** — the starter village in detail and the
  first three quests that introduce the world. The Joppa-equivalent.

## What changed from v1

| | v1 (canon) | v2 (redesign) |
|---|---|---|
| The cataclysm | Six contested accounts; duration also contested. | **The Overwriting**, one event, one cause. |
| Cosmic forces | Choir + Palimpsest with substrate-strata, populated middles, doubled mouths. | Choir + Palimpsest as forces. Powers and apotheoses kept. The middles and the cross-axis are mentioned briefly, not foregrounded. |
| Factions | 5 canon + Choir + Palimpsest + Glassblown amendment, and 1 implied (the founders' contract counterparty). | 5 canon + 1 new antagonist (Sealhand Crusade). Choir + Palimpsest are cosmic, not faction. |
| Substrate-strata | 7 strata + 1 state-claim, each with a faction. | Mentioned only via the Choir's *soil* and the Palimpsest's *page*. |
| Faction grammars | 7 fully-specified grammars (catalogued mood, unsaid mood, layered tense, fire-grammar, count-verbs, etc.). | Mentioned as flavour — *each faction has its own way of speaking* — without specifications. |
| Apotheoses | 9 named end-states + middles + reversals + failures. | 3 named: **Communion** (Choir), **Transcription** (Palimpsest), **the Sealing** (Sealhand). Middles and failures kept as Tier-3 detail. |
| Senior figures | ~24 named seniors with full correspondence triangle. | 1 senior per faction + 1 starter NPC per faction. |
| The cataclysm name | *the cataclysm* (general); each faction has its own name. | **The Overwriting** (everyone's word). |
| The Thermoclaves | Opaque proper noun. | Renamed: **The Ember Order**. |
| Starter village | None defined. | **Reedhaven** — a marshy bookbinder-village on the edge of Choir territory. |
| Starter NPC | None defined. | **Old Beren** — the village's marsh-rye farmer and lay archivist, the Mehmet equivalent. |
| Starter quest | None defined. | "Find the missing pages of the village's old book" — leads the player to all six factions in turn. |
| Antagonist faction | None. | **The Sealhand Crusade** — book-burners who want to *finish the Overwriting* by sealing the cracks shut. |

## How to read this

If you have **15 minutes**: read [World.md](World.md). You will know
the world.

If you have **30 minutes**: also read [Opening.md](Opening.md). You
will know how to enter it.

If you have **an hour**: also read [Factions.md](Factions.md), browse
[Cast.md](Cast.md), and skim [Places.md](Places.md). You will know
the people and where they live.

If you have **longer than that**: the v1 corpus at the parent directory
is the deep work. This v2 redesign deliberately leaves it untouched.
The two coexist. A reader who comes through v2 and grows curious will
find the v1 audits and compendium ready and waiting.

---

## What this redesign cuts

It is honest to say what was sacrificed.

The v1 corpus's two greatest contributions are (1) the faction grammars
as full systems, and (2) the cross-faction triangle of senior
correspondents. The v2 redesign foregrounds neither. The grammars are
mentioned only as flavour; the triangle is mentioned only in the
extended World.md. Anyone who falls in love with the v2 surface and
wants to understand *why every faction speaks differently* or *how a
single sentence travels across three grammars to do a single piece of
work* should read the v1 audits, beginning with audit
[2026-05-04-0845](../audits/2026-05-04-0845-lore-audit.md) — the
cross-faction-correspondents pass.

What v2 gains in legibility, it loses in literary density. The trade
is deliberate. Players need an entrance. Readers can find the depth.
