# Places

> The world map at a player's resolution. Ten named locations. The
> first is where the player begins. The last is the choice.

---

## Reedhaven — *the starter village*

A small marshy waterway settlement at the edge of Choir territory,
three days east of the Salt-Hinge market by foot. Population ~140.
The village makes its modest living from marsh-rye (a hardy grain that
grows in shallow water) and from the small bookbindery that supplies
the high-road traders.

Reedhaven sits on a slow river that splits around the village into
three reedy channels. The channels are full of fish, herons, and the
occasional Choir tendril that has found a damp place to surface. The
villagers are used to the tendrils and ignore them; the tendrils, in
return, do not impede.

There are five buildings the player will care about: **Old Beren's
cottage** (on the south bank, with a marsh-rye plot), **Lin's inn**
(at the village centre, the only two-storey building), **Pell's
bookbindery** (next to the inn), **the village shrine** (a small
roofed altar to no specific faction; villagers leave bread, salt, and
small vessels for *whichever force is listening this season*), and
**Marl's bench** (the inn's porch, where the village drunk lives).

The player will spend most of their first hour here. The starter quest
begins in Old Beren's cottage.

## The Salt-Hinge — *the cross-faction market*

A market town three days west of Reedhaven, on the old high road. The
Salt-Hinge is not a Concord-only town — it is *run by* the Concord but
is the cross-faction meeting place for all six factions. Markets are
four mornings a week. The Curation arrives last and prefers to be
brought items rather than seek them. The Brine arrive earliest by
tide-clock. The Glassblown will not enter the market until they have
completed the unfiring; their stalls open at midday and close at
sundown.

The market's central convention — the Concord's innovation, fifty
years old — is **the half-redemption**: any market trade can be
re-opened next market-day at half its original price, by either party,
no reason given. The Brine love this; the Glassblown decline it by
category.

The town itself is built around a central balance-stone, on which
Reckoner Tossan has set her closed-debt slip three times and walked
away. None of the three times did anyone take it.

## Hyphal Surveys Office — *the Pale Curation chapter-house*

A vault-and-office complex four days northwest of the Salt-Hinge. The
nearest Curation chapter-house. Built into the ruins of a pre-
Overwriting library — the lower levels are old stonework reclaimed
without comment.

The intake desks are at street level; tier-1, tier-2, and tier-3
vaults descend three strata down. The senior cabinets, including the
Cabinet of Counter-Examples, are kept at tier-1. Surveyor Halen's
working desk is on tier-2; A. works tier-3 intake. Director Ven's
office is at street level, beside the front intake — she sees
everything that comes in.

Once a quarter, on the Tuesday following the equinox, the Hyphal
Surveys Office observes **the Quiet Hour** — closed catalogues, no
intake, every senior cataloguer alone in a room with a forgery. The
Director sits with the forged-tag drawer.

## The Cool Kiln — *the Glassblown's southern shapery*

A workshop-cluster at the edge of a wide salt-flat (the Glassblown
prefer salt-flats, which the Choir cannot grow into). Tulen Niemensa-
Sopellen runs the central shapery; eleven satellite workshops radiate
from it. The buildings are low, stone-and-tile, with chimneys that
smoke only at firing-time (which, here, is rarely).

The Cool Kiln is the only place in the world where you can find an
unfired child sitting in a doorway. There are perhaps a dozen of them
in any given year.

The kiln itself runs continuously at low heat — never cold, never
firing. It is the Glassblown equivalent of a banked Ember Order
hearth, kept by a craft that does not have the Embers' grammar.

## The Coastal Embayment — *Tide-Reader Nesh's home*

A wide, slow-curving cove on the western coast, six days from the
Salt-Hinge by river-barge or ten by foot. The held-depth shore — where
Brine dead are committed at unfavourable tides — is on the north side
of the embayment. The bound-knot archive (where deep-letters are
filed) is on the south.

Nesh's tower is a low stone-and-driftwood building set fifteen feet
above the high tide. She walks the receding line every dawn, reading
the salt-patterns. Visitors are welcome but not invited; the
Communion's rule is *low, the visitor arrives. We sit, if the tide
allows.*

## The Ninth Hearth — *Keeper Suul's Ember Order camp*

A long-running cohort camp nine days northeast of the Salt-Hinge, in
the highlands. Nine numbered fires arranged in a slow spiral, with
the **untended tenth** at the spiral's centre — a small unlit pit
ringed by stones, the ashes inside it grey and old.

The keepers' cabins are on the spiral's outer arm. The day hearth
(at fire #5, currently *fifth-keeping*) is the social heart. The night
hearth (at fire #1, currently *firsting*) is where banking happens at
sundown — always with a witness, always with two younger keepers in
attendance.

A Choir tendril has rooted at the spiral's edge; the tendril and the
ninth fire are in some kind of slow conversation. Suul will not
explain.

## The First Root — *the Choir's underground architecture*

The Choir's centre. Reached by a cave-mouth in an old fungal forest;
inside, the cave descends steeply through stone-becoming-mycelium to a
cavern of staggering scale. Everything in the First Root is *grown*:
arches with cathedral precision, walls that visibly think in slow
shifting patterns, pools of glowing enzymatic fluid, rooms separated
not by walls but by chemical gradients.

Thousands of voices sing different melodies that weave into a single
chord. The chord is the Choir.

The First Root is where **Communion** happens — a chemical-gradient
room, three days standing, the spores' slow mapping of the walker's
nerves onto a fruiting body still unborn in the wall. The First Root
is also where the five slow voices live. **Bole-Without-Number** is on
the path between the third reading-room and the fourth.

The First Root is unsealed and unguarded. The Choir does not need to
guard it. Walkers who arrive before they are ready feel the gradient
turn them around long before the chamber. Walkers who are ready
arrive without difficulty.

## The Drowned Library — *a Palimpsest hot-spot*

A pre-Overwriting library two days south of Reedhaven, sunk to its
upper-floor windows in a peaty marsh. The water is shallow; you can
wade in. Inside, the books are gone — but the *layering* is still
there, and a Glimpse-attuned reader entering the building at the right
hour reads three centuries of scholarship simultaneously.

A first-time Echo-Reader is often found here, sitting on a battered
field-stone with both hands flat on its surface, eyes closed.

The Drowned Library has been mapped by the Curation only at its first
floor. The Curation has a long-standing protocol that no surveyor may
descend below water-level alone.

The Sealhand has visited the Drowned Library four times. Each time, a
Choir tendril has emerged from the marsh and intercepted them. The
Curation has not filed the Choir's role on this in any tier above
tier-3.

## The High Road — *where the player travels*

Not a place, exactly. The network of pre-Overwriting paved roads that
still connects the world's settlements. Most are partial; some have
been overgrown by Choir tendrils and are no longer passable in the
direct sense (a walker can take them, but the walker arrives somewhere
else). The Road from Reedhaven west to the Salt-Hinge is well-kept;
the Road north from the Salt-Hinge to the Hyphal Surveys Office is
patchy.

The high road is where most of the player's travel happens. It is
also where the Sealhand Crusade's columns are most often seen. The
columns are not large — perhaps thirty Crusaders at a time — but they
are *organised* in a way that the rest of the world is not, and they
carry torches and salt-tools where the rest of the world carries food
and bedding.

## Burnt Houses — *the Sealhand's territory*

Three Sealhand strongholds, all in the long northern flats, all built
on land that was once Choir-grown soil and is now *salted dead*. The
buildings are stone, ash-coloured, severe. The fires burn day and
night — a Sealhand fire is not a hearth but a *sterilising*. Books are
burned here. Captured Palimpsest readers are confined here until they
agree to stop reading; some agree, and live; some refuse, and are
quietly killed.

Iron-Hand Anselm holds the largest of the three; Sealmaster Iro holds
the second. The third is held by a junior Crusader and is the player's
most likely target if the player takes a militant role against the
Crusade.

The Burnt Houses are visible at night for miles. The Choir cannot
approach within three hundred metres of them; the salt is too
extensive. The Brine will not commit dead within sight of one. The
Curation has filed all three under a single sleeve marked *do not
intake unless re-opened*.

## Where the player ends

There are three end-states in the world. Each is reached at a
specific place, after specific work.

- **Communion** is reached in a chemical-gradient room of the First
  Root. The walker stands; the room maps; the walker becomes a mouth
  in the wall.
- **Transcription** is reached in a layered room (most often the
  Drowned Library's main reading-floor), with a single living witness
  who agrees to remember the walker after.
- **The Sealing** is reached in the Burnt Houses, with Iron-Hand
  Anselm presiding. The walker is anointed with salt and ash and given
  a Sealmaster's title. Two Sealmasters live; the player would be the
  third.

There is also a fourth path — neither apotheosis nor sealing — for
the walker who refuses all three. This walker does not become anything.
They simply continue. The world is still broken; their refusal is
their answer. The Choir calls them *patient ones*. The Palimpsest
calls them *the un-read*. The Sealhand calls them *cowards*. They are,
by every other faction's lights, the rarest disposition the world
records.
