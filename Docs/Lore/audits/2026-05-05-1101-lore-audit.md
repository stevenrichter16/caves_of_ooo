## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible line 208:* *"They don't hate each other the way the
> Thermoclaves and Brine Communion do."*

**Strong.** Asserts hostility as calibration baseline for the Choir–
Palimpsest pair. Three-body intransitive-verb alignment of cataclysm-
accounts (Brine *the deep-coming,* Curation *the unindexed loss,*
Glassblown *the glass-day,* 0901); 1001's two-body agentive alignment
(Thermoclave *the cold morning,* Choir *the world is becoming*).

**Thin.** No body-account of *how the Thermoclave–Brine hostility began.*
The bible names hostility as fact; neither body's mythic-historical
register holds it.

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar, "*The salt-out morning* and
*the bell that drowned* — two accounts of one rupture"):

> By Thermoclave reading, the rupture is *the salt-out morning* — the
> second cold morning, six bells after the first, when a Brine strand-
> warden arrived at the founders' salt-cellar with a stone tied in
> linen and asked to lay it among the founders' coals. The hearth-
> warden refused. The strand-warden left the stone on the cellar-step.
> By Thermoclave folk-account the stone was a *brine-stone* — a sea-
> cold thing meant to dampen the coals from within — and the refusal
> saved the hearth from a second drowning.
>
> By Brine reading, the rupture is *the bell that drowned* — a sea-
> bell, cast in salt-bronze and tuned to the deep-coming, brought
> ashore as a gift for the founders' first hearth-bell. The founders
> refused. The strand-warden left it on the cellar-step. The bell
> rang once at next tide and was washed back. By Brine working
> register the founders refused because *the cold morning* would not
> parse alongside *the deep-coming* in any single bell-tone.
>
> Both accounts name the same morning. Each names the other body's
> offered object as the threat. Both objects are the same object — a
> salt-bronze bell with a stone-knot at its clapper — read by two
> body-grammars as two different objects.

The corpus's first **paired refusal-myth.** The Thermoclave–Brine
hostility now has a mythic-historical floor at the rupture the bible
asserts as fact, and the floor is a single object the two bodies cannot
agree to name.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine Communion

Rotation: Brine last at 0601, five sittings back; the rotation lands on
Brine by topical alignment with R7's seam-content.

**Etymology.** *The Brine Communion.* Strand-walker compound, both halves
preserved in working register, only the second read literally.

*Brine* is the strand-walkers' term for *the carrying salt* — distinct
from *sea* (the body of water) and *salt* (the substance). The carrying
salt is the dissolved salt that performs the strand's working
procedures: cuts cleat-stones, weathers names, conducts tide-cycles, and
(in folk register) *carries the deep-coming inward* one tide at a time.
*Brine* names the working agent, not the substance from which it is
extracted.

*Communion* is the term for *the cycle of being named and re-named at
the cleat-stone.* A strand-walker is in communion when their name has
been cut in the present quarter-tide. The communion breaks at next
quarter-tide unless the strand-warden re-cuts. The cycle's preservation
across quarter-tides is the strand-warden's standing duty.

The full pre-doctrinal term was *the Brine Communion: those who are
re-cut at every quarter-tide by the carrying salt.* Modern usage
retains the cleat-stone, the quarter-tide cycle, the re-cutting, and
the dry-naming as the cycle's only working departure. There is no Brine
doctrine apart from the working cycle.

**The dry-naming.** Every strand-stone, when a strand-walker has tied
themselves to a Thermoclave hearth-member or otherwise removed
themselves from the tide-cycle, performs the dry-naming at the next
quarter-tide. The strand-warden walks the strand-walker to the shoreward
stone — a granite block above any working tide-line, founder-placed, no
strand-warden named — and names them aloud once. The strand-warden
walks back in silence equal in duration to the walk out.

By Brine working register the dry-naming is the carrying salt's
acceptance that this strand-walker has left the cycle. By seam-body
reading it is the closed-pair signing's Brine half: a witnessed walk
(the shoreward stone bears the worn marks of forty-three documented
quarters of named-and-walked-away strand-walkers), a structural witness
(the shoreward stone itself, the only stone the strand-warden visits
without a tide-stone in hand), and a working record (the tide-log entry
*walked-out, fourth quarter, dry-named*). The corpus's most-performed
two-body operation has had its Brine half named only at R7 sitting 1.
