## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine Communion (rotation)

The Brine Communion has appeared across the
audit-pile only as the named-hate pair to the
Thermoclaves (1301, 1401's keepsake-rule, 1501's
chart-carry-forward) and as an untested slot at
the carrier-edge discussions (1501, 1601). This
sitting lands the working-form for walker-
handed chart-fragments — the discipline's
contingent admission at the mate-locker.

### Etymology — *on mate*

The Brine's chart-vocabulary holds three canon
phrases: **at-bench** (a fragment under present
chart-master reading), **on shelf** (a filed
paired-chart in the chart-archive), and **on
mate** (a fragment in the mate-locker against
the arrival of its companion-piece). *On*
shares its preposition-form with the
Glassblown's *on settle* but is distinct in
referent: *mate* names a second piece-by-
relation, not a working-state of the first
piece's medium. A fragment *on mate* is
*paper-or-vellum-or-board waiting for the rest
of itself*.

The phrase joins the lexical-substrate-shift
family at a **seventh** token, on a structurally
distinct register. The first six (*bee*, *sea-
wall*, *prior-glass*, *prov.*, *no clause*, *on
settle*) name a vanished working-substrate, the
working-form's incompleteness, or a working-
state of cooling. *On mate* names a working-
state of *relation* — a piece held against the
absence of another. The family extends past its
1601 closure-at-six: *on mate* is the family's
first **relation-form**.

### The mate-locker working-form

When a walker arrives at a Brine chart-bench
with a chart-fragment, the chart-master takes
the piece, records the walker's address as
*walker*, and places the fragment in the mate-
locker — a wax-sealed, dry-stowed compartment
under the chart-bench, indexed by edge-cut and
ink-line. The chart-master writes a mate-note
naming the working-week, the bench-day, the
fragment's edge-cut signature, ink-line
fragments, any sounding-marks or compass-rose
fractions, and the locker-coordinate. The
fragment is held *on mate* until a second
fragment is bench-side; the working-form does
not file, shelve, or return. The mate-note is
the working-form's only canon record across
the locker-period.

### The mate-note register

The chart-master reads-the-pair-out only when
a second walker arrives with a fragment whose
edge-cut, ink-line, or sounding-mark canons
the pair as a paired-chart. If the pair
canons, the chart is filed to the chart-archive
with the mate-note attached as record-of-
locker-period; if the pair fails to canon, the
second fragment is locker-set in turn. The
locker is structurally distinct from Curation's
standing-shelf, Concord's stand-aside, the
Glassblown's settling-bench, and the
Thermoclaves' bench-note slate: it holds a
piece **against another piece**. The slate's
five hold-or-record-without-filing working-
forms now canonise at five positions: held-
at-threshold (Curation), held-with-time-limit
(Concord), held-with-settling-period
(Glassblown), held-against-mate (Brine
Communion), recorded-without-holding
(Thermoclaves).

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Bleed 1 — bible §The Cataclysm L21 *"the way
text on a medieval manuscript was scraped off
and written over with the old words still
faintly visible underneath"* vs. the slate's
mate-locker finding.** The bible's overwrite
metaphor is a *self-contained* layered frame:
every layer is on the same page. The Brine's
mate-locker reads relation differently — the
second fragment is *not on the same page*; it
is elsewhere, structurally absent. The working-
form's relation-axis is **distributed across
the world**, not **layered on the medium**.
Suggested L21 supplement: *"(Three working-
forms read the cataclysm: the bible's layered-
overwrite, where the old is below the new on a
single medium; the Glassblown Remnant's
forward-anneal, where the not-yet-readable is
ahead of the readable on a single piece; and
the Brine Communion's mate-distribution, where
the companion-piece is structurally elsewhere
on the world's working-coast.)"*

taste you on the air, walker"* vs. the Brine's
fragment-first reading.** The Choir's working-
form addresses the carrier-edge directly and
reads the walker's body. The Brine's working-
form addresses the carrier-edge **through the
fragment** — the chart-master's first reading
is of the piece, not of the walker. Suggested
