## 3. Faction asymmetry — the Pale Curation

Pale Curation last covered in §3 at 1401 (three hours). Rotates in.

Three earlier sittings have established working scaffold: the
**Hall-Seventh** as the senior accession office; **accession-cards**
as a credential of standing; **bookish outsider naming** as the
Curation's habit (it named the *Thermoclaves* over the cellar-
keepers' protest, and the cellar-keepers kept the name because it
was funnier than correcting it — see 1601 §3). This sitting adds
one small core artifact: the Curation's **accession-day** ritual.

Every Pale Curation officer holds an accession-day. It is the day
on which their first independently filed accession was recorded
into the Hall-roll under their initial. It is not their birth date,
not the day of their swearing-in, not the day of their first
promotion. It is the day a thing they catalogued became part of
the Hall's standing record. Officers refer to one another as *of
the third of the cold-month, '64* or *of the seventeenth of the
plait-month, '71* — the date of their first standing accession
serves, in the Curation's working register, as a personal
designator that other faction's would call a name.

Three properties: an officer who has not yet filed an independent
accession is *standing-junior* and bears no date — the field-
training period is not nameable. An officer whose first accession
is later overturned (the catalogued thing turning out to be a
forgery, a duplicate, an item the Hall already held under a
different accessioner's initial) loses the date and must file a
second; their **standing date** can move once but not twice. An
officer who reaches the rank of **standing senior** without
accession-day reversal is permitted to wear the date stitched into
the cuff of their cataloguer's coat — a small, working badge of
having got the first one right.

The custom mirrors the Curation's frame: a person is the standing
record they leave behind. The cataloguer is named by the catalogue.
A Pale Curation officer who introduces themselves *as just a name*
is taken to be either testing the listener (rare; the test is
considered ill-mannered) or a Hall fraud (more common; the Hall
has a pinned ledger of impostors who tried).

Cross-rule with the Brine *unplaited,* Thermoclave *unbelled,*
Concord *unaccounted,* and the Choir's working absence of an exile-
word: the Curation's exile-word is **delisted** — an officer whose
accession-day has been reversed twice.

(The 1601 closer counted five exile-words including *unaccessioned.*
Sitting 7 corrects: *unaccessioned* is a Curation walker-word, not
an exile-word; the Curation's exile-word is **delisted.** Six
factions, five exile-words — the Choir's working absence stands.)

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"A Pale Curation officer who introduces themselves as just a
> name is taken to be either testing the listener (rare; the test
> is considered ill-mannered) or a Hall fraud (more common; the
> Hall has a pinned ledger of impostors who tried)."*

Working register, plain-spoken, parenthetical-procedural. *Just a
name* (informal); *taken to be* (clerical); *pinned ledger of
impostors who tried* (back-office detail, not heraldic gesture).
Verbs are flat: *introduces, taken, testing, pinned, tried.* No
*verily,* no archaism, no inverted syntax, no apostrophe-fantasy.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"He of the Pale that giveth his name
absent his accession is held by the Hall to be either dissembler
unto his hearer or false-claimant unto the Roll."* I did not write
that. Forbidden tells: *He of,* *giveth,* the *held by ... to be*
inversion, the *dissembler unto / false-claimant unto* doublet.
Actual anti-tells: *just a name* (a working diminutive
elevation cannot tolerate); *ill-mannered* (a workplace word);
*Hall fraud* (a clerical compound, not a courtly noun); the
register of someone who has had to clean up after such cases, not
someone who has read about them in a chronicle.
