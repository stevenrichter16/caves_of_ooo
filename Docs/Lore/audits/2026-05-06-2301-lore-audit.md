## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (JSON L481):** *"The Concord calls it 'the Dissolution'
and sweetens their grief with trade."*

**Strong.** The Concord's cataclysm-name is canon and posture-loaded
(grief plus sweetness — a confession that the trade is itself a
mourning practice).

**Thin.** *Sweetens their grief with trade* is the Choir's
characterisation, not the Concord's account of what it does. The
Concord has not been allowed to say what trade-as-mourning *is*
in its own grammar. Grief and trade are linked by the Choir's
syntax, not the Concord's.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm, append):**

> *The Concord's own account of the Dissolution does not pair grief
> and trade as cause and balm. The Concord teaches that the
> Dissolution dissolved the standing of every prior contract on
> the surface — every promise issued before the event was, by
> Concord doctrine, voided in the same instant the cities fell.
> Trade after the Dissolution is not a sweetening of grief. It is
> the rebuilding of the surface on which any promise can again
> stand. A first-generation Concord clerk's morning prayer, if it
> can be called that, is the recital of the date and a single
> declarative clause: "On this day, contracts are again possible."
> The grief is upstream of the trade. The trade is the only
> medicine the Concord knows for the kind of grief that voids
> contracts.*

Resolves the Choir's gloss into a Concord-side first-principle —
trade not as sweetener but as *the precondition for promises*. A
first-generation clerk's date-recital becomes a falsifiable
ritual any future Concord scene can lean on.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (bible L185):** *"What the Saccharine Concord's founder
actually did."*

**Strong.** The bible commits the Concord's founder-secret to a
single line, gestured at as a high-rep Palimpsest revelation.

**Thin.** Two cross-axis problems with that line. (a) The 2201 R3
established that the Concord *has no founder* — only a
*disclaimed non-founder*, a passing senior who read C-1 and named
the body without signing. (b) The L185 line locates the secret
in the Palimpsest's archive, but assumes a *founder* exists to
have *done* something. The L185 sentence is flat-incompatible
with the post-2201 Concord canon.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §Quest Design, revise L185):**

> *"Unsee What Cannot Be Unseen" — High-reputation quests revealing
> things that should have stayed hidden. The true cause of the
> cataclysm. What the Saccharine Concord's first reader actually
> said in the room where C-1 was filed, before the senior left
> the body unnamed in the document and itself unnamed in any other.
> These revelations are permanent perks with permanent costs.*

The reframe preserves the L185 hook (Palimpsest gets a Concord
secret) and moves the secret from *founder's deed* to *first
reader's words*, consistent with 2201. The unrecorded sentence
is an Echo-Reading target — exactly the kind of thing the
Manuscript ability would extract.

### 2.3 Mutating motifs across cultures

**Quote (2026-05-04 22:01 R3, bible sidebar):** *"Concord:
closure — the act of the clause becoming non-pending. Choir: the
seal-fruiting. Palimpsest: the over-stroke."*

**Strong.** A three-way mutating motif on *closure* exists in
canon.

**Thin.** The motif covers the *act of closing.* It does not yet
cover the **physical token of close** — what the parties walk
away with after a Concord clause is sealed. The token is the
piece of canon a player most likely encounters first.

**Concrete enhancement (bible new sidebar, "Names for the close-token"):**

> - **Concord:** *the stone* — a thumb-sized disc of pressed
>   sugar-and-clay in the colour the Concord calls *first-cane*.
>   Light enough to carry by the dozen. Stamped on close. Each
>   stone is a single ledger-stone of value, the Concord's unit
>   of account.
> - **Choir:** *the morsel* — the Choir treats stones as food.
>   It does not value them at the Concord's reckoning; it values
>   the *sugar weight*. A walker carrying a fistful of stones
>   through Choir territory is, by Choir reading, carrying a
>   fistful of small offerings.
> - **Palimpsest:** *the small page* — a stone is a closure
>   *bound in a portable form*, and Echo Reading on a stone
>   recovers the clause's text and the closing parties' hands.
>   A long-held stone reads cleanly; a recent stone reads
>   thinly because the close has not yet settled into its
>   substrate.
> - **Pale Curation:** *the chit* — accepted as legal tender at
>   Curation depots, accepted reluctantly. Curation cataloguers
>   prefer paper instruments because chits *eat* in the file —
>   a stone left in a folder will draw moisture, soften the
>   paper around it, and (the Curation says) introduce a
>   *contamination-of-record* the chit's bearer is held
>   responsible for.
> - **Brine Communion:** *the dry sweet* — accepted at
>   rendezvous, valued at sea-rate (a stone is a quarter of a
>   day's water), eaten when fresh stocks fail. The Communion
>   does not file the closures; the eating *is* the file.
> - **Thermoclaves:** *the bead* — accepted under duress, never
>   eaten (the *first-cane* colour is too close to the colour
>   of a Refusal-hearth's banked ash, and Thermoclaves do not
>   eat what looks like ash).
> - **Glassblown Remnant:** declines to handle stones at all.
>   The Glassblown's reason for the declining is, predictably,
>   not given.

Seven faction columns; each posture mutates the same physical
object. The Choir and Communion *eat* it; the Curation *files
it badly*; the Thermoclaves *hold but reject*; the Glassblown
*refuse to touch*. The Palimpsest reads it. The motif now has
a tangible referent any future scene can put in a walker's
inventory.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Thin.** The Concord's own cosmology (post-Dissolution: contracts
are again possible) does not yet specify what *makes* a clause
hold. If a Concord stone is the close-token, what physical-
metaphysical principle is it relying on?

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Saccharine Concord, when the
faction has its own subsection, append):**

> *The Concord teaches that a closure holds because the close is
> witnessed in the same instant by both parties and a third
> attesting body. The Concord considers itself the third body.
> The stone is not the closure; the stone is the** receipt **of
> the closure. If both parties' stones are destroyed, the close
> is unaffected — the close is in the world, not in the stones.
> If the** Concord's own copy **of the close is destroyed, the
> close is at risk: the third witness is no longer attesting.
> A clause whose Concord copy has been lost enters a state the
> body calls the** unmoored **— legally still closed, but no
> longer underwritten. The body recovers unmoored clauses by
> finding the parties and witnessing again. Some unmoored clauses
> have not been recovered. The Concord does not advertise how
> many. It is, by Concord doctrine, an institutionally embarrassing
> number.*

A mechanic the Palimpsest can interact with (Echo-Read an unmoored
clause to confirm the close still in the world), the Choir can
exploit (consume the Concord's copy), and the player can
encounter as a quest hook (recover an unmoored clause).

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Quote (2233 enhancement, §2.5):** *"The Saccharine Concord
thinks they overpaid."* (proposed bible addition, not yet
absorbed)

**Strong.** The Concord's posture toward apotheosis is sketched
— *overpayment*, an economic frame for the Choir/Palimpsest
endings.

**Thin.** *Overpayment* needs a Concord-grammar explanation. What
*price* did the apotheote pay, in Concord reading, and what
would a *fair price* have looked like?

**Concrete enhancement (continuation of 2233's bible §The Central
Question append):**

> *The Concord's reading is specific. The price the apotheote paid
> was their** standing **— their capacity to be a party in any
> future clause. A Choir-Communion or Palimpsest-Transcription
> walker is, in Concord doctrine, a body that no longer holds the
> standing to issue or receive a promise; consequently, no clause
> the apotheote authored before the apotheosis remains binding on
* their successor-form**. Outstanding clauses with the apotheote
* fall** unmoored at the moment of the ending. The Concord's
* private term for this is** the great unmooring**, and a
* Concord clerk who has watched a walker reach Communion
* understands why the body files the apotheote's outstanding
* clauses with the procedural-close glyph and the standing-
* reservation glyph both.*

The economic frame is honoured (overpayment = forfeiture of
contractual standing) and integrated with the 2201 procedural-
grammar (the standing-reservation glyph reappears, applied to a
new case). The apotheosis endings now have a concrete
*institutional consequence* outside themselves.

### 2.6 Linguistic texture

**Quote (2233 enhancement, §2.6, Brine Communion proposed):**
*"Communion speech keeps two grammars at once: a working register
for the present voyage... and a song register for everything
older than the speaker."*

**Strong.** Two factions now have specified linguistic signatures
— Choir's pronoun-tier (canon) and Brine's two-register switching
(2233-proposed).

**Thin.** The Concord has a known grammatical formality (clause-
filing, glyphs) but no canonised *speech* register.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Saccharine Concord, append
when faction subsection lands):**

> *Concord speech in the body's working register is** appositional
* and clause-numbered**. A senior clerk addressing a walker will
* often re-state the walker's position as a clause and number it
* aloud — "your standing as I read it, third clause — you wish to
* file." The walker is not expected to follow the numbering;
* the numbering is for the** clerk's **memory of the conversation
* later. Outsiders consistently mishear this as legalese or
* condescension. Inside the Concord it is mnemonic, not formal:
* a clerk with twenty walkers in a tally-day cannot reconstruct
* the day from memory unless each walker's request was numbered
* in the moment of speaking. The numbering** is **the recording.*

Falsifiable rule: any future Concord NPC line should re-state the
walker's position as a numbered clause at least once per
substantive exchange. If a Concord NPC ever speaks at length
without numbering, that NPC is either a junior in training, a
Concord defector, or something pretending to be Concord — all of
which are useful narrative possibilities.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord

The 2201 R3 deepened the body's procedural grammar. This stub
deepens the body's **material life** — what a Concord building
smells like, what a clerk eats, what a stone weighs. Anthropology,
not law.

> ### The Saccharine Concord (material life)
>
> The body's working buildings are called *tally-houses* — the
> word *wayhouse* is outsider-borrowed, used in trade circulars
> aimed at walkers. A tally-house is a single long room with a
> ledger-table down its centre, narrow benches along the walls,
> and a pressing-room behind a curtain at the short end. The
> pressing-room is the source of the body's name: it is where
> the day's stones are pressed from cane-syrup, clay, and the
> day's batch-mark. A tally-house at full work smells of *first-
> cane* (a faintly resinous sweetness; outsiders compare it to
> burnt molasses), warm clay, and the cedar oil the body uses to
> keep the ledger-table's surface from gathering dust.
>
> A junior clerk's morning is built around the *opening*. The
> first clerk to arrive recites the body's date-clause aloud to
> an empty room ("On this day, contracts are again possible")
> and stamps the day's first batch-mark into a single test-stone
> to confirm the press is true. The test-stone is set aside in
> a small clay dish at the head of the table. It is not used for
> trade. At the end of the day, the test-stone is broken with
> the back of the senior's pen and the fragments are eaten by
> the clerks present, in silence, as a closing. Outsiders who
> have witnessed the eating describe it as ritual; the body
> describes it as *not letting the test-stone go to waste.*
>
> A clerk's working meal during a tally-day is *clerk's-bread* —
> a flat, tough oat-loaf wrapped around a strip of preserved
> root or dried fish, eaten one-handed at the bench so the
> ledger-hand stays clean. A walker who has been kept waiting
> long at a Concord office and is offered clerk's-bread should
> understand they have been promoted, in the body's reading,
> from *visitor* to *clause-pending* — the body does not feed
> visitors and does not let pending parties go hungry. The
> bread is bad. Walkers are expected to eat it anyway.

**~310 words.** Working-content; not bible-pile candidate yet.
Contributes if absorbed: the *tally-house* term, the *opening*
ritual (date-clause + test-stone + closing), the *first-cane*
smell, *clerk's-bread*, and the social-promotion-by-feeding
custom. Three of these are §2.7's anthropology request; the
*opening* is also §2.1's first-generation date-clause in
operational use.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Bleed 1 — bible L185 vs. 2201 R3.** The bible asserts a Concord
*founder* whose deed is a Palimpsest revelation target. The 2201
audit established that the Concord *has no founder*, only a
disclaimed non-founder. **The bible line is now incompatible with
its own canon.** Pre-absorption, L185 stands as the source of
truth. Post-absorption, §2.2 above proposes the rewrite. Flagging
this as a documented bleed for the editor's pile.

**Bleed 2 — JSON L338 *"offers sweetness"*.** The Choir's
characterisation of the Concord, in canon dialogue, is reductive
to a degree the post-2201 Concord canon does not support. By
2201, the Concord offers *closure*, not sweetness; sweetness is
a side-effect of the press, not the body's product. *In the
Choir's voice, the line is probably canon-correct* (the Choir
genuinely sees the Concord as a sugar-press the surface walks
in and out of), but the editor's pile should note that the
bible voice should not echo this reduction unintentionally.

**Bleed 3 — JSON L338 *"Glassblown Remnant offers nothing at
all"* applied to stones.** The 2233 §4 already flagged this
line for the Glassblown's posture being *non-offering*. §2.3
above adds a specific instance: the Glassblown *decline to
handle Concord stones at all.* That declining is itself an
offering — of a posture, of a refusal — which sharpens the
2233 bleed: the Choir's *nothing at all* is uncharitable, and
the Glassblown's actual offering is *the absence shaped by
the stone they will not touch.* Flag for the next Glassblown
rotation.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence (from §3):** *"A walker who has been kept
waiting long at a Concord office and is offered clerk's-bread
should understand they have been promoted, in the body's
reading, from visitor to clause-pending — the body does not
feed visitors and does not let pending parties go hungry."*

Working modern register. *Kept waiting*, *clerk's-bread*,
*should understand*, *the body does not feed visitors*: all
ordinary contemporary English doing institutional-anthropology
work. The categories (*visitor / clause-pending*) are the
body's own internal jargon, lower-cased, treated as registry
terms not as honourifics. Three plain verbs (*kept*, *offered*,
*feed*); the conceptual hinge (*promoted, in the body's reading,
from X to Y*) is an HR-handbook construction, not a liturgy.
No archaisms (*verily*, *hath*, *unto*), no capitalised mythic
nouns (*the Body*, *the Bread*, *the Pending*), no by-deity-
name oath, no inverted predicate.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"Verily, the Walker who hath
tarried long at the House of the Saccharine and is given of
the Clerks' Bread shall know himself raised, by the Body's
reckoning, from Wayfarer unto Petitioner: for the Body
witholdeth meat from the casual, but suffers no pending soul
to hunger."* Tells: *Verily*; capitalised *Walker*, *House*,
*Saccharine*, *Clerks*, *Bread*, *Body*, *Wayfarer*,
*Petitioner*; *hath*, *tarried*, *witholdeth*, *suffers* (in
the KJV sense); *unto*; the inverted-predicate *witholdeth meat
from the casual*. The actual sentence: a contemporary
sociological description, no liturgical overlay.

**Pass.**
