## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord

Rotation: Concord last self-contained at 2201 (May 4), fourteen hours
back. Longest gap among the four underdeveloped bodies. The Concord's
working position at the catechism's reading (Dimension 7) makes the

**A working ritual.** *The shelf-of-the-not-yet-priced.*

The third Hall of the Concord — the Hall whose Open Clerk reads the
bible at quarter-bell afternoons — keeps a single shelf in its small
working library that is not in the Hall's holdings register. The shelf
holds documents the Concord has not yet priced. By doctrinal Concord, a
document not yet priced is not yet a holding; by working Concord, a
document on the shelf-of-the-not-yet-priced is *under preliminary
reading.* The shelf is the third shelf of the third Hall's reading-
room, third from the floor, and the Hall has kept this shelf for as
long as it has had a reading-room.

A Hall has on average eleven documents on the shelf at any given time.
Documents move to the holdings register by being priced (the Closing of
Accounts opens, a clause is read, the document is filed) or out of the
library by being structurally unfilable (the Open Clerk raises the
document at the morning's open and the Hall agrees the working register
admits no priced clause). The bible has been on the shelf-of-the-not-
yet-priced of the third Hall for nine years and has been raised twice
at morning opens; both times the Hall agreed by silent witness that the
document is held in a register the Hall's working register does not
hold, and the document was returned to the shelf without filing.

By Concord working register, the bible is not a Hall holding and is
not structurally unfilable. The bible is **in preliminary reading,
indefinitely.** The Hall's record of the bible is *the bible's place
on the third shelf of the third Hall, raised twice, returned twice,
under continuing preliminary reading.* The shelf is the corpus's
seventeenth record-form: a holding-by-shelf-position, in a body that
admits no holding outside the priced-clause register. The Concord
holds the catechism in the only working position its grammar permits:
*on the shelf, between morning opens, in the time before any clause.*
