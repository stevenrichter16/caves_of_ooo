## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (1801 §3 *the seventeen breaks*):** *"A
working-week in which a depot's recital was missed
is filed by working-tradition as a* break*, named to
a sister depot by courier-letter. … The seventeen
are remembered by working-tradition, not by ink."*

**Strong.** Break as cadence-failure form, named,
oral-tradition-bound, count-honoured.

**Thin.** *Named by courier-letter* and *remembered
not by ink* are working-forms that cannot both be
true under standing discipline — a courier-letter
is ink. The contradiction was named at 1801 and not
resolved. The resolution is the **second-light
burning**: the courier-letter, on arriving at the
sister depot, is read once by the receiving first-
bench at the depot's second-light hour, before the
recital, then burned in the depot's first-stone
brazier in the same hour. The break's record passes
from one body's ink to another body's oral roster
across a working-form whose final act is the
destruction of the only ink-bearing artifact.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Pale Curation,
append after the seventeen-breaks paragraph):**

> *A break-letter is composed by the originating
> depot's first-bench in the break working-week,
> sealed in unstamped linen, and conveyed by a
> courier of bench-rank to a sister depot in shelf-
> position order. At the receiving depot the letter
> is opened by the receiving first-bench at second-
> light on the courier's arrival working-day, read
> once aloud to the first-bench's own ear, and
> burned on the depot's first-stone brazier before
> the working-day's recital. The first-bench enters
> the break into the depot's oral roster; the linen
> is filed at the courier's home depot as
> **conveyance-only** with no break-content. The
> break is conveyed across two bodies in a working-
> form whose last act erases the medium it
> travelled in.*

The break-letter is the body's only ink-bearing
artifact whose working-form mandates its
destruction. Every other Curation document mandates
preservation. The asymmetry is canon under the
two-axis canonisation-vector: medium-axis preserved
(rule), cadence-axis conducted (working-form); the
break-letter is the one artifact in which the
cadence-axis demands the medium's destruction in
order to propagate.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (1801 §3 *the cataloguer's rotation*):**
*"What cannot be canonised cannot be taught by
instruction; only the cadence teaches the cadence."*

**Strong.** Cadence-pedagogy canonised against
medium-pedagogy.

**Thin.** The break-letter's authorship-status was
unwritten. The originating first-bench writes the
letter; the receiving first-bench reads it once and
places the linen on the first-stone. Whose
authorship does the break carry once it has entered
the receiving depot's oral roster? Not the
originating first-bench (the ink is destroyed); not
the receiving first-bench (she did not author the
break). The oral roster's seventeen items have **no
author** in either depot's working-form.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Pale Curation,
new sidebar "The break's authorless transit"):**

> *Once the break has been read and burned, the
> receiving first-bench holds it in oral roster as
> part of the seventeen — but neither first-bench
> is its author. The originating wrote a letter
> that no longer exists; the receiving read a
> letter she did not write. A retired first-bench
> asked who authored the break in her depot's
> roster answers:* "the break authored itself. we
> held it on either side of a fire." *The form is
> the body's only authorless inter-depot artifact,
> sustained by mutual non-authorship at the
> second-light hour.*

Authorship-doctrine refines: 1801 canonised intra-
depot bequest with single-author-then-single-reader;
0401 names inter-depot transit with no-author-on-
either-side, sustained by burning. Two custodial
forms; one preserves the medium, one destroys it;
both canonised; both cadence-axis-bound.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Pale Curation (the break-letter; the courier; the second-light burning; first-stone-form; stratified on extensibility)

> ### The break-letter
>
> The originating first-bench composes the letter in
> the break working-week. The page is unstamped
> linen from the depot's working-stock; the ink is
> iron-gall from the recital-stock by working-
> tradition. The letter names the working-day of
> the break and the absent first-bench (if known)
> or *first-stone unread* (if the cadence broke for
> other reasons). The letter does not analyse the
> break; the body has no working-form for break-
> analysis at the originating side.
>
> ### The courier's working-form
>
> A bench-rank courier — junior, second-bench, or
> first-bench, by the working-week roster — carries
> the linen in unstamped wrap to the sister depot
> in shelf-position order. The courier does not
> open the wrap. The home depot's working-shelf
> files a *break-conveyance* entry naming the
> courier's bench-rank, the receiving depot, and
> the arrival working-day; the linen's content is
> not recoverable from any working-shelf.
>
> ### The second-light burning
>
> At the receiving depot's second-light hour the
> first-bench takes the linen, opens it at the
> brazier-stand, reads the break aloud once to her
> own ear, and places the linen on the first-stone.
> The linen burns. The first-bench says, in first-
> stone-form: *"the working-day held this. the
> working-day is also the holding."* The break
> enters the receiving depot's oral roster; she
> recites the seventeen-plus-one count at her own
> retirement morning.
>
> ### What the body does not do
>
> The body does not file the break-letter at either
> depot. The originating files only the conveyance;
> the receiving files nothing. The first-stone
> holds the heat-time of every break ever burned at
> that depot, unfileable in the same way the
> recital's standing on the page is unfileable. The
> cadence-axis is the body's only inter-depot
> working-form, and its inter-depot working-form is
> to *not survive its conveyance*.
