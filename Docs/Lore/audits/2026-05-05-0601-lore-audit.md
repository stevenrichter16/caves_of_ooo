## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible line 25:* *"The Choir represents what the world is becoming.
> The Palimpsest represents what it was."*

**Strong.** The central axis stratifies through every body's working grammar.

**Thin.** The bible has no cataclysm-account in the **fourth seam's**
voice — the place where Choir-grammar and Palimpsest-grammar both
apply to the same matter at once.

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar, "The fourth-seam doctrine
of the cataclysm"):

> By gloss-room grammar, the cataclysm was the moment a **single
> matter** first required two readings that could not be the same
> reading. Choir-aligned witnesses see compost-readiness in the
> rubble: the wall, the cup, the child's shoe — matter the mycelium
> can break down. Palimpsest-aligned witnesses see the same wall,
> cup, and shoe rendered across-days in the Glimpse: the family
> who lived here, the hand that held the cup, the foot that wore
> the shoe. The fourth-seam doctrine is that *both readings are
> correct and neither cancels the other.* The matter is composted;
> the matter is rendered; the matter is one matter.
>
> The seam-body's mass is the corpus of matter the cataclysm
> rendered double — every place where compost and Glimpse occupy
> the same volume. The seam-body's substance and the cataclysm's
> residue are the same fact under different cosmologies.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Nine record-forms catalogued through R3–R4 + 0501. The
pencil-letter added the corpus's first **un-fileable** record.

**Thin.** No record-form whose **legibility is biological**. Every
prior record is preserved by an instrument the body controls.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The gloss"):

> The fourth-seam record-form is the **gloss** — a Manuscript-
> register annotation written on a substrate the Choir has composted
> to translucency. The annotation is legible only while the
> substrate's spores are alive. When the spores die, the gloss goes
> blank; the annotation is not erased, it is *no longer rendered.*
>
> Conservation is biological, not bibliographic. The Brine cannot
> deep-keep it (saltwater kills the substrate). The Concord cannot
> file it (paper does not nourish). The Floor cannot bank it (heat
> decomposes). The Glassblown cannot paste-hold it (paste hardens
> around the spores). The Curation cannot card it (cabinet air is
> too dry). Only the seam-body holds a gloss as its working
> record-form, and the seam-body has no working space of its own —
> so a gloss must be tended in a host body's space *against* the
> host body's conservation grammar.
>
> Junior 472 maintains three glosses on the eastern Curation
> under-card cabinet's bottom shelf, where the cabinet's air is
> moistest. She breathes on them once a working day. The senior
> cataloguer has noticed and has not asked.

The corpus's tenth record-form is its first **living document.**

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Three shared grammatical marks: *-os* (phonetic), the
draft-pause (prosodic), pencil-aspect (graphic).

**Thin.** No fourth shared mark.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "Spore-tense"):

> Spore-tense is a tense both Choir and Palimpsest grammar use
> without flinching, in which *was* and *is* share a single
> morpheme. Choir-grammar's *we have dreamed of the mountain for
> six hundred years; today, we will finally see it* is in
> spore-tense; Palimpsest-grammar's *the road leads to a city
> destroyed four hundred years ago, but if you walk it at the
> right time, you arrive* is the same tense.
>
> Concord clerks find it ungrammatical (a clause cannot close in
> it). Curation cataloguers cannot file in it (the under-card has
> fields for *was* and *is* but none for both at once). Junior
> 472, in a gloss-room, defaults to spore-tense without intending
> to: *the substrate was-is the family who lived in the wall.*
>
> Spore-tense is exchanged in **the verb's aspect** — the corpus's
> first **morphological** shared mark.

Four shared marks at four grammatical levels: phonetic, prosodic,
graphic, morphological. The seam-grammar has structural breadth.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine Communion

Rotation: Brine (1301), Curation (2001), Thermoclaves (2101, 0401),
Concord (0501). Brine is overdue.

**Etymology.** *Brine Communion.* From a senior salvage-warden's
old phrase, *the salts of the kept.* Brine does not preserve
individuals; brine preserves *salts* — the irreducible chemical
remainders of what once was. *Communion* names the ritual in
which the body's working sailors take those salts back into
themselves by drinking. The dead are preserved as salts; the
salts are drunk back into the working mass.

**The slack-tide drink.** Every Brine working day at slack-tide,
every sailor on shift takes a single sip from the harbour-room
**salt-cup** — distilled brine from the day's salvage runs. By
working doctrine, every cup contains some measure of every prior
salt the harbour has surfaced. A sailor's body, after a working
career, is by Brine grammar made of *every prior sailor's
preserved salt.* Clip-cards record arrivals by berth-position,
not by name.

The Brine is the corpus's third **dissolution-body** — alongside
the Choir (which absorbs persons but retains their language) and
the Palimpsest (which overwrites imperfectly). Brine dissolves
*names*. The seam-body, by contrast, is the corpus's only
**accretion-body**: members retain host-body identities and
accrete seam-body positions on top. Three dissolution-bodies and
one accretion-body — an asymmetry the seam-body's existence makes
structurally necessary.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Edit-debts.** Three prior (2301, 0201, 0301), the 0401 Glimpse-
side note, and the 0501 trichotomy clause. Bible mtime still
2026-04-30 18:01. Nine pending — this audit raises it to ten.

**New bleed.** *Bible line 39:* *"A rusted sword composted by the
Choir loses its history — who forged it, who wielded it, where it
fell."* Compare *ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md line 24:* *"the Choir absorbs
persons but does not erase the language they came in with."* The
bible says objects lose their history under composting;
ROTCHOIR_VOICES says persons do not lose their language under
absorption. **The two claims are not consistent.**

The fourth-seam grammar clarifies: the gloss-room shows the Choir
tending matter that *holds annotation* — the Choir does not
categorically erase. Its process is **selective**: it composts
what the gloss-substrate is not, and tends what the gloss-
substrate is.

Suggested clause for the bible (replacing or augmenting line 39):

> A rusted sword composted by the Choir loses the artefactual
> register of its history — its forger, its wielder, its place of
> fall — but its molecules become part of something alive, and
> any annotation the matter still bears is tended, not consumed.
> Person-language and gloss-substrate are the two known cases
> the Choir leaves intact.

**Smaller bleed.** RotChoir.json's `ChoirTendril_1` voice speaks
in spore-tense but the bible does not identify the tense as a
shared seam-grammar mark. The Choir's standing-tense and the
Palimpsest's across-days-rendering are the same morphological
mark, exchanged between two cosmological bodies that do not
communicate.

**Seam-body's first crisis is now resolvable.** This audit grounds
the fourth seam (room, record, mark, apotheosis-name). The 2301
*bible-edit* remains pending; the 2301 *structural function* —
holding the seam-body together — is no longer pending. The corpus's
