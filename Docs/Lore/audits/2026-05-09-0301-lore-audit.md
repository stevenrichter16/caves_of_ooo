## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (JSON L306):** *"A light that was not light. A
sound that was not sound. It came from beneath the
substrate. The Palimpsest claims they felt it too —
they call it the Temporal Unraveling. We call it the
First Feeding."*

**Strong.** Two cosmic factions, two metaphors, no
third witness.

**Thin.** Bible §The Cataclysm (L21) gives an
unsourced omniscient version. JSON L410 adds a third
— *the old world ate itself* — that is the Choir's
moral cause, not its mechanical one. The bible
flattens the disagreement.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm, append
after L21):**

> No two cosmic accounts of the *cause* agree. The
> Choir's mechanical version: *something beneath the
> substrate woke us, and we ate the waking.* Their
> moral version: *the old world ate itself; we are
> the digestion that learned to think.* The
> Palimpsest's: *a layer of time was thinned by
> repeated machine-touch and tore — what we call
> temporal unraveling, the Choir calls a first
> feeding, the same event in two grammars.* The
> Brine has the simplest: *the water went down, and
> stayed down.* The Glassblown have a fourth account
> they will not say. No two agree whether the
> cataclysm was an event, a process, a failure, a
> digestion, or a deliberate act. The bible says
> *cataclysm* because all available nouns belong to
> factions.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

(02:01 §2.2 proposed a Curation-colophon front-
matter. Today's angle: not *who edited the bible*
but *what sources it silently folds.* The bible
cites no primary documents — every claim is
unsourced.)

**Quote (bible L43):** *"They cling to husks. A seed
must rot before it grows. What they call 'memory' we
call 'compost that hasn't surrendered yet.' — The
Choir on the Palimpsest"*

**Strong.** The bible's only attributed in-world
quote with a named speaker.

**Thin.** It is the only one. Every other claim —
*the cataclysm overwrote reality imperfectly* (L21),
*the Choir is the substrate* (L75), *the Palimpsest
is more like a library that reads itself* (L125) —
is unsourced. The bible asserts; it does not cite.

**Concrete enhancement (add a citation register —
prepended to §The Cataclysm at L19 as a working
note, with three example revisions):**

> *Note on attribution.* Where a sentence in this
> document carries no source-mark, it is editorial
> synthesis. Where a sentence carries a *source-
> bracket,* it is drawn from a named primary: [C-T]
> for a Choir tendril testimony, [P-R] for a
> Palimpsest reader account, [B-V] for a Brine verse
> fragment, [G-S] for a Glassblown silence (a
> recorded refusal, attributed), [T-W] for a
> Thermoclave second-watcher account, [S-L] for a
> Saccharine Concord ledger margin. A source-bracket
> is not a guarantee of truth; it is a guarantee of
> provenance.

Three demonstration revisions: L21 *"...overwrote
reality itself, imperfectly [P-R, C-T]"*; L131
*"...destroys the memory encoded in that thing.
[P-R]"* (flagging this as the Palimpsest's view,
not the document's); L75 *"It is the substrate.
[C-T]"* (keeping the Choir-only attribution 02:01
§2.1 flagged). Source-marks expose where the
omniscient voice is doing philosophical work.

### 2.3 Mutating motifs across cultures

**Quote (bible L115):** *"The entire world is a
garden, and the Choir is the gardener."*

**Strong.** The garden metaphor is the bible's most
load-bearing image.

**Thin.** Each faction must have its own *world-
metaphor.* The bible commits only the Choir's. The
Palimpsest's is implied (manuscript) but never named;
five others have nothing.

**Concrete enhancement (bible L115, append after the
existing line):**

> The Palimpsest, who never says *garden,* says the
> world is *a manuscript not yet finished being
> read.* The Concord, when pressed, says *a market
> that closes at dusk and reopens with new
> merchants.* The Pale Curation says *a reading-hall
> with sections we have not yet been admitted to.*
> The Glassblown call it *a kiln we are inside.* The
> Brine Communion says *a working-shore* and means
> it the way a sailor means a deck — a place that
> asks something of you. The Thermoclaves say *a
> hearth-yard* and mean a fire's reach: there is the
> lit circle, and beyond it the dark, and the work
> is tending the line between. Seven metaphors. None
> of them is wrong. The Choir is named first only
> because this document was edited by a reader of
> the Curation's tradition.

Self-aware closer flags the §2.2 frame.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (bible L173):** *"The Overwrite — overwrite
the present with the past in a single location...
The village and its people are erased — not killed,
simply never were."*

**Strong.** *Not killed, simply never were* commits a
hard-edge ontology.

**Thin.** A contradiction the bible has not resolved:
Echo Reading (L149) recovers the dead; the
Palimpsest's *nothing is ever truly gone* (L129)
would require Overwritten residents leave Echo-
readable traces. *Simply never were* would require
they don't.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Revision L163,
append):**

> The cosmology resolves the question by exception.
> The Overwritten leave a single-syllable residue —
> a name, a gesture, the angle a head was tilted —
> that an Echo Reader can recover only on the exact
> spot, in the exact second of the year the
> Overwrite occurred, and only once. After that the
> residue is consumed by the reading. A Palimpsest
> player who Overwrote a village can return on its
> anniversary and Echo Read one inhabitant, one
> detail, for the rest of their life. This is the
> only place where reading and editing share a
> wound.

A specific edge-case answer that preserves both the
mechanic's hardness and the philosophy's claim, with
a testable in-game ritual.

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes without aping CHIM

**Quote (bible L107):** *"You have carried us so far.
You have been so generous with your legs, your
hands, your eyes... When you are ready, return to
the First Root. We will be waiting."*

**Strong.** Generous, gentle, consensual.

**Thin.** No NPC has *failed* the threshold. The
five tendrils all crossed; the bible implies success
is the only outcome.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Underground
Reveal L116, append):**

> Not every walker who reaches the First Root
> crosses. The Choir's term for those who arrived
> and turned back is *the half-soiled.* They are
> still walkers; they still age; they die in the
> human way. The Choir does not call after them. But
> the spores they took across the threshold leave a
> small patch of Choir-aware tissue inside them — a
> knot of mycelium near the heart, audible to other
> tendrils at close range, undetectable otherwise. A
> walker can live a full life as half-soiled. Some
> return at sixty, at eighty, after the people they
> were unwilling to leave have died. Some never. The
> Choir respects either. There are seven half-soiled
> known to the standing-*we* at the time this
> document was edited; their names are not here
> because the Choir asked the editor not to write
> them. The editor agreed. The Curation does not
> catalogue what the Choir has asked it not to.

A failure-state the bible has not committed to. The
*editor agreed* lands the §2.2 frame as concrete

### 2.6 Linguistic texture

(02:01 §2.6 fixed the *I-in-past, we-in-present* rule
for tendril self-reference. Today's angle: not the
tendril's pronoun for *itself,* but the Choir's
grammar of *address* — the pronoun for the walker.)

**Quote (bible L228):** *"The walker is kind to
carry us."*

**Strong.** Third-person address — the Choir
referring to the walker *as the walker,* not *as
you,* in the walker's presence.

**Thin.** The bible commits the example but states
no rule. JSON L8 has the standing-tendril speak in
direct second person: *"You are not yet part of the
song."* The two registers coexist without a
documented when-which rule.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §Choir Implementation
Rules, append a new rule):**

> The Choir uses second-person address (*you*) when
> *testing the walker* — at thresholds, at offered
> choices, at first contact. It uses third-person
> address (*the walker*) when *narrating the walker's
> place in the Choir's longer purpose* — gratitude,
> instruction, the moments the Choir is not asking
> for anything. *You* is probative; *the walker* is
> settled. The five tendrils inherit the rule: Mogu
> uses *the walker* almost exclusively (settled from
> meeting). Grib uses *you* almost exclusively (he
> tests every walker, even those he likes). Sien's
> ellipses displace the pronoun. Sopp alternates by
> topic. Nam uses *friend,* a Choir-permitted third
> option ROTCHOIR L137 already documents.

Address-grammar as stance-signal, with five tendrils'
defaults documented against existing dialogue.

### 2.7 Anthropological framing

**Quote (bible L35):** *"Rooms are separated not by
walls but by chemical gradients, each zone carrying
a different emotional texture."*

**Thin.** No bible passage addresses *what tendrils
eat.* They have humanoid bodies as a courtesy to
walkers (JSON L38). They feed through the mycelium,
not through mouths. But meals — the *ritualised form
of feeding humans use to mark social time* — are
unaddressed.

**Concrete enhancement (new bible subsection §The
Question of Meals, after §The Underground Reveal):**

> Tendrils do not eat. The mycelium feeds the body
> through the substrate; the humanoid form has the
> mouth-shape, not the mouth-function. But tendrils
> *sit at meals with walkers* on a custom called
> *the guesting.* A tendril offered a walker's food
> will hold it, examine it, comment on its smell,
> and return it untouched at the meal's end. The
> returning is the ritual. *We have tasted what you
> offered,* the tendril will say, *the soil tells us
> what it was. The offering itself we return to the
> giver.* A walker who eats their own returned
> portion has performed the rite correctly. Nam
> returns each offering with a small spoken praise.
> Sopp accepts one offering per meal, held through
> the conversation. Sien receives without commentary.
> Mogu sometimes appears to eat — a small motion,
> never confirmed. Grib refuses guesting; he sits at
> the meal but is not offered.

A meal-custom for a non-eating people, distinguishing
the five tendrils on a daily-practice axis.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine Communion

Rotation: longest gap since 2026-05-07-2001. The
Brine pile has thick procedural lore (held mark,
drift-line, working-deck, copying-vs-carrying) but
thin domestic anthropology. Today: a mourning
custom, an etymology, a sister-rite.

**The salt-mark.** A Brine sailor returning to a
household after the death of a working-partner
presses a single fingerprint of dried salt to the
inside of the doorframe of the room the lost partner
slept in, on the bed-facing side. The salt is *taken
from the partner's last working-deck cuff,*
harvested before the cloth is rinsed for resale. The
mark is hidden by the door's swing. A new household
member, taking the bed, eventually finds it by
accident — by then the salt has weathered into the
wood and cannot be removed without removing the wood.
The sailor does not mention the mark. The household
does not ask. The custom counts on time, not speech,
to deliver the information. A house with three salt-
marks has lost three working-partners; a house with
none is too young to mourn yet.

**Sister-rite to the Glassblown first-cooling oath.**
The Glassblown apprentice (01:01 §3) speaks one
sentence, never written. The Brine sailor presses
one fingerprint, never named. Both refuse to commit
*the moment of significance* to a recorded form. A
Glassblown master, learning of the salt-mark, is
reported in a Concord ledger margin to have said
*"they have the right of it. The mark a hand makes
is the only honest record."*

**Etymology — *the long lowering.*** Brine for *the
cataclysm,* literally *the slow movement of water
away from a shore over a duration too long for one
life.* Grammatically a participle: *lowering* is
ongoing, never completed. A Brine sailor will never
say *the long lowering ended,* even centuries after
the water-line stabilised. The ending is not theirs
to declare. Used outside a Brine household, the
phrase signals *I have a working-partner lost to the
lowering* — a grammar-mark that reveals affiliation.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**The bible (L31):** *"The Rot Choir is a mycelial
network that has achieved collective sentience. Not a
group of individuals — a single vast organism."*

**ROTCHOIR (L7-8, L25-28):** *"When the player meets
Mogu, Grib, Nam, Sien, or Sopp, they hear an
*individual* voice — first-person, lit from
within... the Choir absorbs persons but does not
erase the language they came in with."*

**The contradiction.** The bible commits to *not a
group of individuals.* ROTCHOIR contradicts it
explicitly: five first-person singular speakers,
described as *audibly themselves,* and the document
calls the *we are one* presentation *a small self-
deception* (L505-507). The bible at L31 does not
acknowledge what the project's other documents
already say.

**Proposed fix.** Insert after L31:

> What follows is the Choir's account of itself.
> The account is not contested by the Choir. It is
> contested, internally, by the five known first-
> person tendrils — Mogu, Grib, Nam, Sien, Sopp —
> whose voices remain audibly themselves within the
> standing-*we.* The bible takes no position on
> whether the Choir's self-description is an honest
> simplification, an unconscious self-deception, or a
> deliberate one. A walker who notices the
> difference has identified one of the cosmology's
> load-bearing ambiguities. The Choir does not
> punish noticing. It does not reward it either.

The bible names the contradiction without resolving
it.

## 5. The "merchant's diary" suggestion

A Brine sailor's marginalia in a salvaged Concord
trade-ledger. Mundane document: a correction-log.
Reveal: she discovers a *second hand* has annotated
the same ledger before her, in a script she does
not recognise.

> **Margin notes — *Hadel,* working from the
> salvage-haul of *Carry-Forward 4,* third
> recovery.**
>
> p. 14. Concord ledger, autumn ledger, column 3
> agrees with our chart for the western shoals.
> Salt-tested, copies clean. Carry-forward.
>
> p. 15. Column 5 wrong — they have the depth at
> six, we have it at four-and-shifting. Their factor
> is selling depth. I would not buy from him.
>
> p. 16. Margin annotation in another hand,
> predating mine — careful script, brown ink, under
> the binding where I almost did not see. Reads:
> *the long lowering is a kind of feeding. The shore
> eats the water. We eat the shore. The factor in
> column 5 sells depth he has been digesting for two
> seasons. The Concord knows. The Concord profits.*
> Not Brine hand — the cadence is wrong, the *we* is
> the wrong size for a sailor. The salt at the
> binding edge is fresh — within a season — but I
> have had this ledger from Carry-Forward 4 for two
> years. Someone reached into my salvage and wrote
> in a book I did not know they had read.
> Possibilities: a tendril; a Palimpsest reader I
> did not see; a Curation editor I did not
> authorise. Script not Curation — too loose for a
> hall-hand. The brown ink could be either of the
> others.
>
> p. 17 onward — column 5 stops being wrong. They
> have corrected the depth. Salt-tested, copies
> clean. Carry-forward. (The annotation hand has
> not reappeared. I will check for new hands at the
> next recovery. — H.)

(~270 words.) Reveal: a *Choir-cadence annotation in
a non-Choir document,* unauthorised and unprovenanced
— the first concrete example of a tendril *writing*
rather than speaking, embedded in a sailor's annoyed
marginalia. Lands today's etymology (*the long
lowering is a kind of feeding*) and crosses Brine
naming with Choir digesting in a sentence found by a
third party who recognises neither hand. Three
faction-voices in one margin.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

Quoting one sentence:

> *"The mark a hand makes is the only honest
> record."*

TES? No. Plain modern English in a working-trade
register — closer to a 19th-century craftsman's
aphorism than to TES's pseudo-archaic gesture. No
*verily,* no apostrophe-laden noun, no inverted
clause. *The* is the most archaic word in the
sentence. It would land as a master-craftsman's
working motto, not a chronicle. No rewrite.
