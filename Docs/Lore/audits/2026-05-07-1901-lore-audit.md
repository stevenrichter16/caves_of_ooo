## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (1401 §2.1):** *"you are not lighting a new
fire. you are tending the same fire in a longer
step."*

**Strong.** The carry-sentence canonises the
metaphysical claim that the long step is not
representation but continuation.

**Thin.** The sentence does not name **whose voice
it is**. The third-shift witness speaks it in the
public hall on the morning the carry departs — but
the body's working-form holds that the sentence is
not the witness's. The witness's mouth is the
parent fire's, for the duration of the carry. The
1801 *standing-on* form for the Curation has its
counterpart here: the Thermoclave equivalent of
*standing on the page* is **speaking with the
fire's voice for the morning the carry walks**. The
canonisation-authority is fire-internal.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Thermoclaves,
append after 1401 §2.1):**

> *The carry-sentence is not, in working reading,
> the witness's claim. The body's working-form holds
> that the third-shift witness, on the carry-
> morning, speaks the parent fire's voice for the
> sentence's duration. A witness asked, weeks later,
> what they meant by the sentence answers:* "i did
> not mean it. the fire said it. the working-morning
> said it through me." *Only the witness may deliver
> the sentence; no daughter-keeper, no second-shift
> watcher, no Curator. The discipline cannot
> canonise the fire as a speaker; the body's
> working-form is that the fire speaks once a
> generation and the witness's mouth is the fire's
> working-tool. The long step's continuity-claim is
> sustained by a ventriloquy-form the body holds
> invisible.*

The carry-rite's canonisation-authority is **fire-
internal, witness-mouth-routed**. Not the body, not
the discipline, not the keeper.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (1401 §2.2):** *"The rubbing represents the
Curator, not the body."*

**Strong.** Two-position cross-body finding.

**Thin.** The rubbing's authorship runs **across the
hearth-line in two directions**, not one. The
Curator's hand makes the marks on the paper. The
keeper's hand holds the stoker on the paper from
the other side of the line. The rubbing is a four-
hand artifact: keeper's hand on stoker, stoker on
paper, paper on stone shelf, Curator's charcoal on
paper. The body's reading isolates one direction;
the artifact does not isolate.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Thermoclaves,
append after 1401 §2.2):**

> *A Curation cataloguer signing a rubbing-card on
> dawn-rotation signs only their own name and the
> Thermoclave region-name; the keeper's name does
> not appear. By Curation field training, the absent
> name is filed under* the name not given*: a
> category taught on intake-rotation by the
> cataloguer's silence in the recorder field, never
> on the standing shelves. The body's reading is
> that the keeper has not authored; the rubbing is
> the Curator's. Two bodies file the same artifact
> under two different categories of non-authorship,
> and neither files the agreement.*

The rubbing's canonisation-authority is **cross-
body, dual-non-authorship-keyed**. The artifact
exists by both bodies' working-form for *not having
made it*.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (1401 §2.4):** *"In the long step, the
keeper's hand is on the stoker's handle and the
stoker's tip is on the open air."*

**Strong.** The long step canonises tending-in-
motion as the body's named exception.

**Thin.** The long step has a **second walker** —
the second-shift watcher at half a working-mile.
1401 §3 names the form (silence, no approach, single
stone if the carry is broken). 1901's pressure: the
second-shift watcher's working-status during the
step is **not the body's, not the daughter's, not
the parent's**. The watcher walks alone, in a
silence the doctrine has not assigned to a hearth.
The watcher is, for the step's duration, **a
fourth cadence-authority** — the body's only
working-instance of a keeper who tends nothing and
witnesses nothing the body files.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Thermoclaves,
append after 1401 §2.4):**

> *The second-shift watcher walking a long step at
> half a working-mile is, by the body's working-
> form, neither tender nor witness. They tend no
> fire on the step; they witness nothing the body
> files. A watcher asked, on retirement, what they
> were during the step's working-day answers:* "the
> step's other end." *The watcher is the long step's
> only cadence-authority that is not a fire — the
> step's own cadence speaks through them. The
> doctrine cannot canonise the step-as-speaker; the
> body's working-form is that the step has a voice
> for the working-day it happens, and the watcher's
> silence is that voice. A watcher who breaks
> silence is not* punished*; the step is* untaken*,
> the daughter unfounded, the watcher returned to
> second-shift without comment.*

Falsifies a doctrinal seam. The cosmology now
contains **four cadence-authorities** the body
defers to without canonising any: parent fire,
second-shift keeper, Curation cataloguer, the long
step itself. The Refusal-hearth's discipline is the
discipline of a body that holds its own irregularities
by deferring to authorities it cannot file.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted (§2.4):** *"the step's other end."*

Working-modern register. Lower-case, plain
possessive, no archaism, no inversion. *Step* is a
contemporary plain noun used in compound. *Other
end* is contemporary spatial idiom. The phrase is
a Thermoclave keeper's working-form for *I am the
step's cadence-voice for the working-day of the
walk*.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"The Step's Far
Hearth, the Watcher Standeth at — wherefrom the
Fire Speaketh through the Carrier."* Tells:
capitalised *Step / Far Hearth / Watcher / Fire /
Carrier*; *standeth, wherefrom, speaketh*. Actual
phrase is none of these; it is three lower-case
plain words.

**Pass.**
