**structural rejection**. The medium-axis dissolves;
the carrier-edge's intake-spectrum canonises

hold** (Curation), **admit-and-hold-with-time-

**Medium-equal and medium-graded fall as productive
non-findings.** *Medium-equal* fails because iron
is admitted by the Curation (in patina-substrate
register) and rejected by the Thermoclaves —
different working-forms, same medium, opposite
dispositions. *Medium-graded* fails because no

read iron as both admitting and rejecting, which is
not a property of the medium but of the working-

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (1401 §2.2):** *"Three relation-forms,
three authorship-configurations, structurally final
at three."*

**Thin.** Authorship-positions canonise on *what is
read* into the working-form. They do not canonise
on *whether the working-form admits the reading*.
This sitting names the **intake-disposition** as a
parallel canonisation-feature: each working-form's
relationship to the carrier-edge sorts at three
positions — admit (the working-form has a hold-
without-filing clause), reject (only arbitration-
clauses, returns the piece), or untested. Authorship
is what is filed; intake-disposition is whether
anything can be filed.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §How They View Each
Other [L208 paragraph], append after the three-
authorship-positions supplement from 1401):**

> *(Authorship and intake are parallel canonisation-
> features. A working-form's authorship-position is
> what is read into the piece during filing; its
> intake-disposition is whether the piece is
> admitted at all. The Curation admits at zero-
> authorship; the Concord admits at zero-authorship;
> the Thermoclaves' working-form on iron rejects at
> the threshold and the question of authorship does
> not arise. Refusal is not a reading. It is the
> only working-state where the working-form does not
> file the piece.)*

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclaves (rotation)

The Thermoclaves are named in the bible (L208) and
in the audit-pile across many sittings (1301 worked
the iron-medium anti-edge with the Brine Communion).
This sitting lands the working-form for walker-
arrival iron pieces — the discipline's structural
rejection at the anneal-bench.

### Etymology — *no clause*

The Thermoclaves' bench-note vocabulary holds three
canon entries: **own-make** (iron worked at the
smith's own anneal-bench), **arbitration-intake**
(iron-piece arrived from the Brine Communion, to be
read against the Brine reading), and **return-to-
merchant-line** (iron-piece arrived from a named
merchant-route). A fourth bench-note register,
**no clause**, marks pieces arrived without any of
the three. *No* is the working-vocabulary's only
negation-particle; *clause* is the bench-note's
term for the working-form's canon intake-categories.
A smith who bench-notes *no clause* has examined
the piece, found no matching intake-category, and
returned the piece to the walker.

The token is the slate's first **negation-register**
— a lexical-substrate-shift family extension to a
**fifth** token (after *bee*, *sea-wall*, *prior-
glass*, *prov.*; cf. 1101, 1201, 1301). *No clause*
is structurally distinct from the prior four: they
name what the working-form *does* with pieces it
cannot file canon-primary; *no clause* names what
the working-form *does not do*. The family is
structurally closing at five tokens (one per body's
primary medium); the negation-form is the family's
structural exterior, marking the working-form's own
incompleteness rather than the medium's.

### The no-clause-return working-form

When a walker arrives at a Thermoclave anneal-bench
with an iron piece, the smith's working-form is
**walker-arrival examination**: the piece is read
for own-make markings (smith's stamp on tang,
anneal-bench stamp on working-edge), Brine
arbitration markings (salt-line tag at haft), and
merchant-line tag (route-name burned at grip). If
none are present, the smith bench-notes the
working-week, bench-day, working-weight, the
walker's address (always *walker*; the Thermoclaves
do not file walkers by name), and the canon phrase
*no clause; returned by smith.* The piece is
returned on the bench-note slate's even days only —
the working-week's discipline of pacing rejection-
rituals so that no working-day holds more than
three rejections at any anneal-bench.

### The bench-note-only register

A walker-handed piece bench-noted *no clause* by a
Thermoclave smith generates **only** a bench-note
entry; the piece is never held at the anneal-bench,
never shelved, never filed under any standing
register. The bench-note is the working-form's
*only* canon record of the rejection. The verb-form
is *to bench-note*. The slate's three **hold-or-
record-without-filing** working-forms canonise at
three structurally distinct positions on a holding-
spectrum: held-as-piece (Curation's standing-shelf,
anti-edges), held-as-piece-with-time-limit
(Concord's stand-aside shelf, carrier-edge cane-
syrup), recorded-without-holding (Thermoclaves'
bench-note slate, carrier-edge iron).
