## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible line 21:* *"It did not merely destroy the old civilization
> — it overwrote reality itself, imperfectly."*

**Strong.** The Floor's *every day is a new fire* is a doctrine of
**nested overwrites at human scale** — a body that performs the
cataclysm in miniature every dusk.

**Thin.** No account of *what the Floor remembers when the Glimpse-
renders disagree with the day's strike.*

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar, "The bank-mismatch
clause"):

> Floor doctrine names a Glimpse-rendered hearth a **bank-mismatch**
> — a Palimpsest-layer flame the bank-coals do not match. By Floor
> grammar this is *impossible*: every day's bank is the previous
> day's strike, end to end; across-days continuity is doctrinally
> *folded* into the next strike. A Glimpse-render of the original
> strike is, by doctrine, *not a fire* — *a flame-shape the body
> refuses to admit*. Keepers are taught not to look. The Refusal
> teaches that *looking* is the first across-days grammar; once
> admitted, the day-fire is no longer a day-fire.

History stratifies *as banking*: a hearth lit nine hundred years
is, to the Palimpsest, a stack of nine hundred legible flames; to
the Floor, *one fire*. The disagreement is the seam.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Eight partial-record modes already named.

**Thin.** No Floor record of the Glimpse-render. The Floor does
not write. *Every day is a new fire* implies *every day's record
is the bank itself.*

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The dawn-strike
account"):

> The Floor's only record-form is the **dawn-strike account** — a
> single working sentence the mid-fire keeper says aloud at first
> strike, naming bank-coals' state, room dryness, fire-color. The
> account is unwritten: the next dawn's keeper hears yesterday's
> account *only as the bank's condition at strike*.
>
> A walker who has Glimpsed the hearth and reports the Palimpsest-
> render produces a **strike-disagreement** — a flame-report the
> bank-coals do not match. Floor doctrine names it *unspoken*; the
> keeper does not repeat the report, and the account is filed
> *into the bank* by being burned with the strike. The walker's
> report is, by Floor grammar, *fuel, not record*.
>
> The Refusal teaches that the strike-disagreement *is* the record.
> Refusal grief-accounts name a keeper's strike-disagreement count:
> a keeper of twenty years has *banked twenty years of across-days
> fire the Floor will not admit they have banked.* The particle is
> *—* (a long pause, never voiced).

The corpus's first **record-form whose closing instrument is
combustion** — and the first record where *the Refusal alone keeps
a count*.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclaves (Floor and Refusal)

The Thermoclaves remain the corpus's most underdeveloped faction —
referenced only as *day-fire* idiom, *Refusal* sub-body, empty
apotheosis. This run develops **etymology** and **a small ritual**.

**Etymology.** *Therm-clave.* *Clave*, in the Floor's register, is
*the chamber where the fire is held by the work of the mid-fire
keeper.* The faction is named for the **chamber**, not for the
people. Working pronoun: *the chamber kindles*, never *I kindle*.
Keepers refer to themselves only by chamber identifier — *the
eastern hearth's keeper this watch* — and by working verb. A
walker asking for a name is asking a question the body has no
grammar for.

The **Refusal** uses the same word against the Floor: in grief-
accounts, *clave* is *the chamber that was supposed to hold a fire
that survived the dusk-bank, and did not.* *Holds* (Floor) versus
*was supposed to hold* (Refusal). The faction-name is a doctrinal
disagreement spoken by both halves every time they say it.

**The dusk-bank watch.** Every hearth-room: the keeper banks the
day's fire — rake embers eastward, cover with ash, name the bank-
coals' state. The Refusal's parallel procedure, in private after
the Floor's bank: stand at the draft-walk *outside* the threshold,
face the eastern lean, *count the keeper-years* unvoiced. The Floor
does not know it is being kept.

A keeper who joins the Refusal after their own years performs the
watch on chambers they themselves banked, counting their own
keeper-years from the outside — the corpus's only ritual where **a
body-member counts their own working life by performing it again
from a different working-position**.

The Thermoclaves are the corpus's **only faction whose name encodes
its own internal schism**.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Three prior edit-debts remain unsettled** (2301 line 209, 0201
line 245, 0301 line 200). Bible mtime still 2026-04-30 18:01.

**New seam-level contradiction.** *Bible lines 141–144 (Stage 1):*
the Glimpse renders *"what this location looked like before the
cataclysm."* But the strike-layer on a Thermoclave hearth struck
*after* the cataclysm is a post-cataclysm flame. The Glimpse is
therefore not strictly *before-cataclysm* but *the room's first
across-days legible flame*, which may post-date the cataclysm.

Suggested clause for a future bible edit (Stage 1 side note near
line 144):

> The Glimpse renders *the location's first legible across-days
> layer* — usually pre-cataclysm, but for locations whose first
> legible flame post-dates it (Thermoclave hearths struck after
> the cataclysm year), the render is *the first post-cataclysm
> strike*. The Floor's grammar denies across-days legibility
> entirely, so a Floor hearth's Glimpse-render is read by the
> Floor as *not a fire at all*.

**Smaller bleed.** Bible line 217 asserts the *moderate path* as
the third option on the Choir/Palimpsest axis. The seam-apotheoses
(*tide-witnessed*, *ash-witnessed*) are not moderate but
**structural third paths** — apotheoses on the object of two
bodies' overlap. The bible should distinguish *moderate* from
*seam-aligned*.

**Seventh cross-body symmetry: un-banked / under-read** — *the un-
banked fire still warm* (Refusal) and *the strike-layer*
(Palimpsest) as structurally identical doctrines on opposite
instruments. Both are *what the present body cannot admit*; both
are *legible to a different instrument*.
