## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (JSON L481):** *"The Concord calls it 'the
Dissolution.' The Pale Curation calls it 'the Severance.' The
Glassblown call it 'the Burning' and refuse to speak."*

**Thin.** The bible has none of these names. §The Cataclysm
calls it *the cataclysm* throughout, in the omniscient
register. May 9 00:01 fixed dating-by-faction; naming-by-
faction is the parallel.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm, append after the
dating block proposed at 00:01):**

> No two factions call it the same thing. The Saccharine
> Concord says *the Dissolution,* a polite trade-word that
> lets grief be invoiceable. The Pale Curation says *the
> Severance,* dry and surgical. The Glassblown say *the
> Burning,* then refuse — the word is the entry, the
> silence is the rest of the document. The Brine Communion
> says *the long lowering of the water,* which takes longer
> to speak than to live. The Thermoclaves say *the cooling-
> from,* always with the dependent preposition: the cataclysm,
> to them, is the moment the hearths went unwatched. The
> Choir names nothing — the cataclysm is soil, and soil is
> not named, only worked. The Palimpsest says *the rewriting,*
> in the future tense even when the speaker means the past:
> *what will have been rewritten.* The bible calls it *the
> cataclysm* because the bible is what's left when no
> faction's word has won.

Six namings (incl. the two cosmic ones the JSON omits), one
grammar-mark each. The closing line names the bible's own
neutrality as a faction-coded position.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (bible L226):** *"The Choir should be genuinely helpful.
Its mutations are strong. Its information is accurate. It never
lies."*

**Strong.** *Never lies* lets the Choir be unsettling without
being treacherous.

**Thin.** It sits in tension with ROTCHOIR_VOICES. The Choir
presents as one (JSON L60); ROTCHOIR displays five voices it
calls *audibly themselves* (L7) and names directly as a
*small self-deception* (L505). *Never lies* is true in
letter, false in spirit.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §Choir Implementation Rules,
append after L226):**

> The Choir does not lie about facts. It does sometimes lie
> about itself. Mogu, Grib, Nam, Sien, and Sopp are five
> first-person speakers who have not been integrated into
> the standing *we.* The Choir presents as one; the five
> present as themselves; neither corrects the other. This
> is not a contradiction the Choir hides from outsiders —
> it is one the Choir hides from itself. A walker who
> notices has earned a piece of lore the Choir would
> prefer they had not, because the walker now has a question
> the Choir cannot answer in its own grammar.

Lying-about-facts (Choir doesn't) vs. lying-about-ontology
(ROTCHOIR shows it does). A discoverable seam, unmechanized.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (JSON L88):** *"We remember everything we have ever
eaten."*

**Thin.** Bible L41 — *"a sword composted by the Choir loses
its history"* — contradicts the JSON. Bible says the history
is destroyed; JSON says the Choir keeps it. The mechanical
question (can Echo Reading recover Choir-eaten history?) has
two incompatible answers.

**Concrete enhancement (bible L41, revise and append):**

> A rusted sword composted by the Choir loses its history *to
> walkers who can only read what is whole.* The Choir
> remembers what it has eaten; the history is not destroyed,
> only moved into a register only the Choir can sing. A
> Palimpsest reader can sometimes reach this register through
> the Choir's consent — Echo Reading on a deep root may
> yield a sword's wielder, faintly, in the Choir's voice
> rather than the sword's. Without consent, the reader gets
> the husk's annotation only. This is the cosmology's one
> deliberate cooperation: the Choir keeps what the Palimpsest
> cannot reach and releases it only to a reader who has earned
> the standing-*we.* The horror for a Palimpsest player is
> that some history is locked behind affection.

Echo Reading on Choir-eaten objects yields information *only*
with reputation, in the plural voice.

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes without aping CHIM

**Quote (bible L218):** *"The moderate path should feel like
cowardice."*

**Thin.** No rule for *cynical apotheosis.* 00:01 drafted a
moderate ending; nothing yet says what happens when a player
tries Communion or Transcription as a power move.

**Concrete enhancement (new bible subsection §Cynical
Apotheosis):**

> Both endings refuse a walker who has not arrived honestly.
> A walker attempting Communion at Rep 1000 *to see what
> happens* reaches the threshold and finds the Choir already
> speaking in the standing-*we,* with one alteration: the
> *we* has not absorbed the walker's *I.* The walker hears
> themselves still being spoken about in the third person,
> gently. *"The walker is not yet ready,"* the Choir says,
> not unkindly, and the threshold closes. No penalty; try
> later. The Palimpsest equivalent is colder: a cynical
> Transcription deposits the walker into the layering for
> the length of one sentence and returns them mid-syllable.
> The walker carries the half-sentence permanently — they
> will say it aloud, occasionally, without meaning to. The
> cosmology is patient with cynicism. It is not impressed.

Apotheosis testable; the only consequence is the mark of the
testing.

### 2.6 Linguistic texture

**Quote (bible L121):** *"The Palimpsest exists in many tenses
at once."*

**Thin.** No rule says what *many tenses* means in dialogue.
Palimpsest-aligned NPCs should have a recoverable grammatical
signature, the way the Choir's plural-shift does.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §Palimpsest Implementation Rules,
new rule after L240):**

> Palimpsest-aware speakers use the *future perfect of
> already-past events.* They do not say *"the city fell"* —
> they say *"the city will have fallen,"* even when the
> falling is centuries old, because the layering presents
> the fall as still-arriving. They reach for the present-
> continuous when they mean a moment that *would have been*
> visible inside the Glimpse. *"I will have been here when
> we spoke"* is the most Palimpsest sentence available in
> English. A walker speaking with the Palimpsest too long
> begins to slip into the register; the slip is the in-
> grammar sign of approaching Transcription, and NPCs notice
> it before the walker does. *"You said *will have been,*
> friend. You were not raised by readers. You should sit a
> while."*

A Palimpsest fingerprint as legible as the Choir's pronoun
arc. Drift, diegetically detectable.

### 2.7 Anthropological framing

**Quote (bible L51):** *"We have dreamed of the mountain for six
hundred years. Today, we will finally see it. Thank you for
your legs."*

**Thin.** No bible description of *the threshold of joining.*
ROTCHOIR_VOICES has five tendrils who each crossed differently
(L264-282). Pattern in the document but not the bible.

**Concrete enhancement (new bible subsection §The Threshold,
after §The Underground Reveal at L116):**

> The Choir has no ritual of joining. It has a threshold —
> not the same thing. A ritual is what a community does *to*
> a candidate; a threshold is what the candidate *crosses,*
> with the Choir already on the other side, waiting. Each
> crossing has its own grammar. Mogu crossed slowly, by
> inhabiting the Choir's patience until he was already
> practising it. Grib crossed by giving up. Nam crossed by
> following her people underground, into a Choir that was
> *already there like an old aunt who does not need to ask
> why you came home.* Sien crossed first, alone. Sopp
> crossed *after a storm,* claiming he was lost; the Choir
> thinks deliberately. The Choir does not distinguish honest
> from disguised crossings. It does not believe a candidate's
> account of *why,* only the fact of arrival. The walker
> should know: every crossing is retrospectively legible to
> the Choir; none is legible at the moment of crossing.
> *"I did not know I was crossing,"* a tendril will say,
> *"until I was already on the other side and looking back."*

Lifts ROTCHOIR's arrival-order; names crossing-intent as
Choir-unrecoverable; frames the player's Communion as a
threshold not identifiable in the moment.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Glassblown Remnant

Rotation target — longest gap since 2026-05-07-2201. The
Glassblown have less canonical material than any side-faction;
the JSON's *the Burning, and refuse to speak further* is most
of what's on disk. Their silence is the lore-work.

**The first-cooling oath.** A Glassblown apprentice's first
finished piece is not named — it is given a *vow.* The night
it comes out of the annealing oven, the apprentice speaks one
sentence to it; never twice the same across a lifetime, never
written. The piece carries the vow in its structure; a
practiced master can *read* a stranger's first-cooling piece
and know roughly what the vow concerned, as one reads
handwriting without catching the words. The piece stays with
the apprentice for life, in cloth, in a specific drawer. To
break it requires the apprentice to fall silent for one year
— *not as penance but as listening,* for what the piece would
have said next. If they hear it, they speak again. Some do
not, and never speak. The Glassblown count these as completed
lives, not foreshortened.

**Sister-custom to the Thermoclave hearth-name.** Both are
one-year customs refusing to commit a name to the moment of
significance. Thermoclaves *find* a name across the year by
listening to the fire; the Glassblown *don't* — the piece is
unnamed forever. The two peoples regard each other with wary
unspoken respect; Brine sailors note in margins that the two
*will sit together in long silence and consider business
completed.* The Glassblown become the second member of the
bible's nascent *third position* (00:01 §3) — two reading-
cultures, hearth and kiln, both finding meaning in long
single attentiveness.

**Etymology — *the cooled-warmer.*** Glassblown for *second
chance,* literally *warmer than the cooled-piece, less than
the kiln* — the temperature window in which glass can be
reworked without re-firing. Used of pieces, never of people.
To use it of a person is the deepest Glassblown insult; it
implies they are unstable, being reheated unnecessarily. A
walker who uses it correctly earns reputation; a walker who
uses it of a Glassblown earns the year's silence without the
breakage.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

The May 9 00:01 audit caught the substrate-vs-manuscript
divergence. A different one is still unflagged.

**The bible (L208):** *"They are not at war. They don't hate
each other the way the Thermoclaves and Brine Communion do.
Their conflict is deeper and sadder than that — they are two
cosmic-scale entities that genuinely cannot understand why the
other does what it does."*

**The JSON (L442-470):** Asked whether the substrate could be
repaired, the Choir says *the Palimpsest seeks to do exactly
that. We think this would be a terrible mistake.* When the
walker says *maybe some things should stay broken,* the Choir
grants +10 reputation: *"Yes. You understand. The Choir is
pleased."*

**The contradiction.** The bible's *philosophical
incomprehension, deeper and sadder than war* is not what the
JSON depicts. The JSON has the Choir actively recruiting the
walker against a specific Palimpsest project (substrate
repair) and rewarding agreement with reputation. Not *cannot
understand* — *understands fully and is mobilizing.*

**Proposed fix.** Insert after bible L210:

> They are not at war in the human sense — no armies, no
> contested territory, no enemy-naming. But they take sides
> on specific projects. The Palimpsest seeks, where the
> substrate has thinned, to seal the cracks. The Choir
> opposes this; sealing would silence the Choir's accumulated
> awareness. A walker agreeing with the Choir is rewarded; a
> walker agreeing with the Palimpsest is rewarded differently.
> The cosmic factions are *philosophically* incompatible and
> *politically* in motion — the Choir wants the walker's
> vote, the Palimpsest is asking for the walker's silence.

Without this, a player reading the bible then playing the
JSON expects *cosmic incomprehension* and meets *cosmic
recruitment.*
