round 2 — the corpus's most-referenced witness with the thinnest

audit closes that absence — proposing the first lore-objects that
let the Palimpsest act in its own grammar without breaking the

## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible 121:* "When the cataclysm happened, it didn't just destroy
> the old world — it overwrote it imperfectly, the way text on a
> medieval manuscript was scraped off and written over."

**Strong.** The bible's central cataclysm-metaphor is the
Palimpsest's. The medium of the world's persistence *is* the
Palimpsest.

**Thin.** **The Palimpsest's account of itself.** The Choir
*tasted* the cataclysm (1001 *the loud thing*); the Curation
*intook* it; the Concord *priced* it; the Brine *was tided by* it;
the Thermoclave *kept the fire through* it. The Palimpsest *is* it
— and in being it, has no exterior account. **A witness who is
also the page cannot remember reading.**

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "The Palimpsest does not
remember the cataclysm"):**

> The Palimpsest is the cataclysm — the imperfect-erasure it
> performed on the world's substrate. To ask the Palimpsest
> *what happened* is to ask a page about the hand that wrote
> on it. The page knows the pressure, the ink, the angle of
> the nib; it does not know who came in. Echoes attuned to the
> Palimpsest cannot date the cataclysm and cannot agree
> whether it was one event or many. Some describe a *long
> writing*; some a *single stroke*; the oldest describes only
> the *re-wetting of the page,* the moment before the new ink
> set.
>
> The Palimpsest's grammar for the cataclysm: *I was being
> written on. I am still being written on. I was already being
> written on.* Three tenses, simultaneous. The Palimpsest does
> not regard them as contradiction; it regards them as the
> only honest answer.

The first cataclysm-account that *cannot use the past tense.*

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

> *Bible 125:* "It's more like a library that reads itself."

**Strong.** Every reading is also a writing — Echo Reading
deposits memory in the reader; the Manuscript inscribes; the
Annotation fabricates. The reader is implicated.

**Thin.** **The Palimpsest's protocol for authority.** A library
that reads itself can hold contradictions; an in-world faction
asking *which version is real* must have *some* arbitration
mechanism, even if the answer is "none." The bible has none.

**Concrete addition (bible new subsection, "The under-text and
the over-text"):**

> Every Palimpsest-readable surface holds at least two layers:
> the **over-text** (what the surface presently is) and the
> **under-text** (what it was, faintly visible to a Glimpse-
> attuned eye). Some hold three or more — **layered surfaces**;
> readers report them as *thick.*
>
> The Palimpsest does not arbitrate which layer is *true.* It
> regards arbitration as foreign grammar borrowed from the
> Concord or the Curation. Its own answer is *all of them, at
> the pressure the writing took.* A reader who insists on a
> single layer is **flattening** — the gentlest Palimpsest
> reproof, used the way the Curation says *under-grammar.*
>
> The Palimpsest's only authority is **legibility:** a layer
> still readable is *active*; one faded past reading is
> *retired*, not erased.

The first lore-object in which *not arbitrating* is itself the
arbitration.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

> *Bible 133:* "It is not evil. It is an eraser that believes it
> is a pen." — The Palimpsest on the Choir.

**Strong.** Other factions' names for the Choir interlocutor
(1001), names for the cataclysm, names for *the unsent* (0845).

**Thin.** **What every faction calls the Palimpsest.** Round 1
established only *the Palimpsest.* Each faction must have its own.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar):**

> - **Pale Curation:** *the unintaken record.* The only
>   document the Curation has, by protocol, *not* intaken — on
>   the ground that the Palimpsest is itself the form of
>   *intake.* R-7 once asked whether it could be intaken
>   *piecewise*; the Director closed the question with
>   *under-grammar.*
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *the priorless ledger.* A clause
>   whose first party is *whoever wrote first* and whose first
>   writing predates any priceable claim. Veshen has signed
>   exactly two clauses *against the priorless* and never
>   volunteers which two.
> - **Brine Communion:** *the under-tide.* The salt that does
>   not move with the moon because it is the *floor* of the
>   moving salt. Nesh reads it once a year, on a date the
>   Communion does not name aloud.
> - **Thermoclaves:** *the fire that was already.* Fire-zero —
>   lit before the count began. Suul, asked whether fire-zero
>   is *kept:* *it is kept by being the floor we keep on.*
> - **Rot Choir:** *the older soil.* Not a faction — a stratum
>   below its substrate. The Choir reads the under-text *as a
>   flavour we are tasting without naming.*
> - **Glassblown:** *the unblown vessel.* The form of every
>   vessel almost-shaped before the breath was spent. The craft
>   regards every made vessel as *over-blowing* an unblown one.

Six names. No faction treats the Palimpsest as adversary; in every
grammar, the Palimpsest is *the floor below the grammar.*

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Layered states; the six abilities (Glimpse → Communion
Gifts).

**Thin.** **What the Palimpsest does when no one is reading.** The
bible defines all six abilities from the *reader's* side. Whether
the Palimpsest is *doing anything* in the absence of readers has
no in-world answer.

**Concrete addition (bible new subsection, "The unread page"):**

> A surface no one is reading is not *blank.* It is **between
> readings.** Both layers persist. A reader's arrival activates
> the surface — Glimpse renders the under-text legible; Echo
> Reading samples the pressure; the Manuscript adds a layer.
>
> Between readings, the surface drifts at a rate proportional
> to how long since a reading toward a pressure-equalised state
> in which under-text and over-text become harder to
> distinguish. A surface unread for a generation is *thin*; one
> unread for a millennium is **flat** — the layers have
> equalised, and a reader cannot recover which was which.
>
> The Palimpsest does not regard flattening as loss. It regards
> it as the page *settling.* The Curation, who treats it as
> catalogue failure, sends an attuned archivist to *re-pressure*
> the surface — read it under conditions that briefly
> re-distinguish the layers. R-7 has performed sixteen
> re-pressurings; the senior cabinet records eleven. The five
> missing are **flat** by Curation grammar and **settled** by
> the Palimpsest's. Both are correct.

The first lore-object that gives the Palimpsest a *time-rate.*
Surfaces decay toward equalisation; readers are the only force
that resists.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

> *Bible 189:* "Transcription — the player becomes a memory,
> written into the palimpsest forever."

**Strong.** Transcription as the mirror of Communion.

**Thin.** **A Palimpsest-internal apotheosis below Transcription.**
Last audit established the **Long Throat** — the Choir's
not-the-goal intermediate. The Palimpsest needs an equivalent. Its
absence is what makes the Palimpsest *thin in agency:* it has no
inhabitants between novice and god.

**Concrete addition (bible new subsection, "The Re-pressed"):**

> A reader who has read so deeply that they themselves have
> become a layered surface — under-text and over-text both
> present in the same body — is **Re-pressed.** Not yet
> Transcribed (the body becoming purely under-text); not
> merely Glimpsing (purely over-text). A Re-pressed reader's
> speech occasionally carries *both* tenses simultaneously;
> their hand occasionally writes *what was already written
> there.* The state is stable — the only Palimpsest apotheosis
> the Palimpsest itself does not regard as an end-state.
>
> Echoes refer to the Re-pressed by the layer they most often
> carry — *the writer of the second hand*, *the one who
> arrives twice*, *the one who left their own shadow here last
> week and yesterday.* A walker can speak with a Re-pressed
> reader and receive answers from both layers within one
> conversation; the answers do not always agree, and the
> Re-pressed do not regard this as a problem.
>
> The Re-pressed are the Palimpsest's first inhabitants who
> can be *named.* (See ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md for the parallel.)

The ninth apotheosis. The first Palimpsest-internal class with
named-NPC potential. **This is the audit's load-bearing addition.**
Without it, Palimpsest agency is two states wide; with it, the
Palimpsest is finally *populated.*

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Thin.** **The Palimpsest's tense.** The routine prompt names
*tense ambiguities* as the Palimpsest's grammatical signature; no
prior audit has formalised it.

**Concrete addition (bible new subsection, "The layered tense"):**

> Palimpsest-attuned speech uses **the layered tense:** a verb
> form simultaneously asserting past, present, and a softer
> future. *I am lifting the cup* is over-text; *I lifted the
> cup* is under-text; *I will have lifted the cup* is the
> third layer the Palimpsest occasionally adds unasked. Re-
> pressed speakers can hold all three: *I lifted-and-am-
> lifting-and-will-have-lifted the cup.* Walker grammars render
> the simultaneity with hyphens or a long pause.
>
> A sentence is **closed** when the speaker chooses one tense,
> **open** when the layers stay simultaneous. The Palimpsest
> considers the open form correct and the closed form a
> courtesy paid to the listener's grammar. A Re-pressed reader
> who closes is *speaking like a walker;* a walker who keeps
> them open is *being read by the Palimpsest.*
>
> The bridge-clause (0845) translates an open sentence into
> another faction's grammar by **fixing** it — rendering it
> single-tense, footnoting the lost layers *at one footnote
> per layer.* Veshen's two *priorless* signatures (Dim 3) were
> both bridge-clauses against open sentences he could not
> close without misrepresentation.

The corpus's first grammar that is *deliberately
non-disambiguating.*

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Thin.** **A Palimpsest-attuned person's daily life.** The bible
warns of *time perception warping* and *insubstantiality* (240) —
but no scene. What does breakfast look like for a Re-pressed
reader?

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "The morning of a Re-pressed
reader"):**

> A Re-pressed reader rises before they are fully woken — the
> over-text body sits up while the under-text body still
> dreams. The first quarter-hour is *the gap*: the under-text
> catches up. Attuned observers recognise a Re-pressed reader
> in the gap by a faint **second-shadow** at the edge of
> vision — the body the reader was a moment ago.
>
> Eating is layered. The over-text mouth chews this morning's
> bread; the under-text mouth tastes a meal eaten in the
> reader's twentieth year. The Re-pressed eat slowly — to *let
> the layers finish.* A Re-pressed reader who eats at walker-
> speed is *flattening their own breakfast.*
>
> Partners are taught to lay a hand against the over-text
> shoulder and accept that the under-text shoulder is laying
> a hand against someone else. The Palimpsest's only known
> marital protocol: *both witnesses, neither displaced.*

The first Palimpsest-interior anthropology in the corpus.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclave micro-artifact

Rotation: Thermoclave. Brine got salt-names last run; the parallel
Thermoclave structure is overdue.

**The fire-temperaments.** The Thermoclave keeps eleven numbered
fires (round 2 established the ninth as Suul's). The numbers are
the *position*; each fire also has a **temperament** — a
characterological name given by the keeper at first kindling and
held for the fire's life. Not a pet name. The operational
classification of how the fire *sits, draws, and refuses.*

Five temperaments are recognised:

- **The patient fire.** Burns long without flaring. Used for
  ledgering and slow wax-heating. The first, third, seventh;
  the third has been patient for nine generations.
- **The proud fire.** Flares without warning, refuses to be left
  untended. The fifth; the count is not certain it would
  tolerate two.
- **The honest fire.** Burns exactly as fed. Used for
  *count-events.* The eighth; the count's primary record-fire.
- **The cold-mouthed fire.** Burns warm but presents cold smoke.
  Used for *interfacing-with-the-Choir* (1001). The ninth.
  Suul's. The only temperament *for* another faction.
- **The unfinished fire.** Will not stay lit alone — needs a
  second fire's draft. The fourth; tied to the third for nine
  generations.

Two are rumoured: **the asking fire** (demands a question before
each feeding; none declared in three generations) and
**fire-zero** (Dim 3; by Thermoclave grammar, has every
temperament and none).

**Etymology.** *Temperament* is a Thermoclave loan from a pre-
cataclysm metallurgical term — a metal's response to heat. The
homonym is treated as correct: a fire's character is *how it
tempers the room.*

**Cross-faction note.** Concord priced-clauses for a fire's
attendance cite temperament — *patient* commands a different rate
than *proud.* Veshen has signed against all five recognised;
never against an asking fire. Halen-Iss objected once to
*cold-mouthed* on the ground it is *substrate, not party-of-
record;* overruled by the *substrate-borne* clause (0845). The
Thermoclave's first lore-object since round 2 to **share a
grammar surface with three other factions.**

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a) Bible 123 vs. the Re-pressed apotheosis.**

> *Bible 123:* "once you see it, you can't unsee it, and it
> starts to change you."

The bible implied a *one-way slide* toward Transcription. The
Re-pressed apotheosis (Dim 5) opens a stable middle. *Starts to
change you* must be qualified: the change can stabilise short of
Transcription, the way the Long Throat stabilises short of
Communion.

**Recommend a single appended sentence to bible 123:**

> *Some readers stabilise mid-change as Re-pressed — both
> over-text and under-text legible in the same body — and live
> long lives in the layered tense without ever fully crossing
> into Transcription. Re-pressed readers are the Palimpsest's
> populated middle; their voices are to the Palimpsest what
> the five Long Throats are to the Choir.*

Third proposed bible amendment in corpus history (after 0845's
208-209 and 1001's 39 + `OldestTruth`).

**(b) RotChoir.json `Cataclysm` node vs. the layered tense.**

The `Cataclysm` node renders the cataclysm in a closed past
tense. Per Dim 1 the Palimpsest's account is *only* the layered
tense. No in-corpus document presents a side-by-side. The
contradiction is not a bug — it is the central thematic axis
bible 25 promises — **but no scene yet lets a player hear it.**

**Recommend a content task:** a Palimpsest dialogue node, opened
only after the player has spoken to `ChoirTendril_1`'s
`Cataclysm` branch, in which a Re-pressed NPC offers the *same
event* in the layered tense and the player can ask either speaker
to *close* or *open* their account. The first in-game encounter
with **the bridge-clause as live mechanic.**

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

**Title (in-game):** *Receipted ledger entry. Saccharine Concord
counting-house, Veshen's hand. Subject: a clause requested but
not signed. Filed under priorless.*

```
[Concord ledger. Stamped at the receipted level. The clause is
 written out in full and then crossed through with a single line —
 the Concord mark for "considered, not signed." Veshen's hand.]

  Counterparty: the unintaken record (Pale Curation form).
  Receipt presented by R-7 on behalf of the third reading-room,
  undated by Curation convention.

  Clause as presented: the Concord shall recognise the priorless
  ledger as a valid first-party in any transaction whose first
  writing predates the founding of the counting-house. Rate: by
  layer; one footnote per under-layer recovered.

  Considered. Not signed.

  Reasons sufficient:

    1. The priorless ledger is not a party. It is the floor
       beneath every party. The Concord cannot transact with a
       floor without standing on a different floor — and the
       Concord owns no other floor.

    2. The clause, signed, would price the Palimpsest. The
       Palimpsest, priced, would be re-pressurable in the
       Curation's grammar. Re-pressuring would close at least
       one layered tense. I am not authorised to close a
       layered tense by clause — and I am not certain anyone
       is.

  This entry is filed at the receipted level in deference to the
  priorless. The clause has been preserved in full because the
  Concord does not destroy considered-but-not-signed text — only
  files it in the *priorless cabinet,* which is the only Concord
  cabinet formally indexed by a non-Concord faction.

  (The priorless cabinet is read once a year by R-7. R-7 reads
   it in the layered tense, which I am told the Concord's
   grammar does not register. I have therefore registered
   nothing. This note is for the cabinet, not the ledger. — V.)
```

**The bombshell is parenthetical:** the priorless cabinet is the
only Concord cabinet formally indexed by a non-Concord faction —
and R-7 reads it annually *in the layered tense, which the
Concord cannot register.* Veshen knows. Veshen has therefore
*registered nothing.* The Concord's most valuable cabinet is, by
Concord grammar, **empty**, while a Curation reader is reading it
in a tense Veshen will not transcribe. The Concord, the Curation,
and the Palimpsest are conducting a triangular correspondence the
Concord's books cannot register, the Curation's catalogue cannot
date, and the Palimpsest considers ordinary Tuesday weather.
**The corpus's first lore-object authoritative in three grammars
and inadmissible in all three.**

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"The Palimpsest's grammar for the cataclysm: I was being
> written on. I am still being written on. I was already being
> written on. Three tenses, simultaneous. The Palimpsest does
> not regard them as contradiction; it regards them as the only
> honest answer."*

No archaisms; no apostrophe-fantasy names; no Aedra, Daedra,
Towers, CHIM, Mantling, Eight; no *verily, hath, lo, ere.* Plain
operational present, two flat past-progressives, one past-already.
A TES rewrite would read: *"And lo, the Page that is the World
declareth: 'I was being writ upon, I am writ upon yet, and ere
the writing was, the writing already had been.' The faithful know
this not as paradox, but as the truest of writings."* Mine has no
*and lo, declareth, ere, faithful, truest.* Operational register;
grammatical claim about tense simultaneity. Confirmed.
