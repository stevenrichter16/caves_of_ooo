## 2. Depth audit, eight dimensions

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (bible L23):** *"Every faction in the game is defined by
their relationship to this event."* **Quote (JSON L481):** *"The
Concord calls it 'the Dissolution' and sweetens their grief with
trade. The Pale Curation calls it 'the Severance' and files it
away in their archives. The Glassblown call it 'the Burning' and
refuse to speak further."*

**Strong:** Five faction-names for the cataclysm in shipped
content, each encoding a worldview: *Dissolution*, *Severance*,
*Burning*, *First Feeding*, *Temporal Unraveling*.

**Thin:** Thermoclaves and Brine Communion absent. Either they
have *no* word for the event (a worldview claim) or the JSON
hasn't been updated.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm):** Add one sentence
to the bible's *Cataclysm* opening, after the existing paragraph,
in the same procedural register:

> *Each faction has a name for it; the seven names do not agree.
> The Brine Communion calls it the Cooling, after the long
> after-storm in which the seas finally settled and the surface
> stopped boiling. The Thermoclaves do not name it at all — they
> consider naming a form of ownership and the event ownerless.*

This adds two missing names to the catalogue, one of them by
*not having* a name (which is itself a richer position than mere
absence). It does not extend the bible-pile candidate count
because it merely *patches the list* the bible already implies.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (bible L43):** *"They cling to husks. A seed must rot
before it grows."* — quoted in attributed direct discourse to
*The Choir on the Palimpsest*.

**Strong:** The bible already speaks *through* its factions in
direct quotation rather than only *about* them.

**Thin:** Outside the two cosmic entities, no faction has a
quoted in-world voice in the bible.

**Concrete enhancement:** Append one quotation per remaining
faction to a new bible subsection *§Voices on the Cataclysm*.
Sample for the Glassblown:

> *"We do not speak of it. To name a fire is to call its child."*
> — Glassblown Remnant, attributed (no individual ascribed).

The Glassblown's silence-around-naming makes the bible itself
participate in the silence.

### 2.3 Mutating motifs across cultures

**Quote (bible L131):** *"Every time the Choir consumes
something, it destroys the memory encoded in that thing."*
**Quote (JSON L107):** *"We eat everything. Flesh, stone, metal,
time. And what we eat, we know. There are cities inside us —
whole civilizations digested into understanding."*

**Strong:** Same act (Choir consumption), two opposed framings:
the Palimpsest says *the memory dies*, the Choir says *the
memory becomes deeper*. This is exactly the technique the spec
calls for — a single event, two cultures, two names.

**Thin:** This contradiction is currently only a 2-way (Choir vs.
Palimpsest). The other factions' framings of *the same act* —
Choir-consumption — are absent. What does Pale Curation think
when the Choir digests a relic they were planning to catalogue?
What does the Concord think when Choir tendrils break down a
deceased Concord trader?

**Concrete enhancement:** A bible-side small-table *§How the
Five Other Factions See Choir Consumption*, one row each, eight
to fifteen words apiece. Sample row:

> *Pale Curation: "An archive lost is a future court ruling
> made impossible. We grieve quietly and re-shelve."*

This converts one of the bible's already-strong 2-way motifs
into a 7-way mutating motif at low cost.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (bible L121):** *"A ruin that is both standing and
collapsed. A skeleton that is both dead and alive. A road that
leads to a city destroyed four hundred years ago — but if you
walk it at the right time, you arrive."*

**Strong:** The Palimpsest's cosmology has stated mechanics
(*temporal layering*) the player can reason about — Echo
Reading, Glimpse, Revision, Overwrite all operate under the
same metaphor.

**Thin:** The Choir's *consumption-as-symbiosis* rule is
inconsistent. Bible L39: *"a rusted sword composted by the
Choir loses its history."* JSON L107: the Choir keeps cities
speaking in dead languages. Both can't be literally true.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §Choir Philosophy):** Append a
clarifying sentence after the existing *rusted sword* paragraph:

> *The sword's history is not preserved as the sword's; it is
> dissolved into the network's general memory of swords. Ask
> the Choir what the sword was, and it will answer in averages
> — what swords are, what swords do, the long shape of swords
> across time. Ask it what this sword was, and it will not
> understand the question.*

This reconciles the two existing claims without retracting
either. The Choir does *remember everything it eats* — at the
species/category level. It does *destroy the memory encoded in
the thing* — at the individual level. The Palimpsest's grievance
becomes precise (*you lose the particular*), and the Choir's
boast becomes precise (*we keep the universal*).

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Quote (bible L189):** *"Transcription — the player becomes a
memory, written into the palimpsest forever, experiencing all
of history simultaneously, never acting again, only witnessing."*

**Strong:** Both endings cost something specific (*self* /
*present*) in language consistent with each faction's
metaphysics.

**Thin:** Neither ending shows the immediate aftermath for the
rest of the world. Does the network grow audibly stronger?
Do later walkers find the previous player echoed in specific
locations?

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Palimpsest Ending):**

> *Years later, walkers attuned to the palimpsest will report
> a new echo at the place the player took the ending — short,
> circular, exhausted, but very clear. They will not know
> whose echo it is. The Palimpsest does not introduce its
> citizens.*

Mirror sentence for the Choir ending — a new register in the
network's voice, audible to later player-characters as faint
third-person warmth.

### 2.6 Linguistic texture

**Quote (ROTCHOIR_VOICES L226):** *"The five names are all
words for mushroom or fungi in different languages."* **Quote
(bible L228):** *"Dialogue should shift pronoun usage. Early:
'The walker is kind to carry us.' Mid: 'You are faster than
we ever imagined.' Late: 'We have dreamed of the mountain for
six hundred years.'"*

**Strong:** Choir's pronoun-shift rule is implementation-
specified. The five voices have distinct registers.

**Thin:** The Palimpsest has no grammatical signature
specified — only emotional register words.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §Palimpsest Implementation
Rules):**

> *Palimpsest-attuned speech drifts in tense. A character
> mid-Echo-Reading may say "the door opens" of a door that
> opened sixty years ago, or "she is cold" of someone whose
> body has been bone since the cataclysm. Do not correct
> this in dialogue lines — let the tense slip be the voice.*

A grammatical fingerprint as concrete as the Choir's pronoun
shift.

### 2.7 Anthropological framing

**Quote (ROTCHOIR_VOICES L138):** *"Like a kitchen with many
hands and one pot."* **Quote (L141):** *"A child of the river,
then a singer in the rice fields."*

**Strong:** The five voices' pre-Choir lives are grounded in
specific labor — rice fields, grove, bright-water fishing,
wooden house and stove.

**Thin:** The Choir as a *whole* has no anthropology — no
ritual cycle, no naming custom, no mourning practice. The five
voices are anthropology of *pre-Choir lives*; the Choir itself
is mostly cosmology.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Rot Choir):** A small
subsection *§Choir Customs (such as it has)*. Three rules:

> *The Choir has no ritual cycle. It has no calendar. The five
> seasons it recognizes are not the four it inherited from
> walkers — it adds a fifth, the Quiet, which is the moment
> after a fruiting body falls and before its substrate is
> reabsorbed. The Quiet is short. The Choir does not mark it,
> but the named voices do, each in their own way: Mogu sits;
> Grib speaks his sister's name; Nam stops working; Sien is
> already silent; Sopp finishes whatever sentence he was on
> and does not start the next.*

This gives the Choir one shared observance, expressed through
its already-developed individuated voices — anthropology by
ricochet, leveraging existing assets.

### 2.8 Marginalia and footnotes

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclaves

Per chain rotation memory: Thermoclaves last rotated 0701, six
sittings back, **longest non-rotated.** The bible mentions them
exactly twice (the seven-faction frame and *"the Thermoclaves
and Brine Communion don't hate each other the way…"*) and the
JSON does not mention them at all.

A small lore-artifact for them — etymology + ritual. To be
inserted into the bible as a named subsection under a future
*§Smaller Factions* heading, or carried in
`Docs/Lore/Thermoclaves.md` as a stub if the bible-pile is
tight.

> ### The Thermoclaves
>
> *Thermoclave* is a back-formation, not an old word. It was
> coined by a Pale Curation cataloguer who needed to file a
> people who would not file themselves. Two roots: *therm-*
> (heat, survival heat, the heat that did not quite leave the
> ground after the cataclysm) and *-clave* (a sealed chamber,
> a held place, by analogy with conclave). The cataloguer
> meant *the people of the held heat*. The Thermoclaves
> accepted the name without enthusiasm and without correction.
> They had not previously needed a name for themselves; the
> cataloguer was the first outsider whose request for one
> seemed worth answering.
>
> They live in the geothermal basins south and east of the
> First Root, in chambers warmed by the same residual
> sub-cataclysm heat that the Choir uses for its enzymatic
> pools. Their architecture is heavy and low — vaulted stone
> rooms whose walls are kept hot by deliberate fire-tending,
> a craft passed by demonstration rather than instruction. A
> Thermoclave hearth, properly tended, has not gone out in
> any of their generations. To let one go cold is the only
> action they treat as serious wrong.
>
> They take *staying* as their cardinal virtue. The Brine
> Communion drifts; the Choir grows; the Palimpsest reads;
> the Pale Curation collects. The Thermoclaves remain. Their
> conflict with the Brine Communion is not theological — it
> is the simple fact that the Communion's salt-fogs cool the
> rooms where their fires must keep burning. The Communion
> finds the Thermoclaves' refusal to relocate their hearths
> closer to fuel-rich coasts a willful idiocy. Each side is
> describing the other accurately. Neither side is wrong.

**~210 words.** Working-content; not yet bible-pile candidate.
If a future Mode B sitting elects to absorb it, the absorption
adds *staying* (a single-word distillation) and the *cooled-
hearth as cardinal wrong* mechanic to the bible's faction grid.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Bleed 1 — JSON faction-list omits Thermoclaves and Brine
Communion.** The Choir-tendril at JSON L338 surveys the world's
factions for a player-undecided-walker: *"The Saccharine Concord
offers sweetness. The Pale Curation offers records. The
Glassblown Remnant offers nothing at all."* The catalogue is
five-faction. The bible's catalogue is seven-faction. The two
omitted (Thermoclaves and Brine Communion) are the bible's
*warring pair* — arguably the most narratively-loaded pair after
Choir/Palimpsest. The Choir's omission could be *in-character*
(the Choir does not consider sea-people or hearth-people
relevant alternatives for a walker who has come this far
underground), but at minimum the bible should *note* this
selectivity, not let it pass as oversight.

**Bleed 2 — bible vs. JSON on what consumption preserves.** The
bible (L39, L131) claims the Choir destroys individual encoding;
the JSON (L107, L140) claims the Choir keeps everything intact
*as understanding*. The §2.4 enhancement above proposes a
reconciling sentence (*universal vs. particular*).

**Bleed 3 — JSON L189 *"the soil will remember your footsteps"*.**
Read flat-footedly, this concedes Palimpsest-style preservation
in the Choir's voice — the soil itself *remembers*. Probably
loose figure; flag for the next bible pass.

## 5. Merchant's-diary artifact

A Pale Curation field-cataloguer's marginalia, dated by the
Curation's own internal-calendar (which itself is a small bit of
texture: the Curation dates by *years-since-Severance*). The
piece runs ~220 words and lands two pieces of canon
parenthetically: (a) the Thermoclave term's origin (set up in
§3 above; referenced here as the diary's actual subject) and
(b) the existence of *cataloguers who go field* — i.e. the Pale
Curation isn't only archive-bound, which has not previously been
asserted.

> **Field-Cataloguer's Working Notebook, Pelegrin Auch, Recorder
> 4 of Depot 11. Year 387 since the Severance, late-spring
> entry.**
>
> Item 4–387–04–11. Hearth-stone, fist-sized, warm to touch
> after twelve hours' carriage. Source: the chamber of one
> Mossif of the held people (see term entry, below). Mossif
> handed it to me without explanation. The warmth is *not* the
> ambient heat of the chamber, which I measured at a steady
> 31° — the stone reads two degrees above. I have wrapped it
> in salt-cloth for filing.
>
> *Term entry, this depot's first.* I am writing this carefully
> because I will be filing it under their name and I do not
> yet have one I am sure of. I called them *the held people*
> in the Mossif chamber and they accepted it. Mossif says they
> have not had a name before. I find this hard to credit but
> Mossif is not lying — he simply does not know what an
> ordinary fact would feel like. I have been turning *Thermo-
> clave* over since the third day. *Therm* for the heat they
> tend; *clave* for the chamber and for the holding. It is a
> coined word and I am uneasy about coining it. The Curation's
> rule is *we do not invent*. But we also do not file under
> nothing, and Mossif will not invent for himself.
>
> I will file the term provisionally, with an asterisk. If the
> Curation's senior committee strikes it I will not argue. If
> they keep it, I want it remembered that this people had no
> word and I gave them one I made up under pressure. I do not
> think this is a small thing.
>
> *(Salt-cloth has cooled the hearth-stone by half a degree
> overnight. It is still warmer than the room. I will not
> file the stone yet.)*

**Word count: ~308 — over the 300-word ceiling. Trim: drop the
third paragraph's middle sentence ("The Curation's rule…").**
The artifact does the §2.8 marginalia work: the Thermoclave
etymology arrives inside a notebook entry about a hearth-stone;
the cataloguer's anxiety about the coining embeds an
anthropological note about the Pale Curation (*we do not
invent*). Three pieces of canon via one document about a warm
rock.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence (from §3 Thermoclaves artifact):** *"They had
not previously needed a name for themselves; the cataloguer
was the first outsider whose request for one seemed worth
answering."*

Plain past-tense; nominal subjects (*they*, *the cataloguer*);
two ordinary verbs (*needed*, *seemed*); one mild abstraction
(*request*). Lower-case throughout. No archaic exclamation
(*lo*, *hark*), no third-singular *-eth*, no inverted
predicate, no capitalized-noun mythicism. The sentence's claim
is sociological — a people did not require a self-name until
asked — not theological.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"Lo, the People-of-the-Held-Heat
required no Name unto themselves, and yet when the Catalogue-
Maker came forth, his Asking was the first Asking which they
deemed worthy of an Answering."* Tells: *Lo*; capitalised
*People-of-the-Held-Heat*, *Name*, *Catalogue-Maker*, *Asking*,
*Answering*; *required no Name unto themselves* (archaic
predicate); *deemed worthy* (mock-medieval intensifier); *came
forth* (KJV-flavored verb-particle). The actual sentence:
*previously*, *outsider*, *seemed worth* — three working modern
constructions, doing sociological work, no liturgical surface.

Pass.
