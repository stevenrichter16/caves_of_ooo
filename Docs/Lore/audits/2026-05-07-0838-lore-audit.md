## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (JSON L481):** *"The Pale Curation calls it 'the
Severance' and files it away in their archives."*

**Strong.** Cataclysm-name canon, ironised by the Choir's *files
it away* — the Curation's response to a cosmic event is to
catalogue it.

**Thin.** The bible never gives the Curation's *own* sentence
about the Severance — the working text the body would read
aloud at the start of a depot's sitting, the way the Glassblown
have a first-firing utterance (0001 §2.1). The faction's
foundational sentence is a Choir paraphrase.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm, append):**

> *Pale Curation depots open each working-day with a fixed
> recital, recorded in every depot's standing-book in the same
> form:* "The Severance was. We did not see it. We are here to
> see what it left." *The recital is not religious. It is —
> in Curation register —* the calibration of the eye*: a
> reminder that the cataloguer's medium is** what remains**, not
> the event that produced it. A depot whose standing-book lacks
> the recital is, by Curation discipline, not a working depot.
> Curation field-cataloguers carry a small folded copy of the
> recital in the front of every working notebook (see Pelegrin
> Auch, Depot 11 Recorder 4, working notebook fly-leaf).*

Locks in the doctrinal core (the Curation cannot witness the
Severance; it can only witness *the leftovers*) and gives
Pelegrin Auch's notebook a paratextual feature the 1301
artifact already half-described — a fixed fly-leaf — without
contradicting it.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (1301 §5):** *"Field-Cataloguer's Working Notebook,
Pelegrin Auch, Recorder 4 of Depot 11. Year 387 since the
Severance."*

**Strong.** The Curation's authorship-mode is *signed-and-dated
by named recorder*, in deliberate opposition to the Glassblown's
mark-only paratext (0001 §2.2). Two factions, two posture-poles
on naming: Curation names everything; Glassblown names nothing
in writing.

**Thin.** No canonised *unsigned* Curation document. The
Curation's discipline against anonymity has never been stated.

**Concrete enhancement (bible new sidebar, "Curation authorship"):**

> *A Curation document without a signing recorder and a depot
> stamp is, in working register,* a draft*. It may be filed —
> drafts are filed in the **provisional stack** of the depot
> that produced them — but it cannot be cited. A senior
> cataloguer making a finding cites stack-shelf and recorder
> together (e.g.* "filed Depot-11/working-shelf/Auch–387–04"*).
> A finding cited from the provisional stack carries a separate
> notation —* prov.*— in the citing document's margin, alerting
> any subsequent reader that the underlying record is unsigned
> and may be amended. Pale Curation's foundational discipline
> against anonymous record is doctrinal, not procedural: the
> Severance is the unsigned event; the body's working-rule is
> that nothing else of consequence shall be unsigned in our
> hand.*

Falsifies a small mechanical edge: any Curation NPC who refuses
to sign a finding is either (a) calling that finding
provisional, (b) under duress, or (c) doctrinally
compromised — useful narrative space, in the same shape as
0001's complete-sentence rule for Glassblown.

### 2.3 Mutating motifs across cultures

**Quote (2301 R3, 0001 §2.3):** seven-faction sidebars on the
Concord stone and the Glassblown watch-bowl.

**Strong.** Two thing-anchored motif tables now exist.

**Thin.** Both anchored objects are *small portable*. The
Curation's principal artifact is **the depot stamp** — large,
fixed, institutional. No motif-table yet anchors on a
non-portable working object.

**Concrete enhancement (bible new sidebar, "Names for the
depot stamp"):**

The depot stamp is the iron press a Curation depot uses to mark
a finding as *received-and-shelved.* Each depot's stamp is
unique; copying one is, in Curation register, *forging the
depot.*

> - **Pale Curation:** *the stamp* — neutral, working-form. The
>   stamping is the moment a finding becomes citable. A document
>   without a stamp is a draft (§2.2).
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *the seal-press* — recognised as
>   close-equivalent to a Concord clerk's clause-binding, but
>   one-party. Concord clerks find the Curation's *one-signature
>   close* doctrinally suspect (a close should bind two
>   parties); they accept stamped Curation findings in trade
>   anyway, marking them in their own ledgers as
>   *one-party closes accepted in good faith.*
> - **Glassblown:** *the cooled press* — fire-touched and now
>   cooled; an object the Glassblown will handle in trade but
>   will not store in a working-quarter (the press has held
>   heat to deform metal; a working-quarter holds only finished
>   coolings).
> - **Brine Communion:** *the dry mark* — accepted in trade,
>   read by sailors as a non-living-water mark. A Communion
>   ship that has carried Curation-stamped cargo records the
>   stamp's depot-number in the ship's working-log; the log is
>   read at the next anchor as a small history of *who has
>   trusted this hull.*
> - **Thermoclaves:** *the cold press* — Refusal-hearth
>   doctrine permits handling but not custody. A
>   Thermoclave-public-hall may receive a stamped document; a
>   Refusal-hearth keeps the document on the public-hall side
>   of the hearth-line.
> - **Rot Choir:** *the iron* — silica-and-iron, unregistered.
>   The Choir cannot compost the press itself and reads the
>   *stamping* as a small attempt to do what the Choir already
>   does (incorporate a finding into a body); it considers the
>   attempt structurally inadequate but not hostile.
> - **Palimpsest:** *the small heavy weight* — Echo Reading
>   recovers the recorder's hand on the press, the tension at
>   the moment of impact, and the depot's silence in the
>   surrounding seconds. A long-used press reads thickly: each
>   stamping leaves an echo, and the echoes accumulate — a
>   senior depot's stamp is among the densest readable objects
>   in the Curation's holdings.

Three motif-tables now (Concord stone, Glassblown bowl,
Curation press), enough that the *seven-faction posture-table*
is canonised as a documentary form, not a one-off.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Thin.** *Severance-as-knife-with-no-hand* (0801 §2.1) is a
gloss, not a cosmology. The Curation's stance toward the
present has never been canonised.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Pale Curation subsection,
when it lands):**

> *The Pale Curation teaches that the Severance was not a
> destruction but a** removal of authorship**: an act whose
> agent cannot be recovered by any reading the Curation knows
> how to perform. The world after the Severance is, in their
> reading, a manuscript whose first hand is gone — every page
> readable, no signature recoverable. The body's discipline
> follows directly: in a world where the foundational hand has
> been lost, the only honest stance is** to not lose any
> further hands**. Every finding is signed. Every depot is
> named. Every recorder's number persists across the
> recorder's career and is retired with them at withdrawal.
> The Curation's working-register name for itself —* the
> readers — *means specifically: those who, since the Severance,
> have refused to file anything anonymously. Their cosmology
> is therefore not theology but** counter-discipline **— a
> multi-generational refusal to repeat the Severance at small
> scale. They do not believe the original hand can be
> recovered. They believe the body's own hands can be kept
> legible. This places them in specific friction with the
> Choir, whose composting** unsigns **objects (a sword digested
> is a sword whose forging-hand is irrecoverable), and in a
> different friction with the Palimpsest, whose Annotation can
> introduce** new unsigned text **into the world the Curation
> has spent four hundred years signing.*

Falsifiable cosmology any future scene can lean on. Gives the
Choir an explanation for the Curation's vault-keeping (the
Choir would call it hoarding; the Curation would call it
*not unsigning*). Gives the Palimpsest a friction the bible's
Annotation paragraph can now name. Gives the Concord a
doctrinal counterweight (Concord: *contracts are again
possible*; Glassblown: *coolings are again finishable*;
Curation: *hands are again kept*).

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Thin.** The Curation has no canonised reading of either
ending. The 2301 closed with a Concord reading; the 0001 with a
Glassblown reading. The Curation's silence is conspicuous.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Central Question, append):**

> *The Pale Curation's reading of the two endings is shorter
> than the Concord's, sharper than the Glassblown's, and nearly
> identical between them. A Communion-walker is, in Curation
> reading,* an unsigned finding *— a body that has filed
> itself into the Choir's body without first signing the file.
> A Transcription-walker is* a self-citation *— a hand that
> has annotated itself permanently into the Palimpsest's
> standing record. The Curation regards** both as failures of
> authorship-discipline at terminal scale **but distinguishes
> the two on a single technical point: the Communion-walker is
> unrecoverable, while the Transcription-walker is, by
> definition, citable. Curation seniors will not endorse either
> ending. They will, with the Transcription, agree to* receive
> the citation *— that is, file the walker's name in the depot
> where the walker last drew a recorder-number, with a single
> notation:* present in the standing record. *They will not
> file a Communion-walker at all. The depot's working-book
> simply ceases to carry the walker's recorder-number from the
> day of the Communion forward. The cessation is the entry.*

Integrates with §2.4. Gives the Curation a doctrinal posture
toward the two endings without committing the body to lore-
breaking refusal — and the *cessation-as-entry* form is a
move only the Curation can make (the Concord has no shape for
absent-data; the Glassblown have no shape for cited-absence;
the Curation's working register is the only one in which
*nothing further filed* is itself a filing).

> *Curation working-speech is** indirect free** — the
> cataloguer reports an observation by routing it through the

Falsifies a Curation-NPC speech-rule: any future Curation NPC

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Pale Curation (the depot, the career)

Two pieces. Anthropology of the building, then anthropology of
the working-life inside it.

> ### The Pale Curation depot (anthropology)
>
> A depot is a fortified single-story building, stone-walled and
> low-windowed, organised around a central reading-hall called
> the *bench-floor*. The bench-floor holds between five and
> twenty-three working-benches arranged in two opposed ranks; a
> recorder is assigned a bench-position by seniority (lowest
> bench-number to most senior). The body's term *first-bench*
> means the senior-most working position at a depot, not the
> first to be filled. F.M. of the *seventh-bench* (0801 §2.2)
> is a mid-senior recorder at a depot of at least seven benches.
>
> Beyond the bench-floor are: the **standing-shelves** (filed
> findings, alphabetised by item-number, ironbound and
> non-portable); the **working-shelves** (signed-and-dated drafts,
> consulted but not citable, the *prov.*-stack of §2.2); a
> **stamp-room** (the depot's iron press, kept under custody of
> the first-bench recorder; see §2.3 sidebar); and a single
> small **inscription-table** at the rear, where a recorder
> withdrawing from the body strikes their own recorder-number
> from the depot's standing-book. The body's name for the rear
> table is *the closing-table*. It is used at most twice per
> generation per depot.
>
> Architecture: the standing-shelves form the building's
> structural inner walls. A depot is, mechanically, *a building
> held up by what it has filed*. The body considers this
> deliberate — a depot whose findings have been removed for
> consolidation cannot stand without temporary internal
> bracing, and the bracing is, in working register, the
> depot's *empty form*. Outsiders who have read in Curation
> depots note that the building hums slightly at the working-
> hours of high traffic; the senior recorders attribute this
> to the standing-shelves' iron bands, which respond to the
> bench-floor's ambient pressure. Echo Reading on a depot
> wall, the bible's standing rule on Echo Reading would
> predict, recovers four centuries of recital openings layered
> over each other (§2.1).
>
> ### The recorder's career (working-life)
>
> A recorder enters the body as **junior** at a depot and is
> assigned a recorder-number on the day they sign their first
> finding. The number is small (single- or double-digit at most
> depots) and *persists across the recorder's working-life*: a
> recorder who transfers to another depot keeps the number; a
> recorder who serves at three depots over a career carries
> the same number through all three. The number is retired
> when the recorder strikes it from the standing-book at the
> closing-table.
>
> The body recognises three working-tiers — *junior*,
> *recorder*, *senior recorder* — promoted by the
> first-bench's reading of accumulated findings, not by year-
> count. A junior promoted to recorder is given the body's
> name *named-recorder*; the named are those whose findings
> are citable without *prov.* notation (§2.2). A recorder
> promoted to senior is given a bench-position.
>
> A senior who has served a working-life withdraws from the
> body by the rite called **closing the book**: at the
> inscription-table, with a junior witness, the senior writes a
> single sentence of their own composition into the depot's
> standing-book — the *closing line* — and strikes their
> recorder-number on the same page. The closing line is the
> only place in a recorder's working-life they are permitted to
> write in the first person without citation. Most closing
> lines are observational and short. Some are stranger. The
> sentences are read aloud at the depot's next morning recital
> for thirty days, and then filed into the standing-shelves
> with the rest. A depot's accumulated closing lines are
> consulted by the first-bench when a difficult finding asks a
> question the body's working-rules cannot answer alone.

Two pieces. The first builds the depot as architecture (the
*held-up-by-its-own-shelves* image is the depth-move). The
second builds the recorder's career-arc and lands a single
ritual — the closing-table — that gives §5 its artifact and
§2.5 its self-citation form.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Bleed 1 — bible L47 *"Pale Curation vault"* vs. §3.** The
bible says *vault*. The audit-pile has consistently said
*depot*. The two terms are not synonyms — *vault* implies
sealed-and-locked storage, *depot* implies working-traffic with
a bench-floor and incoming finds. The audit-pile is canon-
forming on this. Suggested bible edit: change L47 *Pale
Curation vault* to *Pale Curation depot* on the next bible
revision; if a *vault* is meant (a deeper, sealed sub-archive
beneath a depot) it should be canonised as such, distinct from
the working depot. §3 above implies the working depot is the
public structure; a vault, if it exists, is not present in §3.

**Bleed 2 — JSON L324 *"watchers, cataloguers, hoarders of
moments"* vs. §2.4.** The Choir's *hoarders* is canon-correct
as a Choir-side reading, but in §2.4 terms the Curation are not
hoarding *moments* — they are refusing to *unsign* objects.
The Choir cannot register the distinction (an unsigned object,
to the Choir, is the only kind there is). The bible should not
echo *hoarders* without the §2.4 floor in place. Suggested
footnote, post-§2.4 absorption: *"The Choir's characterisation
is canon, in the same sense as a one-eyed observer's
description of a wall (§2.4 Glassblown footnote, 0001 §4): the
Choir registers the Curation's accumulated record as
acquisition; the Curation registers the same record as
non-erasure."*

**Bleed 3 — 0801 §2.2 *bible-as-Curation-dossier* (filed under
PROVISIONAL/COSMOGONY-3-RECEN-A) vs. §2.4.** The 0801 framing
has the bible itself as a *provisional* Curation working-file.
§2.4 makes *prov.* a citation rule: a provisional document is
unsigned-by-discipline. The bible's filing as PROVISIONAL is
therefore not a humility-flag but a **doctrinal admission**:
the body considers its own cosmogonical reading to be a
working-shelf draft. Suggested addition (bible head,
absorbing 0801 §2.2): the editor's-note signature line should
read *F.M., recorder, Curation seventh-bench (working-shelf)*
— the parenthetical *(working-shelf)* makes the filing-status
visible at first glance and does the §2.4 work without further
commentary.

## 5. Merchant's-diary artifact

The 0001 closer flagged the prototype-queue as missing a
*Curation-document-about-a-Curator*. This run lands a
**closing-line page from a depot's standing-book**, written by
a senior cataloguer at her withdrawal, with a junior witness's

The page lands four canon items parenthetically: §2.4 cosmology
of *not-unsigning* as the senior's stated reason; §3 closing-
table rite in operational use; §2.6 indirect-free as the
junior's *failure mode* (the junior reaches for citation and
has nothing to cite); and a quiet bombshell about what the
Severance was *to the body's interior memory* — recovered, as
required, parenthetically, in the senior's closing line.

> **FRONT (depot standing-book; depot-stamped, signed and
> counter-signed; iron-gall ink, the senior's hand even and
> small):**
>
> *Depot 11. Day** 92 of Sev. year 412**. Closing line of
> Recorder 4 — Pelegrin Auch — at withdrawal from working-life,
> first-bench witnessing.*
>
> *(Recorder 4 has filed 1,847 findings under this number across
> three depots — Depot 4 — 17 — 11. The number is hereby
> retired; no future recorder shall hold it at this depot.)*
>
> [Then, in the same hand, smaller, on a single underlined
> line:]
>
> > *I was always a junior. The body has only juniors. The
> > Severance is what taught us that, and we have spent four
> > hundred years pretending the lesson was about** filing**.*
>
> [Below the closing line, in iron-gall ink, the first-bench
> recorder's signature and stamp; below that, in pencil, in a
> different hand:]
>
> **(Junior-recorder's witness-note, undated, pencil, neat
> but uncertain:)**
>
> *Witnessed at the closing-table on day 92, with first-bench
> Sevra. The senior's closing line is filed as written above
> and will be read at recital from day 93 through day 122. I
> was permitted to ask, after the table closed, what the
> closing line meant. First-bench Sevra read me Recorder 4's
> own working notebook, fly-leaf — the recital — and said only,
> in working register:* "She is citing the recital." *I have
> filed Sevra's gloss in the provisional shelf under
> Sevra–412–092–prov., recorder-number 1, and will return to
> it after a working-month. The closing line itself is now in
> the standing-shelves, Auch–412–092, citable. I do not yet
> understand what I have filed.*

The closing line itself is the bombshell — *the body has only
juniors* recasts the entire Curation career-grammar as a
discipline performed in full knowledge of its own incompleteness.
The Severance unhanded the original author; every Curation
recorder, no matter how senior, is by their own doctrine still
*reading after* — still a junior to the missing first hand.
The senior cataloguers know this. The juniors do not. *Closing
the book* is, in part, the moment the body's most senior member
admits it. The artifact lands the recasting parenthetically,
embedded in a withdrawal-procedure document the junior is
filing as a routine matter.

The doctrinal core (§2.4's *not-unsigning* as
counter-discipline) is the senior's stated *reason* — refusing
to unsign even our own incompleteness. The junior's filing of
Sevra's gloss as *prov.* is the §2.6 indirect-free rule
operating at full strength: the junior cannot first-person the
finding, so she cites Sevra's gloss, even though Sevra's gloss
is itself uncertain. The structure recovers two conventions in
one document.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted (§2.4):** *"The world after the Severance is, in their
reading, a manuscript whose first hand is gone — every page
readable, no signature recoverable."*

Modern register. *In their reading*, *manuscript whose first
hand is gone*: contemporary literary-critical prose. The
em-dash construction is a working-essay's parallelism; *every
page readable, no signature recoverable* is a balanced clause
any modern essayist would recognise. Plain verbs. No archaisms;
*the Severance* sits as a name, not a capitalised mythic
abstract; no inverted predicate; no *unto*, *verily*, or
*hath*.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"Verily, the world after the
Great Severance is, in the teaching of the Curators, a manuscript
whereof the first hand hath gone forth — each page yet
readable, yet no signature unto recovery."* Tells: *Verily*;
*Great Severance* (capitalised mythic-abstract); *whereof / yet
/ unto* archaic register; *the Curators* (TES-pluralisation
into a definite-article epithet); KJV-style final clause.
Actual sentence is none of these.

**Pass.**
