## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (1301 §2.1):** *"the recital cites itself."*

**Strong.** The body's standing form for the citation
it cannot perform.

**Thin.** *Cites itself* names the citation-failure;
it does not name the cadence in which the failure is
daily renewed. The recital is read from the same
ironbound page every working-day. *Every working-day*
is not noise — it is the cadence-axis 1301 did not
isolate.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Pale Curation,
append after 1301 §2.1):**

> *The recital is conducted at second-light in every
> depot's bench-floor, by the first-bench, facing
> the depot stamp. The reading is from the same
> ironbound page each working-day; the page does not
> rotate. The recital is not the page's content — it
> is the body's daily relationship to the page. A
> first-bench asked whether the recital is
> performed* on *the page or* from *it answers:*
> "the recital is the working-day standing on the
> page. the page is what the body stood on
> yesterday. tomorrow the body will stand on it
> again." *The cadence is the body's footing. The
> medium is the floor. The discipline cannot file
> the floor; the discipline keeps the body's
> working-day rhythm of standing on it.*

Every body canonises one axis and conducts the
irregular working-form on the other. The four
preceding bodies' single-axis projections were the
body's choice of which axis to make legible.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (1301 §2.4):** *"third-shelf material is
read, cited (as th.), and held in custody without
claim of authorship."*

**Strong.** Found-unsigned doctrine canonised.

**Thin.** *Read* is collapsed: a single verb naming
both silent first-bench consultation and the **daily
recital aloud in the public hall**. The two differ
in cadence — consultation is event-cadence, recital
is working-day cadence. The recital is the body's
only working-form for *reading aloud from a hand
the body cannot recover*.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Pale Curation,
new sidebar "The reader's stance"):**

> *The first-bench reading the recital does not
> hold the page. The page rests on a stand at the
> first-bench's left, opened to its single
> recoverable face; the first-bench reads from
> memory, glancing only to confirm. To hold the
> page would be to author its reading by hand-on-
> medium contact; to read from memory alone would
> risk drift over generations. The stand-and-glance
> form places the reader's hand off the medium and
> the reading aligned to the medium each working-
> day. The stand is itself unstamped — third-shelf-
> adjacent by working-tradition, not adjudication.*

The Curation's two-axis form is the body's foot
held precisely off the page it is standing on.

### 2.3 Mutating motifs across cultures

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (1301 §2.4):** *"…held in custody without
claim of authorship."*

**Strong.** Custody-form canonised.

**Thin.** Custody is static. The body's working-
relationship to third-shelf material is **dynamic** —
the daily recital pulls one third-shelf page into
the public hall every working-day. *Custody* is the
filing-claim; *standing on* is the working-claim.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Pale Curation,
append after 1301 §2.4):**

> *Third-shelf iron-banded boxes are stamped on
> sealing and re-stamped on closure-of-consultation,
> on the* inside *of the lid, by the consulting
> recorder's recorder-number and the date. The
> outside does not record consultations. The
> asymmetry is not concealment — juniors are taught
> it on intake-rotation — but a doctrinal form for*
> a record the body keeps but cannot author
> externally*. The recital's ironbound page does not
> have a box; it does not need one. The recital* is
> *the consultation, daily, hand-off-page. The
> page's working-record is** the depot's daily
> second-light hour** — kept by the body's rhythm
> rather than by ink. The cadence is the box.*

The discipline's central verb is *sign*. The body's
working-verb for the third shelf is **stand**. The
Glassblown's *kept* is intransitive because the
discipline cannot subject the keeper; the
Curation's *stand* is intransitive because the
discipline cannot subject the standing.

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Hold-and-absorb.** 1301 §2.4 canonised that Echo

that has held it. 1801 sharpening: Echo Reading on
the **recital's ironbound page** recovers not a list
of hands but a list of **working-days** — the page
has been stood on, hand-off, every working-day for
four hundred years. The Palimpsest's reading would
be the only external record of the Curation's
cadence. The body does not invite Palimpsest readers
into the third shelf; the doctrinal seam is sharper
than 1301 named.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Pale Curation (the second-light hour, the standing rite)

> ### The second-light hour
>
> Every depot conducts the recital at *second-light*
> — the working-hour beginning when the depot's
> eastern aperture admits direct sun on the bench-
> floor's first stone. Founding-era depots keep the
> aperture as a small window; later depots, as a
> glazed slit. The working-form depends only on the
> *first stone* catching direct light. On overcast
> working-days, the recital is conducted at the
> working-hour the first-bench judges to be second-
> light by clock — a doctrinal tolerance the body
> files as *cadence-substitution*, not *cadence-
> failure*.
>
> ### The standing rite
>
> The first-bench approaches from the left, lowers
> their hand toward the page without contact, and
> recites. Junior benches stand at their stations;
> standing seniors stand a half-step behind the
> first-bench's right shoulder, in bench-number
> order. The recital lasts no longer than the
> hour's first quarter; at its close, the first-
> bench raises their hand and turns to the stamp.
>
> ### What the body does not do
>
> The body does not record that the recital was
> conducted. No working-shelf entry for *recital,
> day 96, by first-bench Sevra*. The cadence is the
> record. A working-week in which a depot's recital
> was missed is filed by working-tradition as a
> **break**, named to a sister depot by courier-
> letter. Breaks have occurred seventeen times in
> four hundred working-years. The seventeen are
> remembered by working-tradition, not by ink. A
> first-bench taking up post is told the seventeen
> by their predecessor on the retirement morning,
> in shelf-position order of the depots in which
> they occurred.
>
> ### The cataloguer's rotation
>
> A junior on intake-rotation is taught the recital
> by attendance. No one explains it. The first-bench
> does not gesture; the standing seniors do not turn.
> The junior learns by watching the hand-off-page
> form. What cannot be canonised cannot be taught by
> instruction; only the cadence teaches the cadence.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted (§2.1):** *"the recital is the working-day
standing on the page. the page is what the body
stood on yesterday. tomorrow the body will stand on
it again."*

Working-modern register. Lower-case body-speech;
plain present and past tenses; no fronted nominal;
no archaism. *Standing on* is a contemporary
compound used precisely; *the body* is the
project's standing collective-third-person. No
*hath, doth, verily*; no capitalised abstract nouns.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"That the Recital is
the Standing of the Body upon the Page; the Page
is what the Body hath stood upon yestermorn; on
the morrow shall the Body stand upon it once more."*
Tells: capitalised *Recital / Body / Page*;
*yestermorn, on the morrow, shall, hath stood*;
fronted *That the Recital is*. Actual sentence is
none of these.

**Pass.**
