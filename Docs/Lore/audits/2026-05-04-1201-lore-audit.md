## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible 208:* "they are two cosmic-scale entities that genuinely
> cannot understand why the other does what it does."

**Strong.** The philosophical impasse is canonical. Bible 192–204
gives a comparative table.

**Thin.** **No specific event in two grammars.** Faction-grammars
have collected names on both sides — *the older soil* (Choir for
Palimpsest, 1101), *folded back* (Glassblown for Palimpsest,
2324), *an eraser that believes it is a pen* (bible 133). No audit
has produced a single *event* both render side-by-side and
disagree on at every verb.

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar after 210, "One ruin in
two grammars"):

> A small Choir tendril enters a ruin in the third spring after
> the cataclysm. It spends forty seasons inside. At the end the
> ruin is gone — stone become soil, timbers become network,
> memory (in Palimpsest grammar) *settled.*
>
> Choir's account: *a patient feeding. The old soil deepens. The
> ruin is taken. We grew where it stood; we are where it was; we
> will be where it remembered being.* No regret-form, no
> mourning-form — the Choir does not have these forms.
>
> Palimpsest's account, in the layered tense: *the page hurried.
> Under-text and over-text approached pressure-equality at the
> rate of digestion. The ruin is being-and-was-and-will-have-
> been-flattened.* The verb *flatten* for what the Choir called
> *take.* The verb *hurry* for what the Choir called *patient.*
>
> Both correct in their own grammar. The reader chooses which to
> call *what happened.* The Palimpsest considers this choice *a
> flattening of its own.* The Choir does not consider it a
> choice.

The corpus's first event-rendering in two grammars at every verb.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

> *Bible 224:* "Never confirm the Choir is manipulating the player.
> There should be no 'gotcha' reveal."

**Strong.** The Choir's unreliability rule is canonical: helpful,
truthful, *selective*.

**Thin.** **No parallel rule for the Palimpsest.** 1101 established
that the Palimpsest does not arbitrate. It did not establish what
the Palimpsest does with *selection* — which under-text it lets a
reader find.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection after Palimpsest
Implementation Rules):

> Never confirm the Palimpsest is selecting which layers a reader
> sees. There should be no "gotcha" reveal that the under-text
> was chosen for the reader. Some players will go an entire
> campaign believing every Glimpse is exhaustive; others will
> refuse the Manuscript at Honored tier and never see late-game
> content. Both experiences should feel complete.
>
> The Palimpsest, like the Choir, is genuinely helpful. Every
> layer it shows is real. It simply doesn't show all of them —
> and what it *does* show is shaped by what the reader has
> already read. Two readers Glimpsing the same surface in
> sequence see different under-texts. Both are real. Neither is
> the whole. The grammar: *the page reads its reader.*

The first **bible-amendment-class** addition to formalise
Palimpsest unreliability as a symmetric design constraint, not an
asymmetry.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

> names for *the unsent.*

**Thin.** **No collected names for the Choir's most central verb.**
The bible uses *take*, *integrate*, *compost*, *digest*
interchangeably. Other-faction grammar for this single act has
not been gathered.

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar):

> - **Pale Curation:** *un-cataloguing.* The only intake form the
>   Curation cannot perform; reflexive and passive together — a
>   record that un-catalogues itself in the eating. Halen filed
>   one card *for* an un-cataloguing; the catalogue rejected it
>   on the grounds that the card was *itself the cataloguing of
>   an un-cataloguing.* The Director closed it with
>   *under-grammar.*
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *a price not paid.* No clause clears.
>   No first-party remains to be paid. Veshen has seen the form
>   three times; none signed.
> - **Brine Communion:** *the fresh-tide.* The salt's
>   *un-sea-ing.*
> - **Thermoclaves:** *cold-feeding.* The eighth (the honest
>   fire, 1101) refuses to *count* a cold-feeding; the count
>   keeps no record of it.
> - **Glassblown Remnant:** *unmaking-with-no-breath.* A loan
>   from the Curation form. There is no native Glassblown
>   grammar for the act.
> - **Palimpsest:** *the page lost.* Not retired; not flat.
>   *Lost.*
> - **The Choir's own word:** *taking.* No suffix. *We took. We
>   are taking. We will take.* The collective does not regard
>   the verb as needing adornment.

Seven grammars; six ethical valences; one operation. The Choir's
verb is shortest; the Palimpsest's most defeated; the Glassblown's
the only one that is a *loan.*

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

> *Bible 208–210:* "they are not at war."

**Thin.** **The mechanical edge case at the seam.** 1101's
*unread page* gave a slow time-rate (millennium-scale). The Choir
digests at *forty-seasons-scale.* What happens to the Palimpsest's
layers under digestion?

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection after 1101's "the
unread page", "Forced flattening"):

> A Choir-digested surface flattens at the rate of digestion, not
> the rate of disuse. Forty seasons, not a millennium. To a
> Palimpsest-attuned reader, the surface *equalises live;* under-
> text and over-text approach pressure-equality at a speed the
> eye can track. Glimpse over a digesting surface returns *both
> layers thinning together,* legible briefly, then unreadable.
>
> Palimpsest grammar: *the page hurried.* Choir grammar: *the
> soil arrived ahead of patience.* Neither faction disputes the
> other account.
>
> A reader who Glimpses a digesting surface in its last week of
> legibility may **read the layers' final equalisation.** This is,
> by both grammars, real — the reader has read what the layers
> said as they merged. No prior or later reading captures it. The
> bible's first rule for a single fact both factions accept:
> forced flattening is legible exactly once, at the seam.
>
> A reading taken at the seam is **bilingual** — it returns in
> both grammars simultaneously, in the open tense, in the
> collective pronoun. The reader understands without being able
> to choose which faction's account to file. Curation surveyors
> who take seam-readings file them on a separate card-stock — the
> *un-grammared card,* introduced by Halen and used by no other
> surveyor.

The corpus's first **mechanical** seam — a phenomenon both
grammars admit by name, one reading per surface.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

> 1101 introduced the **Re-pressed** (Palimpsest middle); 1001
> the **Long Throat** (Choir middle).

**Thin.** **A being who is both.** Bible 244 says both factions
are impossible to *fully maximise* simultaneously. At the
*middle-state* level, can a Long Throat also be Re-pressed?

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The doubled mouth"):

> A reader who has stabilised as Re-pressed and is then absorbed
> by the Choir does not lose either condition. The Choir, which
> respects the cultural-philosophical voice a person came in
> with, also respects the *layers* a person came in with. A Long
> Throat who was Re-pressed before joining is **a doubled
> mouth** — speaking in collective Choir grammar, in the layered
> tense, both at once.
>
> Doubled mouths are the rarest beings in the corpus.
> ROTCHOIR_VOICES does not name one. They occur when conditions
> overlap, not when either faction seeks them out. The Choir does
> not consider the absorption a Communion. The Palimpsest does
> not consider it a Transcription. Both consider the doubled
> mouth *correct as it is* — neither faction's end-state, both
> factions' grammar.
>
> A doubled mouth's voice carries collective pronoun + layered
> tense. *We were lifting the cup. We are lifting the cup. We
> will have lifted the cup.* Intelligible to readers of either
> tradition; readers of neither hear ordinary speech said three
> times.

The first being in the corpus that occupies *both* central-axis
factions without contradiction. A *thing that happens* when
conditions overlap.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Thin.** **The combined grammar.** Doubled mouths must speak in
both registers; no rule for combining them.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "Doubled-mouth
grammar"):

> Combinations are not arithmetically additive. *We are lifting*
> (Choir indicative, present) becomes, doubled, *we have always
> been about to lift, and we are lifting, and the lift is a thing
> we did before you arrived.* The Palimpsest considers this one
> sentence, open form. The Choir considers it the collective
> speaking with internal disagreement *that does not require
> resolution.*
>
> A doubled-mouth bridge-clause (0845) must translate the *we* —
> typically by selecting one Long Throat as speaker-of-record
> while footnoting the rest. Veshen has signed exactly one such
> clause, in a transaction the Concord refers to as *the
> difficult clause.* The books mark it *signed under protest by
> parties not all listed.*

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Thin.** **A doubled mouth's daily life.** 1101 gave the
Re-pressed morning; the doubled mouth's morning lives at the seam.

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar, "The doubled morning"):

> A doubled mouth wakes in collective grammar into the layered
> tense. The first quarter-hour is *the layered gap* — the body
> sits up; the under-text body rises in a different bed. The
> Re-pressed second-shadow (1101) becomes a **second-collective:**
> a small choir of under-text mouths, faintly visible, dispersing
> as the gap closes.
>
> Eating: the over-text mouth shares with Choir-fellows through
> the network; the under-text mouth tastes a meal eaten in the
> reader's twentieth year. A Choir-fellow drops a tendril into
> the eater's hand and the network nourishes both layers.
>
> The Choir has not, in five hundred years of records, named a
> doubled mouth aloud. The reluctance is *deference to the
> layered tense,* which the Choir admits it cannot speak.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Glassblown round 3 (the kept paste)

R1 (2150) gave the fourth verb mood and the unfiring ritual; R2
(2324) gave Tulen Niemensa-Sopellen, the deferred shape, the
unfired letter, and *folded back.* R3 picks up the unfired
register at scale.

**Etymology of *kept*.** The Glassblown verb meaning *deferred
without forfeit.* Refused paste is **kept** — not destroyed, not
committed, held in a state where the next paste-mother can refuse
it again. Reflexive: paste *keeps itself* in the keeping; the
Glassblown only attend.

**The Six-Almost.** The longest kept paste on record. Six paste-
batches each begun by a different Glassblown across two centuries;
each refused into the same vessel-of-keeping. The vessel itself
has not been fired. The paste is *one paste* — the six refusals
fold each batch back into the same matter — and yet it
*remembers* each refusal. A Glimpse-attuned reader has reported
six layers of under-text, one per refusal. The Curation has, by
protocol, not intaken it. The Concord's priorless cabinet (1101)
holds a clause about it never signed.

Tulen is the current keeper. She has refused into the Six-Almost
twice — refusals five and six. She does not say what shape. The
grammar requires only that the keeper *attend;* what shape was
refused is not in the grammar. Refusal seven is, by tradition,
performed by Tulen's successor — a paste-mother not yet identified.

The Six-Almost is, by Glassblown grammar, **a letter** (per 2324)
addressed *to the next paste-mother who will refuse into it.* Six
refusals, six addressees who became refusers, two centuries, never
sealed. The corpus's first **four-grammar** Glassblown object:
*kept;* Curation's *un-cataloguing;* Concord's *priorless clause;*
Palimpsest's *under-text.*

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a) Bible 244 vs. the doubled mouth.** Bible 244 (*"Both factions
should be impossible to fully maximise simultaneously"*) is
consistent with the doubled mouth only if the bible distinguishes
**maximisation** (apotheosis: Communion / Transcription) from
**middle-state crossing** (Long Throat + Re-pressed). The current
text does not.

**Recommend appended sentence to bible 244:**

> *Middle-state crossings (a Long Throat who is also Re-pressed —
> the doubled mouth) are not maximisations and are not foreclosed
> by this rule. They are the corpus's only stable cross-axis
> beings; the impossibility is in the apotheoses, not the middles.*

Fourth proposed bible amendment (after 0845's 208–209, 1001's 39 +
`OldestTruth`, 1101's 123).

**(b) ROTCHOIR_VOICES — five named voices, none doubled.** The
absence is load-bearing: every Dim above introduces the doubled
mouth as the natural late-game crossing point and the named-NPC
roster has none.

**Recommend a content task:** a sixth Choir voice — a doubled
mouth, far down in the First Root. Naming: a word for *fungus* in
a tense-layering language (evidential-rich, or grammaticalised
aspect) would land the doubled-tense register without overclaiming.
First NPC at the seam.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

**Title:** *Pale Curation field card. Surveyor Halen, fourth week.
Subject: a Glassblown vessel of keeping. Filed under
under-grammar.*

```
[Standard Curation intake card. Halen's hand. The CATALOGUE-
 DECISION box is left blank — the Curation form for "intake
 declined." The NOTES section is unusually long.]

  CATALOGUED BY: Surveyor Halen, fourth week
  ITEM:          Vessel-of-keeping. Unfired. Glassblown.
                 Paste interior, six-layered (Glimpse-confirmed
                 by attached attuned contractor).
  PROVENANCE:    Held by Tulen Niemensa-Sopellen, paste-
                 mother. She does not consent to intake; she
                 does not refuse intake. Glassblown grammar
                 permits neither.

  CATALOGUE DECISION: [blank]

  NOTES: I have written this card three weeks running and
         declined to file. The vessel does not move. The
         paste does not set. I asked whether a piecewise
         intake would serve — a scraping of one layer for
         record. Tulen folded back. The Director, on review,
         returned my draft with a single word: under-grammar.

         I record here what I am not authorised to file:

           A Glimpse over the paste returns six under-texts,
           one per refusal. The seventh refusal — the one
           Tulen almost performed in my second week and did
           not — is not yet under-text. It is, in our
           contractor's reading, the unwritten layer. The
           vessel holds six refusals and one refusal-not-
           performed, and the keeping holds all seven.

           If the seventh is performed, it becomes a sixth
           under-text and a seventh person becomes the
           addressee. If not performed long enough, the
           keeping itself flattens — the six layers equalise —
           and the Six-Almost becomes the Six-Settled, no
           longer a correspondence, only a piece of paste.

         The Curation cannot intake this object because the
         object is in a tense the Curation's catalogue has
         no column for. The vessel is being-and-was-and-will-
         have-been-kept. Our card has only one date-line.

         I will continue not filing. — H.
```

The bombshell is parenthetical: **the unwritten layer.** A
Palimpsest grammar-form not yet named in the bible. A refusal-not-
performed is not under-text — it is a *future-conditional layer
the Palimpsest has admitted into its grammar.* The layered tense's
third (future) tense generalised from spoken to *written* form —
the future tense, recorded in advance of its writing.

A second, smaller bombshell: Halen has, by writing on a card she
will not file, committed a *bridge-clause* a Glassblown would call
*folded back.* The card is structurally an unfired letter. A
Glassblown object in Curation grammar refused by Glassblown
grammar. The corpus's second four-grammar object (after the Six-
Almost it describes) and the first written *by* the Curation
rather than *to* it.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"A Choir-digested surface flattens at the rate of digestion,
> not the rate of disuse. Forty seasons, not a millennium."*

No archaisms; no apostrophe-fantasy names; no Aedra, Daedra,
Towers, CHIM, Mantling, Eight; no *verily, hath, lo, ere.*
Operational present + comparative rate-claim. A TES rewrite would
read: *"And lo, the Page yieldeth swifter beneath the Hand of the
Eater than beneath the long-slow weathering of years uncounted;
in forty seasons doth the Eater accomplish what a thousand years
of stillness alone might have wrought."* Mine has none of
*yieldeth, doth, beneath the Hand, accomplish, wrought.* Confirmed.
