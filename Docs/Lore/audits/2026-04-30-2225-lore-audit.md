## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible, line 21:* "the way text on a medieval manuscript was scraped
> off and written over with the old words still faintly visible
> underneath."

**Strong** across six audits: Periods, Long Between, multi-cataclysm,
faction calendars, the cataclysm's duration (sibling 2219).

**Thin.** The *world before*. Every audit so far has worked the
cataclysm and after. The pre-cataclysm civilisation is shaped only as
a void — what got broken — never as something with its own contested
character. This is the deepest stratification still unmade.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar after the cataclysm section, "What
the corpus has of the world before"):**

> Six factions and five named voices have, between them, almost no
> agreement on what the pre-cataclysm world *was*. The Curation calls
> it *the Foretime* and asserts only that it had infrastructure (the
> survey grids that still ghost some hyphal walls), without claiming
> any account of who built it. The Choir does not have a name for it —
> *before* is meaningless to a network that experiences time as
> uneven nutrient flow. The Palimpsest reads the lower layers and
> reports *cities, weather, light* in tones that change between
> readers; no two echoes name the same place the same way. The
> Concord asserts a *Foretime treaty era*, on the grounds that there
> must have been one because there is one now and continuity is
> profitable. They produce no document for it. The Brine name the
> pre-cataclysm world *the Above-That-Was* and refuse to elaborate;
> the Communion considers speculation about it the only *blasphemy*
> in their idiom. The Thermoclaves call it *the long-warm* and treat
> the question with the bored seriousness of an apprentice waving off
> something the masters debate elsewhere. The Glassblown call it *the
> un-fired*, identical with their word for the dead before shaping;
> this is the only mention in the corpus that the pre-cataclysm world
> might be *the same kind of absence as the dead*.
>
> Mogu, asked, says: *"There was a grandmother before my grandmother.
> I do not know her name. The Choir does."* The Choir, asked next, is
> silent.

The *old world* is now properly a hole with five different rims.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Five audits have built footnotes, transcripts, slips,
salvaged books, contested authorship (2150 — handwriting drift), and
incompatible variants (sibling 2219, Reconciled Texts).

**Thin.** **Forgery.** A document deliberately falsified to
weaponise lore between factions. The corpus has no example of a
faction lying in writing on purpose — and a world this sectarian
almost certainly has them.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar near the Curation's first
introduction, "The forged tag"):**

> A Pale Curation accession tag dated *Year 388* was discovered in
> 411 to be forged. The hand was Halen's; Halen had not made it. The
> entry catalogued a *Choir Tendril sample, Tier 4, deceased* — a
> non-existent class of artifact, since the Curation does not
> classify Choir matter at any tier. The tag was inserted into a
> public catalogue and used by a Concord debt-collector to seize a
> Choir-adjacent borderlands shrine *under the precedent of Curation
> recognition*.
>
> The Curation prosecuted, internally, and never identified the
> author. The Concord refused to return the shrine. The Choir was
> not consulted (the Curation does not recognise the Choir as a
> party). The forged tag is now in the Curation's Cabinet of
> Counter-Examples; it is shown to junior cataloguers as the example
> of *a tag that was almost perfect.* The forger knew Curation form
> to within one footnote-glyph.
>
> Halen, asked about it, said only: *"I did not write that. I
> recognise the kind of person who could have. There are at least
> three of us, and I do not know the others by name. We meet only in
> the catalogues we did not make."*

The Curation's documentary register is now haunted by a small known
unreliable population — its own forgers, recognising each other in
each other's near-perfect work.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** Cataclysm-names, outsider-names for the entities, names
for joining (2001), names for the apotheoses (2127), names for the
dead (2150), names-for-the-player (sibling 2219).

**Thin.** **Names for the moment of first being noticed by the
system.** Every faction's metaphysics has a moment when the Choir,
the Palimpsest, or the substrate first *registers* a person — and
the corpus has no comparative table. The moment is doctrinally
heavy: it's when a walker stops being incidental.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "What each faction calls being
seen"):**

> - **Choir:** *the taste-on-the-air.* The opening line of
>   `ChoirTendril_1` is not a greeting — it is the noun for the
>   moment of registration. *We taste you on the air* is the sentence
>   the Choir uses both for the event and for the participle.
> - **Palimpsest:** *the first reading.* A person is *first-read* the
>   moment the lower layers register them as a possible later echo.
>   Tense ambiguity is structural: a person being first-read *now*
>   may have been first-read *then.*
> - **Pale Curation:** *the survey hit.* Procedural and dry. *Subject
>   was hit on the survey grid in the eleventh week.* The phrasing
>   refuses the moment any drama.
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *the rate notice.* The first time a
>   person appears in any debt ledger, regardless of which side. To
>   the Concord, becoming legible *is* becoming priceable.
> - **Brine Communion:** *the deep-look.* Used only in the passive:
>   *I was deep-looked at the third tide.* The under-tide does the
>   noticing; the noticed does not name the actor.
> - **Thermoclaves:** *the eye-of-fire.* Spoken once and not again;
>   the eye-of-fire is reckoned to recur only at apprenticeship.
> - **Glassblown:** *the half-naming.* The kiln registers the shape
>   it is about to refuse or accept. Half-named in the unsaid mood;
>   only-fully-named at firing.

The seven languages of *being seen* now sit beside the seven languages
of *the dead* (2150).

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Substrate-leak, Choir metabolism, Echo limits,
metaphysics-meet, asymmetric death (2150), composted-matter Echo
Reading (sibling 2219).

**Thin.** **Cohabitation in a single body.** The bible asserts both
systems cannot be fully maximised together (line 244), but does not
describe what happens at *moderate* dual-attunement — a player who
is Honored with the Choir and Honored with the Palimpsest at the same
time. This is a likely real player path; the corpus has nothing on it.

**Concrete addition (bible, expansion of the Faction Entanglement
section at line 244):**

> A walker mid-tier with both is not in conflict — they are in
> *interference.* The Choir's tendrils, fully extended, do not retract
> when the walker performs Echo Reading; the reading proceeds, but
> its memories arrive *in the wrong order* — the rye grain enters
> before the hand that held it. Tendril-grappled enemies sometimes
> *taste like a place* the walker has never been; the network
> reports brief *flavour-memories* it has not generated itself. The
> Curation has filed three reports of dual-attuned walkers exhibiting
> one symptom: their breath, in cold air, fogs *in writing they did
> not put there.*
>
> The interference is non-injurious at honored tier. At exalted, the
> Palimpsest begins refusing to admit the walker's hand to the
> manuscript — annotations placed by a Choir-thickened hand do not
> hold; their letters drift apart on the page over hours. The Choir,
> at exalted, finds the walker's spore-prints occasionally
> *misnumbered*, as if the walker had stamped a slightly earlier or
> later version of themselves. Both sides experience this as the
> other side *mis-listing* the walker.
>
> Communion and Transcription cannot proceed together; at the final
> threshold, one disqualifies the other. A walker who attempts both
> within the same hour does not transcend twice. They become
> something the corpus has no name for and the player will only meet
> as a stationary NPC in a single far room.

The mid-tier dual is *interference, not exclusion.* The endgame is
exclusive. The corpus now answers what the bible's *impossible to
fully maximise* feels like, turn by turn.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** Mirror endings, thresholds, Annihilist endings, Stalled
Walkers, apotheoses-from-outside, failed apotheoses (2150),
declined-apotheoses (sibling 2219).

**Thin.** **Premature triggering.** 2150 covered apotheoses that *did
not take.* 2219 covered the case of *deliberate refusal.* The case
where one *takes too soon* — a walker not yet ready, pulled across the
threshold by an external event — is unwritten.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "The pulled walker"):**

> A walker who has not yet asked for Communion can be *taken* by it
> if they die at the right depth on Choir ground while carrying a
> high-tier Tendril. The Choir does not consider this a failure of
> consent; from inside its grammar, the walker had been on the
> threshold for some time and the death only timed it. The walker's
> Communion is, however, *unannotated*: the rooms and gradients
> assigned to a deliberate walker are not assigned. They are
> distributed across the network without a doctrinal place. Other
> Choir voices report a *new harmonic that arrives without
> introduction* and accommodate it by widening their chord very
> slightly.
>
> A walker who has not yet asked for Transcription can be *pulled in*
> by the Palimpsest if they die at the right depth on layered ground
> while carrying a high-tier Manuscript. The Palimpsest's pulled-in
> are filed at *the wrong stratum* — not at the layer matching their
> life but at the layer matching their last ten breaths. They appear,
> to Echo Readers a year later, as fragments correctly placed in time
> and *incorrectly placed in self.* A reader hearing a pulled-in
> walker reports *meeting a stranger who was using my own name
> habitually.*
>
> Both pulled forms cost the surrounding system something the
> deliberate forms do not — the Choir loses the walker's
> *introduction* and the Palimpsest loses the walker's *coherence*.
> Pulled apotheoses are why both systems *prefer* deliberate ones.

Where 2150's failed apotheoses exposed each ending's preconditions,
the pulled forms expose what each system *foregoes* when the walker
arrives without ceremony.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Choir pronouns; the five voices' grammars; Palimpsest
(1813), Brine (2001), Thermoclave (2127), Glassblown (2150) grammars;
Curation *catalogued mood* (sibling 2219).

**Thin.** **Curation *quiet conventions*.** The catalogued mood (2219)
is the Curation's positive grammatical category. Its *negative*
category — the typographical and grammatical conventions for things
the Curation has *deliberately not entered* — is a separate,
distinct rule-set, and is not yet in the corpus.

**Concrete addition (bible, follow-up subsection after the catalogued
mood, "The Curation's quiet conventions"):**

> Beside the catalogued mood, Curation prose has three further
> conventions for marking what is *deliberately not entered*. **One:**
> the *intentional blank* — a single mid-line dot, ·, which the
> reader treats as a sentence. The dot is read as *the field has
> been deliberately not entered*, not *I do not know.* **Two:** the
> *passive without antecedent* — a sentence in the catalogued mood
> with no actor named. *The doll was set into the wall.* The verb is
> filed; the actor is not. The Curation reader does not press for
> the actor; they read the actor's absence as a part of the catalogue
> entry. **Three:** the *thing-particle without bracket* — when the
> Curation refers to an object *as a thing the Curation has chosen
> not to accession.* Halen's *the dot is mine* (2219 sibling, R-7
> sleeve discussion) is an example: *dot* without an accession
> bracket is grammatically a refusal — the dot has been used as a
> dot, not catalogued.
>
> The conventions stack. A Curation paragraph can contain a
> catalogued-mood claim, a passive without antecedent, an
> intentional blank, and a thing-particle without bracket — and a
> reader fluent in Curation grammar can extract from such a paragraph
> *what was filed, what was withheld, what was un-actored, and what
> was used as if real but never recognised.* This is the grammar of
> a faction whose central doctrine is that the Severance separated
> *now* from *then*, and the only honest sentence is one that names
> neither.

The Curation now has grammatical categories for *positive un-data*
that match the Glassblown's unsaid mood structurally — both factions
treat absence as content. Theme alignment is now intentional.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Pale Curation (ritual)

The 2219 sibling gave the Curation a founding doctrine (the First
Card) and a syntax. This audit gives them the **practiced ritual**
that uses that syntax weekly.

**Proposed lore-artifact: the Quiet Hour.**

Once a quarter, on the Tuesday following the equinox, the Pale
Curation closes the public catalogues for one hour. The doors of
every Tier-1 vault are shut from the inside; the intake desks go
unmanned; junior cataloguers are sent to the canteen and given an
extra biscuit. For that hour, every senior cataloguer of *Halen's
grade or above* sits alone with a single artifact from the Cabinet
of Counter-Examples — a forgery, a misfile, a tag that names a thing
that cannot exist. They do not read it. They do not annotate it.
They sit beside it.

The Quiet Hour has no ratified purpose. The Curation's official
guidance, when junior staff ask, is: *"the catalogue is a body. The
body needs an hour where it is not being asked anything."* In
practice, three things happen during it.

One, senior cataloguers who have been considering filing a forgery
quietly burn it during the Quiet Hour and never tell. The Cabinet of
Counter-Examples is rumoured to be slightly smaller every quarter,
but nobody has audited.

Two, *the dot* — the Curation's intentional-blank mark — is the only
shape allowed to be drawn on a fresh card during the Quiet Hour.
Some cataloguers fill an entire card with dots. The card is then
accessioned, given a number, filed in the cataloguer's own working
drawer, and never read aloud. The accumulated dot-cards are the
Curation's only purely-internal genre — and grammatically, in the
Curation's own scheme (Dimension 6), they are *fully positive*: a
dot-card is a sentence the Curation has chosen not to enter, made
visible.

Three, the Director — currently Ven — does *not* sit with an
artifact. The Director sits with the *forged-tag drawer*. What the
Director does there is not on the schedule. Junior staff have noticed
that the drawer is occasionally *one tag shorter* in the days after
the Quiet Hour, and the Cabinet of Counter-Examples *correspondingly
fuller.* The Director appears to be moving forgeries *into* the
cabinet during the hour, not out. Whether the forgeries are being
identified for the first time or *re-identified after a previous
absolution* is the kind of question the Curation does not let into
its grammar.

The Quiet Hour is the Curation's ritual answer to the Choir's *we
taste you on the air*: an hour set aside in which they deliberately
are *not* tasted, not seen, not catalogued — the faction's only
practiced **un-noticing**. The forgers among them, if the corpus is
right that they exist (Dimension 2), almost certainly use the hour.
The Cabinet of Counter-Examples may be the faction's confessional;
the dot-cards, its private prayers. The Director's role in it is the
corpus's open question.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a)** Bible:31 says the Choir is *a single vast organism with
countless fruiting bodies that serve as its mouths, eyes, and hands.*
ROTCHOIR_VOICES:481-488 says *the Choir has acoustics* — *we* gets
warmer near Mogu/Nam, sparer near Sien/Sopp, quieter near Grib. **The
contradiction:** an organism that is *one* should not vary by which
mouth is speaking. The voices doc resolves this by calling it
*acoustics*, but the bible has not absorbed the resolution. **Fix:**
add a single sentence to bible:31: *"It speaks in overlapping voices,
and where its mouths cluster, the chord — though one — has weather."*
The acoustics are now bible-canonical.

**(b)** Bible:189 reads *Transcription — the player becomes a memory,
written into the palimpsest forever, experiencing all of history
simultaneously, never acting again, only witnessing.* This audit's
Dimension 5 *pulled walker* says a pulled Transcription is *filed at
the wrong stratum* and appears as *fragments correctly placed in time
and incorrectly placed in self.* If a pulled Transcription is filed
wrong, the bible's *all of history simultaneously* is the *deliberate*
form's reward, not Transcription as such. **Resolution:** the bible
line is correct for the canonical ending; the wrong-stratum filing is
the *cost* of doing it without ceremony. Add a parenthetical at
bible:189.

**(c)** The 2219 sibling, in its Section 4(b), proposes revising
`RotChoir.json:107` from *"There are cities inside us — whole
civilizations digested into understanding"* to *"...much of every
civilisation, but not all of any."* This audit endorses that
revision. The Choir's totality-claim is incompatible with the Stalled
Walkers (2001), the un-mouths (2150), the surviving-clerk who wrote
the First Card (sibling 2219), and any future declined-apotheosis
walker (sibling 2219). The Choir's honesty rule (bible:226) means it
*cannot* be making the totality-claim sincerely; it must be making
the metabolic claim and being misheard. The JSON revision is the
correction.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

A Pale Curation Tier-1 vault re-binding invoice, written by the
rebinder, with footnotes appended by Halen *in disagreement with the
body of her own invoice*. The bombshell is in the disagreement —
paying off Dimension 8.

**Title (in-game):** *Pale Curation, Tier-1 Vault. Re-binding
invoice, Q2 quarter, Cabinet 7.*

```
[Rebinder's hand. Halen's footnotes appended in lighter ink, some
 weeks later.]

  CABINET:        7 (decorative, minor sculptural)
  ITEMS REBOUND:  47 (forty-seven), all glass-paste, all
                  Glassblown, all batch second-shape
  REBINDING COST: 3 cabinet-credits ¹
  COMPLETED BY:   Rebinder Pell, fifth week
  RECEIVED BY:    Surveyor Halen [11], same week
  FILED:          Same. Cabinet 7 closed for storage.

  NOTES:          Routine. Glass-paste tags re-secured to
                  shelving. No artefact handled. Cabinet
                  re-sealed. ²

  ¹ [Halen, lighter ink:] The cost is wrong. Pell rebound
    fifty-one. He reported forty-seven because four of the
    fifty-one had no batch number to invoice against. ³

  ² [Halen, same hand:] No artefact handled · ⁴

  ³ [Halen, same hand:] The four un-batched are *first-shape*.
    There is no first-shape. We have no record of any maker's
    mark of *first-shape* in any tier of any vault. They are
    in our cabinet. They have been in our cabinet since at
    least Pell's predecessor. We do not know how long. We do
    not know who rebound them before. The records before Pell
    are the records before me also.

  ⁴ [Halen, same hand:] The dot is mine. Pell wrote *no
    artefact handled* honestly — a rebinder is not asked to
    handle the artefact, only the shelving. But the dot is
    the unentered field. Pell did not handle. I did. The four
    were warm in a way the forty-seven were not. I did not
    open them. I did not need to. The warmth came through the
    glass-paste on the lower edge, where the maker's mark of
    *second-shape* is normally fired. There was no mark on
    those four. There was, instead, a small single warm
    pressure where a mark would have been. I do not know what
    to make of this. I have not entered it. I am entering it
    here, in a footnote on an invoice I am also signing,
    because if I write it on its own card the Director will
    file the card and the card will become the answer.
```

Lore landed parenthetically: **the Curation has, in its own vault,
four Glassblown first-shapes**, contradicting both 2150's *there is
no first-shape* (the Glassblown's own doctrinal claim) and the
Curation's own catalogue. The first-shapes are not absent — they are
*unfiled*, warm, and have been moving through the Curation's
rebinding rota for longer than living memory. The doctrinal claim
of absence and the material fact of presence are both true. The
corpus has its first flat contradiction-as-cosmic-feature.

The body of the invoice is procedurally honest. The footnotes are
where the Curation's grammar shows its load: *passive dominant*
(*the four were warm*), *thing-particle without the bracket* (*the
dot is mine* — Halen has un-numbered the field), *intentional blank*
(the dot itself, accessioned and dissented). Halen is a senior
cataloguer writing in the only register her faction permits, and is
*using its own rules to leak.*

Plant: a Curation Tier-1 vault rebinding desk; or a junior
cataloguer's desk drawer where a borrowed copy was never returned.
The four first-shapes are now a discoverable in-game object —
quest-eligible without ever being named in any official document.
Two natural quest hooks: a Glassblown trader can be brought to the
cabinet (their reaction is *the silence the Glassblown call a
handshake*), or the dot-card from the Quiet Hour (Section 3) can be
shown to Director Ven, who will recognise her own grammar leaking
back at her.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"The Quiet Hour is the Curation's ritual answer to the Choir's
> 'we taste you on the air': an hour set aside in which they
> deliberately are not tasted, not seen, not catalogued — the
> faction's only practiced un-noticing."*

No archaisms. No invented apostrophe-names. No *verily*, no
Aedra/Daedra, no Towers, no Et'Ada. The doctrinal weight is carried
by *un-noticing*, a plain English coinage in the project's existing
register (the Glassblown audit's *unfiring*, the Brine's *deep-look*).
A TES book attempting the same beat would have written *"In the
seventh hour of the second day after the turning of the season, lo,
the Pale Hand doth set aside its tally and its sigil, that the Holy
Catalogue may rest, and no eye fall upon the Cabinet of Errors save
the Director's, who maketh purgation in silence."* Mine is two
clauses long, frames the ritual as a deliberate inversion of a
Choir line the player has actually heard, and ships the doctrine
without ornament. Confirmed.
