## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible, line 121:* "A road that leads to a city destroyed four
> hundred years ago — but if you walk it at the right time, you arrive."

**Strong.** Four prior audits stratified *what happened* (multi-cataclysm
hypothesis, Periods, Long Between, the un-named first-shape).

**Thin.** *When* it happened. The bible asserts *four hundred years* —
a single round number with no in-world citation. Each faction in fact
counts differently; the four-hundred figure has no ratifier.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar after the cataclysm section, "How
the factions count"):**

> The corpus's *four hundred years* is the Curation's working figure,
> from the seal-date of their Tier-1 vault. The Curation counts in
> **catalogue years**. The Brine count in **deep-tides** of ~seven
> years; they place the cataclysm at the 51st. The Thermoclaves count
> in **unbroken tens** — tenth-fire chains; their figure is *four chains
> ago*, each of indeterminate length. The Concord counts in
> **trade-cycles** and reads the cataclysm as 23 past, contested by
> their own archivists. The Choir does not count. The Palimpsest counts
> *backward from the next overwrite*, unusable for anyone who does not
> share their sense that the next is imminent.
>
> A Curation card dated *Year 412* and a Brine logbook dated *the 51st
> deep-tide, third turn* may or may not refer to the same week. Each
> calendar drifts in the way that flatters its founding doctrine.

The four-hundred number is now a faction's reading, not the bible's claim.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Anthology framing, footnotes, transcripts, slips, salvaged
book.

**Thin.** Every artifact so far is *what its author meant to write.*
The corpus has no document where author and text disagree — a piece
written by a hand that was guided, or completed by another after death.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "Authorship, contested"):**

> A Curation field-card by Surveyor Halen, written in the Old Hyphae
> crossing, was flagged by Director Ven for *handwriting drift* — the
> loops at midpoint thicken in a way Halen's earlier cards do not. A
> second reviewer (a Palimpsest-attuned contractor, off-record) examined
> the card and reported that *two hands had been writing simultaneously,
> in the same ink, neither aware of the other.* The card catalogues a
> wall. The wall is the second hand. The Curation has filed it as
> *Authorship: Disputed (object-assist)* and refuses to remove it.
>
> The Choir reads this as proof walls can write when given a patient
> enough hand. The Palimpsest reads it as proof the wall remembered
> being written about and reached forward. The Curation finds both
> readings useless.

Every Curation card is now in question.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

substrate, joining; outsider names for the apotheoses (2127).

**Thin.** **Names for the dead.** Every culture in the world generates
this vocabulary; the corpus has no comparative table.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar after the comparison table at line 205):**

> - **Choir:** *the roots.* The dead rejoin the network. There is no
>   Choir word for *corpse* distinct from *root*.
> - **Palimpsest:** *the re-readings.* The dead are layers, accessible
>   to Echo Reading. The plural is mandatory; the singular is ungrammatical.
> - **Pale Curation:** *holdings.* The dead are filed; they have
>   accession numbers; they are theoretically retrievable. (In practice

> - **Glassblown Remnant:** *the unfired* (those lost in the Burning
>   before they could be shaped) and, separately, *the second-cooled*
>   (those who reached firing and then died). Markers are made only for
>   the unfired; the second-cooled receive only the workshop's
>   continued work.

**Thin.** **Failed apotheoses.** Botched versions are not addressed.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "When the apotheoses fail"):**

> **A Communion that does not take.** The walker enters the chemical-
> gradient room and stands. The Choir does not accept them — for reasons
> it does not give and may not itself know. The walker emerges after
> ~three days with the node *muted*: passive sensing dead, healing dead,
> the Tendril cold in the hand. They are known as *the un-mouths*. Some
> return to Choir ground compulsively; the Choir tolerates them and does
> not feed them. The Curation has noted un-mouths weigh slightly less

> less of.
>

> player go thin and *stay thin* — neither sentence nor body. They
> remain in the room, somewhat present, somewhat readable. They can be
> spoken to and answer in voices that do not belong to them — the room's
> previous occupants, in succession. The Palimpsest may not consider

> incompletely transcribed* — a marker rather than a sentence. They
> cannot be moved.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Choir pronouns; the five voices' grammars; Palimpsest (1813),
Brine (2001), Thermoclave (2127) grammars.

**Thin.** **Glassblown grammar.** The silence-faction's syntax is
unwritten. They are not silent because they have nothing to say — they
are silent because their grammar treats *what was not said* as a
positive grammatical category.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "Glassblown Grammar"):**

> Glassblown grammar has a fourth verb mood alongside indicative,
> imperative, and interrogative: the **unsaid mood**. It takes a verb
> and converts it into an action that was not performed, a sentence not
> finished, or a question not asked — and *marks this as positive
> content* of the sentence. Outsiders feel they missed half of every
> sentence; in fact every half is present.
>
> The mood is formed by suffixing the verb with *-han* (from *handle*,
> the moment a glass-shape is released from the maker's tongs and is,
> briefly, no one's). Examples:
>
> - *I went to the well.* — indicative.
> - *I went-han to the well.* — unsaid. The speaker considered and did
>   not. The going is the content; the refusal is the punctuation.
> - *Did she ask-han the question?* — interrogative-unsaid compound;
>   used to ask whether another performed an *intentional* silence.
>
> The unsaid mood does not require justification. To press for
> clarification is impolite. The reader *lets the unsaid stand as said.*
>
> Field 5 of the grief-marker (*WHAT WAS NOT SAID*) is the ritualised
> written form. The blanks elsewhere are unsaid-mood entries, recorded
> by their refusal to be entered.

The 2127 grief-marker is now syntactically explained.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Glassblown Remnant

The Glassblown have one shipped artifact (the 2127 grief-marker) and
the new grammar above. They have no ritual yet.

**Proposed lore-artifact: the Unfiring (a daily ritual).**

Every Glassblown wakes before sunrise and performs the *unfiring*. They
sit at their workshop door, facing whichever direction the previous
day's wind came from, and name the absences from the previous day in
the unsaid mood — *I did-han see Renne. I finished-han the third
marker. I ate-han with my second-shape.* The naming is private; if
overheard, the listener gives the speaker a half-turn of distance. No
formula, no closing word. It ends when the speaker can think of no more
absences. Some unfirings are three breaths; some take half the morning.

A Glassblown who skips the unfiring for three days running is *over-
fired* — too much of yesterday carried into today, the kiln of the
self running too hot — and their work is suspended. To return, they
must unfire in front of a single witness. The witness does not respond,
does not nod, does not breathe in any pattern that could be read as
encouragement. The witness *receives the unsaid*. After, they leave
without speaking. The unsaid has been let stand as said.

The Pale Curation has learned not to schedule appointments with
Glassblown traders before sunrise. A surveyor at a Glassblown camp
pre-dawn reports the camp full of a particular quiet — not absence of
sound, but the *presence of unsounds*.

The unfiring is the inverse of the Thermoclave's fire-keeping (2127).
Where the Thermoclave begins each day *holding* fire, the Glassblown
begins each day naming what was *not* held. Both are organised around
what was not done. Thermoclaves call this failure (*-cold*); Glassblown
call this content (*-han*). The two factions are doctrinal mirrors and
almost certainly related — the Burning that made the Glassblown was
tended, in some sense, by the Thermoclaves' ancestors. The Glassblown
have not forgiven this; the form their unforgiveness takes is *not
trading with Thermoclaves before the unfiring has completed*.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a)** `ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md`:484 claims the Choir's voice has acoustics
— *we* gets warmer near Mogu/Nam, sparer near Sien/Sopp, quieter near
Grib. The shipped JSON's `ChoirTendril_1` voice is fixed. **Fix:** either
downgrade the .md (acoustics belong to the *named* NPCs, not the
generic tendril) or add ChoirTendril variants whose voice shifts by
named-NPC proximity. Bible should pick one.

**(b)** Bible:33 says the Choir's surface presence is *modest*.
ROTCHOIR_VOICES introduces five Tier 2 wandering NPCs. If surface is
modest, where do they wander? **Resolvable** either as: the five are
the Choir's first deliberate surface push in centuries (a major lore
beat), or they wander only in deep-fungal-grove zones the bible permits
(a level-design constraint). Bible should commit.

**(c)** 2127's Dimension 4 says the Choir does not consume Palimpsest
readers without consent. *This* audit's Dimension 4 says the Choir
*spits* a Palimpsest-acolyte it tries to digest. **Resolution:** the
Choir does not initiate consumption, but *can* consume an acolyte who
dies on Choir ground via automatic decay. The bitter-spit happens on
the automatic path, not a deliberate one. The restraint is doctrinal;
the bitter is metabolic.

Lore landed parenthetically: **there is no first-shape.** Only the
second through whatever-numbered. Possibilities: the first-shape is
the burned themselves (the founding generation, who *became* glass and
so cannot also have *made* it); the first-shape is doctrinally
unfileable for the same reason the Thermoclaves' tenth fire is
uncatchable; the first-shape is the Glassblown word for *that which was
not made* — the unfiring's mood applied to the maker-numbering itself,
so that *there is no first-shape* is itself a sentence in the unsaid
mood.

The traders' refusal — answering with the *handshake silence* — is the
doctrine in performance. They are not refusing the question. They are
answering it in the unsaid mood.

Plant: a Curation Tier-3 vault; a Brine salvager's pocket; a Concord
debt-record. The separate card on the Glassblown silence is filed
elsewhere — a future quest hook is finding it.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"The unfiring is the inverse of the Thermoclave's fire-keeping —
> where the Thermoclave begins each day holding fire, the Glassblown
> begins each day naming what was not held."*

No archaisms. No invented proper nouns of the *Mor'kal'thar* family.
No *verily*, no *by Stendarr*, no Towers, no Et'Ada. A clean structural
inversion in plain present-tense, the central word (*held*) carrying
the weight by returning twice. A TES book would have written *"And lo,
where the keepers of the Hot Pits do reverently tend their sacred flame
at dawn, the Glassmaker folk in opposite wisdom doth intone the litany
of the unkindled, that the absent might be honored and the silent
reckoned in due measure."* Mine is two clauses long and ships the
doctrine without ornament. Confirmed.
