## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (JSON L481):** *"The Concord calls it 'the Dissolution'...
The Pale Curation calls it 'the Severance'... The Glassblown call
it 'the Burning' and refuse to speak further."*

**Strong:** Five named cataclysm-words (Dissolution / Severance /
Burning / First Feeding / Temporal Unraveling), each encoding a
posture.

**Thin:** Brine Communion's name has not been canonised. The 1301
audit floated *the Cooling* in passing.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm, append):**

> *The Brine Communion calls it the Cooling — a word for the long
> after-storm in which the seas finally settled and the surface
> stopped boiling. Communion sailors use* the Cooling *as a unit
> of time as much as a name: a sailor born* in the Cooling's third
> generation *means a sailor born roughly two hundred years after
> the event, by the Communion's standing reckoning. They do not
> use Severance years.*

This locks in the name *and* gives it a calendar parallel to the
Pale Curation's years-since-Severance (1301 §5). Two factions, two
calendars, both pegged to the cataclysm — future documents in
either voice can date themselves *in their own cosmology*.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (bible L43):** *"They cling to husks. A seed must rot before
it grows."*

**Strong:** Bible quotes cosmic factions in attributed direct
discourse.

**Thin:** Brine Communion has no quoted voice anywhere.

**Concrete enhancement (sample for whichever subsection collects
faction voices):**

> *"It cooled. The boiling stopped. The dead were the dead and the
> living went to sea because the land was hot under the foot. We
> sing the names of the boats that sailed before us. We do not
> sing the names of the cities. The cities did not save us; the
> boats did."*
> — Brine Communion, attributed to a senior pilot, name unrecorded
> by the Pale Curation cataloguer who took the testimony.

The double attribution (Communion speaker / Curation recorder) is
itself a stratification move — the voice arrives filtered through
the cataloguer who could not get the speaker's name into a file.

### 2.3 Mutating motifs across cultures

**Quote (bible L131):** *"Every time the Choir consumes something,
it destroys the memory encoded in that thing."* **Quote (JSON L107):**
*"And what we eat, we know."*

**Strong:** Choir-consumption is a 2-way mutating motif. The 1301
audit proposed extending to 7-way.

**Thin:** Brine Communion's row was the unsketched one.

**Concrete enhancement (the row):**

> *Brine Communion: "What rots, rots. We do not own the substrate.
> The Choir is welcome to the bones we leave on shore. We carry the
> names away with us; the Choir can have the meat."*

Asymmetric to the others' postures: the Communion does not *grieve*
or *file* Choir consumption — it considers Choir's claim on landed
remains a non-issue, because Communion identity lives in *what it
carries off* (names, songs), not *what it leaves behind*.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (bible L121):** *"A road that leads to a city destroyed
four hundred years ago — but if you walk it at the right time, you
arrive."*

**Strong:** Palimpsest mechanics specified for land — buildings,
roads, battlefields.

**Thin:** No specified Palimpsest mechanic for *water*. The
cataclysm boiled the seas; can a Palimpsest-attuned sailor *see*
beneath the present coastline?

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Palimpsest, append after L125):**

> *The palimpsest's overwrite is uneven across surfaces. Stone
> remembers most cleanly: a ruined wall and the wall it was carry
> nearly the same outline, and Echo Reading reads them like two
> drafts of one sentence. Water remembers very little — the sea
> overwrites itself every wave. Brine Communion sailors who have
> touched the palimpsest report a different reading: not layered
> images of past coastlines, but a faint pull on the wheel, as if
> a current were running that the chart does not show. The
> Communion calls this the* drift-pull. *They do not always trust
> it; they do not always ignore it.*

The cosmology becomes mechanically richer (overwrite-fidelity by
substrate) and the Communion gains a Palimpsest-side mechanic in
the player-relevant register — something a Communion-aligned
player might use, lose, or be wrong about.

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Quote (bible L189):** *"Transcription — the player becomes a
memory, written into the palimpsest forever."*

**Strong:** Two cosmic apotheoses are mechanically distinct.

**Thin:** No apotheosis is offered for any of the five smaller
factions. The *absence* is probably correct, but should be
intentional, not accidental.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Central Question, append):**

> *The smaller factions do not offer apotheoses, because they do not
> believe apotheosis is a thing. The Pale Curation thinks the player
> who took the Choir or Palimpsest ending was misfiled. The
> Saccharine Concord thinks they overpaid. The Brine Communion
> thinks they were caught in the wrong tide and did not let go in
> time. The Thermoclaves think they did not stay. The Glassblown
> Remnant declines to comment. None of these reactions is wrong.*

Locates apotheosis *as a posture* the small factions reject, and
gives each rejection a one-sentence cosmological signature.

### 2.6 Linguistic texture

**Strong:** Choir's pronoun-tier rule is implementation-specified.

**Thin:** Brine Communion has no specified linguistic signature.

Falsifiable rule: any future Communion line should pivot register

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine Communion

Brine Communion is the longest-non-rotated faction. The bible's
single mention (L208) is the comparative clause about its enmity
with the Thermoclaves; the JSON does not mention it at all.

> ### The Brine Communion
>
> *Brine Communion* is a self-name. The Communion has used it of
> itself since the Cooling's second generation; the Pale Curation
> accepted the term unmodified and filed under it. *Brine* for the
> seas the Communion's first sailors found cooled but undrinkable,
> which they crossed anyway. *Communion* for the practice of eating
> from one pot when the boats meet at deep-water rendezvous — the
> Communion's only formal social ritual, observed even between
> sailors who would otherwise refuse to share bearings.
>
> They reckon time in *Cooling-generations* (a generation being the
> working life of an active sailor, roughly seventy years). They
> are presently in the fifth Cooling-generation. They keep no
> central archive; what they remember is in their songs, and the
> songs are kept by working sailors, not by historians. A sailor
> who can no longer crew can no longer keep songs. Communion
> mourning practice is built around this: the ground-bound elder
> is sung *to* by the working crew on each visit, so that the
> songs leak back into the elder before the elder dies, and the
> elder dies full, and the songs are carried on by the crew
> unchanged.
>
> Their conflict with the Thermoclaves is asymmetric in motive.
> The Thermoclaves' grievance is operational — Communion fogs cool
> the geothermal chambers in which Thermoclave hearths must burn.
> The Communion's grievance is doctrinal — the Thermoclaves *stay*,
> and a people who stay cannot be trusted with songs, because songs
> are kept by movement. Each side is describing what the other
> genuinely is. Each side is, in its own frame, correct.

**~270 words.** Working-content; not bible-pile candidate yet.
Contributes if absorbed: the *Communion* etymology, the *Cooling-
generation* calendar, the *sing-back* mourning practice, the
doctrinal asymmetry with the Thermoclaves. Two of these (calendar
and mourning) are the §2.7 anthropology request; the calendar is
also the §2.1 canonisation.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Bleed 1 — Communion absent from JSON faction-lists.** The Choir-
tendril (L338, L481) twice enumerates other factions and twice
omits both Brine Communion *and* Thermoclaves. The 1301 audit
flagged this. Sharper reading: same two factions missing in *both*
lists suggests the omission is intentional in the Choir's voice
(the Choir doesn't recommend surface-going factions to a walker
this far underground). Reframe: *probably canon-correct in voice*,
*probably an editorial gap in the bible*, where the seven-faction
catalogue should annotate which factions the Choir would not
mention, and why.

**Bleed 2 — bible L208 sociological overclaim.** *"They don't hate
each other the way the Thermoclaves and Brine Communion do."* The
bible asserts *hate*. The §3 stub proposes a more textured framing
(operational vs. doctrinal grievance). Pre-absorption, the bible
is the source of truth. Post-absorption, the L208 sentence wants
softening to *the way the Thermoclaves and Brine Communion fail to
share territory* or similar — preserving the comparator without
asserting an emotional state §3 refuses.

**Bleed 3 — JSON L338 *"The Glassblown Remnant offers nothing at
all."*** A substantive cosmological claim by the Choir — that the
Glassblown's posture is *non-offering*. But the Glassblown decline
to speak; that is itself a posture, not nothing. Flag for a future
Glassblown rotation: is the Choir's *nothing at all* uncharitable,
or in some sense literal?

## 5. Merchant's-diary artifact

A salvaged book — a pre-Cooling Pale Curation field manual on
*coastal land surveying*, recovered by a Communion crew during a
bottom-dredge near a flooded headland, and re-margined by a
Communion sailor who used the blank flyleaves and broad outer
margins for working notes. Lands two canon items parenthetically:
(a) the *sing-back* mourning practice (§3 above) in operational
use, not formal description; (b) *salvaged Pale Curation books
circulating among the Communion as working surfaces* — a
non-trivial cross-faction material reality, because it implies the
Curation's archive has losses the Curation does not necessarily
know about.

> **Crew-margin entries, in the broad outer margin of *Coastal
> Survey: A Field Manual,* Pale Curation press-mark indecipherable.
> Margins in the hand of one Veska Olm, second pilot, fifth
> Cooling-generation.**
>
> p. 14, beside a paragraph on *triangulation from a fixed
> shore-mark*: *No fixed marks left. The headland in the diagram
> is under us. Took depth at what should be its base. 11 fathoms.*
>
> p. 22, beside a tide-table for an unnamed bay: *We sang Tossi
> back the bay-songs at the rendezvous. He cried. Said he heard
> three he had forgotten he knew. His daughter said two of them
> were her mother's. The crew kept the third for him. He will go
> full.*
>
> p. 38, beside a chapter heading on *survey ethics*: *The book
> says the surveyor must not invent. Funny. The Curation's sailors
> taught us to invent every name we use for the new reefs. They
> had to. Their old names sank.*
>
> p. 47, blank inner margin, no facing text: *Drift-pull strong off
> the south reach today. Ola asked if I trusted it. I told her I
> trust it the way I trust a song I half-remember. She said that
> was no answer. I said I know, and we sailed on the compass.*
>
> p. 63, the back flyleaf, last entry: *The book is a good book
> for working in. The paper takes ink. The original surveyor knew
> things we are still rediscovering — that water in this region
> takes a long time to forget the shape of the land it covered.
> Whoever filed this in the depot did not know it would be at sea
> again. I am sorry I cannot return it. The depot is gone. The sea
> is here. The book is being useful.*

The §2.4 *drift-pull* shows up p. 47 in operational use. The §3
*sing-back* shows up p. 22 as a working description. The bleed: a
Pale Curation book is in Communion hands and the Curation does not
know — Curation archive losses are *worse than Curation-recorded*.
Three pieces of canon via one sailor's margins.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence (from §3):** *"Communion mourning practice is
built around this: the ground-bound elder is sung to by the
working crew on each visit, so that the songs leak back into the
elder before the elder dies, and the elder dies full, and the
songs are carried on by the crew unchanged."*

Working modern register: *built around this*, *ground-bound*, *on
each visit*, *leak back*, *dies full*. Three ordinary verbs (*sung*,
*leak*, *dies*); two compounded modern adjectives (*ground-bound*,
*working*); a sociological hinge (*so that... before... and...
unchanged*) doing the conceptual work without invoking liturgy.
Lower-case throughout. No archaisms (*verily*, *hath*, *unto*), no
capitalised mythic nouns (*the Elder*, *the Songs*), no inverted
predicate, no by-deity-name oath.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"And lo, the Mourning of the Communion
was such that the Elder Earth-Bound was Sung Unto by the Crew
Working, that the Songs might pass back into the Elder ere the
Elder went Down."* Tells: *And lo*; capitalised *Mourning*,
*Communion*, *Elder*, *Crew*, *Songs*; *Sung Unto*; *ere*; *went
Down* (KJV-flavored verb-particle for *died*). The actual sentence:
*built around*, *ground-bound*, *leak back*, *dies full* — working
contemporary English doing anthropological work, no liturgical
overlay.

Pass.

## Parallel-firing note

A 1633 audit exists in the working tree (uncommitted at sitting-

Brine Communion, on the grounds that 0501 was the last primary

| Element | 1633 stub | 2233 stub |
|---|---|---|
| Self-name | *uld-tide*; *Communion* is a Curation back-translation | *Brine Communion* is a self-name since the Cooling's second generation |
| Cataclysm-name | implied ongoing; no fixed word | *the Cooling*, with calendar |
| Theology | salt-as-sacrament | songs-as-identity |
| Mourning | tideline + three foods + storm-name reading + funeral boat burned | sing-back: working crew sings songs into ground-bound elder before death |
| Outsider-term | *drift* | unspecified |
| Palimpsest mechanic | — | *drift-pull*, water-overwrite asymmetry |

These are not contradictory at the seam-level (a Communion can
have both a sing-back and a tideline burial; can keep the
*Cooling-generation* calendar *and* believe the cataclysm is
ongoing). They are two compatible theological angles on the same
people. A future absorption-sitting can fold both, take one, or
elect the merge: *songs-as-identity is the working metaphysic;
salt-as-sacrament is the funerary practice.* That is the natural
read of having both stubs in the queue and is recorded here as
the audit's recommended reconciliation.

The duplicate-rotation does **not** invalidate the rotation move.
Brine Communion was the longest-non-rotated faction by either
count; both audits agreed on that. What it does mean: post-2233,
the next-rotation slot is unambiguously **Saccharine Concord** or
**Glassblown** — neither has been touched by the parallel firings.
