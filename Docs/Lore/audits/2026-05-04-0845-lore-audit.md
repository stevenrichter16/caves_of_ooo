## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible 21:* "the way text on a medieval manuscript was scraped
> off and written over, with the old words still faintly visible."

**Strong.** Aftermath, breaking-moment, *twenty after the after*
(2315), eleven Thermoclave fires, *unwritten fires* (2333).

**Thin.** **Cross-faction shared events.** Each faction's timeline
is dense; no event has yet been recorded *by all three* of the
correspondents and read differently by each.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "The Year the Caravans Did Not
Cross"):**

> Thirty-one years ago — Concord *Closed Pass*, Thermoclave *long
> ninth*, Brine *high tide that forgot to come back* — no caravan
> crossed the high pass for eight months. Suul kept the ninth alone
> in her sixteenth year of keeping. The salt did not freeze that
> winter; the Communion lost a liturgical beat it has not restored.
> Veshen, then a junior assayer, signed the Concord ledger entry:
> *eight-month standing line; resolved on the return-caravan; no
> rate-notice raised against the keepers or the Communion as the
> standing was substrate-borne, not party-borne.* The *substrate-
> borne* clause is the corpus's first Concord document to treat a
> non-Concord substrate as a transaction party. Halen-Iss objected;
> was overruled.
>
> All three correspondents entered seniority in the years that
> followed. None has spoken of the season to the others. All three
> remember it as the first time they understood their own grammar
> was incomplete.

The first event whose record is *the disagreement of three
grammars*.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Charter Receipt, R-7's redactions, lossy scribes,
slate-soot, deferred shapes, the unfired letter, the fire-tally
signed by *the fire's hand* (2333), the multi-grammar pocketbook.

**Thin.** **A document jointly authored across grammar
boundaries** — where each clause cannot be reconciled, and the
document is preserved *because* it cannot be.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "The standing-floor
correspondence"):**

> Three years ago, Suul, Veshen, and Nesh attempted, by hand-
> carriage across two seasons, one document acknowledging the
> **ember-floor beneath the high pass** as a Concord *standing-
> floor* and a Brine *dry-deeped place.*
>
> The document exists. Three clauses, each in the sender's
> grammar; no clause translates. Veshen closes a priced
> settlement. Suul registers a fire as *fifth-coming* against a
> substrate the Concord has no verb for. Nesh names the floor
> *un-deeped* and consigns its keeping to *a tide that is not
> water.*
>
> R-7 accepted intake under: *the document is the agreement; the
> agreement is the untranslatable.* Clauses filed side-by-side,
> not stacked. A walker who has read one clause has not read the
> document.

The corpus's first *deliberately untranslatable* multilateral
artifact.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** Names for cataclysm, dead, joining, debt, founder,
remainder (2333).

**Thin.** **Names for *the unsent.*** Section 3 turns on three
unsent letters; the factions' grammars for *unsent* are about to
interact and have not been collated.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar):**

> - **Choir:** *spore-not-yet-fallen.* Already in the network; the
>   air has not yet carried it. No Choir distinction between
>   *prepared* and *delivered.*
> - **Palimpsest:** *the lower draft.* Visible in the under-text
>   before the writer is finished. (See Dim 4.)
> - **Pale Curation:** *under-intake.* Filed as a parcel; no
>   annotation until the addressee opens it. R-7 holds three.
> - **Concord:** *un-rendered.* Priced but not closed; aging at
>   the writer's own rate. Veshen's letter to Suul is at 61 days.
> - **Brine:** *un-tide-borne.* Awaiting the tide that will carry
>   it — not a delay; *the tide is the addressee, not the
>   recipient.*
> - **Thermoclaves:** *cold-paged.* The fire that would sign the
>   keeping has not yet been kept.
> - **Glassblown:** no word. *Unsent* collapses to *unfired.*

Three of six grammars treat *unsent* as a condition of *fire /
tide / substrate*, not of the writer. Concord and Curation alone
hold the writer responsible. The Palimpsest reads everything.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Six strata: **material** (Choir), **temporal**
(Palimpsest), **consensual + standing** (Concord), **held-depth**
(Brine), **ember-floor** (Thermoclave, 2333), **deliberately-not-
said** (Glassblown, 2324).

**Thin.** **Inter-stratum interaction** — a *mechanism* by which
two strata interact at a shared point.

**Concrete addition (bible expansion, "Stratum interfaces"):**

> 1. **Choir / Palimpsest** — the Choir composts palimpsest-
>    bearing matter into *humus.* The lower margin survives in
>    Choir metabolism as a chemical signature the Palimpsest still
>    reads. *The Palimpsest reads the Choir's compost.* The Choir
>    does not know this.
> 2. **Concord / Thermoclave** — a priced clause settled against
>    substrate-borne value is *closed* only when the keeper agrees
>    the fire holds. The standing-floor correspondence is the
>    ratification. Without it, every prior such settlement is
>    *technically un-rendered.*
> 3. **Brine / Thermoclave** — a walker seated at a Thermoclave
>    ninth-fire and committed at a Brine going-deep within one
>    season is recorded by the substrate as **doubly-kept** — no
>    analogue in any other grammar. The Palimpsest reads doubly-
>    kept walkers as *thicker than usual.*
> 4. **Curation / All** — the intake form is the only document
>    that crosses every substrate unmodified. R-7's *witness
>    unspecified by document convention* form (2333) accepts pages
>    from each stratum. The Curation is the only faction whose
>    grammar runs on every floor. The Director suspects this is
>    dangerous; R-7 has been asked to reduce intake; R-7 has not.
> 5. **Glassblown / All** — *deliberately-not-said* refuses
>    interface. Vessels are accepted everywhere and interpreted
>    nowhere. The craft *passes through* without sharing.
> 6. **Palimpsest / All** — see (1). The only stratum that reads
>    *every other stratum's residue* as text.

Explains why the Curation has been the corpus's most load-bearing
minor faction: the only neutral grammar.

First apotheosis with two faction-classifications.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Choir pronouns, Palimpsest tense, Brine joining,
Thermoclave fire-grammar + count-verbs (2333), Glassblown unsaid
mood (2324), Curation catalogued mood, Concord trade tense.

**Thin.** **A grammatical phenomenon that occurs only in
correspondence between factions.**

**Concrete addition (bible new subsection, "The bridge-clause"):**

> A **bridge-clause** is a sentence written by a senior figure of
> one faction in *the addressee's grammar*. Rare; always senior;
> always partial. Three are recorded.
>
> 1. *Veshen to Suul (un-rendered, 61 days):* "Senior Keeper, the
>    alloys you do not forge are nevertheless owed to the chain."
>    Uses *owed* (Concord trade tense) and *the chain* (Thermoclave
>    count grammar) in one sentence; juxtaposes, does not bridge.
>    Veshen has revised this line eleven times.
> 2. *Suul to Nesh (delivered orally at the ninth fire, one hour,
>    three years ago):* "the fire and the deep-letters are both
>    kept by what they do not move." R-7's witness card (2333):
>    *both senior figures used their own grammars; neither
>    translated; both understood; the witness did not.* The *did
>    not* is the bridge-clause's signature: a third party who
>    cannot follow.
> 3. *Nesh to Veshen (un-tide-borne, 3 years):* "The salt that
>    settles your accounts is also a tide. We have priced one
>    settlement we did not understand we owed." *Priced / owed*
>    are Concord; *salt / tide* are Brine. The tide has not
>    *asked* to carry this letter.
>
> A bridge-clause is the only sentence the Palimpsest reads as
> *finished even when unsent.* The lower draft (Dim 3) of an
> un-rendered, un-tide-borne, or cold-paged bridge-clause is,
> in Palimpsest grammar, *already in the under-text* — the first
> known mechanism by which the Palimpsest preserves a message
> its writer has not yet released.

The first grammatical category that *requires two factions* to
exist.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Strong.** Tuesday Mornings, Curation initiation, Quiet Hour,
Concord weddings, Closing of Accounts, Brine committal, the
cold-coming (2333).

**Thin.** **Protocol for hand-carrying a senior letter across
faction lines.** Three letters are about to move; there is no
standing protocol.

**Concrete addition (bible new subsection, "The senior carry"):**

> A **senior carry** is a hand-carriage of a document between
> faction seniors. The walker is *not the message* — the message
> is what the addressee reads. Each grammar handles the carrier
> differently.
>
> - **Concord:** the carry is *priced*; an un-rendered carry
>   blocks the un-rendered letter.
> - **Thermoclave:** the walker is seated by carrier-temperature
>   (cold-coming, 2333); a senior letter is heavier than a
>   stranger — the walker is, on entry, *fourth-fire* regardless
>   of body heat.
> - **Brine:** the letter is transferred above the salt. To carry
>   a Brine letter past the tide line counts against the carrier's
>   standing.
> - **Curation:** intakes the carry before the addressee reads,
>   on the principle that the Curation's witness is a precondition
>   of authorship. R-7 has waived this twice. The Director has not
>   been told.
> - **Choir:** has no senior carry; the Choir is the substrate the
>   carry walks across, and considers itself the *true* carrier in
>   every case. A walker who does not know this is, in Choir
>   grammar, *delivering for us.*
> - **Glassblown:** do not send or receive senior letters. The
>   unfired letter (2324) is the closest the craft permits.
>
> A walker carrying letters in three grammars in one season is a
> *triple-carriage witness* — three known instances since the
> cataclysm. Each has produced a document the Curation considers
> the most important of its decade.

The corpus's first formalisation of the player as a category in
faction grammar.

## 3. The cross-faction correspondents cluster

**Veshen, Suul, Nesh — three letters, one season.**

```
   Veshen  (Concord, assayer's chair)
      |  un-rendered, 61 days
      v
   Suul    (Thermoclave, Ninth-Fire, lineage-orphan to Erro)
      ^  oral, 1hr, 3yrs ago, witnessed by R-7
   Nesh    (Brine Communion, Tide-Reader)
      ^  un-tide-borne, 3 years
   Veshen
```

The triangle is *not closed.* Suul has never written to Veshen.
The Concord interprets this as *the keeper does not transact*,
filed under the *substrate-borne* clause (Dim 1). R-7's senior
annotation: *the third leg does not require closure; the
substrate closes it.*

**Why this triangle:** each correspondent is the only living
member of their faction to have used a bridge-clause (Dim 6);
each is *lineage-anchored* to the cataclysm — Suul to Erro
(2333), Veshen to a founding ledger entry her great-aunt signed
in the breaking-moment (2310), Nesh to a going-deep her
predecessor performed in the long-overdue tide (2315). The only
three living seniors with a cataclysm-era *named* lineage; the
criterion by which each was elevated. None considers themselves
connected to a third party.

**Gameplay seam (not commitment):** a walker at high Thermoclave
reputation could be entrusted with Veshen's un-rendered letter.
Carrying it to Suul opens *owed-fifth-coming* on the substrate-
ledger. Suul, accepting, may indicate (in count-grammar the walker
must learn) that *Nesh's un-tide-borne letter is the next the
substrate awaits.* Carrying that across the tide line, against
Brine protocol (Dim 7), closes the triangle. Each delivery costs
something specific: Veshen's letter, a *priced carriage* the
Concord must settle; Nesh's, standing with the Communion *for one
tide*; Suul's oral reply (uncarriable), the walker's *ambient
temperature* for as long as they remain in Thermoclave grammar.

A triple-carriage walker is *recorded in three grammars at once.*
The Palimpsest considers this the deepest reading of a walker
since Erro.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a) Bible 209-210 vs. accumulated cross-faction grammar.**

Bible says: *"They [Choir and Palimpsest] are not at war. They don't
hate each other the way the Thermoclaves and Brine Communion do."*

The accumulated corpus shows **Thermoclaves and Brine in
substantial cooperation** — the dry-deeped place / un-deeped
fragment correspondence (Dim 4), the Suul–Nesh oral bridge-clause
(Dim 6, 2333), Tide-Reader Nesh seated at the Ninth Fire. *The
bible's clause about Thermoclave/Brine antagonism is now factually
obsolete.*

**Recommend bible 208-209 amended to:**

> They are not at war. They don't hate each other the way many
> outside chroniclers assume. The Thermoclaves and Brine, in
> particular, have a senior bridge-clause line that has surprised
> every other faction. The Choir and Palimpsest's incomprehension
> of each other is deeper, and sadder, than the more famous
> antagonisms; theirs is a conflict of *substrates*, not of
> policies.

**(b) RotChoir.json:1 — *"What do you seek from the Choir?"***

The Choir's standing tendril assumes the walker comes with a
request. After thirteen audits' worth of cross-faction work, this
is too narrow. **Recommend a second-encounter `WhatAreYou` opener
for `ChoirTendril_1`, gated on prior cross-faction delivery on
Choir-colonised ground:**

> *"You came back. The soil tells us you have been carrying
> something for someone else. We tasted it through your boots.
> We will not ask whose. We were the floor."*

Rewards the triple-carriage path without exposition; ratifies the
Choir's *delivering for us* claim (Dim 7) without telling the
walker.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

**Title (in-game):** *Annotation on Pale Curation junior-cabinet
card B-114. Subject: a sister's name omitted from a fragmentary
letter by Erro of the Tenth.*

```
[Curation card. Subject: letter-fragment, charred one edge, in
 count-grammar. Sender: Erro, Thermoclave keeper, tenth fire,
 deceased cataclysm-era. Recipient: a single character, partly
 burnt. Senior annotation, R-7's hand:]

  B-114. Recipient line illegible. The surviving character is
  consistent with *Mer* and *Mar* (Concord names of the founding
  generation) and with the Brine reverence-prefix *na-* (rare;
  blood-kin lost to the tide).

  Suul, when shown, identified the recipient as a sister. The
  fragment offers no kin word; Erro's count-grammar does not
  register sibling relationships. Suul did not read the fragment
  to identify. She held it three minutes, returned it, and said
  *the count is sister-shaped.*

  The Concord ledger for the cataclysm-year contains a Mer-Iss
  signature, age fourteen, on a household intake. Mer-Iss died
  three months before the cataclysm. The Brine register a
  *na-Maru* lost to a high tide eight months before. Neither
  party records knowing Erro.

  R-7, by senior judgment: *the count is sister-shaped* is
  sufficient. The card stands as Suul reads it. The Concord and
  Brine entries are recorded for completeness; neither is
  primary. Filed in the junior cabinet.

  Sub-annotation, smaller hand, undated:
  the Choir, asked, said the fragment tasted of three sisters.
  it then said it tastes that way of every fragment by an
  only-child. we did not file the second remark. — A.
```

Three contradictory lineages for one omitted name; no
reconciliation; the Choir's commentary preserved unfiled.
**The bombshell is parenthetical:** *recorded for completeness;
neither is primary.* The Curation has, in this single annotation,
established that **a Thermoclave keeper's identification by
count-grammar — without reading — overrides Concord and Brine
documentary evidence**, on the strength of *the count is
sister-shaped.* The corpus's first cross-faction *priority of
grammars* ruling. Not the Director's policy. R-7 made it alone.

A walker who shows B-114 to Suul gets nothing — Suul has read it.
Veshen, the eleventh revision of the un-rendered letter. Nesh,
*the tide-asked-to-carry* — the tide indicates by salt-pattern
that the un-tide-borne letter is now ready to leave her hand.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"A bridge-clause is the only sentence the Palimpsest reads as
> finished even when unsent."*

No archaisms; no apostrophe-fantasy names; no Aedra/Daedra/Towers/
CHIM/Et'Ada; no *verily, hath, lo.* Single present-tense
declarative. Mechanical claim: one grammatical construction
interacts with one substrate by one rule. A TES rewrite would
read *"And know thee that the bridge-spoken word, alone among the
words of mortals, is held complete in the deep-text ere the
speaker hath drawn breath to release it."* Mine has no *know
thee, alone among the words of mortals, deep-text, ere, hath,
drawn breath, release.* Operational register; engineering of
grammar against substrate. Confirmed.
