### 2.1 🟡 The *-Veril* naming-pool claim is an audit-chain habit, not in-world

**File:** `2026-05-06-0301-lore-audit.md` §5 small-Dim-1
contribution.

**The claim.** The 0301 reads a cross-faction *-Veril* suffix
(2301's Curation **Sept-Veril**, 0301's Thermoclave **Mor-Veril**)
as evidence of "a regional naming-pool rather than a faction-
internal convention."

**Why it's flagged.** Both names are audit-invented. The bible
has no NPCs with the *-Veril* suffix. ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md's five
named NPCs (Mogu, Grib, Nam, Sien, Sopp) carry no *-Veril*.
RotChoir.json carries no *-Veril*. The 0301 elevates a small
audit-chain authorial coincidence (one suffix, two NPCs, both
audit-fabricated) to an in-world Dim 1 contribution about
regional naming.

**Wider pattern the 0301 missed.** Spot-survey of audit-invented
NPCs across R7 §5s:

  - **Mor-Anne** (Curation senior, 0201 §5)
  - **Sept-Veril** (Curation junior, 2301 §5)
  - **Sept-Veril** referenced again (0201 §5 narrator)
  - **Burin-Hath** (Thermoclave senior forger, 0301 §5)
  - **Sela-Hath** (Thermoclave heat-witness, 0301 §5)
  - **Mor-Veril** (Thermoclave apprentice, 0301 §5)
  - **Kelo-of-the-no-shore-mother** (Brine bowl-maker, 0101 §5)
  - **Pell-of-the-second-fold** (Brine posting-keeper, 0101 §5)
  - **Mellis** (Brine posting-keeper, 0101 §5)
  - **Tov** (Brine posting-keeper, 0101 §5)
  - **Sweetening-Three** (Concord factor, referenced 0301 §5)

The audit chain has been freely mixing prefixes and suffixes
across factions: *Mor-* appears at Curation (Mor-Anne) and
Thermoclaves (Mor-Veril); *Sept-* appears only at Curation;
*-Hath* appears only at Thermoclaves (working-forger suffix per

names (Sweetening-Three).

**Finding.** Either (a) the 0301's claim is unsound — the audit
chain has no underlying naming-system, only authorial drift —
or (b) the claim must be retroactively expanded to cover *Mor-*
as well, which would yield not "a *-Veril* regional naming-pool"
but "a Curation-Thermoclave shared naming-pool with multiple
markers (*Mor-*, *Sept-*, *-Veril*)." The wider pattern is more
honest than the suffix-only claim.

**Recommendation.** A future sitting (not 0311) should produce
an explicit **NPC-naming-pool table** as an audit-internal
reference document, listing all audit-invented NPCs and the
naming-pool rules the audit chain wishes to retain or correct.

should be treated as **draft-stage, not in-world.** The Dim 1
contribution downgrades to a Dim 8 marginalia (an audit-chain
habit observed parenthetically), not a Dim 1 in-world fact.

## 5. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"The audit chain has no underlying naming-system, only
> authorial drift."*

Working register, gloss-form. *Authorial drift* is the kind of
phrase a working editor (the audience for the audit chain) would
use about a manuscript that has accumulated quirks across drafts
— procedural, not theological. The sentence's claim is about
documents and the people who write them, not about the world the
documents describe. Lower-case throughout. No allegorical
capitalisation.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"Verily, in the great Chronicle of the
Pale Curators and the Thermo-Forgers there is no Name-Wisdom,
only the Wandering Hand of the Scribe whose Pen knows not where
the Word will fall."* Tells: *Verily*; *Chronicle*, *Name-Wisdom*,
*Wandering Hand*, *Scribe*, *Pen*, *Word* (allegorical
capitalisations); *whose Pen knows not where the Word will fall*
(mythic verb-on-noun); *Pale Curators* and *Thermo-Forgers*
(heraldic compound nouns substituting for the working *Curation*
and *Thermoclaves*). The actual sentence: *audit chain*,
*naming-system*, *authorial drift* — three working-register
nouns and one working adjective, no allegorical noun-phrase, no
register-marker on the abstract terms.

Pass.
