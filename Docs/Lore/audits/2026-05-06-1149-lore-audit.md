## 3. Pale Curation §3 narrow deepening — recorder-numbering formalisation

The 0957 §2.1 cold-eye flagged the 0915 §5's
**Recorder-14 / Recorder-11** references as borderline against
the 0311 NPC-discipline. Recommendation: formalize-or-retire.
The 0915 §5 has been on the chain for two days (eight sittings
since 0915); retiring would require revising the 0915 §5
artifact, which the chain does not do retroactively. The
remaining path is **formalize**.

This deepening *formalises* recorder-numbering as a Pale
Curation working-rule. **Narrow scope** (does not generalise to
other Curation working-roles); **no new bible-side candidate**
(working-rule lives in audit-chain working-content, not
proposed for absorption); **resolves the 0957 §2.1 finding**.

### The recorder-numbering rule

Each Pale Curation depot maintains its own recorder-roster.
Recorders are referenced by **recorder-number + series + day**
in three working-contexts:

  - **Within a single artifact** (a day-page, a negative-blank,
    a release-slip), recorder-numbers may be referenced
    directly: *"Recorder 14"*. Disambiguation happens by
    artifact-internal context (the day-page's own series and
    day are header-level metadata).
  - **Cross-artifact references** append series and day:
    *"Recorder 14, Series 3, Day 47"*. The 0915 §5 used this
    form when introducing the recorder; the form is correct.
  - **Cross-depot references** append depot identifier:
    *"Recorder 14 / Hall-Seventh / Series 3, Day 47"*. Hall-
    Seventh recorders are numbered separately from other
    depots' recorders. *Recorder 14 at Hall-Seventh* and
    *Recorder 14 at the salt-cliff depot* (or wherever) are
    different working-positions, not the same person.

### What the rule does not do

  - Does **not** generalise to Curation cataloguers (per the
    0201 negative-blank rotation, cataloguers use *junior-of-
    standing-position* + bench-letter; per the 1701 rotation,
    seniors carry their own working-name conventions).
  - Does **not** generalise to other factions' working-roles.
    Concord factors are by-name + market-of-rotation (per the
    0001 / 0607 / 0801 chain). Glassblown working-masters are
    by working-bench (per 0404). Brine boatswains are by
    vessel + working-tide.
  - Does **not** elevate to a faction-wide claim about Curation
    naming-customs more broadly. The rule covers recorders
    only.

### Why the rule is depot-scoped, not faction-scoped

The 0706 §3 named the **request-rider** as the Curation
working-position responsible for inter-depot delivery. Request-
riders carry working-forms across depots. If recorder-numbering
were faction-scoped (Recorder 14 unique across all Curation),
inter-depot Recorder-14 contact would create reference
collisions in the day-pages. Depot-scoping prevents this — the
working-forms always identify the depot, so a Hall-Seventh
release-slip referencing *Recorder 14* is unambiguously the
Hall-Seventh recorder, not any other.

This is the Curation's **standing solution to the cross-depot
collision problem** — a working-rule the chain inferred from the
0706 inter-depot delivery infrastructure but did not explicitly
name. The 1149 names it.

### Sequencing closure

The 0957 §2.1 finding closes:

  - **0915 §5 Recorder-14 reference**: artifact-internal,
    correct usage per the rule above.
  - **0915 §5 Recorder-11 cross-reference**: *"the recorder-11
    in the contradiction-table"* — the 0915 §5 used this
    form internally without series-and-day disambiguation. By
    the rule, an external cross-reference would require
    Series 3, Day N specification. The 0915's usage is
    *artifact-internal* (Recorder 11 had filed an earlier
    page in the *same* contradiction-table the 0915's
    Recorder 14 is now reading), so the disambiguation is
    contextually present. **Within the rule.**

The 0957 §2.1 **finding is resolved**.

> *"Depot-scoping prevents this — the working-forms always
> identify the depot, so a Hall-Seventh release-slip referencing
> Recorder 14 is unambiguously the Hall-Seventh recorder."*

Working register, gloss-form. *Depot-scoping* is the working
hyphenate the Curation uses about its own working-rules.
*Working-forms* is the chain's standing compound for the
release-slips, day-pages, negative-blanks, and request-stubs
the 0706 §3 catalogued. The sentence's claim is procedural —
about how a working-rule prevents a working-collision — not
theological. Lower-case throughout.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"And so the Halls of the Pale Order
keep their Records Several, that no Recorder of one Hall be
mistook for the Recorder of another, and the Sacred Slip be
read true wherever it travels."* Tells: capitalisations on
*Halls*, *Pale Order*, *Records*, *Recorder*, *Hall*, *Sacred
Slip*; *several* in the heraldic-archaic *kept-several* sense;
*be mistook* (KJV-archaic passive); *be read true*
(adjectival-mythic). The actual sentence: *depot-scoping*,
*working-forms*, *unambiguously*, *Hall-Seventh* — four working
terms, the rule doing its own work.
