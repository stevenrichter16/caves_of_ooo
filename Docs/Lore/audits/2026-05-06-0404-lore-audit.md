## 3. Faction asymmetry — Glassblown rotation

Last §3 rotation: Thermoclaves (0301). Glassblown not rotated
since 2101; due. No audit-invented NPCs.

**Etymology.** *Remnant* in Glassblown usage carries two
simultaneous senses: (1) the small remaining number of
practitioners, (2) the small remaining quantity of pre-cataclysm
sand of the right grade. The two readings are identical in
working speech — there is no Glassblown without the sand and no
sand without someone willing to draw it. The faction's name to
itself is reportedly just *the working* (lower-case); *Glassblown
Remnant* is outsider-form only.

**Working ritual: the cooling-watch.** A piece, fresh off the
iron, must cool in the presence of two practitioners — one on
the cooling-watch (silent, attentive, recording the cooling-
pattern by hand on a long strip), one on the relief (rotating
the piece so no face is favoured by gravity). The watch-strip is
filed with the piece. *Without the strip, the piece is not
Glassblown — it is a glass object made by Glassblown hands,
which is a different category.* The strip lifts the working into
the same register as the Curation's negative-shelf or the
Thermoclave heat-witness colour-strip: the Glassblown are a
**reading-faction** by this measure, even though their working
medium is the craft itself, not a separable substrate.

**Mourning practice: the twice-witnessed.** A piece breaking
*before* its watch-strip is filed (cracks on the cooling-bench)
is ground back to sand and the strip burned — the pre-filing
state is reversible. A piece breaking *after* filing has its
shards re-collected and the strip *re-filed* with a second strip
describing the breaking; the piece becomes a **twice-witnessed**
artifact, which Glassblown conversation treats as more, not less,
complete than an unbroken piece. The mourning is for the
breaking-witness, who carries the second strip until they hand it
over.

**Bridge to item 28.** The twice-witnessed practice is the
strongest existing precedent in the chain for inter-faction
artifact-custody. A Glassblown-filed piece (one strip, possibly
two) read by the Curation (negative-shelf entry) is *triply-
witnessed* — Glassblown working position: more record is better.
Curation working position: prior filings are protocol-protected.
**Agreement-on-practice / disagreement-on-reason** — the same
texture as the *not-at-war* lattice, expressed in working-artifact
terms. **Recommend item 28's draft, when graduating, cite the
Glassblown twice-witnessed practice as precedent.**

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

A **forge-record sheet** kept by a Thermoclave second-stone over
a single working-day. The artifact-custody bombshell drops mid-
sheet, parenthetically, where a non-working reader would read
straight past it. Anonymous attribution — no audit-invented
names. ~290 words.

> *Iron up at fourth bell. The second-stone today, the
> heat-witness on colour-strip. Nine bond-strikes scheduled —
> seven for the Curation order, two training-strikes for the
> apprentice.*
>
> *First bond-strike clean. Strip reads the standing colour for
> a clean bond. Second strike, the working-forger's grip
> slipped a hair — the strip reads a faint warm note half a
> finger from the bond-line. The forger says the bond will
> hold; the heat-witness files the strip warm-flagged anyway.
> Third clean. Fourth clean. Fifth, the apprentice's training-
> strike, the forger guiding the apprentice's hand — strip
> reads no warm flag, but a guidance-mark goes on the strip,
> which is not a flaw, only a note for the apprentice's later
> reading.*
>
> *Sixth bond-strike: the Curation's request-rider arrived
> mid-strike (request slip in hand, the standing form). The
> forger finished the strike clean before reading the slip. The
> slip said: the Choir requests a spore-witness reading of
> yesterday's three Curation-filed bond-pieces, before they
> ship. The forger set the slip on the second stone unread for
> the rest of the day — the slip is not a forge matter — and we
> held to the schedule. Eighth clean. Ninth the second
> training-strike, apprentice alone — clean, the strip his own
> to keep.*
>
> *Iron down at sixteenth bell. The Curation's slip will go to
> the senior in the morning — the answer is not the forger's to
> give. The senior's standing word is that the Choir is
> welcomed for spore-witness reading (the Choir does not ask in
> bad faith, and the bond-pieces do not lose filing by being
> read), but only after the negative-shelf has accepted the
> filing. The rider was sent before that. Strip-cabinet locked.*

**Lore landed parenthetically:** the Choir does request spore-
witness readings on Curation-filed pieces (item 28's central
question, dropped as a single mid-sheet sentence); the Curation's
standing protocol is *request-slip on standing form, to the
senior*; the Thermoclave's *warm-flagged* strip is a third
strip-form alongside clean and guidance-marked (the bible has
no slot for any of three); a routine Choir-Curation custody-
protocol exists, **welcomed pending negative-shelf entry** —
custody is collaborative as routine, not as crisis.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence (from §3, Glassblown brief):**

> *"The breaking is not mourned — the pre-filing state is
> reversible."*

Working register, plain declarative. *Pre-filing state* is the
flat working noun-phrase a Glassblown second-stone or a Curation
junior would use about an artifact in administrative limbo. No
allegorical capitalisation. The sentence's claim is procedural
(*before this point, the artifact is not yet an artifact*), not
mythological.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"Hark! The Shattering of an
Unwitnessed Glass is no Sorrow, for the Sands of the Forge-
Mother yet hold their Patience, and the Watcher's Strip remains
Unwritten — and That Which Is Not Yet Inscribed cannot be Lost."*
Tells: *Hark*; allegorical capitalisations on *Shattering*,
*Sorrow*, *Sands*, *Forge-Mother*, *Patience*, *Strip*,
*Inscribed*, *Lost*; *Forge-Mother* is a heraldic-compound
substituting for the working *Glassblown practitioner*; *That
Which Is Not Yet Inscribed* is mythic relative-clause
construction. The actual sentence: *breaking*, *mourned*,
*pre-filing state*, *reversible* — three working nouns and one
working adjective, no allegorical noun-phrase, no register-marker
on any abstract.
