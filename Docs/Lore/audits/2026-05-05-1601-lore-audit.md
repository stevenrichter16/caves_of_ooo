## 3. Faction asymmetry — the Thermoclaves

Thermoclaves last covered at 1101 (five hours). Longest gap among the
four underdeveloped bodies; rotates in.

The 1101 sitting established the Thermoclave–Brine rupture — *the
salt-out morning,* the *cellar-step,* the bell-rolls, the *hearth-
warden* working title. This sitting develops one small but core
artifact: the Thermoclave **bell-name custom.**

A Thermoclave child is named by the **bell at hand-touching:** the
first hour-bell that rings after the child's hand first closes around
an adult's finger. Bells are not numbered; they are named by hour and
by the working condition that hour holds in the cellar. The bell-rolls
record five standard bells:

- **First watch-bell** (pre-dawn, cellar cold). *First-watch born* —
  held steady in cold weather.
- **Waking-bell** (sunrise, coals raised). *Waking-born* — held to
  favour bright work.
- **Eaten-bell** (midday, after the household's eating). *Eaten-born*
  — held patient with hunger.
- **Cold-bell** (late afternoon, the cellar's coldest hour, the
  morning's heat gone and the evening's not yet raised). *Cold-bell
  born* and, in folk reading, *given the cellar to mind*; in doctrinal,
  *given the cellar to keep* — duty vs. vocation. Most cold-bell-born
  grow up cellar-keepers; some refuse and are *unbelled* (working
  parallel to Brine *unplaited*).
- **Banked-bell** (late evening, coals banked). *Banked-born* — held
  to favour long, slow work.

A Thermoclave bears no personal name in the walker sense. A cold-bell-
born cellar-keeper is *cold-bell of cellar-three under hearth-warden
Pell;* if Pell dies, *under hearth-warden* (the office, not the name);
if the office is unfilled, *awaiting the warden.* Three properties:
the name is a *position-in-the-cellar's-day*, not a designator; it
updates as the cellar's officers change; a Thermoclave who leaves the
cellar life keeps the bell of hand-touching but loses the cellar-and-
warden suffix. *Cold-bell, no cellar* is the working term for an
exile, and a heavy one.

The grammar mirrors the cellar's frame: a person is not knowable
except by the position they hold against the bells. A Thermoclave who
introduces themselves *as just a name* is taken to be either testing
the listener or already *unbelled.*

**Etymology.** *Thermoclave* is, in the cellar-keepers' own working
register, a bookish-outsider word. They call themselves *the cellar-
keepers* (folk) or *the warden-bodies* (doctrinal). *Thermoclave* is
what the Pale Curation's first accessioner wrote on the Hall-Seventh
ledger, from Greek *thermos* + *clavis* (heat-key); the cellar-keepers
thought it pretentious and have used it ever since because it is
funnier than correcting it. By four hundred years of working
acceptance, the bookish word has become an in-world word — they will
tell a walker they are *Thermoclaves* in the same breath they will
tell another cellar-keeper they are *warden-bodies.* Both are correct;
only one is overheard by outsiders.

(Five theories of bond, five exile-words: *unaccessioned, undeclared,
untraded, unplaited, unbelled.* The Choir is sixth and has none.
Builds against the Brine *plait* at the 1501 audit's Section 3.)

> *(The Choir digests material history but does not digest grammar. A

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

**Title:** *A Saccharine Concord book-keeper's quarterly summary —
the back of the page, in the same hand.*

> *(Front: a Saccharine Concord standard quarterly — receivables,
> payables, the usual columns, firm's seal upper-right. Back:
> unruled, same hand, firm's clerical, firm's response-ink. Held at
> the firm's office at the Crossroads-Three; copy at Hall-Seventh,
> accession 1411.d.)*
>
> Mid-quarter return is light. Middling-tier accounts hold middling
> tier and continue to underperform on the firm's projection model.
> **Margin-note:** the model assumes customers move toward higher
> tiers as they accumulate working capital. The middling-tier
> customers, on present data, are not accumulating. They spend at a
> steady rate against the cellar-bodies, the strand-bodies, the
> field-wardens, and the standing-Choir vendors at the under-grove.
> Throughput high; net flat.
>
> The model treats this as anomaly. The hand-touching reading is that
> the model is wrong about the customers. They are not in transit
> between tiers — they are *stationed in middle.* Middle is the
> working position. They came to middle and have not left.
>
> The firm's standing position is that the model is the model. The
> hand-touching position, in this margin, is that the firm has a
> customer-class it has not catalogued: *those who have decided not
> to move.* The model under-projects them. Adjusting it would require
> admitting the bodies are in steady balance, and the firm's working
> register holds that the bodies are in trajectory.
>
> *Margin-note, smaller hand:* the bodies are, on this quarter's data,
> in steady balance. The book-keepers know. The firm holds otherwise.

**What lands parenthetically.** The moderate-path player as a
customer-class the Concord cannot officially recognise (Dimension 5);
the Concord's tension between *trajectory* model and *steady-state*
observation (a structural lore-bleed the bible has never written); the
unfollowed footnote *the standing-Choir vendors at the under-grove*
(the Choir has commercial vendors where it touches surface
civilisation, and the Concord trades with them); and the bell-bodies,
strand-bodies, field-wardens, and Choir-vendors named alongside as the
Concord's four trade-counterparties — a four-faction summary the
bible has never assembled. The merchant's diary is the bible's working
summary, *seen sidelong,* by the body that prices the others.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"A Thermoclave who introduces themselves as just a name is taken to
> be either testing the listener or already unbelled."*

Working register, plain-spoken. *Just a name* is the diction of an
ordinary working clerk, not a herald. *Taken to be* is procedural —
the kind of phrasing a Hall accessioner might use against a contested
intake. The verbs are flat (*introduces, taken, testing*). No *verily,*
no archaism, no inverted syntax, no apostrophe-fantasy nominal forms.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"He who giveth his name without his bell is
held by the cellar-folk to be either dissembler unto his hearer, or
already cast forth from the keeping of bells."* I did not write that.
The forbidden version's tells: archaic relative *He who* + archaic
verb *giveth*, the inversion *held by the cellar-folk to be*, the
doublet *cast forth from the keeping*, the elevated *dissembler unto
his hearer.* The actual sentence's anti-tells: *just a name* (a
casual diminutive elevation cannot tolerate); *testing the listener*
(a working clerk's working concern, not a herald's); *unbelled*
(a faction-specific term the reader can read because the bible has
been building such grammar for thirty-eight sittings).
