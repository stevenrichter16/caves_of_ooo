## 3. Faction asymmetry — Pale Curation (the first stone, depot-closure, the transposition-rule)

> ### The first stone
>
> Each depot's bench-floor has a *first stone* —
> the founding-era stone the eastern aperture was
> built to admit direct sun on. Second-light begins
> when the sun reaches its surface. The first stone
> is not replaced; cumulative repair around it is
> permitted, but the working-surface itself must be
> the founding stone. A first-bench naming the
> first stone in working-vocabulary calls it *what
> we stand toward at second-light*. The full plain
> form is uncommon. Founding-era depots have first
> stones held in the corner of the bench-floor,
> south-east; later depots have it in the centre,
> south-east-faced.
>
> ### Depot-closure
>
> A depot whose first stone has fractured beyond a
> recorder's working-thumb is closed by working-
> tradition. The recital is conducted thereafter
> at the nearest sister depot; the closed depot's
> third-shelf boxes are walked, in iron-banded
> custody, to the sister depot at next intake-week.
> Three depot-closures by first-stone-fracture are
> recorded in working-tradition (Sev. 287, 314,
> 401); each is named by depot number and working-
> week, never by stone-substance or weather-cause.
> The body does not file the closures by the
> working-shelf form. The succession of first-
> benches teaches them on retirement morning.
>
> ### The transposition-rule
>
> Cross-body archive comes to the third shelf by
> three method-conversions: **Thermoclave** iron-
> and-fire → graphite rubbing on tracing-leaf;
> **Communion** paper-as-tackle → stylus chart-
> comparison on the working-ship's standing-table;
> **Glassblown** glass → silver-halide plate at
> cool-quarter second-light. A fourth body — the
> **Concord** — has no working-form. Somatic medium
> is deleted at pressing-clean before any recorder
> could be admitted. Curation files Concord
> standing-test certificates when offered; the
> seam itself is not received. The body has not
> codified the exclusion. A senior asked whether
> Concord material can be transposed answers:*
> "the body has no working-form for it. the seam
> goes where the seam goes." *The third shelf holds
> three transposed bodies' working-irregularities
> alongside the Curation's bequest notebooks; the
> Concord's absence is the shelf's fifth box,
> never opened, never filled.

Four cadence-authorities: recital (body); third-
shelf indistinction (no-body); transposition-shelf
(cross-body, three transposed, one absent); first
stone (ancestral, unreplaced). The Curation holds
the four-place architecture with the third
authority shelf-aggregated.

### What the body does not do

The body does not codify the transposition-rule,
the Concord exclusion, or the depot-closure
register. Three working-traditions, three non-
textual carriers (bequest notebook; intake-rotation
seniors; seventeen-breaks oral register). The
carriers are the form. Both bodies' silence is the
form, seventh instance.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted (§3, the first stone):** *"A depot whose
first stone has fractured beyond a recorder's
working-thumb is closed by working-tradition."*

Working-modern register. *A depot*, *first stone*,
*fractured*, *recorder's working-thumb*, *closed
by working-tradition* — plain craft-clerical
compounds, lower-case, no archaism. *Fractured
beyond* and *closed by working-tradition* are
contemporary; no fronted nominal, no *whereby /
hath / lieth*.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"Where the First
Stone of the Depot hath cracked beyond the breadth
of a Recorder's Thumb, lo, that Depot shall be
closed by Tradition, and its working transferred
unto the Sister-Depot."* Tells: capitalised *First
Stone / Depot / Recorder's Thumb / Tradition /
Sister-Depot*; *hath cracked, lo, shall, unto*;
fronted *Where the First Stone… hath cracked*.
Actual sentence is none of these.
