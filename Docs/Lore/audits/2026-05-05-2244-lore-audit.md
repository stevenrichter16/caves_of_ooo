## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclaves rotates in

### The cataclysm, three names plus a refusal

The Thermoclaves do not have a single name for the cataclysm.
The sitting-19 (R3) audit named the faction's three doctrinal
schools — Floor, Ember, Refusal — and noted that their
disagreement is *what the rite does to the world*. The
disagreement extends to what the cataclysm was, and therefore
what to call it.

**Floor school: *the Unkept.*** Floor doctrine reads fire as a
thing that *goes down* — fire's natural state is to die; the
keeper's work is to keep it from dying. *The Unkept* is the
Floor school's name for the cataclysm: the world's central
hearth went unkept, for one reason or another, and what fire
does when unkept is what fire did then. The name has no moral
weight in Floor grammar — *unkept* is a count-particle (*an
unkept fire*, *a kept fire*, *a half-kept fire*) — the same
particle that appears in tally-stubs for ordinary cold ashpits.
A Floor keeper saying *the Unkept* is not eulogising; they are
filing.

**Ember school: *the Smothering.*** Ember doctrine reads fire as
a thing that *persists without sign* — embers under ash hold heat
for days; the keeper's work is to read the ash for the warm
beneath. The Ember school does not believe the cataclysm was a
fire dying. They believe it was a fire *covered* — the world's
central hearth still warm, still readable to the right keeper,
but smothered by something that prevents the warmth from
showing. *The Smothering* is therefore an *ongoing* name, not a
historical one: the smother is still in place, the warm is still
beneath. The Floor school finds this consoling and inaccurate;
the Refusal school finds it counted-speech-defending-position
and refuses to discuss it.

**Refusal school: the unnameable, which is itself the third
name.** Refusal grammar will not invoke counted speech to defend
its own position. The cataclysm is the largest possible counted-
speech temptation: any name is a position; any position is a
count; any count a defence. Refusal therefore does not name it.
Their keepers, asked, perform a small flat hand-gesture (palm-
down, level at the navel) meaning *the place where the count
would be if there were a count*. The gesture is the third name.
Floor transcribes it as *(no count)*; Ember as *(warm, unread)*.
Both transcriptions are wrong by Refusal lights. Refusal grammar
has no transcription.

**Cross-school stubs** (a Thermoclave artifact form, see §5) will
sometimes carry both *the Unkept* and *the Smothering* in the
same line, slash-stacked; Refusal stubs leave a blank line, which
Floor reads as *(no count)* and Refusal reads as *the line itself*.

**Anthropological note: the unkept-fire mourning form.** When a
Thermoclave dies, the tally-stub that recorded their last shift
is left unfinished — the chain-count column is left blank, the
date column is left blank, the keeper-mark is left blank, but
the stub is not destroyed. It is filed in the cold-ashpit ledger
under a new column heading: *unkept by absence* (Floor) or
*smothered by absence* (Ember) or with a flat-hand-gesture mark
in the margin (Refusal). The teaching: *the count is paused, not
ended; a chain that had a keeper is a chain that had a keeper
even when the keeper is not there to keep it.* The Choir would
recognise the gesture as composting ledger-state; the Palimpsest
as a deliberate erasure-with-witness. The Thermoclaves call it
*pausing the chain.*

**Insertion target:** new bible sidebar in the *Other Factions —
Brief Notes* section (post-L210), parallel to sitting 11's
Glassblown sidebar. ~330 words. The shipped JSON L338
(*"The Glassblown Remnant offers nothing at all"*) carries no

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

A Thermoclave tally-stub. Lands the *Floor / Ember / Refusal*
distinction parenthetically inside the working artifact-form the
faction uses for ordinary record-keeping.

The Thermoclaves' tally-stub is the working form for a single
fire-keeping shift: cold-ashpit number, chain-count column,
keeper-mark, date, weather-note. Most stubs are mundane the way
a forklift driver's daily inspection sheet is mundane. The
artifact-form survives because it is *exhaustive*: nothing about
a kept fire is too small to note.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"The Refusal school does not name it. Their keepers, asked,
> perform a small flat hand-gesture (palm-down, level at the
> navel) that means the place where the count would be if there
> were a count."*

Working register, plain-spoken, anthropological. The sentence
sits inside an *In Refusal grammar* / *Refusal lights* frame —
faction-internal teaching, with the gesture described in the
neutral register an outside observer would use. *Palm-down,
level at the navel* is a physical-anthropology description, the
way a field-anthropologist would record an observed gesture in a
notebook. *The place where the count would be if there were a
count* is conditional-grammar — the gesture's referent is named
through what the count is *not*, which is the actual Refusal
doctrine made literal.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"Verily, the Faithful of the Refusal
speak no Name; for to Name is to Count, and to Count is to Bind,
and to Bind is to wound the World a second time. Witness then
the Sign of the Empty Place."* Tells: *Verily, the Faithful*
(archaising religious register, capital *Faithful*); the
*Name → Count → Bind* anaphora-of-three with mythopoeic
capitals; *wound the World* (capital *World*, mythic-cosmic
stakes asserted from outside the faction); *Witness then*
(archaising imperative); *the Sign of the Empty Place*
(capitalised compound mystery-noun replacing the actual
gesture-description). The actual sentence: lower-case throughout;
physical-anthropology gesture-description; doctrine named in
conditional grammar; no mythic-cosmic stakes, no triplet, no
archaising imperative.
