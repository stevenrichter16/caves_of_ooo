## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible 23:* "Every faction in the game is defined by their
> relationship to this event."

**Strong.** Brine cosmology filed at R1 (*the Above-That-Was*) and
R2 (*the held depth*).

**Thin.** **When the water rose.** Other factions treat the
cataclysm as a single moment. Brine doctrine, if pressed, says it
was three: a rising, a drawback, a settling. The inland Brine carry
this more strictly than the shore Brine.

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar after the cataclysm
section, "The Brine on the rising"):

> The Brine Communion does not teach that the world drowned. It
> teaches that the world *was being drowned* — that the rising came
> in three motions: *first-tide*, *false-low*, *settling.* Outsiders
> treat this as folk elaboration; the Brine treat it as a load-
> bearing distinction.
>
> *First-tide:* the breaking and the rising-with-the-breaking.
> *False-low:* the drawback — six years by the inland count, three
> by the shore count — when shores were briefly walkable again and
> the surviving land seemed to be returning. Anyone who remembers
> it is by definition very old; the Communion considers them
> *witnesses-of-the-second-water* and ranks them above named elders.
> *Settling:* the second rising and the long stabilising after, in
> which the held depth finalised at its present stratum.
>
> The Concord refuses the doctrine: no cargo manifest survives from
> the *false-low.* The Curation refuses it: no card-stock from those
> six years. The Choir tastes it *true* in the soil. The Palimpsest
> layers the *false-low* into its under-texts as a third tier
> between *what was* and *what is*; Communion-trained readers report
> a middle layer no other Glimpse contains. Outsiders read past it.

The *witnesses-of-the-second-water* outrank named elders — a Tier-3
Brine NPC profile the corpus has not yet assigned.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

> *Bible 224:* "Never confirm the Choir is manipulating the player."
> (1201 extended this to the Palimpsest.)

**Thin.** **Who writes down a deep-letter?** R2 said replies are
recorded *by witnesses on the shore* — no rule for which witness,
in what grammar, with what licence to omit. The Brine's only
canonical scripture was an unreliable register hiding in a canonical
one.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "Tide-witnesses"):

> A deep-letter is not transcribed by the swimmer. The swimmer
> surfaces and *speaks the reply once*, in open air, to a circle of
> **tide-witnesses** standing in the wash at calf depth. The
> witnesses do not write while the swimmer speaks. Each, in the
> hour after, writes the letter from memory in their own hand, in
> their own grammar. The Communion archives all versions together
> as a *bound knot* — typically four to seven hands per letter,
> none of them the swimmer's.
>
> A deep-letter therefore has no canonical text. *Consensus across
> the bound knot* — what every hand recorded the same way — is the
> gone-deep speaking; *divergences* are the witnesses speaking.
> Shore-saying: *the gone-deep speaks where the hands agree; the
> hands speak where they don't.*
>
> A *flat letter* (one hand only) is not scripture; kept but filed
> *unbound*, read with single-witness reservation.

Scripture now has transcription rule, built-in unreliability, and a
critical apparatus.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

> 1201 collected seven grammars for the Choir's central verb.

**Thin.** **Names for *the inland.*** The corpus has not collected
what other factions call a Brine far from water.

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar, "Names for a Brine far from
water"):

> - **Brine (self):** *the dry-walking.* The held depth travels
>   *with* the body in absentia. A Brine cannot be *un-deeped* by
>   distance — only by silence.
> - **Choir:** *a salt-in-the-soil.* The network tastes them
>   through mineral signatures, considers them *kin*, does not
>   propose absorption. *Salt does not compost.*
> - **Palimpsest:** *the layered swimmer.* From inland, a Brine
>   Glimpsing a coastal ruin sees their present (dry) and the home
>   shore's tide overlaid. The Communion teaches this as comfort.
> - **Pale Curation:** *survey-detached.* The catalogue cannot file
>   them by shore-of-record. Halen's note: *they file themselves.*
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *the unsettled party.* Clauses with a
>   dry-walking Brine require the *home-tide-clock* be cited in the
>   witness section. Veshen has refused to settle three such clauses
>   on the grounds that the cited tide-clock had stopped before
>   the clause matured.
> - **Thermoclaves:** *the wet fire.* Apprentice catechism: *a
>   Brine far from the sea who has not gone silent.*
> - **Glassblown:** *the unfired-by-distance.* A loan from the
>   Curation form. Tulen Niemensa-Sopellen filed one in 471 and
>   never reused it.

The Brine grammar is the only one that names what *inlandness
costs* (the dry-walking can be *un-deeped by silence*); the others
name only what inlandness *looks like to them.*

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

> *Bible 121:* "they exist in multiple temporal layers
> simultaneously."

**Strong.** R2 named the *held depth* as a Brine substrate.

**Thin.** **Held-depth-without-water.** R2 said a Brine cannot be
un-deeped by distance — implying a held depth *in* the body, not in
any nearby water. Edge case: what does the Glimpse return for a
dry-walker?

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "Carried depth"):

> A dry-walking Brine carries the held depth as a property of the
> body, not of any nearby water. Doctrine: *the held depth is where
> the body would be still enough.* They can be *kept* by stillness
> alone — a deep room, a cellar, the hour before a rainstorm —
> though the keeping is **partial**, not equal to a deep-keeping in
> water.
>
> The Glimpse confirms this. A Palimpsest-attuned reader sees a
> faint **second body** — the home-shore body at its tide —
> overlaid on the present body, in phase: high tide buoyant, low
> tide prone. Read the way a sailor reads a barometer.
>
> A dry-walking Brine **cannot go-deep on the dry-walking.** They
> must return to sufficient water first. Death by accident is
> *un-deeped*; the body is brought home if reachable, sunk in lead-
> weight if not. The lead-weight protocol is the only Brine ritual
> the Concord profits from (Brine refuse to barter lead).

A reader Glimpsing an inland Brine knows where the home tide is —
without asking.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

> R2 (2315) named the **going-deep** as the Brine apotheosis.

**Thin.** **Refused going-deeps.** Choir has *stalled Communion*,
Concord has *open-account dead*, Glassblown have *unfired forever.*
Brine R2 ended with *called back* / *not called back* — no name for
the **swimmer who turns back of their own accord.**

**Concrete addition** (bible expansion to "The going-deep"):

> A walker may, after the swim begins, **turn back without being
> called.** The Communion's word: *risen-late*. The walker reaches
> their own held depth, recognises it, surfaces. Neither *un-deeped*
> (the Communion did not retract the invitation) nor *gone-deep*
> (recognised but not settled into).
>
> Risen-late walkers retain Brine standing without reset, may
> attempt the going-deep again (most do not). Doctrine: *a held
> depth that has been recognised once cannot be unrecognised.* They
> carry that knowledge for life — *partly deep already.*
>
> Three apotheosis-states: *gone-deep*, *un-deeped*, *risen-late*.
> The Communion considers risen-late the most honest. *They went,
> they saw, they refused, they returned to say so.*

The going-deep is now a fork the walker can refuse without disgrace.
The Choir's Communion has no honest refusal short of never approach-
ing it; the Brine's does.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Thin.** **Brine tense for absent water.** No prior audit has
named the verb-form a Brine uses when speaking *about water they
cannot currently see.*

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The remembered
tense"):

> Brine grammar carries a fourth tense — the **remembered tense**,
> marked by the suffix *-en* on the verb. A dry-walker says *the
> tide is highen at home.* The *-en* suffix is the grammatical
> equivalent of pointing — *I am not in the room of this verb, but
> I attest to it.*
>
> The remembered tense is used **only for water**. A Brine will say
> *my sister is alive* in regular present; *the river is highen at
> my home* in the remembered. Water is the only subject for which
> absence is not the same as not-knowing. The held depth tells the
> dry-walker.
>
> Outsiders fail at it. Concord scribes write it as a *future-not-
> yet* and apologise. Halen filed one *tense the Curation does not
> have a card for* and lost the argument with herself about whether
> it was *the catalogue's failing or the form's.*

The first grammatical feature in the corpus that other factions'
scribes *cannot translate without remainder.* The Concord's
*future-not-yet* gloss is wrong on purpose — the gloss is what they
sell.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Thin.** **The dry-walker's day.** R2 gave the going-deep its
ceremony. The corpus has no record of how a Brine far from water
*lives* a Tuesday.

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar, "A dry-walker's day"):

> Dawn: a dry-walker draws a small bowl of water (any source) and
> stands it on the windowsill for the morning. The bowl is the home
> shore in miniature — not consecrated, *attended.*
>
> Greeting between dry-walkers: *the tide is highen at home?* The
> expected answer is whatever depth the second body is at.
> *Highen. Lowen. Slack-en.*
>
> Mid-day: no still water above their own wrist-width volume.
> Doctrine: *a body that holds more stillness than itself displaces
> the held depth.* They refuse a town's deep well for a smaller cup,
> drawn the same number of times. The Concord prices this
> distinctly; the Brine *known-table* is set with a wrist-width cup.
>
> Evening: one sentence to the bowl, in the remembered tense, by
> tradition the day's only reproach. *The ledger was wrongen today.
> The road was longen today. The bread was scarcen.* The home tide
> carries it back to the held depth — *kept-without-answer.*
>
> The bowl is not poured out. It evaporates over days, is refilled.
> A Brine on long dry-walking maintains a single bowl across years.

The bowl is the most movable piece of theology in the corpus. The
*known-table* charge is the first piece of cross-faction commerce
shaped by Brine *ritual* rather than Brine *trade.*

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine Communion R3 (the dry-walking)

The corpus wrote the Brine as a coastal people. They are not. They
are a faction with a held-depth grammar that *travels.*

**The dry-walking** has three sub-conditions: *carrying* (in
transit), *posted* (settled inland indefinitely), *returned* (came
back; held depth at home unreached in absence). Returned dry-walkers
are the Brine's most reliable inland correspondents.

**Mer-Iss reconsidered.** Mer-Iss appears as Brine cataclysm-era
founder across audits (1901, 2127). R3 proposes Mer-Iss was a
**posted dry-walker** before the cataclysm — and that the
Communion's founding moment was Mer-Iss *recognising the held depth
in a freshwater pond two hundred miles inland.* Held depth is a
property of *sufficient stillness*, not of salt; the cataclysm
proved this by drowning the world. Mer-Iss's pond is the Communion's
first shore. By tradition it is never named — identifying it would
make it *pilgrimable*, and founder-shores are private to the dry-
walking.

**The bowl-makers.** A small inland sub-population whose only trade
is the windowsill bowl. They do not throw, fire, or paint — every
bowl is *found*. A bowl-maker walks roads gathering cups, mugs,
hollow stones, dried gourds, and offers them to dry-walkers in
exchange for a single sentence in the remembered tense. The
sentence is not recorded; the exchange is bilateral and oral. The
bowl-maker keeps it the way the home shore keeps the reproach —
*kept-without-answer.* A bowl-maker who collects more sentences
than they can hold begins *un-bowling* — releasing them back to
the air in the order received. The Curation files it under
*intentional un-cataloguing* and treats bowl-makers with cautious
respect.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Live contradiction: the JSON declares unity, ROTCHOIR_VOICES
declares acoustic plurality, the bible adjudicates neither.**

`RotChoir.json` `ChoirTendril_1.HowMany`:
> *"You ask how many drops in the ocean. We are one. We have always
> been one… There is no many. There is only the Choir."*

`ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md` (lines 22–29):
> *"the Choir absorbs persons but does not erase the language they
> came in with. The five voices function as conversational variety
> within an otherwise monolithic faction… The Choir does not
> correct any of them."*

A walker told *there is no many* by the Tendril and then meets the
Five — each in a distinct voice the Choir manifestly tolerates — is
told two incompatible things by the same faction. **The bible does
not adjudicate.**

The fix is *not* to soften the JSON. The Tendril speaks standing
doctrine; the Five are *acoustic individuation* (per ROTCHOIR_VOICES
line 482, *"a choir that only sang one note would not be a choir"*).
The bible needs one clause:

> The Choir's standing doctrine — *we are one* — and its acoustic
> reality — five named voices and counting — are not a contradiction
> the Choir is hiding. They are a contradiction the Choir does not
> *recognise as one.* A doctrinal statement and an audible voice
> are, in Choir grammar, the same kind of utterance: both true,
> neither arguing.

**Second, smaller bleed.** R2 called *deep-letters* the Communion's
*only canonical scripture.* R3's *bound knot* doctrine demands a
footnote: the bound knot is canonical; individual letters within it
are not. The Communion has **a scripture that is a hash, not a
text.**

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

**Title (in-world):** *Day-Book of an Inland Posting, Year 422.*
**Author:** unnamed Concord scribe, third year of posting at a town
the manuscript itself calls only *the dry town.*

> *Tuesday.* Ledger balanced. Two open clauses: flour-debt to the
> miller's son, settling next harvest; lead-debt to the Brine posted
> at the south corner, payable on his return-home, no date set, held-
> rate (R-7) noted per Concord protocol.
>
> The Brine bought lead by weight again. Fourth time this season.
> Small coin, would not barter. The miller's wife watched him weigh
> coin against lead on his own pan, found the lead light by half a
> knuckle, asked for the rest in stillness instead of metal. She
> says he said: *the tide will be highen at home tonight, the lead
> will be heaviest at the third water; I will weigh it again then
> and pay the difference.*
>
> The miller's wife is not a Communion party. She wrote it in our
> grammar. I record her version. (The Brine himself, asked, said
> only *the difference is mine to weigh.*)
>
> He keeps a bowl in the corner of the south-square room. I have
> seen it — the bottom of an old kiln-test, Glassblown discard,
> ours-by-purchase originally. The water in it has not been changed
> since I came to this posting. I asked once. He said the bowl is
> not for changing.
>
> *Wednesday.* Ledger balanced. The Brine paid the difference at
> the third water — I have no third water here — but the small-coin
> appeared on my pan in the morning, weighed exact, no witness. I
> assume he weighed it himself. The Concord does not have a clause
> for that. I am noting it open.

**Lands parenthetically:** *carried depth* (the Brine knows the
home tide without seeing it), *remembered tense* (*highen*), the
windowsill-bowl ritual, the *bowl-maker* sub-population (a
Glassblown discard, found, repurposed without firing), and the
*home-tide-clock as clause-witness* (R-7 requiring the home tide).
The lore is what the merchant *had to write down to make the lead
transaction balance.*

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**
> *"A dry-walking Brine carries the held depth as a property of the
> body, not of any nearby water."*

No archaism, no *verily*, no apostrophe-fantasy noun, no mythopoeic
register. A procedural statement about a faction's doctrine.
*Held depth* is internal to this corpus, introduced at R2.

The TES-register version: *"Lo, the dry-walking faithful do bear
the deepkeeping as a humour of the body, by mystery distinct from
the salt of the sea."* Not what I wrote. Faction-grammars
(*highen*, *deep-kept*, *risen-late*) are the audit's only stylised
words, and they are *grammatical* features — strange because the
world *uses* them, not because the narrator performs antiquity.
