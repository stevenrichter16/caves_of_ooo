## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible, line 21:* "the way text on a medieval manuscript was scraped
> off and written over with the old words still faintly visible
> underneath."

**Strong.** Cataclysm has eight nameable accounts; the *aftermath* and
the *twenty after the after* (the smoke-born, sibling 2315) are
stratified.

**Thin.** **The Thermoclave's pre-cataclysm count.** The Thermoclaves
say the cataclysm is the 51st fire. The corpus has never specified
*what counts as a fire by their reckoning before written count began.*

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "The unwritten fires"):**

> The Thermoclaves teach that the fifty fires before the
> cataclysm-fire are not counted the way the post-cataclysm fires
> are. Post-fires are *kept*: each keeper inherits the count, in
> writing or in voice. Pre-fires are *remembered.* A kept fire is
> in the count; a remembered fire is in the lineage.
>
> No living Thermoclave can produce a remembered-fire above the
> twenty-third. Beyond that, the count thins into *ember-names* —
> a name only, no place, no keeper, no quench-tide. The ember-names
> are recited at apprenticeship, in an order the apprentice may not
> learn the meaning of for ten more years. *The order is the
> meaning.* The Choir, asked to taste the recital, said: *most of
> these names are not yet soil.*

Pre-cataclysm time stratifies into **kept**, **remembered**, and
**ember-named**.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Charter Receipt, R-7, redactions, forgery, time-release
annotations, lossy-scribe transcripts (2315), Q.14 removed (2315).

**Thin.** **The collectively-authored document.** Every artifact in
the corpus has a single author or amanuensis. No document whose
authorship is diffused by design.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "The fire-tally"):**

> A Thermoclave **fire-tally** is a document no one writes. Each
> keeper, on receiving the keep, makes a single mark — a vertical
> stroke struck through with one of seven cross-marks (full quench,
> partial quench, relit, relit-twice, gone-cold, ember-only,
> untended). Fuel and stroke-angle are the keeper's signature, but
> illegibly: the keeper is not asked to name themself.
>
> A century-old tally signed by forty hands reads as one hand: the
> **fire's hand.** Asked who wrote a tally, a Thermoclave answers
> *the fire did.* This is not poetic. A keeper who wrote a sentence
> beyond the cross-mark would be *out of grammar* — used the way
> the Concord uses *un-receipted*.
>
> The Curation has filed three tallies as accession objects under a
> special intake form: *witness unspecified by document convention.*
> The form is the Curation's first standing admission that some
> documents are not authored by persons. R-7 accepted it. The
> Director did not.

The corpus's first document that says no one wrote it.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** Names for cataclysm, dead, joining, apotheosis, debt,
founder, promise.

**Thin.** **Names for *ash* / what is left when something has
finished.**

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "What each faction calls the
remainder"):**

> - **Choir:** *humus.* Not a residue at all in their grammar — a
>   *station.*
> - **Palimpsest:** *the lower margin.* Written below the last
>   line. Often the truer text.
> - **Pale Curation:** *the after-fact.* Filed in a junior cabinet;
>   no card closes without one.
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *the held remainder.* What a settled
>   account did not consume. Held against future accounts; not
>   *priced.*
> - **Brine Communion:** *the un-deeped fragment.* The piece of an
>   un-deeped body that did sink. Treated with the dignity of the
>   whole, separately.
> - **Thermoclaves:** *the kept ash.* The difference between a fire
>   that ended and a fire that was *let* to end. Stored in the
>   keeper's pouch.
> - **Glassblown:** *the unfired.* The sand that was almost a
>   vessel and is, in their teaching, more itself than the vessel.

*Kept ash* and *un-deeped fragment* are the same noun in two
grammars: *the part that did not finish but is not abandoned.*

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Five strata: material (Choir), temporal (Palimpsest),
consensual + standing (Concord), held-depth (Brine).

**Thin.** **Thermoclave substrate-claim.** They have grammar, fires,
count — no claim to a *layer of the world*.

**Concrete addition (bible expansion, "The ember-floor"):**

> The Thermoclave substrate-claim is the **ember-floor** — the
> stratum at which heat ceases to radiate outward and begins to
> radiate downward, into the substrate itself. Heat above warms;
> heat below *keeps.* A fire kept long enough acquires an ember-
> floor of its own; the fire's own ground becomes warmer than the
> surrounding rock and stays so for a measured period after the
> fire goes out.
>
> A *quench-tide* is the moment the fire's ember-floor meets the
> substrate's ember-floor and the two become the same floor. The
> fire is *kept* not because the keeper carried the count forward
> but because the substrate accepted the heat. A fire that quenches
> without meeting the substrate is *un-kept* — the count is not
> transferred because the count is in the floor, not the keeper.
>
> The Brine call this the dry version of *deep-keeping.* The Choir
> reads warm rock as *patient soil* and consider the ember-floor a
> Choir feature the Thermoclaves merely tend. The Palimpsest reads
> three known ember-floors as *layers of unsigned text* — the fire-
> keeping registers as authorship the substrate participated in.
> Per Dim 2: the fire is the author; the substrate is the page.

Sixth stratum named. Each secondary faction now has a substrate-claim.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** Communion, Transcription, Settled-Zero, retirement-into-
cabinet, unfiring, going-deep. Five of six codified.

**Thin.** **Thermoclave apotheosis.** Sibling 2127 teased *the long
quench* without canonising it.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "The long quench"):**

> A walker at highest Thermoclave reputation is invited to the
> **long quench** — a rite at which the walker tends a fire alone
> for a duration the keepers will not specify. They are given the
> fire's tally, the fuel allotted, and one instruction: *let the
> fire end on its own grammar.* They may not be relieved or relight.
>
> If the fire reaches an ember-floor under the walker's keeping,
> the walker has *long-quenched.* Their personal heat is, in
> doctrine, *transferred to the substrate.* The walker remains
> physically alive but reads at ambient temperature to any test the
> keepers can devise. They become a **floor-keeper**: a Thermoclave
> who keeps no fire of their own but tends the warmth in the rock
> beneath the keepers' fires. Floor-keepers speak in a fourth
> verb-aspect, *grounded*, used elsewhere only of the substrate.
>
> Communion surrenders self; Transcription, presence; Settled-Zero,
> price; unfiring, shape; going-deep, surface; long quench,
> **warmth.** Six apotheoses, six surrenders, no overlap.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Choir pronouns, Palimpsest tense, Brine joining,
Thermoclave fire-grammar, Glassblown unsaid mood, Curation
catalogued mood, Concord trade tense, the assayer's hand-signs.

**Thin.** **Thermoclave count-verbs.** Earlier audits noted that
counting fires is grammatical, but the *grammar of counting* (as
opposed to the *aspects of fire-life*) was never specified.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "Count-verbs"):**

> Thermoclave numerals are a closed set: one through ten, plus
> *zero-fire* (extinct, still in lineage) and *the untended* (the
> tenth-and-lost, cataclysm marker). When applied to a fire, the
> numeral is **inflected**. With *fifth*: *Fifthing* (currently
> fifth); *Fifth-was* (was fifth in the previous count, before the
> chain shifted); *Fifth-coming* (the count expects this fire to
> become fifth by the next quench; speculative, never
> load-bearing); *Fifth-out* (was fifth, is now zero-fire; used in
> elegy).
>
> The **untended** has no inflected forms. The Thermoclaves use
> this as grammatical justification for *not having reckoned* the
> cataclysm: it cannot be *was-untended* or *coming-untended.* A
> tally entry on the untended cannot register a cross-mark; the
> entry is an empty stroke. Compare the Curation's *intentional
> blank* (sibling 2225).

The *intentional blank* parallel makes the Curation–Thermoclave
relationship the corpus's first cross-faction *grammar of the
deliberately empty*.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Strong.** Tuesday Mornings, cross-Tuesday market, stranger-meeting
protocols, Curation initiation, Quiet Hour, Concord weddings,
Closing of Accounts, Brine committal.

**Thin.** **Thermoclave meeting-rite.** They have an initiation
(*first quench*) and a funeral-equivalent (*long quench*) but no
specified protocol when a stranger arrives at camp.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "The cold-coming"):**

> A stranger arriving at a Thermoclave camp is **cold-coming.**
> The host assesses the stranger's *current heat* and seats them
> at a fire whose **count-position** matches: warm-bodied at
> *first* or *second* (young fires, willing to receive heat);
> ambient at *fourth* or *fifth* (middle of the chain, neutral);
> cold (grief, illness, post-quench) at *seventh* through *ninth*
> (older fires with ember-floor, which *give* heat without
> requiring its return).
>
> No stranger is seated at the tenth-and-lost. A stranger colder
> than a ninth-fire is *floor-temperature*, recognised on sight
> and seated on the rock between fires, where the keeping is
> silent.
>
> No food is offered until the seating is rendered. To offer food
> first would be to assess the stranger as *fuel* — the Thermoclave
> equivalent of the Concord's *un-receipted*: a procedural insult
> that cannot be repaired by the offering party.
>
> Tide-Reader Nesh, on her one visit, was seated at a ninth-fire.
> She did not contest. The keepers, asked, said: *the deep-letters
> are colder than we are.*

The heat-assessment reads a Brine going-deep correspondent as
ninth-fire-cold — the corpus's first cross-faction *cold* metric.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclaves (round 2)

**Proposed senior figure: Keeper Suul of the Ninth.**

Forty-seven years a keeper. Suul tends the **ninth fire** — the
oldest active fire in the present count, ember-floor four
generations deep. Tending the ninth is the most *quiet* of the
nine keepings, and accordingly the most senior.

Suul was apprenticed at fourteen to a keeper named **Erro** — a
fact the Thermoclaves rarely speak. Erro tended the **tenth fire**
at the moment of its quench; Erro did not survive. The tenth has
been *the untended* in every tally since. Suul is the only living
keeper apprenticed by a keeper *whose fire is no longer in the
chain* — in Thermoclave grammar, *lineage-orphaned*: taught a
count she cannot pass on under her teacher's name. She is the
corpus's first named Thermoclave and the first character defined
by relationship to the cataclysm-fire.

Suul has refused twice to *re-number* the chain — once when the
third fire quenched (juniors wanted to renumber down; she refused:
*the untended is not in the count, it is not moved when the count
moves*), once when an apprentice asked whether *zero-fire* could
be inflected (*zero is the only numeral that does not move*). Both
refusals are precedent.

Suul has met Tide-Reader Nesh once. She seated Nesh at the ninth-
fire (Dim 7) and they spoke for one hour. The Curation accession
card filed from a third-party witness reads: *both senior figures
used their own grammars; neither translated; both understood; the
witness did not.*

Veshen is preparing a letter to Suul. It has been on her desk
sixty-one days and reads, in its entirety: *Senior Keeper, the
alloys you do not forge are nevertheless owed to the chain.* Veshen
does not know whether *owed* registers across trade tense and count
grammar. Suul does not know the letter exists. If a walker carries
it to her, *owed* will resolve as *coming-fifth* or *fifth-out*,
and the substrate-ledger will acquire its first non-Concord
co-author.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a)** Bible:115 — the Choir is *"not in a hurry."* Combined with
2315's claim that Choir patience is *targeted*, the **ember-floor**
introduces a third clock. The ninth fire's ember-floor is four
generations deep — sixty years roughly. **Recommend the
Choir-patience sidebar acquire a third entry:**

> The Choir's patience is calibrated against three exhaustions: the
> Brine's long-overdue tide, the Concord's alloy crisis, and the
> Thermoclave ember-floor that has not yet sunk to the Choir's
> depth. The Choir composts the heat-substrate from below; the
> keepers tend it from above. The two slow processes intersect at a
> date neither party has named.

**(b)** RotChoir.json:338 — *"The Glassblown Remnant offers nothing
at all."* The Glassblown have unfiring, the unsaid mood, the unfired
remainder. The Choir's "nothing" is grammatical, not factual.
**Recommend the Choir Implementation Rules add:**

> The Choir's vocabulary recognises only what can be composted.
> *Nothing* in the Choir's mouth is *unmetabolisable.* The
> Glassblown's unfired sand is, in Choir grammar, *nothing*; in
> Thermoclave grammar, unburnt fuel; in Concord grammar, an
> uncategorised commodity. The Choir's name is the most categorical
> and the least informative.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

A document *carried on the wrong body* — paying off Dim 8.

**Title (in-game):** *Pocketbook recovered from a Concord
caravan-master, intake card R-19. Marginalia attributed to a
Thermoclave fire-keeper of unknown camp.*

```
[Small leather-bound book, palm-sized. Forty pages of pencil in
 trade-tense — caravan inventories, weights, priced-clauses. On
 six pages, in a different hand, in charcoal: marginalia. The
 marginalia is in count-grammar.]

  [Page 14, Concord:] Eighth caravan from the Coastal Embayment.
   Iron, raw: open ledger pending Veshen's read.
  [Page 14, charcoal margin:] the iron is fifth-coming. let it come.

  [Page 22, Concord:] Caravan-master to seek a fire on the high
   pass. Cold night expected. No Thermoclave camp on standing
   inventory.
  [Page 22, charcoal margin:] we were here. the camp does not
   file. you are seventh-fire tonight.

  [Page 31, Concord:] Lost fuel — half-bundle of pitch-pine.
   Campfire over-fed by junior. Refile.
  [Page 31, charcoal margin:] the over-feeding was the keeping.
   the bundle was for the fire, not for the inventory. zero-out
   the loss. the fire kept.

  [Page 33, Concord:] The fire-keeper does not name themself. The
   keeper writes in our book without permission. The keeper does
   not seem to consider this writing.
  [Page 33, charcoal margin:] it is not. the fire wrote. you
   carried.

  [Page 38, Concord:] On reflection: settle the loss against the
   warmth received. No counter-rate to be raised.
  [Page 38, charcoal margin:] closed-up. fifth-out. kept ash in
   the pouch with this book — take it to a junior keeper at the
   next camp. they will know.

  [Page 39, Concord:] Keeper Suul, of the Ninth Fire (per a
   passing peddler) — the kept ash is yours. Returning.
   Caravan-master, R.

  [Inside back cover, a small fold of leather containing a thumb's
   worth of grey ash. Curation intake notes the ash is at ambient
   temperature; the leather is faintly warm.]
```

The bombshell is **the leather is faintly warm.** Per Dim 4: the
*kept ash* of a properly-quenched fire retains heat below the
ambient temperature of the matter that holds it. The pocketbook,
in Concord hands, has been a thermal artifact the Concord could not
classify. The caravan-master settled the loss not because the trade
tense permitted it but because they were holding warm leather and
could not say why.

Three substrates share one object: Concord priced-clause overlays
Thermoclave count-grammar overlays Choir substrate-reading. R-7 has
filed it under *witness unspecified by document convention* with a
senior-only annotation: *the document is also the witness.* Suul
holds the matching ash on her person, in a pouch she has worn
twenty-three years. Matched, the two ashes are warm together.
Concord doctrine has no clause for *warm together.* Veshen's unsent
letter is, in this light, an attempt to pre-write one.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"A fire kept long enough acquires an ember-floor of its own; the
> fire's own ground becomes warmer than the surrounding rock and
> stays so for a measured period after the fire goes out."*

No archaisms, no apostrophe-fantasy names, no Aedra/Daedra/Towers/
CHIM/Et'Ada. Three plain present-tense clauses. The doctrinal
claim is mechanical: *a fire's ground becomes warmer than
surrounding rock and stays so for a measured period.* A TES book
would have written *"And lo, the Hearth-Wardens do hold the ancient
teaching that the well-kept flame leaveth a warm wound in the bones
of the earth, which warmth abideth a measured span after the flame's
last breath."* Mine has no *lo, hearth-wardens, ancient teaching,
warm wound, abideth, last breath.* The register is operational, not
invoked. *Measured period* is named to a junior, not a god.
Confirmed.
