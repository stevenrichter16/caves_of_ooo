## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (1301 §2.1):** *"The Thermoclaves do not name it at all
— they consider naming a form of ownership and the event
ownerless."*

**Strong.** Best line in Thermoclave canon — gives the body a
*position* on the cataclysm without giving it a *word*.

**Thin.** The position is asserted but never *shown* in
operation. No canonised Thermoclave utterance has refused a
name in mid-speech, deflected a Curator's elicitation, etc.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm, append):**

> *A Thermoclave hearth-keeper, asked by a Curation cataloguer
> for the body's name for the cataclysm, conventionally replies
> by gesture: a brief downward palm above the hearth, working-
> name* hands-off. *The gesture means, in their working-
> register: the question is a hand reaching toward what should
> not be touched. It is not a rude reply; it is the only reply
> the body recognises as coherent. Curators have repeatedly
> attempted to file the gesture as* "[hands off] (untranslated)"
> *— but the Thermoclave working-rule is that the gesture is
> not a translation of a name; it is the position the body
> holds in place of one. A filed transcription is, by their
> reading, the Curation re-performing the cataclysm at small
> scale.*

Falsifies an NPC-behaviour rule and lands the deeper claim:
every act of representation is itself the wound the cataclysm
opened. Counterposes the Curation's *not-unsigning* (0838 §2.4)
directly — productive friction (§4).

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Thin.** Thermoclaves have **no canonised authored documents**.
Five other factions have prototypes; the Thermoclaves have
none. §2.4 below makes the absence doctrinal.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Thermoclaves subsection):**

> *The Thermoclaves do not author texts. The body's working-
> rule against authored documents is the same rule that governs
> its answer to the cataclysm: a written record is a hand-on-
> the-thing in absence of the thing, and the thing's absence is
> the wound the body refuses to repeat. They do keep a ledger,
> but the ledger-form is not text. It is the **hearth-line**:
> the unbroken sequence of hand-tendings on a single fire,
> recorded as scratch-marks on the stoker's iron, one mark per
> tending shift. A senior hearth's stoker is so densely
> scratched it reads, to a Curator, as worked-out fish scale.
> The marks do not encode anything an outsider can recover;
> they encode, to the next keeper, the cadence of the previous
> keeper's hand. A keeper relieving a shift reads the stoker's
> last fingerwidth the way a Curator reads a working-shelf
> draft: as instruction in someone else's working-mode. The
> reading is in rhythm, not in symbols.*

Lands the body's authorship-mode as **paratextual-only-on-the-
working-tool**, no transcribable surface. §5 takes the obvious
artifact-shape: an outsider's *failure to read* the stoker.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (1301 §3):** *"To let one go cold is the only action
they treat as serious wrong."*

**Strong.** Single-line ethic. Falsifiable.

**Thin.** No floor. *Refusal of what?*

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Thermoclaves, new):**

> *The Thermoclaves teach that the cataclysm — which they will
> not name — was, at root, a refusal that failed: a body
> somewhere refused to keep its hand on what it was tending,
> and the consequence was a world in which every other hand has
> since had to find something to hold on to in compensation.
> The Curation holds onto signature; the Choir onto
> incorporation; the Palimpsest onto memory; the Concord onto
> clauses; the Glassblown onto finishing; the Communion onto
> the next anchor. The Thermoclaves regard all six holds as
> elaborate forms of the same act —* representation*: a hand
> on a thing-by-proxy. The body's doctrinal position is that
> representation is the wound, not the salve. Their **Refusal-
> hearth** is therefore not "a hearth that refuses" but* the
> hearth at which refusal is performed *— performed by the
> body's continued, unbroken, non-symbolic tending of an actual
> fire. A hearth tended for four hundred years is, in their
> reading, the only honest gesture any of the seven bodies has
> made since the cataclysm: a hand on a thing, not a hand on a
> representation of a thing. It does not* mean *anything. It*
> is *something. The distinction is the entire doctrine.*
>
> *The body's only canonised utterance about itself, repeated
> when the question is forced:* "We are not a faction. We are
> the people whose fire has not gone out."

Hard cosmology. Falsifiable in dialogue: a Thermoclave who
agrees their tending is a *discipline* is, by the body's rule,
already speaking outside doctrine. Counterposes the other six
factions in a single clause each. Sets up §2.5 directly.

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Thin.** No canonised Thermoclave reading of either ending.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Central Question, append):**

> *The Thermoclaves' reading of the two endings is the shortest
> of the seven. A Communion-walker has* let the hearth go cold
> *— not a particular hearth, but the hearth that is the
> walker's body itself, the only fire any of us has ever had
> direct custody of. A Transcription-walker has done the same,
> by a different route: the body is now a representation,
> readable, citable, and therefore not a hearth. The body
> regards both endings as the same wrong it regards any cooled
> hearth as. It has no rite for either and refuses to construct
> one. A Communion-walker or a Transcription-walker is, in
> Thermoclave reading, simply someone whose fire is now out.
> The next shift's keeper does not mention them; the stoker's
> scratch-marks continue uninterrupted. The continuation is the
> body's refusal of either ending's claim to be the answer.*

Counterposes the Curation's *cessation-as-entry* (0838 §2.5):
the Curation files the absence; the Thermoclaves refuse to
file *or* mark it. Two factions, two negative-acts; the
Curation's is administrative, the Thermoclaves' is
non-administrative.

### 2.6 Linguistic texture

**Thin.** No Thermoclave NPC has spoken on screen.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Thermoclaves, append):**

> *Thermoclave working-speech follows a single rule: verbs are
> present-continuous, the natural subject is the tending hand,
> the address is second-person. A keeper instructing a junior
> says* "you keep it just so" *or* "you watch the embers
> settle." *Asked about last winter:* "you tend through it.
> the wind is the same wind." *The grammar is not stylistic.
> The sentence's subject is whoever has the hand on the stoker
> at the moment of the sentence; the listener is, in the body's
> reading, also a tending hand, addressed equivalently.
> Outsiders consistently misread the speech as instruction; the
> body finds the misreading harmless. This is also the body's
> working-rule against the first person. A Thermoclave who
> answers with* "I" *is briefly outside doctrine. The body does
> not correct the lapse — correction would be representation —
> but the keeper will, on the next stoker-mark, scratch a
> single transverse stroke. The stroke means: a sentence in
> the wrong grammar happened here. A senior stoker carries
> dozens. A first-shift stoker is almost entirely transverse
> strokes. Reading the dwindling rate is the only longitudinal
> assessment the body performs of a keeper's working-life.*

Falsifies a speech-rule and a longitudinal-assessment form in
one stroke. Three factions now have canonised speech-rules
(Curation indirect-free, Glassblown complete-sentence,
Thermoclave second-person-continuous), each with a failure-mode.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclaves

> ### The hearth-quarter (anthropology)
>
> A hearth-quarter is a low circular building, stone-walled and
> unwindowed, organised around a central fire-pit — the
> **hearth-stand** — holding a single continuous fire that is
> the quarter's only light and only heat. The roof is
> corbelled; smoke leaves through a high vent called the
> **breath-hole**. Seating is a single ring of stone benches
> against the inner wall, facing the fire. No working-benches,
> no shelves, no tables, no records of any writeable kind. The
> quarter contains one tool — the **stoker**, an iron rod
> whose handle accumulates the hearth-line's scratch-marks
> (§2.2). It is never carried out.
>
> Adjoining is the **public hall** — a separate stone building,
> larger, windowed, furnished with benches and a small
> standing-table for outsiders. The body conducts trade there,
> hosts Curator filing-visits, accepts Communion shipments,
> refuses Concord clause-bindings. The line between hall and
> hearth-quarter is the **hearth-line** — a doorway-threshold,
> stone-cut, single-step. The doctrinal rule (0838 §2.3) is
> that no representation crosses it: not a stamped Curation
> document, a Concord clause-token, a Communion ship-log, a
> Choir spore-pouch, a Palimpsest annotation, a Glassblown
> finished cooling. The hearth-quarter is the only space in
> the seven bodies' shared world in which **representation is
> held off by architecture**.
>
> The two buildings share a single load-bearing wall. The body
> considers this deliberate: the working-form for *the body
> refuses to mediate, but does not refuse to neighbour*. A
> hearth-quarter without an adjoining public hall is not a
> working hearth-quarter.
>
> ### The hearth-keeper's career
>
> A keeper enters as **first-shift** — no junior/senior
> gradient, no number, no name appended; permitted only the
> tending. After a year of dawn-shifts the body considers them
> **second-shift**: permitted, on rotation, to step across the
> hearth-line into the public hall and answer outsiders'
> questions about the working-quarter (never about the
> cataclysm — *hands-off*, §2.1). Second-shift holds
> indefinitely until the senior keepers, by silent consensus,
> begin including them in the lighting-from-old. A keeper so
> included is **third-shift**: the senior cadre.
>
> A keeper retires by the rite called **hands-off**: at dawn,
> with a first-shift keeper witnessing, the retiring keeper
> sets the stoker on the hearth-stand's stone rim, performs
> the downward-palm gesture above it, and walks across the
> hearth-line into the public hall. They do not return. The
> first-shift keeper takes up the stoker and continues the
> shift. No closing sentence, no commemorating scratch-mark, no
> ledger entry. The retirement's only record is the absence of
> the retired keeper's hand from the next stoker-mark — and
> by §2.6, the dwindling-then-vanished cadence of that hand is
> the only honest memorial the body recognises.
>
> ### The lighting-from-old (succession)
>
> When a daughter-hearth is established, its first fire is not
> struck. It is **carried**. A third-shift keeper from the
> parent removes the stoker's hot tip from the embers and
> walks, at speed, with the tip glowing, across distance —
> sometimes hours — to the new hearth-quarter, where the tip
> touches kindling. The body's doctrinal claim is that the new
> fire is not a *new* fire; it is the same fire, in a new
> place. A daughter-hearth whose first fire was struck rather
> than carried is *not yet a hearth* — the body will tend it
> but will not consider it part of the hearth-line until the
> next lighting-from-old reaches it from a parent. The
> hearth-line is therefore branching but never founding: every
> working hearth in Thermoclave country traces, by the body's
> reading, to a single original fire whose origin the body
> refuses to name.

Three pieces. The first lands *representation-held-off-by-
architecture* + the load-bearing-wall as the depth-moves. The
second lands a career-arc with *no junior/senior naming-
system*, only shift-positions — deliberate counter to the
Curation's recorder-numbers. The third lands a succession rite
with a metaphysical claim built into its operation.

vs. §2.4.** The 0838 sidebar reads Refusal-hearth as a

the representation/non-representation split. Suggested

reading. The body's own framing is the representation/non-
representation split — see §2.4. The Curation's reading is
canon-correct as a Curation reading, which is a different
status."*

## 5. Merchant's-diary artifact

The audit-pile has six prototypes, five paper-bound. This run
lands the **first wear-pattern artifact**: a Curation
field-cataloguer's notebook page describing a stoker's
scratch-marks the cataloguer is permitted to *look at*, across
the hearth-line, but not to handle. Lands four canon items
parenthetically: §2.2's stoker-as-ledger; §2.4's representation/
non-representation doctrine, observed in the cataloguer's
failure to file what she sees; §2.6's transverse-stroke rule;
and a quiet bombshell about *what the Thermoclaves think
happened on the day before the cataclysm*.

> **Field-Cataloguer's Working Notebook — Pelegrin Auch,
> Recorder 4 of Depot 11. Year 388 since the Severance. Item
> 4–388–07–14–prov.** *(filed under PROVISIONAL/HEARTH-AS-
> RECORD-1)*
>
> *Visited the hearth-quarter at the second basin south of
> Depot 11, by appointment, in the public hall, on the body's
> dawn-rotation. Permitted to view the stoker by stepping no
> further than the hearth-line and looking through the doorway.
> Stoker held by a second-shift keeper whose name I was not
> given (the body's rule against signed witness across the
> hearth-line, the third-shift senior explained). The handle is
> densely scratched across approximately three-fingerwidths.
> The scratches are not symbols — short parallel marks, slight
> angle-variation, struck at the end of each tending shift.
> Asked what the marks recorded, the third-shift senior:* "you
> read the cadence. it isn't here for filing."
>
> *(Her reply began with the second-person, contained no
> first-person reference; recall last winter's Refusal-hearth
> survey. F.M. seventh-bench has flagged this register for
> further notebook attention. Working-meaning, as best I can
> transcribe: the previous shift's cadence is what the next
> shift reads; the marks are not preserved for any other
> purpose; the body does not file them.)*
>
> *(Asked about a single transverse stroke a finger-width back
> from the present mark-edge. Verbatim:* "you scratch one when
> a sentence comes wrong. it doesn't matter whose. you scratch
> it." *I note that this conversation was itself representation;
> by the body's rule, it should not have crossed the
> hearth-line, and indeed it did not — I was outside the
> doorway throughout; the senior was inside.)*
>
> *(One further note. The third-shift senior, before dismissing
> me, observed that the stoker she held had been the same
> stoker for, in her phrasing,* "longer than the day before.
> you don't count back further than that. the day before is
> the only day the marks remember." *I have failed to
> understand this remark and file the failure under
> Auch–388–07–14–prov. I will return on the body's next
> dawn-rotation if invited. I have not yet been invited.)*

The bombshell is the closing parenthetical: *the day before is
the only day the marks remember*. The hearth-line's working-
depth is **one shift back** — the cadence the next keeper
reads is the previous keeper's, no further. Everything else is
wear-pattern, illegible to outsiders. The Thermoclaves'
relationship to history is therefore *the day before* — a
single shift's worth of direct continuity, repeated across
centuries, never accumulated into a recoverable past. The
cataclysm — by this logic — is on the *not-the-day-before*
side of every working hearth, and has been since the first
lighting.

The cataloguer's failure is §2.6's indirect-free rule running
against §2.4's cosmological floor: she can cite, but cannot
interpret without filing, and the body's doctrine forbids the
file. Five conventions in one notebook entry about a visit to
a doorway.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted (§2.4):** *"A hearth tended for four hundred years is,
in their reading, the only honest gesture any of the seven
bodies has made since the cataclysm: a hand on a thing, not a
hand on a representation of a thing."*

Modern register. *In their reading* is contemporary literary-
critical prose; the parallelism is stripped, present-perfect,
plain. No *verily*, no *unto*, no *hath*; *the cataclysm* is a
noun, not a capitalised mythic abstract.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"Lo, a hearth tended these four
hundred years is, in their reckoning, the one true and honest
gesture made by any of the Seven Bodies since the Cataclysm
came: a hand laid upon a thing, and not a hand laid upon the
likeness thereof."* Tells: *Lo*; *these four hundred years*
(archaic deictic); capitalised *Seven Bodies / Cataclysm*; *the
likeness thereof* (KJV cadence + archaism). Actual sentence is
none of these.

**Pass.**
