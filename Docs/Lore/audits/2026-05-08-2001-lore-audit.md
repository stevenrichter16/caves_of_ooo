## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclaves (rotation, second sitting)

The Thermoclaves last landed at 1501 (bench-note
slate, *no clause*, structural rejection, *to
bench-note* verb). This sitting lands the
discipline's **transfer-out working-form** — the
fork in refusal where a piece is forwarded under
bench-note to a different working-form rather
than returned to the bringing-walker.

### Etymology — *forwarded to bench*, *bench-runner*

The Thermoclaves' vocabulary holds three canon
phrases: *bench-note slate* (the working-record),
*no clause* (the refusal-clause), and now
**forwarded to bench** (the closing-line of a
transfer-out bench-note). *Forwarded* is the
discipline's first **direction-form** — *forward*
names the piece's onward path, contrast with
*returned* which names the piece's reverse path.
The pair sorts on the direction-of-onward-
handling register (§2.6). A piece *forwarded to
bench* is one whose bench-note has been signed
by the smith-on-shift, sealed by the smith-of-
the-day, and whose onward path is to a named
keeper-of-bench at a named other-discipline's
working-form.

**Bench-runner.** The Thermoclaves canonise a
working-form actor not previously keyed on the
slate: the **bench-runner**, a smith's apprentice
or junior-bench whose working-day passes between
the Thermoclaves' bench and the receiving
working-forms named in the day's transfer-out
column. The bench-runner carries the forwarded
piece under bench-note seal, hand-delivers it to
the receiving keeper, and returns with the
receiving keeper's counter-mark. The bench-runner
is the slate's first **inter-discipline messenger
working-form**; the only working-form actor whose
working-day is structurally distributed across
two or more disciplines' benches.

### The two-column bench-note slate

The Thermoclaves' bench-note slate now canonises
on a two-column working-form: a *return-to-
source* column (the slate's primary register
since 1501, the closing-line *returned to walker*)
and a *transfer-out* column (new, the closing-
line *forwarded to bench [keeper-name]*). The
smith-on-shift exercises forwarding-grade
discretion at the moment of refusal: a piece
whose iron reads as sea-wrought (working-coast,
salt-corrosion patina, fish-line wear-mark) is
canonically forwarded to the Brine chart-bench;
a piece whose iron reads as canon-line (named
maker-mark, Curation working-line at the bench
seven-east) is canonically forwarded to the
Curation vault-front; all other refusals are
return-to-source. The forwarding-grade is
single-grade (no contingent-grade equivalent of
the Curation's witness-shelf); a forwarding-
disposition canons or is return-to-source by
default.
