## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord rotates in

### The Sweetening — the rite that closes a trade

The Concord's *Dissolution* cosmology is partially flippant on
its surface (bible L481: *"sweetens their grief with trade"*).
The 1901 coverage gave the Concord an etymology for the Dissolution
but did not give the trade-as-grief practice a procedural form.
Sitting 14 lands the form.

**The Sweetening** is the closing rite of any Concord trade
exceeding three named items, or any single item priced above what
the Concord calls *the standing comfort* — roughly a winter's
measured firewood plus a sealed jar. It is performed by seller
and buyer together, in the open, the moment goods change hands.
Both parties state aloud what the trade *replaces*. Not what they
bought — what is *no longer here* because the trade happened.

A wool-merchant selling four blankets to a salt-cliff cellar-
keeper says: *"The wool no longer warms the sheep that grew it,
which are dead, and no longer warms my mother's bed, which is
empty since the autumn."* The cellar-keeper says: *"The salt no
longer salts the sea it came from, which is the sea, and no
longer pays the man who waded for it, which is me, and that is
why my hands are paid."* Each party then takes a small sweet
(a honey-curd, a pressed-fig, a sugar-twist — each Concord market
has its own house-sweet) and eats it, in unison. The trade is
closed.

The doctrine: every transaction is a small dissolution. Something
is no longer where it was. The Concord refuses to pretend
otherwise. **The sugar is not a reward for the trade. It is a
procedural acknowledgement that the trade required something to
dissolve.** The Concord's name for itself — *the Saccharine
Concord* — is a working description, not a flourish: *we agree
to sweeten what dissolves.*

**Public Sweetenings** are three-item-and-up trades; **private
Sweetenings** are inheritances, breakups, ends-of-services. A
grandmother handing a workbench to her grandchild performs a
private Sweetening over a single sugar-twist. A house dissolved
by death takes its largest Sweetening — every named item, every
named loss, a pressed-fig per pairing.

### The Dissolution metaphysics, in trade-form

Two corollaries the Concord works through without naming. (a)
Trade is *not* zero-sum even though Sweetening declares both
sides' losses; the wool moves *to* the cellar-keeper *as wool*,
but its history of being-with-sheep, being-on-mother's-bed
dissolves regardless. The history is what the Sweetening grieves.
(b) The Concord sees the cataclysm as the *largest standing
Sweetening*, permanently open, with no second party to perform
the ritual across. This is why the Concord trades constantly:
trade is the only form of Sweetening still possible. *Every
Concord market is a small reply to a Dissolution that had no
buyer.*

### The Concord-Choir ritual seam

The Choir, observed by a Concord trader, does *not* sweeten. It
takes a thing into itself with no spoken acknowledgement that the
thing's history is dissolving in the take. To a Concord trader,
this is the Choir's deepest offence — not consumption per se (the
Concord eats too) but *unsweetened consumption.* Reciprocally:
the Choir finds Concord traders disgusting in a way it does not
find Pale Curation cataloguers disgusting. The cataloguer at
least makes the dead thing's record useful. The Concord eats
sugar and *says* the loss out loud and then *moves on*. *They
taste of cane and of refusal* is a recurring tendril-phrase,
working in both senses of *refusal* (unyieldingness; table-
scraps, what is not consumed).

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"Every Concord market is a small reply to a Dissolution that
> had no buyer."*

Working register, gloss-form. One declarative; the metaphor uses
trade-vocabulary the Concord owns (*reply*; *buyer*). The
metaphysical claim — that the cataclysm was a Sweetening with no
second party — is delivered in the same noun-set the Concord
uses for any Tuesday transaction.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"Verily, every Mart of the Saccharine
Concord standeth as a small Answering unto that great Dissolving,
the which had no Other to Sweeten It."* Tells: *Verily*; *standeth*;
*the great Dissolving* (capital-D abstracting an event the Concord
names with a working noun); *Other* with capitalisation. The
actual sentence: lower case throughout, no abstract-noun
capitalisation, the verbs *reply* and *buy* are the same verbs
an apprentice would use about an ordinary Tuesday.
