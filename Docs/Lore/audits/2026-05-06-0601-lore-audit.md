**Add (paragraph after L125):**

> *The Palimpsest is its own enforcement. A read returns the

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord rotation

The Concord is named in JSON twice (`OtherVersions`: *Dissolution
/ sweetens-their-grief-with-trade*) and the bible once (L185,
founder reference). Last rotated: never. This sitting takes the
chain's loose Concord vocabulary (*unsweetened year*, *factor*,
*primer*) and assigns it stable in-fiction meaning.

### Etymology

The Concord's two-word name is a register-shift the Concord made
deliberately at founding. *Saccharine* is the trade-word for the
sweetening agent the Concord adds to every transaction — not
sugar; a chemical analog refined from a pre-loss process the
Concord still keeps and will not share. *Concord* is the legal
word for an agreement that has been *sweetened* — sweetening is
the Concord's signature on a deal. Outsiders translate the name as
*sugar-treaty* and the Concord does not correct the translation,
because the translation is wrong in a way that benefits the
Concord. (A treaty is a thing two parties sign once. A concord is
a thing the Concord renews quarterly, and the renewal is where
the price of the sweetening adjusts.)

### The unsweetened year

A Concord working-period, four full quarters in length, declared
unilaterally by the Concord's standing-bench whenever the
standing-bench believes the trade-network is forgetting why it
trades. During an unsweetened year, every Concord transaction is
recorded *without* the saccharine — the deal is real, the price
is real, but the sweetening is omitted. Outsiders believe an
unsweetened year is an austerity. Concord factors know it is the
opposite — an unsweetened year is when the Concord remembers what
its prices would be if it were not the Concord. The
standing-bench has called four unsweetened years since founding.
The most recent ended seven standing-loss years ago. The Concord's
own factors will not say whether the next is overdue, but
factor's-ledgers are running thin in the parenthetical column;
see §5.

### Mourning practice: the sweetened slip

A Concord factor who dies in the field is mourned by their
factor-of-record — the factor at the next-larger Concord depot —
who writes a final transaction in the dead factor's ledger, one
line, in the dead factor's hand if such a hand can be borrowed.
The transaction is *the sweetened slip*: a record of the dead
factor's last unsold inventory, *sold at sweetened price to no
one.* The slip closes the dead factor's ledger. The Concord then
reissues the inventory, unsold, to the next factor in line — who
now begins their first quarter carrying the dead factor's
unsweetened weight. This is how Concord seniority is measured:
not in years, but in *unsweetened weight inherited.*

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds — *we are one* vs. the named NPCs

The Choir's standing dialect (`ChoirTendril_1.HowMany`):

> *There is no many. There is only the Choir.*

The same JSON ships `Mogu_1.Start`:

> *I am Mogu — once. Now, also a syllable in the deeper sentence.*

VOICES.md L25 frames the tension in-fiction (*"the Choir absorbs
persons but does not erase the language they came in with"*) and
at L506 is willing to call the Choir's *we are one* a *small
self-deception*.

The bible carries none of this. Its Choir-philosophy paragraph
(L37–L41) treats integration as full erasure: *"A rusted sword
composted by the Choir loses its history — who forged it, who
wielded it, where it fell — but its molecules become part of
something alive."* That is contradicted by Mogu's *"Names of
plants my grandmother named, in a tongue we no longer speak. Now
the names live in the spores. Nothing of value is ever lost —
only translated."* (`Mogu_1.Before`).

| Source | Doctrine | Practice |
|---|---|---|
| Bible L37–L41 | Integration is full erasure (history-of-sword lost). | (No counterexample acknowledged.) |
| `ChoirTendril_1.HowMany` | *"There is no many. There is only the Choir."* | (Standing-dialect denial of multiplicity.) |
| `Mogu_1` / `Grib_1` / etc. | (Implicit: integration preserves voice.) | Named individuals retain pre-Choir cadence, names, kin-memory. |
| VOICES.md L25, L506 | The Choir-as-such *insists* on unity but *practices* preservation. | Acknowledges the doctrine/practice gap; names it a self-deception. |

**Recommended correction.** Adopt the §2.7 paragraph as the
bible's anthropological note on persons. Smallest fix that
resolves the bleed: the bible no longer contradicts the JSON,
and the Choir's full-erasure doctrine becomes the Choir's
*self-account* rather than the designer's assertion. The
contradiction stays in the fiction (where it is generative) and
exits the fiction-vs-canon register (where it was a bug). L116
seam, ≈3 editor-minutes.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion — Concord factor's quarter-ledger marginalia

A page from a Saccharine Concord factor's quarterly ledger,
filed at a regional depot. The headline is a quarter-end
inventory reconciliation; the cosmology is the parenthetical.
≈285 words.

> *Quarter close, third quarter, standing-loss year 419. Ledger
> reconciled to depot's. Sweetening ratio: 0.143 — within band,
> low end of band, the third low close in a row. Inventory
> short by two pressings of cane-spirit and one bolt of
> grease-cloth, all three accounted for in the loss column at
> the standing rate.*
>
> *Renewed three concords this quarter. Two with the Threshing
> bench (standing-rate), one with a Brine ship's-cooper
> (sweetened heavy, the cooper drives a hard renewal). Notable:
> the cooper asked, conversationally, when the next unsweetened
> year would be called. I said it was not for a factor to
> answer. The cooper laughed and said the factors are the only
> ones who would know — the standing-bench learns from the
> ledgers, and the ledgers are the factors. He is not wrong.*
>
> *(The ratio has been low for eleven straight quarters now. In
> a sweetened year, low ratio means the trade is profitable
> without the sweetening — which means the sweetening is
> performative — which means the standing-bench will have read
> these ledgers already and will be deciding. The founder, in
> her primer, wrote that the Concord will fail when its
> sweetening becomes a habit. A habit, she wrote, is what
> survives the reason for it. I do not know if eleven quarters
> is a habit. I know fifteen would be. The cooper's question
> was not innocent. The Brine sing under their keels what their
> coopers do not sing aloud. Watch for it.)*
>
> *Ledger closed. Sweetened slip drawn for the cane-spirit
> pressings, addressed to no one — the depot will reissue them
> to whichever factor opens fourth quarter. I do not expect to
> open it.*

**Lore landed parenthetically:**

  - The Concord *founder* (L185) wrote a *primer* for new factors;
    one line of it quoted: *the Concord will fail when its
    sweetening becomes a habit.*
  - The unsweetened year (§3) has a measurable trigger (the
    sweetening-ratio band) and a count (eleven quarters,
    fifteen = habit). Mechanism, not metaphor.
  - Cross-faction acoustics: a Brine ship's-cooper asks about the
    next unsweetened year — *the Brine sing under their keels
    what their coopers do not sing aloud*. Cross-links the 0501
    sailor's marginalia keel-song mechanic.
  - The factor's last line — *I do not expect to open it* —
    doubles as personal premonition and sweetened-slip
    foreshadow. The factor is closing their own ledger.

Candidate found-document for `Docs/Lore/`. Not proposed for the
bible directly.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence (from §3, the Concord etymology):**

> *Outsiders translate the name as sugar-treaty and the Concord
> does not correct the translation, because the translation is
> wrong in a way that benefits the Concord.*

Working register. Flat trade-nouns (*translation*, *treaty*),
one flat verb (*correct*), one transactional clause (*wrong in a
way that benefits*). Procedural — a faction chooses not to correct
a profitable mistranslation. No allegorical capitalisation, no
appositional mythologisation.

TES rewrite (forbidden):

> *Outlanders speak the Name in their broken tongues as*
> Sugar-Treaty *— and the Saccharine Lords, in their wisdom and
> their long quiet, do not correct the error, for the error is a
> sweetness in itself, and the Saccharine grows fat upon sweetness,
> as all true Concords must.*

Tells: capitalised *Name / Sugar-Treaty / Saccharine Lords*;
appositional *in their wisdom and their long quiet*;
generalising-aphoristic *as all true Concords must*. The actual
sentence does its rhetorical work through the rule, not through
mythologised vocabulary.

Pass.
