## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible, line 21:* "the way text on a medieval manuscript was scraped
> off and written over with the old words still faintly visible
> underneath."

**Strong.** Aftermath, breaking-moment, duration, world-before all
stratified across prior audits.

**Thin.** **The first generation born after.** Every account treats
the cataclysm and its long aftermath as a clean handoff between *the
doomed* and *the survivors* — but the children born in the first two
decades after, under the smoke, are unaccounted for.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "The smoke-born"):**

> The Pale Curation files the surviving generation born in the first
> twenty years after the cataclysm under a single bracket — *the
> smoke-born* — too few to be a cohort and too many to be a footnote.
> Most factions inherit the term and mean different things by it. The
> Choir says they tasted *sweet* (milk had a different mineral profile
> in those years and the network noticed). The Brine say the
> smoke-born are the only generation that *crossed the tide both
> ways*; their dead are filed *deep-kept* without negotiation. The
> Concord refuses rate-notices on the smoke-born until age forty, in
> writing, and breaks its own rule about a third of the time. The
> Thermoclaves count them as the *eleventh fire* — the bridge between
> the cold one and the next chain — and apprentice them by seniority
> of birth-year. The Glassblown have no doctrine for the smoke-born
> and consider that doctrinal. The Palimpsest reads them as *thicker
> pages*, harder to turn.

The corpus had *four hundred years of after.* It now has *the twenty
after the after.*

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Charter Receipt, Reconciled Texts (2219), R-7, redactions,
forgery, footnote-disagreement (2225), time-release annotations
(2220), buyer's-ledger sleeve R-11 (2307), short-counted
open-discrepancy (2310).

**Thin.** **The dictated document.** Every artifact so far is the
work of a hand that knew what it was writing. No corpus document is
a transcription — a scribe writing down *another voice* the scribe
does not entirely understand, where the bias of the voice and the
bias of the hand interfere with each other.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "Concord transcripts"):**

> The Saccharine Concord's longest-form documents are *settlement
> transcripts* — verbatim records of high-value negotiations, written
> by a scribe whose role is to be *the slowest hand in the room.* The
> scribe, alone of the parties, may not negotiate inside the
> documented sentences; the transcript is a third grammar held over
> the two negotiating ones. Three known transcripts contradict their
> eventual contract on later reading. The Concord does not consider
> the transcripts wrong. It considers the contracts to have been
> signed by parties *the transcript was no longer describing*. Two
> were voided on this basis.
>
> Scribes who let their own grammar leak into the transcript are
> *lossy.* A lossy scribe is cheaper to hire and cheaper to litigate
> against (the bias is on the surface). The trade in lossy
> transcripts is its own small market. The dishonest move is to
> charge the honest rate for lossy work.

Author and amanuensis are now distinct variables. A document's
*grammar* may belong to one party and its *content* to another, and
the Concord prices the gap.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** Cataclysm-names, dead-names, joining-names,
apotheosis-names, names-for-the-player (2219), names-for-being-seen
(2225), names-for-remembering (2220), names-for-the-world-before
(2225), names-for-debt (2307), names-for-founder (2310).

**Thin.** **Names for a promise.** The companion to debt: an
obligation not yet incurred but already binding.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "What each faction calls a
promise"):**

> - **Choir:** *a held breath.* The network suspends a small portion
>   of itself awaiting fulfilment. A broken Choir-promise is a *stale
>   gradient*; the network reabsorbs it. No word for *promise to the
>   dead* — the dead are already integrated.
> - **Palimpsest:** *a foreshadowing.* The promise is read backward
>   as if already kept. Breaking it introduces a *misreading*; the
>   layer that held it becomes harder to read for years.
> - **Pale Curation:** *a rider.* A clause appended to a filed card.
>   Broken promises occupy cabinet space.
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *a held rate.* A price agreed for a future
>   transaction. The strongest Concord-promise includes its own
>   settlement date; an open-ended promise is *an offer not a promise.*
> - **Brine Communion:** *a set tide.* Timed by water — *I will see
>   you at the third tide after the equinox.* A Brine-promise without
>   a tide-clock is *a wish*, treated with affectionate suspicion.
> - **Thermoclaves:** *a fire to be kept.* A literal small fire, lit
>   at promising and tended until fulfilled. *A fire that goes out*
>   is the visible failure; the grammar requires fuel-budgeting.
> - **Glassblown:** *an unfired shape.* Held as glass-paste, never
>   fired. The shape can be *broken without breaking*: the glass is
>   reabsorbed into the next batch and the promise is *unsaid.*

A Concord-promise without a settlement date and a Brine-promise
without a tide-clock are *the same word translated badly.* The
Curation Lexicon (2220) earns a fourth entry.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Substrate-leak, Choir metabolism, Echo limits,
metaphysics-meet, composted-matter readings, dual-attunement
interference, *consensus-stratum* (2307), the *standing line* (2310).

**Thin.** **The Brine's substrate-claim.** The Concord has *consensus*
and *standing line.* The Choir has *metabolism.* The Palimpsest has
*layered text.* The Brine has *deep-keeping* used as if it were a
verb only, never named as a *stratum*.

**Concrete addition (bible expansion, "The held depth"):**

> The Brine Communion's substrate-claim is the **held depth** — the
> stratum at which water becomes still enough to hold a body, an
> object, or a promise without dispersing it. Physical (a measurable
> layer in any sufficient body of water) and metaphysical (the Brine
> teach that *every* thing has a held depth at which it would be
> fully kept, and the under-tide knows it).
>
> *Deep-kept* is the participle of being held at one's own held
> depth. A Brine-dead is *deep-kept* if the body reached its held
> depth before dispersal began. Bodies that did not are *un-deeped*
> — neither *kept* nor *lost*, and the Communion's only word that
> admits unfinished business with the dead.
>
> The Brine's quarrel with the Concord (sibling 2307's substrate
> ledger) is at the held-depth level: an open-discrepancy ledger is,
> to a Brine reader, an *un-deeped* book — *a body the Concord
> refuses to let sink.*

The Brine substrate-stratum is now named. Material (Choir),
temporal (Palimpsest), consensual (Concord), standing (Concord
again, per 2310), held-depth (Brine). Five strata.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** Mirror endings, Annihilist (1901), Stalled, failed,
refused, pulled, half-said/half-tasted, Balanced Account (2307),
open-account dead (2310).

**Thin.** **The Brine's apotheosis-shape.** Choir has Communion;
Palimpsest has Transcription; Concord has Settled-Zero; Curation
has *retirement-into-cabinet* implied at 2225; Glassblown have
*unfiring* (2150). The Brine has none.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "The going-deep"):**

> A walker at highest Brine reputation is invited to the
> **going-deep** — a swim from a Brine-tended shore, at a tide they
> name, into water of the walker's choosing. The walker swims out
> until the Brine call them back. They may be called back. They may
> not be. The Brine do not promise.
>
> *Called back:* the walker is *un-deeped*. They retain Brine
> standing but reputation resets; the Communion has decided they
> were not ready. Most swim again within a year.
>
> *Not called back:* the walker has *gone-deep*. They reach their
> own held depth and remain there — physically alive (the held depth
> sustains the body, per Brine doctrine, contested by no faction for
> lack of evidence either way) but no longer surfacing. Brine swim
> down on ceremonial occasions and report on the world above. The
> gone-deep reply, sometimes. Their replies, recorded by witnesses
> on the shore, form the **deep-letters** — the Communion's only
> canonical scripture.
>
> The going-deep is the loneliest of the apotheoses. The walker
> retains awareness, language, memory, even visitors, but surrenders
> the surface entirely. They do not become a network, a witness, or
> a closed account. They become *kept.* The Communion considers it
> the gentlest of the endings. Walkers who choose it tend to be
> those who have lost something specific the surface cannot return.

The fifth apotheosis-shape is named. Each surrenders one register:
Communion surrenders self, Transcription surrenders presence,
Settled-Zero surrenders price, going-deep surrenders surface.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Choir pronouns; Palimpsest tense; Brine joining;
Thermoclave fire-grammar; Glassblown unsaid mood; Curation
catalogued mood and quiet conventions; Concord trade tense (2307)
and receipted clause (2310).

**Thin.** **Concord non-verbal grammar.** A faction whose business
is conducted across counters develops gestural conventions invisible
to outsiders. The hand-signs of the assayer are unwritten.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "The assayer's
hand-grammar"):**

> Concord assayers communicate during high-value negotiations
> through a stable system of finger micro-gestures, performed below
> the counter where the counterparty cannot see. The signs are made
> *to the witness*, not the counterparty.
>
> The seven canonical signs:
>
> - **Index resting on thumb.** *Fair within tolerance.*
> - **Index lifted from thumb.** *Off-tolerance high*; will accept
>   but not benchmark.
> - **Index pressed firmly to thumb.** *Off-tolerance low*; note in
>   the day-book before close.
> - **Two-finger curl.** *Alloy uncertain.* Veshen's most-used sign.
> - **Three-finger fan.** The opposite party *does not understand the
>   trade tense* and is being asked to commit to a *closed-up*
>   settlement they will read as *open*. Witness's protocol: pause.
> - **Open palm flat to the underside of the counter.** *Refuse.* The
>   witness may not ask why.
> - **Closed fist held still.** *I am leaving.* Trade closes at last
>   open price; assayer recused; a junior takes their place.
>
> The hand-grammar is not taught in the first year. It is taught
> only after an apprentice has been *witnessed* by a senior assayer
> — a small reciprocal initiation. Apprentices who pass become
> *signed-to*. Apprentices who fail spend their careers *not seeing*
> the counter from the assayer's side.
>
> The Choir, told of the hand-grammar, said: *we have always known.
> the soil under the counter is alive. it remembers the press of the
> palm.* The Concord has considered installing barriers between
> counter and soil. It has not done so.

The Concord's verbal grammar (trade tense, receipted clause) now has
a parallel non-verbal layer — the hand-signs are how *the Concord
witnesses itself.*

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Strong.** Tuesday Mornings (all six), cross-Tuesday market (2219),
stranger-meeting protocols (2220), Curation initiation (2220),
Quiet Hour (2225), Concord weddings (2307), Closing of Accounts
(2310).

**Thin.** **Brine funerary customs.** Sibling 2310's closing line
explicitly named this as the next gap. Brine joining-words exist
(2001); their leave-taking does not.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "How the Brine release"):**

> A Brine death is announced by the under-tide before the surface
> notices. A Brine-attuned witness *feels* it as a tide-pull running
> counter to the surface tide for a single breath. Protocol: name the
> time aloud, as precisely as the witness can. The named time is the
> body's *first tide* — the tide on which it begins to keep.
>
> The body is carried to the held-depth shore (each Brine settlement
> has one) and committed at the next tide the Communion has computed
> *unfavourable to the deceased's opening price*. The opening price
> (per 2307's wedding grammar, repurposed) is what they brought into
> the world — birth-tide minus death-tide. Committal at an
> unfavourable tide is a kindness: the body is released with its
> account *over-paid*, and the held depth holds it the deeper for
> being un-owed.
>
> The committal is silent. The senior present speaks one phrase,
> only after the body reaches its depth: *to the depth that you
> found, and the tide that did not come.* The phrase encodes the
> Communion's only formal grief — every leave-taking is, in their
> grammar, *the same tide that did not come*: the cataclysm-tide of
> round 1's *Long Between*.
>
> The Concord cannot price a Brine committal. It does not try. The
> Curation files the witness's named time in a separate cabinet;
> Brine times are not interoperable with surface times. The Choir
> composts what the held depth releases, in seasons, and considers
> the slow uptake a courtesy.

Brine funerals now match the Concord's, the Curation's, and the
Glassblown's in doctrinal weight. The third member of the
heat/water/cold-grammar trio (Brine, Thermoclave, Choir) is the only
one whose committal is *to a depth that holds*.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine Communion (round 2 opens)

The Concord has been saturated across today's four siblings. The
Brine has had only round 1 (2001 — Long Between, joining-words,
salvaged sailor's marginalia). Round 2 opens here, with two
artifacts: the *held depth* (Dimension 4 above), the *going-deep*
(Dimension 5), the *committal phrase* (Dimension 7), and now a
**named senior figure** to anchor the next pass.

**Proposed senior figure: Tide-Reader Nesh of the Coastal Embayment.**

Forty-one years a tide-reader. Brine custom is that a tide-reader is
named for the body of water they read; *Nesh* is the Brine syllable
for *the long swell that does not crest.* One of nine living
tide-readers, and the only one who has never declared a tide
*wrong* — she names what comes and refuses to litigate it.

Nesh has computed the going-deep tides for one hundred and eleven
walkers. Forty-three called back; sixty-eight went-deep. She visits
seven of the gone-deep on the ceremonial calendar — the seven who,
before going under, asked her to come back. *I do not refuse the
asked,* she says. *The other deep do not need me.*

Nesh is the Brine's only correspondent with Senior Assayer Veshen
(sibling 2307). They write on ordinary stock — the Brine consider
water-resistant material a vanity — and the letters degrade in
transit. Each woman keeps the unreadable portions. Veshen files her
fragments in the private drawer that holds Director Ven's
open-debt receipt; Nesh files hers at the Coastal Embayment's held
depth, where they are kept by the water itself. Neither faction has
audited.

Nesh's working belief: the Concord's eleven-year alloy crisis and
the Communion's long-overdue tide (the cataclysm-tide that has not
returned for four hundred years) are *the same arrears*, named by
two grammars. She has not said this to Veshen. The deep-letters of
three gone-deep walkers contain the sentence *the tide is the
alloy.* Nesh is collating.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a)** Bible:115 — the Choir *"has been waiting six hundred years.
It is not in a hurry."* — and sibling 2307's Veshen-estimate of
*Foretime alloys exhausted in eleven years*. The Choir's clock is
six hundred; the Concord's is eleven; the Brine's, per Nesh, is
*the same long-overdue tide*. **Implication:** Choir patience is
*targeted* — calibrated to a specific exhaustion. **Recommend the
bible add a sidebar at the Choir-patience line:**

> Patience as long as the Choir's is not abstract — it is calibrated
> to a specific exhaustion. The Brine's *long-overdue tide* and the
> Concord's *alloy crisis* are the same arrears named twice. The
> Choir is waiting for both to finish.

This is the corpus's first claim that *Choir patience is targeted*.

**(b)** Bible:39 — *"there is no parasitism, only symbiosis."* And
`RotChoir.json:107` — *"We eat everything."* The verb *eat* is
one-sided. **Resolution:** Choir *honesty within an ontology* (per
2219). **Recommend the Choir Implementation Rules add:**

> The Choir uses one-sided verbs (eat, take, draw, bring) for what
> its ontology classifies as two-sided relations. The walker is
> permitted to notice. The Choir is reporting a distinction its
> grammar does not contain.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

A Saccharine Concord apprentice's catechism, in which the lore is in
*what is not asked* — paying off Dimension 8 directly.

**Title (in-game):** *Apprentice's Catechism, Saccharine Concord,
junior section. Sixth issue, revised.*

```
[Pamphlet, palm-sized, twenty-eight stitched pages. The catechism
 is taught aloud; the printed form is a reminder. Questions
 numbered. Answers short.]

  Q.1   What is the open ledger?
  A.    The default condition. A ledger not yet closed-up.

  Q.2   What is the closed-up ledger?
  A.    A ledger settled against its agreed price.

  Q.3   When may a ledger be re-opened?
  A.    Only by mutual agreement, witnessed.

  Q.4   When must a ledger be closed?
  A.    When both parties have agreed it is settled-up. Not before.

  Q.5   What is the substrate-ledger?
  A.    The book that holds debts owed by the Choir against
        composted collateral. It does not close.

  Q.6   How is the substrate-ledger maintained?
  A.    Honestly. We enter every claim. We collect none.

  Q.7   Why?
  A.    Because the Choir is a party. Because we have decided that
        the Choir is a party. Because consensus admits parties that
        do not consent.

  Q.8   What of the Palimpsest?
  A.    A reachable counterparty through a willing reader.

  Q.9   What of the Pale Curation?
  A.    A trustworthy witness whose card outlives our day-books.

  Q.10  What of the Brine Communion?
  A.    The tide is the witness. Adjust the ledger to the cadence.

  Q.11  What of the Thermoclaves?
  A.    Spot-trade only. They will not lend; we will not press.

  Q.12  What of the Glassblown?
  A.    Trade in unfired sand only. Decline declined trades politely.

  Q.13  What is the Balanced Account?
  A.    The doctrine. The limit case. Not a thing to seek.

  Q.14  [REMOVED. Referenced in older issues; not in the present
        catechism. The numbering is preserved.]

  Q.15  Who taught us?
  A.    Two parties on a road. Their names are not asked.

  [End of junior section. Senior catechism is taught only by a
   master and is not printed.]
```

The bombshell is **Q.14 REMOVED.** The Concord preserves the
*number* of the removed question — its grammatical mark for *a
question that exists but is not asked.* The number is the doctrinal
hole. (Compare the Curation's *intentional blank*, the dot, from
sibling 2225: both factions have conventions for the *deliberately
unfilled.*)

The senior-only Q.14, documented in oral tradition:

> *Q.14  What does the Concord do when the Choir agrees to pay?*

The Concord considers the question *premature*. That it has been
asked seriously enough to be written, then deliberately removed
from the printed catechism, is the bombshell. The Concord is
preparing for a future in which the Choir *settles its
substrate-ledger* — a future its public doctrine claims will never
come. Per Section 4(a), Q.14 is the apprentice-form of *what
happens when the Choir's patience expires?*

**Q.15** — *Their names are not asked* — is sibling 2310's
founder-stories rendered as catechetical refusal. Naming would
*price* the founders, who are doctrinally outside the Concord's
pricing-grammar.

Plant: any junior apprentice's belt-pouch. Senior assayers carry
copies with Q.14 hand-restored in the margin in their own ink;
Veshen's restoration is two columns — the question, and her own
provisional answer, unshared. Tide-Reader Nesh (Section 3), shown a
copy, would read Q.14 once and say only: *the tide is the alloy.*
Q.14 and Nesh's working belief are the same sentence across two
grammars.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"The Brine's quarrel with the Concord is at the held-depth level:
> the Brine considers an open-discrepancy ledger an un-deeped book.
> The Concord's open ledgers, to a Brine reader, are bodies the
> Concord refuses to let sink."*

No archaisms, no apostrophe-fantasy names, no Aedra/Daedra/Towers/
CHIM/Et'Ada. Two plain present-tense sentences. The doctrinal claim
rides on a single technical translation — *open ledger* read as
*un-deeped book*, *Concord ledgers* as *bodies refused sinking* —
and the Brine grammar (*held depth, kept, un-deeped*) does the
discriminating work. A TES book attempting the same beat would have
written *"And the Sweet-Hearted Coiners do hold their accounts open
to the very firmament, while the Children of the Deep do say in
their hidden tongue that what is not let-fall is not yet at peace,
and lo, this is the heart of the long sorrow between them."* Mine
has no *firmament, lo, hidden tongue, long sorrow.* The
sorrow-content is in the noun *bodies the Concord refuses to let
sink* — operationalised, not invoked. Confirmed.
