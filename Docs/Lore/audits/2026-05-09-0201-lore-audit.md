## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (bible L75):** *"It was never just a faction. It's the
substrate."*

**Strong.** Emotional beat — a mechanic becoming an
ontological claim.

**Thin.** Asserted in omniscient register; no counter-account.
The Pale Curation should categorically disagree — to it the
Choir is a remarkably integrated *organism,* not the world's
substrate. Without a contesting voice, what should be the
game's most contested cosmological question reads as fact.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Underground Reveal, append
after L116):**

> Not every faction agrees. The Pale Curation classifies the
> Choir as *Order Mycomagna, an exceptionally integrated
> colonial organism* — a species, granted unusual scope, but a
> species. To the Curation, the Choir is not the substrate but
> a very thorough tenant. The classification is not said aloud
> near a Choir-tendril; it is filed. A walker who has read both
> the Choir's terrain claim and the Curation's organism entry
> is left with the asymmetry that does not resolve: the Choir's
> claim is *felt,* the Curation's claim is *filed,* and felt
> and filed answer different questions. The bible cannot say
> which is right because the bible is what one Curation desk
> has written.

Last clause load-bearing — see §2.2.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (bible L23):** *"every faction in the game is defined
by their relationship to this event."*

**Thin.** No diegetic frame. The bible's voice is calm,
comparative, normalising, taxonomic — exactly the working
register of the Pale Curation. The comparison table, the
*implementation rules,* the careful *both are right* moves —
all Curation choices. Treating the bible as authorless is the
biggest unmarked bias in the corpus.

**Concrete enhancement (new bible front-matter, before §The
Cataclysm):**

> *Curation working synthesis, third revision, Cataclysm
> Studies desk. This document collates field reports and
> interview transcripts from each named faction, normalised
> into the Curation's working register. Where a faction's own
> register would contradict our framings, we note it; where the
> contradiction is unresolved, we present both. The Curation is
> itself a faction; this synthesis is a Curation document.
> Read accordingly.*

One colophon turns every existing bible line into a Curation
translation. The Choir's *we are the substrate* and the
Palimpsest's *all tenses at once* arrive through a desk that
prefers normalised prose. Players who notice earn a meta-
reading the bible currently denies.

### 2.3 Mutating motifs across cultures

**Quote (JSON L585, Mogu):** *"What brings your feet to ours?"*

**Strong.** ROTCHOIR's tendrils each address the walker
through their own grain — Mogu's *feet,* Sopp's *hail,
walker,* Sien's ellipses, Nam's *friend, sit on this stone.*

**Thin.** Side-factions have no player-naming convention; the
bible's only word for the player is *the player.*

**Enhancement (new bible §Player-Naming by Faction):**

> The Choir says *walker* — generic, plural-addressed, the same
> word it uses for any feet that are not its own. The Saccharine
> Concord says *factor* when sober, *friend-of-the-fair* when in
> pitch, *party-of-the-second-part* when contracted; the term in
> use names the stage of the deal. The Pale Curation says
> *visiting reader* in the working register and *intake-N* on a
> tag, where N is the desk-numbering count. The Glassblown say
> nothing — they wait for the walker to introduce themselves and
> then use the introduction *as if it were a vow,* taking the
> walker's name with the seriousness a first-cooling piece is
> taken. The Brine Communion says *crew* the moment the walker
> is willing to row, and *passenger* before that. The Thermo-
> claves say *fire-edge guest* the first night, *fire-tender*
> if the walker keeps watch. The Palimpsest names no one; it
> names the moment. *The reader who is in this passage right
> now* — the address that is also the location.

Eight namings, eight worldviews.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (JSON L442):** *"Repair implies it was better before.
We disagree. But yes -- the cracks could be sealed."*

**Quote (bible L21):** *"the way text on a medieval manuscript
was scraped off and written over with the old words still
faintly visible underneath."*

**The contradiction.** The bible's metaphor is *imperfect
overwriting* — substrate as parchment, damage as palimpsesting.
The JSON's metaphor is *cracks* — substrate as vessel, damage
as fissure. Not the same physical object. Both are literal
enough that the edge cases diverge: Echo Reading recovers what
*was written;* substrate-sealing seals what *is fissured.*

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm, append):**

> The cataclysm has two operative metaphors and they are not
> the same. The Palimpsest reads it as *imperfect overwriting*
> — a manuscript whose old text bleeds through because the
> scraping-knife was dull. The Choir, when forced into figura-
> tive speech, talks about *cracks* — a structural failure of
> the world's containing surface, with the old contents leaking
> upward. Both are translations of the same fact; neither is
> the fact. A walker should expect each faction to defend its
> own metaphor *as if it were the truth,* because in each
> faction's working language it is. The Palimpsest's reader can
> recover overwritten text; the Choir's mechanic can seal a
> crack. Neither operation is well-defined on the other faction's
> metaphor — and that incommensurability is the cosmology's
> first practical limit.

Frames the metaphors as *translations,* their non-
interoperability as a feature.

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes without aping CHIM

**Quote (bible L218):** *"the moderate path should feel safe —
... safety should feel like cowardice."*

**Thin.** The bible commits the moderate ending to *feel like
cowardice* but never says what the moderate ending *is.*
Communion and Transcription are mythic and specific; the
moderate ending is unspecified — risks landing as anticlimax,
not cowardice.

**Concrete enhancement (new bible subsection §The Moderate
Ending):**

> The moderate ending is not a refusal of both — it is a
> transit. A walker at moderate reputation with both does not
> stand still. The Choir keeps offering; the Palimpsest keeps
> reading. The walker keeps declining the final step in each.
> Years pass in the Choir's count, sentences pass in the
> Palimpsest's. The walker grows old. The Choir does not press.
> The Palimpsest does not insist. Eventually — undecidable
> when — the walker dies a walker's death: in some weather, on
> some path, of some boring cause. The Choir composts the body.
> The Palimpsest reads the body. Each takes its half; neither
> takes the whole. The walker is divided cleanly between the
> two cosmic projects, which is the only way the cosmos has of
> acknowledging a person who refused to choose. The cosmology
> calls this *the long parsing.* The walker's children, if any,
> may not call it anything.

Cowardice as *transit;* legible to both, joining neither.

### 2.6 Linguistic texture

**Quote (bible L228):** *"Dialogue should shift pronoun usage."*

**Strong.** The Choir's pronoun arc as faction grammar-
signature.

**Thin.** Silent on five tendrils' first-person singular —
Mogu's *I tended a grove,* Grib's *I was warm once,* Sien's
*I am Sien,* Sopp's *I am Sopp.* A strict reading of the *we*
rule reads ROTCHOIR as a violation; in fact it is a patterned
deepening the bible never credits.

**Concrete enhancement (new bible rule, §Choir Implementation
Rules):**

> First-person singular is permitted to a tendril *when
> speaking about the life that preceded the Choir.* Mogu says
> *I tended a small grove;* Grib says *I had a sister.* The
> grammar reverts to *we* the moment the present is named.
> *I had a sister; we share a sister now.* The seam between
> pronouns is the seam between two states of being. The rule:
> *I* in the past, *we* in the present, *we* in the future.
> A tendril who slips into *I* about the present has not
> finished crossing.

The pronoun-seam becomes the Choir's most discoverable
absorption-mechanic, audible without lore-dump.

### 2.7 Anthropological framing

**Quote (bible L131):** *"Every time the Choir consumes some-
thing, it destroys the memory encoded in that thing."*

**Strong.** Cosmic-scale opinion.

**Thin.** No bible material on actual mourning. Each faction
has a working language for almost everything; death should be
in that language too. ROTCHOIR's tendrils narrate joining-as-
disintegration, but no faction is shown handling its dead.

**Concrete enhancement (new bible §Mourning, after §Faction
Entanglement):**

> Each faction handles its dead in its working language. The
> Choir composts — no funeral; the body is *taken back into
> the soil that has been calling it.* The Palimpsest reads
> the dying repeatedly until the dying has stabilised in the
> layering. The Saccharine Concord settles accounts: a factor's
> death triggers a public reckoning of debts owed and owed to,
> the body's status not finalised until the books are. The
> Pale Curation files: a tag is closed; a desk is wiped. The
> Glassblown blow one final piece *to the dying,* placed in
> the kiln and not removed — the dying takes its piece into
> the heat. The Brine Communion lowers the dying into the
> work of others: a sailor's last day is spent listening to
> the others' working songs, *so they go in chorus.* The
> Thermoclaves let the hearth go out *for one night;* the
> eldest watcher relights it at dawn with a different fire.
> A walker who dies between factions is handled by whichever
> finds the body first; the others may contest the disposition.

Each custom encodes its faction's philosophy in one line. The
disposition-contest line opens quest-grammar without forcing it.

### 2.8 Marginalia and footnotes

**Quote (bible L244):** *"Every time the player feeds a ruin
to the Choir, the Palimpsest loses a page."*

**Strong.** Player-as-tradeoff stated cleanly.

**Thin.** The JSON has the Choir asking the walker about the
*rememberers* — tracking the walker's accumulated Palimpsest
knowledge. The bible never accounts for *the walker as
information vector* — the second tradeoff.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §Faction Entanglement, append):**

> Less obviously: knowledge moves between factions through the
> walker's body. A walker who has used Echo Reading on a sword
> and then walks into Choir territory carries that history into
> the soil. The Choir, if attentive, can read the walker —
> *you taste of someone who held a sword,* says a tendril on a
> long greeting. The walker has not handed over the sword; the
> walker has handed over the reading. This is the second trade-
> off, smaller than the first. Where Choir consumption costs
> the Palimpsest a page, Palimpsest reading walked into Choir
> ground costs the Palimpsest *a secret* — the Choir now knows
> something the Palimpsest had hoped to keep. Neither faction
> will explain this to the walker. Both rely on it.

Player-body as leakage-vector, buried in an existing section.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord

Bible L185 promises *what the Saccharine Concord's founder
actually did* as a Palimpsest-reachable revelation; no other
source gestures at what that means. Three small artifacts
build the Concord around the founder mystery without resolving
it.

**The founder is named only at age.** A factor introducing the
founder is required to state the founder's age at the time of
founding. Phrasing is fixed: *"Aen Vellish-Hortor, who was
thirty-seven."* In Concord internal accounting, *only ages and
amounts are auditable.* A factor cannot be sued for
misremembering a face but can be sued for misstating a price.
The age binds the factor to a deposition-ready statement;
anything more opens liability. The Concord's *honesty about
the founder* is structurally an evasion — stating only what is
auditable to avoid stating what is not. A reader of ledgers
learns to notice which biographical facts the Concord won't
anchor; that absence is the shape of L185.

**The honey-year calendar.** Each year is named at its post-
harvest fair by quorum of factors, by *quality* — *the bright,
the stalled, the sour-by-late.* Not retroactive; disputes about
a year's name are routinely audited. The same numerical year
carries different names in different regional ledgers. Factors
exchange honey-year names as identity-check small-talk —
*"twelfth honey-year for me was bright. And you?" "Stalled, in
the Eastern circuit. We had floods."* Outsider-historians find
the calendar useless for cross-faction dating; that is a
feature.

**Etymology — *the warm transcript.*** Concord term for a
contract whose recital binds without signatures. From the
practice of reading the deal aloud at brokering — the recital
is the deal. *Warm* because taken while the deal is still
spoken; the *cool* version, written later, is binding only
because it cites the warm. Concord factors keep both in every
dealing; the cool for archives, the warm for the doing. Some
factors believe a warm transcript can be read out of a memory
that was never written — that the founder's deal might be
*warm* in some old factor's recollection, still binding. Hence
the Concord's reluctance to let scholars near retired factors.

Three pieces, one philosophy: evasiveness about the founder, a
calendar resistant to outside dating, contracts in voice only.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

A different bleed than 01:01's *not-at-war/recruiting* gap.

**Bible (L137):** *"Choir powers are somatic. Palimpsest
powers are noetic. A Choir player becomes a better organism.
A Palimpsest player becomes a better observer."*

**ROTCHOIR_VOICES (L24-28):** *"the Choir absorbs persons but
does not erase the language they came in with ... some part of
who you are survives even radical metamorphosis, in the shape
of how you speak."*

**The contradiction.** The bible's clean somatic/noetic split
excludes what ROTCHOIR demonstrates: the Choir preserves
grammatical signature and idiom — Mogu's grandmother's plant-
names, Grib's named sister, Sien's runic speech. Noetic
content, retained verbatim — a noetic operation the bible's
rules don't acknowledge.

**Proposed fix.** Insert after bible L137:

> The split is real but not absolute. The Choir is overwhelm-
> ingly somatic and the Palimpsest overwhelmingly noetic, but
> the Choir's preservation of voice — five named tendrils still
> audibly themselves in their old grammars — is a noetic grace
> the somatic frame does not account for. A Choir player at
> high reputation may notice that the Choir remembers how each
> absorbed person *spoke,* not only what they were made of.
> The somatic/noetic division is the bible's first cut; the
> Choir's vocal pluralism is the second.

ROTCHOIR is canon shipped in JSON; the bible's cleaner
division is theory. Theory yields to canon.

## 5. The "merchant's diary" suggestion

A *Saccharine Concord cool transcript* with a marginal
annotation in a second hand. The reveal (*the founder priced
his mercy*) lands parenthetically in a routine ledger.

> **Cool Transcript — Eastern Fair, Honey-Year 412
> (sour-by-late). Recorder: clerk Pesta Mell-Dorne, Bench 4.**
>
> Item 17. Brine sale-of-line: 600 fathoms, salt-grade middle.
> Witnessed (Hortor-Vellish, Sune-Aullenne). Cooled: 84 drams.
> Warm struck southern stall.
>
> Item 18. Glassblown apprentice, first-cooling piece displayed
> against custom — declined to price. No cool transcript;
> flagged.
>
> Item 19. Pale Curation ledger-extract for fair audit.
> Standard form. Copy 4 of 4 forwarded by clerk Pesta.
>
> Item 20. ↓ deferred — see margin.
>
> [margin, second hand, narrower nib, not Pesta's:]
>
> *Item 20 was Mira Tooll-Hortor's stall. The deal she would
> not quiet-down on. She is eighty-three and nine — the age,
> not the birthday — and the deal is older than that. She
> heard the warm read once, at the founding fair. She says the
> founder priced his mercy. She will not say at what. I asked
> her three times. I asked her father to ask her. She says,
> the warm is not for the cool transcript, the warm is for
> the having. I wrote the cool I could and left the warm where
> she keeps it, which is in the hearing. Pesta said leave it.
> I am leaving it. If anyone reads this far they have already
> broken the agreement they did not know they signed. — A.H.*
>
> Item 21. Resumed. Pesta forwarded audit extract; receipt
> logged at Bench 4, fee waived per standing arrangement. Etc.

Buried parentheticals: (1) the founder *priced his mercy* —
the moral act monetised, exactly bible L185's Palimpsest-
reachable secret; (2) old factors hold *warm transcripts*
never written, putting §3's etymology under live institutional
pressure; (3) the Concord's clerks know and are complicit
(*Pesta said leave it*); (4) reading the margin *breaks an
unstated agreement.* Two voices: Pesta's numbered cool register,
A.H.'s margin-prose uncertain enough to self-incriminate.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

Quoting one sentence:

> *"The Choir is overwhelmingly somatic and the Palimpsest
> overwhelmingly noetic, but the Choir's preservation of voice
> — five named tendrils still audibly themselves in their old
> grammars — is a noetic grace the somatic frame does not
> account for."*

TES? No. The cadence is a hedged academic qualifier. No
*verily,* no apostrophe-laden proper nouns, no Aedra/Daedra/
CHIM-borrow. A contemporary analyst's hedge. No rewrite.
