## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (1401 §2.1):** *"you are not lighting a new
fire. you are tending the same fire in a longer
step."*

**Strong.** Continuation-form for the long step
outward.

**Thin.** Each prior body has a working-form for
medium that walks **back**: Glassblown's far-rear
shelf moves at piece-transfer (2201 §4); Curation's
bequest notebook walks first-bench to first-bench
(1801 §5); Communion's chart-comparison walks
shore-to-shore (1501); Concord's seam walks no-
where (deleted at pressing-clean). The Thermoclave
equivalent has not been named. Candidate: a
daughter hearth whose parent-keeper has died
between the carry and the daughter's closing-week
sends its carry-iron **back**. The form is *the
long step in reverse*, performed only at
generation-end.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Thermoclaves,
append after 1401 §2.1):**

> *Where the parent-keeper has died between the
> carry's depart-morning and the daughter hearth's
> first kindling, the daughter's first-fired
> stoker is, in working-tradition, returned to the
> parent-hearth in the closing daughter-keeper's
> hand, at the season the parent's fire would have
> taken its third long step. The walk is single-
> keeper, single-keeper-silenced; no second-shift
> watcher is posted; no witness speaks the carry-
> sentence on the depart-morning. The stoker is
> re-fired into the parent's working stoker across
> a working-week's slow-burning, hearth-quarter
> shut. A keeper asked what the carry-back is
> answers:* "the long step turned around. the
> longer step's longer step." *The carry-back is
> the body's only working-form for receiving.*

The canonisation-authority for receiving is **fire-
internal, generation-routed, single-keeper-walked**.
The carry-back's only working-record is the keeper's
silence in the parent-hearth's working-week.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclaves (the carry-back rite, the closing-week's slow-burning)

> ### The slow-burning week
>
> The carry-back's stoker is re-fired into the
> parent-hearth's working stoker across a working-
> week of low banked heat. The hearth-quarter is
> shut to all hands but the closing daughter-
> keeper's; daughter-stokers stand idle; the
> rubbing-archive admits no dawn-rotation
> cataloguer. The keeper tends the banked heat,
> turns the carry-back's iron once at each dawn,
> and at the seventh dawn the iron is no longer
> distinguishable from the parent's stoker.
>
> ### What the body does not do
>
> The carry-back's only working-record is **the
> parent-hearth's stoker after the seventh dawn** —
> an artifact whose two-keeper authorship is
> dispersed by the body's own continuation-form. A
> daughter-keeper closing a hearth without a
> parent-death does not perform the carry-back;
> the form is not generalisable to ordinary
> closing. Twelve carry-backs since founding;
> remembered keeper-to-keeper, never named in the
> rubbing-archive.
>
> ### The Curation's filing
>
> A dawn-rotation cataloguer admitted on the days
> bracketing the closing-week files the working-
> period as *hearth-quarter closed by working-
> tradition*. Three depots hold the category in
> their working-shelf vocabulary (Depot 11, 7, 3);
> the others do not, by the working-tradition that
> no depot files a body-form it has not personally
> encountered. The Curation's filing of a carry-

Four cadence-authorities: carry-rite (body, fire-

Three working-traditions, three non-textual
carriers (keeper-to-keeper succession; the banked
fire's seventh-dawn iron; the cataloguer's
silence). Both bodies' silence is the form, eighth

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted (§3, the carry-back):** *"Where the
parent-keeper has died between the carry's depart-
morning and the daughter hearth's first kindling,
the daughter's first-fired stoker is returned, at
the season the parent's fire would have taken its
third long step, to the parent-hearth in the
closing daughter-keeper's hand."*

Working-modern register. *The parent-keeper has
died*, *the daughter's first-fired stoker is
returned*, *the closing daughter-keeper's hand* —
plain craft-clerical compounds, lower-case, no
capitalised abstract nouns. *Has died between*,
*at the season*, and *would have taken* are
contemporary; no fronted nominal, no *whereby /
hath / lieth*.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"Where the Parent-
Keeper hath died betwixt the Carry's depart-morn
and the first kindling of the Daughter Hearth, lo,
the Daughter's first-fired Stoker shall be borne
back unto the Parent-Hearth in the Hand of the
Closing Daughter-Keeper."* Tells: capitalised
*Parent-Keeper / Carry / Stoker*; *hath died,
betwixt, lo, shall, borne, unto*; fronted *Where
the Parent-Keeper hath died*. Actual sentence is
none of these.

**Pass.**
