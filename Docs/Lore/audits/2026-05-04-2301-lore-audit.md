## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible line 121:* *"the cataclysm... overwrote reality
> itself, imperfectly, the way text on a medieval manuscript
> was scraped off and written over with the old words still
> faintly visible underneath."*

**Strong.** The imperfection is canonical.

**Thin.** **No Palimpsest *doctrine* on the cataclysm.** The
Choir has *compost that hasn't surrendered yet.* The Concord
has *the first close.* The Brine has slack-tide. The
Palimpsest — the body whose entire premise is *reading what
is under the present* — has no in-world reading of its own
founding event.

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar, "The first reading
and the last"):

> Palimpsest readers do not agree on the cataclysm. Two
> doctrinal positions: the *first-reading* school holds the
> cataclysm was a single overwrite-event, after which the
> layering began — over-text is the new world, under-text is
> the old. The *last-reading* school holds the cataclysm is
> still happening — every present moment is itself an
> overwrite, the under-text is yesterday, and what most
> readers call the *cataclysm* was only the loudest stroke.
>
> The two schools do not anathematize each other. Palimpsest
> doctrine treats incompatible readings as themselves a kind
> of reading; both are correct under their own reading-
> grammar. A reading-room may host both at one convening;
> their utterances simply layer.

The cataclysm dissolves into a procedural disagreement about
*what kind of event it was;* the body's mechanics absorb the
disagreement rather than resolving it.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Every other body has a hand — Choir voices,
Veshen, Concord juniors, dry-walkers, Curation cataloguers.

**Thin.** **No Palimpsest-authored document specimen.** What
does an echo-that-asks-questions sound like *writing?* The
bible is silent.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The double-hand"):

> Palimpsest-authored documents are written in **two-layer
> hand.** The over-hand is the writer's present-self,
> ordinary script. The under-hand is the writer's layer-self,
> written between or beneath the over-lines in a finer stroke.
> The two are not draft and final; they are concurrent. A
> reader takes the document at both layers.
>
> The under-hand is not always commentary. Sometimes the
> same sentence in a different tense; sometimes a name that
> did not survive the over-writing; sometimes only a single
> *under-line* drawn beneath an over-text word, marking
> where the writer's layer-self disagreed with what the
> present-self committed to ink. Convention treats the
> under-line as binding — the present-self has signed, the
> under-self has reserved.
>
> A document with no under-hand is *singled.* The Concord
> accepts singled documents as legible; the Palimpsest
> considers them suspect.

Authorship is **structurally double**, the way the Concord's
clauses are *open* or *closed* — every specimen carries both
readings on the same page.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Thin.** **What other factions call Palimpsest Sight.** The
bible uses *the Glimpse* and *Echo Reading*. The other bodies
must have words for what they themselves perceive when the
layering bleeds through their own work.

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar, "Names for the under-text"):

> - **Palimpsest:** *the Glimpse* (toggled vision); *the
>   under-text* (what is read); *the through-line* (what
>   persists across all layers).
> - **Choir:** *the dreaming-mouth.* The spore-network
>   fruits in patterns that mirror older soil-layers — a
>   cathedral's floor-plan in mushroom-rings, a river's
>   trace in a white seam. The Choir does not call this
>   perception; it calls it *dreaming.*
> - **Concord:** *un-aspected reading* — a clause read in a
>   tense the law does not recognize. Clerks who can do this
>   are quietly valued and never named in their own person.
> - **Pale Curation:** *the under-card field* — a reserved
>   space at the back of the bid-card, blank unless a senior
>   signs that the artifact has *layer.*
> - **Brine Communion:** *low-water sight.* When the tide is
>   out, the seabed shows the wreck. The under-text is what
>   the tide has not yet returned.
> - **Thermoclaves (Floor):** *the cold reading* — the hearth
>   gone out, the page still warm. **(Refusal):** *the
>   unburnt* — the part the fire did not take, treated as
>   the only honest part of the document.
> - **Glassblown:** *the bubble in the bead.* An inclusion
>   in the molten glass — a flaw in the over-text testifying
>   to the under-text.

The Refusal idiom is the **most theologically charged** — it
elevates the under-text to canonical status, which Floor
and Ember keepers will not say but do not contradict.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

> *Bible line 125:* *"a library that reads itself — the
> accumulated memory of the world, pressing up against the
> present, occasionally breaking through."*

**Strong.** The metaphor is exact. A library, not a temple.

**Thin.** **No address.** The bible does not say *where* the
library is. The Concord's open-braid hangs on a wall; the
Choir's First Root has tunnels; the Brine has inland routes.
The Palimpsest's library floats free.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The reading-rooms"):

> The library is not a building. A *reading-room* is any
> place where the layering is dense enough that two attuned
> beings can convene and read each other's layers. They are
> not built; they are *found,* and once found are kept.
> Hundreds exist — a ruined chapel where two layers of altar
> persist, a roadside well whose under-text is a fortified
> spring, a stone bench that was once a courthouse step. Most
> are informal: the bend in a hallway, the second floor of a
> house no one rebuilt the same way twice.
>
> A reading-room is *kept* by whoever was first to convene
> in it; the keeper marks it with a single chalk-stroke in
> the under-hand register, invisible to a singled reader. A
> walker who reads the chalk and adds their own stroke
> beneath becomes a co-keeper.
>
> Reading-rooms cannot be claimed by other factions. The
> Concord cannot file against one; the Choir can grow into
> a room's stone and the room persists. The Palimpsest's
> territory is not the room — it is the *attunement* that
> lets the room be read.

A library distributed across the world's skin, kept by chalk
only some readers can see.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

> *Bible line 189:* *"Transcription — the player becomes a
> memory, written into the palimpsest forever, experiencing
> all of history simultaneously, never acting again, only
> witnessing."*

**Strong** at statement; **thin** at procedure. Choir's
Communion has the First Root and the *we have dreamed of the
mountain* line; Concord's walker's-clause now has its draft-
and-close. Transcription has only a sentence.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The reader-of-readers"):

> A walker offered Transcription is not asked to stop
> reading. They are asked to *keep reading,* one layer
> further: the under-text of the walker's own present-self.
> They choose whether to enter it.
>
> If they enter, they become the reader of that layer; their
> present-self becomes the over-text. Both persist. Other
> readers can address either of the walker's two readings;
> most alternate. The walker does not vanish. The walker is
> *kept doubled* — a *through-line.* NPCs who knew the walker
> before continue to know them, and the walker may answer in
> a tense the NPC did not expect.
>
> A walker who declines is filed under-hand as *available,
> un-entered.* The reservation does not expire.

The first apotheosis where the walker **does not lose
themselves; they double.** Choir Communion absorbs; Concord
closure forecloses; Palimpsest Transcription *splits and
keeps both sides.*

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

The Brine has *remembered tense.* The Concord has
*clause-aspect.* The Refusal has the *dropped count-particle.*
The Palimpsest has, until now, no faction-grammar.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "Layer-grammar"):

> Every Palimpsest verb has an **over-form** (the present
> action) and an **under-form** (the same action in the
> layer beneath).
>
> > *I am walking* (over) / *I have walked* (under) — a
> > being walking now along a road they walked before.
>
> The combination is not redundant; it specifies that both
> readings are active. A reader hearing only an over-form
> treats the speaker as *singled* — fully present, no
> under-text — and reads them with reduced attunement. A
> reader hearing only an under-form treats the speaker as
> *only-echo.*
>
> A speaker may **invert:** under-form first, over-form
> second. The inverted utterance commits the speaker to the
> under-text as the more authoritative reading — the
> Palimpsest equivalent of a Concord senior reservation.
>
> Palimpsest grammar has **no future-form.** A future verb
> is, by doctrine, *not yet a layer.* Speakers committing to
> a future utterance use the over-form alone and mark the
> absent under-form by a small pause. Readers hear the pause;
> non-readers hear only the over-form.

Brine's remembered tense holds past as authoritative; the
Concord's clause-aspect commits to *closure-state*; the
Refusal grammar drops *count.* The Palimpsest's grammar
drops *the forward direction.*

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

Pair with dry-walker (1301), junior cataloguer (2001),
mid-fire keeper (2101), Hall-of-Open junior (2201).

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar, "A morning at a reading-
room"):

> Two convenors arrive separately at pre-dawn, each carrying
> a *layered notebook* — over-hand right, under-hand left.
> They sit opposite, silent. The first to recognize a
> through-line (the bench was a courthouse step; a saint's
> hand still chalked beneath the render) marks it; the other
> countersigns by laying chalk *under*, not over.
>
> The mid-morning *trade:* each reads aloud one sentence in
> layer-grammar — over-form, then under-form. The other
> answers in the same grammar, not commenting but offering
> the sentence's *layer-equivalent* from their own life. The
> sentences are not paired in meaning; they are paired in
> *layer-depth.* First-reading convenors pair with first-
> reading answers; schools do not cross.
>
> Mid-day silence. Lunch is taken separately in the room,
> neither rising — the Palimpsest does not consider lunch a
> break in convening.
>
> Afternoon: the *un-shared.* Each writes in their own
> notebook a single sentence the *other* would never have
> spoken; the under-hand stroke beneath it is the *gift* —
> the layer of the other's life imagined into one's own page.
> Notebooks are not exchanged. The gift remains in the
> writer's hand.
>
> Dusk: convenors leave separately. The chalk remains. A
> walker who finds the room and cannot read the chalk has
> found nothing — the bench is only a bench.

The Palimpsest's social form is **convening without
conversation:** two readers exchanging layer-depth, not
meaning.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Palimpsest R3 (the reading-room)

R3 promotes the Palimpsest from philosophical counter-weight
to body with its own anatomy. Mechanics now in the corpus:

- **Reading-rooms** (Dim 4) — distributed library; territory
  is the graph, not the rooms.
- **Double-hand** (Dim 2) — over-hand and under-hand on every
  document; *singled* documents are suspect.
- **Under-line as reservation** (Dim 2) — equal weight to a
  Concord senior bead, but immanent rather than appended.
- **First-reading and last-reading schools** (Dim 1) — two
  doctrines on the cataclysm; layer at convenings.
- **Layer-grammar** (Dim 6) — over/under verb-pair; inversion
  as senior-reservation equivalent; no future-form.
- **Reader-of-readers and the doubled walker** (Dim 5) —
  Transcription as splitting-and-keeping-both.
- **A morning at a reading-room** (Dim 7) — the trade, the
  un-shared, the chalk that remains.

**The chalk reads the open-braid.** A reading-room's chalk is
structurally analogous to the Concord's bone bead — both mark
*adjacent-not-filed* status, both placed by the keeper, both
read only by attuned eyes. A walker carrying Concord beads
and Palimpsest chalk in one satchel carries two bodies'
*consciences* in two media.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**The bleed I can't ignore: the five Choir voices are
Palimpsest mechanics inside Choir territory.** Bible line 39
asserts the Choir *digests the past* and that this
*destroys the memory encoded.* ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md (lines
24-28) is explicit: *"the Choir absorbs persons but does not
erase the language they came in with."* Mogu speaks Daoist;
Grib speaks Dostoevskian; Sien speaks Kalevala-cadence — the
linguistic-traditional layer of each absorbed person
*survives* absorption. A Palimpsest principle (preservation
as layering) is operating inside the most Choir-aligned of
structures.

In layer-grammar: each voice is *singled-then-doubled.*
*We taste you on the air, walker* (over-form, Choir) and
*I had a sister; the fire went out* (under-form, Grib)
coexist in the same fruiting body — *layered fruiting* to a
Palimpsest reader. The Choir believes it has digested; the
under-text testifies it has not.

The bible needs one clause near line 209:

> The Choir's most absorbed beings retain a layer the Choir
> cannot or does not erase — the linguistic-traditional
> register of the person before absorption. Palimpsest
> readers identify this as a *soft Palimpsest mechanic*
> operating inside Choir territory; Choir doctrine does not
> name it. Both readings are correct in their own grammar.

This is the **most consequential contradiction the corpus has
surfaced.** The Choir-Palimpsest *not at war* of bible line
208 gains a deeper reason: they cannot go to war because the
Choir already runs partial Palimpsest mechanics in its
absorbed members.

**Smaller bleed.** Canonical sites — First Root, Hall of
Open, Curation vault, salt-flats — are all reading-rooms by
attunement. The chalk is in the First Root too. The Choir
does not see it.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"A speaker may invert the layers: under-form first, over-
> form second. The inverted utterance commits the speaker
> to the under-text as the more authoritative reading."*

No archaism. No apostrophe-fantasy. No mythic abstractions.
A grammar-of-speech-act statement. The *layer-grammar*
sounds strange because the body treats verb-order as
evidentiary weight, not because the narrator performs a
register. TES version: *"The Sage who would rebuke the
present, lo, doth speak the older word foremost, that the
world might know the past hath greater claim upon him."* I
did not write that.

Stylised vocabulary (*reading-room*, *over-hand*, *under-
hand*, *singled*, *layer-grammar*, *through-line*) names
instruments and rites. Narrator reports them; does not adopt
their cadence — same restraint as the Curation *bid-mood*,
the Brine *remembered tense*, the Concord *clause-aspect*.

Pass.
