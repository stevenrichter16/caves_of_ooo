**Finding: an eighth value — *interim*.** The
splinter-not-yet-ash interval cannot be sorted by
any of the seven existing values. *Admits* and
*forbids* both presuppose the medium-extensibility
question, but the splinter is not under that

canonised separately. *Trivial* would name a no-

day of bench-splintering on one side, the year-

incompatible answers; the two membranes here give
**sequential** answers — the splinter enters the
drawer, the splinter leaves for the furnace — not
incompatible but successive. *Delegated* would
route the question outward; the Concord routes
nothing. *Already-extended* names a past-tense
question; the splinter is in *open present*, the
drawer-week is real working-time, charcoal weighed
weekly, the bundle's own working-life ongoing.
*Provisional* names a slot whose closure is
deferred to evidence the body cannot generate;
the Concord has the closing-form (the winter-

**knows when it ends**. **Interim** names the

## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (2101 §2.1):** *"the bench is splintered at
its working-week's ending; the splinter is bundled
into the drawer; the bundle is fired at year-turn;
the ash returns to the workshop's mortar."*

**Strong.** Three-membrane cycle canonised across
a working-year.

**Thin.** Cycle-time has not been counted in *off-
cadence years*. The ordinary year runs
splintering / drawer / winter-furnace / ash-return.
But occasionally a winter-furnace **fails to draw**
— flue choked on a salt-wind, charcoal wet, the
weigh-master's apprentice ill. The Concord files
this as the **unburn-day**: a single roster-line
naming the splintering's week, the failed firing,
and the rescheduled firing — sometimes a month
later, sometimes the *next* year-turn. The drawer-
week extends to a drawer-month or in worst cases
a drawer-year. The splinter is not re-classed; a
splinter held nine weeks and one held one year
and nine weeks are the same working-form. **The
interim's interval is canonised as elastic** —
fixed in *kind*, variable in *length*.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Saccharine
Concord — append after the winter-furnace
absorption-paragraph when written):**

> *The unburn-day is the working-form for a winter-
> furnace that does not draw on its appointed
> morning. The drawer-week extends — by a roster-
> entry, never by silent default — to the next
> available furnace-morning, which in severe draw-
> failure is the next year's winter-furnace. A
> splinter held nine weeks and a splinter held one
> year and nine weeks are the same working-form.
> The body's only change is the furnace-roster
> line:* "splinter held interim, firing rescheduled
> to [date]." *The line is read aloud at the next
> splintering-week's opening recital: the body
> acknowledges the held splinter before it
> splinters a new bench.*

Stratification canonises as **interim's elastic
interval**, with the unburn-day as the working-
form for the variability.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (2101 §2.2, the drawer-bundle):**
*"the bundle is wrapped in linen, tied in three
loops, marked at the third loop with the splintering-
week's date in graphite."*

**Strong.** Bundle-marking canonised.

**Thin.** The mark's authorship is unsorted. Each
bundle is rolled by the bench's *retiring carpenter*
— the one who worked the bench through its last
year, splinters it with their own hand, folds it
into linen and ties the third-loop graphite mark.
The signature is the splintering-week's date; the
carpenter's hand is unsigned; the body **suppresses
authorship below the working-week**. A bundle from
year 412's splintering-week 41 reads *412-41* in
graphite, no indication of which of the collegium's
seven carpenters folded it. The linen itself,
however, carries the **fold-hand** — the quarter-
crease, the third-loop knot's tightness, the
graphite-pressure. A weigh-master lifting a bundle
to the furnace can name the carpenter without the
roster. The body has no working-form for *citing*
fold-hand; only for *reading* it. The ninth
authorship-position canonises as: **hand-legible
without hand-credit** — structurally unsigned,
operationally readable.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Saccharine
Concord, new sidebar "The bundle's fold-hand"):**

> *A drawer-bundle's authorship is suppressed at
> every working-form: the third-loop graphite
> carries the splintering-week's date and no
> carpenter's name. The fold-hand — the linen's
> quarter-crease, the third-loop tightness, the
> graphite-pressure — carries the carpenter's
> signature operationally; a weigh-master can name
> the folder without the roster. The body has no
> working-form for citing fold-hand; only for
> reading it. Authorship survives in the gesture
> that folded and is destroyed in the firing — a
> firing the retiring carpenter, by working-form,
> is not present at, having retired the same year.*

Authorship-doctrine refines: 0701 named *attribution-
pending* (Curation unknown-origin) where the author-
bench is the intake and the origin-body is open;
0801 names *hand-legible without hand-credit*
(Concord drawer-bundle) where the body has the
authorship operationally but suppresses it at every
working-form. The two together canonise the
difference between **structurally unidentified**
and **structurally unsigned but legible** as two
distinct authorship-positions.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord (the splinter-not-yet-ash interval; drawer-week-form; unburn-day; interim on extensibility)

> ### The drawer-week
>
> A bench splintered at its working-year's end is
> bundled into linen, tied in three loops, marked
> at the third loop with the splintering-week's
> date in graphite, and laid in the drawer. The
> drawer is opened each working-week by the weigh-
> master, who reads aloud every bundle's third-loop
> date and the days remaining until the year-turn
> morning. The bundles are not handled, only
> counted. The drawer-week extends from autumn's
> splintering-week to the year-turn firing.
>
> ### The drawer-week-form
>
> The retiring carpenter, folding, says to her own
> ear: *"the bench is no longer the bench. the ash
> is not yet the ash. we hold the bundle. the
> drawer is the holding."* The form is single-
> instance per bench per cycle; never repeated
> unless the bench is somehow reconstituted (which,
> by working-form, cannot occur).
>
> ### The unburn-day
>
> When the winter-furnace fails to draw on its
> appointed morning — flue choked, charcoal wet,
> apprentice ill — the body files an unburn-day:
> a single-line furnace-roster entry naming the
> splintering-week, the failed firing-date, and
> the rescheduled firing. The drawer-week extends.
> The bundles are read each working-week with the
> unburn-line spoken before the date. A bundle
> may be held one extra cycle in worst-year cases.
>
> ### The fold-hand
>
> The body suppresses carpenter-credit at every
> working-form but the linen's fold-hand carries
> the gesture: a weigh-master at year-turn, lifting
> a bundle to the furnace, can name the carpenter
> without the roster. The body has no working-form
> for citing fold-hand; only for reading it. The
> firing destroys the gesture-trace along with the
> medium.

Ten cadence-authorities across the Concord pile
(prior nine across recital / inward-generate /
splintering / drawer / furnace / ash-return /
empty-box-archive / structural-closure / deferral-
floor; tenth at this sitting) — **the drawer-week-
form is the only working-register whose every
utterance binds the body to a calendared interval
that the working-form's own calendar will close**.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted (§3, the drawer-week-form):** *"the bench
is no longer the bench. the ash is not yet the
ash. we hold the bundle. the drawer is the holding."*

Working-modern register. *No longer*, *not yet*,
*hold the bundle*, *the drawer is the holding* —
plain craft-clerical, lower-case, no archaism.
*Is*, *hold* are contemporary. No fronted nominal,
no *hath / lieth / unto / verily / wherein /
betwixt*.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"The Bench is no
longer the Bench. The Ash is not yet the Ash.
We hold the Bundle. The Drawer is the Holding,
verily, until the Firing-Day come unto us."*
Tells: capitalised nominal compounds; *verily*;
*until... come unto us*; the rhetorical fronting
and the anchoring on a Capital-D Day. Actual
sentence is none of these.
