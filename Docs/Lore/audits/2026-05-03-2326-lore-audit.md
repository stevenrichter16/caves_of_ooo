## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible, line 21:* "the way text on a medieval manuscript was
> scraped off and written over."

**Strong.** Aftermath, breaking-moment, duration, world-before,
smoke-born twenty (sibling 2315).

**Thin.** **The false dawn.** No middle-aftermath: no
near-recovery whose failure is itself contested.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "The thaw"):**

> Roughly eighty years after the cataclysm, the surface warmed
> for seventeen years. Crops returned where they had not grown
> in three generations. Two coastal towns refounded. The
> Concord dates it from *the spring of the short tide*; the
> Brine concur. The Pale Curation files it as the *interregnum
> thaw* and notes the original index-card has been rewritten
> twice.
>
> The thaw ended. Accounts diverge. The Concord says *a held
> rate broke*. The Brine say *the long-overdue tide arrived
> early and was lost*. The Curation has the seventeen-year
> entry physically locked; the key is held by the senior on
> duty, briefed never to open without a non-Curation witness.
> The Choir does not distinguish the thaw at all. The
> Palimpsest's reading shows the layer *brighter than the
> layers around it* — suspicious, since bright layers were
> always edited. The Glassblown have one sentence: *we did not
> blow during the thaw.*

A near-recovery whose failure is itself a contested event.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Charter Receipt, R-7, redactions, time-release
annotations, lossy-scribe transcripts (2315).

**Thin.** **The non-human author.** No corpus artifact is
*Choir-authored*. What does Choir writing look like?

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "What the Choir writes"):**

> The Choir writes through a single fruiting body acting as
> scribe; the body produces a cellulose sheet from its own
> mass, and writing is impressed by chemical pressure, not
> ink. Choir documents are bone-coloured, damp when fresh, and
> bear no date. The pronoun is always *we*; verbs avoid past
> tense (the past is composted into the present and not
> separable). The Choir does not sign. A document is
> identifiable by absence of signature and by the faint living
> warmth it retains for the first day; afterward it stiffens
> and is indistinguishable from old paper, except it does not
> yellow.
>
> The Pale Curation accepts Choir documents as *primary
> sources*. The Concord does not treat them as commercial
> paper — they cannot be sworn against. The Choir does not
> mind: it writes to be read, not honoured.

A non-human register: plural pronoun, no date, alive on day
one.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Substrate-leak, Choir metabolism, Echo limits,
consensus-stratum, standing line, held depth (2315). Five
strata.

**Thin.** **Thermoclave substrate-claim.** Fire-grammar exists;
a *stratum* does not.

**Concrete addition (bible expansion, "The kept ember"):**

> The Thermoclaves' substrate-claim is the **kept ember** — the
> stratum at which heat persists in the absence of visible
> fuel. Physical (mineral aggregates, banked coal-beds) and
> metaphysical (every sustained system has a kept ember; cold
> death is the failure of an ember to be kept).
>
> Of the six strata named — material, temporal, consensual,
> standing, held-depth, kept ember — only the kept ember is
> defined by *absence of the obvious sign of itself*. The
> hearth-keeper's craft is **reading-the-warm**: a senior can
> hold a hand above a hearth and tell within a minute whether
> the ember is kept or the residual warmth is decay.
>
> The Choir, told of reading-the-warm, said: *we have always
> known. the soil under a kept hearth is alive after the fire
> goes visible-out. the ember walks down.*

Substrate-as-negative-image: absence is a presence.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** Mirror endings, Annihilist, Balanced Account
(2307), open-account dead (2310), going-deep (2315), unfiring.

**Thin.** **Thermoclave apotheosis.** None.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "The long warming"):**

> A Thermoclave at highest reputation is invited to **the long
> warming**: the Hearth-Keeper offers a place at a banked
> hearth kept since the cohort's cold one. Apprentices feed
> the hearth in shifts at the walker's preferred rate. The
> walker may leave at any time. Most do not.
>
> They remain until the kept ember is fully *passed across* —
> the warmth at which their hand reads the ember becomes
> indistinguishable from the warmth they themselves give back.
> This is **coming level with the hearth**. At level, the
> walker is not dead; they are *kept* — a persistent warmth at
> the hearth-side with presence and a measurable temperature
> differential. They can be addressed; replies are felt as a
> brief rise in the hearth's heat. Some are chatty. Most are
> not.
>
> The slowest apotheosis — weeks, not moments — and the only
> one reversed by *external interruption*: a hearth allowed to
> fully cool releases the walker into ordinary embodiment. The
> Brine consider it the *tide-image* of the going-deep.

Seven apotheosis-shapes. The long warming surrenders
**heat-difference** — the walker stops being warmer or colder
than what holds them.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Choir pronouns; Palimpsest tense; Brine joining;
Thermoclave fire-grammar (round 1); Glassblown unsaid mood;
Concord trade tense; assayer hand-grammar (2315).

**Thin.** **Thermoclave verbal grammar past fire-counting.**

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "The tending tense"):**

> Thermoclave speech distinguishes four primary fire-verbs.
>
> - **Kindle.** Beginning a fire from cold material.
>   Thermoclaves *kindle once* — the cohort's founding fire is
>   the only canonical kindling; subsequent fires are
>   *carried*, never *kindled*. *I kindled my morning fire* is
>   small heresy.
> - **Carry.** Bringing a fire by ember from one hearth to
>   another. An interrupted carry is a *dropped fire* — civic
>   shame, confessed in the hearth-log.
> - **Tend.** Maintaining a fire at-hearth. Continuous aspect
>   only — *I am tending*, never *I tend*. The simple form is
>   reserved for the *role*.
> - **Bank.** Reducing a fire to its kept ember without
>   extinguishing. The hardest verb. **Banking requires a
>   witness** — a Thermoclave banking unwitnessed is,
>   grammatically, *not banking but extinguishing*.
>
> *I will kindle* is doctrinally suspect (the kindling has
> happened). *I will bank* requires a witness named in the
> same clause.

**Banking requires a witness** is the doctrinal punch.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Strong.** Tuesday Mornings (six factions), Quiet Hour,
Concord weddings, Brine committal (2315).

**Thin.** **Thermoclave Tuesday Morning.**

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "A Thermoclave Tuesday
morning"):**

> The cohort wakes by hearth-warmth, not clock. The
> hearth-keeper banks the night fire to a kept ember whose
> temperature falls predictably; members rise as their
> sleeping-spaces drop below the personal warmth threshold
> each negotiated at apprenticeship. A Thermoclave does not
> own an alarm.
>
> The morning's first act is the **carry** — every active
> member takes ember from night hearth to day hearth in a
> single procession. Children walk the route to learn it. A
> dropped carry is the smallest civic event of the week; the
> dropper buys the hearth-keeper a gift in the next market.
>
> Breakfast cooks on the day hearth, shared. Many mornings are
> silent in the *banked* sense (Dim 3). The hearth-keeper then
> banks the day hearth; members disperse to the cohort's
> surface-occupation. The hearth is *tended* by whomever is
> nearest, but *banking* is always the hearth-keeper's.
>
> Visitors are welcomed at the day hearth, never the night. A
> Brine guest receives salt for their drink; a Concord guest
> the floor across the hearth; a Choir tendril, soil-side
> seating (most hearths have a Choir patch by mutual
> arrangement); a Curationist a chair, not spoken to until
> they have written their first line — a joke the Curation
> consider *welcoming*.

The day hearth is the social organ; the night hearth, the
doctrinal one; the carry between them, the daily liturgy.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclave round 2

Round 2 contributions are above (Dims 4–8).

**Proposed senior figure: Hearth-Keeper Tossin of the Eleventh.**

Tossin keeps an eleventh-fire cohort (smoke-born bracket,
sibling 2315). Fifty-three years banked-tending; among the
eleventh-fire's seniors. Her cohort's carry-history reaches
the cold one through ten chains.

Tossin is the only hearth-keeper to have **banked
unwitnessed**, doctrinally — once, during the cataclysm-
anniversary year, when her assigned witness died on the road.
She declared the bank *witnessed by the road*; her cohort
accepted after a long meeting. The other ten hearth-keepers
agreed *the road* is not generally permissible, but Tossin's
specific bank stands. Unrepeatable by deliberate construction.

She corresponds with Tide-Reader Nesh of the Coastal Embayment
(sibling 2315) on the held-depth/kept-ember symmetry. Working
hypothesis: the two are *the same stratum sampled by water and
by heat*. Nesh's *the tide is the alloy* has its grammar-form
in Tossin's *the ember is the tide* — each written down once,
in stock that degrades. Neither has shown the other; each
believes the other has thought further and refuses to ask.

Tossin computes nothing. Apprentices come to be witnessed; she
witnesses; she does not lecture. Her single circulated phrase,
recorded against her wishes: *a fire that does not need to be
tended is not yet kept; it is only not extinguished.*

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a)** Bible:39 — *"The Choir does not distinguish between
mutual benefit and self-interest."* And ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md:94
(Grib) — *"Sentimental things rot — and not even in a useful
way, like we do."* Grib's *like we do* distinguishes useful
(Choir) from useless (sentimental) rot — the discrimination the
bible says the Choir cannot make. Mogu's *nothing of value is
ever lost — only translated* answers no. The named voices are
*not aligned* on the bible's symmetry doctrine.

**Recommend bible sidebar at the symmetry-doctrine line:**

> The named absorbed do not all agree with the network's
> symmetry-grammar. Mogu and Nam do; Grib openly does not;
> Sien and Sopp are ambiguous. Whether the Choir's tolerance
> is *true equanimity* or *strategic* (a Choir requiring
> symmetry-doctrine could not absorb honest persons) is
> unclear. The Choir, asked, says only: *the song wants every
> voice it has ever heard. it does not want them to agree.*

First acknowledgment that **the Choir contains internal
disagreement** — by design, not failure. The player who joins
does not have to agree.

**(b)** Bible:208 — *"They are not at war"*. And
RotChoir.json:442 — the Palimpsest's substrate-sealing would
*silence* the Choir. Their non-war is asymmetric to peace.

**Recommend "How They View Each Other" add:**

> Each would unmake the other if the other's project
> succeeded — the Palimpsest's substrate-sealing would silence
> the Choir; the Choir's full integration would compost the
> readable layers. They are not in conflict because neither
> expects to succeed in their generation. The next four
> hundred years are unallocated. The conflict, if it comes,
> will be over allocation.

Non-war as **deferred existential opposition**.

  Carry clean. T finally looked at me. Said: 'a fire that does

The bombshell is Mira's last marginal note — a sixteen-year-old
smoke-born independently arriving at the unifying hypothesis
Tossin and Nesh have privately formed and Veshen has approached
through the substrate-ledger. The smoke-born are the
bridge-cohort, and the bridge is being made *in the
marginalia*, not the doctrine. The Concord's removed Q.14, the
Brine's *the tide is the alloy*, and Mira's *all the same
waiting* are three independent witnesses to **the same coming
event**. The bible's *six hundred years and not in a hurry* is,
parenthetically, on a calendar.

Plant: any apprentice bench. Reading another's hearth-log is
shame, not crime. Mira, asked, would deny it.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"A fire that does not need to be tended is not yet kept;
> it is only not extinguished."*

Tossin's phrase. Plain present tense; no archaisms, no
Aedra/Daedra/Towers/CHIM, no apostrophe-fantasy. Doctrine
rides on a single technical distinction — *kept* versus *not
extinguished* — operational, not mystical: a Thermoclave hand
reads the difference. A TES book would have written *"And lo,
the Lord of the Hearth-Spire did teach unto the cold-faithful
that a flame which kindleth itself unbidden is no flame at all
but only the ghost of warmth."* Mine has none of that
vocabulary. Doctrine is in the noun *kept* — operationalised
by *needs to be tended*, empirically observable. Confirmed.
