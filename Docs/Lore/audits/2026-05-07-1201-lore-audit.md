## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (0001 §2.1, proposed bible append):** *"The fire
that did not finish — I am here to finish it."*

**Strong.** First-firing utterance and the Communion
variant (*"...and to be the witness of its finishing"*) are
canon. They place the practitioner inside a multi-
generational argument-by-demonstration.

**Thin.** The utterance is the **moment**. The bible has
not canonised what came **before** it: the years of pre-
firing apprenticeship. The first-firing reads as a wedding
without a courtship.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Glassblown Remnant
subsection, append after the first-firing block):**

> *Before first-firing an apprentice is* unpoured *— the
> body's working term for any practitioner who has not yet
> drawn molten material from a working furnace. An unpoured
> apprentice may attend the cooling-watch, fetch fuel,
> sweep the floor, polish a finished piece, and (under
> senior supervision) hold a piece during its second
> witnessing. They may not strike the batch-glyph; they may
> not pour; they may not speak in the kiln-house above the
> hearth-line (the height of a kneeling senior's mouth) for
> the duration of any working pour. The unpoured period is
> not measured in years. It is measured in* watches stood —
> *the number of cooling-watches the apprentice has
> attended without intervention. The body's working
> standard is six hundred. A senior who first-fires an
> apprentice with fewer than six hundred watches stood is,
> by discipline, hasty; with more than nine hundred,
> withholding. Either is filed against the senior, not the
> apprentice. The apprentice does not know the count. The
> count is kept on a small dark-glass tablet in the watch-
> house; the senior runs a cool finger across it before
> calling first-firing.*

The unpoured period is the body's *long inhale* before the
held breath of the first pour. The count-on-tablet, kept
silently, is symmetric to the Concord's day-list: a working
record the apprentice never sees. Its existence is
canonised here.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (0001 §2.2):** *"A piece bearing one mark and not
the other is an orphan."*

**Strong.** Glassblown authorship is paratextual: batch-
glyph + pourer's-mark, both anonymous, conjunction-
identified through the watch-house strip-archive.

**Thin.** Both glyph and mark are made *by the practitioner*.
The body has no canonised authorship-form **for the
unpoured.** An unpoured apprentice contributes labour to
many pieces and authors none.

**Concrete enhancement (bible new sidebar, "The
unmarked count"):**

> *An unpoured apprentice keeps no register. The body
> forbids it: a marked record by an unpoured hand is, in
> working reading, a forgery of authorship. Apprentices
> nevertheless track their own watches stood by an
> unmarked physical count — most commonly a small handful
> of cooled glass beads, kept in a pocket of the apron,
> one bead added at the close of each watch the apprentice
> attended without intervention. The beads are unmarked
> and indistinguishable. The body does not name this
> practice and does not forbid it. A senior who finds the
> beads in an apron during pressing-clean does not remark
> on them. At first-firing the apprentice empties the
> beads into the cool-quarter's drainage trough — the
> body's working-form for* the unmarked count, surrendered.
> *The trough is sluiced once a year; in the residue, on
> the rare occasion a Pale Curation cataloguer is permitted
> to sample, beads are recovered in numbers that do not
> match any watch-house's official count. The discrepancy
> has not been canon-resolved.*

Authorship-mode now triangulates: working-pieces (batch-
glyph + pourer's-mark, post-firing); side-pours (mark only);
unmarked count (no marks, pre-firing, surrendered at
threshold). The Curation discrepancy is the bombshell-seed
for §5.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (0001 §2.4):** *"The cooling is possible."*

**Strong.** Demonstrated-cooling doctrine is canon. The
faction's cosmology is argument-by-physical-object, not
theology.

**Thin.** *Demonstrated cooling* is a private senior's
reading. The bible has not canonised the **institutional**
form: the cooling-watch as a discipline (multiple
witnesses; tie-breaking; declaration; appeal), and what
happens when the watch's reading is later contradicted by
the piece itself.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Glassblown Remnant
subsection, append after the cosmology block):**

> *A cooling-watch is conducted by* three witnesses *—
> the* pourer*, the* steady-watch *(a senior who has not
> handled the iron during this pour; her reading is the
> doctrinal anchor), and the* cross-watch *(a senior from
> a different working-pour, called in for cooling only;
> her reading is the body's check on local bias). All
> three must declare the piece cooled before the cooling-
> watch concludes. A two-of-three reading is a* held watch
> *— the piece returns to the cooling-table for a second
> period and is re-witnessed. A held watch concluding on
> three-of-three is canon-cooled. A held watch concluding
> a second time on two-of-three is a* refused cooling*: the
> piece is broken and returned to the kiln. The pourer is
> not at fault. The body's discipline is that a refused
> cooling is the watch-house's working failure, not the
> pourer's; both seniors and the pourer share the filing.*

> *A piece that has been declared canon-cooled and
> subsequently cracks, deforms, fogs, or otherwise
> changes is* a re-warming *— the body's working term for
> a piece that has not, after all, finished its cooling.
> Re-warmings are returned to the watch-house and held in
> the cool-quarter (§2.7) for a working-year, observed
> daily by an unpoured apprentice on remediation rotation.
> Most re-warmings stabilise; some do not. A piece that
> has not stabilised in a working-year is, by the body's
> reading, **uncoolable**. Uncoolable pieces are not
> destroyed. They are kept.*

Cosmology now has two grades: doctrinal core (a piece is
cooled if three witnesses declare it so) and operational
under-truth (the declaration can be retracted by the piece
itself). Doctrine survives by treating the under-truth as
*failure*, not *evidence against the doctrine* — quiet
structural pressure on the body's central thematic claim.

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Quote (0001 §2.5):** *"She did not finish the firing."*

**Strong.** Communion-walker as *poured but not cooled*;
Transcription-walker as *re-fired*. Idiom is canon and
usable in any future NPC line.

**Thin.** The body's reading of **its own** practitioners
who reach apotheosis-adjacent states (long-rotation
remediation; cool-quarter habituation) has not been
canonised. The body cannot consistently hold *Communion-
walkers fail the firing* if the body's own discipline
includes a working-year cool-quarter rotation that some
apprentices never fully leave.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Glassblown Remnant
subsection, append after the *did not finish the firing*
idiom):**

> *A small fraction of unpoured apprentices, on remediation
> rotation in the cool-quarter, are not returned to active
> watches at the rotation's end. The body's working term
> is* held back *— a working-form distinct from
> apprenticeship-failure. A held-back apprentice continues
> the cool-quarter's daily observational work indefinitely;
> they are housed, fed, and counted in the kiln-house's
> cool-shelf without ever first-firing. The body's reading
> is that a held-back apprentice has* a finer cooling-eye
> than the firing would credit *— their attention is more
> useful as a continuous watch on uncoolable pieces than as
> a pourer's hand. Held-back apprentices author no pieces
> and surrender no beads. They are* never first-fired
> *and** never declared apprentices-out**: a working-form
> the body sustains for the duration of a held-back
> practitioner's life. A walker who reads a Glassblown
> kiln-house carefully will identify the held-back among
> the unpoured by a small absence — they wear the apron but
> not the pocket the beads go in.*

The held-back is the body's institutional analogue of a
practitioner who has refused both endings — not Communion
(no integration), not Transcription (no inscription); the
body's quiet third form, available only inside the
discipline. A future Glassblown NPC who is held-back is a
usable narrative shape; the player who learns to read the
apron-pocket recognises the form unprompted.

### 2.6 Linguistic texture

**Quote (0001 §2.6):** *"A half-sentence is dropped work."*

**Strong.** Completed-sentence rule canonised; falsifiable
in any future Glassblown line.

**Thin.** The unpoured apprentice's **acquisition** of the
rule has not been canonised. A small child cannot be
expected to speak only in completed sentences.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Glassblown Remnant
subsection, append after the completed-sentence rule):**

> *The completed-sentence rule applies only inside the
> kiln-house and only above the hearth-line (the height of
> a kneeling senior's mouth). An unpoured apprentice below
> hearth-line is permitted any speech-form, including
> trailing-off, half-thoughts, and hum. The body teaches
> the rule by* posture *rather than by correction:
> apprentices are told only that the air is different
> above hearth-line, and that working-speech is what one
> says when one stands. A small child in the kiln-house
> learns to crouch when she is unsure of a sentence. The
> body considers this the right teaching-shape: the rule is
> not a punishment for incompletion but a recognition that
> some sentences are not yet ready for the air a pour
> breathes.*

Falsifiable: any apprentice line above hearth-line must
complete; below hearth-line is free speech. Posture
becomes the working-form for asking an apprentice to
*commit*.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Glassblown Remnant (cool-quarter & cooling-watch)

### 3.1 The cool-quarter as room

A long, low room on the cool side of the watch-house, no
heat-source admitted, floor dark stone sluiced once a year.
Walls hold chest-height shelves of pieces under observation,
arranged by date-of-cooling-declared (oldest at the door,
newest at the rear, uncoolables on a separate rear shelf).
The unpoured apprentice on remediation rotation walks the
room twice a day, first light and last light, without
speaking. The walking is the working-form of the watch.

### 3.2 The held-back's corner

The held-back occupies the corner most distant from door
and rear shelf — a small flat stone at floor-height, no
chair. They are not idle: each morning they recite the
day's *uncoolable register* (pieces declared uncoolable in
the previous watch-year) from memory to the rotating
apprentice. The list is unmarked. The apprentice carries
the recitation in their head through the day's two walks.

### 3.3 The watch's adjudication

The three witnesses do not deliberate aloud. Declarations
are spoken in turn — pourer, steady-watch, cross-watch —
each a complete sentence in working register: *"By my
reading the piece is cooled."* A witness who is uncertain
holds the silence; a held silence is, by working-form, a
declaration of *not yet*. Watches concluding on silence
rather than explicit two-of-three are filed to the *quiet
shelf* and wait for a second pourer-and-watch trio. A
witness who declared in the first watch is barred from the
second.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Bleed 1 — re-warmings vs. demonstrated-cooling doctrine.**
The body's central cosmological claim is that *cooling is
possible*. The body's institutional procedure for re-warmed
pieces is *observe for a working-year, declare uncoolable
if not stabilised, keep*. *Keep* is a doctrinally awkward
verb: the body's own discipline cannot dispose of an
uncoolable piece because doing so would acknowledge that
the cooling does not, in this case, finish. The bleed is
not a contradiction in canon; it is a contradiction the
canon **cannot resolve without becoming Palimpsest-shaped**
(every cooling is a partial cooling; no cooling is final).
The body's working position is that uncoolables are *the
exception that proves the rule*. A senior who has worked
forty years and watched the rear shelves of three watch-
houses fill steadily with uncoolables holds this position
less firmly. The shelves do not.

**Bleed 2 — JSON L338 *"offers nothing at all"* vs. §3.**
The cool-quarter's working life sharpens 0001's flag: what
the Glassblown *offer* is the cool-quarter's daily silence
— an offering the Choir cannot taste, which registers, in
its frame, as nothing. A future Choir gloss-line should
pair *offers nothing* with *teaches nothing aloud*. Both
readings canon-correct within the Choir's frame.

**Bleed 3 — Pale Curation discrepancy in the trough.**
§2.2's parenthetical: bead counts in trough residue do not
match watch-house official counts. Two readings: (a) some
unpoured apprentices add beads for watches they did not
fully attend (small daily falsification); (b) the trough's
bead-count includes beads from held-back apprentices who
should not be adding any. Canon-clean if (b) is allowed —
the body forbids the held-back to surrender beads at
first-firing because the held-back never first-fires;
nothing in the body's discipline forbids a held-back from
**adding a bead each evening** to the trough directly. The
held-back has, by this reading, an authorised authorship-
form the body has not noticed: a count, surrendered
nightly, of watches stood at a station the body counts as
non-active. **(b) is the cleaner canon.** The body's
working-form for the held-back's quiet life turns out to
include a quiet authorship the body's stated rules forbid.

**Bleed 4 — held-back vs. *did not finish the firing*
idiom.** A held-back apprentice has, by the idiom, *not
finished the firing*. The body's reading is that the
held-back has been re-classified before the firing was due,
so the idiom does not apply. This is a doctrinal sleight:
the idiom is preserved by re-categorising the practitioner.
A future Glassblown senior NPC asked about held-back kin
should pause before answering; the pause is the working-
form for *the idiom does not apply, and I notice it does
not apply.*

The bombshell is the third paragraph: **a piece declared
uncoolable forty-three working-years ago changed shape
during a single working-day, and the held-back's gesture
in response is the watch-conclusion gesture — not a
mid-watch gesture.** The held-back is, by this reading,
**conducting an unsanctioned cooling-watch on uncoolable
pieces** — alone, daily, without the three-witness
discipline. The body has no canonised form for this. The
held-back's quiet authorship in §2.2 (beads at the trough)

not formally include, conducted by a practitioner the body
has classified as *non-active*. A senior who saw this and
named it would either have to file the held-back as a
pourer-equivalent (impossible: the held-back is not first-
fired) or revise the discipline (impossible: the body
does not author revisions). The body's working-form for
the situation is **to not see**.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence (from §2.4):** *"A piece that has been
declared canon-cooled and subsequently cracks, deforms,
fogs, or otherwise changes is a re-warming — the body's
working term for a piece that has not, after all, finished
its cooling."*

Working modern register. *Declared*, *canon-cooled*,
*re-warming*, *the body's working term* — institutional-
sociological prose, lower-cased technical jargon, no
inversion. The shape *the body's working term for X* is a
modern definitional move; *after all* is contemporary
adverbial. No fronted nominal, no archaic verb-form, no
capitalised abstractions.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"That Piece which, having
been declared by the Watch to be Cool, doth thereafter
crack, or deform, or fog, or by any sign decline its
state, is named a Re-Warming, the which the Body holdeth
to signify a Cooling that hath not, in the end, made an
end of its work."* Tells: capitalised *Piece / Watch /
Cool / Re-Warming / Body / Cooling*; *doth*, *hath*;
relative *the which*; the fronted *That Piece which*; the
predicate-stretch *made an end of its work*. Actual
sentence is none of these.
