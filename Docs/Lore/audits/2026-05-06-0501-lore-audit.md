## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine Communion rotation

The Brine Communion has not been rotated for §3 in the chain.
The bible mentions them once (L208 — *not at war the way the
Thermoclaves and Brine Communion do*); the JSON does not name
them at all (the Choir's `OtherVersions` list at L481 catalogues
Concord, Curation, Glassblown — Brine and Thermoclaves are
absent). Working-drafts have given Brine the *under-tide
reading*, the *deposit-token* at the *shoaling-house*, *the tide
that took the shore* (the 0404 item 29 draft). Sitting 19 brings
all three together. **No audit-invented NPCs** (per 0311 §2.1).

### Etymology

*Communion* has two simultaneous registers in Brine usage: (1)
*the gathering of the singing*, the working term for any
assembly of singers above a given depth of water, (2) *the
sharing of the salt*, the working term for the meal taken
before a long voyage. The two are not separate — a Brine
assembly *is* a meal of salt, and a meal *is* a singing.
Outsiders hear *Brine Communion* as collective-noun + qualifier
in the shape *Glassblown Remnant* / *Pale Curation*; Brine
speech does not share the construction. The faction's name to
itself is the present-participial *the singing* (lower-case) —
or, to specify the shape, *the singing-at-anchor*, *the
singing-under-way*. *Communion* is the substrate of either.

### Working ritual: the salt-drawn line

Before any voyage longer than a single tide, a vessel's
boatswain draws a line of dry salt across the gangway threshold.
Once drawn, every soul who steps across is **salt-marked** —
a dab to the back of the hand — and the gangway-line closes
behind them. The vessel sails with as many marked hands as
crossed. **No additional hand may be brought on under way.**
The mark does not survive the tide; by destination port it has
weathered off. But the boatswain's tally, kept on a slip in the
boatswain's pocket, holds the count for the duration. If
arrival count differs from departure tally, the difference has
a working-name: a *tide-debt*. Tide-debts are paid before the
next voyage, in a ritual that need not be described — no boat
that arrives short of its tally sails again with the same
boatswain.

### Mourning practice: the under-tide reading

A Brine death at sea is not a body-recovery practice. The body
is given to the water at the moment of dying, or as soon
afterward as the wind permits. What is **kept** is the soul's
last *working-position* — the rope they were holding, the line
they were tending, the place on the deck where they fell. That
position is read at the next port by a senior boatswain of the
receiving harbour, in a ritual called the *under-tide reading*.
The reading is filed at the harbour's *shoaling-house*, the low
building at the harbour-edge where Brine death-positions are
kept on deposit. (The *deposit-token* drafted 0404 §3 as the
in-place-reading procedural form is the Brine receipt for a
filed death-position; the body is in the water, the position is
in the shoaling-house, the token is in the next-of-kin's
pocket.)

### Bridge to §4

The 0404 draft proposed *the tide that took the shore* as the
Brine cataclysm-name. The brief above does not contradict the
proposal — *the tide* is load-bearing in Brine working-speech —
but reveals that **the JSON's cataclysm-naming dialogue does
not include Brine at all.** The Choir's `OtherVersions` node
(`RotChoir.json:481`) lists Concord, Curation, Glassblown and

footer carries the Brine name. **A small mechanical alignment

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion — Brine sailor's marginalia

A scrap of marginalia in a Brine vessel's boatswain's-book.
The book is one of those small ledgers a boatswain keeps for
the working-year — daily tide-debts, hand-counts, weather-marks.
This page is a single voyage. The marginalia lands a piece of
Brine cosmology parenthetically, beside an unrelated tide-debt
entry. ~265 words.

> *Tide-debt: none. Hand-count crossed eleven; arrived eleven.
> Weather: half-mark south by morning, flat by evening. Strip-
> sail used the second watch and stowed clean.*
>
> *Salt drawn light at the gangway today — the cooper said the
> dry-stores were thinning, and the boatswain agreed without
> argument, which means we are running shorter than we like.
> Note for the next port: take a full press if the salt-house
> can spare it; we do not want to be drawing thin twice in a
> row, and the singers at port will hear if we do, and the
> singing-at-anchor will sound thin under the keel for a tide.*
>
> *(The keel-singing is older than the gangway-line. The line
> is for the souls. The keel-singing is for the water — the
> water remembers the tide that took the shore, which was a
> long, slow tide, not a wave; that is why we sing under the
> keel and not at the bow. A wave would be sung at the bow.
> The slow tide we sing under, where the slow tide reaches.
> The cooper knows this. The boatswain knows. Apprentice will
> learn.)*
>
> *Apprentice's first reading-rehearsal at the next port. He
> has the position right and the breath wrong. The breath is
> the whole reading. Tell him again. Tell him until the
> shoaling-house door opens and tell him afterward.*
>
> *Tide-tomorrow: full at fourth bell, slack at sixth.*

**Lore landed parenthetically:**

  - The Brine cataclysm-name is *the tide that took the shore*,
    and the in-world reason for the working-grammar of *the
    long, slow tide, not a wave* is named: **why** keel-singing
    rather than bow-singing — a fast event sings at the bow, a
    slow event sings under the keel, the cataclysm being the
    latter.
  - The *gangway-line / keel-singing* distinction (souls /
    water).
  - The under-tide reading is rehearsed before being filed —
    apprentices learn at the shoaling-house door, not at the
    deposit. *Breath* is the working-discriminator (position
    is the easier half).
  - The *tide-debt* is the headline; the cosmology is the
    parenthetical. Working-reader's eye reads through; the
    out-of-fiction reader has the bombshell.

Candidate found-document for `Docs/Lore/` proper, separate
editorial pile from bible-absorption. Sitting 19 does not
propose this for the bible directly.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence (from §5 marginalia):**

> *"A wave would be sung at the bow. The slow tide we sing
> under, where the slow tide reaches."*

Working register, working-grammar. *A wave* and *the slow tide*
are flat working nouns the boatswain would use about the actual
sea, not figurations of it. The sentence is a procedural rule —
fast events at the bow, slow events under the keel — the kind a
boatswain teaches an apprentice. No allegorical capitalisation.
The terminal *where the slow tide reaches* is a working-grammar
inversion (verb after adverb-clause), not a high-register
fronted-clause.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"The Wave is sung at the Bow, for the
Wave is the Sudden Coming. But the Slow Tide — that ancient
patience of the Sea — is sung beneath the Keel, where its
unhurried Sorrow reaches its dark hand."* Tells: capitalisations
on *Wave*, *Bow*, *Sudden Coming*, *Slow Tide*, *Sea*, *Keel*,
*Sorrow*; *that ancient patience of the Sea* is appositional
mythologisation; *its dark hand* is figurative anatomy of an
abstract noun. The actual sentence: *wave*, *bow*, *slow tide*,
*sing*, *under*, *reaches* — six lower-case working terms, the
rule (where you sing what) doing the rhetorical work.

Pass.
