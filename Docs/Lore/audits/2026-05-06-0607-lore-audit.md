## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord rotates in

### The factor's primer — the working-pedagogy

The 0001 sitting landed the **Sweetening** (the closing rite),
the **Dissolution** metaphysics, public/private Sweetening
forms, and the Concord-Choir ritual seam. The 0001 stopped at
the doctrinal layer. Sitting 20 develops the **working-pedagogy
layer** — what a new factor learns in the apprentice working-
year before performing a Sweetening alone.

Every Concord market maintains a **factor's primer** — a working
document of standing length (about 30 hand-pages, recopied each
generation), held by the senior factor of the market and read
by the apprentice in three working passes. The primer is not
a doctrine-text; it is a *procedural* handbook in working-
grammar.

### The three passes

  - **First pass — name the dissolved.** The apprentice reads
    the primer's first ten pages aloud to the senior factor.
    These pages drill the working-grammar of the sentence-shape
    — *the wool no longer X, which Y, which Z* — every category
    a Sweetening can declare *no longer here*. The apprentice
    must fluently produce three-part declarations on demand.

  - **Second pass — feel the dissolved.** The apprentice
    witnesses twenty Sweetenings over a working-month, eating
    the *spare sweet* — every Sweetening after the first
    generates a third sweet set on the working-table for the
    witnessing apprentice. They learn the timing: *when the
    dissolved-line lands before the sugar reaches the lip;
    when after.* Timing is the working-difference between a
    clean Sweetening and a hollow one.

  - **Third pass — speak the dissolved.** The apprentice
    performs Sweetenings under the senior's standing eye. The
    senior never corrects in front of parties; correction is
    given afterward at the senior's table, with sugar between.
    *Correction is itself a small Sweetening — what the
    apprentice did is no longer here, and a better version
    dissolves into its place.*

### The kin-recusal rule

A factor may not perform a Sweetening on a trade in which their
kin is either party — buyer, seller, or named beneficiary
(inheritance Sweetenings included). The recusal is doctrinal:
a factor cannot *grieve* their kin's loss in the Sweetening's
sense without compromising the ritual's working-honesty.
Kin-Sweetenings are performed by a *foreign* factor — at least
three working-days' travel away — at standing recusal-fee.

The working-shape: *a Sweetening is procedural acknowledgement
of dissolution, not emotional. Family cannot hold the
procedural register over family.* This is the Concord's
quietest agreement with the Brine boatswain's *no return short
of tally* rule (0501 §3) — both factions recognise **kinship as
working-impurity** in ritual contexts.

### The market-rotation

Factors do not hold the same market more than three working-
years. After three years a factor is moved to a market at least
seven working-days' travel away. The rotation prevents *local
market capture* — the position that, after long enough in one
place, a factor's Sweetenings grieve *their own* dissolution-
history rather than the trade's. A factor who has loved their
market too long performs Sweetenings that are emotionally
honest but procedurally compromised.

Rotation is mandatory; refusal results in a *standing
Sweetening* — the factor performs a private Sweetening over
their own factor-role, takes the spare sweet, and ceases to be
a factor. The working-name for an ex-factor is *sweetened-out*,
used affectionately.
