## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (1501 §2.1):** *"the chart is paper, and is
filed: across voyages, across generations, working-
features carry forward."*

**Strong.** Chart-as-tackle persistence canon.

**Thin.** The body's *outside-life* of the chart
has not been named. Curation files keepsake-charts
under accession (1501 §3); long-trading outside
hands carry them across mooring-circuits the body
has no register for. A keepsake's working-life
extends decades past the body's release of it. The
form is the chart's *third life* — first as
working-tackle (overboard at end-of-life); second
as Curation's filed copy (the third shelf, by
medium-transposition, 2301 §2.4); third as the
outside hand's carried object, which sometimes
returns to a Communion dock.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Brine Communion,
append after 1501 §2.1):**

> *The keepsake-chart has a working-life the body
> does not file. A long-trading outside hand who
> received a retired bearings-pilot's chart at a
> retirement-landing carries it through their own
> trade-circuit; some hands return, decades later,
> to a Communion mooring with the keepsake still
> aboard. The body has no working-form for the
> return; the dock-watcher does not log it; the
> senior who gave it has, by working-tradition,
> retired or died. A dock-watcher's apprentice
> sometimes glances at the chart while the outside
> hand is at the rope-table — the body's only
> working-encounter with a chart-feature carried
> by a hand the body cannot file.*

The chart's third life is the only body-medium
across five bodies that walks **outside the body's
working-form** without losing its working-features.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (1501 §2.2):** *"by the body's working-
form, no chart-page has ever been authored; chart-
pages have been carried forward."*

**Strong.** Carry-forward as not-authoring canon.

**Thin.** Carry-forward presumes the chart's
working-features migrate by trust-as-wear within
the body. The keepsake's outside-life violates this
implicitly: the apprentice's glance is *trust
without inheritance* — she trusts a feature she has
not learned by working-voyages with her own senior.
The body's authorship-doctrine has a hidden fifth
case: features that arrive *by glance, from outside
the body, without sanction*.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Brine Communion,
append after the chart-trade paragraph):**

> *A dock-watcher's apprentice who has glanced at a
> keepsake-chart on the rope-table during an
> outside hand's mooring-day carries the glance
> forward into her own working-life. The body has
> no working-form for it; the apprentice's senior,
> asked, will say:* "you have looked at a chart
> that did not ask you to look. the looking is the
> chart's; you are the looking." *Five migration-
> modes: cadence-bodily; count-by-handing; trust-
> as-wear-on-paper; sea-time threshold; **glance-
> without-sanction**. The fifth the body neither
> sanctions nor refuses.*

Authorship-doctrine refines to five: cadence
(authored by singing); count (authored by handing);
trust-as-wear (authored by voyaging); sea-time
(authored by surviving); **glance (authored by
having been in the room)**. The fifth is the only
authorship the body cannot teach.
