## 3. Faction asymmetry — Pale Curation rotates in

### The negative-catalogue — the working shape

The 2301 sitting landed the Pale Curation's **firm-ink / field-
ink** distinction (the cataloguer's coat, the two pinned strips,
the Hall-Seventh duplicate-memo register). The 1701 sitting
landed the **standing-junior drawer** and the **pinned ledger
of impostors** — the working position that the Curation's
greatest professional hazard is *the artifact that lies about
itself.* Sitting 16 develops a class the prior sittings have
not: the artifact that *cannot* be catalogued — not because it
lies, but because no working accession-form survives contact
with it.

### The negative-blank

When a cataloguer encounters such an artifact, they do not — and
under Curation rule cannot — return the artifact to the field or
abandon it on the receiving table. They reach instead for the
**negative-blank**: a pre-printed accession-form cut down the
spine to two facing pages, with the right-hand page (the
artifact's *positive description*) bordered and overprinted in
the Curation's **thinned ink** — a working pigment cut with
witness-water until it sits at roughly half opacity. The form is
visibly empty before the cataloguer touches it. The cataloguer
fills the *left-hand* page only — the page reserved on standard
accession-forms for the cataloguer's working notes — with a
description of the **failure**: where the cataloguer was, what
the artifact resisted, and how (which working categories the
artifact dissolved on contact). The right-hand page stays empty
in thinned ink, witness to its own absence.

### The two-hand rule, in negative

A standard accession is signed by two cataloguers in two inks
(forgery requires two different hands). A negative-accession is
signed by two cataloguers in **two different thinned inks** —
one cut with witness-water, one with the cataloguer's own
breath-condensate (the Curation's lightest carrier, otherwise
used only on deaccession-slips). Visibly thinner than any
positive-accession ink, visibly different from each other. A
forger of a negative-accession has to forge two thinned hands
at once. The rule is the same; the materials are inverted.

### The negative shelf

Hall-Seventh's east wall, behind the duplicate-investigation
desk (per 2301), carries the **negative shelf**. Filed not by
artifact (the artifact is, by definition, a failure of subject-
classification) but by the *working category* the artifact
dissolved on contact — *date-failure*, *origin-failure*,
*subject-failure*, and so on. A handful are filed under
*every-failure* — the cataloguers' darkest entries, never shown
to junior staff alone.

### The doctrine — *unwritability is itself information*

The Curation does not phrase this aloud, but the negative-
catalogue is its quietest agreement with the Palimpsest. Where
the Palimpsest reads through layers and the Choir consumes
through layers, the Curation **writes the missing layer.** A
negative-blank is a *positive record of an absence.* The
Curation does not consider an artifact unrecorded if its
unrecordability is on file.

### The seam to the bible

L67 (the Choir's spore-witness) and L137 (the Palimpsest's
noetic register) both treat **information that is not in
language**. The negative-catalogue is the Curation's closest
analogue: information that is in the *visible thinness of the
ink.* Not a §2.7 candidate this sitting — sitting 16's bible
candidate is the Concord-Brine four-class species above, and the
Curation-Palimpsest seam needs another sitting's development
before it can absorb in two sentences. **Flagged for sitting 17
or later as a candidate twenty-seventh pending item.**

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"The Curation does not consider an artifact unrecorded if its
> unrecordability is on file."*

Working register, gloss-form. *Unrecordability* is a working
abstract noun the Curation would use about its working files
the way a clerk uses *receivability* about a delivery. The
sentence's claim is procedural, not theological — the Curation
is making a working-rule explicit, not pronouncing a doctrine.
Lower-case throughout. No allegorical capitalisation.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"Verily, the Pale Curators hold that
no Artefact escapes the Great Record — for what cannot be Writ
upon the Page is yet writ in the very Absence of Writing, and
that Absence is itself a Sign and a Witness."* Tells: *Verily*;
*Pale Curators* (heraldic substitution for the working
*Curation*); *Artefact*, *Great Record*, *Sign*, *Writing*
(allegorical capitalisations); *Writ upon the Page* (mythic-
archaic verb-on-noun). The actual sentence: *consider*,
*unrecorded*, *unrecordability*, *on file* — working-register
verbs and abstracts, no allegorical noun-phrase.
