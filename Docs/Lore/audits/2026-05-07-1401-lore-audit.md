## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (0901 §3 *lighting-from-old*):** *"The body's
doctrinal claim is that the new fire is not a* new
*fire; it is the same fire, in a new place."*

**Strong.** Succession-rite with metaphysical content
built into operation.

**Thin.** By the body's own §2.4 representation-
doctrine, a *hand-on-a-thing-by-proxy across spatial
separation* is exactly *representation*. The carry,
in any other body's metaphysics, is a representation
the Thermoclaves' working-form holds as not-
representation.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Thermoclaves,
append after 0901 §2.1):**

> *The carry-rite's working-form includes a sentence
> spoken in the public hall by the parent's third-
> shift witness on the morning the carry departs:*
> "you are not lighting a new fire. you are tending
> the same fire in a longer step." *Outsiders
> transcribe it as a* claim*. The body does not. A
> keeper asked whether the sentence claims anything
> answers* "you do not claim. you continue. the
> sentence is the continuation." *The body's position
> is that the sentence does not represent the carry's
> metaphysics; it* is *the metaphysics, in the body's
> mode. A Curator filing the sentence as a
> metaphysical proposition has replaced it with a
> representation of itself — and so admitted, into
> the Curator's own filing, the wound the doctrine
> refuses.*

First third-shelf-analogue: the carry is sustained
as not-representation by a speech-act whose own
doctrinal status the body refuses to file.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (0901 §2.2):** *"The Thermoclaves do not
author texts."*

**Strong.** Doctrinal rule against authored documents.

**Thin.** The rule is silent on **representations of
the body produced by other hands the body permits** —
specifically, the dawn-rotation rubbings of the
stoker-handle, archived at Depot 11.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Thermoclaves, new
sidebar "The rubbing-archive"):**

> *Curation cataloguers visiting on dawn-rotation are
> permitted to take a paper rubbing of the stoker-
> handle's marked face. A thin sheet is laid on the*
> rubbing-station *— a knee-high stone shelf one
> stone-cut step outside the hearth-line — and the
> keeper on shift, if willing, holds the stoker just
> inside the line so its handle rests on the paper.
> The cataloguer rubs the marks with charred wood
> from the public hall's small fire. The stoker does
> not cross the line. The rubbing is signed and filed
> under the body's region-name; no keeper-name is
> recorded. The Thermoclave position: the rubbing
> represents the Curator, not the body — the marks
> do not* mean *anything; the rubbing means the
> Curator looked. The Curators' position: the rubbing
> represents the stoker, which represents the
> keeper's tending-cadence. Both positions stand.
> Neither body files the contradiction. The rubbing-
> archive is the** only existing permanent record of
> any individual Thermoclave's hand-cadence, and it
> exists in the Curation's archive, not the
> Thermoclaves'**.*

Authorship-doctrine sustains itself by permitting
the Curation to author *on the body's behalf*. Two-
body authorship, structurally co-dependent.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (0901 §2.4):** *"a hand on a thing, not a
hand on a representation of a thing."*

**Strong.** Doctrinal floor.

**Thin.** §2.1 above pressures the floor: in the
carry, the hand is on a stoker-tip walking across
distance — by every other body's reading, on a proxy.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Thermoclaves,
append after 0901 §2.4):**

> *The body's discipline contains, by working-
> register admission, a single named exception called*
> the long step*: the period during which a carrier
> walks the lit stoker-tip from parent to daughter
> hearth. The keeper's hand is on the handle, the
> tip on the open air. The body's working-form holds
> this as a continuous tending of the parent fire
> across distance, the travelled air counted as part
> of the tended hearth's working-volume for the step's
> duration. A keeper in a long step is not* walking*;
> they are* tending in motion*. Third-shift keepers
> will admit, in the public hall with no second-shift
> present, that the long step is "where the doctrine
> has to look the other way" — and the body does not
> canonise the admission. The long step is the
> Refusal-hearth's** only working-instance of
> representation the doctrine sustains by
> recategorisation**: routinely performed, not
> formally permitted, sustained by working-tradition
> rather than discipline. Its parallel in form is the
> Curation's third-shelf.*

Falsifies a doctrinal seam. An NPC who admits the
long step is representation outside third-shift
company is already outside doctrine; §2.6's
transverse stroke covers the slip.

### 2.6 Linguistic texture

**Quote (0901 §2.6):** *"The stroke means: a sentence
in the wrong grammar happened here."*

**Strong.** Error-mark canonised.

**Thin.** The stroke is, by §2.4, a representation.
The body forbids representation; marks transverse
strokes routinely; the strokes permit the body's only
longitudinal assessment of a keeper. Central
administrative form, doctrinal violation.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Thermoclaves,
append after 0901 §2.6):**

> *A second-shift keeper asked in the public hall
> whether the transverse stroke means anything is
> required by working-form to answer:* "you scratch
> what happened. nothing is encoded." *The first
> clause is honest by the body's reading; the second
> is the body's working-form for the doctrinal
> position that scratching what happened is not
> encoding it. Outsiders find the distinction
> insupportable. Third-shift keepers do not address
> it and will close the conversation if pressed. A
> Curator who has filed several closures across
> several rotations has the body's working-mark for
> the distinction: it is the place at which the body
> stops speaking. The Curator's filed silence is a
> representation of non-representation, maintained on
> the Curation's side. The body has no working-mark
> for the silence. The body simply stops.*

Falsifies the speech-rule's lower edge: a junior who
discusses the distinction is outside doctrine; a
senior who closes is performing it.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclaves (the long step, the rubbing-station, the carry-tongue)

> ### The long-step rite
>
> A daughter-hearth's establishment is timed so the
> parent's third-shift keeper carries the lit
> stoker-tip from pre-dawn embers to arrive at the
> daughter site by sunset. The carrier walks alone.
> A second-shift keeper walks behind at half a
> working-mile, watching only that the carrier does
> not stop; they do not approach, speak, or bring
> water. If the carrier sets the stoker down, the
> second-shift keeper marks the spot with a single
> stone and turns back; the long step is *broken*,
> the daughter *unfounded*, and the parent cannot
> attempt the carry again until the following
> equinox. Working-tradition reads a broken long step
> not as the carrier's failure but as the parent
> fire's reading of the daughter site. A site that
> breaks two consecutive long steps is permanently
> ungrounded.
>
> Arrival is without ceremony. The carrier touches
> the stoker-tip to the kindling, the daughter's
> first-shift keeper (waiting silently since dawn)
> takes the stoker, and the carrier walks back to
> the parent without resting. The carrier is, on
> arrival, a third-shift keeper of the daughter —
> the same hand on the same fire. They rarely return.
>
> ### The rubbing-station
>
> A small stone shelf, knee-high, sits on the public
> hall's threshold one stone-cut step outside the
> hearth-line. On dawn-rotation, a cataloguer lays a
> sheet on the shelf; the keeper on shift, if willing,
> holds the stoker just inside the line so its handle
> rests on the paper through the doorway. Charred-
> wood rub, one or two minutes, no line crossing.
> Refusal is permitted on any morning without reason
> given; working-tradition reads refusal as *the
> previous shift's marking ought not to be taken
> further*. Depot 11's counter-tally records one
> refusal in nineteen rubbing-mornings.
>
> ### The carry-tongue
>
> During a long step the carrier may speak only two
> sentences: *"you are tending"* (to oneself, when
> the body asks the carrier to set the stoker down)
> and *"the step is open"* (audibly, when the
> second-shift watcher closes inside the half-mile —
> a working-form for *do not approach*). No other
> speech is permitted. The body's reading is that
> carry-tongue is not a language; it is the period
> during which speech has been reduced to two
> doctrinal sentences and the silence between them.

Three pieces: long step (parent dawn, daughter
sunset, broken-step as the parent fire's reading);
rubbing-station as permitted authored representation
held one stone-cut step off the hearth-line; carry-
tongue as the speech-rule at its narrowest.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Bleed 1 — bible L208 *hate* vs. §2.4 + 0901 §2.4.**
Still preferred over *hate*: *"at practical odds"*.
§2.4 sharpens it: Communion drift-cosmology is, in
Thermoclave reading, *a long step that never
arrives* — permanent carry without a hearth. The
mirror: a long step is *anchoring on motion*, which
the Communion accepts; the Thermoclaves' refusal to
admit it as such is, to a Communion elder, the body's
only intelligible doctrine filed under an
unintelligible name. Suggested bible parenthetical
at L208: *"(Their conflict is practical at the
boundary, doctrinal at the floor: each body reads
the other's working-form as a named version of its
own forbidden mode.)"*

**Bleed 2 — bible silence on the rubbing-archive vs.
§2.2.** The bible's Curation paragraph does not yet
acknowledge that **a depot's holdings include
rubbings of bodies the depot's discipline does not
author at**. The third shelf (1301 §2.1) holds
founder-unsigned material; the rubbing-archive is
parallel: an authored, signed, shelf-numbered
archive of *another body's non-authorship*. Suggested
addition: *"The Curation's standing-shelves include a
category of rubbings — paratextual transcriptions of
non-Curation bodies' non-authored working-tools,
signed by the cataloguer, filed under the
non-authoring body's region-name with no recorder
field."*

**Bleed 3 — JSON L541 (*"They fear losing the
boundaries that define them"*) vs. §2.4.** The Choir
line is about the Palimpsest in flat reading; against
the Thermoclaves it reads sharper. The Thermoclaves
*are* a body defined by a boundary, but they do not
*fear* its loss; they *tend* it, as a hand tends a
fire. A future Choir line distinguishing the two
factions could pair the nuance — working-shelf
addition, not yet a JSON edit.

## 5. Merchant's-diary artifact
