## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible, line 23:* "Every faction in the game is defined by their
> relationship to this event."

**Strong.** Six factions plus the five voices have named the
cataclysm six different ways with at least three chronologies.

**Thin.** **The Curation's cataclysm card.** R2's *First Card*
gave the catalogue a founding myth but not a *founding record.*
The Curation files things; what does its earliest filed thing
look like?

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar after the cataclysm
section, "Card 0001"):

> The earliest accession is a single card, most fields blank.
> *Object:* none recorded. *Provenance:* none recorded. *Date
> received:* the date filled in by the cataloguer who eventually
> filed it; cataloguers disagree whether this is the cataclysm or
> the catalogue's birth, and the field does not distinguish.
> *Description:* *the breaking, in so far as a breaking can be
> filed.* *Marginalia:* twenty-seven hands at last count, in
> twenty-seven inks. None overwrite the prior; the rule is to
> add, not revise. The Curation calls Card 0001 *the long card.*
>
> Halen, on its provenance: *the catalogue began the morning the
> catalogue noticed it had a yesterday.* Filed in her hand.
>
> The Concord considers the long card *uncloseable* and refuses
> to witness clauses citing it. The Choir considers it *compost
> with a label.* The Palimpsest reports it produces a Glimpse of
> every card the Curation has ever filed, simultaneously, and is
> not read by any sane reader twice.

The cataclysm now has a Curation document. Fittingly, mostly the
absence of one.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

> *R2 (sibling 2225):* the *un-noticing* — the Curation's private
> practice of declining to notice what would force a re-filing.

**Thin.** **Who fills in a blank field, and on what authority?**
R2 gave the practice; R3 needs the *grammar.*

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "Reservation and
bid-cards"):

> A field that cannot yet be filled is marked with the
> **reservation glyph** — a small open square. Not ignorance:
> *recorded ignorance*. The cataloguer attests the answer is not
> yet available, declined to guess, held the field open.
>
> A later cataloguer with the answer does not fill the square.
> They file a **bid-card**: a smaller card naming the parent, the
> field, the value, the evidence, the bidder's hand. Bid-cards
> are kept adjacent, not merged. The Curation does not promote a
> bid to filled until uncontested for a *generation* — twenty-
> three years by protocol, long enough for a contesting hand to
> file.
>
> A field filled and later contested is reverted to a reservation
> glyph; the prior fill becomes a bid-card. The catalogue cannot
> lose information by being wrong, only by being un-filed.
>
> A Curator describing an artifact uses the **bid-mood** — *the
> bowl is bid Brine, by claim 4127, hand Halen, since 469* — for
> any field carried only on bid-cards. They extend it to ordinary
> speech. Halen, on whether her own father was Curation: *he is
> bid Curation, by my own hand, since the year I was old enough
> to file.* She has not promoted the bid. The seniors have not
> contested it.

Authorship is now layered, attributed, contestable, auditable —
without overwriting a prior hand. The apparatus structurally
resembles the Brine *bound knot*: the corpus's two written
traditions are converging on consensus-as-overlap.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Thin.** **Names for the unfilled field** across factions. The
*empty space on a record* is a near-universal concept the corpus
has not yet cross-named.

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar, "Names for the unfilled
field"):

> - **Curation (self):** *the open square.* Not an absence; a
>   held place.
> - **Choir:** *the soft spot.* The network reads ungerminated
>   ground the same way — *something intends to be here later, and
>   we will compost what arrives.*
> - **Palimpsest:** *the under-glyph.* A reserved field still
>   carries a faint Glimpse — of the answer that *would have been*
>   filed, on the timeline most adjacent. Palimpsest-attuned
>   readers find the Curation an unusually rich Glimpse surface
>   for this reason. The Curation finds Palimpsest readers
>   unhelpful for the same reason.
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *the open clause.* Veshen will not
>   witness an open-clause contract; *not yet a clause.* The
>   Curation's reservation is, to the Concord, a permanent
>   pre-clause — neither closed nor closeable.
> - **Brine:** *the wave-not-yet.* In bid-mood: *the wave is
>   wave-not-yet at the third hour.* Closest the Brine come to a
>   Curation idiom; possibly borrowed.
> - **Thermoclaves:** *the unlit ash.* Fuel not yet touched by
>   fire. Catechism: *the unlit ash is also the catalogue.*
> - **Glassblown:** *the unblown bubble.* Tulen Niemensa-Sopellen
>   filed it once and immediately filed *the unfired-by-distance*
>   on top; both glosses survive in marginalia.

Each name encodes the faction's metaphysics for absence. The
Concord's *not yet a clause* is the only one that refuses the
Curation's premise that a held place is already part of the record.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

> *Bible, line 121:* "they exist in multiple temporal layers
> simultaneously."

**Strong.** Palimpsest layering, Choir substrate, Brine held depth.

**Thin.** **Does cataloguing alter the substrate?** The Curation's
public doctrine is *witness-only, never causal.* But edge case —
the Palimpsest's Annotation power lets the player *write memories
that didn't happen* (bible 171). What happens when the Curation has
already filed one of those false memories as fact? Does the
Annotation alter the catalogue's prior cards?

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The catalogue and
the overwrite"):

> The witness-only doctrine collides directly with Palimpsest
> Annotation. If a walker writes an echo of an event that never
> occurred, and the Curation has filed a card describing the
> *real* version, what is the relationship between card and
> Annotation?
>
> Halen, in a memo filed under reservation: *the catalogue does
> not amend in response to annotation. An annotated past is bid
> against the filed past; the bid is held adjacent.* The catalogue
> acquires a new bid-card whenever a major Annotation lands
> within Glimpse range of an open cabinet. Curators recognise the
> **bid-arrival** — a dryness in the cabinet's air, like dust
> pre-figuring rain. They file the bid without inspecting the
> Annotation; inspecting would be to *act on* the Palimpsest,
> violating witness-only.
>
> The Palimpsest finds this maddening. The Choir finds it elegant
> — the catalogue is composting Annotations the way the network
> composts everything. The Curation considers itself adjacent to
> the cosmology, never inside it. Halen: *we keep the receipts.
> The events are someone else's business.*

The catalogue is the faction with the least metaphysical claim
that nevertheless registers every other faction's cosmology — a
cosmology in itself.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

> Choir = Communion. Palimpsest = Transcription. Concord = Closure.
> Brine = going-deep. Glassblown = Unfired Forever. Thermoclaves =
> ash-of-the-master.

**Thin.** **The Curation has none.** Eighteen audits and the
Curation is the only faction with no apotheosis. R2's
*refusal-of-apotheosis* (sibling 2219) framed this as deliberate.
R3 names the form properly.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The held card"):

> The apotheosis is **the held card.** A senior cataloguer, at
> the end of tenure, is permitted — once — to file *themselves*
> as a card. They complete every field they can, leave
> reservations where they cannot, file the card adjacent to Card
> 0001. Then they walk out of the archive and do not return. Not
> dead. *Held.*
>
> A held cataloguer continues to exist. They no longer file, no
> longer speak about the catalogue. Curators who meet them
> describe an unsettling intelligibility: *the held one answers
> in the bid-mood about their own present.* They say *I am bid
> alive, by hand 4127, since this morning* and mean it
> procedurally. They have submitted themselves to the verification
> cadence and accept the catalogue's answer about their own state.
>
> The Curation calls this *the long card's longest card.* Three
> Curators are currently held. Halen has filed her own card-draft
> in reservation but has not walked out.
>
> Mirror: Communion dissolves the self into the network,
> Transcription into memory, Closure into the ledger, going-deep
> into the held depth. **Holding dissolves the self into the
> catalogue's verification cadence.** The held cataloguer is not
> gone, not consumed, not transcribed — they are *cited.*

The Curation has an ending shaped to its philosophy. The only
apotheosis a player could describe as *bureaucratic* without it
being a joke.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** R2 gave the *catalogued mood* (deliberately-not-said).

**Thin.** **The bid-mood.** Above, dimension 2, gave the
grammatical form. R3 needs the rule for switching between catalogued
mood and bid-mood mid-sentence.

**Concrete addition** (bible footnote to "Reservation and
bid-cards"):

> Curation grammar permits — requires, in formal speech — the
> **mood-switch**: a single sentence beginning in catalogued mood
> and shifting to bid-mood at the moment a fact drops below
> filed. *I file the bowl Brine 469, and I bid the bowl pre-
> Communion, hand mine, since this morning.* The semicolon is a
> grammatical joint. Outsiders find the joint jarring; the
> Curation considers a sentence without a joint *suspect of
> overclaim.*

The Curation now has a grammar where the *epistemic status* of
each clause is morphologically marked. No other faction is this
honest about what it knows. That honesty is itself a worldview.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

> R3 (1301) gave a Brine dry-walker's day.

**Thin.** **A Curator's day.** Pair structure with the dry-walker.

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar, "A junior cataloguer's
day"):

> Dawn: a junior draws one card from the *unfiled drawer* and
> sits with it before lighting any lamp. *Read the card by
> available light first*, before judgment.
>
> Mid-morning: the field-walk. Oilskin book of blank cards, one
> stick of graphite, folding-rule. No ink — ink commits, and the
> field-walk is reservation territory. Inking happens at the desk
> under a senior's witness.
>
> Mid-day: the *adjacency check*. Every card filed before noon
> is walked to its adjacent cards — does it contradict, complete,
> sit cleanly? A contradiction triggers a bid-card draft against
> the new arrival; the junior writes in graphite, leaves it for a
> senior.
>
> Afternoon: the **un-noticing hour**. No filing. The junior
> walks the cabinets without writing. Halen: *the un-noticing is
> when the catalogue has its private speech with itself, and we
> have agreed to be in the room without listening.*
>
> Evening: the inking. A senior who declines to witness is *not*
> raising an objection — they are filing one, in the un-said
> grammar; the junior reverts the card to graphite without
> comment. The only Curation practice resembling a refusal.
>
> Night: a junior sleeps in the room of the cabinet they were
> assigned. Rotation is slow — a season per cabinet. *A cataloguer
> who has not dreamed against a cabinet has not yet filed anything
> from it.*

Cataloguing is a diurnal craft with rituals at every threshold —
the most sedentary apotheosis-track in the corpus, consistently.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Pale Curation R3 (the missing field)

The corpus filed the Curation as recorders. R3 names them as
*honest about not knowing,* a stronger claim.

**The reservation glyph** is the operational heart. Every other
faction has *certainty mechanics* — Concord clause-close, Choir
compost-as-knowing, Palimpsest Glimpse, Brine held depth,
Thermoclave ash. The Curation alone has a mechanic for *recorded
uncertainty* — the only kind that can be aged into knowledge. The
twenty-three-year contestation window is the catalogue's slowest
and most load-bearing mechanic.

**The held card** (Dim 5) mirrors going-deep asymmetrically: the
Brine submit to the held depth; the Curator to the verification
cadence. Both non-violent, both retain the body. Brine answer:
*kept.* Curator answer: *cited.*

**Halen, reconsidered.** R3 names her **unable to hold.** Card
filed in reservation; not walked out. Seniors have not bid she
should. *Bid open, hand none, since the year of the draft.* She
continues to file.

**The bid-arrival** — dryness in cabinet air before a Palimpsest
Annotation — is the Curation's only *sensory* phenomenon. Junior
cataloguers recognise the smell *like dust before rain* before
they are taught what it indicates.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Live contradiction: the bible's fruiting-bodies model versus
ROTCHOIR_VOICES' absorbed-individual model.**

`Palimpsest_Lore_Bible.md` line 33:
> "Fruiting bodies serve as NPCs, each roughly humanoid, each a
> 'mouth' of the larger network."

`ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md` lines 19–28:
> "Each is a former individual whom the Choir absorbed… The Choir
> absorbs persons but does not erase the language they came in
> with."

Two ontologies for the same NPC class. **Bible:** the Choir
*grows* humanoid mouths on demand; no prior identity. **VOICES:**
the Choir *absorbs* persons and the person retains a voice from
inside the network. The 1301 audit adjudicated *unity-vs-
plurality*; this is a *different* axis (origin-of-the-mouth) and
is unadjudicated.

**Proposed resolution** (bible new clause near line 33):

> The Choir produces fruiting bodies of two kinds, distinction
> unimportant from its side. **Grown mouths** — sensoria extended
> into a region for the first time, no prior identity, speaking
> in standing dialect. **Held mouths** — fruiting bodies grown
> from absorbed persons, speaking in the person's voice and
> idiom, uncorrected. The network considers these the same organ
> (*a mouth is a mouth*); a held mouth's individuated speech is
> the most recent compost-layer. ChoirTendril_1 is the canonical
> grown mouth; Mogu, Grib, Nam, Sien, Sopp are canonical held
> mouths. A held mouth that does *not* know it was once a person
> remains an open seam.

The contradiction was load-bearing — it produced the Five's
distinctness from the Tendril without naming why.

**Smaller bleed.** The *bid-mood* (Dim 6) and the Brine's
*remembered tense* (1301) are the corpus's only two
epistemically-marked grammars. The bible should note the pattern
as open: borrowing direction unknown, possibly a substrate the
Palimpsest has not yet shown either.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**
> *"A field that has been filled and later contested is reverted
> to a reservation glyph and the prior fill is moved to a
> bid-card."*

No archaism, no apostrophe-fantasy, no mythopoeic register. A
procedural statement of a filing rule. *Reservation glyph* and
*bid-card* sound strange because the *Curation uses them*, not
because the narrator performs antiquity. TES version: *"And lo,
when a thing once known is found to have been falsely set down,
the scribes of the Pale Order do unmake the mark and bear the old
answer aside, that the truth might not be bound to the lie."* I
did not write that.

The corpus's stylised vocabulary (*the open square*, *the held
card*, *the bid-mood*) names mechanics. The narrator reports it,
does not adopt it. *Ink commits* is a procedural fact, not
liturgical.

Pass.
