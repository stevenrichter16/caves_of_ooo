## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible line 121:* *"a road that leads to a city destroyed four
> hundred years ago — but if you walk it at the right time, you
> arrive."*

**Strong.** Every body has stratified its founding event.

**Thin.** **No Brine ↔ Palimpsest stratigraphic dispute.** When
the same artifact is *deep-kept* and *layered* — a wreck that
was also a courthouse — no audit has asked which body's
stratification governs.

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar, "What the tide reads"):

> When an artifact carries both Brine *deep-kept* status and
> Palimpsest *layered* status, the bodies do not negotiate
> precedence. Each treats the artifact as theirs in its own
> grammar. A keeper of deep-kept things will refuse to raise a
> wreck; a Palimpsest reader will read its layer regardless,
> chalking the under-text without disturbing the over-water.
> The bodies' doctrines are mechanically incommensurate: the
> Brine measures by *how deep, how long*; the Palimpsest
> measures by *how many readings persist*.
>
> A Brine sailor who has learned the chalk is, by Communion
> doctrine, *over-attuned* — not condemned, but watched. A
> Palimpsest reader who has learned the held-depth ledger is
> not flagged; the Palimpsest does not believe in contamination.
> The asymmetry is canonical.

Two grammars looking at the same wreck call two different events
*what happened here*.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Brine sailors' marginalia (referenced); Palimpsest
*double-hand* (2301).

**Thin.** **No specimen of marginalia held in two grammars at
once.** The Concord *carried column* (0001) is one body's
sub-archive for another body's instrument. There is no parallel
for Brine and Palimpsest.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The salvaged
under-hand"):

> A book recovered from a Brine wreck is, by Communion custom,
> *salt-marked* before it is read — a single ink-stroke on the
> spine, the Brine's claim that the book has been *kept and
> returned*.
>
> A Palimpsest convenor who finds a salt-marked book may add an
> under-hand stroke beneath the salt-mark. The under-hand stroke
> is, by both bodies' grammars, **non-contradicting**: the
> Brine's claim is unreduced (the sea kept it), the Palimpsest's
> is added (a layer persists).
>
> A salvaged book with both marks is *double-attended*. Brine
> doctrine treats it as a returned thing not raised again (a
> layer is a reading, not a raising). Palimpsest doctrine treats
> it as a salt-marked through-line — survived both over-writing
> and drowning. Both bodies regard double-attended books as
> *strongest evidence* in their own register, for opposite
> reasons.

The Concord, when asked, will open a clause-table for a *reading
dispute* neither body has asked to bring. The clerks call this
the *quiet table*.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** R3 catalogued *names-for-refusal* (R2), *names-for-
the-under-text* (2301), *names-for-the-irreversible* (0001).

**Thin.** **No catalogue of names for *what comes back*.**

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar, "Names for what comes back"):

> - **Brine:** *the surfacing.* What the tide brings back, by
>   its own measure. Not raised — *surfaced.*
> - **Palimpsest:** *the bleed.* The under-text pressing through
>   strongly enough to be read without the Glimpse. Bleeds are
>   the body's gift; readers do not chalk them, only record
>   their direction.
> - **Choir:** *the fruiting.* A spore-pattern that mirrors a
>   long-composted shape — a building's footprint, a body's
>   outline — recurring above ground.
> - **Pale Curation:** *re-bid.* An artifact previously bid-for
>   and lost reappears in the field. Always a procedural
>   opening, never a continuation.
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *re-opened.* A clause closed in the
>   first close that has been reopened by later testimony. The
>   open-braid lengthens.
> - **Thermoclaves (Floor):** *the re-bank.* A fire let die that
>   re-takes from the warm ash. Doctrinally rare, doctrinally
>   feared. **(Refusal):** *the un-coming-back.* What the Floor
>   calls re-bank, the Refusal denies has happened.
> - **Glassblown:** *the re-folded.* Paste-stock folded out at a
>   death without apprentice, then folded *back in* by a later
>   Glassblown's act. Doctrinally allowed, seldom; the heaviest
>   paste in any working.

The Refusal idiom is again the **theologically loudest**: to
deny the un-coming-back is the position closest to the
Palimpsest's *bleed* — both insist what is *gone* may not be
gone, on opposite grounds. Their disagreement and their agreement
are the same disagreement.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Thin.** A wreck on the seabed under a chapel that is also a
reading-room: the chapel's chalk is under-water by Brine measure.
Does the chalk *count*?

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The under-water
chalk"):

> Reading-rooms occasionally fall under tide. The Palimpsest does
> not consider these rooms lost — chalk persists by attunement,
> not by line of sight.
>
> Brine doctrine considers *anything under tide* to be in the
> sea's keeping. Communion sailors who have learned the chalk
> can *read* the under-water mark and not raise it; they can
> not chalk a new stroke beneath one. The Brine treats under-
> water chalking as *raising in another grammar*.
>
> The compromise — never doctrinally agreed, only practiced — is
> the *witnessed read*. A sailor and a convenor convene at a
> tidal reading-room at slack-tide: the convenor reads the
> existing chalk aloud, the sailor witnesses; no new chalk is
> laid; the witness becomes the carrier of any new layer the
> convenor would have written. The chalk that was not written
> is *paste-equivalent* in Glassblown grammar — the corpus's
> second artifact whose authority lives in another faction's
> memory.

The under-water chalk is **the third cross-body sub-archive**
(after Concord *carried column* and Curation *under-card field*).
Its keepers are the rarest combination in the world: chalk-sighted
sailor, depth-aware convenor, both willing to forgo mark-laying.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** Five apotheoses now in corpus by name: Choir
Communion, Palimpsest Transcription (2301), Concord walker's-
clause (2201), Glassblown unfiring (0001), and the Refusal
*un-banking* for Thermoclaves (0001).

**Thin.** **No Brine apotheosis written.** The body has been a
setting, not an invitation.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The walker who goes
under"):

> A walker offered the Brine apotheosis is asked to *go under*
> in the Communion's own grammar — not to drown, not to abandon,
> but to consent to be *kept* in the sea's measure. The walker
> remains singular and remains themselves, but their *remembered
> tense* becomes the only tense the world will address them in.
> The walker is no longer addressed in the present.
>
> NPCs answer in remembered tense — *you have come to the harbour
> again*; *you had asked me about the mill*. Quest objectives
> shift to past form: *you will have stopped the raid* (the
> future absorbed into the past, consistent with Brine tense).
> The Palimpsest reads the walker as *singled in the over-text,
> deep-kept in the under-text*. The Choir tastes the walker as
> *salted* and declines to absorb.
>
> The walker cannot un-keep. Save-state remains writable;
> ontological tense does not. A walker who reaches the end of
> play in this state experiences the corpus's only apotheosis
> whose ending is *unchanged by the ending* — the tense had
> already moved.

Five apotheoses now form a closed grid — each surrenders one of
*self* (Choir), *futurity* (Concord), *singular hand* (Palimpsest),
*the irreversible* (Glassblown), *the present* (Brine).

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Thin.** The Brine treats past as authoritative; the Palimpsest
treats under-form as evidentiary weight. The two grammars *should*
collide somewhere; no audit has audited the collision.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "Tide-and-layer"):

> A Brine speaker addressing a Palimpsest reader uses *remembered
> tense* in the over-form and the *under-form* in the
> Palimpsest's own grammar — *I have spoken to you* (Brine over)
> / *you are speaking to me* (Palimpsest under). The Brine
> speaker claims the conversation as *already held*; the
> Palimpsest under-form notes the conversation is *also being
> held now*. Neither grammar retracts.
>
> The Palimpsest reader replies in *inverted layer-grammar*
> (under first, over second), with the under-form in *Brine's
> remembered tense* — *I had been listening* (under) / *I am
> listening* (over). Neither speaker has conceded; both have
> leased.
>
> Speakers fluent in both grammars are vanishingly rare. Where
> they exist, they are *witnesses* by both bodies' own standing.

**Tide-and-layer** is the corpus's first **bilingual grammatical
artifact**. Curation files such conversations as *singled* in
error.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

Rotation: dry-walker (1301), junior cataloguer (2001), mid-fire

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar, "A slack-tide convening at
the harbour-step"):

> Sailor Vesh comes at the bottom of the ebb, half an hour before
> the turn. The harbour-step is a stone bench whose under-layer
> was a courthouse step; the chalk is under tide for most of the
> day. Vesh is one of perhaps nine Communion sailors who have
> learned the chalk-mark.
>
> Convenor Heth comes from the opposite direction along the
> shore-path, carrying a layered notebook in oilcloth. They have
> met four times. Neither knows the other's old name.
>
> Vesh names the wreck under the bench — *the* Saltwise Brava,
> *kept four hundred years and one storm*. Heth reads aloud the
> chalk on the seat: one under-hand stroke, a second beneath it,
> a third smudged by a rope's dragging. Vesh witnesses without
> speaking. Heth does not chalk a fourth.
>
> Heth speaks the layer in inverted layer-grammar with Brine-
> remembered under-form. Vesh nods once. The reply is the
> witnessing. The next walker who finds this bench will read
> three chalk-strokes, not four. The fourth lives in Vesh.
>
> The turn comes. The sea takes the seat. Heth packs the oilcloth.
> Vesh stays on the dry stone until the over-water has covered the
> chalk. The step is now only a step. Neither chalked. Neither
> raised. Both kept.

The Brine-Palimpsest convening is **the body of the corpus's
quietest convention**: the rite of *both bodies declining to mark*.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine ↔ Palimpsest seam (R4 begins)

R4 promotes the corpus from *bodies-with-anatomies* to *bodies-
in-conversation*. Seam mechanics now in the corpus: *what the
tide reads*, *the salvaged under-hand*, *names for what comes
back*, *the under-water chalk*, *the walker who goes under*,
*tide-and-layer*, *the slack-tide convening*.

**Anatomy of a seam.** A seam is not a body. A seam is *the
specific way two bodies hold the same site without erasing each
other*. Brine ↔ Palimpsest is the corpus's first seam written as
its own structural object. The witnessed-read mechanic is its
pivot: a Brine sailor and a Palimpsest convenor both decline, in
the moment, to make the mark each body's grammar tells them to
make. The decline is the seam.

**The corpus's three sub-archives, now named.** Concord *carried
column* (0001), Brine *salvaged under-hand / under-water chalk*
(this run), Curation *under-card field* (2301). The Palimpsest
*is hosted by every body that records* — its layer-grammar has
propagated into the corpus's filing infrastructure.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**The 2301 edit-debt remains unsettled.** Bible mtime is still
2026-04-30 18:01. Two prior audits have proposed the clause near
line 209. This run does *not* re-propose it; the trail is clear
enough to land in a manual session. A third re-statement would
dilute, not strengthen.

**New seam-level contradiction (this run).** Bible line 121
asserts the Palimpsest is *certain places, objects, and beings
got caught in the overwrite — they exist in multiple temporal
layers simultaneously*. The Brine's deep-kept doctrine asserts
that what is held by the sea is *not in temporal layers* — it is
in *one held depth, for one held duration*. The witnessed-read
mechanic is the corpus's de facto resolution; the bible should
explicitly note the contradiction is structural, not negotiable.
Suggested clause for a future bible edit (near line 125):

> Where the sea has covered a place that the Palimpsest reads in
> layer, both bodies' claims are incommensurate and both are
> correct under their own grammar. The Communion's *deep-kept*
> measures depth and duration; the Palimpsest's *layer* measures
> readings persisting. The two cannot be added or reconciled. The
> witnessed-read convening is the practiced compromise; it is
> doctrinally tolerated by both bodies and formally endorsed by
> neither.

**Cross-body symmetries, now four.** The corpus has surfaced:

1. Choir-Palimpsest — consume vs preserve (bible).
2. Glassblown-Thermoclave-Refusal — irreversibility doctrines (0001).
3. Glassblown-Choir — circulation-as-archive on opposite materials (0001).
4. Brine-Palimpsest — return-as-mark on opposite media (this run).

Dense enough to treat cross-body symmetry as a generative axis.
R4's continuing trajectory should look for the fifth, sixth,
seventh: Brine ↔ Concord, Curation ↔ Glassblown, and Thermoclaves
↔ Palimpsest are the obvious next pairings.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

**Title (in-world):** *Sailor Vesh's wet-book, salvaged from the
Saltwise Brava (recovered four hundred years and one storm). The
last fly-leaf, in Vesh's own hand, written above the salt-line.*
**Author:** Sailor Vesh (chalk-attuned, name not entered in any
Communion ledger).

> *Wet-book of Vesh of the harbour-step. Kept dry above the third
> course of the page. Salt-line at line nine; below the salt-line
> is the* Brava's *own marginalia, which I do not retread.*

> *Convenor Heth came at the bottom of the ebb. We have done this
> four times now. Heth does not say my name; I do not say theirs.
> The chalk was visible only because the slack was unusually low —
> the storm last week pushed the harbour out a hand's breadth.*

> *Three strokes on the seat. I named the wreck. Heth read the
> chalk. I did not reply. Heth spoke the layer aloud in the
> grammar where the under-hand is in the tense I keep. I had heard
> it once before; I will have heard it once more. The turn came.
> I stayed until the chalk was again kept by the sea, not by us.*

> *(Beneath the salt-line, in a finer hand the* Brava's *own
> sailor would not have written:)*
>
> *I have not chalked. Heth did not chalk. The fourth stroke
> lives in me. I am not a ledger. I am not a witness in the
> Concord sense — no junior writes me down. I am a witness in
> the harbour sense: I stay until the tide takes the question
> back. The next walker who finds this bench will read three
> strokes. If I die before another convenor comes, the fourth
> dies with me; Heth will lay it again, or not, depending on
> what they remember alone.*

> *(Lower marginalia, written days later in pencil and rubbed
> half-out:)*
>
> *I asked Heth, the fifth time, what their grammar calls a mark
> a body declines to make. They said:* the absent under-stroke.
> *They said the absent under-stroke is read by a reader attuned
> to absence. I asked who that is. They said it is the reader
> who has read every other stroke in the room and noticed which
> one was not laid. They said this reader is rare. They said
> they had only met two. I think they meant me. I did not ask.*

**Lands parenthetically:** the **witnessed read**; the
**harbour-step** by name; the **absent under-stroke** as a
Palimpsest category for what was declined (corpus's first
instance); the **chalk-attuned sailor** as a category Communion
ledgers cannot file; **fold-back as silence between two bodies**;
structural continuity from 0001's witnessed pause, never explicit.
The page itself is a reading-room — three layers on one fly-leaf:
*Brava-sailor* (under-salt), *Vesh* (over-salt), *Vesh's pencil*
(later-self, rubbed).

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"The bodies' doctrines are mechanically incommensurate: the
> Brine measures by how deep, how long; the Palimpsest measures
> by how many readings persist."*

No archaism. No apostrophe-fantasy. No mythic-abstract verbs. A
measurement-comparison statement: each body has a metric on the
artifact, and the metrics are not commensurable. TES rewrite would
be: *"In sooth, the Drowned Lords measure by the fathom and the
long age, while the Readers of the Stratum measure by the count
of memories yet legible upon the page; and these measures, being
of differing essence, cannot be made one."* I did not write that.
Seam vocabulary (*witnessed read*, *absent under-stroke*,
*salt-mark*, *double-attended*, *tide-and-layer*) names
instruments; the narrator reports without performing the bodies'
cadence — same restraint as Glassblown *hand-grammar*, Concord
*clause-aspect*, Palimpsest *layer-grammar*.
