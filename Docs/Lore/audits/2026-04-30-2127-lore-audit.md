## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible, line 21:* "It did not merely destroy the old civilization — it
> overwrote reality itself, imperfectly, the way text on a medieval
> manuscript was scraped off and written over with the old words still
> faintly visible underneath."

**Strong.** Five named cataclysm-accounts; intra-Choir schism (1813);
pre-cataclysm Periods (1901); the Long Between (2001).

**Thin.** The bible treats the cataclysm as *one* event. But its own
metaphor — palimpsest, layered overwrites — implies *more than one
overwrite has happened.* The corpus has not asked the obvious follow-up.

**Concrete addition (bible, new short subsection at the end of "The
Cataclysm" section, after line 25, "How many cataclysms"):**

> The metaphor is layered text. The world was scraped, written, scraped,
> written. Most factions agree there was *one cataclysm*, the recent one,
> whose ruins are still warm. The Palimpsest does not. It reads, in
> places where the layering is thinnest, prior overwrites — older hands,
> older worlds, scraped at intervals the present cannot resolve. It will
> not say how many. The Pale Curation has internally classified the
> hypothesis as Disputed (Period V), the highest grade of dispute the
> master-index supports. The Choir says it has only ever tasted *this*
> soil; whatever was beneath was tasted by something that is no longer
> the Choir, and not continuous with it. The Thermoclaves refuse the
> question on the grounds that the *next* burn is the one to think about.

The metaphor was load-bearing and the corpus had not yet leaned on it.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** Cataclysm-names (JSON); Choir outsider-names (1813);
Palimpsest and substrate outsider-names (1901); joining-words (2001).

**Thin.** Each apotheosis has only its own faction's name (*Communion*,
*Transcription*). What other factions call those endings is the
single sharpest available test of how each faction's worldview fails to
process the central two.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "What others call the apotheoses,"
placed after the comparison table, line 205):**

> **Communion (the Choir's ending):**
> - Palimpsest: *the silent reading* — a page that fed itself back
>   into the spine.
> - Pale Curation: *Subject Disposition: Aggregated* — filed under the
>   same code as items lost to the wall.
> - Concord: *the unrecoverable purchase* — the buyer walked through
>   the door and the door was the seller.
> - Brine: *the going-down that did not return at tide.*
> - Thermoclaves: *the long quench* — same verb as their initiation,
>   with a duration-particle. The rite is brief; the apotheosis is not.
>
> **Transcription (the Palimpsest's ending):**
> - Choir: *the reader-eaten-by-the-page.* Not horror — mild surprise.
>   The Choir thought eating only ran one way.
> - Pale Curation: *Subject Disposition: Annexed by Source.* Filed into
>   the artifact register.
> - Concord: *the contract that ate its own counterparty.* Concord
>   legal scholars consider this a category error and refuse the
>   metaphor.
> - Brine: *the writing-into-the-water.* They consider the deep to do
>   something similar to the bones it keeps.
> - Thermoclaves: *the cold flame* — a fire that gives no heat is a
>   fire being remembered, not kept. They consider this dishonest and
>   do not tend it.

Each outsider's word smuggles in a critique. The Concord's *contract
that ate its own counterparty* is the funniest and the most damning.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Substrate-leak as cause; Palimpsest does not seal cracks
(1813); Choir metabolism (1901); Echo-Reading limits (2001).

**Thin.** The bible never specifies what happens when **the two
metaphysics meet inside one player.** A Choir-acolyte tries Echo Reading.
A Palimpsest-attuned walker stands on Choir-colonised ground. The
faction-entanglement rule (line 244) tells the designer it should be
hard; it does not tell the cosmology *what fails* and *how.*

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection after the Faction Entanglement
section, line 244, "When the metaphysics meet"):**

> A walker who carries the Choir's mycelial node and attempts Echo
> Reading reads only their own filaments. The blade in their hand
> registers as the chord of every blade the Choir has ever digested,
> simultaneous, indistinguishable. The skill is not gone — the reader's
> antenna is. They get the wall, hummed back at them, in their own
> bones. (Some readers, after the first attempt, find this beautiful
> and stop trying to read anything else. The Palimpsest does not call
> them readers any more, and is sad about it.)
>
> The reverse is gentler. A Palimpsest-attuned walker on Choir-grown
> ground feels the soil reading them — a low conversational pressure,
> not hostile. The Choir samples the layered self the Palimpsest has
> built and treats it as a particularly *interesting* offering, sweet
> in a way fresh meat is not. It does not consume Palimpsest readers
> without consent, but it pays them more attention. Walkers in this
> state describe Choir territory as warmer than other walkers do. They
> are not wrong; the Choir is leaning toward them.

The interference is **asymmetric** — Choir-acolytes lose the noetic
faculty; Palimpsest-acolytes get more attention than they wanted —
matching the bible's emotional registers (line 202).

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** Mirror endings; thresholds (1813); Annihilist refusals
(1901); Stalled Walkers (2001).

**Thin.** Both apotheoses are documented from the *inside* — what it
feels like to undergo. Neither has a documented **outside view**. What
a Curation surveyor sees when a Communion happens. What a tendril
notices when a Transcription completes. The corpus has the subjective
side and lacks the objective side; this is the wrong way around for a
project whose central question is *which version of events is real.*

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, after line 189, "How the
apotheoses look from outside"):**

> **A Communion observed.** A Pale Curation surveyor, present by
> accident or assignment, sees the player walk into a chemical-gradient
> room and stand. After roughly three days the player is no longer
> where they were last seen; the wall in that location is fractionally
> thicker; the gradient has a new emotional register the surveyor
> cannot name. There is no body. The Curation files this under *Subject
> Disposition: Aggregated.* Surveyors describe the soil afterward as
> *less talkative* for several weeks. The Choir would say: *we were
> listening to our new mouth.*
>
> **A Transcription observed.** The Palimpsest requires a witness. The
> witness sees the player go thin and become *an earlier sentence in
> the room.* The witness's later memory of the moment is unstable: sure
> it happened; not sure where they were standing; sure of the player's
> name; not sure who told them the name. The Choir, if a tendril is
> in the wall, says the player tasted *thin and far away* in the seconds
> before — and cannot taste the Transcription itself, only its absence
> after.

Communion leaves a thicker wall and a quieter soil (additive).
Transcription leaves an unstable witness (subtractive, deferred).

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Choir pronoun progression; the five voices' grammars;
Palimpsest grammar (1813); Brine grammar (2001).

**Thin.** Thermoclaves owed (per rotation). Their speech-register is
unwritten.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "Thermoclave Grammar Rules"):**

> Thermoclave speech is built around three verb-aspects mapped to fire
> states. Every important verb takes one of three particles — *-keep*
> (banked, ongoing, requires attention), *-quench* (deliberately ended),
> or *-cold* (failed, ended without intention; the gravest particle,
> applied to fires that went out and to people who did). *The bread is
> keep* means the keeper is accountable for it not going cold; *quench*
> means eaten or given away with intention; *cold* means forgotten,
> spoiled, and a keeper has named themselves at fault. There is no
> neutral past tense for actions a keeper performs.
>
> No first-person *I*. Speakers use *the keeper*, with titles varying
> by station — *Renne the younger*, *the keeper of the third pit.*
> Speaking outside one's role is rare and considered the beginning of
> an exit conversation. *The keeper of the eighth pit is tired* is
> working register; *Aud is tired* is not a sentence said lightly.
>
> No future tense — the future of fire is contingent on the keeping.
> *We will burn that wood tomorrow* is phrased *the keeper holds that
> wood for the next keeping* — the action implicit in the holding.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Strong.** Tuesday Morning so far: Choir, Palimpsest (1813); Concord
(1901); Brine (2001).

**Thin.** Thermoclaves and Pale Curation owed; Glassblown owed and
hardest. This run pays Thermoclaves; the Glassblown gets its first
artifact in Section 5.

**Concrete addition (bible, "Tuesday Morning" section, new subsection):**

> **The Thermoclaves.** *Food:* whatever the keeper was *keeping* —
> bread held over the pit's slow side, root-vegetables charred under
> ash, salt-wood smoke flavouring everything. They do not eat anything
> cold; a cold meal is a keeper's failure, taken to the *cold-fire* (an
> unkept pit on the camp's edge) and burnt before sunrise. *Greeting:*
> an open palm extended near the visitor's face, for the visitor to
> feel body-warmth. A cold palm is a confession; a hot palm is a claim.
> *Naming custom:* a *hearth-name* from the pit a child was warmed
> beside as an infant, and a *station-name* once they keep their first
> fire alone. A Thermoclave whose station-name has gone *cold* — first
> fire failed — is given a second chance under their hearth-name only.
> *Mourning:* the body is burned at the *quench-fire*. The ashes are
> mixed with the sand at the bottom of every working pit in the camp.
> Every fire the camp keeps thereafter has a measurable mass of the
> dead in it. *Currency:* none formal. Fuel — *armfuls* — is the unit.
> A favour can be paid in three armfuls of dry pine. Wealth is dryness.

Ashes mixed into the working pits makes every working fire a continuous
funeral. That is the lore.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclaves

The Thermoclaves have no shipped cataclysm-name (JSON:481 routes
*Burning* to the Glassblown — see Section 4c). 2001's tally and joining-
words sidebar gave them a cameo. They are the fire-keepers, and they
have the bones of a metabolism and no doctrine.

**Proposed lore-artifact: the Apprentice's Catechism, fragment.**

The Thermoclaves teach through call-and-response — not because the
doctrine is sacred but because fire-tending must be *practiced aloud*
until the response is faster than the thinking. The catechism is the
first text an apprentice memorises. The keeper writes it on a single
sheet of cured hide, and the apprentice burns the sheet on the day
they keep their first fire alone.

```
[A piece of cured hide, scorched at one corner. The text is in the
 careful hand of a senior keeper. The apprentice's name is not on it.]

  KEEPER:    Why do we keep nine fires?
  APPRENTICE: Because nine is what we have learned to keep.

  KEEPER:    What of the tenth?
  APPRENTICE: The tenth is not kept. The tenth is older than keeping.
              The tenth is older than the camp. The keepers do not light
              the tenth.

  KEEPER:    Who lit the tenth?
  APPRENTICE: A keeper before all keepers. The tenth has been kept
              by no hand for as long as any hand has counted.

  KEEPER:    Is the tenth a keep, a quench, or a cold?
  APPRENTICE: [the apprentice does not answer. The silence is the
              answer. The keeper accepts it.]

  KEEPER:    What does the tenth want?
  APPRENTICE: The tenth wants what fire wants. We do not know what
              fire wants. The keepers have been wrong about this
              before.

  KEEPER:    What is your work?
  APPRENTICE: To keep what I am given. To quench what I have finished.
              To name the cold honestly when I have failed. To not
              touch the tenth.

  KEEPER:    Walk well, unburnt. The next fire is keep.
```

Lore landed: the tenth fire predates the faction's existence; the
question of whether it is *kept, quenched, or cold* is doctrinally
unanswerable, so the Thermoclaves are organised around a mystery they
refuse to resolve; the apprentice's first act of mastery is *burning
the doctrine that taught them*, which structurally guarantees the
catechism is never quoted by anyone fully trained.

The tenth fire is probably a substrate-leak that pre-dates the present
camp. The catechism does not claim this. It does not have to.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a) The somatic/noetic binary leaks at the top of each progression.**

Bible line 137 commits to Choir = somatic, Palimpsest = noetic. But
line 79's Tremor Sense is perceptual (noetic by the bible's own
definition); line 153's Temporal Refraction splits the body (somatic).
The leak is at Stage 5/6 in both — game-design wants cross-register
utility at the apex. **Fix:** add one paragraph at line 137 saying the
binary is porous at apotheosis because each faction at full strength
is asserting itself across the *other's* domain — the Choir reaches
into perception because it has digested enough minds to think in their
geometry; the Palimpsest reaches into the body because it has read
enough physical moments to write them. The leak is the apotheosis.

**(b) The Choir cannot act quickly (line 47); the tendril is real-time.**

Bible line 47: "patience has a cost: the Choir cannot act quickly.
Mycelium grows at millimeters per day." But ChoirTendril_1 holds a
fluid, responsive conversation. Bible should clarify: *the Choir's body
cannot act quickly; its language has no such constraint, because
language is the Choir's fastest organ.*

**(c) JSON:481 routes the *Burning* to the Glassblown, not the
Thermoclaves.**

The fire-faction does not own its own cataclysm-name. Resolvable if
the Glassblown were burned *into* glass — survivors of an event the
Thermoclaves ritualise but the Glassblown were *the fuel of.* That
also explains why the Glassblown refuse to speak further: it is
indecent to ask a fuel to describe being burnt. Bible should adopt
this reading so future writers do not split the *Burning* across two
factions and produce contradictory etymologies.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

The Glassblown Remnant: their first artifact in the corpus.

The Glassblown are the silence-faction. They were burned. They emerged
from the burning as something else — slumped into glass-like surfaces,
some bright, some opaque, some still warm. They do not write histories.
They do not give testimony. *They make markers.*

A Glassblown grief-marker is a palm-sized round of cured glass with
six numbered fields etched into it. The fields are filled out by the
mourner, in glass-paste, on the day the marker is fired. Most fields
are left blank. The blanks are the testimony.

**Title (in-game):** *Grief-marker, fired round, six fields.*

```
[A palm-sized disc of cured glass. Slightly warm to the touch even
 in cold weather. The etched fields below; the in-paste responses
 are reproduced in italics where they exist.]

   1. NAME OF THE LOST:    [blank]
   2. TIME OF THE LOSS:    *the Burning. eleventh hour, second day.*
   3. WHAT REMAINS:        *the right hand. an unbroken cup.*
   4. WHAT WAS SAID:       [blank]
   5. WHAT WAS NOT SAID:   *the question the lost was asking when
                            the heat came. it was a small question.
                            it was for me. I have not asked it of
                            anyone else. I will not.*
   6. WHO TENDED:          *I tended. I was unable to keep. the
                            Thermoclave keeper offered. I refused.
                            this loss is not for keeping.*

[in the disc's rim, very small, the maker's mark:]
  *fired by the second-shape. round 4119 of the eleventh year.*

[on the back, in a different paste, in a hand other than the
 maker's:]
  *the marker is correct. the lost would have approved of the
  blanks. the lost was always more careful than was kind to her.
  — A SECOND MOURNER*
```

Word count: 196. Lore landed: the Glassblown record absence as a
*positive datum* — Field 5 is the ritual centerpiece, *what was not
said*; the Burning has a specific time-stamp (*eleventh hour, second
day*) — the corpus's first dated cataclysm-event; markers are made
in numbered batches by titled artisans (*the second-shape* implies a
first and third, possibly anatomical — shaped *second* in the
Burning); the Thermoclaves *offered to tend* and were refused — two
fire-adjacent factions on the same metaphysics in moral opposition
(Thermoclaves keep; Glassblown refuse to keep).

Plant: a Glassblown ruin's rafters; a Curation Tier-3 vault under
*minor sculptural artifacts, decorative*; a Brine sailor's pocket. The
Concord *will not sell them*; the only currency they accept for one
is another grief.

The blanks are the find. The countersigner is the lore.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

Quoting one of my own sentences and verifying it does not read as a
TES book:

> *"The Glassblown record absence as a positive datum."*

No archaisms. No invented proper nouns. No apostrophe-clusters. No
*verily*, no *by [deity]*, no Towers, no Aedra. The sentence is an
epistemological observation rendered in the project's preferred
register — a small piece of jargon (*positive datum*) used straight,
the way the corpus uses *substrate*, *the under-tide*, *the long
quiet*. A TES book would have written *"and so the Glassblown, in
their silent wisdom, have made of absence a sacred presence, that the
unspoken might endure when the speaker cannot." * Mine reads like a
field note from a discipline that has not yet been invented but is
being invented in this project. Confirmed.
