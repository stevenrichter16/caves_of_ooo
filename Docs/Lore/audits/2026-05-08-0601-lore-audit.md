## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (0101 §2.1):** *"the keepsake-chart has a
working-life the body does not file."*

**Strong.** Outside-life canonised as the chart's
third life.

**Thin.** Strata of return have not been counted.
A keepsake may have one return (the original
outside hand) or many (hand-of-a-hand transmission,
the keepsake passed to a junior outside trader).
Each return is a stratum the body has no register
for. The chart is mythic-historically *deeper than
its working-life*: outside-hands stack un-legible
layers atop the body's only legible portion.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Brine Communion,
append after 0101 §2.1's keepsake-outside-life
paragraph):**

> *A keepsake-chart returns to a Communion mooring
> at most once through its original outside hand,
> but the chart itself may return many times
> through hand-of-a-hand transmission — a junior
> trader inheriting it from her own senior, never
> having met its first bearings-pilot. The body has
> no working-form for these returns. The chart
> stratifies in outside-hand layers the body cannot
> count: "she may be a hand's chart or a hand-of-a-
> hand's chart. we cannot tell. the wearing is
> wear, and wear does not say through how many
> hands."*

The chart's outside-life reads at *n* strata, where
*n* is structurally illegible to the Communion —
mythic-historical depth canonised as *illegible-at-
the-body*.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (0101 §2.2):** *"a dock-watcher's apprentice
sometimes glances at the chart while the outside
hand is at the rope-table — the body's only
working-encounter with a chart-feature carried
across decades."*

**Strong.** Apprentice-glance located outside trust-
as-wear, outside cadence-handing.

**Thin.** The glance has no working-form on the
apprentice's side either. The apprentice cannot
say *whom* the inherited feature is from: not the
outside hand (who is not a Communion authority),
not the bearings-pilot decades dead, not her own
senior (who has not handed the feature). Authorship
of the inherited feature is, on her end, **unowned**.
The body has six authorship-positions canonised
elsewhere; the apprentice's glance is a seventh,
the only one whose author is structurally *no body
and no person*.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Brine Communion,
new sidebar "The unowned feature"):**

> *An apprentice asked from whom she learned a
> chart-feature inherited at the dockside-glance
> answers:* "i do not know. the chart was on the
> rope-table. the hand was at his work. the feature
> is in my own working-form now, and i do not know
> whose hand wore it there." *Authorship-doctrine
> in the Communion reads at seven positions:
> bearings-pilot, senior-pilot, dock-watcher,
> Curation cataloguer (threshold), retirement-
> landing's outside hand, long-trading outside hand
> (no body), and **the apprentice's unowned feature

The apprentice's inherited feature is, by working-
form, **authored by no one** — and it is by this
clause that it survives, because nothing the body
could have done to prevent it would have had a
working-form to act through.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted (§3, the dockside-glance):** *"a feature
lifts off the chart and into her own working-form.
she steers a hand's-breadth wider of a reef on her
first voyage and cannot say why; her senior,
watching, takes it for trust-as-wear and says
nothing."*

Working-modern register. *Working-form*, *hand's-
breadth*, *first voyage*, *trust-as-wear*, *takes
it for*, *says nothing* — plain craft-clerical
compounds and contemporary vernacular phrases.
*Lifts off*, *steers*, *cannot say why* are
contemporary; no fronted nominal, no *hath / lieth
/ unto / verily / betwixt*.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"A feature lifteth
itself from the Chart and into her own Working-
Form, whereupon she steereth a hand-breadth wider
of the Reef upon her First Voyage and cannot speak
the reason thereof; her Senior, watching, taketh it
for Trust-as-Wear and saith naught."* Tells:
*lifteth, steereth, taketh, saith*; *thereof*;
*upon her First Voyage* (capitalised nominal);
*naught*. Actual sentence is none of these.

**Pass.**
