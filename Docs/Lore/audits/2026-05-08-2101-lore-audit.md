## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (bible L21):** *"The cataclysm is the
founding event of the entire world. It did not
merely destroy the old civilization — it overwrote
reality itself, imperfectly, the way text on a
medieval manuscript was scraped off and written
over with the old words still faintly visible
underneath."*

**Thin.** The bible's overwrite-as-imperfect-
erasure metaphor reads as a single-temporal-
frame imperfection: one writing, one scraping,
one re-writing. The Glassblown's settling-bench
finding canonises a structurally distinct
imperfection — **the imperfection that develops
between two readings of the same piece**. Glass
read at threshold and glass read at season's end
are not two pieces; they are the same piece read
through two bench-days separated by an annealing
season. *Settle-drift* is the imperfection that
the cataclysm-as-overwrite metaphor cannot model
because the metaphor presumes a single act of
overwriting; settle-drift is **drift-without-an-
overwriter**, the piece changing through its own
medium under no agent's hand. The cataclysm-as-
manuscript metaphor admits one scribe; the
Glassblown's settle-card admits a second imperfection-
without-scribe.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm
[L21 paragraph], append after the manuscript
metaphor):**

> *(The Glassblown reads the cataclysm through
> a second metaphor: forward-anneal. Glass cooled
> too quickly carries stress-lines that crack a
> season later in still air. The cataclysm is not
> only an overwriting; it is a piece on the
> cooling-bench whose drift-clause has not
> finished writing itself. Some pieces settle true.
> Others drift, and the drift-clause is the
> working-record of where the overwrite was
> thinnest.)*

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Glassblown Remnant (rotation, second sitting)

The Glassblown last landed at 1601 (settling-
bench, *on settle*, *to set* verb, settle-card,
bench-clerk, forward-anneal as second canon
working-frame). This sitting lands the discipline's
**season-end working-form** — the second bench-
day reading that bifurcates the piece's record
on a temporal-discrepancy binary.

### Etymology — *clear*, *drift*, *drift-clerk*

The Glassblown's vocabulary holds *settling-
bench* (working-record), *on settle* (preposition),
*set* (intake-verb), and now **cleared** /
**drifted** (season-end reading-pair) and
**drift-clerk** (working-form actor). *Clear* is
the discipline's first **agreement-form**:
season-end reading not-different from threshold.
*Drift* is its first **deviation-form**: season-
end reading different from threshold, without
specifying which reading is the piece's true
reading. The piece is filed as *drifted*; both
readings carry on the settle-card as co-equal
canon entries. The Glassblown is the slate's
first working-form whose canon filing is
**structurally double-keyed without resolution**.

**Drift-clerk.** A senior bench-clerk whose
working-day specialises in close-reading of
cooled glass against threshold marks. The only
working-form actor on the slate whose canon
working-action is **reading a piece against a
prior bench-clerk's bench-mark**; the drift-
clerk's canon-entry can name a deviation the
threshold bench-clerk did not predict. The two
bench-clerks are not in conflict — the working-
form admits the readings are made by different
working-eyes on different working-days, and
does not require continuity between them.

### The bi-temporal settle-card

The settle-card now canonises on a bi-temporal
form: a *threshold-side* (working-week of arrival,
threshold bench-clerk's hand, four-mark bench-
mark) and a *season-end-side* (working-week of
season's end, drift-clerk's hand, four-mark
season-end reading, and a *cleared* / *drifted*
closing-line with drift-clause if drifted). The
slate's first working-record whose canon entries
are **separated by a working-season of bench-
quiet** — the piece sits untouched and the season-
end-side is filled only at the season's end. The
working-form admits a piece's filing takes a
season to canon; threshold reading is not yet
filing.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Bleed 1 — bible §The Cataclysm L21
*"overwriting reality itself, imperfectly"*
vs. the Glassblown's drift-clause.** The bible
reads cataclysm-imperfection as an act-of-
overwriting whose imperfection is the visibility
of the underlying text. The Glassblown's settle-
card finding admits a structurally distinct
imperfection — **drift without an overwriter**.
A glass-piece drifts through annealing under no
agent's hand; the working-form's record carries
*the drift itself* as canon. Suggested L21
supplement (extending the manuscript metaphor
without altering it):

> *(The manuscript metaphor admits one scribe.
> The Glassblown's forward-anneal frame admits
> none — drift is what a piece does in still
> air, on its own medium, between two readings
> by potentially two different hands. The
> Palimpsest's reading of the cataclysm canons
> both: the overwrite is the scribe's
> imperfection, the drift is the medium's
> imperfection. Each reading-frame catches the
> other's blind spot.)*

**Bleed 2 — bible L189 *"Transcription — the
player becomes a memory, written into the
palimpsest forever"* vs. the settle-card's
bi-temporal authorship.** The Transcription
ending implies a single *writing-into* — one
authoring act, finalising the player's
preservation. The settle-card finding canonises
a working-form whose record is **two writings
across a season's quiet**. Suggested L189
supplement (extending the Transcription gloss):

> *(The Transcription ending, read through the
> settle-card, is bi-temporal. The first writing
> is the player's threshold mark — who they are
> at the moment of the act. The second writing
> is the season-end reading — what the world's
> working-record says of them after the cooling.
> The settle-card's canon admits both readings
> on the same card; the Palimpsest's Transcription
> canons the player's threshold-and-cooling on
> the same archive-line.)*
