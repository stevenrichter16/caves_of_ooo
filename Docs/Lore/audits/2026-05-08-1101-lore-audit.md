## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord (rotation)

The Concord is named in the bible as a faction
with a "founder" whose darkest secret is permanently
revealed by a Tier-4 Palimpsest quest (bible L185).
It has not been built out beyond that pointer.
This sitting lands a working-form for one register
and an etymology for one of its standing rituals.

### Etymology — the Standing Pour

The Concord's central practice is the **Standing
Pour** — a working-week-end ritual at any
confectioner's bench. The verbal form is *to stand
a pour*, never *to pour*. The grammatical inversion
encodes the doctrine: in Concord working-tradition,
the syrup's sweetness is held by the *standing*
of the working-form, not by the act of pouring.
A confectioner who pours without standing has
produced syrup the working-tradition reads as
*unstood* — not failed, not spoiled, but
uninhabited by the working-form that would have
made it the bench's product.

The Concord's etymology for *stand* derives from a
pre-cataclysm honey-trade vocabulary in which the
verb meant *to remain present at a hive while the
bees worked*. The Concord retained the verb across
the cataclysm without retaining the bees; the
verb's working-form is now the only living trace
of a vanished apiary tradition. A Concord
apprentice asked what *to stand* originally meant
answers: *to stay where the working-bodies are
working*. The apprentice does not know the
working-bodies were bees. The Concord holds the
verb across a substrate-shift the practice has not
named.

### The apprentice's tasting-card

A Concord apprentice's first-month working-form
is the **tasting-card** — a small folded card
carried bench-side, on which the apprentice records
a single taste-reading per working-day. The card's
three columns are *front* (the syrup's first
taste), *middle* (the standing-minute taste), and
*back* (the after-taste, read at second-light from
the door-side). The columns are not flavours; they
are *positions in the standing*. A tasting-card
filed at week-end with three identical readings
is filed as *unstood*; differentiated readings
file as *stood*.

The Concord's working-form does not name what
should differentiate the three columns. The
differentiation is the apprentice's working-form
to discover; the bench-senior reads the card at
week-end without comment. A senior asked what
makes a card *stood* answers: *the front, middle,
and back are not the same syrup*. The apprentice
is left to find the three syrups in one pour.

### The bench's silence

The Concord bench does not speak during the
standing-minute. A single bell at the standing's
start, a single bell at its end, silence between.
The apprentice who speaks is filed as *unstood-
the-pour* regardless of the tasting-card. The
working-form's first lesson: **the working-pour
is read by the bench, not heard by the bench**.
The Concord's standing-discipline and the
Glassblown's cooling-watch are working-form
parallels at two media — sweetness and glass —
both reading slow-change at three positions, both
filing on paper at week-end, both silent during
the central minute.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

*"a confectioner who pours without standing has

Working-modern register. *Confectioner*, *pours*,
*standing*, *working-tradition*, *unstood*,
*uninhabited*, *bench's product* — plain craft-
clerical and contemporary trade-vocabulary, lower-

*made* are all contemporary. No fronted nominal,
no *hath / lieth / verily / unto / wherein*; no
capitalised abstract nouns; *standing* is a
working-tradition technical term, not *the
Standing*.
