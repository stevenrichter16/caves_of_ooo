## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *R2/twelfth-audit summary:* *"The Glassblown have no doctrine for
> the smoke-born and consider that doctrinal."*

**Strong.** The cataclysm-account is canonically *a form, not a
text*. The unfired-state cosmology is anti-symmetric to the
Thermoclave refused stratum.

**Thin.** **No second-generation stratification.** R2 gave the
Glassblown a doctrine of *no doctrine*. The corpus has not asked
what happens when a Glassblown disagrees with a *previous*
Glassblown's deferred shape — internal succession in a body whose
first principle is non-firing.

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar, "The folded inheritance"):

> A Glassblown's apprentice does not inherit the master's archive,
> because there is no archive of disagreements — only fired shapes,
> the *received agreements*. Inheritance is the *paste-stock*: the
> running batch of unfired material into which every disagreement
> the master ever folded back is now distributed. An apprentice who
> takes their master's stock takes the master's *unfired record*
> by mass.
>
> A senior Glassblown can, in extremis, *re-derive* an old refusal
> by working a portion of the inherited paste back to the
> consistency of a known shape. The recovery is approximate; two
> seniors working the same paste-stock will arrive at related but
> non-identical shapes. The Glassblown consider both correct: the
> point of the unfired letter is that the *refusal* was never a
> single thing in the first place.
>
> A *purchased* paste-stock is *honest paste, foreign stock*. The
> buyer inherits no refusals from it; the seller is understood to
> have folded their own refusals out before the sale. The Concord,
> when adjudicating Glassblown bequests, treats inherited paste as
> the heaviest non-fired asset in the corpus.

History stratifies *as material*: the further back, the more the
paste-stock has been folded, the less any single refusal can be
recovered intact. The cataclysm itself is, in this register, the
oldest fold every Glassblown's stock contains a fraction of.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** R2 *unfired letter* — the corpus's first non-document.
Curation transcripts, slate-soot register, double-hand: four
registers of partial-record now.

**Thin.** **What a Glassblown writes when paste is unavailable.**
Apprentices in the field — at a Concord clause-table, on a
Thermoclave fire-shift, in a Curation vault — cannot fold paste
back. Yet they are sometimes asked to commit a position.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The witnessed pause"):

> A Glassblown asked to commit in a setting where paste is
> unavailable performs a **witnessed pause** — they stop, hold
> empty hands at the height they would hold paste, and remain
> silent for a duration measured by the witness in breaths. The
> position is recorded by the witness as *paste-equivalent*: the
> shape the Glassblown would have made, had paste been available,
> is held in the witness's memory rather than in any material.
>
> A witnessed pause is not a fired shape. It binds the witness, not
> the Glassblown. The witness becomes the *carrier* of the shape:
> obligated to remember it, obligated to recognize a Glassblown's
> later paste-shape if it matches. If the Glassblown later fires
> the same shape into paste, the witness's memory is superseded
> and may be released. If the Glassblown later folds it back, the
> witness's memory is *the only record that the deferral ever
> happened*.
>
> Concord clerks who have witnessed pauses keep a separate ledger
> for them — the *carried column* — and never countersign the
> column on behalf of another clerk. *A witness is not paste,* a
> Glassblown saying goes; *a witness forgets.*

The corpus's first artifact whose *authority lives in another
faction's memory*. The Concord runs a Glassblown sub-archive
without ever filing for it.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** R2 *names-for-refusal* across seven factions. R3-2301
*names for the under-text* across seven factions.

**Thin.** **Names for the fired-and-final.** Every body has a word
for *the irreversible* — the moment after which a thing cannot be
unmade. The Glassblown grammar has *fired*; the others have not
been asked.

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar, "Names for the irreversible"):

> - **Glassblown:** *fired.* Re-batching is doctrinally impossible.
> - **Choir:** *composted.* The thing's nutrients are distributed.
>   The Choir does not consider compost *irreversible* in the
>   Glassblown sense — to a Glassblown, this is *fired-by-erosion.*
> - **Palimpsest:** *over-written-clean.* A page worn by reading
>   until no second layer remains. The Palimpsest mourns these.
>   The Glassblown approve.
> - **Pale Curation:** *catalogued.* The card is stamped, filed,
>   archive-numbered; never *uncatalogued*. The Curation's
>   irreversibility is *bureaucratic*, which the Glassblown find
>   reassuring.
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *standing-closed.* Procedural-close
>   (2201) is, in this grammar, *fired-but-warm.*
> - **Brine Communion:** *deep-kept.* Re-raising is technically
>   possible and doctrinally forbidden.
> - **Thermoclaves (Floor):** *banked.* The fire is now the coal
>   under the next fire. **(Refusal):** *un-banked* — the fire let
>   die without being banked, the only honest end.

The Refusal grammar is again the **theologically loudest**. To
*un-bank* is to insist the irreversible is a doctrine, not a fact.
On this question, Glassblown and Refusal are in the quietest
agreement in the corpus.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

> *2301 closer:* *"a future audit should consider whether
> Glassblown is a body in the same sense or a craft-tradition
> crossing bodies — its R3 shape may not be anatomical at all."*

**Strong.** R2 *unfired state* — a state-claim, not a substrate-
claim, anti-symmetric to Thermoclave's stratum.

**Thin.** **No room.** Six bodies have rooms. The Glassblown have
*paste*. The corpus has not yet decided whether that is its body.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The kiln-that-is-not"):

> The Glassblown have no central place. They have no First Root, no
> Hall, no vault, no shrine, no keep, no reading-room. What they
> have is a **distributed kiln** — every Glassblown's working hearth
> is, by doctrine, a node of the same kiln. There is no central
> fire. The kiln is the network of small fires.
>
> The body's anatomy is not a structure but a *consistency*: the
> paste-stock as it moves between Glassblown by inheritance, gift,
> sale, and theft. A Glassblown is not a person who *belongs* to
> the body — they are a person who *holds a portion of the body's
> paste*. The body is what the paste, in aggregate, has not yet
> been fired into.
>
> When a Glassblown dies without an apprentice, their stock is not
> inherited — it is *folded out* by neighbouring Glassblown into
> their own batches, and the dead Glassblown's deferrals are
> redistributed across the body. There is no funeral. There is a
> *folding-out.*

The Glassblown is a body whose anatomy is **the absence of a
single working surface**. It crosses other bodies' rooms — a
Glassblown hearth in a Curation back-room, in a Concord vestibule,
on a Brine shore — but never builds its own. It is a body *and* a
craft-tradition. The answer to 2301's question is *both, by design.*

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** R2 named *unfiring* and the act *deny the firing's
reality, work as though it were not.*

**Thin.** **The Glassblown apotheosis is the only one whose
practitioner cannot say they have achieved it.** A Choir-communion
walker is communed; a Palimpsest-transcribed walker is doubled. An
*unfired* walker, by doctrine, must not commit the claim —
*committing it would be firing.*

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The walker who is not
fired"):

> A walker offered *unfiring* by the Glassblown is not asked to
> become anything. They are asked to *not become* — to refuse, in
> their own person, the irreversibility of every commitment they
> have made through play.
>
> The mechanic: a walker who accepts is *re-batched*. Reputation,
> faction-standing, and quest-completion states from the run so
> far are folded back into the walker's base-state, partially
> recoverable but no longer load-bearing. The walker may continue
> to play. NPCs who knew them remember a walker who had *almost*
> committed to them; the Concord opens an adjacent-not-filed
> entry; the Choir tastes the walker as *unsettled*; the
> Palimpsest reads them as *singled*.
>
> The walker cannot tell anyone they have unfired. Doctrine
> forbids the claim; if the walker speaks the word, the Glassblown
> treat the walker as having *fired* the unfiring, which
> doctrinally undoes it. The unfired state is preserved only by
> the walker's silence about it.

The first apotheosis whose **mechanical signature is the rollback
of mechanical signatures**. Choir Communion absorbs; Concord
closure forecloses; Palimpsest Transcription doubles; Glassblown
unfiring *un-irreversibles.* Kept by *not telling another NPC.*

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Brine *remembered tense*, Concord *clause-aspect*,
Refusal *dropped count-particle*, Palimpsest *layer-grammar* (2301).

**Thin.** **Glassblown grammar is shape-literacy, not speech.** The
corpus has named ~400 shapes (R2) and *correspondence-by-refusal*
(R2) but no spoken-grammar correlate. What does a Glassblown
*sentence* sound like?

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "Hand-grammar"):

> A Glassblown speaks every full sentence with their **non-
> dominant hand cupped at chest-height**, holding the position the
> sentence would hold if it were paste. The hand moves while the
> sentence is being formed; it stops when the sentence stops. A
> sentence committed to without the hand-position is, in their
> grammar, an *unmade sentence* — heard, but not held.
>
> Hand-grammar has two further marks. *Closing* the hand around
> the imagined paste at sentence-end commits the sentence;
> *folding* the hand open is the spoken equivalent of folding
> paste back — the sentence rescinded, the imagined paste returned
> to the imagined batch. Witnesses who can read hand-grammar treat
> a folded-open sentence as never spoken; witnesses who cannot,
> hear only words and assume the speaker contradicted themself.
>
> Concord clerks who deal with Glassblown regularly have learned
> *passive hand-grammar* — they cannot speak it but they recognize
> it. A clause-table at which a Glassblown sits has a small mat
> placed at chest-height on the Glassblown's side, by Concord
> custom. The mat is empty. It is the place where the hand may
> imagine the paste.

The Glassblown's grammar is **the body's only somatic grammar** —
every other body's lives in syntax, tense, or aspect. The
Glassblown's lives in the hand.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

Rotation: dry-walker (1301), junior cataloguer (2001), mid-fire
keeper (2101), Hall-of-Open junior (2201), reading-room convenor
(2301).

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar, "A Tuesday at a Glassblown
hearth"):

> Pre-dawn: the Glassblown rises before the small fire and tends
> it. The fire is not the night-fire — it was banked at dusk and
> let cool. Each working day starts on a *cold-fire re-light*, by
> doctrine, so that no day's paste inherits another day's heat.
>
> Mid-morning: the *folding pass.* Yesterday's unfired paste is
> brought out and worked. A Glassblown who has remembered
> yesterday's deferrals correctly will feel them in the
> consistency of the paste — denser where folded more times,
> thinner where folded less. The hand reads the body of the work.
> New deferrals from morning correspondence are folded in. None
> of this is fired.
>
> Mid-day: the *firing window* — typically an hour, no more. Only
> shapes that have survived overnight in the paste-stock are
> candidates. A Glassblown who fires more than three shapes in a
> firing window is *hasty*; one who fires none for nine consecutive
> working days is *careful*; one who fires none for thirty is
> *un-paste* — the paste-stock is consuming the Glassblown faster
> than the Glassblown is committing it.
>
> Afternoon: errands. Wet-cloth packets carried to a Concord
> clause-table, a Curation vestibule, a Brine shore. Returned at
> next visit, fired or folded. The errand-walking is the body's
> circulation.
>
> Dusk: the hearth is *banked* — the day's fire put down, the ash
> kept, the kiln-stones left warm. The next day will start cold.
> The bank is the only ritual the Glassblown share with the
> Thermoclave Floor; both bodies, on this single act, do the same
> thing. Neither comments.

The Glassblown's social form is **un-centred work**: a body of
hearths none of which is the body's hearth. The Tuesday is not
performed *at* a place — it is performed in the rhythm by which
paste moves between places.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Glassblown R3 (the kiln-that-is-not)

R3 promotes the Glassblown from craft-tradition to *body whose
anatomy is a circulation*. Mechanics now in the corpus:

- **Folded inheritance** (Dim 1) — paste-stock as historical
  archive; senior re-derivation; purchased paste vs inherited.
- **Witnessed pause** (Dim 2) — paste-equivalent commitment held
  in another faction's memory; the Concord's *carried column*.
- **Names for the irreversible** (Dim 3) — *fired*, *composted*,
  *over-written-clean*, *catalogued*, *standing-closed*,
  *deep-kept*, *banked*/*un-banked*. The Refusal's *un-banked*
  is the theological pair to the Glassblown's *folded-back*.
- **The kiln-that-is-not** (Dim 4) — distributed kiln; the body
  is the paste-stock in motion; folding-out instead of funeral.
- **The walker who is not fired** (Dim 5) — apotheosis-by-
  rollback; preserved only by silence.
- **Hand-grammar** (Dim 6) — somatic grammar; the empty mat at
  Concord clause-tables.
- **A Tuesday at a Glassblown hearth** (Dim 7) — cold-fire
  re-light, the folding pass, firing window, errands, dusk bank.

**Anatomy as circulation.** Six other bodies are graphs of *places*;
the Glassblown is a graph of *moving material*. The R3 closer's
question is now answered: the Glassblown is a body **and** a
craft-tradition, and its distinguishing structural fact is that
*its anatomy is a verb, not a noun*.

**The kiln reads the reading-room.** A Glassblown's hearth founded
in a discovered reading-room is, by both bodies' grammars, doubly
*found* — the reading-room is *kept* (Palimpsest), the hearth is
*hosted* (Glassblown). A walker who reads the chalk and watches
the hand-grammar in the same convening is reading two bodies at
once; neither asserts priority.

their own grammar: *record* is a fired-shape category, *mass* is
a paste-stock category. Complementary, not contradictory. The
bible should explicitly note the distinction when R3 mechanics land.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"A Glassblown is not a person who belongs to the body — they
> are a person who holds a portion of the body's paste. The body
> is what the paste, in aggregate, has not yet been fired into."*

No archaism. No apostrophe-fantasy. No mythic-abstract verbs. A
mereological statement: *body = aggregate(paste, not-yet-fired)*.
TES rewrite would be: *"Verily the Glassmaker is not a son of his
order, but a vessel through which the Order's molten word doth
pass; and the Order itself is the unspoken syllable that hath not
yet found its mouth."* I did not write that. The Glassblown
vocabulary (*paste-stock*, *folded-back*, *cold-fire re-light*,
*firing window*, *hand-grammar*, *witnessed pause*, *carried
column*) names instruments and rhythms; the narrator reports them
without taking on their cadence — same restraint as Brine
*remembered tense*, Concord *clause-aspect*, Refusal *dropped
count-particle*, Palimpsest *layer-grammar*.

Pass.
