### 3.1 Closure 1 — quota-window-reading resolution

The chain elects **Reading A** (window includes the override
sitting). Three rationales:

  1. **Originator precedent.** Reading A is the 1002's reading
     — the original framing of hygiene 8. The 1053's quota-
     state table also used Reading A explicitly (sitting 28 =
     "override-quota refreshes" entry, consistent with 0957's
     window being 24-27). The 1201's Reading B is the
     deviation, not the standard.
  2. **Counter-state cleanliness.** Under Reading A, the
     window count and the override-firing event coincide at
     the anchor sitting; under Reading B, the override "fires
     at" sitting N but the window starts at sitting N+1, which
     creates a parsing gap (does the override count toward
     window N's allowance, or window N+1's?). Reading A keeps

     override at strict 4-sitting spacing. Reading B gives a
     five-sitting effective spacing (the override sitting plus
     the four sittings after). The 1002 §4 explicitly named
     the spacing as *one per four sittings*, not *one per
     five*.

programming compound the chain uses about its own counter-
mechanics — the way a clerk uses *single-pass* about a working-
column. The sentence's claim is technical-procedural, about
keeping a count from breaking into ambiguous sub-states, not
moralised. Lower-case throughout. No allegorical capitalisation.

Counter may not be torn asunder by ambiguous beginnings."*

*Sacred Count*, *Counter*; *of the* fronted-genitive (heraldic
syntax); *torn asunder* (KJV-archaic verb); *ambiguous
beginnings* (mythologisation of the technical term *anchor*).

three working terms, the technical claim doing its own work.
