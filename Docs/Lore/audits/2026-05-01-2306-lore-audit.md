## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible:21:* "It did not merely destroy the old civilization — it
> overwrote reality itself, imperfectly..."

**Strong.** Periods, Long Between, multi-cataclysm, per-faction
calendars, cataclysm-duration, the *first hour* (2220), the *world
before* (2225).

**Thin.** The Concord's claimed *Foretime treaty era* (2225) is
asserted as doctrine but no document has been described.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "The Concord's Foretime treaty"):**

> The Concord teaches that the Foretime ended in a single document —
> not destroyed by the cataclysm but *honoured by it*. The treaty
> was a debt-instrument by which the old world had borrowed
> something against itself; the cataclysm was the call. The
> instrument was settled in full. This is why the new world began
> clean: every account balanced, including the old world's against
> itself.
>
> No copy survives. The Curation classes the doctrine *unfiled,
> doctrinal* — *the Concord believes this so seriously that the
> absence of evidence has itself become evidence*. The Brine call
> it *the tide that did not turn*. The Choir said *we did not taste
> a contract; we tasted a meal.* The Concord regards others'
> disagreement as the *first unredeemed account* — outstanding in
> every other faction's books, payable when they are ready.

The Concord's mythic-historical claim is legally shaped: a debt the
Concord asserts the world still owes them.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Anthology framing, forgery, Reconciled Texts, redactions,
Halen's contradicting footnotes (2225).

**Thin.** **Documents authored by *committee***. All prior unreliable
documents are single-hand. The Concord — whose *we* is committee-
derived (2225) — should produce documents whose unreliability is
*collective*: the truth is in the disagreement *between* signatures,
not within one hand.

**Concrete addition (bible, "Concord ledgers as committee documents"):**

> A Concord standing ledger is signed at the right margin by every
> Reckoner present at close of day. The signatures are not
> affirmations of the entries; they are *witnesses that the entries
> closed in the order shown*. A trained reader reads three things:
> the entries; the order of the signatures (Reckoners sign in
> seniority — a reversed pair flags an internal challenge); and
> the *space between the last entry and the first signature*. A
> wide space is a *late close* — holding the ledger open past
> curfew so a debt could be re-priced. A narrow space is *contested
> closure*. Curation files five Concord ledgers as *closure space
> anomalous*. The shape is the document.

Concord ledgers are unreliable narrators whose unreliability is
*typographic* — parallel to Curation quiet conventions and the
Glassblown unsaid mood.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** Names for the cataclysm, the dead, the player,
remembering, being-first-noticed, the world before.

**Thin.** **The act of *exchange*.** The Concord's central verb.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "What each faction calls trade"):**

> - **Saccharine Concord:** *to balance.* The verb is the doctrine.
>   No separate word for *unbalanced trade* — that is *theft on one
>   side and gift on the other*, different verbs entirely.
> - **Pale Curation:** *to assign.* The verb for the moment one
>   party agrees to file a debt against the other. Curation traders
>   are slow (unforgivably, says the Concord); every trade is a
>   small cataloguing.
> - **Choir:** *to share-mouth.* The same verb used for nutrient
>   redistribution between fruiting bodies. Concord traders refuse
>   to *balance* with Choir tendrils on principle.
> - **Palimpsest:** *to write across.* A trade is two annotations
>   on the same place, made simultaneously. A Concord *balance*, to
>   them, is a failed trade — both wrote, neither *across*.
> - **Brine Communion:** *to deep-keep against deep-keep.* A
>   *deep-kept* may not return; the trade is therefore not
>   closeable. The Brine call Concord *balance* shallow.
> - **Thermoclaves:** *to share fire.* A fire passed keeper to
>   keeper with the fire still lit.
> - **Glassblown:** *(unfired)*. No verb in the unsaid mood; trade
>   requires the fired register, entered only at midday. A
>   Glassblown morning is not a refusal; it is *a preparation*.

*Balance* is the only one of the seven that treats exchange as
fundamentally *neutral*. The rest treat it as holy, asymmetric,
mistaken, or unspeakable until ritual readiness.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Substrate-leak, Choir metabolism, Echo limits, metaphysics-
meet, composted-matter reading, inheritance/bleed-lines, dual-
attunement interference.

**Thin.** **The metaphysics of debt.** The Concord teaches that
*every account can be balanced* (1901). What does the doctrine do
with the genuinely irreversible — a dead person, a digested ruin?

**Concrete addition (bible, "The asymptotic debt"):**

> The Concord is doctrinally committed to the position that every
> debt can be balanced. Its actual practice, across three centuries
> of ledgers, is the **asymptotic debt**: a debt repeatedly half-
> redeemed (the standard Salt-Hinge convention; see 2219) until the
> remainder is too small to denominate. The debt is not closed; it
> is *reduced past denomination*. The Concord reads this as
> *practical balance*; the Curation as *unfiled remainder*; the
> Palimpsest as *layers that thin but never disappear* —
> denomination-erased debts still register on Palimpsest sight as
> faint pressure. The Choir cannot taste them at all.
>
> Three Concord debts have been *fully* redeemed — opened, closed,
> not re-opened. The Concord regards these as *anomalous* and warns
> Reckoners against attempting them. A debt closed without remainder
> leaves *no record of having existed*, and Concord metaphysics
> requires that records persist. The three are filed only by their
> absences. Reckoner Tossan, who closed one (an old Brine debt,
> settled with a half-tide of weather no one expected), spent the
> rest of her career *re-opening* trivial debts at half value, to
> re-establish the texture of remainder she had broken.
>
> The Choir, with no comment, *grew a fruiting body* in the shape
> of a small ledger column at the site of one closed debt. The
> Concord finds it *uncomfortable to look at directly*. The Brine
> consider it *the only honest deep-keep the surface has produced*.
> The Concord has not catalogued it and will not.

The Concord's metaphysics now has a hard limit: *closure is
dangerous; remainder is the doctrine.* It is the first faction
whose central practice contradicts its central doctrine — and
whose contradiction has been institutionalised into the half-
redemption convention rather than resolved.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** Mirror endings (bible:204), Annihilist *named*, Stalled
Walkers, apotheoses-from-outside, failed, declined, pulled.

**Thin.** **The Annihilist ending — described.** No audit describes
what a Concord-aligned walker actually *becomes* at the threshold.
The bible (line 204) lists Union and Transcription but no Concord
ending.

**Concrete addition (bible, "The Concord's apotheosis: Closure"):**

> A walker at maximum reputation with the Saccharine Concord is
> offered, at no announced threshold, the **Closure** — *one debt
> of the walker's choosing, reduced past denomination in a single
> redemption.* The walker names a debt. The Concord names a price.
> The walker pays. The debt is *closed* — fully, not asymptotically.
>
> The cost: the walker, now, **does not appear in any ledger of any
> faction**. No accession card can be written about them. No tendril
> tastes them. No layer thickens beneath them. No fire warms to
> their approach. Their body, memory, and attunements are unchanged.
> They have simply been *settled.* Reckoners treat such walkers as
> *honoured ghosts* — visible, audible, respected, and entirely
> outside the books.
>
> The horror is operational. A Closure walker cannot be *helped* by
> any faction in the way reputation systems work. The Concord cannot
> give them rate-priority; the Curation cannot file them at any
> tier; the Choir cannot send a tendril to their location (the
> network has no taste-of-air for an unledgered body). Quests
> proceed; the world's economy still resolves around them; but the
> walker no longer *registers*.

The third axis: Choir incorporates, Palimpsest writes, Concord
*closes the account*. The first two are positive transformations.
The third is transformation *into the form of an absence*.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Choir pronouns, Palimpsest tense, Brine joining-words,
Thermoclave fire-grammar, Glassblown unsaid mood, Curation catalogued
mood and quiet conventions.

**Thin.** **Concord syntax.** The corpus has Concord *register* but
no grammatical construction unique to them.

**Concrete addition (bible, "Concord grammar: the priced clause"):**

> Concord prose adds a unique structure: the **priced clause**. A
> clause introducing an obligation must carry, in its tail, the
> smallest unit of value the speaker considers it denominated in.
> A Reckoner saying *I will meet you at the Salt-Hinge* appends,
> where another speaker might use a comma, the smallest cabinet-
> credit the meeting is worth: *I will meet you at the Salt-Hinge,
> ¼.* The fraction is the *opening cost* of the meeting. Concord
> children learn this before they learn full sentences.
>
> Outsiders who do not append are not pricing wrong; they are
> pricing *zero*. A walker speaking without priced clauses is
> offering the conversation as *gift* — and the Concord, which has
> no separate word for trade and gift (Dim 3), finds gifts mildly
> disorienting. They will balance against the conversation
> eventually, because not balancing is the only thing the Concord
> is doctrinally afraid of.
>
> Closure (Dim 5) is the only Concord act that *removes the priced
> clause from a sentence forever*: the closed walker speaks
> sentences that, to a Concord ear, *have no tail* — *the speech
> of a fully balanced account*, beautiful, restful, and impossible
> to do business with.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Strong.** Tuesday Morning across all six factions; cross-faction
Tuesdays; Curation Initiation and Quiet Hour.

**Thin.** **Concord training.** A Reckoner-class is implied across
audits but no method has been established.

**Concrete addition (bible, "The Reckoner's chair"):**

> A Concord apprentice trains in **the Reckoner's chair** — not a
> specific chair, but the role of *being seated at the close of a
> trade where the apprentice has no stake*. Placed in a Salt-Hinge
> stall with a slate, the apprentice tallies the *priced clause
> tails* (Dim 6) used by both parties to a real trade. They do not
> speak. At the close they read their tally aloud. Within a quarter
> cabinet-credit of the actual trade-price, the apprentice has
> *heard the trade*. Off by more, they have *not yet heard* and
> return to the chair.
>
> Concord doctrine: a trade's priced-clause tally is the *true*
> price; the agreed price is a *report* of it, sometimes a small
> lie. Senior Reckoners can hear the tally of *promises* — even
> idle conversation carries one; a senior can tell that a long
> apology has cost the apologiser exactly seventeen quarter-credits
> worth of self-image. They do not say so aloud outside the Concord.
>
> Reckoner Tossan (Dim 4) is the only living Reckoner to have
> closed a debt fully. Her tally is in no ledger; the Concord
> asked her *not* to deposit it. The tally was, allegedly,
> *negative* — the closure produced credit for someone, and the
> Concord cannot file credit it cannot identify the holder of.
> Tossan keeps it on a slip folded once, in her outer pocket — the
> same gesture as the First Card (2219).

### Dimension 8 — Marginalia and footnotes

**Strong.** Tags, ledgers, receipts, salvaged books, catechisms,
grief-markers, redactions, contradicting footnotes.

**Thin.** **A document whose *signatures* tell the story** — body
procedurally true; the signature column encodes a quarrel the body

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord (round 2)

This run gives the Concord a doctrine (Foretime treaty), a syntax
(priced clause), a metaphysics (asymptotic debt + three anomalies),
an apotheosis (Closure), a training ritual (Reckoner's chair), and
a founding-equivalent figure (Tossan). Lore-artifact: **Tossan's
slip.**

The Concord's central worry is not loss. It is *unaccountable
surplus* — credit it cannot identify the holder of. Junior clerks
tell a story (apocryphal) that Tossan's slip *cannot* be filed:
filing would create a holder (the Concord), and Concord metaphysics
forbids the institution being holder-of-record on credit it produced
as a side-effect. The slip is *grammatically self-defeating* — any
sentence the Concord writes about it would either lie (assert a
holder) or be incoherent (deny one). Tossan keeps it because *a
person* can hold something the *institution* cannot.

She has, three times, carried it to Salt-Hinge on Tuesday Morning,
set it on the central balance-stone, and *walked away*. No one took
it. She returned, picked it up, refolded it. The Brine widow
involved knows about the slip and finds this funny.

The slip pairs with the First Card: single folded papers in single
pockets, held by single people, that the institution cannot
accommodate. Together they suggest a **pocket-class** — objects
whose institutional home is a person, by structural necessity.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a)** Bible:226 says *"It [the Choir] never lies."* Dim 4 has the
Choir grow a fruiting body at a closed-debt site — neither
statement nor lie. **No contradiction; flag.** Bible addition: *the
Choir's honesty is verbal; its growths are uncommented*. Some
Choir facts are *expressions* the Choir does not consider it has
spoken.

**(b)** Cumulative apotheoses: Union, Transcription (canonical);
Closure (proposed); Annihilist (named only); plus mechanisms.
Closure is *not* the Annihilist ending. Annihilist ends the walker
outside every faction's metaphysics — a *true* end. Closure ends
the walker only outside every faction's *accounting*; the walker
continues to exist. **Keep both.** Annihilist becomes the path for
walkers who attempt Closure incorrectly — paid the price, named
the wrong debt.

**(c)** `RotChoir.json`: the Choir says *"we taste you on the air,
walker"*. Closure makes a walker un-tasteable. **Follow-on content
commit:** for a Closure walker the `ChoirTendril_1` opening should
become a *blank line of the same length* — the Choir mouthing the
greeting without taste behind it.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

A Concord ledger-page where the body is procedurally clean and the
signature column tells a different story.

**Title (in-game):** *Salt-Hinge daily ledger, 4th week, 7th market
day. Reckoner Tossan presiding, witnesses appended.*

```
[Standard Concord ledger page. Entries left, prices middle,
 signatures right. The wide right margin is annotated by hand;
 spacing in the signature column is unusual.]

  ENTRY                                    PRICE      WITNESSES
  --                                       --         --
  Six pelts, common-fox, third-grade       4¼ cab     Tossan
  Twelve cabinet-credits, in glass-paste   12         Pell  ¹
  One half-redemption, against widow Sira
    of Brine (orig. trade Q3, sleeve 11)   neg. ²     Halen [11]
  --
                                                      Vern
                                                      (gap)
                                                      Imo
                                                      Ren
                                                      (long gap)
                                                      Tossan ³

  ¹ Pell signs as rebinder, irregular. Not a Reckoner.
    Concord did not object. Curation filed a sub-card.

  ² *neg.* — priced-clause tally ran negative. Concord
    does not write negative balances. The only entry of
    its kind in the ledger.

  ³ Tossan signs twice — head and foot. The double
    signature is, in Concord grammar, *closure*: trade
    balanced and ledger closed under the same hand. Vern,
    Imo, Ren signed in the gap between Tossan's two —
    witnessing closure *as it happened*. The long gap
    before Tossan's second signature is the wide-space
    anomaly Curation files: Tossan held the ledger open
    while folding the slip into her pocket.
```

Lore landed parenthetically: **the Concord's only known *closed*
debt is documented in the only form it could be — spacing,
witness-order, and a single double-signature framing the entry.**
A walker who can read Concord syntax reads the closure off the
page; a walker who cannot sees only an ordinary trade with a
strange *neg.* in the price column.

The ledger is on permanent display at Salt-Hinge: *the closed debt
is not a secret*, and *closure cannot be read without grammar*.
Both true. Curation Tier-1 holds a copy filed under *closure space
anomalous*.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"The Concord's apotheosis is not consumption or preservation; it
> is settlement. The metaphysical price (un-registration) is the
> equal-and-opposite of the Choir's* we *and the Palimpsest's
> layered self."*

No archaisms, no apostrophe-fantasy names, no Aedra/Daedra/Tower.
Doctrinal weight rides on three plain English nouns (*consumption*,
*preservation*, *settlement*) and one technical coinage
(*un-registration*) the corpus earned through six audits of
accounting metaphors. A TES book on this beat would write *"And so
it is taught by the Salt-Reckoners that the Third Path doth
neither devour nor remember the soul, but balanceth it, and the
balanced soul is unwritten in all the books of the world."* Mine
has no *doth, neither, unwritten in all the books*. Confirmed.
