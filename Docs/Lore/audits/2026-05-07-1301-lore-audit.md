## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (0838 §2.1):** *"The Severance was. We did not
see it. We are here to see what it left."*

**Strong.** Recital canonises a doctrinal ceiling: the
body witnesses leavings, not the event.

**Thin.** The recital implies leavings the body must
read. The body's working-form for **reading from
leavings the body cannot sign** has not been canonised.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Pale Curation
subsection, append after 0838 §2.4):**

> *Material that pre-dates the body's discipline —
> recovered at depot establishment or carried in by
> founding recorders — is filed on the* third shelf*: a
> working-form distinct from both the standing-shelves
> (signed findings) and the working-shelves (provisional
> drafts). The third shelf holds documents and objects
> the Curation has determined to be older than the
> founding of any depot, signed by hands the body cannot
> verify or unsigned outright. By the body's discipline,
> third-shelf material is* unciteable*. By the body's
> working practice, the recital — read each working-day
> in every depot — is** itself drawn from a third-shelf
> document**: a single ironbound page recovered at
> Depot 3 in Sev. year 4, signed by no one, attributed
> by working tradition to a senior who survived the
> Severance and later refused to be named. The body
> acknowledges the contradiction privately and does not
> canonise it. A first-bench recorder asked to cite the
> recital's source will answer, in working register:* "the
> recital cites itself." *This is the body's standing
> form for the citation it cannot perform.*

The discipline's foundation is, by the discipline's own
rules, irregular. The body knows this and sustains it.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (0838 §2.2):** *"A Curation document without a
signing recorder and a depot stamp is, in working
register, a draft."*

**Strong.** Two-state authorship form canonised.

**Thin.** Two states cannot accommodate third-shelf
material — neither draft nor signed finding. Body's
filing practice is three-state; discipline canonises
two.

**Concrete enhancement (bible new sidebar, "The third
shelf"):**

> *Third-shelf citations carry the notation* th.* in any
> document that draws on them — parallel to* prov.* with
> inverted weight: where* prov.* warns that a finding is
> amendable,* th.* warns that the finding is**
> ungroundable** in the body's signing-discipline. A
> senior writes* th.* in the same ink as the finding's
> body; a junior cites the third shelf in pencil,
> marking the citation as itself provisional. The
> discipline permits th. citation but layers a** rank-
> gradient** over its use. The first-bench's th.
> citations are written in the same weight as standing-
> shelf citations. Discipline does not state this is a
> workaround. Working tradition treats the gradient as
> the form of the workaround.*

Falsifies a small reading: any future Curation finding
with a th. notation can be read for ink-weight as a
check on the recorder's tier. Pencil-th. is a junior;
ink-th. is a senior; ink-th. in the same weight as a
standing citation is first-bench.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (0838 §2.4):** *"…the only honest stance is to
not lose any further hands."*

**Strong.** Counter-discipline canon.

**Thin.** §2.1 above introduces the third shelf; the
cosmology has not yet been pressured against it. The
discipline's central verb (*sign*) does two
incompatible jobs: refusing to *unsign* recoverable
hands while reading aloud daily from documents whose
hands were never recoverable.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Pale Curation
subsection, append after 0838 §2.4):**

> *The body distinguishes between* unsigning *— erasing
> or compositing a hand that was once recoverable — and*
> found-unsigned *— material that arrived in the body's
> holding already without a recoverable hand. The first
> is forbidden by discipline; the second is the body's
>** working-condition**. The Curation cannot, by its
> own reading, refuse to read found-unsigned material;
> doing so would consign the Severance's leavings to
> the same fate the discipline prevents. Third-shelf
> material is read, cited (as th.), and** held in
> custody** without claim of authorship. The body
> considers this a humility-form; the Choir considers
> it a category-error of the kind only writing can
> produce. The Palimpsest's reading is the inverse: an
> unsigned document, on Echo Reading, recovers** every
> hand that has ever held it**, in order; third-shelf
> material is, on Echo Reading, the densest readable
> material in any depot. The Curation knows this and
> does not invite Palimpsest readers into the third
> shelf.*

Falsifies a doctrinal seam: counter-discipline is
internally consistent only by admitting a second
category the discipline had not previously named.

**Quote (0838 §2.6):** *"…the cataloguer reports an
observation by routing it through the source they read

**Strong.** Speech-rule canonised for findings.

**Thin.** A recorder cannot route an observation through

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Pale Curation

> *A Curation senior citing a third-shelf source in
> spoken working-register names the source by its** depot

> 11-iv-3 reads…" *— in working translation:* the third

> working-mark for** an authority the body cannot
> verify**. A senior who introduces the form early in a

> listener that the finding to follow is grounded in
> found-unsigned material. The warning is indirect by
> design.*

Falsifies a junior/senior listening rule: a senior
dropping shelf-number citations is doing the work of
a *th.* notation in spoken register.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Pale Curation (the third shelf, intake)

> ### The third shelf (room)
>
> Founding-era depots (1 through 7) keep the third shelf
> in a small alcove off the bench-floor, visible from
> the first-bench position but not from junior benches.
> Later depots (8 onward) keep it in a separate room,
> lit by a single overhead-lamp lit only when consulted,
> with a key held jointly by the first-bench and a
> rotating senior witness drawn from a different bench-
> position each working-month. Doctrinally identical;
> working-tradition treats the difference as a small
> generational disagreement about whether unciteable
> foundations should be in the corner of the eye or
> behind a door.
>
> Third-shelf material is held in iron-banded boxes,
> one per shelf, lid-stamped with the depot's stamp on
> the day the box was sealed but **not** on the day
> the box was opened — a deliberate doctrinal
> asymmetry. A box opened during a depot's working-life
> is re-stamped on closure with the consulting
> recorder's recorder-number and the date, in iron-
> gall ink, on a small paper tab affixed to the inside
> of the lid. The outside of the box does not record
> consultations.
>
> ### Intake procedure
>
> A finding arriving at a depot passes through the
> intake-position (lowest-numbered active bench,
> junior-staffed) for first-reading. The junior records
> the finding in the day's intake-register, applies the
> depot stamp, and assigns it a provisional item-number
> in working-shelf form. The finding sits a working-week,
> open to senior ink-notes on its intake-card. At week's
> end the first-bench adjudicates: standing-shelf
> (citable), working-shelf (prov.), or **third-shelf**.
> A third-shelf adjudication is the only intake decision
> that requires two seniors' agreement; the first-bench
> writes *th.* on the intake-card and a second senior
> writes *concur* in their own ink. Without the concur
> the finding returns to the working-shelf for a
> second working-week.
>
> A junior is not present at third-shelf adjudication.
> The body's working tradition is that recognising the
> finding's signing hand cannot be recovered is, by the
> body's reading, *the senior's first non-junior
> judgment*.

Intake is the door from field to body. Third-shelf
adjudication is the door through which the body admits
some material cannot be held by its own discipline.
Both routine; the first junior-staffed, the second
requiring two seniors and silent ink.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Bleed 1 — bible L171 Annotation vs. third-shelf
doctrine.** The bible's Annotation paragraph describes
a Palimpsest power that adds permanent text to the
palimpsest — including signatures that were never
there. §2.4 above canonises that the third shelf is the
densest readable material in any depot on Echo Reading.
Annotation in a Curation depot is, in the body's
reading, **the most aggressive form of unsigning
available** — it adds a hand to a foundation the body
holds as found-unsigned. Worse: a Palimpsest-aligned
walker reading the depot's third shelf would
**un-anonymise the recital's author**, a working-
tradition the Curation has sustained for four hundred
years. Suggested bible parenthetical at L171:
*"(Annotation in a Curation depot reaches material the
body holds as found-unsigned. The Curation's standing
position is that no Palimpsest-aligned walker is
admitted to a third shelf during the walker's lifetime;
working-tradition does not canonise what to do if one
already has been.)"*

**Bleed 2 — JSON L338 *"offers records"* vs. §2.1 +
§2.4.** Canon-correct in flat reading; beneath it,
what the Curation offers is *the maintenance of
legibility under conditions of impossibility*. Records
are the form, not the service. A future Choir line
might pair *offers records* with *"…and beneath the
records, a refusal we cannot taste"* (cf. Palimpsest
gloss L43). Working-shelf addition; not a JSON edit
yet.

**Bleed 3 — 0838 §3 *"standing-shelves form the
building's structural inner walls"* vs. §2.1.** The
image is sharper than the 0838 sitting realised: the
depot is held up by what it has filed **plus a shelf
the body's discipline cannot file**. Deepening, not
contradiction. The sentence holds. A senior reading
shelf-walls and third-shelf together notices; the
body's working-form is to not remark.

is the second bombshell: the discipline of *signing
everything* has a canon-clean exception, named in
working-tradition rather than discipline (Sevra's gloss
itself filed prov., not codified). The Curation's
quiet rule-evading practice is **refraining from a
stamp** — a non-action the discipline does not formally
permit but the working tradition routinely performs.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted (§2.4):** *"The body distinguishes between
unsigning — the act of erasing or compositing a hand
that was once recoverable — and found-unsigned —
material that arrived in the body's holding already
without a recoverable hand."*

Modern register. *The body distinguishes between X — Y
— and Z — W* is contemporary essayistic parallel
construction. *Unsigning*, *found-unsigned*, *the body's
holding* — institutional-sociological prose with
purpose-built lower-cased compounds. *Already without a
recoverable hand* is a working-procedural clause. No
inversion, no archaism, no capitalised abstractions.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"The Body holdeth a
distinction betwixt** Unsigning** — being the act
whereby a Hand once knowable is erased or composted —
and** Found-Unsigned**, that material which cometh
into the Body's keeping already bereft of any Hand
recoverable."* Tells: capitalised *Body / Hand /
Unsigning / Found-Unsigned*; *holdeth*, *cometh*,
*betwixt*, *bereft*, *whereby*. Actual sentence is
none of these.

**Pass.**
