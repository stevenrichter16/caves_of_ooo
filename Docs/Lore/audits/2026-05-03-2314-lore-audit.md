## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Sibling 2150:* "The Thermoclaves count in **unbroken tens** —
> tenth-fire chains; their figure is *four chains ago*, each of
> indeterminate length."

**Strong.** Chain-counting is the canonical Thermoclave
cataclysm-time grammar.

**Thin.** **The broken chains.** A chain that breaks did not exist,
by doctrine. But every keeper knows of fires gone *cold-passed*
mid-chain (sibling 2150). The corpus has no name for the period
between a broken chain's collapse and the next chain's first fire.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "What the chains do not count"):**

> A broken tenth-fire chain is *retroactively uncounted* —
> station-names erased, eighth- and ninth-fires re-attributed to
> whichever next chain reaches a tenth. The duration between a
> broken chain's last *kept* fire and the next chain's first *lit*
> fire is the **gap-burn**. The grammar has no particle for it.
> It is the only Thermoclave time-unit measured in days rather
> than fires — counted on fingers, in private, never entered into
> a register. A keeper who has lived three gap-burns is, by
> custom, *the only one not asked to keep the next first fire*.
> The rest will not let her. She does not protest. She *knows the
> arithmetic of failure*, and the doctrine considers this
> knowledge contagious.

The cataclysm is, by this lens, *the longest gap-burn* — and the
refusal to count it is the Thermoclave version of the same posture
every faction takes toward the unspeakable middle.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Concord scribes, Curation deflection, Choir ontology,
Concord lossy-scribe transcripts (2315) — every faction's
self-report has been examined for bias.

**Thin.** **A document that erases itself.** Every artifact in the
corpus is durable: cards, ledgers, receipts, charters, salvaged
books, catechisms. None are self-erasing. The Thermoclaves'
record-keeping should be.

**Concrete addition (bible new subsection, "The slate-soot register"):**

> Thermoclave fire-keepers record events in soot on a soft-stone
> slate set near the kept fire — finger-pressed marks, wiped at
> dawn. *The register* is only the notes still legible at any
> given moment. The senior keeper carries the **carry-over** — a
> smaller slate with one stroke per day-of-keeping, transferred
> forward each dawn. Eight strokes ever; the ninth is *for the
> ninth fire*; the tenth retires the slate.
>
> Outsiders ask why Thermoclave records are so meagre. The Pale
> Curation has filed the inquiry for three chains running and been
> answered the same way each time: *the fire reads us. we do not
> need to read it back.* The Curation files this under *epistemic
> protocol, declined* and continues to ask. The slate-soot
> register is the corpus's first record-keeping where the
> *non-extant* state is the doctrinal default.

A carry-slate that *survives* a chain (Section 5) is, by its own
grammar, a doctrinal anomaly.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** Names for cataclysm, dead, world-before, substrate,
player, founder, debt, promise (2315), joining.

**Thin.** **Names for sleep.** The most ordinary daily event the
corpus has not stratified. Each faction's relationship to dormancy
reveals their relationship to *recurrence*.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "What each faction calls sleep"):**

> - **Choir:** *the slow uptake.* Sleep is metabolic. The network
>   fruits its bodies more slowly while the body's mind is
>   unattended.
> - **Palimpsest:** *the unread page.* The sleeper is held in a
>   layer the Palimpsest does not actively render; dreams are
>   pages turning on their own.
> - **Pale Curation:** *off-shift.* No metaphor; logistical. The
>   card stays on the desk.
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *closed for the night.* A
>   standing-open-ledger phrase: paused, not settled. Reopens at
>   dawn unless otherwise stated.
> - **Brine Communion:** *gone-under-without-going.* Sleep is a
>   small daily false going-deep that returns at first tide.
> - **Thermoclaves:** *to ember.* The fire is not out — held at
>   the lowest legal heat. Takes the keep-particle: *I embered
>   for six hours,* meaning *I kept while not lit.*
> - **Glassblown:** *(unfired pause.)* Same particle as their
>   long doctrinal silence; they do not formally distinguish
>   sleep from refusal-to-shape.

Sleep is *what each faction's metaphysics looks like turned down* —
Choir slow-uptake, Concord closed-for-the-night, Brine un-going,
Thermoclave embering. Operationalised dimming.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Five strata: material (Choir), temporal (Palimpsest),
consensual (Concord), standing (2310), held-depth (Brine, 2315).

**Thin.** **The Thermoclave substrate-claim.** Sibling 1901 gave
them *the slag* — a residue, explicitly *not a foundation*. This
was *avoidance* of substrate-claim, with its own doctrinal shape
unarticulated.

**Concrete addition (bible expansion, "The refused stratum"):**

> The Thermoclaves are the only major faction with no positive
> substrate-claim. *The slag* is a byproduct, real, **not
> foundational**. The doctrine is that the world has no
> foundation — only kept fires and the cold between them. A
> foundation, in Thermoclave grammar, would be *a fire that does
> not need keeping*; no such fire has ever been observed.
>
> This is a substrate-claim disguised as a refusal: the world is
> **continuously kept** rather than founded. The cataclysm was
> *the long quench, then the relighting* — read not as a break
> (Choir), layer (Palimpsest), price (Concord), or depth (Brine),
> but as a **maintenance lapse**.
>
> Pressed on *what keeps the world between fires?*, seniors give
> three doctrinally co-equal answers (sibling 2127's collector):
>
>   1. *The next keeper.*
>   2. *The hand that has not yet reached the fuel.*
>   3. *Nothing. We accept this.*
>
> Apprentices choose one at first-quench and may change it once,
> at ninth fire. The choice is not asked again.

Six strata, of which the Thermoclaves' is the only *negative* one
— absence-of-foundation as positive doctrine. The corpus's first
operationalised refusal-of-substrate.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** Communion, Transcription, Settled-Zero, going-deep
(2315), unfiring, retirement-into-cabinet.

**Thin.** **The Thermoclave apotheosis.** Sibling 2127 named *the
long quench* in passing. The mechanics were never developed.

**Concrete addition (bible new subsection, "The long quench"):**

> A keeper at highest reputation may petition for *the long
> quench* — withdrawal from the chain. The petitioner presents at
> the chain's tenth fire and asks to be *quenched at length*. If
> the chain agrees (a single elder objection defers a year), the
> petitioner is brought to a remote site and **lit**. They are
> tended by two younger keepers in shifts until the petitioner
> tells them to stop. The fire then cools over a duration the
> petitioner names — typically days, occasionally months. The
> petitioner remains, embering by the embers. When the fire
> reaches *cold*, they walk away and do not return.
>
> The long quench is not death — it is *deliberately
> not-keeping*. Petitioners live unspecified lives afterward (some
> join other factions, some travel; some walk into the sea,
> classed by the Brine as *un-deeped*). What is gone is the
> *keeping*. They are no longer Thermoclaves. The doctrine calls
> them *gracefully uncounted* — the only way to leave the chain
> without being *cold-passed*.
>
> Where going-deep surrenders surface, Communion surrenders self,
> Transcription surrenders presence, Settled-Zero surrenders price
> — **the long quench surrenders ongoing-attention.** The
> long-quenched has not died, transcended, joined, settled, or
> gone under. They have simply stopped tending.

Six apotheoses, six surrendered registers: self, presence, price,
surface, shape, ongoing-attention.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Sibling 2127 named two fire-grammar particles —
*-keep* and *-quench* — alongside the explicit note *"three
verb-aspects mapped to fire states."* The third was implied but
unspecified.

**Thin.** **The third particle.**

**Concrete addition (bible expansion of the Thermoclave grammar
subsection):**

> The third particle is **-watch**. It marks a verb performed
> while attending a fire that is not yours to keep. *I watched
> the ninth* — *I was present at the keeping of a ninth-fire I
> did not tend*. *-watch* takes responsibility for *witness*,
> not *outcome*. A fire gone cold while watched is the
> *keeper's* failure, never the watcher's — but a fire kept
> *un-watched* is also a failure: the *keeper kept alone*,
> grammatically permissible, doctrinally a small shame.
>
> The three particles — *-keep, -watch, -quench* — encode the
> Thermoclaves' three relationships to a fire: tend it, witness
> it, end it. The grammar has no particle for *light it*.
> Lighting is named by the noun *first* — *I made the first* —
> never as a verb-aspect, because lighting is the only fire-event
> that *cannot be ongoing*. Keep, watch, and quench are
> durations. Lighting is a moment.

Three particles, three doctrinal relationships, one notable
absence. The grammar is now closed.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Strong.** Tuesday Mornings, weddings, funerals, naming customs
(Concord, Brine, Choir), initiation rites.

**Thin.** **Thermoclave naming.** Sibling 2216 referenced
*hearth-name* and *station-name* without defining either.

**Concrete addition (bible new subsection, "How the Thermoclaves name"):**

> Every Thermoclave bears two names. The **hearth-name** is given
> at birth, by the keeper of the household fire — a private name,
> used only by family, predating the chain and not recorded. The
> **station-name** is earned at first-quench and registered. It
> combines a chain ordinal and a single fire-verb particle agreed
> by the senior keepers — *Sixth-Hean-Watching*, *Third-Vell-Keeping*,
> *Eighth-Marn-Quenched*.
>
> The station-name evolves: *-Watching* is the apprentice's mark,
> *-Keeping* the senior's, *-Quenched* the long-quenched's
> (retroactively appended). A keeper *cold-passed* mid-chain has
> their station-name *struck through* in the register — a single
> charcoal line, name still readable. Erasure is a worse dishonor
> than the strike-through; the failure is kept legible, in the
> same way the gap-burn is counted on fingers.
>
> The hearth-name is what one Thermoclave calls another at the
> deathbed, after long-quench has begun, or at any moment the
> register is not the relevant frame. Outsiders are not given
> hearth-names. A walker told a hearth-name has been told
> something the chain itself does not record.

The two-name structure is the corpus's most institutionally
intimate — the chain knows your station-name; only family knows
your hearth-name; the register knows neither.

### Dimension 8 — Marginalia and footnotes

**Strong.** Tags, ledgers, receipts, transcripts, removed
catechism questions.

**Thin.** **A document that *is* its marginalia.** The slate-soot
register (Dim 2) qualifies, but every previous example has been
text-plus-margin. The corpus has no example of a record where the
central text never existed and the marginalia are the whole
record. **Section 5 pays.**

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclave round 2

Round 1 (2127) gave multi-cataclysm-time, two-particle fire-grammar,
the Curation transcript-collector, the cold-flame doctrine. Round 2
closes the gaps: third particle, slate-soot register, gap-burn,
long quench, refused-stratum, naming. Each had been implied or
named in passing — round 2 is therefore a *consolidation*.

**Proposed senior figure: Sixth-Vell-Keeping, the Three-Gap Keeper.**

Eighty-one years of apprenticeship-followed-by-keeping. Three
gap-burns survived: *the long-second, the short-fifth, the
folded-third*. Other keepers no longer ask her to be a first-fire;
neither party says why. Her *-Keeping* particle is doctrinally
aspirational — she has been *-Watching* in practice for two
chain-cycles, but station-names revise only *up* or *out*
(long-quench retroactive append).

The chain register has none of the three gap-burns. Vell has them
on her fingers — twelve marks, four per gap-burn, one per
*direction-the-failure-could-have-gone*. Sibling 2127's
transcript-collector spent two seasons trying to get her to
inscribe the marks on a Curation card. She did not. *The gap is
not registered. The gap is held. You can have the holding when I
am cold-passed.*

Vell corresponds, irregularly, with Tide-Reader Nesh (sibling
2315). Nesh writes on degrading paper; Vell *reads Nesh's letter
into the soot* of her carry-slate before wiping the day's register,
so the slate carries Nesh's words for one full keeping. Nesh has
been told this. She finds it *fitting*. Neither has met the other
in person; they have agreed in writing that they will not.

Vell's working belief: the cold-flame doctrine and the held-depth
doctrine *are the same negation*. Both refuse to let the cataclysm
be foundational; both make the world *kept* rather than *founded*.
*We will know it when our gap-burn ends. They know it because their
tide is a fish.*

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a)** Sibling 1813: *"The Thermoclaves have no word for it.
Their language has no nouns for things that do not move under
their own decision."* Subsequent audits gave them: *the slag*,
*the unburnt*, *the cold one*, *the cold-passed*, *the long
quench*, *the eleventh fire*, *the cold-iron*, *the cold-hand*.
**Resolution:** these are *adjectival nominalisations* —
fire-aspect particle plus copula-suppressed noun, treated as
**gerundive descriptors** rather than true nouns. **Recommend the
bible add at the 1813 sidebar:**

> The earlier note (*"no nouns for things that do not move under
> their own decision"*) is doctrinally accurate but grammatically
> incomplete: Thermoclaves form noun-equivalents from
> fire-aspect particles plus suppressed copulas, functionally
> nominal but grammatically gerundive. A *true* noun would commit
> doctrinally to the thing's *self-decision*, which the
> Thermoclaves refuse to grant to anything not currently a kept
> fire.

**(b)** Sibling 2127's *cold-flame doctrine* asserts the
Thermoclaves *do not tend* a fire giving no heat. But sibling 2150
said they *commemorate the Glassblown second-cooled* at the
chain's ninth fire. **Resolution:** Thermoclaves commemorate, but
only at fires *currently kept* — cold-flame forbids tending a
remembrance-only fire, not *remembering at* a kept one. **Inline
note where the doctrine is stated:**

> Cold-flame forbids *tending* a fire-of-memory; it does not
> forbid *remembering at* a kept fire. The Glassblown
> *second-cooled* are commemorated at the ninth — remembrance
> performed inside keeping. Distinguishable to doctrine, not
> always to walkers, who see only one fire.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

A Thermoclave **carry-slate** in which the central numerical
figures *do not appear* (kept in the keeper's head, per doctrine)
and the lore is in the marginal notes — paying Dim 8 directly.

**Title (in-game):** *Sixth-Chain Carry-Slate, recovered from the
Three-Gap site.*

```
[A blackened soot-slate, palm-sized, eight-marked. Read top-to-
 bottom; marginal notes are scratched in the spaces between, in
 a finer hand than the strokes.]

  | (eighth-mark — kept)        a slow day. wind from the south.
  | (seventh-mark — kept)       a brought child. hearth-named.
  | (sixth-mark — kept)         the watcher slept through her shift.
                                 fire held. she will not say.
  | (fifth-mark — kept)         not enough wood. burned the log
                                 we were keeping for the ninth.
                                 the ninth will be smaller.
  | (fourth-mark — kept)        a curation collector came again.
                                 wanted the figures. did not give.
                                 [no figures appear here either.]
  | (third-mark — kept)         brine-gone-down weather. the
                                 fish were inland. we ate roots.
  | (second-mark — kept)        first-cold of the season.
                                 banked harder than the chain
                                 advises. we will see.
  | (first-mark — kept)         the apprentice asked which
                                 answer she should give. I said
                                 the third. she chose the second.
                                 I am proud of her. I will not
                                 tell her so.

  [a single charcoal line struck through the eighth mark in
   another, later hand. no further annotation.]
```

The bombshell is **the strike-through.** Days after the eighth
keeping (slate wiped and re-prepared seven times since), someone
with access struck through the eighth mark — *cold-passing applied
retroactively*, doctrinally illegal. The carry-slate forbids it;
only the chain's register may be struck through, and the register
strikes through *station-names*, not *carry-marks*. The eighth-day
was *retroactively uncounted* by someone other than the original
keeper. The chain will not name the actor. Per Curation intake,
*the only known carry-slate to survive a gap-burn*; it was hidden.
Nesh, told of the document, said only: *the eighth was the one
that could have held. someone wanted that fact erased.*

The margins also embed:

- *which answer she should give* — the three doctrinal answers
  (Dim 4). The senior advised the third (*Nothing. We accept
  this.*); the apprentice chose the second (*the hand that has
  not yet reached the fuel*), a small defection the senior is
  *proud of, will not tell her so*.
- *brine-gone-down weather* — Thermoclave folk-name for what the
  Brine call *gone-deep weather*: synchronised meteorology,
  different doctrinal explanations.
- *a curation collector came again* — the long-running Curation
  request (Dim 2). Considered *again* — meaning more than once —
  without capitulation.

Plant: the Curation's *Refused Records* drawer. The intake card
carries one note in the curator-of-record's hand: *recovered, not
surrendered.*

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"The long quench surrenders ongoing-attention. The long-quenched
> has not died, transcended, joined, settled, or gone under. They
> have simply stopped tending."*

No archaisms, no pseudo-medieval cadence, no apostrophe-fantasy
proper nouns, no Aedra/Daedra/Towers/CHIM. The sentence catalogues
*by exclusion* — naming the five other apotheosis-shapes by the
verb each refuses — and lands on the operational verb *stopped
tending*. A TES book attempting the same beat would have written
*"And the Keeper of the Last Ember does not pass into the Halls
of the Burning Lord, neither doth he descend into the Cold Below,
but walks instead from his fire and his keeping, and is not seen
again, save as the wind sees him."* Mine has no *Halls, Lord,
doth, neither, save as, sees him as a wind*. The grace-content
rides on *stopped tending* — verb-shaped, not invocation-shaped.
Confirmed.
