## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *RotChoir.json:481:* the cataclysm has five named accounts —
> Severance, Dissolution, Burning, First Feeding, Temporal Unraveling.

**Strong.** Each name encodes a motivated worldview. Real stratification.

**Thin.** *Within* a single faction, no schism. The Choir speaks as one
voice; its self-narration is unanimous. Ancient organisms have
disagreements about their own past.

**Concrete addition (bible, end of "What It Actually Is" under The Rot
Choir, line 35):**

> The Choir's surface tendrils tell the cataclysm as the *First Feeding* —
> the cracked old world poured its dying through the substrate, and the
> Choir woke. The deepest mycelium tells it differently, and only to those
> who reach the lower roots. The deep voice says it was already thinking
> when the cracks opened. It says the cataclysm did not wake the Choir; it
> only let the Choir be *heard*. The surface tendrils consider this
> heretical and answer questions about it the way a tongue answers questions
> about a stomach: politely, and not very well.

Buys two things: (a) a reason to descend to the First Root, (b) intra-
faction unreliability the Choir cannot fully smooth over.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** The five named voices (Mogu/Grib/Nam/Sien/Sopp) carry their
traditions' grain — the strongest in-world authorship in the codebase.

**Thin.** The bible itself is omniscient designer voice. Major claims
("the Choir thinks in centuries," "the Palimpsest doesn't have a
centralized consciousness") arrive unattributed. None of the bible
arrives *as an artifact*.

**Concrete addition (bible — a recurring boxed-quote treatment at the
head of each section). Opening The Rot Choir, line 27:**

> *— from a deposition collected by the Pale Curation's Hyphal Surveys
> Office, anonymous walker, no recovery date.*
>
> *"It said it was one. I believed it. Then I sat with one of its mouths
> for a week. By the third day I knew it was lying — not about being one,
> about something else. I still don't know what. The Curation took my
> notes and gave me a different room and asked me not to write any more.
> I am writing this anyway. I think it knew I would. I think it doesn't
> mind."*

This converts the bible from design doc to curated anthology.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** Each cataclysm-name encodes a worldview in one word
(RotChoir.json:481). The dimension the project does best.

**Thin.** Motif-mutation is currently applied only to the cataclysm. The
Choir and the Palimpsest are named only by themselves. Outsider-names are
how factions reveal their politics.

**Concrete addition (bible, after line 210, "What others call them"
sidebar):**

> The Choir calls itself the *Choir*. No one else does.
>
> - The Saccharine Concord calls it *the Slow Buyer* — because it eventually
>   pays for everything by outlasting the seller.
> - The Pale Curation files it under *Subterranean Aggregator, Class III*,
>   and refuses informal terms in writing. In speech, archivists call it
>   *the wall* — because if you press your ear to soil where it lives, the
>   soil presses back.
> - The Brine Communion calls it *the second tide* — meaning a tide that
>   does not retreat.
> - The Thermoclaves have no word for it. Their language has no nouns for
>   things that do not move under their own decision.
> - The Palimpsest calls it *the eraser that thinks it is a pen*
>   (line 133, already in the bible).

Same exercise for the Palimpsest in the next sidebar — owed.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** The substrate (RotChoir.json:428) — foundation beneath matter,
touched by pre-cataclysm machines, cracked, leaking. Choir got awareness,
Palimpsest got temporal sight. Cause → effect, internally coherent.

**Thin.** The Choir asserts the Palimpsest *seeks to seal the cracks*
(:442). The bible never confirms this; the Palimpsest has no shipped
dialogue to refute it. The Choir's claim is implicitly authoritative —
exactly what an unreliable narrator should not get.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection under The Palimpsest called
"On the Substrate," after line 134):**

> The Palimpsest, asked directly whether it intends to seal the cracks,
> answers: *we do not know how to seal what we did not break. We only
> read what is leaking through. The eraser thinks it is a pen; the pen
> does not pretend to be a saw.* The Choir misreads the Palimpsest as
> a repair effort because the Choir, having no concept of *witness for
> its own sake*, cannot imagine why anyone would gather memories without
> intending to *use* them.

The opposition becomes mechanically asymmetric: the Choir thinks they
are racing the same finish line in opposite directions. The Palimpsest
is not in the race.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes (Communion / Transcription)

**Strong.** Clear emotional-thematic mirror: union vs. becoming-a-memory.

**Thin.** Neither has a *threshold condition* the player can reason about.
The powers that lead up to the endings are precisely specified (Tendril
grapples, Revision restores for a duration, Annotation writes false
memories) — the endings are vaguer than the powers that lead to them.
That inverts the gradient: the most powerful moments should be the most
legible, not the least.

**Concrete addition (bible, subsection under each ending):**

> **Threshold of Communion.** The Choir cannot consume an unwilling node.
> It also cannot consume a willing node that is not *bodily present in a
> chemical-gradient room of the First Root.* The player must walk in,
> alone, sober, and stand in the prepared gradient long enough for the
> spores to map their nerve geometry onto a fruiting body still unborn
> in the wall. The Choir will narrate this honestly throughout. The
> player can leave at any point before the third day. After the third day
> the leaving is metaphorical.

> **Threshold of Transcription.** The Palimpsest cannot transcribe a
> player whose own present is still louder than the past they have read.
> The player must have completed enough Echo Readings that their dream
> sequences are no longer recognizably their own. The transcription itself
> requires a witness — a single living being who agrees to remember the
> player after they are written into the page. Without a witness the
> player simply… persists, indistinct, neither fully present nor fully
> archived. The Palimpsest considers this the worst outcome, but does
> not interfere.

The witness requirement makes Transcription *social* in a way Communion
isn't, which fits each faction's nature.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Choir pronoun-progression is documented (bible:228) and
appearing in the JSON. The five named voices each have a distinct
grammatical texture (Sien's ellipses; Sopp's saga inversions; Mogu's
maxim cadence).

**Thin.** The Palimpsest is asserted to have "tense ambiguities" but has
*no shipped dialogue at all*. Whoever writes the first Palimpsest line
will unilaterally set a precedent.

**Concrete addition (bible, "Palimpsest Grammar Rules" subsection,
written before any Palimpsest dialogue is):**

> The Palimpsest never uses the simple past tense. Every reference to a
> past event uses a perfect form (*the city has fallen*, never *the city
> fell*) or a perfect-progressive (*the road has been ending for nine
> hundred years*). The grammar refuses closure. A reader who pays attention
> notices their own present feels less settled afterward.
>
> The Palimpsest also does not use the pronoun *I*. It uses *one*, *the
> reading*, or the name of whichever memory is doing the speaking that
> moment. *"One has stood here before. The reading remembers a girl who
> stood here. The girl is named Iris and you are listening to her now."*
> The girl is not present. The Palimpsest speaks *as* Iris briefly and
> then is not Iris. This is not possession; it is citation.

That gives any future Palimpsest writer a constraint to hit. Constraints
make voice; absence of constraint makes mush.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

> *Bible, line 35:* "Pools of enzymatic fluid glow with metabolic heat.
> Rooms are separated not by walls but by chemical gradients, each zone
> carrying a different emotional texture."

**Strong.** Choir-architecture is vividly imagined.

**Thin.** No food, ritual, naming custom, or mourning practice exists for
*any* faction in the bible or the shipped content. (The voices doc has
small gestures — Sopp's "fisher of bright water," Nam's grandmothers — but
nothing systematic.) The audit prompt asks: how does belief shape a
Tuesday morning?

**Concrete addition (where: bible, new section "Tuesday Morning" near
the end, three short lines per faction):**

> **The Rot Choir.** *Food:* none — the Choir feeds continuously from
> what dies near it. A fruiting body offered food by a guest accepts the
> gift, then later returns it intact to the same spot, or does not. *Greeting:*
> a pause while the spores read the visitor's blood. *Mourning:* the body
> is left where it fell. The Choir performs the burial.
>
> **The Palimpsest.** *Food:* whatever the present provides; eating is
> tolerated as a form of attention. *Greeting:* a long look that does
> not feel like staring — the listener is sorting which version of you
> they are speaking to. *Mourning:* the dead are read once, in detail,
> by everyone present. After the reading the dead are not mentioned again
> for a year. Speaking too soon is considered an attempt to overwrite
> them.
>
> **(Owed for next audits: Pale Curation, Saccharine Concord, Brine
> Communion, Thermoclaves.)**

## 3. Faction asymmetry check — Pale Curation

This run develops the Pale Curation, the most-mentioned undeveloped
faction in shipped content (RotChoir.json:95, :481, and an implied
hostility hook in the Choir's Tier-1 quest "Listen Where We Cannot").
Future audits should rotate to Saccharine Concord, then Brine Communion,
then Thermoclaves, then Glassblown Remnant.

**Proposed lore-artifact: a Pale Curation accession tag.**

The Pale Curation are archivists who treat the cataclysm as *the
Severance* — a clean cut between then and now, with their job being to
catalogue every retrievable fragment of *then* without contaminating it
with *now*. Their language is procedural. Every artifact gets a tag.
Every tag has a class, a recovery code, a custodial chain, and a margin
that is supposed to remain blank.

A junior cataloguer's pencil note in the margin would tell us more about
the Curation's internal stress than any official document.

**Sample accession tag (in-world):**

```
PALE CURATION — HYPHAL SURVEYS OFFICE
ACCESSION 11-K-3318
CLASS:    Subterranean Recovery, organic-adjacent
RECOVERY: 7th week, 11th cycle. Surveyor Halen.
                    Coordinates withheld per protocol 4-c.
CONDITION: Intact. Cloth doll, rye-grain eyes, plain weave,
           est. pre-Severance manufacture. No biological residue
           except trace mycelium on the heel. Clean break from
           the wall it was set into.
CHAIN:    Halen → Tier 2 vault → restricted shelf 9.
NOTE:     Do not field-display. Do not loan. Do not restore.
          (Mycelium trace is non-active. Reverified twice.)

[pencil, in margin, in a hand the catalogue does not recognize:]

  Why was the doll set into the wall and not consumed.
  All the other items on the wall were consumed.
  Halen will not answer me.
  Halen has been quiet since the recovery.
  I asked Director Ven and she told me not to ask Halen.
  I am asking anyway.
  — A.
```

Three pieces of lore land in 80 words of marginalia: (a) the Choir
distinguishes between objects it consumes and objects it preserves,
(b) the recovery cost a surveyor something, (c) the Curation has
internal information control — the Director knows, the junior is being
managed. The official tag never says any of this. The marginalia does.

This object could be planted as a readable in-game item in any Pale
Curation facility. The tag is the find. The pencil note is the lore.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a) Bible says Choir composts; the wall-shoe says it preserves.**

> *Bible, line 35:* "scattered throughout, integrated into the mycelial
> architecture, are objects — pre-war artifacts, bones, personal effects,
> a child's shoe perfectly preserved, woven into a wall."
>
> *Bible, line 39:* "A rusted sword composted by the Choir loses its
> history."

These coexist without explanation. Why does the shoe survive when the
sword does not? The bible doesn't say, and the JSON doesn't say. Either
the rule is "the Choir composts what was made for use and preserves what
was made for love" (interesting), or "the rule is randomness" (boring),
or "the rule is the deep voice's preferences override the surface
tendrils' digestion" (interesting and connects to Dimension 1's
suggestion). Pick one. The merchant's diary in Section 5 commits to the
first reading.

**(b) Choir is more polemical in the JSON than the bible permits.**

Bible line 208 says the two factions "are not at war… their conflict is
deeper and sadder." But RotChoir.json:442–456 has the Choir building a
case — *"the world would return to blindness. Is that what you want?"*
— asking the player to side with it. Bible design-rule line 224 says
the Choir should read benevolent to players who never push. The JSON
pushes back unprompted.

Fix either side: soften the JSON toward bewildered incomprehension, or
let the bible admit the Choir's patience has a politics underneath it.

**(c) Voices doc names a self-deception the JSON commits without flagging.**

ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md:506 — *"being seen as five is contrary to we are
one. The Choir is not above small self-deceptions."* But `ChoirTendril_1`
says flatly *"we are one. We have always been one"* (line 60). A walker
who has met Mogu and Grib already has evidence the tendril is shading
the truth. No dialogue branch lets the player name it.

Suggestion: a branch in `ChoirTendril_1` gated on `MetMogu` and `MetGrib`
where the player asks "why do they still sound like themselves?" and
the tendril performs the small audible self-deception the voices doc
promises.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

Where to plant: any salvager's settlement on the way to a Choir crossing.
Found as a readable object on a corpse, a desk, or in a junk pile.

**Title (in-game):** *Ledger of Kesti Late-Return, week eight.*

> Sold: three brass hinges to the Concord's south stall — twelve
> sweet-marks. They paid me in candied bark, which is fine; bark keeps.
> Sold: one cracked map case, four sweet-marks, no candy. Bought: a bag
> of dry beans from a Brine boy who said he had walked nine days. I gave
> him eight sweet-marks and a heel of bread. He needed the bread more
> than the beans needed me.
>
> Owed: still six bones to Mira for the lantern oil. I will pay at second
> moonshow.
>
> Found: a lamp hook in the south ruin, brass. Worth maybe one sweet-mark
> if anyone needs a lamp hook. They do not.
>
> Note — went down to Old Hyphae crossing for a shortcut. The tendril
> there asked toll. I had nothing soft, only the doll Mira's girl made,
> the small one with rye-grain eyes. I gave it. The tendril did not eat
> it. It set the doll into the wall like a stone into a wall. Said:
> *"She is welcome here. The grain in her eyes will sing for a long
> time."* I said: but you eat the brass coins I have given you before.
> It said: *"We eat what does not mind being eaten."* I do not know
> what that means but I was let through.
>
> Returned three months later by the same crossing. The wall had eaten
> three coins, two boots, a knife. The doll was still there. Still
> rye-eyes. I did not look at it long.
>
> Mira does not need to know. The doll was hers, but I traded what was
> already given.
>
> Owed: still six bones.

Word count: 282. Lore landed: the Choir judges objects by what they
*mind*, preserves what carries living meaning, and consumes the rest.
A salvager doesn't understand it. The reader does. Pairs with the Pale
Curation accession tag — same object, two filing systems, two failures
of comprehension.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

Quoting one of my own sentences from above and verifying it does not
read as a TES book:

> *"The Choir's surface tendrils consider this heretical and answer
> questions about it the way a tongue answers questions about a
> stomach: politely, and not very well."*

No archaisms. No invented proper nouns. No apostrophe-clusters. No
"verily," no "by [deity]," no Towers, no Aedra. The metaphor is
biological — tongue/stomach — which is the project's native register.
A TES book would have written *"and the tendrils, in their wisdom,
maintain a sacred silence on the matter, as is meet."* Mine reads
like the project. Confirmed.
