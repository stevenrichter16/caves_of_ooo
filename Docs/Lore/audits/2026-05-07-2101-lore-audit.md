## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (1601 §2.1):** *"The body's working-organ
for the third-witness function is the seam — a
senior's somatic reading of the room at the
moment of closure."*

**Strong.** Three doctrinal strata canon: pre-
Dissolution standing-promise; post-Dissolution
triadic; the seam as somatic working-organ
generationally rebuilt.

**Thin.** The seam reads *the room*. The room
itself has not been historicised. Across the
cycle, every body has the "what the discipline
cannot read" position covered by an inert third
party (parent-stone, held mark, third shelf,
bench-wear). The Concord's *public hall* has
not been examined on the same axis.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Saccharine
Concord, append after the seam-rebuilt
paragraph):**

> *The public hall is not a working-form. It is
> a working-place. The seam reads it; the body
> does not file it. Senior endorsements three
> generations apart describe the hall in working-
> register without doctrinal vocabulary: the hall
> is* long, *the hall is* held, *the hall is*
> drifting today. *A senior asked what the hall
> is, in a doctrinal frame, answers in working-
> form:* "the hall is what i read through. it is
> not what i read." *The hall has been the same
> hall, in the same stones, for as long as the
> body has had a founding clause; senior endorse-
> ments have changed seventy times. The hall has
> not been recategorised. The seam is generationally
> rebuilt; the hall is not.*

A fourth stratum, by working-place: discipline
transmits; seam rebuilds; **the hall persists**.
The Concord's parallel to the Thermoclave parent-
stone and the Communion's sea-time.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Thin.** The drawer-bundles' authorship-mode is

The standing-endorsement-three-prior failed to

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Saccharine

> clean is not a counter-form but a* form-

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord (bench-wear-line, drawer-class, hall-length)

> ### The bench-wear-line
>
> The seam-bench's senior side carries a thin
> wear-line — hand-shaped, six fingers wide,
> running the bench's length at the height
> where a standing senior's palms rest during
> the read. The line has deepened across the
> body's history at the rate of one knuckle's
> depth per endorsement-generation; it now sits
> a working-finger below the surrounding wood.
> No senior has sanded it; no senior has authored
> it; no senior has named it. A junior's working-
> notebook from the second endorsement-generation
> describes it as *the bench remembering*; the
> entry was filed under *N-poetic-non-form*. The
> wear-line has not been pressed-clean. Pressing-
> clean is for paper; the bench is wood. The body
> has no working-form for pressing-clean of wood.
>
> ### The drawer-class
>
> A class of containment the working-form does
> not name. The standing-endorsement's drawer is
> the working-canon instance; junior desks have
> drawers without working-status; the seam-
> bench's underside has a single shallow drawer
> tradition holds is *for clerks' working-pencils*
> and which the discipline does not file. *Drawer*
> is not a working-clause; it is a plain-noun
> reach for an object the body uses without
> filing. The class includes anything held without
> a *shelf-form* — a shelf is filed, a drawer is
> held. The drawer-class is the Concord's working-
> substitute for Curation: what the Communion
> files at Depot 11, the Concord files in a
> drawer that has no working-clause, **by leaving
> rather than filing**. Form-absence as filing-
> mode.
>
> ### The hall-length
>
> The public hall is twenty-three working-paces
> long by the body's standing measurement and
> has been so for the body's full working-history.
> Senior endorsements report the hall's *felt
> length* in working-register and the felt-length
> varies. *Long* after a closure that the seam
> read clean against itself; *held* after a
> closure read clean forward; *drifting today*
> for a hall the senior reads as *not where it
> is*. The body has no working-form for *drifting
> today* as anything but a senior's reading-state.
> But the standing endorsement's drawer holds
> the cumulative count of *drifting today*
> readings across her working-life, sealed with
> a binding-knot. **The hall's drift is the
> body's only archive of its own working-place.**

Four cadence-authorities: standing-test (body)
closes the seam; bench-wear-line (no-body)
records the seam in wood; drawer-class (internalised
cross-body) archives discipline-failures as the
body's working-place for what cannot be filed;
hall-length (ancestral) is read through during
the seam, never filed, persistent across all
endorsement-generations.

The Concord has the four-place architecture the
Thermoclaves and Communion hold, with the third
authority generated rather than deferred.

### What the body does not do

The body does not canonise the bench-wear-line
as a record. The body does not canonise the
drawer-class as a class. The body does not
canonise the hall-length as filed. A Curation
cataloguer's intake in Depot 12, working-shelf
since 401–11–14–prov, has filed the four
pieces under *Concord working-architecture,
four-place, three non-doctrinal, one no-body*.
The Concord knows the filing exists; the
Concord does not ask. Both bodies' silence is
the form, fifth instance.

deepened* is plain present-perfect.

deepened, across the Body's History, by the
