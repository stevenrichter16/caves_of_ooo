## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord (rotation, second sitting)

The Saccharine Concord last landed at 1401 (stand-
aside shelf, taster's cane-syrup intake, *stood-
aside* etymology). This sitting lands the
discipline's working-form for **handover between
tasters across the stand-aside working-week** —
the structural mechanism by which the Concord's
intake survives the first-taster's bench-tenure
ending before the working-week's reading-day.

### Etymology — *for the working-week*

The Concord's vocabulary holds four canon
phrases: *stand-aside* (intake-position), *stood-
aside* (filing-attribute), *taster* (working-form
agent), and **for the working-week** (the time-
clause attached to a stand-aside admission). *For*
is the discipline's first **duration-preposition**
on the slate: where *on settle* and *under witness*
name a working-state and a governance without
duration, *for the working-week* names a
**structural duration whose calendar outranks any
individual taster's bench-tenure**. A piece is held
*for the working-week* even if the first-taster
steps off-bench mid-week; the working-form is
held by the working-week, not the hand. The phrase
joins the family at an *anthropological* register
rather than the slate-level substrate-shift
register.

### The handover-note working-form

When a walker arrives at a Concord taster-bench
with a piece, the first-taster takes the piece,
records the walker's address as *walker* (per the
keepsake-rule, 1301), assigns the piece to the
stand-aside shelf with a working-week index, and
writes a **handover-note** naming the admission-
day, recovered medium and marks, first-taster's
bench-mark, and walker-account, plus the **working-
week's reading-day** (calendar-fixed seventh day)
at which the piece will be second-tasted.

If the first-taster is off-bench on the reading-
day — common, because Concord bench-tenure rotates
by working-month — the handover-note is read by
the **second-taster** bench-side on the reading-
day. The second-taster reads the piece against the
note and files under *stood-aside* with a **joint-
bench-mark** naming both tasters.

### The joint-bench-mark register

The Concord's joint-bench-mark is structurally
distinct from the Curation's two-hand witness-
note (1801): witness-note records *contingent
attestation* across an indeterminate working-
period; joint-bench-mark records **structural
handover** across a fixed working-week. Curation
holds *against an absent walker*; Concord holds
*for a fixed duration*. The slate's two-hand
filings now canonise at two distinct positions:
handover-by-duration (Concord) and attestation-
by-rank-or-quorum (Curation witness-shelf).

The slate's hold-or-record-without-filing working-
forms now canonise at **six positions** — held-at-
threshold (Curation prov.-filed), held-under-
witness (Curation witness-shelved, sub-binary
office/quorum), held-with-time-limit (Concord,
joint-bench-mark on handover), held-with-settling-
period (Glassblown), held-against-mate (Brine),
recorded-without-holding (Thermoclaves) — with
**two** of those positions now signed by two hands
on the slate's archive (Curation witness-shelved,
Concord joint-bench-mark).
