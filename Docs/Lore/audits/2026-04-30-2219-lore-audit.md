## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible, line 21:* "It did not merely destroy the old civilization —
> it overwrote reality itself, imperfectly..."

**Strong.** Periods I–VII *before* (1901), Long Between *after* (2001),
multi-cataclysm hypothesis (2127), faction calendars (2150).

**Thin.** **The cataclysm's *duration*.** Treated as a moment — a
verb, *broke*. But the bible's metaphor is layered text, where
overwriting takes hours. Event or process?

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar inside "The Cataclysm", "The
cataclysm's hour"):**

> The Choir says the cataclysm was *one feeding* and could not have
> lasted longer than a swallow. The Pale Curation has filed a working
> estimate of *seven to eleven days*, drawn from carbonised tree-rings
> in three Tier-1 vault samples; the estimate is unratified. The Brine
> Communion describes it as *one full deep-tide* (~seven years) of
> weather they cannot otherwise explain — a rolling, not a snap. The
> Thermoclaves teach that fire was *withheld* during the cataclysm,
> then returned; the duration is the period of the withholding,
> knowable only to the tenth fire. The Saccharine Concord has no
> opinion: nothing was traded during it. The Palimpsest reads the
> layering as *a hand still moving when the ink dried* — a partial,
> not-yet-complete overwrite. The cataclysm, in the Palimpsest's
> grammar, is *ongoing*.
>
> The duration is the doctrine. A faction's answer to *how long did
> the breaking take* tells the player what shape they think reality
> is: a swallow, an estimate, a tide, a withholding, a transaction
> that did not occur, a hand still in motion.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Anthology framing (1813), footnotes (1901), salvaged
marginalia (2001), grief-marker blanks (2127), Halen's contested hand
(2150).

**Thin.** Authorship cases so far concern *one document with one or
more hands.* No case of **the same document existing in multiple
incompatible copies** — textual variants, the everyday condition of a
real palimpsest. The Curation is where this would surface.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "Reconciled Texts"):**

> The Pale Curation's *Reconciled Texts protocol* applies when two or
> more recovered copies of the same pre-cataclysm document disagree.
> The protocol is not to choose a winner. It is to file *all variants
> together*, in a single sleeve, bound by a brass pin and assigned a
> single accession number — but the brass pin marks the sleeve as
> *plural*. A scholar consulting it must read every variant; the
> Curation will not extract a consensus reading.
>
> Reconciled sleeves currently total fourteen. Twelve are clerical
> disagreements (a date misread, a name misspelled). Two are not.
> Sleeve R-3 holds four versions of a pre-cataclysm song, each with a
> different final stanza, none recoverable as the original. Sleeve
> R-7 holds two versions of what appears to be the Pale Curation's
> own founding charter. Director Ven has not opened R-7 in eleven
> years. The Curation has no protocol for that.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** Names for the cataclysm; outsider-names for Choir,
Palimpsest, substrate, joining (1813/1901/2001/2127); names for the
dead (2150).

**Thin.** **Names for the player.** Every faction in the corpus has
spoken of *walkers* in passing, but the JSON's `ChoirTendril_1` uses
*walker* as if it were universal. It is the Choir's word. No
comparative table exists.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar after the comparison table at line 205):**

> - **Choir:** *walker.* From the literal — what the player has that
>   the Choir does not. The word is mildly envious. *Walker* in Choir
>   speech is never neutral.
> - **Palimpsest:** *the next reader.* The walker is a future Echo —
>   pre-recorded, in the Palimpsest's view, by the very fact of moving
>   through layered ground. Some Palimpsest-attuned NPCs greet the
>   player by referring to *what you will have done*, in a tense the
>   player's language does not have.
> - **Pale Curation:** *unfiled subject.* A category, not an insult.
>   Subjects who have not yet generated paperwork. The Curation
>   prefers the player to remain unfiled (paperwork is finite); the
>   Curation does not always remember to mention this.
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *unredeemed account.* The walker is a
>   ledger entry not yet balanced. The Concord considers all walkers
>   debtors by default — the world owes them a life, they owe the
>   world its end, the books are open. The greeting *welcome,
>   account* is mercantile and tender at once.
> - **Brine Communion:** *the surface-borne.* From the verb — *they
>   carry the surface on them.* The Brine reach for the player's
>   shoulder before speaking; the gesture is the greeting, not the
>   word. *Surface-borne* is not addressed to the player; it is
>   addressed *about* the player to the under-tide.
> - **Thermoclaves:** *the cold-coming.* Anyone who arrives at a
>   Thermoclave camp is, by classification, *coming from the cold.*
>   The word is not pejorative; it is logistical — the camp will
>   need to warm them.
> - **Glassblown Remnant:** *the unshaped-other.* The player is a
>   walking blank — a vessel the kiln has not yet attempted. The
>   word is structurally polite; it places the speaker as *kiln* in
>   the relation. A Glassblown who calls a walker *unshaped-other*
>   is offering, gently, to be the maker.

The Choir's *walker* is one word in a chorus of seven. The bible's
voice should not adopt any of them as default narration.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Substrate-leak (1813), Choir metabolism (1901), Echo Reading
limits (2001), metaphysics-meet (2127), death-edge-cases (2150).

**Thin.** **Echo Reading on already-composted matter.** The Choir
digests memory along with material (bible:39). The Palimpsest reads
memory off material (bible:147). Their interaction at the object
surface is unwritten.

**Concrete addition (bible expansion of the metaphysics-meet section):**

> A Palimpsest reader, touching matter the Choir has already digested
> and re-deposited, reads *nothing the object did before being eaten*
> and *much the Choir has done since.* The reading is bright and
> recent — the chemical pulse of mycelial growth, occasional fragments
> of the Choir's distributed attention. A reader can sometimes hear
> the Choir *thinking about the object*, faintly, as if eavesdropping
> on a kitchen conversation about a meal long eaten. This is
> unsettling not because the Choir is hostile but because the reading
> is *first-person plural*. The reader feels themself inside the
> Choir's *we*, briefly — the closest a Palimpsest acolyte comes to
> the Choir's apotheosis without taking it.
>
> The shared rule: at the surface where the two metaphysics meet, the
> *direction of memory-flow* matters. The Choir composts forward
> (object → mycelium → distributed attention). The Palimpsest reads
> backward (object → past → impressed history). When their flows
> cross, the reader gets *the wrong direction* — not the object's
> past, but the Choir's ongoing present *about* the object. Three
> Palimpsest acolytes are recorded as having made exactly this
> mistake. Two have recovered. One is filed under R-7.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** Mirror endings (bible:204), Annihilist endings (1901),
Stalled Walkers (2001), apotheoses-from-outside (2127), failed
apotheoses (2150).

**Thin.** **The act of declining apotheosis when it is offered.** The
bible (line 224) gestures: *some players refuse the node at Honored
tier.* But the in-world account of *refusal* is unwritten. Refusal is
not failure (2150). It is a *third disposition.*

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "When the apotheosis is
refused"):**

> A walker offered the node and declining is, in the Choir's grammar,
> *a guest who has eaten and not yet eaten* — not insulted, not
> rejected, not absent. The Choir holds the offer open indefinitely;
> *patience* is its primary virtue and refusal is simply the offer
> ungrounded. Subsequent dialogue with that walker shifts subtly: the
> *we* loses its envelopment and becomes *almost-formal* — closer to
> the Curation's register than to earlier warmth. The Choir is being
> polite with someone who has, by declining, become a stranger again.
>
> A walker offered Transcription and declining is, in the
> Palimpsest's grammar, *a sentence chosen not to be written.* The
> moment passes, and the layering at that location *thickens slightly*
> in a way readable by other Palimpsest acolytes for years. The
> walker has left a *non-mark*: a place where the palimpsest was
> offered the page and the page declined. Other readers feel the
> thickening as a *quiet dignity at the level of the substrate*. The
> Palimpsest does not punish refusal; it records it.
>
> The walker who declines both offers is simply who they were before.
> The rarest disposition the bible records — neither integration nor
> transcription nor annihilation. It has no name in the corpus.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Choir pronouns (line 228), Palimpsest tense (1813), Brine
joining (2001), Thermoclave fire-grammar (2127), Glassblown unsaid
mood (2150).

**Thin.** **Pale Curation grammar.** Audits have established Curation
*register* (bureaucratic, hedged) but not Curation *syntax.* What does
the Curation's grammar do that no other faction's does?

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "Curation Grammar"):**

> Pale Curation grammar adds a fifth verb mood to the four ordinarily
> recognised in the corpus's other faction-grammars: the **catalogued
> mood**. It takes a verb and converts it into an action that has been
> *observed, recorded, and assigned an accession number*. Outsiders
> hear it as a passive — *the wall has been catalogued* — but the
> mood is active in Curation grammar; it places the speaker as
> *witness-of-record* and the action as *bound, retrievable, weighed.*
>
> The mood is formed by infixing *-fil-* between the verb stem and its
> ending — from the noun *file*, the Curation's first sacrament.
> Examples:
>
> - *The wall stands.* — indicative.
> - *The wall stand-fil-s.* — catalogued. The wall has been observed
>   by Curation-recognised eyes; a card exists; the wall is, in the
>   strong Curation sense, *real.*
> - *The wall fall-fil-s.* — the wall fell, and the falling has been
>   recorded; the wall is in the past tense and the file is in the
>   present.
>
> A wall the Curation has not catalogued *does not stand-fil*. It
> may stand. It does not *stand of record.* This is not metaphor.
> A Curation surveyor will refuse to write down what they have not
> personally seen, and will refuse to *speak in the catalogued mood*
> about anything not yet filed. Their plain-mood speech (the wall
> stands) is taken by the Curation itself as *uncertain* — the
> ordinary tense of the world before the file closes.
>
> The Choir does not grammatically distinguish *catalogued* from
> *consumed*; for the Choir, both reduce the object to part of a
> larger whole. The Curation considers this an offence so basic that
> they will not be drawn into argument about it.
>
> Director Ven speaks more frequently in the catalogued mood than any
> other surveyor. Halen, notably, speaks less — surveyors who deal
> with disputed-authorship objects develop a hesitation about the
> mood, and Halen's R-7-adjacent caseload has cost her about a third
> of her catalogued-mood frequency over the past three years. The
> Curation has noticed. The Curation has not commented.

The Curation's *register* is now its *syntax*. The catalogued mood
gives the Curation what every grammar in the corpus has — a shape its
worldview is forced to take.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Strong.** Tuesday Morning across all six factions (1813, 1901, 2001,
2127, 2150).

**Thin.** **Cross-faction Tuesdays.** The current corpus describes
each faction *alone in its morning.* No section addresses what happens
when two factions' Tuesdays collide — a Brine sailor at a Concord
quay, a Glassblown trader at a Curation intake desk, a Thermoclave
crossing Choir ground.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "When Tuesdays cross"):**

> The Concord market at Salt-Hinge runs four mornings a week and
> draws all six recognised factions. The Curation prefers to arrive
> last — surveyors believe what is brought to them, and want the
> traders to assemble first. The Brine arrive earliest by tide-clock
> and are usually finishing their joining-words while the Concord
> is still setting out scales. The Glassblown will not enter the
> market until they have completed the unfiring; their stalls open
> at midday and close at sundown — half a day shorter than every
> other faction's. The Thermoclaves attend with one apprentice and
> one fire-keeper; the apprentice deals, the keeper holds. Choir
> tendrils do not formally trade but two have been observed at the
> market's edges, *listening*. The Concord considers the listening
> a form of due diligence and does not chase them off.
>
> The market's central convention — a Concord innovation, fifty-odd
> years old — is *the half-redemption*: trades closed at the market
> can be re-opened on the next market-day at half their original
> value, by either party, no reason given. The Brine love this; it
> matches their grammar of return-tides. The Curation finds it
> faintly destabilising and has filed it under *Disputed (workable)*.
> The Glassblown, who do not trade fired markers, decline the
> half-redemption by category, which the Concord respects without
> understanding.

The bible now frames cross-faction commerce as a *site* — not a list
of trades but a Tuesday morning all six factions are doing
simultaneously, with their grammars audibly leaking into each other.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Pale Curation (round 2)

The Curation has Halen, Director Ven, Surveyor Kesti, intake forms,
internal-review slips, the Reconciled Texts protocol, the catalogued
mood, sleeve R-7. It has bureaucracy. It does not yet have a
**founding myth.**

**Proposed lore-artifact: the First Card.**

Every Curation surveyor learns, in their first week of training, the
story of the First Card. The story is not written down — surveyors
note this is itself doctrine — but a verbal version, agreed across
all five Curation chapter-houses, runs roughly as follows.

After the cataclysm, when the survivors of the old administrative
class were still finding each other across the wreckage, a clerk
whose name has not been preserved sat down in a ruined office, took
the last unburned sheet of card-stock from a drawer, and wrote on it,
in ordinary indicative tense: *the office stands. the door is gone.
the window is gone. the desk is here. I am here.* Then they ruled the
card with the standard accession lines from memory, dated it, and
filed it in their pocket because no cabinet remained.

The clerk walked out of the office and lived for fifteen more years.
They never wrote a second card. When they died, the First Card was
found in their inner coat pocket, folded once. A Curation founder
read it and understood what cataloguing was *for*: not to control
the world, not to preserve it, but to *witness the survival of the
ordinary against odds that made the ordinary almost impossible to
say.* The card has been re-copied many times; the original is
believed lost, possibly to fire, possibly to the unfounded chapter
at South Hinge.

The catalogued mood (Dimension 6) is, in this telling, the clerk's
grammar made institutional. The verb *stands* in the First Card is
ambiguous between indicative and catalogued; later surveyors read it
as catalogued, and the convention has held. Director Ven, asked
about the First Card, has given the same five-word answer for thirty
years: *I have not seen it.* The answer is, deliberately, in the
indicative mood. The Curation is reading her silence about R-7
through this answer; many surveyors believe Director Ven is waiting
to *catalogue R-7 herself* before she will speak in the catalogued
mood about the founding charter.

The Choir, on hearing the story of the First Card, is reported (by
one Halen field-card, filed under *informal, not for circulation*)
to have said: *we have eaten the cabinet. we have not eaten the
pocket. we will be patient with the pocket.* The card, in the
Choir's reading, is not yet compost — it is held against the body
that walks. Halen's marginalia adds: *they are correct. that is what
the card is.*

The First Card gives the Curation what every other developed faction
in the corpus has — a doctrine that is not a procedure but a *story
about why the procedure exists.* It also pins Director Ven: her
silence on R-7 is no longer evasion, it is **a senior surveyor
exercising the discipline of the catalogued mood.**

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a)** `RotChoir.json:338`: the Choir says *"The Glassblown Remnant
offers nothing at all."* The bible asserts (line 224, design rule):
*"The Choir should be genuinely helpful. ... It never lies."* But the
Glassblown produce grief-markers, blanks, fired shapes, an entire
trade economy in unfired markers (2150), and a doctrine (the unfiring)
the Choir cannot consume. The Choir's claim is false in plain reading.

**Resolution:** the Choir does not *lie*; the Choir has an ontology in
which *what cannot be composted* is *not a thing*. Fired markers leave
the Glassblown trade-economy by being fired (2150); to the Choir,
which reads existence through digestion, fired markers are
non-entities. The line *the Glassblown offer nothing at all* is
metabolically true and ontologically partial. **Recommend the bible
add a one-paragraph design note** (under the Choir Implementation
Rules section, after line 226):

> The Choir is honest within its ontology. It does not lie about
> *what is*; it is silent about *what its grammar cannot register as
> existing.* When the Choir says the Glassblown offer nothing, this
> is metabolically true (nothing they make is compostable). It is
> not a moral judgement and the Choir would not understand the
> question if a player asked. The player should not be told this is
> a lie. The player should be permitted to *notice* it on their own.

**(b)** `RotChoir.json:107`: the Choir says *"There are cities inside
us — whole civilizations digested into understanding."* The Curation's
First Card story (Section 3 above) places one survivor outside the
Choir. The pocket-card is *not* in the Choir. The line in JSON, as
written, claims totality. Audits have softened this in pieces (Stalled
Walkers, un-mouths, R-7) but the JSON line itself still reads as
*"we have eaten everything."* **Recommend the JSON line be revised
to: "There are cities inside us — much of every civilisation, but not
all of any."** The Choir's honesty (per (a)) does not let it claim
total digestion. The pocket survives.

The redactions are mechanical (brass-pin lines), not ink — meaning
the original text is still legible by candle. **Held to a candle**
(an in-game interaction), the redacted text resolves:

> *We are running out of things to file. The cataclysm-ruins are*
> *finite. The Tier-1 vaults have been complete for two years; the*
> *Tier-2 vaults will be complete inside a decade at current intake.*

Lore landed parenthetically: **the Curation's mission has an
end-date.** The bible's *six hundred years of Choir patience* has a
counterpart in the *twenty years of Curation work.* The Curation is
not eternal. It is *almost done.* And R-7 — the founding-charter
sleeve Director Ven has not opened in eleven years — is being held
against that end-date as a kind of strategic reserve.

Plant: the redacted memo is in the Q3 procurement file at any
chapter-house. Holding it to a candle is the act. The candle is the
Thermoclave's gift to the Curation reader, in a small cross-faction
joke the bible can choose to acknowledge or not.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"The duration is the doctrine. A faction's answer to* how long did
> *the breaking take* *tells the player what shape they think reality
> is: a swallow, an estimate, a tide, a withholding, a transaction
> that did not occur, a hand still in motion."*

No archaisms. No invented apostrophe-names of the *Mor'kal'thar* family.
No *verily*, no Tower-borrowings, no Aedra/Daedra. The sentence works
by listing six concrete answers in plain present tense, with the
weight on the verbs (*swallow*, *withholding*, *occur*, *motion*). A
TES book attempting the same beat would have written *"And in this
manner, the doom-of-days is sung differently by every people: the
Mushroom-folk name it a swallowing, the Lorekeepers a season of seven
years, the Fire-tenders a denial of holy flame, and so on, until the
truth is many-throated and known to none."* Mine is plainer, sharper,
and trusts the reader to hold six items without ornament.

second. The Pale Curation now has a doctrine (the First Card), a
syntax (the catalogued mood), an end-date (twenty years of work
remaining), and a senior surveyor whose silences mean specific things.
The corpus's documentary depth is now slightly thicker on the side of
the institution that, in the fiction, is supposed to be doing the
documenting.*
