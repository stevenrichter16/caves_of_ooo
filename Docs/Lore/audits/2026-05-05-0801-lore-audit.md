## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible line 21:* *"It did not merely destroy the old civilization
> — it overwrote reality itself, imperfectly."*

**Strong.** Eight body-readings; the seam-body's ninth (*the matter
the body does not get to choose,* 0701).

**Thin.** The ninth is told from the cataloguer's side. No
corresponding harbour-side account from a Brine member-by-presence.

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar, "The deep-coming, in
warden 217's reading"):

> By Brine reading, the cataclysm was *the deep-coming* — not the
> moment land broke, but the moment the deep stopped being able to
> keep its dead. Salvage the deep would have held in sediment
> forever began surfacing on the tide. The first salvage-wardens
> are remembered as *the receivers,* not wreckers.
>
> By the seam-body's reading, *the deep-coming,* the *glass-day,*
> and the *first overwrite* are one event in three registers. The
> deep is the strike-layer is the under-text. 217's reading is the
> corpus's first cataclysm-account whose central verb is
> intransitive: *the deep stopped being able,* not *the deep was
> emptied.* The deep did not lose its dead. It lost the capacity
> to keep them.

Choir: *the world is becoming.* Palimpsest: *the world was.*
Brine: *the deep no longer can.* The seam-body holds all three as
one loss-of-capacity.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** The signing as the only two-body mechanic; not-asking
as its working operation.

**Thin.** The signing requires senior-presence *while* the junior

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The harbour-passive

> The harbour-passive signing is the corpus's only signing performed
> by **a non-witness** — an absent senior whose not-asking is
> structurally guaranteed by their distance from the working
> operation. The harbour-master never reads the back-face of a
> tide-log; the back-face is a place the harbour-master is *trained
> not to look.* The not-asking is built into the binding-pin and
> the page-fold.
>
> The two-body requirement is satisfied at the level of *standing
> practice* rather than *single moment.* The harbour-master's
> entire career is the senior's not-asking, performed continuously
> by the harbour-master's working continuity itself.

The seam-body's mechanic gains a second mode: the **single not-

(Brine, harbour-passive).

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord

Rotation: Brine (1301, 0601), Curation (2001), Thermoclaves (2101,
0401), Concord (0501), Glassblown (0701). Concord three back; no
self-contained etymology + ritual sitting since R3 material at 2201.

**Etymology.** *Saccharine Concord.* Both words literal in founders'
usage; both have lost the literal sense in modern readings.

*Saccharine* names a real chemical condition. Founders worked in
three sugar refineries on the eastern sweetwater plain,
decommissioned by the cataclysm and repurposed in the first
generation as labour-arbitration halls. Meetings were held in
rooms where the air still tasted of evaporated sugar; the
founders' compact was negotiated with sleeves sticky from the
bench.

*Concord* is the noun-form of *concord-sign,* the marginal mark
made by every signatory at the bottom of the compact when reading
another signatory's clause. Eleven signatories; eleven concord-
signs in a column became the compact's signature register.

The full pre-doctrinal term was *the saccharine concord:* *the
agreement reached in the sticky air.* Modern usage loses both
senses. The Concord no longer makes sugar. The concord-sign is no
longer a witness of co-reading; it is a witness of self-reading,
and refusing to make one renders one's account *standing-open at
the writer's edge.*

**The Reading-Across.** Every Concord clerk, on the founders'
compact-day (spring tide-rise, by Concord calendar), takes a
single account from a predecessor at random from the archive and
reads it aloud, alone, in their working chamber. Silent in the
sense that no other clerk hears it. The reading-aloud is for the
predecessor's account itself.

By Concord doctrine, an account never read aloud after its writing
is *standing-open at the writer's edge,* a degraded form of
standing-open. The Reading-Across closes thousands of small
standings each year. The archive is sized so that, over a clerk's
working lifetime, every account is read at least once.

For a senior approaching retirement, the Reading-Across becomes
the last working duty: read every account they themselves wrote,
alone, in their last week of office. By doctrine, an unreread
retired clerk's accounts re-open in the archive and produce a
faint metaphysical pressure on the next generation's reading —
apprentices report unease with no identifiable source, traced
back two or three generations later to an unreread retiree.

The Reading-Across is the Concord's only ritual that admits no
witnesses, and the only ritual whose efficacy depends on its
being unwitnessed. By the seam-body's reading, the Concord has
been operating a body-internal **held-without-witness** practice
for at least four generations and has not noticed it as such.

slack tide is a slack tide. The not-turning is a working
observable that has accreted twenty-six years of working content
