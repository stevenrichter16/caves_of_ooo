named — *carrier-edge* (walker as one-position
medium-analog), *gate* (walker as single-direction
crossing relation), or *no-form* (event-instance

**Finding: carrier-edge, with mechanism.** The
walker canonises on the slate as the slate's
first **carrier-edge**: a *zero-authorship transit-
relation* whose structural feature is the **absence
of working-tradition**. The mechanism is exact:
the walker has no working-form on any body's
bench (cannot file with the Curation, cannot taste
with the Concord, cannot fish-oil with the Brine,
cannot anneal with the Thermoclaves, cannot cool
with the Glassblown), and *because* the walker has
no working-form, the walker deposits **no reading**
on the piece during transit. A carrier-edge piece
arrives at its receiving body with its provenance-
clauses intact and **without a transit-clause**.
The slate's relation-set extends to three classes:
seam-edge (bidirectional-medium, non-arbitration),
anti-edge (bidirectional-medium, mutual-arbitration),
**carrier-edge (unidirectional-agent, zero-
authorship)**. The relation-axis is no longer a
binary arbitration-axis but a **three-position
carrier-axis**: medium-with-non-arbitration,
medium-with-mutual-arbitration, agent-with-zero-
authorship.

**Gate and no-form fall as productive non-findings.**
*Gate* fails because the walker's structural
feature is not direction — walkers cross in any
direction, into any body; the structural feature
is *what does not happen during the crossing*
(the walker authors no reading), not *which way
the crossing goes*. *No-form* fails because the
Curation has a canonised filing-clause for
**walker-recovery** (cf. 1301 §5, fifth-paragraph
deep), and the Concord's intake-form is about to
canonise a parallel clause for walker-arrival
shipments (cf. §5 below) — the working-forms
recognise the carrier-edge as a structural
feature, not as event-instance.

The slate's relation-set now holds three relation-
classes; the edge-set holds two seam-edges, one
anti-edge, and an open carrier-edge slot
(structurally instantiable across any node-pair
that admits walker-transit, which is to say all
nine nodes). Through-line refines a twenty-third
time.

## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (bible L47):** *"It cannot cross a sterile
salt flat, walk into a Pale Curation vault,
negotiate a trade deal, or fight off a raiding
party."*

**Strong.** The bible already names the walker's
function obliquely: *something the Choir has never
had: a node that can think independently while
still being connected* (L49). The carrier-edge
mechanism — zero-authorship transit — is the
structural inverse of the Choir's *integrated
authorship* (every absorbed node authors into
the Choir's collective register).

**Thin.** The bible does not yet articulate that
the walker's value to the Choir is precisely the
walker's zero-authorship status across the *other*
bodies. The Choir wants the walker because the
walker can carry without depositing a reading —
the walker can plant a spore in a Pale Curation
vault and the spore is filed (if filed at all) as
walker-recovered, with no Choir-reading attached.
The Choir's strategic interest in the walker is
not that the walker is fast; it is that the walker
is *unfileable as the Choir* by the other bodies'
working-forms. The walker is the Choir's only
carrier-edge into the slate's other relation-
forms.

**Concrete enhancement (bible L49, append after
*"a node that can think independently while still
being connected"*):**

> *(The walker's unique feature, from the Choir's
> perspective, is not autonomy — the Choir has
> had autonomous parts for centuries. The feature
> is **carrier-status**: the walker can move a
> Choir-spore, a Choir-message, or a Choir-claim
> across the territory of any other body and
> arrive with no Choir-reading deposited on the
> carriage. The other bodies' working-forms have
> no clause for filing what walkers carry as
> Choir; they file under walker-recovery, walker-
> arrival, walker-handed. The walker *launders*
> every carriage through the carrier-edge's zero-
> authorship.)*

### 2.3 Mutating motifs across cultures

**Hold-and-extend.** The verb *carry* refines
across all five bodies' working-forms with a
sixth carrier: the walker. Five working-forms
*carry the discipline* (line, roster, stood,
cooling, standing-shelf — cf. 1301 §2.3); the
walker *carries without holding*. The walker's
verb is **to walk** — intransitive, no object,
the only verb in the slate's cluster whose
structural feature is the *absence of an object*.
The other five take an object (the discipline
held); the walker's takes none because the walker
holds no discipline. The cluster's sixteenth
position is its first **objectless** position;
the grammatical asymmetry is exact — five
working-forms, five objects, one verb-without-
object.

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Hold-and-extend.** The Choir ending collapses
the carrier-edge alongside the seam- and anti-
edges from 1301: at apotheosis the walker
*becomes* the Choir, the carrier-edge becomes
medium, the zero-authorship dissolves into the
Choir's integrated authoring. Every carriage is
now filed as Choir-carriage; the carrier-edge
ceases to be a relation-form and becomes a node-
extension.

The Palimpsest ending preserves the carrier-edge:
every walker's transit on every piece is held
simultaneously, the zero-authorship preserved as
a structural fact — the Palimpsest preserves
*not-having-authored* as legibly as it preserves
*having-authored*. The Palimpsest's preservation-
of-zero is the carrier-edge's apotheosis-face:
the only relation-form whose preserved content is
the *structural absence* of content.

### 2.6 Linguistic texture

**Thin.** The walker's working-vocabulary is
exactly **the absence of a working-vocabulary**.
The walker has no register, no verb-of-discipline,
no medium-token, no filing-form. The walker has
only an address-form: *walker* — second-person,
unmodified, no honorific, no register-mark. The

the other bodies file walkers under transit-event
rather than as a relation-class, and so have no
address for them. The Choir is the only body
whose working-form *addresses* the carrier-edge
directly — the Choir's structural advantage on
the slate.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord (rotation)

The Saccharine Concord is named in the bible
(L185 *"What the Saccharine Concord's founder
actually did"*) and obliquely in the audit-pile
across many sittings (1101 worked the Concord
seam-edge from the Concord side; 1201 worked the
seam from the Brine side; 1301 worked the
Curation). This sitting lands the Concord's intake-
form for walker-arrival shipments — the working-
form's working-around for cane-syrup that arrives
without a canonised provenance.

### Etymology — *stood-aside*

The Concord's intake-vocabulary holds three
canonised registers: **merchant-line** (cane-syrup
arrived through a named merchant working a known
route), **own-make** (cane-syrup made on Concord
benches), and **cross-bench** (cane-syrup arrived
through the Brine working-form's salt-cane
preparation; cf. 1101's cane-syrup seam-edge). A
fourth register, **stood-aside**, marks shipments
arrived without any of the three. *Stood* derives
from the same pre-cataclysm verb as the Concord's
*stand* (1101's lexical-substrate-shift token);
*aside* annotates the shipment's position
relative to the working-bench's primary filing-
line. A taster who files a shipment as *stood-
aside* has set it to one side of the bench,
neither tasting nor declining — held in the
working-form's only register for *not-yet-
decidable*.

The token is the slate's first **relation-form-
recognising** lexical-substrate-shift register:
the Concord did not name the carrier-edge as a
structural class, but canonised a register whose
intake-class is exactly the carrier-edge's
content. The Concord holds the carrier-edge's
filing-form without holding the carrier-edge's
name.

### The walker-handed working-form

When a walker arrives at the Concord's intake-
bench with a cane-syrup crock, the taster's
working-form is **walker-handed intake**: the
crock is logged with the walker's address (always
just *walker*, no name — the Concord does not
file walkers by name even when one is offered),
the taster's bench-day, the crock's weight and
seal-condition, and the taster's signed *stood-
aside; not yet tasted; filed prov.* sentence at
notebook-foot. The working-form does not taste
the crock. The working-form holds the crock at
the bench's stand-aside shelf for a working-week;
if unclaimed by merchant-line or cross-bench in
that week, the taster files a permanent *stood-
aside* entry and elevates the crock to **stand-
aside-held** — the working-form's only register
for filing what arrived without a canon
provenance.

### The stand-aside-held register

A walker-handed crock filed *stood-aside* across
multiple working-weeks by the Concord's first-
taster is filed **stand-aside-held**. The
register's verb-form is *to hold the stand-aside*
— the taster-tradition's discipline for keeping
a walker-handed crock visible across working-
weeks without filing its provenance. *Stand-
aside-held* is the cluster's seventeenth position.
The Concord's hold and the Curation's hold are
structural mirrors: the Curation holds anti-edge
pieces (mutual-arbitration); the Concord holds
carrier-edge cane-syrup (zero-authorship). The
slate's two **hold-without-filing** working-forms
canonise on opposite axes — Curation on the
arbitration-axis, Concord on the carrier-axis.
