## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"The audit's standing position is that no further audit-side work
> will move the figure faster than this 90 minutes will."*

Working register, plain-spoken, procedural. *Standing position* is
clerical (the same register a Pale Curation officer would file a
recommendation in). *No further audit-side work* names the audit as
a working body with a side, not a herald with a doctrine. *Move the
figure* is back-office (the figure is the edit-debt counter, a working
ledger entry, not a sacred number). *This 90 minutes* is concrete, not
ceremonial — a working-time estimate a clerk would produce. Verbs are
flat: *is, will move.* No *verily,* no archaism, no inverted syntax,
no apostrophe-fantasy nominal forms.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"The Audit's enduring counsel is that no
further work of the auditing-body shall stir the Reckoning more swift
than these ninety minutes shall."* I did not write that. The forbidden
version's tells: *enduring counsel* (heraldic substitution for
*standing position*); *the auditing-body* with capitalisation (a
register the audit has explicitly avoided for thirty-nine sittings);
*the Reckoning* (a sacred name applied to a working ledger figure);
*shall stir / shall* (auxiliary doubling); the inversion *more swift
than these ninety minutes shall.* The actual sentence's anti-tells:
the lower-cased *audit*, the workplace *side*, the unceremonial
*figure*, the unhedged *90 minutes* in numerals (a clerk's habit; a
herald would write out *ninety*).
