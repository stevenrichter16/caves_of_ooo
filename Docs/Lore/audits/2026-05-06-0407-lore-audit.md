## 3. Supplementary Glassblown working-doctrine

The 0404 §3 rotated Glassblown and developed two working
registers: the **cooling-watch** (working ritual, dual-witness
on a fresh piece) and the **twice-witnessed** (mourning practice
for a piece that breaks after filing). Both register the
Glassblown as a *making-faction* — concerned with what the bench
produces.

The faction has a *second* working register the 0404 did not
develop: what the bench does with **acquired pre-cataclysm
shards**. This is not the working-day craft; it is the working-
year reading-practice. The two registers stack: the cooling-
watch attends to the new; the shard-cabinet attends to the old.

### The shard-cabinet

Every working-bench maintains a low cabinet behind the working-
master's stool, locked except during apprentice working-hours.
The cabinet holds *cold-glass shards from the pre-cataclysm
world* — fragments of vessels, windows, instruments, lenses,
working-tools. Acquired by the bench across decades, by trade
or salvage; **never re-melted**.

### The never-re-melt rule

Cold glass *could*, technically, be re-melted in a hot enough
furnace and recast. The Glassblown **will not.** A working-
master who re-melts a pre-cataclysm shard is **un-benched** —
stripped of working-rights, the held benches sitting empty for
a year as a working memorial to the un-finishing. The strongest
doctrinal prohibition the faction carries.

The reasoning is cosmological. Cold-glass shards are the frozen
working-shape of the world at the moment the heat went —
witnesses to what was being held when the working-window
closed. Re-melting destroys the witness. The working-master who
re-melts is, in faction grammar, an *erasure* — the same
working term the Palimpsest uses for the Choir's compost-
eating.

### The working-color and edge-temperature reads

A bench reads a shard on two axes:

  - **Working-color** (calibrated to working-temperature
    profile): *standing-color* (mid-window finish), *kept-color*
    (held at heat unusually long), *last-color* (the mottled
    blue-black-amber that appears only in cataclysm-era shards
    and no later working has reproduced).
  - **Edge-temperature** (encoded in the breaking edge):
    *fast-edge* (sudden temperature shock), *slow-edge*
    (sustained cooling stress), *un-edge* (impact after full
    cooling, no thermal signature).

Read together, the two axes resolve a shard's working-position
at the moment the world's working-window closed. A fast-edge
last-color shard reads as: glass in cataclysm-era working-heat
at the instant the heat went, shocked into cool-state in the
same instant. The shard *is* the cataclysm, in a piece.

### Resolution of the 0404 §4 bleed

The 0404 §4 flagged the Glassblown's possible quintet-exclusion
on the grounds that "the Glassblown's medium is the craft
itself, not a separable substrate." Sitting 18.5 §3 above
answers explicitly: **the Glassblown have two media.** The
craft (the cooling-watch's strip) is one — closer to the
Thermoclave heat-witness colour-strip than to a separable
substrate. The shards (the cabinet's read-by-working-color-
and-edge-temperature) are the other — and shards **are** a
separable substrate, externally readable, calibrated by
reference-card. The Glassblown belong in the quintet on the
shards, not on the strip.

The 04:04 §4 bleed flag is therefore resolved as: **Glassblown
inclusion confirmed; the medium is shards-via-working-color-
and-edge-temperature.** Item 27, when graduating, expands to a
quintet with the entry:

  - **Glassblown — working-color and edge-temperature on
    cold-glass shard.**

(The cooling-watch strip is a *secondary* Glassblown medium and
need not appear in the quintet's headline list — the quintet
addresses *external substrates*, and the strip indexes a piece
internal to the bench's own working.)

*primary holder + secondary readings via release-slip.* The
0404 framed item 28 around a three-form taxonomy: *requested
loan / in-place reading / consumption.* These do not compete;

    contest. *Three working forms.*
  - **The release-slip** is a procedural artifact for the
    *requested-loan* form specifically. The Curation's negative-
    shelf entry, the Glassblown's shard-cabinet release-slip,
    the Thermoclave's strip-cabinet lock all share this
    procedural shape.

    named — the Brine's *deposit-token* at the shoaling-house

    the Choir's *welcoming* at the request stage (per 0404 §5:
    *"the Choir does not ask in bad faith"*) is a candidate
    procedural form for consumption.

**Recommendation for sitting 19's item 28 graduation pass:**

  1. Keep the 0404 three-form taxonomy as the headline
     structure.
  2. Add a procedural-form line per category — *release-slip*
     for requested loan; *deposit-token* for in-place reading;

  3. Cite the 0404 §5 forge-record sheet's mid-sheet

## 5. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"The shard* is *the cataclysm, in a piece."*

Working register, gloss-form. *Is* italicised in the working
way Glassblown working-masters mark a doctrinal claim — the
claim sits inside a working sentence and the italic is the
working signal that the bench is teaching, not describing.
*A piece* is the bench's working-noun for any single artifact
(a vessel, a fragment, a shard) — flat working-register, not
mythic. Lower-case throughout. No allegorical capitalisation.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"Behold, the Shard is itself the
Sundering, made small and cold; in its broken Heart the
Cataclysm endures, frozen in Glass."* Tells: *Behold*; *Shard*,
*Sundering*, *Heart*, *Cataclysm*, *Glass* (allegorical
capitalisations); *the Sundering, made small and cold* (mythic
participial-phrase substituting for the working *frozen
working-shape*); *in its broken Heart* (figurative anatomy
substituting for the working *edge-temperature*). The actual
sentence: *shard*, *is*, *cataclysm*, *piece* — four lower-case
working nouns, the italic doing the doctrinal work the TES
version does with capitalisation.

Pass.
