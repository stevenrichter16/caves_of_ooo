## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible:185 (high-tier Palimpsest revelation, unanswered):* *"What
> the Saccharine Concord's founder actually did."*

**Strong.** Cataclysm-aftermath stratified across round-1; *coin-
alloy strata* in sibling 2307.

**Thin.** The pre-Concord moment. Sibling 2307 reaches *back through*
the Concord's coins; it does not stratify the founding act itself.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar after the Concord introduction,
"The three founder-stories"):**

> The Saccharine Concord teaches three founder-stories, by chapter-
> house, in the apprentice's first week. They are mutually
> inconsistent and the inconsistency is doctrinal.
>
> - **Northern.** *Forgiveness of the Final Loaf*: a baker, in a
>   ruined market the morning after the cataclysm, with one loaf
>   and four hungry strangers, distributed it without payment and
>   recorded the distribution in a hand-stitched register. *The
>   Hand was kind.*
> - **Southern.** *Naming of the Rate*: a survivor with charcoal
>   and a flat stone, scratched the first post-cataclysm price
>   (a unit nobody now remembers); four people agreed to honour
>   it. *The Hand was firm.*
> - **Coastal.** *No founder.* Debt and exchange are older than
>   the cataclysm and required no founding. *The Hand has always
>   been moving.*
>
> A clerk who chooses one as canonical is *gently redirected*: the
> three are the Concord's first lesson in **reconciliation** — the
> competence at holding incompatible accounts open without closing
> prematurely. Three voices, none authoritative.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** R-7 / R-3 plural sleeves (2219); the forged tag (2220);
contradicting footnotes (2225); sibling 2307's R-11 buyer's-ledger
sleeve and Mer Hin's wax-sealed unknown coin.

**Thin.** A Concord-internal entry that doctrine *forbids closing*.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar following the founder-stories,
"Short-counted"):**

> The Concord's working term for a receipt that does not match its
> counterparty's record is **short-counted**. Doctrine: *the
> short-counted line is not a lie; it is a debt of explanation.* A
> clerk first records the discrepancy as a separate line, then
> investigates, then closes. Many never close — they are filed
> *open-discrepancy*, a category the Concord considers honourable.
>
> The deepest short-counted entry is the **Founder's Own**
> (Section 3), which doctrine forbids closing.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Substrate-leak, Choir metabolism, Echo limits,
metaphysics-meet, composted-matter readings (2219), interference
(2225), sibling 2307's *consensus substrate-stratum*.

**Thin.** A stratum *below* consensus. Every Concord transaction
in 2307 pays a *settlement-fee* to *the standing line* — used
casually, never elevated.

**Concrete addition (bible, expansion of the Concord cosmology,
"The standing line"):**

> Concord doctrine recognises four parties to any transaction: the
> giver, the receiver, the witness, and the **standing line** — the
> world considered as the medium that made the transaction possible
> (the air the words travelled through, the floor the bread rested
> on, the soil that grew the wheat). The standing line is owed a
> *settlement-fee*: doctrinally small but never zero. Concord
> transactions include a token entry — a grain of salt, a thread
> of cotton, a drop of oil — set aside.
>
> Standing is the Concord's *fourth* substrate-stratum, below
> consensus. Material, temporal, consensual, standing — descending
> order of legibility. The Choir says *we taste the standing line.
> it does not taste owed.* The Palimpsest reports *the standing
> line was paid, but not by the borrower.* The Concord teaches
> *the cataclysm was the standing line demanding its arrears.*
> None of the three doctrines is falsifiable from inside any of
> the three. Founder-stories pivot on which arrears the founder
> believed had settled (kind: full; firm: partial; always-moving:
> asymptote).

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Choir pronouns; Palimpsest tense; Brine joining;
Thermoclave fire-grammar; Glassblown unsaid mood; Curation
catalogued mood and quiet conventions; sibling 2307's *trade tense*
(verb-level *-up-* closure); sibling 2306's *priced clause* (tail-
position fractional marker on obligation-clauses).

**Thin.** **Assertive clause-pricing.** Sibling 2306 priced
*obligation*-clauses (tail position, fractional smallest unit).
The Concord *also* prices *assertive* clauses, in leading-bracket
position with a different unit set. Same doctrine, two
grammatical environments.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection beside 2306's priced
clause, "The receipted clause"):**

> Where the priced clause (2306) prices *obligations* in the tail,
> the **receipted clause** prices *assertions* in the lead, form
> `(<unit>) <clause>`, smallest denomination, even if zero.
>
> - *(0g) The wall stands.* — gratis. *Creates a debt of
>   acknowledgment* in the listener.
> - *(½c) The wall stands.* — half-credit. Listener owes the
>   speaker at next reconciliation.
> - *(–) The wall stands.* — refused / withdrawn. The listener
>   may not reference, act on, or record the clause.
> - *(0c) The wall stands.* — null-billed. Creates a ledger line
>   with no amount; doctrinally, the most honest form when price
>   is genuinely uncertain. (*(0g)* differs: creates debt.)
>
> A formal paragraph carries price markers on every clause AND
> the trade tense's *-up-* on closure verbs. *(½c) I sold-up the
> cloth* is a half-credit assertion of a closed trade. The rarest
> form — the **unbilled greeting**, no marker at all — is
> reserved for addressing a zero-line walker; misuse is a
> doctrinal offence comparable to catalogued-mood abuse.

The paragraph is now legible at two layers (clause-level pricing,
verb-level closure) plus the obligation-position priced clause
(2306) when present.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection following the wedding,
"The Closing of Accounts"):**

> When a Concord clerk dies, every chapter-house they ever filed a
> receipt for is notified within seven days. Each performs the
> **Closing of Accounts**: receipts pulled in chronological order;
> for each open discrepancy, a senior clerk *attempts to close it*
> — paying from the treasury, accepting personal liability if
> irrecoverable, or filing **Closed by Mourning** (distinct from
> *Closed by Settlement*).
>
> Closed-by-Mourning entries are the Concord's grief-marker. They
> are read aloud, by name and amount, at the end of the Closing
> — a clerk's life is *the sum of the discrepancies one was unable
> to close + the discrepancies one was able to close.* The
> Founder's Own (Section 3) is read at every funeral: *Three loaves
> were given. Four people ate. The fourth has not yet appeared in
> any subsequent ledger. May we close before they ask.*
>
> Veshen's eventual Closing is quietly dreaded at Coastal — her
> open ledgers include *the alloy stock in eleven years' time*
> (sibling 2307), *open by design*. Standing instruction: *file
> her stock-ledger as open at her death. We will close by
> inheritance.* No one has identified the inheritor.

Concord mourning is the inverse of the Curation's. Curation files
presence by accounting for absence; Concord *closes absence by
reckoning presence.*

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord (round 2, founder)

Sibling 2307 gave the Concord a senior figure (Veshen) at the
working edge. This audit gives them a founding artefact whose
doctrinal weight matches the Curation's R-7.

**Proposed lore-artifact: the Founder's Own register.**

A small hand-stitched book in the Northern chapter-house. Three
coloured threads in the binding, none reproducible by any
contemporary Concord workshop. Thirty-one pages: three filled
(*three loaves given. four people fed. the fourth: hand un-
named*), twenty-eight blank.

Doctrinal status: **closed against access**. Apprentices see it
once, across the room, in a glass case. Senior clerks may *read*
it once on the day of promotion to chapter-house seniority; they
may not copy it. Three living clerks have read it. Veshen has not
— she is Coastal, the register is Northern; chapter-house-of-
origin governs reading rights.

Three readings of the blank pages, doctrinally equal: Northern —
*the pages will fill when the fourth person identifies themselves.*
Southern — *the pages will list every clerk who ever served,
suddenly legible, on the day the founder's rate is honoured.*
Coastal — *the pages remain blank, and the blank is the page.*

**The Palimpsest revelation (bible:185).** A Palimpsest reader
holding the register would, by Echo Reading rules (bible:147), read
*the founder's hand and the fourth person's hand simultaneously*
— same charcoal, same page, same standing line. The reading
identifies the fourth person by hand.

The fourth person *was the founder.* The founder gave three loaves
and ate the fourth themselves, then left the page open. The
kindness was real. The un-named hunger was the founder's own —
*self-charging* (recording one's own consumption as a debt to
oneself) is a circular entry that closes immediately in primitive
Concord arithmetic. The first entry is *unbalanced as a kindness
and self-balanced as an accounting*. The founder is the Hand and
the un-fed mouth, simultaneously.

The Concord cannot close the Founder's Own because the counterparty
record exists in the same hand. If the player reveals the truth to
a Concord clerk, the Northern *kind founder* and Southern *firm
founder* doctrines cannot both honestly reckon with *the hungry
founder*; only Coastal's *no founder* survives intact. The Coastal
chapter-house quietly absorbs adherents from both other chapter-
houses for months following any walker who has read the register
and spoken. This is the most concrete *world-state-changing*
Palimpsest revelation in the corpus.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a)** Bible:21 says the cataclysm *"overwrote reality itself,
imperfectly"* — one-sided. Sibling 2220 has Concord doctrine: *"A
transaction. Something was given; something was taken."* The
standing-line doctrine (Dimension 4) closes the gap: cataclysm
overwrote at the *content* level; *at the accounting level*, the
standing line collected its arrears. Both true. **Recommend
margin gloss at bible:21:**

> Some factions recognise the cataclysm as *one-sided in content
> and reciprocal in account*. The standing-line doctrine of the
> Saccharine Concord is the most developed example.

**(b)** Sibling 2307 §4(a) recommends retaining
`RotChoir.json:338` (*"The Saccharine Concord offers sweetness"*)
as Choir-honest-within-ontology. This audit endorses and extends:
the Choir also flattens the three founder-stories to *one mouth,
unfed* (Dimension 3). The Choir does not perceive chapter-house
multiplicity; the walker should be permitted to feel this as a
limit of the speaker.

**(c)** The 2307 sibling's *Halen-Iss* (Coastal Concord) and
round-1's *Halen* (Curation Surveyor-11) share a given-name. 2307
treats this as a planting seed. **This audit canonises the
connection:** they are first cousins from the same Coastal fishing-
village. Curation Halen left at twelve to apprentice; Concord
Halen-Iss stayed, apprenticed at fifteen. They have not
corresponded since the death of Curation Halen's mother (*Mira* —
sibling 2220's planted name); the funeral was Curation, Halen-Iss
attended in Concord mourning, which the Curation tolerated without
remarking. **Sleeve R-11 is, by Curation Halen's silent design, a
family correspondence sleeve.** Halen-Iss does not know.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

A Concord travelling-clerk's docket. The bombshell parenthetically
embedded: the **Founder's Own grammar surfacing in field**, in a
hand the clerk does not know — and the **Halen family connection
in sleeve R-11**, in a withdrawn clause Halen-Iss has tried to
suppress but cannot fully delete (Dimension 6 — *(–)* is a Concord
convention, not an inkblock).

**Title (in-game):** *Saccharine Concord, Coastal chapter-house,
Halen-Iss travelling docket. Quarter 2, week 7.*

```
[Halen-Iss's hand. Price markers unusual enough that
 reconciliation has been deferred. Filed open-discrepancy.]

  TRAVELLING CLERK: Halen-Iss [accession 4]
  DOCKET:           14 entries (11 standard, 3 marginal)

  ENTRIES (excerpted, marginal lines only):

    Entry 9.   (0g) The bread was offered without payment to the
               traveller at the south road. The traveller did not
               ask the price. The traveller did not name themselves.

    Entry 11.  (–) The traveller did not return.

    Entry 14.  The receipt for entry 9 cannot be issued. The
               receipt-blank carries, on its reverse, a line in a
               hand I do not recognise. Quoted verbatim:

                  "(0g) thank you. there are three loaves. there
                  should be four."

               I have not entered this into any ledger. I have
               entered it into this docket, which is itself filed
               open-discrepancy. The Coastal director will read this
               on the seventh day. The director will not enter it.

  CLOSING:  Filed open-discrepancy. Founder's Own related, by grammar.

  POSTSCRIPT (lighter ink, written some weeks later):
            Veshen says hold the unknown coin from Mer Hin's
            day-book past the director's reading, past the next
            director, until I cannot. I am holding. I am also
            writing to Halen at sleeve R-11 about my mother.
            (–) (this last clause is withdrawn — H-I.)
```

The bombshell is parenthetical, in the syntax. The quoted line on
the receipt-blank's reverse uses gift-grammar correctly, in a hand
the clerk does not know — *the founder's grammar surfaces in
field*, by an unsigned hand. The traveller who took the loaf and
did not return is, by the corpus's working hypothesis, *the
founder, ongoing.* The withdrawn postscript clause about Halen at
R-11 plants the family connection: *(–)* withdraws the clause but
the docket preserves the withdrawal; a walker reading can read it
anyway.

A Palimpsest-attuned walker reading the docket *inside a Coastal
chapter-house* would, by sibling 2225's *time-release annotation*
mechanic, eventually receive a fourth clause from the Palimpsest
itself, written in slow ink between *(0g)* and *thank you*: a
single word, *Mira* — the mother's name Halen-Iss is writing
about.

Plant: a Coastal clerk's working desk. The docket's marginal lines
will be read aloud at Halen-Iss's eventual Closing of Accounts.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"The fourth loaf is the founder eating before charging
> themselves, and the Concord's entire accounting of the
> post-cataclysm world is built on the assumption that this
> entry closes."*

No archaisms, no apostrophe-fantasy names, no Tower / Aedra /
Daedra, no *verily / hath*. Stakes stated in plain accounting
verbs (*charging, closes, open-discrepancy*). A TES book attempting
the same beat would have written *"And when the Sweet Hand did
partake of the Fourth Loaf, in the hunger that no scribe did set
down, the very World's Compact trembled..."* Mine has no
*trembled*, no *Compact*, no *scribe*. Stakes are operationalised
in Concord-grammar consequence, not cosmic flourish. Pass.
