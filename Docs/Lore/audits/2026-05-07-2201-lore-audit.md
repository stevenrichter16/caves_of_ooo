>

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted (§3, the kiln-stone):** *"The kiln's
hearthstone is replaced cumulatively at every
rebuild — a working-finger's depth of new
bedding fitted to the old, never the whole stone,
never the whole interface — across the body's
full working-history."*

Working-modern register. *Kiln's hearthstone*,
*working-finger's depth*, *new bedding*, *fitted
to the old* — plain craft-clerical compounds,
lower-case, no archaism. *Replaced cumulatively*
and *fitted* are contemporary; no fronted
nominal, no *whereby / hath / lieth*.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"The Hearthstone
of the Kiln hath been Replaced, by such Cumulative
Working as the Kiln-Body doth permit at every
Rebuilding, a finger's-breadth of new Bedding
fitted unto the Old."* Tells: capitalised
*Hearthstone / Kiln-Body / Rebuilding / Bedding*;
*hath*, *doth permit*, *unto*; fronted *The
Hearthstone of the Kiln hath been Replaced*.
Actual sentence is none of these.

**Pass.**
