## 3. Faction asymmetry — Pale Curation rotates in

### Field ink and firm ink

The 1701 diary mentioned *field ink* and *firm ink* in passing
without doctrine. Sitting 13 lands the doctrine.

**Firm ink** is the iron-gall ink the Hall-Seventh accessions in.
It is brewed in the Hall, by a single appointed *ink-keeper*
(the senior-most standing-junior in the post-graduation cohort,
serving a year before promotion to standing-junior proper). The
batch is dated; every bottle is initialled by the ink-keeper;
every accession-card recorded in firm ink carries, somewhere on
its back, the ink-keeper's initial above the accessioner's.
Firm ink does not fade in archive conditions for two centuries
under measured trial. It also does not erase. A firm-ink stroke
is a standing record, period.

**Field ink** is anything else. Galls steeped in rainwater on a
two-day march; soot suspended in lamp-oil; the Pale Curation
officer's own blood, once, in a margin-note from the salt-cliff
expedition '93 (the officer was not censured; the firm record
was not corrupted; the margin survived); industrial chemical
inks bartered from the Saccharine Concord in the cold-month
shortages of '07. Field ink is *what was available.* It fades.
It runs. Some field inks erase entirely under sun. The Curation
treats this not as a failure but as a feature: **a margin-note
written in field ink declares its own provisionality.** A Hall
reader of '93's salt-cliff card knows by the ink alone that the
margin is the officer's working judgement, not the standing
record. The two cannot be confused because the substance refuses
to let them be.

The doctrine: the Curation's epistemology is in the ink. *Firm
record* is what the Hall stands behind under standing-senior
review. *Field record* is what the cataloguer saw before the
Hall stood behind anything. The cataloguer's working hand is
the soft outer layer of the artifact; the Hall's standing hand
is the bone. To compress field-ink margins into firm-ink record
without standing-senior review is the offence the Curation calls
**compression** — the worst rules-violation an officer can
commit. A delisting-by-compression is the only kind of delisting
the Hall logs to a separate ledger (the *compression-roll*,
shelved beside the impostor-ledger sitting 7 named).

### The cataloguer's coat

Mentioned at 1701 §3 (*"a small, working badge of having got the
first one right"*) without further detail. The coat is mid-grey,
heavy linen, hip-length, with seven external pockets (one per
Hall, with the Hall-Seventh's pocket worn at the dominant hip).
The cuff-stitch carries the standing-date once accession-day is
unreversed for a full year of standing-senior service. The
inside lining is plain unless the officer has filed a delisting
of an impostor, in which case the lining carries one black stitch
per standing-impostor logged — visible only to the officer and
the tailor. An officer with three lining-stitches has caught
three frauds; this is, in standing-senior tearoom register,
called *a quiet coat.* The phrase carries cellar-keeper-style
respect: a quiet coat says nothing on the outside.

### Cross-faction note

When the Choir composts a Pale Curation officer's body (rare;
the Curation cremates, but salt-cliff expeditions do not always
return the body), the spores read the coat as *the most legible
thing the body wears* — cuff-stitching, lining-stitches, and
firm-ink-stained pocket lining as readily as tree-rings. The
Choir cannot accession; it can only carry the shape. A walker
with the node may, rarely, *smell a quiet coat* without being
told what they are smelling. Neither side volunteers.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"This is what the cataclysm does. It does not erase. It
> scatters and quietly renames."*

Working register, Eldris's clerical voice, two flat declaratives
plus one slightly raised line. *Is what the cataclysm does* — the
register a senior accountant or senior archivist would use to
characterise an event without climbing the cosmic ladder.
*Scatters and quietly renames* — verb compound; *quietly* the only
softening; *renames* a clerical verb (the Curation renames things
all the time as part of its work).

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"Verily, this is the Wound the Sundering
hath wrought: not Erasure, but the Scattering of all Names, that
they be Spoken anew by Strangers' Mouths."* Tells: *Verily* (the
universal flag); *the Wound the Sundering hath wrought* (capital-
W-Wound, capital-S-Sundering, archaic verb); *the Scattering of
all Names* (capitalised abstract-noun mythicising the everyday
verb); *Strangers' Mouths* (apostrophe-fantasy + capitalised
collective-noun). The actual sentence: lower-case throughout, no
abstract-noun capitalisation, the verbs *scatter* and *rename*
are the same verbs a delisting-clerk would use about any twice-
filed object, and the metaphysical claim is delivered in the
register of a procedural observation.

Pass.
