## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

(05:01 worked the *genre* of cataclysm-record
each faction holds. Today: **what kind of fact
each tradition admits has been lost.**)

**Quote (bible L21):** *"the way text on a
medieval manuscript was scraped off and written
over with the old words still faintly visible
underneath."*

**Strong.** The bible commits that *something*
is partly readable.

**Thin.** Each faction presumably knows it has
*lost* particular kinds of fact. The bible does
not commit which kinds.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm,
append after the genre paragraph proposed
05:01 §2.1):**

> Each tradition admits a different kind of
> loss. The Choir lost *the names of things it
> has eaten* — working-line in soil, untied
> from forge. The Palimpsest lost *the verb-
> tense:* deep readings return nouns without
> finite grammar; a reader cannot tell if a
> remembered action was completed. The Pale
> Curation lost *the file-mark* — pieces with
> working-lines but no clause-line tying them
> to the shelf they should sit on. The
> Saccharine Concord lost *the closing-press,*
> clauses surviving without the disc that
> would have held them. The Brine Communion
> lost *the ninth bar;* every old-world song
> still carried breaks at the eighth. The
> Glassblown lost *the handing-off* — a master
> can finish a piece but not pass the kiln-
> discipline cleanly. The Thermoclaves lost
> *the first hearth-name;* every named fire
> the living remember was named *after.*

Seven losses, each register-specific, each
naming a *missing operator* rather than a
missing fact. The Thermoclave loss lands their
own §3.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

(05:01 set Concord *closure* with a three-
condition rule. Today: the Thermoclave
counterpart — what makes an *iron measure
clean.*)

**Thin.** The Thermoclave trade-form
(*measuring the iron,* per 05:01 §2.3) names a
working-form but the body of the form is
absent.

**Concrete enhancement (new bible subsection
§The Iron Measure, after §Concord Closure):**

> A Thermoclave measures iron clean by three
> tests at the hearth's working-heat.
> **First:** the piece rings at the watcher's
> tap with a tone the watcher recognises in
> the household's hearth-name. A piece whose
> ring does not hold a syllable of the name is
> *outside-iron,* tradeable but not adoptable.
> **Second:** the piece does not crack on
> three quenches. A first-quench crack is the
> metal's; a second, the watcher's; a third,
> the fire's — and a fire that has cracked
> iron three times has *signalled for a re-
> naming,* the second of the body's two
> ceremonies (the first being the year-end
> name-finding). **Third:** the piece does
> not *sing back* — when the watcher taps it
> again at standing-temperature the next
> morning, the piece is silent. A piece that
> sings back is *holding the fire,* not
> harmful and not tradeable; it is kept in the
> household, hung above the hearth, and is the
> only object permitted to share the fire's
> name. A walker who carries a singing-back
> piece off-hearth is carrying a household, in
> working-form, in their hand.

Three-test rule, two ceremonies, a
classification of pieces (outside-iron / hearth-
iron / singing-back), and a player-side load:
*the singing-back piece is a portable hearth-
name.*

### 2.7 Anthropological framing

(05:01 set the Concord endorsement-rite.
Today: **Thermoclave mobility** — the load-
bearing 00:01 phrase *"even when the house is
moving."*)

**Thin.** The 00:01 audit committed *the
house can move* in passing. The bible has not
absorbed this.

**Concrete enhancement (new bible subsection
§The Hearth-frame, after §The Iron Measure):**

> A Thermoclave dwelling is a *hearth-frame:*
> a low timber-and-iron house built around a
> portable fire-vessel, the *cradle.* The
> cradle is iron, three-handed, and is the
> household's only structurally-immovable
> piece — except that four to six times in a
> household's working-life the cradle *moves.*
> The household disassembles the hearth-frame
> around the lit cradle, carries the cradle
> without dousing the fire, and reassembles
> the frame at the new site. Moves are
> seasonal (summer high-ground, winter coast)
> or ceremonial (a watcher's first child
> takes their first cradle-shift, marked by a
> move). A move that doused the fire would be
> *the long minute* in miniature; a move that
> succeeds is the household's only standing
> test of itself across a working-life. Long
> cradles accumulate working-line on the rim —
> small marks per carry — and a full-rimmed
> cradle is *retired* as a household piece,
> fire put out ceremonially, and a new cradle
> named into being from a piece that has been
> *singing back* for a generation.

Mobile architecture; a portable fire-vessel
named the *cradle;* a working-line accumulating
on iron; a path from singing-back-piece (§2.4)
to next-generation cradle. Lands the 00:01
phrase as canon.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclaves

(Rotation target. The 00:01 sitting did the
hearth-name rite and the long minute. Tonight:
**the first hearth-name** (§2.1's
Thermoclave-loss) and the watcher-line that
keeps it.)

**The watcher-line.** Every Thermoclave
household traces its watchers in a list —
unfiled, kept by recital. The eldest watcher
recites the line at the year-end name-finding,
before the new fire-name is heard. Lines are
short (rarely more than nine names): each
name is the named-watcher of a single fire
across one fire's life. A watcher of two
fires is a *long-watcher;* three, a *line-
keeper;* four is unrecorded — no line-keeper
has named four, the discipline says, because
a fourth naming would be *pressing the year
against itself* and the year cannot be so
pressed.

**The first hearth-name.** A watcher-line ends
not at the founder of the household but at a
position the Thermoclaves call *the first
hearth.* The first hearth's name is not
recited; the recital uses *the first* as
placeholder. Some lines say the name was
given; some say it was not given because the
fire arrived already named. The Thermoclaves
have not authorised either account. The
bible's §2.1 loss-claim — *we lost the first
hearth-name* — is therefore not a lost fact in
the standing accounting; it is the fact whose
loss is the body's working-position. A fire
whose name *resonated* with the first
hearth's name (a coincidence the watcher would
hear) would be a re-finding the body has no
working-form to receive.

**The cradle's working-line.** A long
household's cradle bears a working-line on the
rim — small marks per watcher per move (§2.7).
The line is read backward by the next watcher
on each move; the cradle is therefore the
household's only piece whose working-line
*grows* in the body's working-life rather than
arriving complete. A cradle's line is not the
watcher-line — it is the *moving-line.*

**Player-side hook.** A walker carrying a
piece that *sings back* (§2.4) at a tone the
watcher recognises in their own hearth-name
is presenting a re-finding the body has no
working-form to receive (above). The watcher
will not trade, test, or speak; they sit at
the cradle, palm-down, until the walker
leaves. A walker who reads the silence well
enough to leave the piece on the cradle's
rim has performed an act the Thermoclaves
have no working-form for. The piece is not
retrieved.

Watcher-line, first-hearth, cradle's
working-line, the player can carry a singing-
back piece into a hearth and present the
faction with a working-form they have no
working-form to receive — late-game, this is
the Thermoclave equivalent of the §2.8
P-disc moment. The body's *only* unauthorised
gesture.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Bible (L208):** *"the way the Thermoclaves
and Brine Communion do."*

**The contradiction.** The bible commits the
Thermoclaves and Communion *hate* each other.
The Brine mariner of the 00:01 ledger calls
the keepers *tired of us* and treats the long
minute as a working condition, not a hatred —
and crosses the keepers off *people we cannot
work with.* Hatred is the bible's omniscient
register; *tiredness* is the in-world working-
form. The bible's word over-renders.

**Proposed fix (revise L208):**

> They are not at war. They don't hate each
> other the way the Thermoclaves and Brine
> Communion do — though *hate* is the bible's
> word for what the in-world parties call,
> respectively, *the count* and *the song,*
> and the working-condition between them is
> not enmity but a tiredness so old it has
> become a *working-form for not-closing.*
> The Concord (§2.8) maintains the only
> diplomatic instrument for it. The bible's
> *hatred* survives in this passage as a
> register-error neither Thermoclave nor
> Communion would speak in.

Demotes *hate* to a register-mismatch and
elevates *tiredness* to the working-form it
already is in 00:01's mariner. The bible's
omniscient voice corrects itself, in
character.

## 5. The "merchant's diary" suggestion

A Thermoclave watcher's hearth-log: the daily
working-form a watcher keeps for their named
fire. The bombshell — that the watcher has
heard a piece in a walker's pack *sing back at
a tone the watcher recognises in their hearth-
name* (§3 player-hook) — lands in a working-
form so terse the watcher does not name what
they are recording.

> **Hearth-log, household Stenu-Three, watcher
> Mer Stenu, second of three. Fire-name: ash-
> evening (long-form). Year four of nine.**
>
> *Day 41.* Quiet through the working-day.
> Coal at second-shift even, no crack. Aunt
> Soren came for second meal; greeted ash-
> evening as *evening,* which is correct for
> second-meal household-presence. Did not ask
> after the cradle. The cradle has not asked
> after her. The household is well.
>
> *Day 42.* A walker in for water at the road-
> edge. Did not enter the hearth-frame. Was
> carrying a piece in the upper bag — small
> bronze, not a knife — that *sang back* at
> the road-tap when I struck the post-iron at
> standing-temperature. The piece sang in a
> tone I am not prepared to put in this log.
> I will return to the household and not
> tap the post-iron at standing-temperature
> again until I have spoken with Soren and
> with the long-watcher Korn-Ault. This entry
> is the working-form for *I have heard
> something I do not have a working-form for.*
> The walker did not see me strike. I do not
> believe the piece sang to me; I believe I
> heard the piece sing to itself. I was in
> the way.
>
> *Day 43.* Quiet through the working-day.
> Coal at second-shift even, no crack. I have
> not tapped the post-iron. Soren has not yet
> arrived. The fire is well.
>
> *Day 44.* The fire is well. — M.S.

(~265 words.) Reveal: the watcher has heard a
walker's piece in a tone she will not name —
and the working-form she uses for *I have heard
something the working-form does not have a
working-form for* is itself unwritten,
deployed once, followed by three days of *the
fire is well* that on rereading is the
watcher holding the fire steady through a
fact she has not yet placed. Two side-facts
land parenthetically: a watcher-line cross-
reference to a second household (*long-watcher
Korn-Ault*), and the walker is the same one
whose working-line refuses to read in the
04:01 ledger. The piece is presumably the
bronze button of L-K-1. The button has been
singing back.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

Quoting one sentence:

> *"This entry is the working-form for I have
> heard something I do not have a working-form
> for."*

TES? No. *Working-form* is project-internal;
*I have heard* is a watcher's first-person
clerical register, not a mythological voice;
the recursion is the point — a working-form
admitting it is the working-form for the
absence of a working-form. No *verily,* no
inverted clause, no apostrophe-laden noun, no
appeal to gods or saints. Reads as a careful
watcher's hearth-log by a person aware that
the log will be read by the long-watcher next
working-quarter. No rewrite.
