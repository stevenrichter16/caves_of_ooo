## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible, line 23:* "Some try to preserve what was lost. Some try to
> consume it and build from the remains. Some try to ignore it. Some are
> products of it."

**Strong.** Cataclysm has five competing accounts (JSON:481). 1813 added
intra-Choir schism (surface vs. deep voice). 1901 added pre-cataclysm
period-stratification.

**Thin.** The corpus stratifies the **before** and the **during** but
treats the **between** as a single grey expanse. Six-hundred years of
Choir patience is asserted (bible:47) and never narrated. Faction
emergence has no chronology.

**Concrete addition (bible, new short subsection after line 25, "The
Long Between"):**

> Of the six hundred years between the breaking and the present, every
> faction tells the same story and means a different one. The Choir
> calls it *the Slow Tasting* and dates events by mycelial depth. The
> Pale Curation calls it *the Recovery Phases*, I through VII, with a
> disputed VIIIᵇ. The Saccharine Concord refuses to name the period —
> nothing was sold then that anyone now remembers buying. The Brine
> Communion divides it into seven *low tides*, each ending when a
> particular bird returned. The Thermoclaves measure it in burns: nine,
> or eleven if small ones count. The Palimpsest reads the period as a
> single very long sentence with the punctuation worn off.

Six historiographies in one paragraph. No additional canon required.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** Cataclysm-names; outsider-names for Choir/Palimpsest/
substrate/player (1813, 1901).

**Thin.** No faction yet has its own word for **the act of joining**.
A culture's word for *initiation* tells you what it thinks happens
during that ceremony.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "What each faction calls becoming
one of them"):**

> - **Rot Choir:** *the long taste*. Not an event but a duration the
>   joiner gradually stops noticing.
> - **Palimpsest:** *the first reading*. The moment a person first
>   reads themselves as a layer rather than as a person. The Palimpsest
>   is not certain anyone has *finished* the first reading.
> - **Pale Curation:** *accession*. New members are tagged, classed,
>   and shelved alongside their predecessors — the same verb used for
>   objects.
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *first sweetness*. The newcomer is paid a
>   single piece of candied bark. Eating it is the contract. Refusing
>   it is also the contract — the Concord has shipped both forms.
> - **Brine Communion:** *the going-under*. New members spend a night
>   submerged in a sealed tide-pool with one Communion member who does
>   not speak, only breathes nearby. Survivors do not describe what was
>   decided. Initiates who do not survive are described as *having
>   decided otherwise*.
> - **Thermoclaves:** *first quench*. A new member is briefly held
>   under cold water by an older one. The older one decides when.

Brine *going-under* and Thermoclave *quench* mirror — same element,
opposite doctrines.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

> *Bible, line 138:* "A Choir player becomes a better organism. A
> Palimpsest player becomes a better observer."

**Strong.** Choir somatic / Palimpsest noetic. 1901 added Choir
digestion rules.

**Thin.** Echo Reading has no documented limits (bible:147). Without
limits the power is magic; with limits it is cosmology. *Can the player
read another reader? A Choir fruiting body? Yesterday's object?*

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection under The Palimpsest, after
line 149, "What Echo Reading cannot read"):**

> Echo Reading touches only objects that have *settled* into having a
> history. A blade sharpened this morning reads as a flat bright hum —
> too recent. A blade last touched in anger thirty years ago reads
> cleanly. The reader waits for the present to thin.
>
> Echo Reading cannot read other readers — the experience is mutually-
> recursive and unpleasant, and Palimpsest-attuned beings do not do it
> twice.
>
> Echo Reading cannot read Choir mycelium directly. The Choir is too
> distributed and too currently-eating; the reading returns a single
> overwhelming chord with no narrative. (Surveyors call this *the wall
> hums back*.) The Choir's *prey* — bones consumed and woven into a
> wall — read normally, with the digestion as a slow swallowing motion
> in the background.
>
> Echo Reading cannot read what the Palimpsest has annotated. The
> annotation is the only history available; the original is overwritten
> by being marked. The Palimpsest considers this acceptable. Some
> readers do not.

Annotation is a one-way erasure — the irony the bible dodges.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** Mirror endings; thresholds (1813); Annihilist refusals (1901).

**Thin.** No documented **partial / interrupted** apotheosis. A player
who takes the node and refuses Exalted is in lore-limbo. The bible says
this should feel complete (line 224) — but doesn't say what it *is*.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection after line 218, "The Stalled
Walkers"):**

> Some walkers accept the node and never proceed. They are not refusers
> — they have been changed — but they are not pilgrims either. The
> Choir calls them *the patient ones* and is genuinely patient; the
> spores feed them quietly for years and ask nothing. Other walkers
> see them in the small hours, sitting still longer than people are
> usually still. They sleep less. They remember conversations they did
> not have. They are not unhappy. They are also not coming back to the
> question.
>
> The same shape exists on the Palimpsest side. Walkers who learn Echo
> Reading and stop there are called *the listening ones*. They touch
> walls before entering rooms, apologise to objects for moving them,
> pause at thresholds long enough that other walkers go around them.
> Also not unhappy. Also not finished.
>
> The design rule that the moderate path should feel like cowardice
> (line 218) does not apply to the stalled. Cowardice requires a choice
> the stalled have already made, halfway, and not unmade.

Half-apotheosis is the most common real end-state. The lore should
hold a place for it that isn't *failure*.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Choir pronouns; the five voices' grammars; Palimpsest
grammar (1813: perfect tense, no *I*).

**Thin.** Brine Communion has no documented register. By rotation, this
is the run that pays it.

**Concrete addition (bible, "Brine Communion Grammar Rules" subsection):**

> The Brine Communion conjugates verbs against the **tide**, not the
> speaker. Most sentences open with a tide-mark — *low, the boat
> returns*; *full, we sing*; *turning, you ask*. Omitted tide-marks
> read as a foreigner's mistake or as *uncertain*, by context.
>
> Three first-person pronouns: *I-now* (present moment, shallowest),
> *I-then* (remembering, deeper), *we-of-the-deep* (ritual; the
> Communion's longer memory). Switching mid-sentence is unremarkable.
> *Who is speaking* is a question with multiple correct simultaneous
> answers.
>
> No future tense. Future events are spoken imperative — *we go*, not
> *we will go* — because the future the Communion takes seriously is
> the one being acted toward, and a sentence that does not act toward
> it is not, to them, about it.

A Brine NPC's first line writes itself: *low, the visitor arrives.
I-now greet. I-then was a fisher; we-of-the-deep are still. Sit, if
the tide allows.*

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Strong.** Tuesday Morning so far: Choir, Palimpsest (1813); Concord (1901).

**Thin.** Brine Communion still owed (per rotation).

**Concrete addition (bible, "Tuesday Morning" section, new subsection):**

> **The Brine Communion.** *Food:* whatever the tide gave back. The
> Communion does not fish the falling tide — that is taking. They fish
> the rising tide, on the grounds that what is brought is offered.
> *Greeting:* a lifted palm, slightly wet. Palm-water is from a glass
> at every dwelling's entrance, refilled at high tide. A dry palm
> implies the visit is too short for the tide to matter. *Naming
> custom:* two names given at the going-under — a *shore-name* used
> by outsiders, a *deep-name* used only in ritual and never written.
> Learning a member's deep-name unbidden is the gravest Communion
> offence. *Mourning:* the body is rowed out at the next high tide
> and released. No marker. The deep-name is spoken aloud by everyone
> present; the shore-name is not. The shore-name belonged to the
> world; the deep-name belonged to the deep, and the deep is taking
> it back. *Currency:* no coin. Trade in *tide-debts* — promises
> measured in tide-turns, due at the agreed high tide. Not enforceable;
> remembered. A defaulter is named, once, *one-the-deep-did-not-keep*,
> and not refused trade afterward, but not invited under either.

Currency-by-memory inverts the Concord's currency-by-food. Two
factions quietly contesting what *value* even is.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine Communion

The Brine is named at JSON:481 and in 1813's Kesti-ledger ("a Brine
boy"). 1901 gave them *the under-tide* (Palimpsest) and *the deep*
(substrate). Sections 6–7 above gave grammar and Tuesday morning.

**Proposed lore-artifact: a salvaged book with Communion marginalia.**

The Brine read what washes up. Most of it is pre-cataclysm — laminated,
water-resistant in ways the present cannot replicate. The Communion does
not throw away a salvaged book even if it is unreadable. They write in
the margins of what they cannot read; the margin-writing is the
relationship.

```
[A salt-warped pre-cataclysm book. The body is illegible: the
 paper has fused into a single brick. The margins are still
 readable — the Brine writer used a pen the salt did not eat.]

Inside front cover, in pencil:
  Found: low tide, third moon, after the storm that took
  Pell's roof. Book is closed. I will not open it. The
  closing is the book.

Margin, page edge, ink:
  Full, this page wants to be read. I-now cannot read it.
  I-then could not read it either. We-of-the-deep are
  patient.

Margin, midbook, different ink, different hand:
  Turning, the under-tide reads it for us. I have asked.
  The under-tide will not say what it reads. This is
  correct of it.

Margin, near the back, the first hand again, much later:
  Low, the book is heavier. I weighed it. Three tides ago
  it was 1100 grams. Today it is 1140. Nothing was added.
  Books do not gain weight from sitting. The deep is
  putting something into it.
  We will return it at the next full tide.
  We will not write in the margin again.

Inside back cover, very small:
  Returned. Book did not sink. The deep would not take it.
  We have it back. It is heavier still.
  The under-tide is laughing. Which is also correct of it.
```

Lore landed: the Brine treat the **Palimpsest** as a literate party
that reads with them; pre-cataclysm objects can **gain mass** near
substrate-leakage (new mechanic, Choir-adjacent); the Brine have a
**return ritual** for what the deep refuses; the under-tide *laughs*,
which neither Choir nor Curation believes of the Palimpsest. Plant in
Communion rafters, a salvager's crate, or a Curation Tier-3 vault
mis-classed *water-damaged, unreadable*. The mass-gain is the bombshell.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a) The Choir says it eats *time*. The bible never addresses this.**

> *RotChoir.json:107:* "We eat everything. Flesh, stone, metal, time."

A faction that eats time is temporal. The bible's somatic-vs-noetic
opposition (line 137) rests on the Choir being non-temporal. Three
readings: *hyperbole* (time = memory-of-things); *literal* (Choir owes
a mechanic); *Choir misunderstanding* (cannot distinguish object from
its centuries — most interesting; JSON stands, bible glosses). Pick one.

**(b) The Palimpsest's Overwrite power *erases living things* — and
the bible doesn't flag the irony.**

> *Bible, line 173:* "anything currently living there is overwritten
> too. A village built in ruins comes back as the original building.
> The village and its people are erased — not killed, simply never were."

The preservation faction's ultimate power deletes the present. The
bible introduces this without remarking on it. Land it on purpose:

> The Palimpsest does not consider this killing. The Choir, asked,
> agrees: *killing requires that someone was there to be killed.*
> This is one of the few points on which the two cosmic-scale
> entities concur, and they concur from opposite ends of the same
> argument.

Two cosmic opposites agreeing on something is the sharpest available
scene. The bible can have it for one sentence.

**(c) Salt: bible says the Choir cannot cross sterile flats (line 48);
JSON says it eats *everything* (107) with no salt exception.** 1901's
salt-aversion proposal is not in the bible. Adopt the 1901 subsection
or have the tendril revise: *"we eat almost everything; salt knows
its own name and is not us."*

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

Where to plant: a Thermoclave fire-keeper's hut, in the oilskin pouch
the keeper uses for tinder. The Thermoclaves are the most undeveloped
faction in the corpus; this artifact gives them a working metabolism
mostly parenthetically.

**Title (in-game):** *Tally of fires kept, eleventh keeping, Renne the
younger.*

> Eleventh keeping. The pit ran cold twice this week. I rebuilt the
> draw-stones on the south side. Fire wanted air more than fuel. Fire
> always wants what you do not yet have.
>
> Burned: three armfuls dry pine, two of the green oak the river-people
> traded us, one bundle of the black salt-wood Keeper Aud insists is
> wood. (It is not wood. Aud knows. I know. We burn it anyway because
> Aud is older and the fire takes it.)
>
> Quenched: one initiate. Held under at fourth bell, up at fifth. He
> coughed, sat, drank. One of us now. He did not cry, which is correct,
> and did not laugh, which is also correct. The ones who laugh I have
> seen later choose otherwise.
>
> The Brine boy came again. He is allowed the outer ring. He brought
> dry beans and two questions. The first I answered: yes, we keep nine
> fires; no, the tenth has not been lit since before the first keeping
> and will not be lit by me. The second I did not answer — he asked
> whether the deep was the same thing as the inside of a fire. I told
> him fire and water are arguments and I do not have time for arguments
> in a working pit. He left a bean on the stone before he went. I have
> not eaten it. I do not know if it is for me or for the fire.
>
> Aud has been quiet since the last burn. I think she misses the tenth.
> I will not ask.

Lore landed: the Thermoclaves keep **nine fires and one untended
tenth** — ordinary to the keeper, significant in Aud's silence; the
Section-3 *quench* ritual appears as practice; the **Brine wonders
whether fire and water are the same substance**, asked of the wrong
faction; *salt-wood* burns despite not being wood — a silent gesture
toward Choir salt-aversion (1901) and the mass-gaining book (Section 3).

The tenth fire is the lore. The bean on the stone is the seam.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

Quoting one of my own sentences and verifying it does not read as a TES
book:

> *"The Communion treats who is speaking as a question with multiple
> correct simultaneous answers."*

No archaisms. No invented proper nouns. No apostrophe-clusters. No
*verily*, no *by [deity]*, no Towers, no Aedra. The sentence is a
grammatical observation rendered as a doctrinal one — the project's
native move, where vocabulary *is* metaphysics. A TES book would have
written *"and the speaker, by sacred custom, is held to be manifold
and singular at once, as the wisdom of the Brine doth teach."* Mine
reads like a linguistic field-note. Confirmed.
