## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible line 21:* *"It did not merely destroy the old civilization
> — it overwrote reality itself, imperfectly, the way text on a
> medieval manuscript was scraped off and written over."*

**Strong.** The cataclysm-account is canonically a *partial-erasure*
event. Brine's deep-kept and Concord's standing-closed are
structurally about *what cannot be re-opened*.

**Thin.** No account of the cataclysm in **Concord clause-aspect**.
The Brine has its sea-account; the Choir has compost-as-history;
the Palimpsest has the under-text. The Concord — the body whose
working life is *closing what is open* — has no doctrine of *what
the body did when the cataclysm was open and could not be closed*.

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar, "The first standing-open
clause"):

> Concord doctrine names the cataclysm *the first standing-open
> clause* — the founding case the body could not procedurally
> close because every potential witness was either in the under-
> text (Palimpsest), composted (Choir), deep-kept (Brine), or
> folded-out (Glassblown). The clause is filed in cabinet A,
> shelf zero, card zero. It is not turned. The Concord's working
> doctrine is that *every closure since is a partial closure of
> A/0/0 by analogy*.
>
> Senior clerks who have served forty years are entitled, once, to
> read the card. They report it is blank-faced and that the
> witness-field reads *the four bodies, by absence*. The ink is
> faded. The card has not been re-inked. The Concord interprets
> the fading as *the body's only confession that the founding
> closure was never complete*.

History stratifies *as procedure*: the deeper back, the more the
working-day clauses are partial closures of clauses the Concord
inherits as standing-open. The cataclysm itself is the body's only
clause whose closure is *doctrinally deferred to the next forty-
year senior*.

> the body's main hall, kept at a Brine-leased harbour-room. The

> joint clerk-and-Brine pair; neither alone may turn a card.
>

> surfacing event. On surfacing, two clerks countersign with the
> Brine convener; the clause closes *standing-closed-by-sea*. The
> particle is *-os* in spoken Concord, the only sentence-final

The corpus's first **time-shared closure-instrument**: a clause
whose closing event is not on the Concord's calendar but on the
