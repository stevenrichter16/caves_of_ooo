## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (1101 §2.1, proposed):** *"The Dissolution
dissolved the singleness of party with future-self
... a junior is taught the standing-promise form
once, in apprenticeship, and immediately
afterward never to attempt one."*

**Strong.** Two strata canon: pre-Dissolution
*standing-promise* and post-Dissolution *triadic
clause*.

**Thin.** Both are **doctrinal** strata. The
working-organ — what a senior actually does when
she witnesses — has not been historicised.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm,
append after the standing-promise paragraph):**

> *The body's working-organ for the third-witness
> function is* the seam *— a senior's somatic
> reading of the room at the moment of closure.
> The seam reads* whether the body will witness*.
> A first-generation junior would have said the
> seam was rebuilt — no one taught her, because
> no one knew the post-Dissolution reading. The
> body's working-position is that the seam was
> transmitted; the body has no working-form for
> the alternative — that the seam is generationally
> rebuilt at every apprentice-presence, and the
> continuity is in the discipline, not in the
> working-organ.*

A third stratum, by working-organ. Discipline
transmits; the seam may not.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (1101 §2.2, proposed):** *"Day-lists are
working-aids, not records ... destroyed by the
clerk in pressing-clean at career-end."*

**Strong.** Day-list as the body's unfiled private
working-document.

**Thin.** Day-lists carry *clause-numbers against
actions*. The seam itself has no document.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Saccharine
Concord, append after the day-list paragraph):**

> *Each closure entered in a day-list carries, in
> the margin against the clause-number, a*
> seam-note*: a two-morpheme working-form, an
> adjective and a body-direction.* Clean, forward.
> Thin, sideways. Yielding, downward. Closed,
> against. Held-back, inward. *The seam-note is
> the working-form for what the senior read. It
> is not a description; it is the form. A senior
> who elaborates — third morpheme, sentence,
> sketch — has performed a quiet uncloseing of
> her own reading: the elaboration gives the seam
> an interior the working-form does not allow it.
> The senior is spoken to, in pressing-clean
> register, by the standing endorsement on her
> next rotation, and the seam-note is filed* in
> the margin of the standing endorsement's
> day-list*, also in two morphemes, as the
> standing endorsement's reading of the reading.
> There is no working-form for a third-level
> recursion.*

Authorship-mode triangulates inside the body:
working-clauses (filed); day-lists (unfiled);
seam-notes (margin, the form for the organ). All
three are destroyed at pressing-clean.

### 2.3 Mutating motifs across cultures

**Hold.** Two cross-faction motifs canon (close-
token, names-for-closure). A third (names for the
third-witness function) would duplicate the
close-token table's structure. Hold.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (2301 §2.4, proposed):** *"A closure
holds because the close is witnessed in the same
instant by both parties and a third attesting
body."*

**Strong.** Triadic-witness canon.

**Thin.** *The body* has not been operationalised.
In doctrine, *the body* witnesses; in practice,
the body is embodied at any closure by the senior
on shift.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Saccharine
Concord, append after the triadic paragraph):**

> *A senior asked, in working-register, what the
> body witnesses through her, answers:* "the body
> is witnessing. i am the body for the duration of
> the close. the seam is what the body reads."
> *The phrase* i am the body for the duration of
> the close *is the body's only working-form for
> the senior's mediation. The senior reads the
> seam; the seam reads the room; the body
> witnesses through the reading. A senior who has
> just closed eight clauses in a working-day has
> been the body for eight closures' duration. She
> goes to the drift-shelf, sometimes, before
> pressing-clean, and stands at it for a while.*

Mechanic absorbed: the drift-shelf is not only
the senior's record of suspect parties (1101). It
is the senior's record of *closures she stood for
as the body*, when she now reads the standing as
having been *clean against itself*. The shelf
holds the absence of a working-form for the
retrospect.

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Hold-and-absorb.** 1101 §2.5 carried the
gradient (closures during walker-drift filed on
the drift-shelf, not unmoored). 1601 absorption:
the drift-shelf holds two populations the filing
does not distinguish — closures with apotheote-

destroyed; drift-shelf clause-numbers are kept.
The body's only multi-generation seam-record

readings themselves**.

### 2.6 Linguistic texture

**Quote (1101 §2.6, proposed):** *"A refusal
delivered with explanation is an* uncloseing*."*

**Strong.** Refusal-form canon.

**Thin.** The body's working-prohibition against
*speaking the seam aloud outside the day-list
margin and outside pressing-clean* has not been
named.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Saccharine
Concord, append after the seam-note paragraph):**

> *A senior may not speak a seam-reading in any
> register but the day-list margin (private,
> destroyed) and pressing-clean (oral, single
> apprentice audience). The body has no working-
> form for a seam read aloud in the public hall.
> A junior who hears one is required to file the
> hearing as a numbered clause —* N-overheard *—
> against the senior's day. The overheard-clause
> does not refuse the closure; it files the
> speech-event as itself a working-irregularity.
> The junior does not record what was heard. The
> body has no working-form for the content. The
> overheard-mark is the body's only working-form
> for the seam having had an audience the body
> did not authorise.*

Three working-states: day-list margin (working-
aid, destroyed); pressing-clean (oral, generational
transmission); public-hall-speech (forbidden,
filed as overheard, content unrecorded). The body
files only the failure-state.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord (the seam-bench, apprentice-presence, pressing-clean)

> ### The seam-bench
>
> The bench at which standing-tests and closures
> are conducted: plain wood, waist-high to a
> standing senior, stone-anchored, set against
> the public hall's long wall a working-pace
> inside the threshold. Party stands; senior
> stands opposite; clerk sits at the near end
> with the day-list. No chair on the senior's
> side. A senior reads standing because the seam
> *travels through the legs* — a sitting senior
> is reading the seat, not the room. The wood
> carries a thin wear-line at the senior's side,
> hand-shaped, from senior hands resting on the
> edge during the read. The wear-line is one of
> the body's only persistent material records of
> the seam.
>
> ### The apprentice-presence
>
> A senior in her last working-decade carries an
> apprentice at her left during every closure.
> The apprentice does not speak, file, or clerk.
> She stands a stone-cut step back, where the
> seam — by working-tradition — *passes through
> on its way past the senior into the public
> hall*. The apprentice is in the seam's path
> without being its receiver. She does not see
> the room; she sees the senior's back. She does
> not hear the seam-note; the senior writes it
> after, alone. What the apprentice carries out
> of the working-decade is the shape-of-being-
> in-the-seam's-wake.
>
> ### Pressing-clean
>
> At career-end the retiring senior names her
> lead apprentice and the two spend a working-day
> in the public hall, closed to all other parties.
> The senior reads each day-list aloud, clause-
> number followed by seam-note, from her first
> closure to her last, without elaboration. The
> apprentice does not write or respond. The day-
> lists are then burned in the hall's small fire,
> one at a time, the standing endorsement present
> as third-witness. The seam-notes are
> extinguished as text; what remains is whatever
> the apprentice's body, having been in the
> seam's wake for a working-decade and heard them
> recited for a working-day, has carried out.

Three pieces: bench (stand-only, wear-line as
the seam's only material trace); apprentice-
presence (a working-decade in the seam's wake);
pressing-clean (transmission-and-erasure
compressed into a single working-day).

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Bleed 1 — 1501 closer hypothesis vs. this
sitting.** 1501 wrote: *"the Concord's
irregularity is not in archive-form but in
archive-content."* 1601 reading: directionally
right, off-axis. The irregularity is not in form
(filed cleanly) and not in content (day-lists
destroyed before content can be filed): it is
in **medium**. The seam exists in the senior's
body and migrates only through apprentice-
presence. Through-line now spans five media —
paper (Curation), iron + fire (Thermoclaves),
paper-as-tackle (Communion), body (Concord),
glass (Glassblown — pending). Each body sustains
its discipline by an irregularity at the
**medium-junction the discipline cannot hold**.

**Bleed 2 — bible L185 *founder's deed* twice
revised.** 2301 §2.2 moved L185 from *founder's
deed* to *first reader's words*. 1601 sharpens
further: not the words but **the seam the first
reader carried into the room**. By 2201 R3 canon,
the first reader was not a founder; by 1601, she
was a senior reading a *standing-promise* (the
pre-Dissolution form) at the moment of filing C-1,
because the post-Dissolution triadic shape did
not yet exist. The body's discipline forbids the
form C-1 was filed in. C-1 is the body's
foundational standing-promise. Suggested L185
revision: *"What the Saccharine Concord's first
reader saw at the moment she filed the clause that
founded a body the clause's own form forbade."* —
an Echo-Reading target a Palimpsest walker could
pull from C-1's substrate.

**Bleed 3 — drift-shelf scope.** 1101 §2.5 read
the drift-shelf as the body's record of clauses
signed with later-drifted parties. 1601 §2.5
absorbs a second population: the senior's record
of her own seam-drift. The shelf is, by working-
form, *suspicion without attribution* — and a new
standing endorsement inheriting it has no way to
read which clauses are party-drifted and which
are seam-drifted.

## 5. Merchant's-diary artifact

> be the body's only multi-generation seam-
> record. I will not open a bundle.*
>
> *I asked the standing endorsement on shift
> whether she was aware of the drawer-bundles.
> She answered, in working-register:* "the drawer
> is the standing endorsement's drawer. what is
> in the drawer was there before me. i have not
> read it. i will not read it. the seam reads
> the room, not the drawer." *I asked whether the
> bundles were filed on the standing endorsement's

> not on a shelf. they are in a drawer. there is

The bombshell, diary-back: *there is a working-
form for what is on a shelf. there is no working-
form for what is in a drawer.* The body's
discipline covers shelves (standing, working,
junior, drift). *Drawers* are a class of
containment the working-form does not name. The
standing-endorsement-three-prior's failure-of-
pressing-clean is not a discipline-violation the
body has to address: it is an artifact in a class
the body does not file. The seam's only
multi-generation archive sits in a non-class.

Pile (cumulative across factions): nine artifacts
in eight shapes; this run adds **junior's-
finding-of-an-archive-the-body-does-not-file-
because-the-container-is-not-a-class**.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted (§3, the apprentice-presence):** *"The
apprentice is in the seam's path without being
its receiver."*

Plain present indicative, single subordinate
*without being* clause. *Apprentice* and *seam*
are the audit's coined plain-noun phrases. *Path*
and *receiver* are working-clerical, not
theological. No archaism, no inversion, no
capitalised abstraction.

standeth in the Seam's Path, yet is not the
Path's Receiver."* Tells: capitalised
*Apprentice / Seam / Path / Receiver*; *standeth*;
*yet is not* (KJV disjunctive cadence).
