## 2. Depth audit, eight dimensions

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (bible L21):** *"Every faction in the game is defined by
their relationship to this event."*

**Strong:** *Time-since-the-cataclysm* now has texture (the 1301
notebook dated *Year 387 since the Severance*, Pale Curation's
internal calendar).

**Thin:** Exactly *one* dating system. The bible never says
several factions count differently — and several should. A
worldview that rejects naming the event rejects counting from it.
Right now *time itself* is implicitly Curation-shaped.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm, after 1301's §2.1
addition):**

> *No two factions agree on when the cataclysm ended, and several
> deny it has. The Pale Curation count from a single recorded year
> they call the Severance and append every subsequent year to it.
> The Saccharine Concord count from the year of their founder's
> first sweetening and pretend the years before did not happen.
> The Brine Communion do not count years at all, only tides — they
> believe the cataclysm is still ongoing in the deep water and
> will be over only when the sea stops salting itself. The
> Thermoclaves count generations of fire and consider any other
> count an outsider's question.*

Five ways of dating the same world, each motivated. Also extends
the 1301 Thermoclaves-do-not-name claim into a parallel non-
counting position.

### 2.3 Mutating motifs across cultures

**Quote (JSON L338):** *"The Saccharine Concord offers sweetness.
The Pale Curation offers records. The Glassblown Remnant offers
nothing at all."*

**Strong:** 1301 enhanced the *factional names for the cataclysm*
into a 7-way table.

**Thin:** Other repeating motifs are still 2-way or 0-way.
Specifically: *the player.* The Choir calls them *walker.* The
Palimpsest, by inference, calls them a *reader.* The other five
factions have no settled term.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §How They View Each Other or a new
§What They Call Walkers):**

> *Each faction has a word for the kind of person who shows up
> alone, armed, and curious. The Choir says* walker *, after the
> footsteps that press the soil. The Palimpsest says* reader *, or,
> in formal address,* the one who underlines. *The Saccharine
> Concord says* customer *, and means it without irony. The Pale
> Curation says* informant *, and writes it down in a column. The
> Brine Communion says* drift *, the same word they use for
> driftwood and for storm-orphaned sailors. The Glassblown say
> nothing — not because they have no word, but because addressing
> a stranger is itself the offering of a name, and they are out of
> names. The Thermoclaves say* guest *, but only after the guest
> has stayed three nights without letting the hearth go cold.*

A 1.5-way motif becomes 7-way grammar for how each worldview
categorizes the player. Cheap leverage — every word can drive
dialogue tags later.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (bible L171):** *"The Annotation — permanently alter the
palimpsest itself. Write memories that didn't happen. Create
echoes of events that never occurred. Fabricate history."*

**Strong:** Mechanically described (permanent; Rep 1000).

**Thin:** No stated *constraint* on admissible fabrications. If
the player can write any false memory, the metaphysics has no
edges. The Choir's mechanics have edges (millimeters per day,
no sterile salt flats). Palimpsest needs a parallel hard rule.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §Stage 6 Annotation):**

> *The palimpsest does not accept every fabrication. It rejects
> annotations that contradict the recorded grain of a place — the
> direction water flowed there, the dominant weather, the species
> of the trees. Inventions consistent with the grain take and
> persist; inventions against it fade within a season. Skilled
> annotators learn to lie with the grain. The lies that hold are
> the ones the place would have welcomed.*

Annotation gets an internal test (*does this fit the grain?*),
converting it from arbitrary-history-editing into
guided-fabrication-along-existing-constraints. Generates quest
seams: how do you *learn* a place's grain before annotating?
Echo Reading — which makes earlier abilities prerequisite to the
highest in the cosmology, not just the power curve.

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Quote (bible L107):** *"You have carried us so far. You have
been so generous with your legs, your hands, your eyes. We want
you to know — we felt every step. We remember every breath."*

**Strong:** Communion ending has emotional register before it's
taken. 1301 added the *later-walker echo* aftermath.

**Thin:** No *threshold* — what must the player *do* to be
offered the ending? Bible says *Rep 1000* + *return to First Root*.
Without a threshold action, the ending is a button-press.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §Tier 4 Communion):**

> *The Communion is not offered automatically. The Choir requires
> a final, voluntary act of substrate-trust — the player must
> bury an object they have carried for the entire game, in soil
> the Choir has chosen, and leave it there for one in-game
> season. The act is not magical. The Choir is checking whether
> the player will let go of one thing without instrumental return.
> If the buried object is later dug up — by the player or by any
> other actor — the offer is rescinded permanently. There is no
> second offer.*

Substrate-trust converts the threshold from a reputation gate
into an irrevocable gesture. The mechanic embodies the philosophy
(*relationships dissolve possession*) instead of asserting it.

### 2.6 Linguistic texture

**Quote (bible L228):** *"Dialogue should shift pronoun usage.

**Strong:** Choir has a pronoun-shift rule; Palimpsest now (post-

**Concrete enhancement (bible §Faction Voice Rules — new
subsection):**

> *Saccharine Concord: declaratives end in a softening particle
> (a sweet "yes?" or "—then.") converting statements into
> invitations. Even contracts close this way. A trader who omits
> the particle has decided you are not a customer.*
>
> *Brine Communion: speech runs in tide-pairs — a long sentence
> followed by a short one, the long rising and the short falling,
> the way surf does. Children learn the rhythm before vocabulary.
> Do not edit the long sentences into clipped clauses.*
>
> *Pale Curation: parentheticals embed at almost any noun phrase,
> usually catalog references. A cataloguer asking the time will
> say "the time (Curation Standard, Auch-Pelegrin tabling, third
> revision)." Drop the parentheticals only at risk of losing what
> the speaker most wanted recorded.*
>
> *Glassblown: fragments separated by long exhalations rendered
> as em-dashes. They prefer not to finish sentences. Whether this
> is grief, theology, or breath-control from glasswork is unclear
> and they will not be asked.*
>
> *Thermoclave: verbs first, in the manner of a kept fire —
> action carries; the actor is the second consideration. "Tend
> the hearth, the elder will." On the page this sounds odd;
> spoken, it sounds settled.*

Five grammars, one paragraph each. Parallels the existing Choir-
pronoun and Palimpsest-tense rules and finishes the seven-faction

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine Communion

The Brine Communion last rotated at 0501. Five sittings later, no
canonical anthropology exists for them. This sitting supplies
two seams: a *mourning practice* (anthropology) and an *etymology*
(linguistic).

> ### The Brine Communion (working stub for bible absorption)
>
> The Brine Communion are a coastal-and-littoral people whose
> theology is downstream of saltwater. They believe the
> cataclysm is not over — that the seas are still settling,
> still working out what they will be — and that no land-based
> account of the event can be complete because the deep water
> has not yet finished saying its part. They name themselves
> *Communion* because, to them, salt is the sacrament: every
> body is briefly salt water and is returned to the larger salt
> water at death.
>
> *Communion* is a back-translation through the Pale Curation,
> like *Thermoclave*. The Communion's own word for themselves
> sounds like *uld-tide*; it has no clean rendering, and they
> have stopped insisting on one. The Curation cataloguer who
> rendered them *Communion* (Auch-Pelegrin's predecessor, by
> three filings) chose the word because the Communion's burial
> practice involved a meal taken in full view of the dying, and
> the cataloguer recognized the shape of the meal as
> communion-shaped without recognizing the theology.
>
> Communion mourning: the dying are taken to the tideline at
> low tide, given a meal of three foods (one bitter, one salt,
> one sweet), and read aloud the names of the storms they sailed
> through. After the reading, the dying are left on the line as
> the tide returns. They are not abandoned — a relative remains
> within sight at all times — but the tide is permitted to do
> what the tide does. The body is recovered after one full tidal
> cycle and returned to the brine on a small boat made for that
> purpose and that purpose only. The boat is burned afterward.
> No Communion family keeps a *funeral boat* twice; each death
> requires its own.
>
> *Drift* (their word for outsiders, see §2.3) reflects this
> theology: an outsider is anything that has not yet been salted
> long enough to know what it is becoming.

**~270 words.** Working-content; not yet bible-pile candidate.
Absorption adds *salt as sacrament*, *uld-tide* (self-name),
the *three-food / storm-reading / one-boat* mourning pattern,
and *drift* as the Communion term for outsiders — cross-linking
§2.3's seven-way walker-name table.

## 5. Merchant's-diary artifact — Communion sailor's marginalia

The spec lists *a Brine Communion sailor's marginalia in a
salvaged book* as an example. With Brine Communion as this
sitting's §3 faction, the form-and-faction line up. The artifact
is a marginal note, not a standalone document — landing canon
*parenthetically* in the spec's strictest sense.

The host book is a pre-cataclysm navigational pilot for a
shoreline that no longer exists; the sailor has been writing in
it for years.

> *Page 47 of the Eastern Pilot (pre-cataclysm imprint, edition
> uncertain, salvaged 17 tides ago south of the second reef).
> Marginalia in three different inks across at least four
> writing sessions.*
>
> [Beside a printed paragraph describing a harbor approach:]
> "This bay is gone. Approach from the south now. The bottom is
> false here — the old chart bottom. Trust the lead, not the
> page."
>
> [Below the same paragraph, in a different ink:]
> "Returned. Approach was correct. Lead read four fathoms where
> the page says ten. The page is not the sea."
>
> [In the page's bottom margin, in a third ink, smaller, later:]
> "Old Pell sang the storm-names for Esa today. Three foods.
> Salt-meal, bitter root, the last of the honey from the trade
> in spring. Esa was awake for the bitter and asleep for the
> sweet. Tide came at the third reading. Pell stayed the
> watch. Burned the boat at sundown — kept a splinter no
> bigger than a tooth. (Don't tell Pell. Don't tell anyone.
> A splinter is not a boat. I had to keep something.)"
>
> [In the top margin, fourth ink, very small:]
> "If a drift opens this book and reads this — yes, you are
> reading it correctly, we burn the boats. No, the splinter
> doesn't count, and yes I know that, and I am keeping it
> anyway. Don't ask the Communion about the splinter. Ask
> the sea."

**~270 words.** Lands three canon items parenthetically: (a)
mourning practice (already established §3, re-grounded in personal
voice); (b) the *splinter heresy* — some Communion individuals
keep tokens of the funeral boat against faction rule, *new* canon,
seeds drama; (c) *drift* used naturally, not dictionary-style. The
host page is a harbor approach; the mourning paragraph is a
bottom-margin aside. Canon without lecture.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence (from §3 Brine Communion stub):** *"They believe
the cataclysm is not over — that the seas are still settling,
still working out what they will be — and that no land-based
account of the event can be complete because the deep water has
not yet finished saying its part."*

The sentence's surface: *believe*, *settling*, *working out*, *no
land-based account*, *deep water*, *finished saying its part*.
Modern present-progressives (*are still settling*); a colloquial
phrasal verb (*working out*); a working-modern qualifier (*no
land-based account*). The metaphor (*deep water saying its part*)
is plainspoken anthropomorphism, not theological declamation. No
*Lo*, no capitalised abstracts (*the Deep, the Saying*), no
inverted predicate (*hath not the Sea spoken*), no archaic
auxiliary, no *unto*, no apostrophe-name.

TES rewrite (forbidden, contrast): *"Verily, the Folk-of-the-Salt-
Sacrament hold that the Cataclysm hath not yet reached its End —
that the Seas, in their Deepening, have not yet uttered the whole
of their Witness."* Tells: *Verily*; capitals on *Folk*,
*Cataclysm*, *Seas*, *Deepening*, *Witness*; the auxiliary *hath*;
the construction *the Folk hold that*. Six fingerprint markers in
one line.

The actual sentence keeps its claim epistemological — *who has
the right to narrate the cataclysm* — not liturgical.

Pass.
