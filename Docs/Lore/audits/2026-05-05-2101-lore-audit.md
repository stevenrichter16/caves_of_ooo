## 3. Faction asymmetry — Glassblown Remnant rotates in

### The Glassblown name for the cataclysm: *the Last Heat*

The shipped JSON has the Choir reporting the Glassblown's name as
*the Burning* (L481, *"refuse to speak further"*). True at first
order — the Glassblown do say *the Burning* to outsiders — but it
is the *outward* name. Internally they call it **the Last Heat**,
and the difference is the faction's foundational craft-doctrine.

In glassblowing, *heat* is not a synonym for *fire*. Fire is the
fuel; heat is the *worked condition* of the glass — the state in
which the material is plastic enough to take shape. A working
glassblower talks about *bringing the glass up to heat*, *holding
it at heat*, *letting it come off heat*. *The Last Heat*, in the
faction's grammar, is the cataclysm understood as **the moment the
world was last plastic enough to be reshaped** — and was not.

The Glassblown teach (in the rare cases they teach outsiders) that
the cataclysm was not destruction but missed *opportunity*. The
world came up to heat the way a crucible comes up to heat.
Something held the glass at temperature for a window of perhaps
hours, perhaps days. The window closed. The glass cooled in the
shape it had at closing — broken, splintered, scattered. The
world is now *cold glass*, and cold glass cannot be reshaped. It
can only be ground, polished, or shattered further.

Working idiom-inventory:

  - *the Last Heat* — the cataclysm proper; the plastic window.
  - *cold-glass years* — the centuries since. A glassblower would
    call cold glass *finished* in the technical sense — meaning no
    further work without first re-melting. The Glassblown do not
    believe re-melting is coming.
  - *the Burning* — the public-face name; offered to walkers, the
    Choir, the Concord. The name carries deliberate misreading:
    *burning* implies destruction, which the Glassblown do not
    believe happened. Outsiders prefer this story.
  - *the missed pour* — private grief-language, between Glassblown
    only. A *pour* is the moment a glassblower commits to a shape;
    *the missed pour* is the technical term for a pour that did
    not happen because the glass cooled before the glassblower
    could act. The Glassblown believe the world was a missed pour.
    They do not believe anyone is to blame. They believe no one
    was at the bench.

**The closing-the-bench rite (anthropological note).** When a
Glassblown dies, the surviving members of their atelier perform a
small private rite: an ingot of cullet is brought up to working
heat in the deceased's own crucible, and held at heat for the
length of one breath taken by each surviving member. Then the heat
is cut. The ingot cools without being shaped. The doctrinal
teaching: *every breath we take is a heat we hold; when we are not
there to take the breath, the heat is given back.* The cullet is
ground into frit and added to the atelier's working stock. The
deceased becomes part of every future pour — not as a memorial,
but as a literal granular component. The Choir would recognise the
gesture as composting; the Palimpsest as inscription. The
Glassblown call it neither. They call it *closing the bench*.

**Insertion target:** new bible sidebar in a *Other Factions —

L481 *"refuse to speak further"* is **already consistent** with
this framing — the public name is *the Burning*, the private name

outsiders. No JSON edit required.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

A Glassblown crucible-tag, in support of §3. Lands the *Last
Heat* / *missed pour* idiom parenthetically inside a working craft
artifact.

The Glassblown do not write books. They write **crucible-tags** —
short labels tied to working crucibles with copper wire,
identifying the cullet, working temperature, date of last use, and
the journeyman responsible for the next pour. A crucible-tag is
mundane the way a hospital chart is mundane. It is also,
occasionally, where a Glassblown lets something slip.

---

> ash-pit of an abandoned Glassblown workshop in the salt flats.
> Hand: journeyman, by the loop of the *V*. Date smudged, two-digit
> year reads either *14* or *44*.**
>

> Crucible:    Veld-3, third reseat.
> Cullet:      mixed atelier scrap, 70% / pre-Last Heat 30%.
> Working temp: 1080 to 1140. Hold no longer than a slow seven-count.

>              this crucible was Cale's before it was anyone's.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"Fire is the fuel; heat is the worked condition of the glass —
> the state in which the material is plastic enough to take shape."*

Working register, plain-spoken, technical. *Fire is the fuel; heat
is the worked condition* is a working glassblower's distinction,
the kind of thing a journeyman would tell an apprentice on a slow
afternoon. *Plastic*, in materials grammar, means *deformable
without fracture*, not the modern polymer. *Take shape* is
colloquial and process-oriented; it does not personify the glass.
The sentence sits inside an *In glassblowing* / *the Glassblown
teach* frame — faction-internal teaching, not omniscient narration.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"Know ye the truth: Fire is but the
servant; the Last Heat is the World's plasticity, that brief Hour
when all things might yet have been re-Wrought."* Tells: *Know ye
the truth* (archaising imperative); *the Last Heat* capitalised at
first mention without faction-internal grounding; *the World's
plasticity* (genitive allegorisation, capital *World*); *that brief
Hour* (capital-H archaic chronicle-noun); *re-Wrought* (passive-
voice mythic-archaic with implicit world-shaping agent + capital).
The actual sentence: lower-case throughout; technical *plastic* in
its materials sense; colloquial *take shape*; explicit teaching-
frame preserving authorial uncertainty.

Pass.
