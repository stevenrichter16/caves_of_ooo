## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible, line 21:* "the way text on a medieval manuscript was scraped
> off and written over."

**Strong.** Prior audits stratified the cataclysm's *aftermath* in
five incompatible ways.

**Thin.** *The first hour.* No faction-account exists of the
cataclysm-in-progress — only immediate before and immediate after.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "The first hour"):**

> No two factions agree on what the inciting moment *was*.
>
> - **Choir.** A *taste* came through the soil. Spores tasted it and
>   woke. The Choir treats its own awakening as the first event;
>   outsiders find this self-centering conspicuous.
> - **Palimpsest.** A sentence was overwritten in real-time. The
>   Palimpsest claims to have *read* the overwrite as it happened and
>   has *not finished reading.*
> - **Curation.** A single sound, recorded in three pre-cataclysm
>   logs whose pitches disagree by enough that they cannot all be of
>   the same event. All three filed at Tier 1, none ratified.
>   *Provisional pluralism* is policy.
> - **Concord.** A *transaction*. Something was given; something was
>   taken. Doctrine does not specify which side the world was on.
> - **Brine.** A tide that did not turn. They were waiting for it,
>   and will not say for how long.
> - **Thermoclaves.** The tenth-fire of the chain they call *the
>   cold one*. Nine fires before, none after, until they began
>   counting from one.
> - **Glassblown.** The silence around which all their grammar
>   organises. The unsaid mood was the first thing said.

The corpus's *four hundred years* (2150) was *when*. This is *what*.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Contested authorship (2150's Halen card), anthology
framing, transcripts, marginalia.

**Thin.** **Forgery.** No corpus document is yet a forgery — written
by one faction in another's documentary register, for strategic gain.
Forgery is contestation's inverse: who profits if the wrong faction
is believed to have written this?

**Concrete addition (bible, "Forgery"):**

> The Choir has, at least once, *grown* a Curation card. A surveyor
> found one in a vault he had not been assigned to: a hand he did not
> recognise, in the Curation's house style — Tier-3 stamp, cabinet
> number, the Halen flourish on the *4*. The card catalogued a
> chamber that did not exist. The card itself was thin pressed
> mycelium; the ink, a vegetable secretion the chemists identified
> as a substance only the deepest fruiting bodies produce.
>
> Three seasons later, a surveyor found the chamber. The card had
> been correct.
>
> The Choir does not call this a forgery. It calls it *anticipatory
> cataloguing*. The Curation has informally considered the question
> of whether the Choir is now their most reliable contributor; the
> discussion does not appear in the archive.

A document's *register* is now a separate variable from its *author*.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** Names for cataclysm, dead, joining, apotheoses.

**Thin.** **The act of remembering.** Each faction's word for *to
remember* encodes what it thinks memory IS.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, after the comparison table):**

> - **Choir:** *to be tasted again.* The same nutrient through a
>   different mouth. No word for *to remember in vain*: if tasted
>   again, it has been used.
> - **Palimpsest:** *to read backward.* Recursive; each remembering
>   is a new line in the layered text. The verb is intransitive —
>   one *reads backward*, and what is read is what is read.
> - **Pale Curation:** *to retrieve.* A filing operation with two
>   arguments, cabinet and index. *I retrieve* with no object will
>   earn a frown.
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *to settle.* Memory is debt-resolution.
>   Forgetting is theft from the self.
> - **Brine Communion:** *to surface.* The same word used for a body
>   that has come up after a wreck.
> - **Thermoclaves:** *to relight.* Two suffixes — *relit briefly*
>   and *kept lit*. The grammar respects the difference.
> - **Glassblown:** *to second-fire.* What survives the second fire
>   is what is remembered; what does not was, by doctrine, *not
>   remembered to begin with* — the failure is the memory's own.

A Choir-acolyte and a Palimpsest-acolyte both saying *I remember the
old grove* are not, in their own languages, talking about the same
thing.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Substrate, metabolism, Echo limits, metaphysics-meet,
death edge cases.

**Thin.** **Inheritance.** The bible is silent on whether
faction-traits pass to children.

**Concrete addition (bible, "On inheritance", in Stage 1 mechanics):**

> The Choir node does not pass through the germline. Choir mother and
> walker father produce a walker child; the Choir is unbothered, *we
> are recruited, not bred*. The two recorded cases of two-Choir-parent
> children — both born ordinary, recruited later.
>
> Palimpsest sight is different. It *occasionally* passes — what the
> Pale Curation calls a **bleed-line**. Three confirmed: a child born
> partial-Glimpsing; a child born with an echo of an ancestor's
> memory two generations back; a child born only able to see the
> Glimpse layer and not the present (this third did not survive
> infancy — *the child read forward only, which is also a kind of
> reading*, says the Palimpsest, which does not consider this a
> tragedy exactly).
>
> Brine attunement is environmental; the under-tide does not
> recognise the unborn. Thermoclave fire-keeping is initiatory.
> Glassblown shape-naming is taught. Curation employment is
> contractual.

Choir is recruitive, Palimpsest is bleed-leaky, the rest are elective.
The asymmetry maps onto each faction's relationship to time.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** Mirror endings, Annihilist, Stalled Walkers, failed
apotheoses.

**Thin.** **Mid-apotheosis interruption.** A walker forcibly
extracted *during* acceptance. 2150 covered non-acceptance; this is
force during acceptance.

**Concrete addition (bible, append to failed-apotheoses):**

> **The half-said.** A walker extracted from the Transcription chamber
> before completion. They emerge speaking twice: the surface sentence
> in their own voice, and a second sentence beneath it, audible but
> not phonologically present — listeners report having heard it but
> cannot quote it back. The half-said cannot be lied to; the second
> sentence corrects the first if it was sincere but wrong, and
> refuses the first if it was a lie. They do not consider this a
> power. They consider it a *condition*.
>
> **The half-tasted.** A walker extracted from the chemical-gradient
> room before integration. The node is partially threaded — Stage 1
> sensing works at random; the player feels someone else's hunger or
> warmth at unpredictable moments and cannot distinguish their own
> from the network's. The Choir calls them *the leaky-mouths*. About
> half return unprompted within three years; the Choir does not stop
> them.

The mirror holds: half-said carries the Palimpsest's burden of
correcting *what was said*; half-tasted, the Choir's burden of
sharing *what was felt*. Force-extracted apotheoses are partial in
the direction the apotheosis was taking the walker.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Per-faction grammars (1813→2150).

**Thin.** **Translation between factions.** *False-friends* — words
that look like they translate but betray their source on close
inspection — are not yet catalogued.

**Concrete addition (bible, "The Curation Lexicon, false-friends"):**

> The Pale Curation maintains a working **Lexicon of Useful
> Mismatches**. Three representative entries:
>
> > **Choir *we***. Translates as Curation *we (institutional)*. **Do
> > not.** The Choir's *we* is metabolic — a single-mouth pronoun
> > borne by many shapes. The Curation's is committee-derived — a
> > multi-mouth pronoun on a single body of policy. Three field-cards
> > in which a surveyor appeared to be speaking on behalf of the
> > Choir were corrected; one was framed in the Director's office.
> >
> > **Brine *deep-kept***. Translates as Curation *holdings (sub-
> > tidal)*. **Partial.** *Holdings* implies retrievable; *deep-kept*
> > implies *keeping does not promise return*. A surveyor who told
> > a Brine widow her *deep-kept* husband was recoverable committed
> > a doctrinal cruelty.
> >
> > **Palimpsest *re-reading***. Translates as Curation *citation*.
> > **No.** Citation produces a copy; re-reading produces a new layer
> > of the original. Surveyors who confused the two have, unknowingly,
> > contributed to the Palimpsest by trying to file it.
>
> The Lexicon is itself Tier 2 — its existence implicates the
> Curation in *making* meaning rather than recording it. Director
> Ven keeps it in a drawer.

Every cross-faction translation is now a faithful betrayal, not a
neutral act.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Strong.** Tuesday Morning across all six factions.

**Thin.** **Stranger-meeting.** Two faction-members on neutral ground
— the protocol of the first three sentences. Tuesday Morning was
*internal*; this is *contact*.

**Concrete addition (bible, "How factions greet strangers"):**

> - **Choir → walker.** *We taste you on the air.* Declares awareness
>   without claim. The walker may ignore.
> - **Palimpsest → walker.** *I read you forward.* Implies the
>   Palimpsest sees the walker's near-future, and is sharing *that
>   it sees*, not *what*.
> - **Curation → anyone.** A surveyor states their name, tier, and
>   the cabinet they are filing toward, in that order. Omitting any
>   is forward; offering a *fourth* is a sign of stress.
> - **Concord → anyone.** A small bow and the price of the next
>   sentence, in the smallest possible unit. Not naming a price means
>   the conversation is either a refusal or a gift; tone alone tells
>   the recipient which.
> - **Brine → anyone.** A pause longer than the listener finds
>   comfortable, then *which tide brought you?* The listener is
>   judged by whether they answer the question or the category.
> - **Thermoclaves → anyone.** A glance at the listener's hands, then
>   their face. *Are you keeping?* The expected answer is *kept*.
>   *Cold* is honest; *holding* is a forgivable beginner's error.
> - **Glassblown → anyone.** *(One full breath of shared silence.)*
>   A stranger who fills the silence has initiated a transaction.
>
> Cross-faction friction concentrates at *Concord-Glassblown* and
> *Brine-Thermoclave*. The Concord cannot price what is unsaid; the
> Glassblown cannot trade what cannot be said. Brine tides and
> Thermoclave fires are mutually exclusive heat-doctrines. Both pairs
> maintain professional fictions to make commerce possible — fictions
> the Curation collects.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Pale Curation

The Curation has a documented *register* (cards, intake forms) and
*characters* (Halen, Kesti, Director Ven). What it lacks is **how a
surveyor becomes a surveyor.** Initiation is the anthropology that
makes the register possible.

**Proposed lore-artifact: The Curation Initiation.**

A candidate is given one object, drawn at random from a drawer the
Curation has held for this purpose since long before the candidate
was born. Three days, no peers, no archive access, one blank intake
card. They must catalogue the object.

The card is read by three senior surveyors, each affirming silently,
by initial, that it is *neither too much nor too little.* *Too much*
is projection, narrative, opinion, presumption beyond evidence. *Too
little* is incomplete fields, missed details, refusals to commit.

Failure is non-violent. The candidate is not dismissed — they are
*recategorised*, given a Tier number and assigned to cabinet support.
Tier-3 candidates handle the filing of decorative objects. Tier-2
candidates handle *forms about* decorative objects. Tier-1 candidates
handle form-filing forms. There is no Tier-0 work; Tier-0 candidates
sit in the chair labelled with their tier. Some attempt the
initiation again at the quinquennial review. Some sit for thirty
years. The Curation does not regard this as cruel. It regards it as
honest.

The object is never returned. If the candidate passes, the card is
filed. If they fail, card *and object* are filed together — the
object accessioned under the candidate's tier number. The Curation
is, in this way, partially built of its own failed initiations.
Cabinet 9 is reportedly mostly this.

Director Ven's intake card is in Cabinet 1, the only artifact there.
The Director is the only surveyor permitted to read it, and is
reported to do so once a year, on a date the staff have agreed not
to ask about.

The Initiation is the inverse of Glassblown unfiring (2150). Where
the Glassblown name what was *not* done, the Curation card-files
what *was*. Both record presence by accounting for absence.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a)** `ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md`:601 has Mogu say *"Names of plants my
grandmother named, in a tongue we no longer speak. Now the names live
in the spores. Nothing of value is ever lost — only translated."*
Bible:131-133 has the Palimpsest accuse the Choir of destroying
encoded memory — *"It is an eraser that believes it is a pen."*
These cannot both be straightforwardly true. Either Mogu is sincerely
wrong (the Choir-acolyte from inside cannot see what is destroyed),
Mogu is right (the Palimpsest's accusation is a slander born of two
cosmologies failing to translate), or both are true at different
levels — the artifact dies, the function survives — and the apparent
contradiction is a *vocabulary* problem that maps cleanly onto
Dimension 3 above (*to be tasted again* vs *to read backward* are
not the same operation; Mogu and the Palimpsest are talking past
each other in good faith).

**Resolution proposal:** the bible adds one line to the comparison
section — *Both factions believe they preserve memory. Both are
partly right. Their disagreement is not about preservation; it is
about which losses count as losses.*

**(b)** Bible:226 says *"It [the Choir] never lies."* JSON:250 has
the Choir say *"Honesty is a courtesy we extend to those who can
hear it."* The second implies selective honesty — not lying, but not
unconditional. **Fix:** rephrase the bible to *"The Choir does not
state falsehoods, though it may decline to volunteer truths"*, or
trim the JSON line. The current pair has a player-noticeable seam.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

A pre-cataclysm cookbook, catalogued by the Curation and — over
decades, in slow ink visible only at certain hours — annotated by
the Palimpsest. The cookbook is mundane. The annotations are not.

**Title (in-game):** *Pre-cataclysm domestic codex (recipes), Tier 2.
Catalogued by Surveyor Hesh; Palimpsest-annotated, status unresolved.*

```
[Curation card affixed inside the cover. The cookbook is a slim
 leather-bound volume, well-thumbed. Hesh's hand on the card;
 annotations on the recipes are in a fainter, slow ink reported to
 appear only between the third and fifth hours after dawn.]

  ACCESSION:        CB-2-0418
  FOUND AT:         Old Hyphae crossing, surface ruin, sealed cellar
  CONDITION:        Sound. Pages 12, 19, 27 carry annotations in a
                    hand and ink not original to the volume.
  TIER:             2 (domestic, intact, partially annotated)

  NOTES (Hesh):

      The recipes are unremarkable for the period — fennel-braised
      lamb, three starapple preparations, a winter soup, two breads,
      a preserve.

      (The annotations are the reason for the rating. The hand is
      not on file. The ink takes hours to develop and fades by
      midmorning. The chemists confirm: it is not ink. It is a
      *temporal residue*. The Palimpsest is, by slow degrees,
      writing in this book.)

      The annotations:

        Page 12 (fennel-braised lamb), beside *"a small fire,
          maintained at low heat"* — *she liked it slow.*
        Page 19 (winter soup), beside *"add the soup-stones from
          the previous winter's keeping"* — *we kept three. One
          was hers.*
        Page 27 (the preserve), bottom margin, in a slightly
          different hand — the single word *Mira.*

      (The third annotation is the file-changing one. The first
      two describe the cook. The third names her. The cookbook
      was Mira's. We did not know she existed. We have no other
      record. Until the third hour after dawn, on the morning of
      the seventh week, we did not.)

      (I am leaving the book on the open shelf. The Palimpsest
      has not finished writing in it. The Director has asked
      that I not move it.)
```

The bombshell parenthetically embedded: **the Palimpsest is, slowly,
*naming the dead* by writing in the margins of their domestic
objects.** Not Echo Reading on demand — *time-release annotation*,
a passive process that reveals names the world no longer holds.

The implication for play: Curation cabinets contain a slow-release
archive the Curation is not entirely the author of. At the right
hour of the right day, the player can read names that are in no
other source. Mira's name is the first such. Future audits can plant
others.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"The Choir's* we *is metabolic — a single-mouth pronoun borne by
> many shapes. The Curation's* we *is committee-derived — a multi-
> mouth pronoun on a single body of policy."*

No archaisms. No invented fantasy proper nouns. Two parallel
analytical clauses; the technical terms (*metabolic*,
*committee-derived*) do the discriminating work. A TES book would
have written *"Lo, the Folk of the Damp do speak as a single beast
through many mouths, while the Pale Sorters do speak as many minds
through a single tongue, and behold ye well that this is the deepest
lesson of grammar in this fallen age."* Mine has no *lo, behold, ye,
fallen age*; discrimination is in the vocabulary, not the ornament.
Confirmed.
