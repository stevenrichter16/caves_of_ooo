## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Sibling 2333:* "Pre-fires are *remembered.* A kept fire is in
> the count; a remembered fire is in the lineage."

**Strong.** Pre-cataclysm fires stratify into *kept*, *remembered*,
*ember-named*. The cataclysm is the 51st fire by tally, the longest
*gap-burn* by sibling 2314.

**Thin.** **The numbered chains.** The corpus says fires are
counted; chains are counted; gap-burns are *not* counted. It has
never said *which chain we are in.* If chains retroactively
uncount themselves, the present-tense chain-number is itself a
doctrinal stake.

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar, "The current chain"):

> The Thermoclaves disagree about which numbered chain the
> present is. Southern hearths count the **fourth chain since
> the cataclysm**, with the cataclysm's relit fire as the
> first of First Chain. Northern hearths count the **second**
> — they retro-broke the original First on the grounds that
> its third tenth-fire was lit from a coal brought, not a coal
> kept (*brought fire is gap-burn coal*), and the chain ended
> without anyone declaring it ended. The numbers are not
> reconciled. A southern keeper invited north receives a
> safe-passage token with two stamps — four and two — and is
> expected to use whichever is correct to the room.
>
> A walker with reputation in both regions is asked at audience
> *which number do you give us?* Either answer is accepted.
> *No answer* is also accepted, and is the most common.

The corpus's first **non-reconcilable count** within a faction —
a confession of the schism Section 3 promotes.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

> *Sibling 2333:* "the keeper is not asked to name themself."

**Strong.** Fire-tally as collectively-authored document; the
fire as the named author.

**Thin.** **Cross-school transcripts.** What does it look like
when a *southern* keeper records a *northern* keeper's tally —
or refuses to? The corpus has no specimen.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "Cross-school
attestation"):

> When a tally is brought south for adjacency, the southern
> hearth does not transcribe it. The northern stroke is *traced*
> in dry chalk on a separate slate — a single dotted line beside
> the original cross-mark. The dotted line is the southern
> attestation: *this stroke exists, we have read it, we have not
> filed it.* Tracings do not enter the southern fire-tally.
>
> The reverse — **un-tracing** — is a northern keeper shown a
> southern tally laying a fingertip on each cross-mark in turn
> and *lifting it slightly,* as though weighing whether it is
> hot. The lifting leaves no record except in the witnesses'
> memory. Northern doctrine: *a southern stroke read with the
> hand has been weighed; we owe it nothing further.*
>
> The Pale Curation has filed both customs under the special
> intake form (sibling 2333) and notes — in reservation — that
> these are the corpus's only two forms of *attestation that do
> not transmit information.*

A record whose form *refuses to copy* the record it attests.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

> *Sibling 2333:* "The Thermoclave substrate-claim is the
> **ember-floor**."

> *Sibling 2326:* "The Thermoclaves' substrate-claim is the
> **kept ember**."

> *Sibling 2314:* "**The slag** is a byproduct, real, **not
> foundational.** … The world is **continuously kept** rather
> than founded."

**Three substrate-claims, one faction.** R3 names the three as
the schism's three doctrinal positions, not as audit drift.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The three hearths"):

> The Thermoclaves are not one teaching. They are three, kept
> separately by three regional hearth-clusters. Each names a
> different substrate-claim; the three are not reconcilable
> within the grammar.
>
> - **The Floor school** (southern). Substrate: the **ember-
>   floor.** The substrate accepts heat downward; a quench-tide
>   is the moment two floors meet. *Fire goes down.* Apotheosis:
>   the **long quench**, floor-keeper outcome (sibling 2333).
>   Verb-aspect: *grounded.*
> - **The Ember school** (eastern). Substrate: the **kept
>   ember.** Heat persists in absence-of-fuel; the world is
>   read by the warm. Apotheosis: the **long warming**, level-
>   with-the-hearth survival (sibling 2326). Verb-aspect:
>   *level.*
> - **The Refusal school** (northern). Substrate: there is
>   none. The world is *continuously kept* (sibling 2314).
>   Apotheosis: the **long quench**, *gracefully uncounted*
>   exit — the petitioner walks away and is not a Thermoclave
>   any longer. Verb-aspect: *uncounted.*
>
> All three share fire-grammar (count-verbs, ember-of, the
> keep-particle) and tally form. They differ on what the keeping
> *does to the world.* Floor and Ember disagree about which
> direction heat goes; Refusal disagrees with both by declining
> the question.
>
> Cross-school tenders meet at the **mid-fires** — annual
> gatherings where representatives keep a single hearth between
> them for one tally-day. The mid-fire is in no chain's count;
> in all three grammars it is *outside the tally.* No school
> will be the first to file it.

The schism is now the load-bearing fact. Three substrate-claims
become three schools become three apotheoses, with a shared
grammar that *describes* keeping while disagreeing about *what
is kept.*

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

> Choir = Communion. Palimpsest = Transcription. Concord =
> Closure. Brine = going-deep. Glassblown = Unfired Forever.
> Curation = the held card.

**Thin.** **A walker offered all three Thermoclave apotheoses.**
A walker who reaches Exalted with all three schools could, in
principle, accept any one. The corpus has not said whether they
*can*, or what happens if they ask for two.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The walker's
hearth"):

> A walker at Exalted with all three schools is not invited to
> a single apotheosis but to the **walker's hearth** — a
> hearth the three schools assemble jointly, kept for a
> tally-day under a roof bearing all three numerical stamps.
> The walker is asked to keep the hearth for the day. At dusk,
> each school in turn names what they have witnessed: the
> Floor whether the ember-floor met; the Ember whether the
> warm passed across; the Refusal whether the count was kept
> or the walker ended *gracefully uncounted*.
>
> The three readings are not negotiated. A walker may be
> *grounded*, *level*, *uncounted*, or any combination — or
> *none of the three* (Refusal proposes *unread*; Ember
> proposes *cooled*; Floor declines to name a non-result). The
> walker chooses which reading to accept, which to decline,
> which to hold open. A walker who accepts all three is — in
> the schools' simultaneous voice — *kept by all three hearths
> and beholden to none.* The corpus has no precedent. The
> Choir, asked, says: *we will compost whatever is left over.*

The first apotheosis with **multiple readings of the same rite.**
The walker is the cross-school document the schools cannot write.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Thin.** **The verb-aspect of refusal.** The Refusal school's
*uncounted* is a state but not yet a verb-aspect. The other two
schools have *grounded* and *level* as productive aspects of any
verb. The Refusal needs symmetry.

**Concrete addition** (bible footnote to "The three hearths"):

> The Refusal school inflects verbs in the **uncounted** aspect
> by *omission of the count-particle.* Where Floor and Ember
> grammars require a particle on every fire-verb (*the sixthing
> was kept*), Refusal grammar drops it (*the was kept*). The
> dropped particle is itself the inflection. Outsiders read the
> sentence as broken. Refusal speakers consider the outsider
> reading correct: *the broken is the sentence.*
>
> A Refusal keeper who switches to count-particle grammar mid-
> sentence is *re-counting* — permitted, but obligates a walk
> to a Floor or Ember hearth that season and an explanation.
> Most do not switch. The few who have, switched back.

A grammar with **a positive absence** — the missing particle is
the meaning. No other corpus grammar reads silence as a
constituent.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Thin.** **The mid-fire day.** Pair with the dry-walker's day
(1301) and the junior cataloguer's day (2001).

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar, "A mid-fire day"):

> Pre-dawn: the three school representatives arrive separately
> at a hearth-site agreed three years prior. Nothing is yet
> kindled. A walker may attend, bringing nothing.
>
> Dawn: the **first stroke** is laid by lot. A three-sided
> splinter of fire-glass; the up-face names the school. That
> school's keeper kindles. The other two stand to either side.
> No instruction is given.
>
> Mid-morning: the **fuel-share.** Floor keepers bring exactly
> the day's median weight; Ember keepers bring whatever burns
> warmest in their experience; Refusal keepers bring an
> *uncounted* portion — a quantity nobody asked them to weigh.
> The hearth accepts all three.
>
> Mid-day: silence. None of the three speak.
>
> Afternoon: the **tally**. Each keeper in turn lays a single
> cross-mark — the mark is school-agnostic in form (sibling
> 2333). The mid-fire's tally is the only tally that bears
> three cross-marks for one fire. The mid-fire's slates are
> kept jointly in a cabinet between regions, never moved.
>
> Dusk: the **keep-pass.** The first-stroke keeper passes a
> single ember to the next school's representative, who passes
> it to the third. The third does not return it; they are the
> *holder of the next mid-fire's first ember.* If the third
> dies before that, the holder-rite must be redone — the next
> mid-fire is delayed by one tally-day per redo. Longest delay
> on record: eleven tally-days. The reason is unspoken; the
> keeper who held the ember is unspoken.

The first **trans-faction sub-rite** a faction performs *with
itself.* The schools enact a confederacy none of them
theologically endorses.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclaves R3 (the three hearths)

The corpus filed the Thermoclaves as fire-keepers. R3 names them
as **three fire-keepers in one grammar** — and declares the
disagreement to be the faction, not a bug in the audit cluster.

**The three schools** (Floor / Ember / Refusal) share a grammar
and tally-form; they disagree on substrate, apotheosis-outcome,
chain-count. The most consequential intra-faction fracture in
the corpus — the only one where the disagreement is about *what
the rite does to the world*, not who the authority is.

**Cross-school instruments** the corpus now carries: dotted-line
tracing; the fingertip lift; the two-stamp safe-passage token;
the mid-fire's three-cross-mark tally; the walker's hearth.

**The kept hand reconsidered.** Sixty years across both numbering
systems. Northern keepers cite her as proof their count is
correct (*kept across two chains by us*); Southern keepers cite
the same fact as proof of theirs (*across four*). She has not
said. The Refusal school does not invoke her — *kept* is a
count-particle, and Refusal grammar will not invoke counted
speech to defend its own position.

**A walker's path through.** Floor-first reads the world as
*fire goes down*; Ember-first as *warm persists without sign*;
Refusal-first as *the keeping is the world*. The corpus does
not say which is right because — by Refusal lights — it cannot.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Live contradiction: the bible's silence on Thermoclave
substrate vs the three R2 audits' three different claims.**

`Palimpsest_Lore_Bible.md` line 208 names the Thermoclaves only
as the Brine's enemy. The bible has no substrate-claim for them.

R2 audits 2314, 2326, 2333 each filed a *different* substrate-
claim under the heading *the Thermoclave substrate-claim*. Each
audit reads as if it were the only one. They are not. The bible
has never adjudicated.

**Resolution adopted by R3:** the contradiction is in-world.
Dim 4 promotes the three claims to the three schools. Bible
needs one clause near line 208:

> The Thermoclaves are three — Floor, Ember, Refusal — sharing
> fire-grammar and tally-form while disagreeing about whether
> heat travels downward, persists without sign, or is the
> keeping itself. A player at maximum reputation has earned it
> with a specific school.

**Smaller bleed.** The three R2s each named a *long quench* with
different outcomes. Dim 5's walker's hearth resolves it: the
apotheoses are the schools', not the faction's. The faction is
unified in *what it does* and three-fold in *what that means.*

**Tertiary bleed.** Sibling 2326 reports the Choir saying *the
ember walks down* (Floor idiom); sibling 2333 reports the Choir
reading warm rock as *patient soil* (Ember-friendly). Both true:
the Choir has a deepest mouth at every hearth and speaks each
school's idiom. The Refusal hearth's deepest mouth speaks
*uncounted* — the only documented case of the Choir using *no
count-particle.* Refusal keepers consider this confirmation;
Floor and Ember keepers consider it the Choir being polite.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**
> *"The dropped particle is itself the inflection — uncounted
> speech is grammatically missing a constituent."*

No archaism, no apostrophe-fantasy, no *verily*, no mythopoeic
register. A linguistic-mechanics statement. The Refusal school's
*missing-particle-as-positive-meaning* sounds strange because
the Thermoclaves use it that way, not because the narrator
performs antiquity. TES-register version: *"And it came to pass
that the Northern Brethren did refuse the count, that the
silence of their tongues might bear witness to that which the
heat itself revealeth."* I did not write that.

Stylised vocabulary (*Floor*, *Ember*, *Refusal*, *kept hand*,
*mid-fire*, *uncounted*, *grounded*, *level*) names schools and
rites. Narrator reports, does not adopt — same register as the
Curation *bid-mood*, Brine *remembered tense*, Concord
*open-clause*.

Pass.
