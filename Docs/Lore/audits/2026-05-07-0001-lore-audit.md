## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (JSON L481):** *"refuse to speak further."*

**Strong.** Cataclysm-name canon, posture-loaded; 0915 gave the
refusal a working-form (named once at first-firing).

**Thin.** The first-firing utterance has never been canonised.
0915 gave occasion and penalty; not the sentence.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm, append):**

> *The Glassblown's first-firing utterance — said once at the
> moment a practitioner is first permitted to draw molten
> material from the working furnace — is recorded by Pale
> Curation field-cataloguers (when permitted to attend) in a
> remarkably consistent form across watch-houses:* "The fire
> that did not finish — I am here to finish it." *The Curation
> files this as a doctrinal formula. A small minority of strips
> witnessed by Communion sailors at coastal kiln-houses record
> a variant ending:* "...and to be the witness of its finishing." *
> Whether the variant is older, dissident, or local has not
> been established.*

Locks in the doctrinal core (the Burning is unfinished heat;
working-life is its delayed cooling — see §2.4). The variant
opens room for future schism content without committing.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (bible L185, post-2301 §2.2):** *"What the Saccharine
Concord's first reader actually said."*

**Strong.** Authorship now framed as Echo-Reading targets, not
signed documents.

**Thin.** No equivalent for the Glassblown. The faction's
authorship-mode has never been canonised.

**Concrete enhancement (bible new sidebar, "Glassblown authorship"):**

> *The Glassblown do not produce documents in their own working
> register. Their authored output is** the batch-glyph **(struck
> into a finished piece at the moment the cooling-watch concludes)
> and** the pourer's mark **(a smaller mark indicating which
> practitioner held the iron). Neither carries a name. A piece's
> authorship is the conjunction: the batch-glyph reads back to a
> specific cooling-strip on file at the watch-house; the strip
> identifies the pourer by mark. A piece bearing one mark and
> not the other is** an orphan**, sent to the watch-house of
> nearest kin for re-witnessing or — if the strip cannot be
> recovered — re-firing. Echo Reading on a Glassblown piece
> recovers the pourer's hand-shape, the kiln's heat-curve, and
> the room's silence; it does not recover words, because the
> room contained none.*

Resolves what every prior Glassblown audit left implicit
(authorship is paratextual, mark-based, name-free) and gives
Echo Reading a Glassblown profile distinct from echo-reading
Concord stones (clauses, parties) or Choir nodes (digestion).

### 2.3 Mutating motifs across cultures

**Quote (2301 R3):** seven-faction *names for the close-token*
sidebar, anchored on the Concord stone.

**Strong.** Deepest mutation-table in the editor's pile.

**Thin.** It is Concord-anchored. There is no *Glassblown-
anchored* mutation — every existing Glassblown row is a posture
*toward* something else.

**Concrete enhancement (bible new sidebar, "Names for the
watch-bowl"):**

A *watch-bowl* is the small clear vessel that holds the cooling-
watch's test-droplet.

> - **Glassblown:** *the bowl* — neutral, working-form. Not numbered.
> - **Pale Curation:** *the witness-vessel* — accepted at depot.
>   Curation has experimented with storing field samples in them
>   on the theory that a bowl that has held a cooling-watch
>   acquires *witness-quality.* Filed inconclusive.
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *the clear cup* — tally-table side-
>   vessel for water. Clerks prefer them because the *first-cane*
>   colour of an unstamped stone is visible through the glass.
> - **Brine Communion:** *the dry bowl* — for the singer's water
>   during night anchor. Sailors hold that a bowl that has held
>   heat without breaking carries *heat-without-breaking* into

> - **Thermoclaves:** *the cooled glass* — *fire-touched* under

> - **Palimpsest:** *the small clear page* — Echo Reading recovers
>   the heat-curve and the room's silence. A long-shelved bowl

Two anchored objects now (Concord stone, Glassblown bowl), each
with its own posture-table. Future *thing-anchored* sidebars can
be added without each having to be the first.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Thin.** The Glassblown have a *practice* but no stated
**cosmology.**

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Glassblown Remnant subsection,
when it lands):**

> *The Glassblown teach — when they teach at all, never aloud —
> that the Burning was an** uncooled event**: heat that did not
> finish its cooling. The world after the Burning is, in their
> reading, a substrate the original heat is still slowly
> leaving. A Glassblown piece is** a small cooling **performed
> deliberately, in miniature, to demonstrate that the cooling
> is possible. The faction's cosmology is therefore not theology
> but** demonstration **— a multi-generational argument in
> physical objects that heat ends. They do not believe the
> Burning can be undone. They believe its cooling can be
> finished, one piece at a time. This places them in specific
> opposition to the Palimpsest, whose** Revision **ability would
> re-warm cooled work. Glassblown practitioners decline to
> handle Palimpsest-revised objects of working-craft — the
> revision is, in their reading, a re-firing of work that was
> already finished.*

Falsifiable cosmology any future scene can lean on. Gives the
Palimpsest a friction-target, gives the Choir an explanation for
its inability to register watch-bowls (the working object is the
demonstration of an end-state the Choir's cosmology does not
include), and gives the Concord a doctrinal counterweight
(Concord: *contracts are again possible*; Glassblown:
*coolings are again finishable*).

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Quote (2301 §2.5):** Concord reading — *the walker overpaid;
their outstanding clauses fall unmoored.*

**Strong.** A non-Choir, non-Palimpsest faction has a canonised
reading of the two endings.

**Thin.** No Glassblown reading.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Central Question, append):**

> *The Glassblown's reading is shorter, and harder to elicit. A
> Communion-walker is, in Glassblown reading,** a piece poured
> but not cooled**: a working-form that left the watch-house
> before the cooling-watch concluded. A Transcription-walker is
>** a piece returned to the kiln's heat **— work that was once
> cooled and has now been re-fired. Both are** failures of the
> cooling-watch **at a scale the working-craft cannot redress. A
> Glassblown senior who has watched a walker reach Communion
> does not lament. The senior says, in working register:* "She
> did not finish the firing." *The phrase is now an idiom for
> any premature end — a death before working-name, an apprentice
> who leaves the watch-house before first-firing, a kiln let to
> die. The cosmic and the local share the same shape: a firing
> finished is a firing finished, and a firing not finished is
> the same shape at any scale.*

Integrates with §2.4. Gives the body a working-idiom usable in
any Glassblown NPC dialogue.

### 2.6 Linguistic texture

**Thin.** The Glassblown have a working-form for silence (0915)
but no canonised speech-form.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Glassblown Remnant subsection,
append):**

> *Glassblown speech, when it occurs, is** completed-sentence
> only**. Apprentices are taught to either speak the full sentence
> or hold the silence; trailing-off, half-thoughts, and
> interjections are read as** breaks in pour**. A Glassblown who
> begins a sentence aloud will finish it. Outsiders read this as
> deliberateness or formality. Inside the body it is mechanical:
> a half-sentence is dropped work, and dropped work has to be
> witnessed (in the same sense as a dropped pour) by a senior on
> the cooling-table. An apprentice who routinely drops sentences
> is rotated to the cool-quarter for a working-month. The
> remediation is not punitive; it is the same shape as remediating
> any mid-pour failure.*

Falsifiable rule: any future Glassblown NPC line should be a
complete sentence. If a Glassblown ever trails off, that NPC is
either an apprentice in remediation, ill, or pretending — useful
narrative possibilities, in the same shape as 2301's Concord-
numbering falsification.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Glassblown Remnant (the product)

Every prior Glassblown rotation worked the practitioner. This
stub works **the thing they make.** Anthropology of the table.

> ### The Glassblown Remnant (working-craft output)
>
> The body's named output is small and few. Three working forms:
> *the watch-bowl*, *the breath-vial*, and *the seal*. Other
> forms — windowpanes, drinking vessels, ornament — are made
> occasionally, on commission, and considered *side-pours.* They
> carry the pourer's mark but no batch-glyph. The body does not
> regard them as working-craft.
>
> *The watch-bowl* — three fingers across, with a flattened rim.
> Several per day per kiln-house; the working medium of the
> cooling-watch (§2.3). Watch-bowls leave the kiln-house only by
> gift or trade and have an unusually wide cross-faction life.
>
> *The breath-vial* — a thumb-length tube, sealed at one end,
> drawn at the other to a fine open mouth. Made during a single
> controlled exhale; the breath is the medium that opens the
> tube. A breath-vial is, in the body's reading,* a held breath**.
> Given on three occasions: to a child at first walking, to an
> apprentice at first-firing, to a senior on the day they
> withdraw from the watch. Not used for storage. Kept on a
> small shelf in the recipient's sleeping-quarter and **broken
> at the recipient's death** — the breath released, the working-
> life ended. A breath-vial unbroken at a death is rotated to
> the watch-house and released, with the appropriate witness, on
> the day the dead's name is twice-witnessed.
>
> *The seal* — a flat disc of dark-cooled glass, two fingers
> across, with a single batch-glyph and a single pourer's mark.
> Made for outside use: a Concord clerk's clause-binding ornament,
> a Curation depot's regulation door-stamp, a Communion ship's
> sailing-charm. The body's only working-form intended explicitly
> for external hands. Also the only form the body** will not
> re-fire**: a cracked seal is broken and the pieces given back
> to the witnessing party. The Glassblown will not pour a new
> seal to replace it. The original close, sealed once, is sealed
> once.
>
> A Glassblown house's standing is read partly from its
> *cool-shelf* — the wall of breath-vials kept against the
> sleeping-quarter wall, one for each member of the household
> living and (until twice-witnessed) recently dead. A child
> visiting a Glassblown house counts the cool-shelf without
> being told to. The number on the shelf is the household.

Contributes if absorbed: three named working-forms; cool-shelf
custom; breath-vial death-ritual (integrating 0404's twice-
witnessed); the seal's no-re-fire rule (integrating §2.4); and
the side-pour distinction (which explains why a Glassblown
windowpane in a Pale Curation building does not break silence-
discipline — it is side-pour, not working-craft).

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Bleed 1 — JSON L338 *"offers nothing at all"* vs. §2.4.**
Canon-correct *as a Choir-side reading* — the Choir cannot
compost silica, and more pointedly, the Glassblown's working-form
(demonstrated cooling) is the inverse of the Choir's *time as
digestion that has not yet completed.* The bible should not echo
*offers nothing* without context. Suggested footnote: *"The
Choir's characterisation is canon, but in the same sense as a
one-eyed observer's description of a wall: accurate to what the
observer can register, silent on what the observer cannot."*

**Bleed 2 — 2301 §2.3 sidebar (*Glassblown decline to handle
Concord stones; their reason, predictably, not given*) vs. §2.4.**
§2.4 *does* give the reason: the *first-cane* colour reads to the
Glassblown as *a small Burning the Concord have chosen to make
repeatedly, on purpose.* The 2301 *predictably, not given* is
correct to a Glassblown's *spoken* response, incorrect as a
statement of *whether the reason exists.* The reason is
doctrinal. It is just not voiced. Suggested 2301 footnote: change
to *given only by the silence — the cosmology is in §2.4.*

**Bleed 3 — bible L161-L165 (Revision) vs. §2.4.** The bible
describes Revision as "physically real for the duration." §2.4
asserts Glassblown working-craft, once cooled, cannot be re-warmed
without breaking the body's cosmology. **A Palimpsest player who
Revises a watch-bowl, breath-vial, or seal back to its un-cooled
state has, by Glassblown doctrine, performed a re-firing of
finished work.** Suggested addition (bible §Revision):
*"Working-craft of factions that consider their craft a
demonstration of finishing — the Glassblown most prominently — is
read by those factions as having been re-fired by the Revision.
Glassblown practitioners decline to handle objects Revised in
their presence; whether this extends to objects Revised at
distance is doctrinally open."*

## 5. Merchant's-diary artifact

The 2301 §2.8 widening invited a non-document artifact-type. This
run lands a **Glassblown working-craft cooling-strip with a Pale
Curation cataloguer's footnotes appended** — strip-as-found-object
on the front, cataloguer's attempt-to-file on the back. The
strip's "text" is a series of cooling-temperature notations the
Glassblown read as a sentence and the cataloguer reads as numbers.

> **FRONT (Glassblown working-strip; iron-stylus on prepared
> grey paper):**
>
> *Watch-house** Vell-Two**, day** 19 of the long firing**.
> Pourer:** Hesh**. Cooling-table:** north**. Watcher:** Maro**,
> relieving** Una **at the second-bell. Witness:** Una.*
>
> [a column of small notations: pairs of glyphs with numerals,
> reading downward. The first glyph in each pair is a
> temperature; the second is a held-time. The column ends with
> a single glyph the Glassblown call** the close-mark**, and a
> short ruled line beneath. Below the close-mark, a small
> pressed batch-glyph and a smaller pourer's mark — Hesh's mark —
> faintly off-centre.]
>
> **BACK (Pale Curation cataloguer's hand, three sittings,
> different days; iron-gall ink, neat, faint underlining):**
>
> *Acquired at depot intake by Cataloguer Auch (jr.) on*
> 14 Sev. *Item is a working-strip from watch-house Vell-Two.
> The strip records what appears to be a single firing's full
> cooling-watch. Curation has acquired twelve such strips since
> Severance year 184 and has filed the prior eleven in storage
> with no further action.*
>
> *I read the strip as a temperature-and-time table on intake day
> and found the table internally consistent. Subsequent attempts
> to read it as a sentence — per Field-Manual §IX: "Glassblown
> strips are spoken among the body, not read" — were unsuccessful.
> It may be the sentence requires the watch-house's oral training;
> it may be the sentence is in the pourer's mark, not the column.
> The mark is off-centre. Field-Manual §IX is silent on whether
> the off-centre mark is a sentence-feature or a pour-fault.*
>
> *I asked, at the depot's kiln-house adjunct, whether a Glassblown
> reading the strip would tell me what it said. The adjunct's
> senior pourer, a body named Veshen, looked at the strip for the
> time it took her to draw a single breath, returned it to me,
> and said:* "The firing finished." *I asked whether that was
> the sentence. She said:* "That is the strip." *I have filed
> her response in the Glassblown-side comments column of §IX as
> a permitted gloss. Whether it is a translation, a refusal, or
> a tautology, I cannot determine. The Field-Manual permits any
> of the three.*

The artifact lands four canon items parenthetically: (a) §2.2
paratextual-authorship in operational use; (b) §2.4 cosmology of
finishing as the *content* of the strip's sentence (*the firing
finished*); (c) Pale Curation's good-faith inability to register
what a Glassblown actually communicates, prefiguring §4 Bleed 1;
(d) a different-faction Veshen, casually, to register the name is
not vanishingly rare. The cataloguer's three-sitting filing is
itself the depth-move: a document written *about* something the
document cannot contain.

Bombshell — *the strip's whole content is a single sentence the
Glassblown speak in three words and the Curation files in three
sittings* — lands as a footnote about an off-centre mark.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted (§2.4):** *"The faction's working-craft is, by their
reading, the only honest response to the cataclysm: not to grieve
it, not to remember it, not to consume it, but to finish it."*

Modern register. *Working-craft*, *by their reading*, *honest
response*: contemporary sociological prose. The four-clause
parallel is a cumulative parallelism any modern essayist would
recognise. Plain verbs. Abstract noun *honest response* is
analytical, not liturgical. No archaisms; *the Burning* sits as
a name, not a capitalised mythic abstract; no inverted predicate.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"Verily, the working of the
Glassblown is, by their own teaching, the only honest answer
unto the great Burning: not to grieve it, nor to remember it,
nor to devour it, but to finish it, that the cooling may be made
whole at the last."* Tells: *Verily*; *unto the great Burning*;
*nor / nor / but* archaic parallel; *that the cooling may be made
whole at the last* (KJV final-clause shape). The actual sentence
is none of these.

**Pass.**
