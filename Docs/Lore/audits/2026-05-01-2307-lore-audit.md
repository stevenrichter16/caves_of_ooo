## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible, line 21:* *"the way text on a medieval manuscript was scraped
> off and written over with the old words still faintly visible
> underneath."*

**Strong.** Round 1 stratified the *aftermath*; round-2 Curation work
stratified the *moment-of-breaking* and the *duration*.

**Thin.** **Material witness.** No artifact-class has been treated as
a *historian* in its own right. The Concord's coins are the obvious
candidate: they pre-date the Concord, they travel between every
faction, and their *alloys* encode pre-cataclysm metallurgy.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "The metal remembers"):**

> Concord coins are not minted. They are *gathered.* Every coin in
> circulation pre-dates the cataclysm. Concord assayers know fourteen
> distinct alloys by tooth-feel — five Late Foretime, four Bright
> Period, two Dim, three of unknown origin. The trade-secret is that
> the alloys are running out: salvage-fields are picked over, new
> finds flat for ten years. The number of *unknown-origin* coins is
> rising, not by new finds but by re-classification — assays the
> Concord cannot place are increasingly written *unknown* rather than
> *Foretime*. Outsiders read this as imprecision. Within the Concord
> it is *honesty under inventory pressure.*

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Charter Receipt (1901), Reconciled Texts and R-7 (2219),
redactions, salvaged marginalia.

**Thin.** **The buyer's ledger.** Every Charter Receipt has a
*seller's* voice; the corpus contains no *counter-receipts*. A trade
has two memories. The Curation, with its Reconciled Texts protocol,
would file matched seller/buyer pairs as plural sleeves. None exist.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "Why no buyer's ledger survives"):**

> The Concord teaches buyers to keep ledgers. Apprentices copy *the
> buyer who does not record is the merchant's prey* in their first
> week. The teaching is unenforced; in practice buyer's ledgers are
> kept on cheaper stock, in kitchens not vaults, lost to small fires.
> The Concord knows and does not correct. A surviving paired archive
> would force the Concord to *ratify* its own history; the Concord
> prefers history *commercial* — open to renegotiation.
>
> Three buyer's ledgers are extant. One is held by the Curation under
> sleeve R-11, paired with a Concord Charter Receipt that contradicts
> it on price (4 silver versus 9). Director Ven has not adjudicated.
> The Concord has not requested the file. Both parties prefer the
> disagreement *unfiled.*

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** Cross-faction names for cataclysm, joining, the dead, the
substrate, the Choir, the player.

**Thin.** **Cross-faction names for *debt*.** The Concord's central
concept; every faction translates it through its own metabolism.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "What the Concord calls *debt*"):**

> - **Concord:** *the open ledger.* Neutral. The default condition.
> - **Choir:** *the unfed.* Debt is the world owed nourishment and
>   not yet receiving it. Concord coin is *food postponed.*
> - **Palimpsest:** *the unwritten line.* A long-overdue account
>   *flickers* the way an unstable Glimpse layer does.
> - **Pale Curation:** *the open file.* Closing it is clerical;
>   morally weightless. Concord and Curation transact easily.
> - **Brine Communion:** *the held breath.* The Brine clear all
>   accounts at the turn of each tide; Concord traders dealing with
>   them adjust to a four-times-daily cadence.
> - **Thermoclaves:** *cold-iron.* Iron not yet forged. They do not
>   lend, only spot-trade — the open-ledger principle is morally
>   suspect because it lets iron stay cold.
> - **Glassblown Remnant:** *the unfired.* Debt is a vessel still in
>   sand-state. Closing the account *fires* it; the closing is the
>   making. The Concord has stopped trying to extend them credit.

The Concord's *open ledger* and the Choir's *unfed* are the same
shape read backwards. The factions disagree only on whether the
metabolism is *economic* or *biological*.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Substrate-leak, Choir metabolism, Echo Reading limits,
metaphysics-meet, composted-matter readings.

**Thin.** **The Concord's metaphysics of *worth*.** The bible treats
the Concord as a *gentle* faction, but a faction that says *everything
is tradeable* is making a substrate-claim as bold as the Choir's
*everything is composted*. The claim has not been written.

**Concrete addition (bible expansion):**

> The Concord doctrine: *value is a layer of the substrate the
> cataclysm did not break.* The substrate has at least three known
> strata — material (the Choir), temporal (the Palimpsest), and
> *consensual* (the Concord). The cataclysm cracked the first two;
> the third was untouched, because consensus is renegotiable. Value
> is not stored *in* an object but *between* parties. The cataclysm
> could not break a thing that lived between people, because it broke
> nobody's willingness to agree.
>
> Edge case: a debt incurred to a party who has died (or, in the
> Choir's case, dispersed) is held by the Concord itself as *open
> ledger* and resolved against the next available equivalent party
> — heir, descendant, faction successor. The Concord considers itself
> *consensus-of-last-resort*. This is not advertised. Outsiders who
> learn of it sometimes conclude the Concord is *running the world*;
> Concord traders find this charge baffling — *we are only holding
> the open accounts, friend.*
>
> The Choir, by *we are one*, claims to inherit *every* pre-cataclysm
> Choir-adjacent debt. The Concord has, with great courtesy, declined
> to honour this claim for fifty years. Both consider the dispute
> *open ledger* in the strong sense.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** Choir Communion, Palimpsest Transcription, Annihilist
endings, Stalled Walkers, refusal-of-apotheosis, pulled-form failed
apotheoses.

**Thin.** **Concord apotheosis.** The Concord has been treated as a
faction that *trades with* apotheoses, not one that has its own.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "The Balanced Account"):**

> A trader who closes every open ledger they have with every party in
> the world — including the Concord itself, which holds debts on
> behalf of the dead — becomes a *closed party*. Doctrine holds that
> a zeroed party is exempt from further consensus: they no longer
> require agreement to act. The doctrine has never been verified. The
> Concord teaches it as a *limit case*, the way mathematicians teach
> division by zero. Two traders are alleged to have approached: one
> closed every ledger the Concord could file and was thereafter *not
> seen*; one made it to a single open account, owed to a child who
> refused payment, and stopped. The Concord considers the second the
> *true* limit case — *the doctrine works only for those with no
> living refusers.*

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Choir pronouns, Palimpsest tense, Brine joining,
Thermoclave fire-grammar, Glassblown unsaid mood, Curation catalogued
mood.

**Thin.** **Concord grammar.** Round 1 gave their voice but no syntax.

**Concrete addition (bible, "The trade tense"):**

> Concord traders speak in two tenses where outsiders hear one. The
> distinction is *whether the action has been balanced.*
>
> - *I sold the cloth* (open) — the trade happened; the ledger is
>   open.
> - *I sold-up the cloth* (closed) — the trade happened *and the
>   ledger is closed*. The infix *-up-* (taught as a clipping of
>   *upright*) marks the closure, not the action.
>
> A trader who says *I sold the bread* without the closer is not
> lying — they are flagging an open ledger. Outsiders hear the form
> as completed; the apprentice within earshot hears *do not assume
> the price is the price.*
>
> *I closed-up the account* is correct Concord. *I closed the
> account* is grammatical but reads as *I terminated the relationship
> without satisfying the ledger* — the Concord's word for what other
> factions call *betrayal*, and the only thing the Concord can do
> that another Concord trader will refuse to deal with them about.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Strong.** Tuesday Mornings, calendars, dead-name customs,
cross-Tuesday markets.

**Thin.** **Concord weddings.** No marriage-customs are written for
any faction. The Concord — whose entire metaphysic is *consensus
between parties* — is the obvious place to ground them.

**Concrete addition (bible, "Concord weddings"):**

> A Concord wedding is a public *opening* of a new ledger. The two
> parties, in the presence of an assayer, exchange one coin each.
> Each coin must be of an alloy the receiving party has not previously
> held. The ledger so opened *cannot be closed*: there is no *we
> married-up* in the trade tense. Marriage is the only ledger
> doctrine permits to remain *open by design*.
>
> A divorce is rare but not absent. The two coins are returned to
> their origin alloy-pair via the assayer's registry; both parties
> are issued a single small token of *unfired Glassblown sand* — the
> Glassblown supply this for free, by treaty older than living memory
> — and the ledger is *unmarried*, not closed. Divorce is *a ledger
> that proved to be not-a-ledger after all*.
>
> The Glassblown's role in the divorce ritual is the only formal
> duty any faction holds in any other faction's domestic life.
> Neither faction will discuss why. The Choir thinks the answer is
> obvious — *sand is what cannot be remembered without firing; the
> ledger that was not a ledger needs a substance that cannot witness
> itself.*

### Dimension 8 — Marginalia and footnotes

**Strong.** Tags, ledgers, receipts, redactions, intake cards,
catechisms, R-7.

**Thin.** **The endorsement-line.** Concord coins, by ancient
practice, are *signed* by their last assayer with a single chisel-mark
on the rim. The chain of marks is a coin's *endorsement-line*. Section
5 pays.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord (round 2)

The Concord has Periods, the Charter Receipt, an alloy-stock crisis,
the *open ledger* doctrine, the Balanced Account, the trade tense,
weddings. It does not yet have a *named senior figure* the way the
Curation has Director Ven and Halen.

**Proposed senior figure: Senior Assayer Tobir Veshen.**

Thirty-one years in continuous service. A *third-tooth* — Concord
term for an assayer who has lost the front teeth and works by the
molars. She has never been wrong about an alloy, including the seven
cases where the Curation independently disagreed; in all seven the
Curation's cards were eventually amended to her reading.

Veshen carries the alloy-stock crisis quietly. She is the one
re-classifying *Foretime-uncertain* coins as *unknown*. Her private
estimate, told only to two apprentices: Foretime stock will be
functionally exhausted in *eleven years*. The Concord has never
operated without Foretime alloys.

Her doctrinal work-around: she is teaching her apprentices to assay
*Choir-deposited matter* — mycelium-bound mineral that sometimes
carries trace alloys the Choir has digested and re-deposited
unaltered. The hierarchy has not endorsed this. Veshen has not
concealed it. She is, in the catalogued mood the Curation respects,
*experimenting-fil-s* — open of record. She would rather the Concord
*fail at a new thing* than *succeed at the maintained one.*

The Choir calls her *the slow tooth.* It has, more than once, offered
her the Communion — gently. She has declined each time using a phrase
written into the Concord apprentice catechism: *the ledger is not
closed-up. I am not yet a closed party. Thank you for the offer.*
Per round-2's refusal-of-apotheosis finding (sibling 2219), Veshen
is the most *almost-formal* of the Choir's interlocutors.

Veshen and Halen correspond. Cordial and slow. Halen has filed three
of Veshen's letters in sleeve R-11 — the buyer's-ledger sleeve from
section 2. R-11's connection to Veshen has not been disclosed to the
Concord. The Curation is waiting.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a)** `RotChoir.json:338` has the Choir saying the Concord *"offers
sweetness"* — almost dismissive — while subsequent audits treat the
Concord as a substrate-claim faction with a coherent metaphysics.

**Resolution.** The Choir is honest within its ontology. To its
metabolism-grammar, the Concord *does* offer only sweetness —
sucrose-class molecules plus the small psychological warmth of a
closed ledger. The Choir cannot perceive *consensus* as a
substrate-stratum. The line is in character and should *not* be
revised. **Recommend the bible add a margin gloss:**

> When the Choir lists what other factions offer, it is naming what
> *its grammar can metabolise.* Sweetness, records, memory, sand are
> things its ontology permits. Consensus, fire-discipline, the
> unsaid, the catalogued mood are not. The Choir's lists are
> therefore *short* by walker standards — the player should be
> permitted to feel this as a limit of the speaker, not the world.

**(b)** `ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md:443` (Sien-Nam vignette: *"...we
understand."*). The whole vignette is a *closed-up ledger between two
parties without coin*. It would be a Concord wedding, structurally,
but for the absent assayer and absent coin. The vignette argues,
quietly, that *the Concord's metaphysics is wrong*: consensus does not
require an assayer or a coin. **Keep the contradiction.** The Concord
is a faction whose doctrine the Choir embodies a counterexample to,
daily, in the persons of its own absorbed.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

A Concord document. The bombshell rides on the *endorsement-line*.

**Title (in-game):** *Day-book of trader Mer Hin, Salt-Hinge market,
fourth Tuesday of the rains.*

```
[Concord day-book — coarse paper, ruled with the four-column grid:
 GIVEN / TAKEN / CLOSED-UP / OPEN. Cheap stock; one corner water-
 damaged. Unremarkable except for a single coin pressed into the
 binding margin, wax-sealed there by the trader. The seal is unbroken.]

  GIVEN                  TAKEN                CLOSED-UP   OPEN
  3 spool brine-rope     1 Brine joining-bead    yes        —
  1 stick salt-resin     2 silver (Bright)        yes        —
  4 portion dried fish   1 silver (Foretime)      yes        —
  1 Glassblown sand-tok  ▓ (none — declined)      —          (note)
  6 thread cotton        2 silver (Dim)           yes        —
  2 portion dried fish   1 silver (UNKNOWN)       —         (note)

  NOTE on the unknown: Veshen looked at it. Said *third-tooth not
  certain.* I asked which alloy she thought. She said *the alloy is
  not yet a thing I have a name for.* I closed the trade because the
  buyer was leaving in the morning, but the ledger remains OPEN. V.
  asked me to keep the coin from circulation. It is sealed in the
  binding margin. If the day-book is lost, the coin is lost.

  NOTE on the sand-token: Glassblown declined. Cordial. They did not
  say why. Veshen says declined Glassblown trades are increasing —
  fourteen this season at Salt-Hinge alone. She did not say what she
  thinks it means.

  Closed three. Open one (the unknown). Sand-token still on hand.

  — Mer Hin, fourth Tuesday of the rains.
```

The wax-sealed coin in the binding margin, if extracted, has six
chisel-marks on its rim — its endorsement-line. The first five are
known assayers. **The sixth is Veshen's.** The first mark is
Foretime, illegible. The next four step through Bright, Dim, late
Dim, and *unknown* — the *same coin* re-classified *downward* through
a sequence of assayers, each more uncertain than the last. Veshen's
mark is *unknown*. She did not make a new classification; she signed
her name to *not knowing*.

The bombshell, parenthetically: **alloy-recognition is degrading
across generations of assayers, not just stock.** The Concord is
losing the *language* in which it identifies its own substrate.
Veshen has stopped pretending. The coin is *evidence she does not
yet want filed*; she has asked Mer Hin to hold it because the Concord
has no protocol for what to do with a coin whose endorsement-line
tells the Concord's own decline. The Glassblown's increasing declined
trades is the day-book's quieter note — they may already know.

Plant: Mer Hin's day-book at any Concord apprentice's bench. The
coin must be deliberately extracted (a small destruction mini-action)
for the endorsement-line to be readable. The Concord will not ask the
player for it back; the Curation will, and offer sleeve filing in
exchange.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"A Concord wedding is a public* opening *of a new ledger. The two
> parties, in the presence of an assayer, exchange one coin each.
> Each coin must be of an alloy the receiving party has not previously
> held."*

No archaisms, no apostrophe-names, no Tower/Aedra/Daedra. Three plain
present-tense sentences; the weight rides on mercantile vocabulary
(*opening*, *ledger*, *assayer*, *alloy*) and the constraint clause
(*not previously held*). A TES book would open *"In the holy custom
of the Concord-folk it is meet that two souls shall exchange the
coin-of-troth..."* — adjective front-loaded, ritual posture, divine
sanction. Mine has no sanction; the assayer is a clerk, the
constraint is logistical, and the gravity comes only from work the
audit has already done to make a *ledger* feel like a
substrate-stratum. Pass.
