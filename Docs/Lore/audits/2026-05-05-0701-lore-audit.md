## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible line 21:* *"The cataclysm is the founding event of the
> entire world. It did not merely destroy the old civilization —
> it overwrote reality itself, imperfectly."*

**Strong.** Eight bodies hold eight cataclysm-readings. The
fourth-seam doctrine (0601) holds compost and Glimpse as one
matter under two cosmologies.

**Thin.** Every existing reading names *what broke;* none names
*what was tended through.* The seam-body's affirmative register
needs a cataclysm-account.

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar, "The counter-cataclysm
in tended-grammar"):

> By the seam-body's affirmative register, the cataclysm was the
> moment a single matter first **required tending across the
> overlap.** The wall, the cup, the child's shoe — matter the
> Choir could compost and the Palimpsest could render — were
> tended in the gap between those two operations.
>
> *Tended,* in this register, is neither preserved nor consumed.
> It is the act a body performs when both preservation and
> consumption are its working grammars and the matter is one
> matter. The corpus's central thematic axis is not, in tended-
> grammar, *transformation versus preservation.* It is *the
> matter the body does not get to choose.* The cataclysm did not
> break the world. It tended what the world's two great forces
> could not divide.

The eight bodies remember the cataclysm by what each lost. The
seam-body remembers it by what was tended *while* each lost.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Four seam-rooms, four seam-apotheoses, four shared
grammatical marks. Working anatomy.

**Thin.** No mechanical rule for **how** a working member becomes
tended. The eight body-names describe the *state*; nothing names
the **transition.**

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The signing"):

> A working member becomes tended by the **signing** — a single
> moment in which a senior of the host body **does not ask**
> about a working-task the member has performed off-roster, while
> the member is performing it. The senior's not-asking is the
> signing. There is no document. The signing is recorded only in
> the senior's subsequent willingness to sign the tended-stub at
> the senior's own retirement.
>
> The signing has no opposite. Once tended, a member cannot be
> un-tended. A senior who later asks about the off-roster task
> retroactively unsigns nothing; the tending is held in the
> senior's prior not-asking. Two seniors in the corpus have asked
> their juniors and recanted; both asked too late and the tending
> held.
>
> The signing is the seam-body's only mechanical operation that
> requires **two** bodies present. Every other seam operation can
> be performed by one body in private. The signing requires the
> host body's senior, the host body's junior, and the seam-body's
> working presence between them.

The seam-body now has rooms, marks, apotheoses, record-forms, and
its first mechanic. Internal mechanical logic complete.

Five shared marks at five grammatical levels — phonetic,

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Glassblown Remnant

Rotation: Brine (1301, 0601), Curation (2001), Thermoclaves
(2101, 0401), Concord (0501). Glassblown last had dedicated
asymmetry attention through R3 depth-audit material at 0001.
Glassblown is overdue.

**Etymology.** *Glassblown Remnant.* From the survivors' own term
*the blown,* which in the founders' generation meant *those whose
breath was returned to them by the kiln.* *Remnant,* by founders'
usage, is not what is left over; it is what *remains breathing*
after a fire that should have ended breathing. The full term was
*the blown remain.* Modern usage truncates the verb phrase into a
noun and loses the breathing.

The founders' burning is the cataclysm in Glassblown reading.
Their term for it is *the glass-day* — the day matter and breath
were forced briefly into the same condition. Survivors spent the
following decade refusing to fire any vessel they had blown.
Bowls, cups, panes — all left as cold paste, *un-bid* by any
kiln. By the third generation, the refusal had become the craft.

**The un-firing.** Every Glassblown working day at the morning
hearth-stir, every apprentice on shift carries one cold-paste
piece from the long shelf to the inspection bench, examines it
for spoilage, and returns it un-fired to the shelf. This is the
*un-firing.* The piece has been on the shelf, by tradition,
longer than the apprentice has been alive. The inspection adds
and removes nothing. Its purpose is to *not fire it.*

By doctrine, every cold-paste piece on the long shelf would, if
fired, become the founders' last vessels. The founders' last
vessels were never made; their absence is what *the blown remain*
names. A piece's biography reads: *not-fired this morning, not-
fired at the second bell, not-fired by apprentice [stub-number],
not-fired by master [stub-number].* Biographies are kept in
slip-cases, like the gloss-shelf.

The seam-body's signing is performed once per member, off-roster.
The un-firing is performed daily, on roster, by every apprentice.
Same operation, different frequencies — the maintenance of a
working absence as the body's primary working object. By the
seam-body's reading, Glassblown is the corpus's only body whose
**entire working life is held.**
