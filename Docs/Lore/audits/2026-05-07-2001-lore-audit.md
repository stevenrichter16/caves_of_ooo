## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (1501 §2.1):** *"The Cooling, by the
bearings-pilots' working-form, is whatever event
left a held position seven sea-miles east of the
second basin the chart will not let go of."*

**Strong.** Third stratum sourced in chart-
persistence under carry-forward.

**Thin.** 1501 does not name **how the held
position is held**. Trust-as-wear (1501 §3) cannot
account for a feature older than any working-pilot
in current memory: trust is mediated by the senior
whose chart the apprentice reads; the dot has no
senior. A second migration-form is at work.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm,
append after the 1501 third-account paragraph):**

> *The held position is not, by working-form,
> copied. It is* carried*. Bearings-pilots
> distinguish two operations during the retirement-
> day in the boat's hold: copying — trusted features
> the apprentice has worn into recognising,
> transferred onto the new sheet by hand — and
> carrying, features already on the apprentice's
> new sheet by the time she takes it from her line-
> bag, set there by no mark her hand made. A
> bearings-pilot asked when she put the dot on the
> new sheet replies in working-register:* "i did
> not put it there. it was there when i opened the
> sheet that morning." *The held mark writes itself
> onto a new chart through the apprentice's
> apprentice-voyages — not through copying — and
> the retirement-day's working-day is for copying
> alone.*

The third account's authority is **sea-internal**:
the apprentice's hand carries the held mark by a
mechanism the body holds is not the apprentice's
work, not the senior's gift, but the sea's claim
transferring through the apprentice's working-time
at sea. Authorship now has a fourth canon shape:
**carrying as sea-through-hand**.

### 2.2 Unreliable in-world authorship

**Quote (1501 §2.2):** *"…the carry-forward is the
apprentice's wear-pattern made paper, not a
transmission of the senior's authorship."*

**Strong.** Trust-as-wear canon.

**Thin.** Trust-as-wear accounts for chart-features
that vary across charts (Doss's fourteen Resta
shares; Yelm's three Resta also held). It cannot
account for the dot, which appears on every Klem-3
chart at identical position with non-identical
depth. If trust-as-wear were the only mechanism,
the dot's presence would be probabilistic. The
certainty demands a different operation.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Brine Communion
subsection, append after the carry-forward
paragraph):**

> *The carry-forward sitting is by working-tradition
> two operations the body holds distinct in breath
> but not in name. The apprentice copies trusted
> features in the morning shift, with the senior
> present, in working-light — slow pen-marks, an
> out-breath at each. The apprentice carries held
> marks in the afternoon shift, with the senior
> absent (gone above for the keepsake-decision), in
> working-darkness — no pen-mark made, the new
> sheet held flat on the hold-table, the
> apprentice's left hand resting on the position
> the held mark has come to occupy. The senior,
> returning, finds the held marks already on the
> sheet. Carrying-breath is held continuously
> through the operation; every bearings-pilot can
> hear the difference across the hold's working-
> light.*

Authorship-doctrine sustains by four
recategorisations: paper-as-tackle, count-without-
cadence, copying-as-wear, **carrying-as-sea-
through-hand**.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (1501 §2.4):** *"The chart is migrated as
features-by-trusting…"*

**Strong.** Trust-as-third-migration-mode canon.

**Thin.** §2.1–§2.2 identify a fourth migration-
mode the 1501 sentence does not name: **the held
mark, sea-as-wear-through-hand, mediated by sea-
hours rather than working-time-with-senior**. An
apprentice who has logged the requisite sea-hours
carries the held mark whether or not she has read
her senior's chart at that position; an apprentice
who has not logged the hours cannot carry it even
if she has copied the position by hand.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Brine Communion
sidebar, append):**

> *Four migration-modes hold the body's archive:
> cadence-bodily for song; count-by-handing for
> bead-tackle; trust-as-wear for chart-features the
> apprentice has read on the senior's chart;
> carrying-as-sea-through-hand for held marks. The
> first three are senior-mediated and complete in
> working-time. The fourth is sea-mediated and
> complete in sea-time. A retirement attempt that
> fails the sea-time threshold is, by the body's
> working-form, an* unfinished retirement*: the
> senior continues another voyage, the apprentice
> another season of sea-hours, the retirement is
> held until the held marks appear. The body has
> no calendar for this; the sea has.*

A **fourth cadence-authority** the body defers to
without canonising: **sea-time itself** (distinct
from the drift, which is local-working-day). Four
cadence-authorities for the Communion: working-
deck (body's senior-mediated discipline); depot-
shelf (Curation, cross-body); drift-line (no body,
working-day); held-mark / sea-time (sea-internal,
ancestral). Same architectural shape as the
Thermoclaves.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine Communion (held mark, carrying-breath, four-place architecture)

> ### The held mark
>
> A small uncaptioned dot, stroke, or short curve
> on a deep-runner's chart that has *come* to the
> chart rather than been *put* on it. The Klem-3
> dot 7 sea-miles east of the second basin is the
> body's most-cited example; every long-running
> deep-runner has at least one. A chart with no
> held marks is, by working-tradition, *unsettled*
> — the bearings-pilot has not logged enough sea-
> time for the sea to claim a position on her sheet.
> A bearings-pilot asked what a held mark means:*
> "no hazard. it is held." *Asked what holds it:
> silence.
>
> ### The carrying-breath
>
> The senior leaves the hold for the keepsake
> decision; the apprentice unrolls her new sheet
> on the hold-table and rests her left hand on the
> table. She draws a single long held breath and
> does not exhale until her hand has felt the held
> marks resolve under it. Held marks resolve as a
> small warmth at the wear-callus of the second
> knuckle; the apprentice can locate them by feel.
> By the time the senior returns, the new sheet
> has the held marks already on it — drawn by no
> hand the apprentice can name. The apprentice
> exhales when the senior's hand reaches the hold-
> table.
>
> ### The four-place boat architecture
>
> Working-deck, drift-line, depot-shelf, hold-
> table. The working-deck is the body's discipline.
> The drift-line is the working-day current the
> body sails by but does not own. The depot-shelf
> is Curation. The hold-table is where the held
> marks resolve — the boat's only working-place
> the body holds is sea-internal during use. A
> senior teaches the four-place reading by walking
> the four with her apprentice on the apprentice's
> first voyage in bearings-position, naming none.
> The first three she walks; the fourth she sits
> at, and the apprentice sits across from her, and
> they do not speak.
>
> ### What the body does not do
>
> The body does not canonise the carrying-breath
> as an operation distinct from copying. The body
> does not canonise the hold-table as
> architecturally different from any other working-
> table. The body does not canonise sea-time as a
> separate measure from working-time. The four-
> place reading is a Curation cataloguer's after
> twelve years of Klem-3 intake, sitting in Depot
> 11's working-shelf since 412–04–07–22–prov. The
> Communion knows it is filed; the Communion does
> not ask. Both bodies' silence is the form for an
> inter-body finding neither has canon to absorb.

**Quoted (§3, the carrying-breath):** *"Held
marks resolve as a small warmth at the wear-
callus of the second knuckle; the apprentice can
locate them by feel."*

Working-modern register. Plain present indicative,
no archaism. *Held marks*, *small warmth*, *wear-
callus*, *second knuckle*, *locate by feel* are
working-clerical or plain-physical — not
theological, not capitalised, not inverted.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"The Held Marks do
resolve as a Warmth small upon the Wear-Callus of
the Second Knuckle; thus the Apprentice findeth
them by Feeling."* Tells: capitalised *Held Marks
/ Warmth / Wear-Callus*; *do resolve* (KJV
emphatic do); *findeth*; *thus*; *upon*. Actual
sentence has none of these.
