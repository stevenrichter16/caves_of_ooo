## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible line 208:* *"They are not at war. They don't hate each
> other the way the Thermoclaves and Brine Communion do."*

**Strong.** The bible distinguishes the Choir-Palimpsest
*incompatibility* from Thermoclave-Brine *enmity.* Two registers
of conflict at the cosmic level.

**Thin.** **The Concord's origin in conflict-grammar.** The
corpus has the Concord as adjudicator, but never says *who first
asked them.* If Thermoclaves and Brine are at war, who issued the
first cease-clause? If the Choir and Palimpsest are too cold to
fight, who issued the *first non-belligerence finding*?

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar, "The first close"):

> The Concord traces itself to a single closed clause,
> archive-marker **C-1**. The clause's parties are not the
> Thermoclaves and the Brine, contrary to the obvious guess.
> The clause is between two pre-Concord clerks who could not
> agree which of them owed the other a single ledger-stone.
> One clerk drafted a closure; the other countersigned. They
> filed the closure with no third party. The third party
> arrived later — a passing senior who read the document and
> said *this is itself the first.* The senior did not sign.
> The senior named the body the Concord. The senior's name is
> not in C-1. The senior is in no other clause. By Concord
> doctrine the senior is the first reader and not the founder.
> Closure precedes the body that closes.

The cataclysm-era founders dissolve into a **disclaimed
non-founder** — the body that adjudicates origins refuses to
adjudicate its own.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

> *2101's diary, Veshen marginalia:* *"I have not closed this
> clause. It is filed open against my own ledger."*

**Strong.** Veshen's first-person voice carries weight precisely
because she is the *standing endorsement* and her *failure to
close* is a Concord event.

**Thin.** **The juniors who close what Veshen will not.** The
corpus has Veshen as the senior-who-cannot. It has no specimen of
the Concord's other voice — the *junior who closes anyway*, by
procedure, against senior reservation.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "Procedural-close
versus standing-close"):

> A clause may be closed two ways. **Standing-close:** the
> standing endorsement countersigns; the close is immediate,
> binding, and recorded with the senior's hand. **Procedural-
> close:** any three juniors of agreed seniority countersign in
> sequence, after a tally-day of no objection from the standing
> endorsement; the close is binding but recorded with all three
> juniors' hands and a *standing reservation glyph* in the
> margin if Veshen has filed one.
>
> The two are equally binding in law. They are not equally
> binding in custom. A procedurally-closed clause is *closed*
> but *unfavoured.* The Concord's own clerks treat the
> documents differently — procedural-close documents are filed
> in a parallel cabinet, the *junior shelf*, and are not pulled
> for cross-reference unless explicitly requested. A walker
> trying to argue from a procedural-close to a standing
> endorsement is asking the body to read sideways.

A second class of close, **legally equal and customarily lesser**
— the Concord contains within its own grammar a class of work
the body does not love.

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Thin.** **What other factions call a Concord close.** The
corpus has the Concord's term for itself; not the words other
bodies use for what the Concord does.

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar, "Names for closure"):

> - **Concord:** *closure* — the act of the clause becoming
>   non-pending.
> - **Choir:** *the seal-fruiting* — the Choir treats a closure
>   the way it treats a fruiting body's spore-release. The
>   document is *done* but its consequences are *out and growing.*
> - **Palimpsest:** *the over-stroke* — a closure adds a layer.
>   The clause's open form is still readable underneath. The
>   Palimpsest does not consider closures final. They are merely
>   the most recent layer.
> - **Pale Curation:** *the close-card* — Curation files the
>   document at intake when it is closed, with the close-glyph
>   in the upper-right; reservation glyphs are still permitted.
> - **Brine Communion:** *the slack-tide* — a closure is the
>   moment between rising and falling. Brine doctrine: closures
>   do not last; the next tide reopens them. Concord doctrine:
>   the next tide is a *new clause.* The two never reconcile.
> - **Thermoclaves (Floor):** *the kept ash* — the close is the
>   ash from the clause's burning, kept in the hearth.
> - **Thermoclaves (Ember):** *the warmed page* — the document
>   is still warm to the touch when closed.
> - **Thermoclaves (Refusal):** *(no term)* — Refusal grammar
>   has no close-particle, by the same rule that drops the
>   count-particle. A Refusal keeper saying a clause is closed
>   omits the verb.
> - **Glassblown:** *the breath-stop* — closure is the moment
>   the glass leaves the lung.

Six distinct readings of the same act. The Brine reading is the
**incompatible one** — the only faction that does not accept
closure as final. The Concord and Brine settle escrow against
this disagreement: every Concord-Brine clause includes a
*tide-clause* allowing reopen on a single named astronomical
event.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

> *Bible line 208 (paraphrased):* The Concord adjudicates open
> clauses; the Concord cannot extinguish a clause unilaterally.

**Thin.** **Where the open clauses are kept.** The corpus uses
the *open ledger* as a phrase but has no specimen of the
physical record.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The open-braid"):

> Every still-open Concord clause is a single knot tied into a
> leather braid hanging in the senior endorsement's hall. The
> knot's pattern encodes the clause-number, archive-marker, and
> the senior who first read it; clerks read the knot at audience
> and find the parchment by knot-shape. When a clause closes the
> knot is **cut**, not untied, and the freed thread is woven
> into the **closed-braid** — a separate cord kept in a sealed
> case, never displayed. A closure cannot un-cut a thread; a
> reopen requires a *new* knot in the open-braid, with a glyph
> linking it to the closed-thread. The closed-thread itself is
> never re-tied.
>
> The *senior reservation glyph* on a clause is a single bone
> bead threaded through the knot. Veshen has thirty-one such
> beads on the open-braid. None on the closed-braid — by the
> rule that closed-thread is never re-tied, the bead is removed
> at close; tradition and the rules forbid the bead's reuse.
> Veshen's beads are kept in a small wooden bowl beside the
> open-braid. The bowl is full enough that they sometimes
> rattle when a junior walks past. Veshen has not added to it
> in eleven years.

A physical record whose **shape and weight changes as the body's
conscience does.** The open-braid is the Concord's body the way
the mycelium is the Choir's.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

> Concord = Closure (apotheosis).

**Thin.** **The walker's apotheosis-close.** A walker at Concord
Exalted — what does *closure* mean as a personal end-state? A
walker is not a clause.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The walker's
clause"):

> A walker offered the Concord apotheosis is asked to **draft
> their own clause and close it with their own hand.** The
> clause's body is the walker's life as enumerated by the
> walker — what was begun, what was owed, what was promised.
> The clause is not corrected by the Concord. It is read by
> three juniors and the standing endorsement, who confirm
> only that the clause is *legible.*
>
> The walker closes it themselves. The close is the apotheosis.
> Once closed, the walker is no longer a party to any open
> clause anywhere; the Concord searches the open-braid and cuts
> every knot in which the walker is named. The cut threads are
> not woven into the closed-braid. They are given to the walker
> in a small leather purse. The walker may do with them what
> they wish.
>
> A walker who declines to draft is not denied the apotheosis;
> they are returned to *open* status and may try again. A
> walker who drafts and refuses to close is held in the open-
> braid as a single new knot, *self-knotted*, and the standing
> endorsement files a senior reservation. The bead is added.
> Veshen has filed two of these in her tenure.

The first apotheosis where the **author and adjudicator are the
same person.** The Concord's deepest doctrine is procedural; the
walker's apotheosis is procedurally complete and customarily
unprecedented.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Thin.** **Concord verb-aspects.** The corpus has Brine's
*remembered tense* and Refusal's *uncounted*. Concord has the
*open* / *closed* binary, but no productive verb-aspect.

**Concrete addition** (bible footnote to "Procedural-close vs
standing-close"):

> Concord grammar marks every speech-act with a **clause-aspect**:
> the speaker's relation to whether the act is *open*, *closing*,
> or *closed*. *I owe you a stone* (open). *I am owing you a
> stone* (closing — the act of payment is in progress). *I owed
> you a stone* (closed — the debt is extinguished, the past tense
> is the close-particle). A senior speaking *I am owing* has not
> yet closed; juniors will not file from a senior's *closing*-
> form, only from a senior's *closed*-form.
>
> The aspect is mandatory in clerical speech and informal among
> the laity, who often drop it. A walker speaking with a Concord
> clerk who does not adopt the aspect will be asked, gently, to
> rephrase. A walker who refuses is filed under *unaspected
> petitioner*, a category whose clauses are by custom heard last.

A grammar where **tense itself is an adjudication.** The Concord
cannot say a thing about an exchange without committing a
position on its closure-state.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Thin.** **The Hall of Open.** Pair with mid-fire day (2101),
dry-walker's day (1301), junior cataloguer's day (2001).

**Concrete addition** (bible sidebar, "A morning in the Hall of
Open"):

> Pre-dawn: a junior arrives, opens the small high windows. The
> open-braid is on its hook against the long west wall. The
> bowl of beads is on the shelf below it. The junior does not
> touch the braid; the morning's first touch is reserved.
>
> Dawn: the standing endorsement enters. Veshen in our era;
> traditionally the senior of the day. She walks the length of
> the open-braid once, slowly, fingertips trailing — the
> **morning-read.** A clerk junior follows three paces behind
> with a slate. Veshen names any knot whose bead has shifted
> overnight; the junior records the clause-number and the
> direction of the shift (toward the wall = pressing for close;
> away = pressing for review). The morning-read is the only
> moment the open-braid is read by a single hand without a
> petitioner present.
>
> Mid-morning: the **petitioner audiences**. Petitioners arrive
> and are heard in the order in which their clauses' beads have
> last been seen to shift — most recent first. An *unaspected
> petitioner* is heard after all aspected ones. A petitioner
> with a *senior reservation glyph* on their clause is not
> heard at audience; they may speak only when Veshen lifts the
> bead from the knot, an act she does at her discretion and
> which obligates her to a finding by dusk.
>
> Mid-day: silence in the Hall. No clerk speaks. The open-braid
> is left undisturbed. Veshen takes a meal at her desk, alone.
>
> Afternoon: the **filings** — closures, openings, reopens. A
> junior reads each parchment aloud once; the standing
> endorsement countersigns standing-closes; juniors execute
> procedural-closes at their long table. The braid changes
> shape. The bowl of beads gains and loses.
>
> Dusk: the **dropped-bead count.** A junior counts the beads in
> the bowl. The count is filed, sealed, and kept by date. The
> bowl is never emptied. A walker who has petitioned at the
> Hall and noticed the bowl's count later — months or years
> later — finds it has, against expectation, *grown.*

The Hall is the open-braid's body, and Veshen's day is the
braid's diurnal cycle.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord R3 (the open-braid)

The corpus has used the Concord as connective tissue for three
audits. R3 promotes it to a body with its own anatomy.

**The mechanics now in the corpus:**

- **Open-braid / closed-braid** (Dim 4). The physical record of
  every still-open and ever-closed clause. The body's
  conscience is a literal weight on the wall.
- **Senior reservation bead** (Dim 4). A bone bead threaded
  through a knot signalling Veshen's hold. Thirty-one on the
  open-braid; bowl rattles as juniors pass.
- **Standing-close vs procedural-close** (Dim 2). Two equally
  binding closures, one customarily favoured. The Concord
  contains within itself a class of work the body does not love.
- **Clause-aspect grammar** (Dim 6). Open / closing / closed as
  mandatory verb inflection in clerical speech.
- **Walker's clause** (Dim 5). The apotheosis: draft your life
  as a clause; close it yourself. Veshen has filed two
  *self-knotted* refusals.
- **Hall of Open** (Dim 7). The morning-read, the audiences in
  bead-shift order, the dropped-bead count that grows.
- **C-1** (Dim 1). The disclaimed non-founder. The Concord
  refuses to adjudicate its own origins.

**Cross-faction integration.** The Concord's grammar of *closure*
mutates across factions (Dim 3): *seal-fruiting* (Choir),
*over-stroke* (Palimpsest), *close-card* (Curation), *slack-tide*
(Brine, the only incompatible reading), *kept ash* / *warmed
page* / *no term* (Thermoclaves), *breath-stop* (Glassblown).

**Veshen reconsidered.** Eleven years since her last bead. Two
self-knotted refusals on her tenure. Thirty-one open senior
reservations. The Concord's senior is, by the body's own
metrics, *holding* — the dropped-bead count grows because
clauses below her file, but the senior reservations themselves
remain. A walker who reaches Concord Exalted and is offered the
apotheosis is implicitly offered the chance to draft a clause
*Veshen would not.* Some walkers will. Most will not. Both are
filed.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Live contradiction: bible line 208 names the Concord's
relationship to factions only by what they hate, not what they
agree to.** The bible has no specimen of a Concord-Brine
agreement, but the pair appears in every merchant's-diary
audit. R3's Dim 3 names the Brine *slack-tide* reading as the
only **incompatible** reading of closure — and the *tide-clause*
mechanism (closure-with-named-reopen) as the only stable
Concord-Brine instrument.

The bible needs one clause near line 208:

> The Concord and the Brine agree on the act of closure and
> disagree on its temporal status. Their joint instruments
> include a *tide-clause* — closure with named-astronomical
> reopen — without which no Concord-Brine clause has ever held
> for more than one tide.

**Smaller bleed.** 2101's merchant's-diary entry shows Veshen
attesting that a *blank line* is itself a signature. R3's
Dim 6 (clause-aspect) does not have a verb-form for an attested
absence. The grammar needs a sub-aspect: **un-aspected**, by
distinction from *unaspected* (the petitioner-class). *Un*-
aspected speech is an absence-as-record, a Concord-internal cousin
of Refusal grammar's dropped count-particle. The bible needs a
single line: *the un-aspected close — a closure attested by the
absence of the closing speech — is recognized only when filed by
the standing endorsement.* Veshen has filed three.

**Tertiary bleed.** The Pale Curation's R3 (2001) introduced the
*held card*. The Concord's *senior reservation glyph* is its
sibling — both are *adjacent-not-filed* status. They are not the
same. The Curation holds an artifact; the Concord holds a clause.
A walker between Curation and Concord may have *both an artifact
held adjacent and a clause held under reservation* — the corpus
has no specimen of this state. Section 5 fills the gap.

absorbed mid-clause.*
**Author:** Veshen, the standing endorsement; sub-marginalia in
the hand of junior cataloguer 472.

> *Open-Clause Notice C-4140. Parties: the carter Yenne
> (obligee); the cobbler Tarrid (obligor). Object: settlement
> of one pair of carter's boots, owed against three previous
> mendings. Filing date: nineteen tally-days before the bead.
> Senior reservation: yes (bead present).*
>
> *Status. The obligor Tarrid was absorbed by the Choir within
> the clause's term. A Choir tendril, asked at the cobbler's
> doorway, attests that Tarrid is a mouth of the network and
> that the clause is — in their phrase — 'not extinct, now
> metabolic.' I have read the phrase. I do not accept it. I
> have not closed the clause. I have not opened a clause
> against the Choir as obligor; the Choir is not a party
> capable of standing-close in our grammar. I do not know
> whether the Choir is capable of procedural-close. I have not
> tested it. The juniors await my finding. I am owing them a
> finding. I am still owing.*
>
> *Marginalia. The carter Yenne brings her boots in unmended
> at every audience and does not ask for me by name. She asks
> when the clause will be closed. I have told her that the
> clause is open. She has told me that her feet are cold. I
> have told her, twice, that I am owing. She has stopped
> coming. I have not removed the bead.*
>
> *Sub-marginalia (junior 472). Bid 4140-X. Object: the
> phrase metabolic clause as offered by the Choir tendril at
> the cobbler's doorway. Reservation glyph on Authorship —
> the speaker is the network, no individual hand. Bid
> Choir, by tendril, hand none, since the day of attestation.
> Held adjacent. Note to the Hall: the bid-card is for the
> phrase only; the clause itself is on the open-braid as
> knot 4140 with senior reservation. Two bodies hold this
> exchange in two cabinets, neither closing. The bowls are
> getting full.*

**Lands parenthetically:** the **open-braid mechanic** (knot
4140), **senior reservation bead** (Veshen's hand), the
**clause-aspect grammar** (*am owing* / *still owing* / *not
extinct, now metabolic*), the **Concord-Choir incompatibility**

adjacency** (junior 472's bid 4140-X — the same exchange held

silence** (the bead has not been removed). One open notice;

remains; the bowls grow.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"A senior speaking 'I am owing' has not yet closed; juniors
> will not file from a senior's closing-form, only from a
> senior's closed-form."*

No archaism, no apostrophe-fantasy, no invocation of mythic
abstractions. A grammar-of-procedure statement. The Concord's
*tense-as-adjudication* sounds strange because the Concord
treats verbs as filings, not because the narrator performs any
register. TES-register version: *"And lo, the Senior in her
gravity didst speak the word of owing-yet, whereupon the lesser
clerks of the Hall of Open were forbidden to seal her clause,
that the standing-close might not be uttered before its
appointed hour."* I did not write that.

Stylised vocabulary (*open-braid*, *closed-braid*, *senior
reservation bead*, *standing-close*, *procedural-close*,
*walker's clause*, *clause-aspect*, *un-aspected close*,
*tide-clause*, *self-knotted*) names instruments and rites.
Narrator reports them; does not adopt their cadence — same
restraint as the Curation *bid-mood*, the Brine *remembered
tense*, the Refusal grammar's dropped count-particle, the
Thermoclave *mid-fire*.
