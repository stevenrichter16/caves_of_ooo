## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine Communion (the chart, the carry-forward, the keepsake-rule)

> ### The chart's working life
>
> Each deep-runner sails on a single chart for the
> length of a single bearings-pilot's working life.
> The chart begins blank — clean unmarked sheet,
> waxed at the corners, edges sealed with line-
> pitch — at the bearings-pilot's first voyage in
> position. The pilot adds running-reckoning marks
> every working-shift; revises features as drift-
> pull and chart conflict; preserves what holds.
> The chart accumulates twenty to thirty years of
> one hand's working-judgement. At the bearings-
> pilot's retirement the chart is *retired*: wrapped
> in working tackle, weighted with a stone from
> the boat's ballast, and committed overboard the
> morning of the new bearings-pilot's first voyage.
> The crew watches in working-register; song-
> register is forbidden for the disposal. The
> retired chart has, by the body's working-form,
> gone into the same water it was drawn from.
>
> ### The carry-forward
>
> The day before the retired chart goes overboard,
> the retiring and incoming bearings-pilots sit
> together in the boat's hold for one full working-
> day. They do not speak in song-register. The
> retiring bearings-pilot does not instruct. The
> incoming bearings-pilot copies, in her own hand,
> onto her own new sheet, the features of the
> retiring chart she trusts. Trust is wear: the
> incoming pilot has spent her apprentice voyages
> reading the retiring chart, and her hand has worn
> into knowing which marks are reliable, which are
> drift, which are scribed-over correction. She
> copies the reliable. She does not ask. The
> retiring pilot does not correct her. The body's
> working-form is that the carry-forward is the
> apprentice's wear-pattern made paper, not a
> transmission of the senior's authorship. A
> feature that survives three carry-forwards is,
> by working-tradition, *held*.
>
> ### The keepsake-rule
>
> The retired chart goes overboard, except when it
> goes to an outside hand. A bearings-pilot who has
> traded with a single outside party — a Curation
> bearings-trader, a Concord working-stay,
> occasionally a coastal Thermoclave depot — across
> the length of her working life may, at retirement,
> give the chart to that party as a working-keepsake
> instead of committing it overboard. The gift is
> given in working-register, without ceremony. The
> body has no working-rule for what the outside
> hand does after. The body, asked, declines the
> question:* "the chart is no longer ours."
> *Curation Depot 11 holds, at present count, four
> retired charts of the Klem-third deep-runner —
> three from the same boat across three bearings-
> pilots, twenty-eight working-years' span. Filed
> under PERMANENT/COMMUNION-CARRY/KLEM-3.*

The carry-forward sustains the authorship-doctrine
by recategorisation; the overboard-rule sustains
the no-archive doctrine by working-disposal; the
keepsake-rule is the third irregularity, the
disposal-rule's named exception. Three working-
irregularities, three loci. The Thermoclave shape
(1401 §2.1, §2.4, §2.6: three irregularities at
three loci) holds for the Communion.
