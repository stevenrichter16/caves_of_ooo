## 3. Faction asymmetry — Pale Curation (rotation, second sitting)

The Pale Curation last landed at 1301 (prov.-
etymology, double-decline, walker-recovered-fish-
hook). This sitting lands the discipline's
working-form for ambiguous walker-arrival —
pieces whose prov. cannot be read at the vault-
front. The witness-shelf is the working-form's
response to the post-cataclysm legacy-condition:
a piece whose working-line is recoverable but
not by the working-form alone.

### Etymology — *under witness*

The Curation's vocabulary holds four canon
phrases: *standing-shelf* (intake-position),
*prov.* (filing-attribute read off the piece),
*walker-recovered* (filing-class), and **under
witness** (holding-state for pieces whose prov.
is unread at the vault-front). *Under* is the
family's first **governance-preposition**: where
*on settle* and *on mate* name a working-state-
of-medium and a relation-to-piece, *under* names
**subordination to an absent attestation**. A
piece *under witness* is governed by a person
not yet bench-side; the working-form has nothing
to file until the governing attestation arrives.
The phrase joins the lexical-substrate-shift
family at an **eighth** token: *under witness*
is the family's first **governance-form**, past
the 1701 closure-at-seven.

### The witness-shelf working-form

When a walker arrives at a vault-front with a
piece whose working-line cannot be read by either
walker or keeper (no maker-mark, no working-coast
tag, no Curation docket), the keeper takes the
piece, records the walker's address as *walker*
(per the keepsake-rule, 1301), and sets the piece
on the **witness-shelf** — a locked, dust-stowed,
double-keyed compartment at the vault-front,
indexed by recovered-medium and arrival-week.
The keeper writes a witness-note naming the
working-week, the vault-day, the recovered
medium, distinguishing marks, the prov.-question,
and any walker-account of recovery. The piece is
held *under witness* until a second walker bench-
side — a separate walker-arrival, or a circuit-
keeper returning from field-rounds — attests to
the working-line by independent recognition. The
working-form does not file under *prov.*, does
not double-decline, does not return.

### The witness-note register

The keeper reads-the-attestation-against-the-piece
only when a second walker arrives bench-side with
recognition: *I have seen this filing-mark at the
working-line of so-and-so*; *the working-line on
this piece is canon-grade by the circuit-keeper
at bench eleven-east*. If the attestation canons,
the piece is filed under *prov.* with the
witness-note attached as record-of-witness-period
and the attesting walker noted as second-walker.
If no attestation arrives, the piece remains
under witness indefinitely; the working-form has
no time-clause and no closure-clause. The
witness-shelf holds a piece **against another
walker**, structurally distinct from standing-
shelf, stand-aside, settling-bench, mate-locker,
and bench-note slate. The slate's six hold-or-
record-without-filing working-forms now canonise
at six positions: held-at-threshold (Curation
prov.-filed), held-under-witness (Curation
witness-shelved), held-with-time-limit (Concord),
held-with-settling-period (Glassblown), held-
against-mate (Brine), recorded-without-holding
(Thermoclaves).

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Bleed 1 — bible §The Cataclysm L21 *"the way
text on a medieval manuscript was scraped off
and written over with the old words still
faintly visible underneath"* vs. the witness-
shelf finding.** The bible's overwrite metaphor
treats the old as *layered beneath* the new on
a single medium. The witness-shelf reads the old
differently: the old working-line of a piece is
*not on the piece*; it lives in a walker's
circuit-memory or a circuit-keeper's field-
knowledge. The working-form's relation-axis is
**distributed across the world's living
attestors**. Suggested L21 supplement (extending
the 1701 supplement):
*"(Four working-forms read the cataclysm: the
bible's layered-overwrite, where the old is
below the new on a single medium; the Glassblown
Remnant's forward-anneal, where the not-yet-
readable is ahead of the readable on a single
piece; the Brine Communion's mate-distribution,
where the companion-piece is structurally
elsewhere on the world's working-coast; and the
Pale Curation's witness-distribution, where the
old working-line is structurally elsewhere in
the circuit-memory of a walker the working-form
has not yet met.)"*

**Bleed 2 — `RotChoir.json` Tendril's *"We taste
you on the air, walker"* vs. the Curation's
witness-shelf reading.** The Choir reads the
walker's body. The Brine reads the piece (1701).
The Curation's witness-shelf reads **across
walkers** — the walker is the working-form's
voice only across the relation between bringing-
walker and attesting-walker. The Curation hears
the carrier-edge as a *two-walker chord*.
Suggested bible supplement (extending the 1701
§The Cataclysm supplement): *"(Walker-greeting
refines a sixth time: greeting-at-threshold
(Choir), filing-without-greeting (Curation prov.-
readable), filing-deferred-to-witness (Curation
witness-shelved), greeting-deferred-to-settling
(Glassblown), bench-noting-and-returning
(Thermoclaves), and fragment-mediated greeting
(Brine Communion). The Curation is the only
discipline that greets a walker-arrival twice —
first at arrival, and again at attestation —
separated by a working-period that may be days
or working-years.)"*
