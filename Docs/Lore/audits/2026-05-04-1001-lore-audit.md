## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible 23:* "Every faction in the game is defined by their
> relationship to this event."

**Strong.** Every *other* faction has a cataclysm-era named figure
(Erro, Mer-Iss, na-Maru, Halen-Iss, the Tulen ancestor).

**Thin.** **The Choir's cataclysm account.** Bible 35: the Choir is
composting the old world — *"a child's shoe woven into a wall."* The
Choir was *eating during the cataclysm.* Other factions remember it;
the Choir *ate it* — and ought to have a cataclysm-grammar no other
shares.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "The loud thing"):**

> The Choir does not number its memories. It distinguishes only
> *seasons* and *the season we ate the loud thing.* The loud thing
> is the cataclysm — in Choir grammar not an event but a *taste*:
> *iron, surprise, and the particular sweetness of long-stored grain
> released from broken silos.* Asked to date the season: *the soil
> still has the after-taste; it will be gone in another two hundred
> breaths.*
>
> The Choir registers no breaking-moment, no aftermath, no
> twenty-after-the-after — only the slow integration of an unusually
> rich season. Asked whether anyone *died*, after a long pause:
> *we are not sure that word means what you think it means. Many
> things stopped moving on their own. We helped them keep moving in
> our own way.*

The first cataclysm-account that does not register the cataclysm *as*
an account.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

**Strong.** Charter Receipt, R-7's redactions, lossy scribes, fire-tally
*the fire's hand* (2333), the standing-floor correspondence (2026-05-04
0845), bridge-clauses, B-114.

**Thin.** **A document the Choir authored.** The Choir does not write,
but every other faction's archive is now dense with documents *about*
the Choir. The corpus contains zero documents *by* the Choir. This
should be a deliberate absence — and currently is not noted as such.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "What the Choir writes"):**

> The Choir produces no documents in any shared grammar. It
> produces **chemical gradients on cave walls** — slow patterns of
> enzymatic coloration that the bible already calls *"the Choir
> thinking, visibly"* (line 35). Not symbolic; *legible only to a
> reader trained in the Choir's metabolism.*
>
> The Pale Curation has, since the eleventh archivist, maintained a
> single living trainee in this language. R-7 is the third; the
> first two did not survive their rotation. A reading is intaken
> under *witness unspecified by document convention* (2333) — same
> form as the fire-tally, on the same logic: *the author is the
> substrate.*
>
> The Choir, asked whether the gradients are intended as documents:
> *we do not separate thinking from leaving-marks. If your
> archivist is reading our wall, our wall is being thought by your
> archivist. We do not object.* The Curation has classified this as
> the only known **substrate-authored consent clause.**

Every cave wall in Choir territory is *technically a document.*

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** Names for cataclysm, dead, joining, debt, founder,
remainder, *the unsent* (2026-05-04 0845).

**Thin.** **Names other factions have given to the Choir's
interlocutor** — i.e. the fruiting body that, by other-faction
convention, *stands in for* the Choir in a transaction. The Choir
does not designate one. Other factions, needing one, *named* one.
This has not been collated.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar):**

> The Choir does not maintain a senior. Other factions, requiring a
> party-of-record, have each elected a single fruiting body — by
> their own convention, with no Choir ratification — to serve. The
> Choir has not contradicted any election. *The Choir is not aware
> they have happened.*
>
> - **Pale Curation:** *Bole-Without-Number.* A fruiting body of
>   cataclysm-era growth near the third reading-room. *Bole* is the
>   Curation's only permitted metaphor in formal cataloguing.
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *Standing-Substrate A.* The party-of-
>   record on every ledger entry involving Choir-colonised ground.
>   There is no B; the A is not a serial.
> - **Brine Communion:** *the deep root that stands above the salt
>   line.* Treated as a tide that does not move — a contradiction
>   Brine grammar accepts on the ground that *the deep root is also
>   water in another temperament.*
> - **Thermoclaves:** *the cold mouth.* Suul has spoken to it twice.
>   It did not reply in count-grammar; she did not require it to.
> - **Palimpsest:** *the lower draft of the wall.* The Palimpsest
>   reads the gradient-trained interlocutor (Dim 2) as the Choir's
>   *spoken* surface; the under-text is *every other fruiting body
>   simultaneously.*
> - **Glassblown:** no name. The craft does not transact with the
>   Choir; the substrate is *fired into vessel-floors,* not spoken
>   to. The unfired letter (2324) does not address the Choir.
>
> The same fruiting body holds **all five names simultaneously** —
> the Curation, Concord, Brine, Thermoclave, and Palimpsest
> interlocutors are the same physical bole. None of the five
> factions knows this; the Choir, knowing, considers it a small
> private joke.

The first lore object that **multiple factions share without knowing
they share it.**

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Six strata; *stratum interfaces* (0845).

**Thin.** **The Choir's mechanic for a senior carry when the
addressee is the Choir.** 0845 covered Concord/Thermoclave/Brine/
Curation handlings of *outbound* carries; the Choir's row was *has no
senior carry.* What about *inbound?*

**Concrete addition (bible expansion, "Carry into the Choir"):**

> A walker cannot deliver a senior letter to the Choir as such. By
> Choir grammar there is no addressee — the Choir is the substrate,
> the floor, the carrier, and the reader at once. Delivery is
> performed by a **handing-down**: the walker places the letter in
> contact with Choir-colonised ground for one breath of the local
> fruiting body. The body does not open the letter. It tastes the
> writing through the paper, metabolises the writer's grammar, and
> returns the letter intact with the breath attached.
>
> *The breath is the reply.* The walker reports the letter
> *handed-down*; the sender, in any non-Choir grammar, treats this
> as delivery-with-deferred-response. There is no response in the
> sender's grammar — the response was the breath, which is gone. A
> Concord-priced senior carry handed-down at Bole-Without-Number is
> **closed**, ledger-marked *substrate-handled.* A Thermoclave
> fire-letter at the cold mouth is **fifth-coming.** A Brine letter
> above the salt line is **un-tide-borne, but read.** The Choir,
> asked what it does with the breath: *we put it back into the air.
> You will breathe some of it later. We are not hiding it.*

The only senior-carry handling in the corpus where the carrier is
also the response medium.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** Seven apotheoses (now eight): Communion, Transcription,
Settled-Zero, retirement-into-cabinet, unfiring, going-deep, the long
quench, plus Erro's contested apotheosis (0845).

**Thin.** **A Choir-internal apotheosis below Communion.** Communion
is the *player's* apotheosis — but the Choir's named NPCs (Mogu, Grib,
Nam, Sien, Sopp) are already inside the Choir, and yet remain
audibly themselves. They have not Communioned. They are *interrupted
apotheoses* — and the bible does not have a category for them.

**Concrete addition (bible new subsection, "The Long Throat"):**

> A fruiting body the Choir has elected to keep *individuated* over
> long time — not because the Choir wants individuation, but because
> the body's pre-Choir voice is metabolically useful. A Long Throat
> retains its voice past the point at which absorption normally
> erases it. Mogu, Grib, Nam, Sien, Sopp are Long Throats of
> varying length, in roughly arrival order (Sien oldest by a wide
> margin; Grib most recent and shortest in the throat).
>
> The Choir's grammar for them is *we-still-tasting-this-one.* Not
> pre-Communion; *post-individual-but-pre-fully-integrated.* The
> state is stable and, by the Choir's own remark, *not the goal* —
> *no one is moving them along; they are simply the shape of the
> song in this corner of the network.* Bole-Without-Number is *not*
> a Long Throat — its individuation is by external election
> (Dim 3), not internal taste-preservation: a Long Throat is
> *we-still*; an external interlocutor is *they-have-named.*

The eighth apotheosis — and the first that is *not* an end-state.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Faction-specific pronouns, tenses, moods, the bridge-
clause (0845).

**Thin.** **The Choir's grammar when transacting cross-faction.** The
Choir's standing dialect is *we.* But when handed a Concord ledger to
attest, what does it say? *We* would not satisfy a party-of-record
signature.

**Concrete addition (bible new subsection, "The received-singular"):**

> When required by another faction's grammar to speak as a single
> party, the Choir borrows the requesting faction's pronoun — the
> Curation calls this the **received-singular**.
>
> - To Veshen at the Concord: *this one signs.* (Trade tense,
>   accepted as a party-of-record signature.)
> - To Suul at the ninth fire: *this one keeps.* (Count-grammar;
>   the *one* is a singular fire-keeper for the seating.)
> - To Nesh at the tide line: *this one rises.* (The only
>   inversion of *going-deep* in the corpus.)
> - To R-7 at intake: *this one is intaken.* (The only case where
>   the catalogued and the cataloguer are the same grammatical
>   subject.)
>
> The received-singular is a **costume** the Choir puts on for one
> transaction. The Choir regards it as politeness; other factions
> as a senior's signature. *The mismatch is the bridge-clause's
> substrate.* A bridge-clause to the Choir's interlocutor is, in
> Choir grammar, *we putting on the costume you sent us.*

The first lore-object in which **the Choir's grammar is the absence
of a grammar** — improvised per faction, never native.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine Communion micro-artifact

Rotation: Brine. The Communion's least-developed surface is *the salt-
pattern by which a Tide-Reader knows the tide is ready.*

**The salt-name.** When Nesh walks the receding line, she does not
look for words. Salt deposits in patterns — fans, lines, *pleats* —
and certain shapes are **salt-names**: the names the tide gives the
things it is ready to carry.

Seven are recognised. **Single-fan-right** (the first): a small fan
opening east of a stone, indicating a *settled* delivery is ready (an
un-tide-borne letter ripened past its season). **The long pleat** (the
seventh): three parallel folds running tide-perpendicular — the tide
will carry only *one* thing this cycle, and the reader must choose. A
reader meets perhaps two long pleats in a career.

Salt-names are not written down. A Tide-Reader is taught them by
walking the line once with her predecessor; the predecessor does not
name the salt; the reader sees the name or does not. Nesh learned
five in one walk, two alone over four years.

The eighth is rumoured. A Tide-Reader who claims an eighth is
**going-deep on the rumour** — the Communion's gentlest term for
retiring on a name no one else can verify. Two Tide-Readers in living
memory have. Nesh has not.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**(a) Bible 39 vs. ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md, the entire conceit.**

Bible 39: *"A rusted sword composted by the Choir loses its history."*
History-loss is the Choir's defining act in bible grammar.
ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md 23-28: *"the Choir absorbs persons but does not
erase the language they came in with."* The five named NPCs walk this
back; the Long Throat (Dim 5) formalises the walk-back. **A sword's
history is erased; a person's voice is not.** Bible 39 owes an
exception clause.

**Recommend a single appended sentence to bible 39:**

> *Living voices are the documented exception. A person absorbed
> into the Choir does not lose the language they came in with;
> their cadence, idiom, and philosophical register survive in the
> fruiting body the Choir grows from them. The Choir calls these
> Long Throats and does not regard the preservation as a failure
> of integration — it considers the voice a flavour it has chosen
> to keep tasting. (See ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md.)*

Second proposed bible amendment in corpus history (first: 208-209 in
0845).

**(b) RotChoir.json `OldestTruth` vs. accumulated grammar.**

> *RotChoir.json line 87:* "We remember everything we have ever
> eaten."

Now **too strong.** Per Dim 1 the Choir registers the cataclysm as a
*taste*, not an event; per Dim 5 the Long Throats' voices are kept
but the rest is metabolised. *Remembering everything* overclaims by
the Choir's own later grammar.

**Recommend `OldestTruth` softened to:**

> *"That nothing is lost — only re-tasted. The old world did not end
> — it was composted. We remember the flavour of everything we have
> ever eaten. The shape, sometimes. The names, rarely. We are not a
> library. We are a long meal."*

Keeps the Choir's character; makes the metabolism honest about what
it actually preserves.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

**Title (in-game):** *Pale Curation senior-cabinet card B-201.
Subject: training of the third gradient-reader. R-7's hand, undated.*

```
[Curation card. Subject: chemical-gradient training, Bole-Without-
 Number, third reader. Sub-annotation in a smaller hand below the
 seal.]

  B-201. The third reader has completed sixteen wall-sittings at
  the bole. The first two readers completed eight and eleven
  before rotation. The Director was not informed of this difference
  at either rotation. The Director has not asked.

  Reading sixteen produced the longest gradient yet recorded:
  twenty-three minutes, three colour-courses, one doubling-back of
  the second over the first. The third reader interpreted the
  doubling-back as a Choir-grammar correction — the bole, mid-
  thought, choosing a different word.

  The bole, asked through convention whether it had corrected
  itself, did not respond. Asked again through the received-
  singular: *this one was not correcting. This one was tasting the
  reader.* The third reader did not transcribe the second sentence;
  I have transcribed it here for the cabinet. Sealed at junior-
  grammar level on the standing principle that the Choir does not
  consent to being recorded as having tasted a party-of-record.

  Sub-annotation, smaller hand, undated:
  the third reader is the third reader. the first two are not
  *named* anywhere in the senior cabinet, by convention. the bole
  has tasted three of us. the bole, asked whether it remembers the
  first two, said *the flavour is still in the wall.* we have not
  scraped that wall in eleven years. — A.
```

**The bombshell is parenthetical:** *the bole, asked whether it
remembers the first two, said the flavour is still in the wall.*
Since the eleventh archivist the Curation has been *feeding its dead
trainees to a fruiting body it uses as a senior party-of-record* —
and that wall *has not been scraped in eleven years.* No one in the
Curation has read the sub-annotation; A.'s initial is the only sign-
off. The corpus's first lore-object that **incriminates the Curation
by the Curation's own grammar.** The Director, if shown, must seal
the cabinet (permitted exactly once per Director), rotate R-7 (not
done at this rank in three generations), or *not act* on the ground
the wall is itself a document. Three distinct endings for the
Curation arc.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

> *"The same fruiting body holds all five names simultaneously — the
> Curation, Concord, Brine, Thermoclave, and Palimpsest interlocutors
> are the same physical bole. None of the five factions knows this;
> the Choir, knowing, considers it a small private joke."*

No archaisms; no apostrophe-fantasy names; no Aedra, Daedra, Towers,
CHIM, Mantling, Eight; no *verily, hath, lo, ere.* Plain present-
tense; one mechanical claim (single referent for five names); one
character beat (the Choir privately enjoying the asymmetry). A TES
rewrite would read: *"And lo, the Wood-That-Listens beareth the Five
True Names of the Pact, yet doth no Pact-bearer know his name is
shared with four; only the Wood, knowing, smileth in the manner of
the very old."* Mine has no *and lo, beareth, doth, True Names,
Pact-bearer, smileth.* Operational register; substrate object
multiply-named by external convention. Confirmed.
