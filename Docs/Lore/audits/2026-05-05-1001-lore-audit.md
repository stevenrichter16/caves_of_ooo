## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> it overwrote reality itself, imperfectly."*

**Strong.** Nine body-readings of the cataclysm; the *capacity-grammar*

audits have not yet tested whether it does.

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar paragraph, "The cataclysm in
Thermoclave reading, the verb-shape"):

> By Thermoclave reading, the cataclysm is *the cold morning* — the
> bell on which the founders' hearths went out and could not be re-lit
> from any preserved coal. The Thermoclave account differs from
> Brine's, Curation's, and Glassblown's at one structural point: the

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The hearth-roll's un-name,

> the binding-fire. A member's name is removed only by the un-name

> hearth-warden strikes the name through with three lateral lines in
> charcoal (not ink), pronounces the name aloud one final time, and

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Three signing-modes after 0901 (witnessed-pause, harbour-
passive, cross-body); the seam-body's grammar predates its discovery by
at least four generations.

**Thin.** The cross-body signing's senior-side is *un-authored* by
their body of origin (a married-out Curation cousin). The mechanic
permits this because the body of origin's refusal is *passive* — the
chapter-warden does not turn, does not record, does not pursue. The
mechanic does not yet account for a senior-side whose body of origin
has performed an *active* refusal — a structurally permanent un-name.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The negative-form signing"):

> The negative-form signing is the fourth mode of the seam-body's
> signing mechanic. Like the witnessed-pause, it occurs in a single
> moment and a single body; like the cross-body, it survives the
> signers' working lives. Its distinguishing feature is that the
> recorded content of the signing is **the formal absence of a name.**
>
> A Thermoclave hearth-warden, performing the un-name at the
> apostasy-call, pronounces the apostate's name aloud one final time
> in the presence of two hearth-witnesses (the second-warden and the
> oldest-of-the-hearth), strikes the name through with three lateral
> charcoal lines, and does not pronounce it again. By Thermoclave
> doctrine the apostate has ceased to be of the hearth at the third
> stroke. By seam-body reading, the apostasy-call is a witnessed-
> pause: a working position (the hearth-warden, senior), a witnessed
> event (the three strokes), a record (the struck-through name), and
> two structural witnesses whose registered presence is itself the
> signing.
>
> The negative-form signing differs from the witnessed-pause in two
> mechanical respects. First, its content is null: the record marks
> a name *not held,* not a fact held. Second, it is doctrinally non-
> reversible: the un-named is not recoverable to the hearth-roll,
> and the seam-body mechanic accepts this — once a negative-form
> signing is performed, it stands.
>
> A hearth-warden has performed at least one un-name in roughly half
> of all hearth-warden working lives across the eight known hearths.
> The seam-body's mechanic now has a fourth mode that the
> Thermoclaves have been performing approximately once per
> generation per hearth, unrecognised, for as long as the hearths
> have been bound.

The seam-body gains a fourth signing-mode; the corpus's first
**doctrinally non-reversible** signing. The Thermoclave refusal is the
seam-body's most-performed signing and was the audits' last to be
named.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Thermoclaves

Rotation: Curation (0901, 2001), Concord (0801, 0501), Glassblown (0701),
Brine (0601, 1301), Thermoclaves (0401, 2101). Thermoclaves last six
sittings back; longest gap.

**Etymology.** *Thermoclaves.* Founders' compound, both halves preserved
in working register but neither read literally.

*Thermo-* is the founders' working term for *kept warmth* — distinct
from *fire* (which the founders feared) and from *coal* (which they
prized). Kept warmth is the warmth of an embered hearth, banked at
night, capable of being drawn back into flame at first bell without
striking flint. The founders' first hearth was a banked-coal hearth in
a salt-cellar that had survived the cold morning intact; the warmth
they kept was the warmth they had not extinguished and had not had to
re-light. *Thermo-* names the keeping, not the warmth itself.

*-claves* is the founders' working term for *bound members* — from the
binding-fire, the founders' adoption-rite in which a new member's hand
was held above the banked coals long enough to feel warmth without
burning. The binding-fire's witnessing parties (the same three positions
as the present-day apostasy-call: hearth-warden, second-warden, oldest-
of-the-hearth) certified that the new member's hand had accepted the
keeping. *-claves* names the bound persons, not the binding.

The full pre-doctrinal term was *the thermoclaves:* *those who are bound
to the keeping of warmth.* Modern usage retains the binding-fire (now
abbreviated to a hand-pass over a charcoal brazier in the founders'
hearth-room) and the hearth-roll, but has lost the literal warmth-
keeping; the doctrinal Thermoclave hearth is heated by ordinary fuel,
and the founders' banked-coal practice survives only at the founder's
bell, when each hearth-warden draws a single ember from the founders'
brazier and places it in her own hearth's coal-bed.

**The apostasy-call.** Every Thermoclave hearth, when an apostasy is
declared, performs the apostasy-call at the next founder's bell after
the declaration. The hearth-warden, second-warden, and oldest-of-the-
hearth gather at the hearth-room. The hearth-warden reads the apostate's
name aloud one final time, strikes it through three times with charcoal
in the hearth-roll, and does not pronounce it again.

By Thermoclave doctrine, the apostasy-call witnesses the apostate's
removal from the hearth. By Thermoclave observation, the apostasy-call
has admitted no witnesses outside the three-position founding triad
since the doctrine's binding. By seam-body reading, the apostasy-call
is a witnessed-pause whose senior is the hearth-warden, whose two
witnesses are the second-warden (who does not look up) and the oldest-
of-the-hearth (who reads the breath at every subsequent founder's bell
as the name they never learned), and whose record is the corpus's
fourteenth record-form.

The Thermoclave hearth has been performing the seam-body's negative-
form signing since the founders bound their first hearth, and the
corpus's most-performed signing is the one the audits named last.
