## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible line 21:* *"It did not merely destroy the old civilization
> — it overwrote reality itself, imperfectly."*

**Strong.** Seven body-doctrines on the cataclysm, plus four body-
pair seam-doctrines. The cataclysm-account stratifies through
every body's working grammar.

**Thin.** No account of the cataclysm in **the seam-body's voice**.
The four seams individually treat the cataclysm as the founding
case for their two-body overlap. None treats it as the founding
case for *the seam-body itself* — a body that came into existence
the moment two other bodies were both required and could not be
the same body.

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar, "The first overlap"):

> By seam-body grammar, the cataclysm is the **first overlap** —
> the founding moment when no single body could close the room and
> two bodies were required to. Every body's working doctrine names
> a unilateral closing instrument the cataclysm refused: Choir
> compost, Palimpsest under-text, Brine deep-kept, Concord
> standing-closed, Curation under-card, Glassblown un-bid, Floor
> day-fire. The seam-body's grammar holds that *each refusal is
> the seam-body's first record of itself*.
>
> The seam-body cannot be older than its first overlap, but the
> first overlap *is the cataclysm* — which makes the seam-body
> the corpus's only body whose etymology and the cataclysm's
> etymology are the same fact. Junior 472 carries a pencil-letter
> whose first line, in margins, reads *we were not before we were
> both*. The hand is unattributable. The pencil is not Concord
> standard.

History stratifies *as overlap*: the deeper back, the more the
working bodies are partial closures of refusals the seam-body has
been keeping. The corpus's only body whose name and the founding
event's name are co-extensive.

> graphite the Concord's clerks do not stock. Junior 472 carries

The pencil-letter is the corpus's first record-form whose
*unfileability is the recordkeeping*.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** Two seam-rooms named: harbour-room (0301, anatomy by
ownership), hearth-room (0401, anatomy by sight).

**Thin.** No third seam-room. R5 needs a third before the seam-
body has a working anatomy.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, "The pencil-room"):

> A pencil-room is not a room. It is the procedural moment when a
> junior of one body carries an instrument the body does not stock
> into the working space of another body. The Curation has no
> graphite of the kind 472 writes with. The Concord has no folio
> for an unsigned letter. The Brine has no salvage-grade for a dry
> document. The Floor has no draft-walk seat for a body that does
> not warm.
>
> The pencil-room is constituted *by* the carrying. It exists only
> while 472 is in transit between two bodies' working spaces. It
> dissolves the moment she sits down and resumes a host body's
> working procedure. By seam-body grammar, this is the body's
> third room and its first **transient** room — anatomy by motion.
> The harbour-room and the hearth-room are stable; the pencil-room
> is procedural.
>
> A walker who has Glimpsed 472's transit cannot describe what
> they saw afterward. The Glimpse renders no across-days flame for
> a transient room. The Palimpsest treats the pencil-room as the
> corpus's only place where the Glimpse is *correctly empty*.

Three seam-rooms — *ownership* (harbour-room), *sight* (hearth-
room), *motion* (pencil-room) — give the seam-body its first
working anatomy: a body whose three organs are all the *places
where two other bodies overlap and the overlap is the organ*.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord

Rotation through the under-developed factions: Brine (1301), Pale
Curation (2001), Thermoclaves (2101, 0401), Concord (2201). The
0301 seam touched Concord clause-aspect but did not develop the
body. This run develops **etymology** and **a small ritual**.

The corpus has clause-aspect (0301) but no Concord etymology. The
body's name is its most overt theological self-criticism: *the
agreement that is too sweet to last*.

**Etymology.** *Saccharine.* From a senior clerks' adjective,
*saccharinus* — naming the brief moment after a clause is
procedurally closed when every party present *agrees*, before the
matter unfolds again into new clauses. The senior clerks' working
doctrine: every clause-closure produces, briefly, a saccharine
moment — a body-state of full agreement that will not survive the
next day's working roster.

The Concord names itself for the *unsustainable* form of its own
product. The body's own etymology is a doctrinal warning that
closure is temporary — the working life is not in the clause being
closed but in the next clause that will re-open it. *Concord* (the
agreement) is *saccharine* (sweet, then gone) by the body's own
admission.

The Refusal does not exist in the Concord — but a parallel sub-
body does: the **closer's reservation**, an unwritten doctrine
that every senior clerk, after closing a clause, may privately
reserve a **counter-clause** in their working memory: the clause
this closure would have been if a witness who did not appear had
appeared. The reservation is unrecorded. The clerk takes it to
retirement. At a senior clerk's funeral, junior clerks observe a
*witness-pause* — a long unvoiced moment naming the dead clerk's
reserved counter-clauses by absence.

**The witness-pause ritual (working-day form).** Every Concord
working day at slack-tide ends with a witness-pause: every clerk
on shift, at the bench, holds a single unvoiced beat for the
witnesses who did not appear in any of the day's clauses. The body
procedurally counts absences as the day's *theologically loudest*
event. Working doctrine: a day with no witness-pause is a day no
clause was closed.

The Concord is the corpus's only faction whose *etymology and
apotheosis are the same word*: *saccharinus* — the briefly-sweet
agreement, the body-state of full closure, the moment before the
next opening. The body's apotheosis is to be, briefly, in
agreement with every party in a closed clause; the body's name is
the warning that this state cannot last; the body's working life
is the cycle of producing it again.
