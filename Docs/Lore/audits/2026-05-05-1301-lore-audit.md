## 3. Faction asymmetry — Glassblown Remnant

**An etymology in working register.** *The kiln-out-of-count.*

The Glassblown working register has a working term — *kiln-out-of-
count* — that has not yet been audited in the corpus. The term names
a specific working position: a kiln-firing whose count-keeper, after
the firing, declares the firing not in the day's count. The fired
pieces of a *kiln-out-of-count* firing are not held in the kiln-line's
working tally; they are not pieces by working register; they are
matter-fired-in-a-kiln-out-of-count, which is a position the working
register holds but does not enumerate.

A kiln-line declares a *kiln-out-of-count* firing roughly twice per
firing-year. The doctrinal Glassblown reason is administrative
(a kiln-bell missed, a count-keeper absent at the wrong moment, an
apprentice's tally lost). The folk Glassblown reason is grammatical:
the firing's product is in a position the kiln-count cannot hold,
because the matter or the firing or both are *un-fireable in the
count's grammar.* The folk register has no further explanation — the
kiln-count is what the kiln-count is, and the un-fireable is what
the un-fireable is.

By Glassblown elder reading, the bible itself is, in kiln-line
working grammar, *a kiln-out-of-count of the kiln-count's own
register* — a working object whose count-keeper-equivalent is the
seam-body, whose firing-equivalent is the doctrinal signing, and
whose product is the catechism (sixteenth record-form). The bible
is not pieces, in Glassblown working register; the bible is matter-
fired-in-a-kiln-out-of-count, held in the working register's only
position for that matter, which is the position of being held but
not enumerated.

The *kiln-out-of-count* etymology is the corpus's eighteenth — the
nineteenth, this audit's working count corrected for the body-
internal content audit named at Dimension 2 — working term whose
folk and doctrinal meanings differ at the register-edge. The
folk register names the catechism's working position; the
