## 3. Faction asymmetry — Glassblown Remnant (rotation)

The Glassblown Remnant has appeared across the
audit-pile only at the lexical-substrate-shift
register (1201's *prior-glass*) and as an untested
slot at the carrier-edge discussions (1401, 1501).
This sitting lands the working-form for walker-
arrival glass pieces — the discipline's deferred
admission at the settling-bench.

### Etymology — *on settle*

The Glassblown's bench-vocabulary holds three
canon phrases: **at-anneal** (a piece in the
working-form's primary annealing-oven), **on
shelf** (a piece filed in the standing-archive),
and **on settle** (a piece on the settling-bench
for the working-season's anneal-pause). *On* is
the working-vocabulary's only aspect-preposition;
*settle* names the working-state of cooling-into-
legibility. A piece *on settle* is not yet a piece
by canon — it is glass in the process of becoming
glass-as-readable. The bench-clerk's settle-card
records the piece's arrival but not its filing,
because the working-form has not yet a clause to
file.

The phrase joins the lexical-substrate-shift
family at a **sixth** token — but on a structurally
distinct register. The first five (*bee*, *sea-
wall*, *prior-glass*, *prov.*, *no clause*) name
either a vanished working-substrate or the working-
form's incompleteness. *On settle* names neither —
it names a working-state that is itself the
working-form's clause. The family extends past its
1501 closure-at-five: *on settle* is the family's
first **aspect-form**, opening a sub-register the
family had not before held.

### The settling-bench working-form

When a walker arrives at a Glassblown bench with a
glass piece, the bench-clerk's working-form is
**walker-arrival reception**: the piece is taken,
the walker's address is recorded as *walker* (the
Glassblown do not file walkers by name), and the
piece is set on the settling-bench in the bench-
shed's seasonal anneal-pause. The bench-clerk
writes a settle-card naming the working-week, the
bench-day, the working-weight, and the projected
legibility-week — the bench-day on which the
working-season's anneal will canon the piece as
glass-readable. The piece is held *on settle*
across the working-season; the working-form does
not file the piece, does not shelve the piece,
does not return the piece. The settle-card is the
working-form's only canon record across the
settling-period.

### The settle-card register

The bench-clerk reads-out-the-settle on the
season's last bench-day and either files the
piece or returns it. The settle-card is
structurally distinct from Curation's standing-
shelf, Concord's stand-aside, and the Thermoclaves'
bench-note slate: it holds a piece *pending* its
own legibility, with the filing-decision deferred
to the season's end. The slate's four hold-or-
record-without-filing working-forms now canonise
at four positions: held-at-threshold (Curation),
held-with-time-limit (Concord), held-with-
settling-period (Glassblown), recorded-without-
holding (Thermoclaves).
