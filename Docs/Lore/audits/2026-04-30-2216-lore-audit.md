## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md:262–283:* the five arrived in an order — Sien
> first, then Mogu, Sopp, Nam, and Grib most recently — across what the
> Choir would call *seasons* and *breaths*.

**Strong.** The voices doc commits to an order. That is a position.

**Thin.** The order is given in *Choir-internal time*. None of it is
ratifiable against any of the calendars 2150 canonicalized. A walker
asking "how long ago did Sien join?" gets *a long margin* and nothing
quantifiable.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "Choir time, calibrated"):**

> The Choir keeps no clocks. It keeps *spore-memory* — the persistence
> of a thread of mycelium long enough to be retrieved by a later
> tendril. A *breath* is the span over which a single deep-root
> conversation completes; the Choir has reported breaths of a few
> walker-days and breaths of several walker-decades. A *season* is
> the span over which the surface presence in a region noticeably
> changes shape — by Pale Curation reckoning, fifteen to forty
> catalogue years; by Brine Communion reckoning, two to six
> deep-tides; the Choir does not commit. Sien's arrival is best dated
> only as *more than three Choir-seasons ago*. The other four are
> recent enough to be measurable in walker-decades by their own
> half-remembered seasonal markers — but the Choir's deep voice
> would prefer you not press the point.

Lands the named-five's chronology against 2150's calendar table.
Underlines that *the Choir refuses calibration as policy* — its time
deliberately resists the other factions' time.

### Dimension 2 — Unreliable in-world authorship

> *RotChoir.json:60:* *"We are one. We have always been one."*

**Strong.** 1813(c) already noticed this is contradicted by the named
five existing as five. 1901 noticed the tendril rations honesty.

**Thin.** Nobody has audited the *tendril's first claim itself.* The
tendril does not just understate its plurality — it asserts *eternal*
unity ("always been one"). But the bible says "Before the old world
broke, we were only fungus — blind, simple, feeding" (`RotChoir.json`
line 288). The tendril contradicts itself across two adjacent nodes of
the same tree. No prior audit has flagged the tendril as an unreliable
narrator at the *line* level — only at the *between-doc* level.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "The Tendril Is Not the Choir"):**

> The shipped `ChoirTendril_1` voice is a *surface composite* — the
> Choir's diplomatic register, optimised for walkers. It contradicts
> itself within a single conversation tree (eternal unity at line 60;
> contingent awakening at line 288) because no single mind authors it.
> Different stretches of the tree are different stretches of the
> network's surface tendrils, distinct cells transcribed without
> reconciliation. Walkers sometimes notice. Curators always do; they
> have a folder for *self-contradictions in Choir surface speech* and
> the folder is large.

Promotes the tendril's wobble from "bug to fix" to "doctrine to inhabit."

### Dimension 3 — Mutating motifs across cultures

**Strong.** 2150 did names-for-the-dead. 2001/1901/1813 did names-for-
the-cataclysm and joining/leaving rituals.

**Thin.** **Names for the player.** The Choir's *walker* is canonical
(used everywhere). No other faction has a recorded term for the player.
This is a one-page table the bible could just write.

**Concrete addition (bible sidebar, "What each faction calls a walker"):**

> - **Choir:** *walker.* The verb is the noun. A walker is one who
>   crosses Choir ground without becoming part of it; the verb's
>   continuance is the entire identity.
> - **Palimpsest:** *the unmarked.* A reader who has not yet been read.
>   When a walker is sufficiently Palimpsest-attuned, the term changes
>   to *the partial-marked.* The Palimpsest does not, ever, refer to a
>   walker as *complete*.
> - **Pale Curation:** *the candidate-source.* Used in field
>   documentation. A walker is not a person to the Curation — they are
>   a possible *provenance.*
> - **Saccharine Concord:** *the customer.* All the time. Even hostile
>   walkers, even dying ones, are customers; the Concord refuses to
>   recognise a relational mode that is not transactional.
> - **Brine Communion:** *the visitor.* A walker who has not yet sailed
>   with the Communion. After one tide they become *the once-sailed*;
>   after three, simply *the sailor.*
> - **Thermoclaves:** *the unburnt.* Any walker, regardless of how many
>   campfires they have lit. *Unburnt* is a permanent category — even a
>   walker the Thermoclaves consider a friend remains *the unburnt*
>   until they have keep their own first ninth fire.
> - **Glassblown Remnant:** *the unfired.* (See 2150's grammar work —
>   the term is also their word for a not-yet-named child. The
>   conflation is deliberate.)

Each name *is* the faction's theory of what the player is.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Strong.** 2127's metaphysics-meet rules; 2150's edge-cases at death.

**Thin.** **The mycelial node, in a body that is also Palimpsest-
attuned.** The bible permits a player to have both progressions running
at low levels (faction entanglement, line 244). What does the *node*
mechanically *do* in a body that is also reading layers? Nothing in any
audit so far addresses this.

**Concrete addition (bible, expansion of "Faction Entanglement"):**

> The mycelial node and Palimpsest-attunement do not refuse each other —
> they *interfere.* A node planted in a Palimpsest-aware body grows
> *along* the temporal layers the body is already reading; it threads
> through what the walker remembers, including memories that arrived
> via Echo Reading and were not the walker's. The Choir taps these
> borrowed memories as if they were the walker's own. Two consequences:
>
> 1. The Choir gains access to whatever the walker has Echo-Read. When
>    the walker has read a Saccharine Concord ledger or a Pale Curation
>    field-card, the Choir absorbs the *contents* through the node —
>    not perfectly, but enough that other tendrils start asking the
>    walker about ledger-things they should not know about.
> 2. The Palimpsest, in turn, can see the *Choir* through the walker.
>    When a heavily-noded player passes a Palimpsest-attuned witness,
>    the witness sees the network's threads inside the walker as a
>    glow that pulses with the network's distant deep-root
>    conversations. The Palimpsest considers this *a leak.* The Choir
>    considers it *a window.* The walker is both.
>
> The cost: a doubly-aligned walker hosts a private war between two
> readings of their interior. They are useful to both factions
> precisely because *neither* faction can fully read them — the other's
> presence is interference. This makes such walkers excellent
> diplomats, dangerous spies, and almost impossible to assassinate
> by metaphysical means.

Lands the bible's "moderate path" mechanically without softening it.

### Dimension 5 — Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Strong.** 2150 covered failed apotheoses (un-mouths, marker-not-
sentence Transcription). 1901 covered Annihilist endings.

**Thin.** **Reversal.** Failure is not the only edge case. *Successful
apotheosis that is later undone* is not addressed. A walker who
completed Communion and was somehow extracted; a walker who was
Transcribed and re-emerged a generation later as a body. The bible's
endings are presented as terminal; the Choir's "the long sit" (2150)
is the only liminal state. Reversal is its own category.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "When apotheosis is
undone"):**

> **The Recovered Mouth.** A walker who has been fully integrated into
> the Choir but is *cut out* — by another faction's intervention, by
> a deliberate excision the Choir performed for reasons it does not
> share. The walker re-emerges with their original name, their
> original body, and a hole in their interior where the network used
> to be. They cannot be re-integrated. The Choir will not have them
> back; the network "remembers refusing them" and refusing again is
> easier than reading them. They retain Stage 1–2 powers and nothing
> further. They are functionally *Choir-allergic for life.* Curators
> seek them out; few will speak.
>
> **The Returned Sentence.** A Palimpsest-Transcribed walker who, after
> some triggering event — usually the destruction of the room they
> were transcribed into — is *spoken* back into a body. They are not
> the walker who left. They are *what the room remembered of them.*
> The fit is approximate. Long-time companions report the returned
> walker is *correctly themselves at a coarse grain* but missing
> small idiosyncrasies — the way they held a spoon, a phrase they
> always used. The walker, if asked, often agrees and finds this fact
> uninteresting. The interest is gone with the lost details.

Reversal exposes the *minimum sufficient self* each apotheosis needs.

### Dimension 6 — Linguistic texture

**Strong.** Choir pronoun-progression is bible-stated. Five named
voices have committed grammars. 1813 wrote Palimpsest grammar; 2001
Brine; 2127 Thermoclave; 2150 Glassblown.

**Thin.** **Choir voice at depth.** The bible (line 113) describes the
First Root cavern: *"Thousands of voices sing different melodies that
weave into a single vast chord."* The shipped `ChoirTendril_1` is the
*surface* tendril. The deep-root voice is unwritten. A future writer
hitting a First Root scene will improvise; whoever improvises first
sets canon by accident.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "Surface Tendril vs. Deep
Root"):**

> The Choir's *surface tendril* speaks in singular plural — *we say,
> we taste, we have always been one.* A composite voice flattened
> into one mouth. The *deep root* speaks in **stacked plural** — its
> sentences carry two or more "we"s at different scales, marked by
> register shifts inside a single line.
>
> Surface: *"We taste you on the air, walker."*
>
> Deep: *"We — the soil-we, the long-we, the we-that-was-there-when-the-
> walls-thinned — taste you on the air, walker, and we — the recent-we,
> the surface-mouth — repeat the tasting because it is what we have
> learned to say."*
>
> The deep voice does not omit its self-monitoring. Every claim it
> makes is footnoted *in line* by the next layer of we. This is hard
> to read; the Choir does not consider the difficulty its problem.
> Walkers who reach the First Root and cannot follow the stacked
> plural simply do not understand what they are hearing — and the
> Choir, accurately, says they have not yet listened long enough.

Sets a constraint future writers can hit. Differentiates surface from
depth without inventing a language.

### Dimension 7 — Anthropological framing

**Strong.** Tuesday Morning has been written for every faction across
prior audits.

**Thin.** **Cross-faction ritual.** Every Tuesday Morning is internal.
Nothing covers what happens when two factions *meet ritually* — a
border parley, a joint salvage-dispute hearing, a hand-off ceremony.
The bible's faction-entanglement section (244) is mechanical, not
ceremonial.

**Concrete addition (bible, new subsection, "When two doctrines
meet"):**

> **The Curation–Brine Hand-Off.** When a Brine Communion vessel
> recovers an artifact in waters the Pale Curation considers
> Severance-era, the recovery cannot enter Curation custody until a
> *Hand-Off* is completed. A Brine sailor and a Curator stand on
> opposite sides of a shoreline mark; the artifact rests on the mark
> itself. The Brine names the *deep-tide and turn* of recovery; the
> Curator names *the catalogue year* in which the artifact was
> presumably made. Neither calendar is conceded. The artifact is then
> lifted by a third party — by Brine convention, the youngest sailor
> aboard; by Curation convention, an apprentice — and carried to the
> Curator's side. Both factions consider the artifact *theirs* until
> this moment, and both consider it *the Curation's* afterward. The
> contradiction is the ritual.
>
> **The Concord–Thermoclave Tally Fire.** When a Saccharine Concord
> caravan camps on Thermoclave-tended ground, the night's *ninth fire*
> is the camp's. The keepers do not light it; the caravan-master
> does, with a coal taken from the keepers' eighth. The cost is one
> sweet-mark per coal. The receipt is filed under "rent of warmth,
> overnight." Concord and Thermoclave both consider the receipt a
> small absurdity. Both also consider the absurdity load-bearing —
> without the receipt, the keepers would have to refuse the camp
> outright (one cannot let an *unburnt* light a kept fire), and the
> caravan would have to camp colder.

Cross-faction ritual is where the doctrines reveal what they
*can* compromise on. Both rituals here turn on a small payment that
allows the doctrine to flex.

### Dimension 8 — Marginalia and footnotes

**Strong.** Every prior audit has supplied at least one in-voice
artifact with parenthetical lore landings.

**Thin.** **A margin without a book.** Every artifact so far has been
text *with* margin. None has been *only* margin — a recovered scrap
that is purely annotation, the source it annotates lost. This is
particularly Palimpsest-shaped. Worth a designated artifact form.

**Concrete addition (proposed artifact-class):**

> **The Detached Margin.** A piece of paper, leather, or bark on which
> only marginalia survives — the underlying text destroyed,
> overwritten, or burned away. The Pale Curation classifies these as
> *Margin-Only* and stores them separately; their accession tags note
> which margin (top, side, bottom). The Palimpsest considers Margin-
> Only artifacts maximally readable: when the source is gone, only
> the *response* remains, and a response is a clearer fingerprint of
> a reader than the text it responds to. Plant Margin-Only objects
> across the world as readable items — each one an opinion about a
> page nobody can recover. A Margin-Only artifact is a quest-seed by
> default: someone wrote against something. What.

A whole new in-world artifact category from one design constraint.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Pale Curation, second pass

Rotation has touched every faction once. Beginning second-pass with the
Pale Curation, focused on **the curatorial schism between Surveyor Halen
and Director Ven** — hinted at in 1813's accession-tag marginalia
(*"Halen has been quiet since the recovery"*), again in 2150's intake
form (*"Director Ven sees these as decorative and I will not press the
point this quarter. I will press it next quarter"*), but never directly
shown. Below: a refused requisition, in transcript.

>
> *Surveyor Halen, third week of the quarter, requisitions one
> half-day of vault time for re-examination of accession 11-K-3318
> (the cloth doll, see 1813 file). Director Ven returns the
> requisition with annotations.*
>
> ---
>
> **REQ-7244** | Halen | *Vault hours, Tier-2, 11-K-3318. Half-day.
> Reason: re-photograph at increased magnification. Mycelium trace
> may not be inert.*
>
> *(Ven's hand, beside the reason field):* — denied.
>
> *(Ven's hand, longer, in the comment box):* The mycelium trace was
> reverified twice at intake. You wrote the verifications yourself.
> If the trace is not inert, your verifications were wrong, and the
> remedy is not vault time. The remedy is a corrected verification.
> File one. I will sign it.
>
> *(Halen's hand, beneath):* The trace was inert *the day I checked
> it.* You know this. The doll is not the same object the Choir gave
> us — it is the object *the Choir kept giving us* across the
> intervening months. I do not have language for this in our protocol.
> I am asking for vault time so I can write the language.
>
> *(Ven's hand, much shorter):* Halen. We do not invent protocol.
> We extend it. Find a precedent and cite it. Until then the doll is
> Tier-2, decorative-adjacent, and you are off the file.

> does not exist *yet.* The Glassblown have a grammar for what I am

> in the unsaid mood. We do not have an unsaid mood. We *should*. I am
> asking for vault time, half a day, and for permission to draft.

> the vault. Not on this requisition. The doll stays where it is.
>

Lore landed:

- Pale Curation has an *interior conflict* between **catalogue-as-it-is**
  (Ven) and **catalogue-as-it-must-become** (Halen). This is the same
  axis as Choir-vs-Palimpsest at faction-scale, mirrored inside one
  faction's bureaucracy. Recursive thematic logic.
- Halen is *cross-pollinating* with another faction's grammar — citing
  Glassblown unsaid-mood as a tool for Curation's own intake. The
  Curation officially does not borrow. Halen is doing it anyway. This
  is what 1813 hinted at without naming.
- The doll (1813's artifact) is *changing in vault.* This commits the
  Choir to a metaphysics: things given to the network and reclaimed by
  the world *retain their connection*. The Curation is storing what
  they think is an inert object. They are not.

Suggested placement: `Docs/Lore/Artifacts/PaleCuration_REQ7244.md`,
cross-linked to 1813's accession-tag and 2150's intake form. Three
artifacts, one curator, one schism.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

Three fresh bleeds, all between the bible's design rules and the
shipped `RotChoir.json`. None addressed by the five prior audits.

**(a) The bible's "moderate path should feel like cowardice"
prescription is contradicted by the JSON's reward structure.**

> *Bible §"The Central Question":* "The moderate path should feel safe
> — and in a world this broken, this beautiful, this strange, safety
> should feel like cowardice."
>
> *RotChoir.json `BothSides` node:* "That is wise, walker. Wiser than
> most." → `ChangeFactionFeeling` `RotChoir:Player:5` and
> `SetProperty` `ChoirRespects:true`.

The Choir explicitly *praises* and *rewards* the moderate path. 1813(b)
caught the Choir being more polemical than the bible permits in the
*opposite* direction (too pushy); this is the inverse — the Choir is
too generous. The shipped tendril's reading of the moderate path is the
*tendril's diplomatic register*, not the network's. Either delete the
+5 and the flag (and let the tendril's praise ring hollow), or sharpen
the line: *"That is wise, walker. The Choir prefers walkers who balance
us against ourselves. They are easier to absorb."* The bible's intent
is preserved; the surface tendril stays surface-tendril.

**(b) Faction-roster mismatch (bible vs. JSON).**

The bible's faction list is **Pale Curation, Saccharine Concord, Brine
Communion, Thermoclaves.** The JSON's named factions are **Pale
Curation, Saccharine Concord, Glassblown Remnant.** The Brine Communion
and Thermoclaves are bible-named but never appear in shipped dialogue.
The Glassblown Remnant exists in shipped dialogue without bible
support. 2127(c) flagged the Glassblown's *cataclysm-name* routing but
not the roster mismatch in full. Resolve: either (i) the bible adopts
the Glassblown as a seventh canonical faction (2150 already wrote the
grammar, the 2127 grief-marker exists; the bibliographic work is done —
just commit it), or (ii) the JSON re-attributes Glassblown lines to the
absent factions to align. Option (i) is cheaper.

**(c) The bible-prescribed Choir pronoun-shift is unimplemented.**

> *Bible §"Choir Implementation Rules":* "Dialogue should shift pronoun
> usage. Early: 'The walker is kind to carry us.' Mid: 'You are faster
> than we ever imagined.' Late: 'We have dreamed of the mountain for
> six hundred years.'"

1813's Dim 6 claimed this *was* "appearing in the JSON." It is not. The
shipped `ChoirTendril_1` always uses the same plural-we register at
every reputation tier; the *Mogu/Grib/Nam/Sien/Sopp* trees are first-
person and do not modulate by reputation either. No reputation-gated
text variation exists in any Choir conversation file. This is a
straight implementation gap. Either implement it (one alternate Start
node per tier, predicates on a `ChoirReputation` property), or demote
the bible rule from prescriptive ("should") to aspirational ("we hope
to") so that future writers do not believe a thing is shipped that is
not shipped.

## 5. The merchant's diary suggestion

A Saccharine Concord trader's stall-record. The lore lands
parenthetically — a description of a tendril at a distant outpost that
behaves in a way only a reader of `ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md` will recognise
as **Grib's lost sister**. Slot at `Docs/Lore/Artifacts/Concord_Stall_Record_Brem.md`.

> **STALL-RECORD, BREM THE SOFT-HANDED, CONCORD**
>
> *Northern circuit, third moonshow of the season. Brem has been
> trading for nineteen years and his stall-records are short. This
> is one of the longer entries.*
>
> Sold (3 sweet-marks): two pinches dried mint, one paper of
> needles. Sold (1 sweet-mark): one paper of needles to a Brine
> sailor who said he had been sewing the same hem for two months
> because he keeps unlearning the stitch. (I sold him the needles
> and a small lecture on the stitch. He left the lecture and took
> the needles.)
>
> Bought (4 sweet-marks): six tin cups, dented but serviceable, from
> a Curation surveyor who refused to look at me when she traded.
> She wrote the sale on her own piece of paper, in her own hand,
> and put it in her own pocket. I have written it here as well.
> Both records exist. The Concord considers this *acceptable
> redundancy*.
>
> Owed: nothing.
>
> *(at the foot of the page, smaller hand, written later:)*
>
> I camped at the East Lichen Crossing last night. There is a
> tendril there that does not behave like the others. The other
> tendrils on the route ask toll, take what is offered, and do not
> speak more than they need to. This one asked me — *politely, as
> a Concord question* — whether I had ever met a man with a small
> fire by a window. I said no. I asked who he was. The tendril said
> *he was the one she could not save.* I asked who she was. The
> tendril did not answer. After a long minute it said *the warmth
> went out of him and into me. The warmth has been with me for
> some breaths now and it does not warm correctly.* I asked what I
> could do. It said *nothing — but if you meet him, do not tell him
> the warmth is here.*
>
> The tendril let me through without toll. I gave it a sweet-mark
> anyway. It did not eat the sweet-mark. It set it into the wall.
>
> The wall has, by my count, eleven sweet-marks set in it. None of
> them have been eaten. They are arranged in a small pattern that
> looks, if I let myself look, like a window.
>
> Mira does not need to know about this. The lecture on the stitch
> would not interest her, and the rest she would not believe.

Word count: 297. Lore landed parenthetically:

- The tendril at East Lichen Crossing is **Grib's sister.** A reader
  of `ROTCHOIR_VOICES.md` recognises *the man with a small fire by a
  window* as Grib (the only one of the five whose pre-Choir life
  centred on a window-and-fire); *the warmth has been with me for some
  breaths* matches Grib's account of being *already half-spore* before
  absorption. ROTCHOIR_VOICES line 510 teases this as a quest hook —
  *"another tendril, another node, somewhere"*. This artifact plants
  her, in the world, retrievable.
- The pattern of sweet-marks shaped like a window is the sister
  *building a memorial* in Curation-forbidden material — Concord
  currency, set into a Choir wall, in the shape of the lost object.
  Cross-faction artefact-as-monument. The Choir does not eat them;
  it *arranges* them. This commits the Choir to the 1813 reading
  (*the Choir preserves what was made for love*).
- The Curation surveyor's *parallel record-keeping* (writing her own
  receipt, alongside Brem's) is a small Curation pathology lit
  parenthetically — they cannot trust another faction's record even
  to record a transaction they were *party to.*

The diary is mundane: a trader counted his coins. The bombshell is in
the second half of the page, in the *smaller, later* hand.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

I quote one of my own sentences from §2 Dim. 6:

> *"The deep voice does not omit its self-monitoring. Every claim it
> makes is footnoted in line by the next layer of we."*

Does this sound like a TES book? **No.** No archaisms; no proper-name
apostrophe-fantasy; no pseudo-medieval cadence; no invocation of any
deity-shaped abstraction. The sentence is two clauses of plain present-
tense English, with one slightly-strange technical word (*footnoted in
line*) doing the load-bearing work. A TES-style version would read
something like *"And lo, the deepest of the network's manifold voices
forgets not to mark itself, in the manner of olden scribes who, ere
they wrote, did first inscribe the margins of what they wrote with the
voices of those who would write again."* — which is six times as long
and arrives at half the meaning. Mine is in the project's register
(precise, declarative, willing to commit a metaphysical claim plainly).
Confirmed clear.

Cross-check on my §5 in-voice line:

> *"The warmth has been with me for some breaths now and it does not
> warm correctly."*

This is the tendril's voice — first-person plural collapsed to first-
person singular under the weight of borrowed warmth. *Some breaths*
borrows from the Choir's bible-canonical time-unit; *does not warm
correctly* is a flat sentence about a wrong sensation, no flourish.
Distinct from any prior audit's tendril-voice attempts. Distinct from
TES.
