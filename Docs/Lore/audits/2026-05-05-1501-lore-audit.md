## 2. Depth audit

### Dimension 1 — Mythic-historical stratification

> *Bible line 21:* *"It did not merely destroy the old civilization —
> it overwrote reality itself, imperfectly, the way text on a medieval
> manuscript was scraped off and written over with the old words still
> faintly visible underneath."*

**Strong.** The metaphor doubles as cosmology *and* as reading-
instruction. Every other lore claim about the cataclysm hangs from
this one image.

**Thin.** The bible has *no in-world disagreement about when the
cataclysm happened.* "Six hundred years" appears once (line 47, the
Choir's patience) and is presented as fact. A real cataclysm of this
scale would generate incompatible chronologies — every faction with
motivated reasons to count differently, and each chronology revealing
what its keepers care about.

**Concrete addition** (bible new sidebar, after line 26, before "The
Rot Choir"):

> **How long ago the cataclysm was, in five accounts**
>
> The Choir says six hundred years, and means it as a count of
> breaths — a long inhale and an exhale longer still. Mycelium does
> not measure in years; the Choir borrowed the word from a walker it
> absorbed and has used it ever since the way a borrowed coat is
> used: warmly, and not always when the weather requires it.
>
> The Pale Curation says four hundred and thirty-one. They have a
> record. The record begins at year zero, and year zero is the year
> the first Hall was opened, and the first Hall was opened *after*
> the cataclysm — so the four-hundred-and-thirty-one is the count of
> years the Halls have been holding, which is not the count of years
> since the world ended. The Curation knows this. They keep counting
> from the Hall anyway, because the alternative is to pick a year zero
> they cannot defend.
>
> The Saccharine Concord says it was *recently enough that profits
> have not yet stabilised, and long ago enough that the old prices no
> longer apply.* They do not count.
>
> The Brine Communion says nine tides. Each tide is the span between
> two great storms severe enough to redraw the shore. Nine tides is
> roughly seven hundred years, but the Communion does not convert.
> The conversion would imply that years are the unit and tides are a
> derivation; for the Communion, the tide is the unit and years are a
> clerk's habit.
>
> The Palimpsest says it is happening. The Palimpsest does not place
> the cataclysm in the past. To it the overwrite is a present tense —
> *the page is being scraped, the new ink is going on, the older ink
> is still wet under it.* When pressed for a date the Palimpsest will
> offer one, and a different one each time, and each one will be true
> at the moment of offering.
>
> No two of the five accounts can be reconciled. The disagreement is
> the scale.

Each chronology reveals what its keeper cares about. Choir count is
biological-borrowed; Curation count is procedural; Concord count is
commercial-evasive; Brine count is geological; Palimpsest count is
grammatical. Every faction gets a tense.

### Dimension 4 — Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

> *Bible lines 161–165* (The Revision): *"Temporarily restore a
> location to a previous state… When the duration ends, it crumbles
> back to ruin. Every use is a small grief."*

**Strong.** Revision and Overwrite have rules — duration-bounded
restoration, permanent overwrite displacing whoever lives in the new
state.

**Thin.** Two edge cases unaddressed:

1. **A place with no past.** What does Palimpsest Sight render in a
   location that was empty before the cataclysm — open ocean, untouched
   wilderness, virgin desert? The bible assumes every place has a
   pre-cataclysm layer to read.
2. **Choir-occupied ruins.** What happens when the Palimpsest tries
   to Revise a place the Choir has actively integrated? The two
   factions are described as philosophically opposed but the
   *mechanical* collision is unwritten.

**Concrete addition** (bible new subsection, after line 174, before
"Quest Design"):

> **The two edge cases of Palimpsest writing**
>
> *Where there is no page.* In open ocean, in old-growth wilderness
> untouched before the cataclysm, in a stretch of desert older than
> civilisation, the Palimpsest's Sight returns nothing — not blank,
> not dark, but *absent in a way the eye registers as a kind of
> vertigo.* The Palimpsest cannot read where there is nothing
> written. The Brine Communion knows this; their sailors cross long
> stretches of open water and report the *unreadable distance* —
> water the Palimpsest's gaze passes through without finding ground.
> The Palimpsest does not enjoy these passages. Where it can be said
> to enjoy anything, it prefers a page.
>
> *Where the page has been eaten.* When the Palimpsest tries to
> Revise or Overwrite a location the Choir has actively integrated,
> the writing does not take. The mycelium has already metabolised the
> structural information the Palimpsest would have used as ink. The
> Choir says, of these places, *we have already read it; we do not
> need it written again.* The Palimpsest experiences the same place
> as *a section of the page where the older ink is gone for good.*
> Both descriptions are correct. The Choir composts the past; the
> Palimpsest cannot rewrite what has been composted. This is the real
> conflict between the two — not philosophical, mechanical. Every
> patch of Choir-integrated ground is a patch of unreadable page.

This rewrites the central faction conflict from "philosophical
disagreement" to "mechanical incompatibility." The Choir and the
Palimpsest are erasing each other's working surfaces. That's a
stronger conflict than the bible currently carries — and it
foreshadows the Communion's "unreadable distance" as a third,
geographic, not-readable-by-anyone case.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Brine Communion

Brine last self-contained at 0301 (twelve hours back) and seam-mode at
1101. Longest gap among the four underdeveloped bodies; rotates in.

**A small lore-artifact: the Communion's working unit, the *plait.***

A *plait* is the Communion's working term for a unit of social and
physical bond. Three working uses:

- **Plait-as-knot.** A literal three-strand braid worked into a
  sailor's gear — a length of personal hair, a length of best cord, a
  length of a chosen kin's hair (mother, sibling, lover, drinking-
  partner — the Communion does not rank). The plait is worked into
  the rigging of the sailor's primary vessel. As long as it holds in
  the rigging, the sailor is *plaited in*; if it is cut by storm or
  by hand, the sailor is *unplaited* — held by the Communion as a
  sailor in working position but no longer in working bond. Most
  sailors are plaited and stay plaited their whole working lives.

- **Plait-as-procedure.** The mourning ritual at Dimension 7 — the
  dead sailor's hair plaited with their best cord, worked into the
  next outgoing vessel, carried to delivery over three sailings. The
  plait is the Communion's working answer to *the wrong sentence.*

- **Plait-as-trust.** The verb form. *To plait* a piece of new
  information is to add it to the working register the strand-wardens
  carry — usually as a single new knot in the long strand that hangs
  from each warden's belt. Each warden's strand is a working ledger
  of every plaiting they have witnessed. The strand grows by knot;
  old knots loosen and are retied; some knots, by warden tradition,
  are tied in a way that cannot be re-tied if loosened — *the once-
  plaiting,* used for testimony the warden swears not to retract. A
  warden who breaks a once-plaiting is *unplaited from the strand* —
  banished, working register revoked, not killed. The Communion
  considers the working revocation worse than killing.

The three uses share one structural property: *a plait is held by
working agents (the rigging, the warden's belt, the next sailing)
outside the plaited individual.* The Communion's working philosophy
is that *no person can hold their own bond.* Bond requires a third
witness — the rigging holds the marriage, the warden's strand holds
the testimony, the next sailing holds the dead. A sailor who attempts
to plait themselves in their own working register is held to be doing
something not unholy but *useless* — like writing a contract you keep
in your own pocket.

The plait is the Communion's analogue to the Curation's accession-
card and the Choir's network-membership. The Halls hold by recording;
the Choir holds by integrating; the Communion holds by plaiting
outward into a third agent. Three working theories of bond.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence:**

> *"Mycelium does not measure in years; the Choir borrowed the word
> from a walker it absorbed and has used it ever since the way a
> borrowed coat is used: warmly, and not always when the weather
> requires it."*

A working sentence in the bible's register — image-driven, plain-
spoken, philosophical. The image (a borrowed coat) is from the
working life of an ordinary walker; the philosophical content (the
Choir's borrowed measurement-grammar) is delivered through it. Verbs
*measure*, *borrowed*, *used*, *requires.* No *verily,* no archaism,
no inverted syntax, no apostrophe-fantasy nominal forms.

TES rewrite (forbidden): *"Verily, the Mycelium counts not in years,
