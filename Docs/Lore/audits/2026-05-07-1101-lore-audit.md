## 2. Depth audit

### 2.1 Mythic-historical stratification

**Quote (2301 §2.1, proposed bible append):** *"On
this day, contracts are again possible."*

**Strong.** A first-generation date-clause that frames
the Dissolution as the moment *promises* (not cities)
ended.

**Thin.** *Contracts are again possible* presupposes a
prior shape that was. The bible has not canonised the
older shape. The Dissolution names what was lost only
by negation.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Cataclysm, append):**

> *The Concord teaches that pre-Dissolution promises
> were of a single shape — the* standing-promise *—* a
> *party promising itself, written in its own hand,
> sealed in working-register against a future the party
> expected to inhabit. The standing-promise had one
> signatory and one witness, both the same person at
> different points along their own life. The Dissolution
> dissolved the singleness of party with future-self:
> the surface stopped reliably producing the future-
> self the standing-promise was written for. The post-
> Dissolution clause is therefore* triadic — *party,
> party, and the body as third witness. The body is a
> substitute for a future the surface no longer
> guarantees. A junior is taught the standing-promise
> form once, in apprenticeship, and immediately
> afterward never to attempt one. Some juniors keep
> the apprenticeship exemplar in the back of a
> personal day-list as a reminder of what is no longer
> working.*

Two strata: standing-promise (older, broken, taught
once and disused) and triadic clause (post-Dissolution,
working). The *future-self* framing makes the
Dissolution mechanically specific without naming any
external cause.

### 2.4 Cosmology with internal mechanical logic

**Quote (2301 §2.4, proposed bible append):** *"A
closure holds because the close is witnessed in the
same instant by both parties and a third attesting
body."*

**Strong.** The triadic rule is canon. Unmoored
clauses are the operational consequence.

**Thin.** *Both parties* presupposes both parties
*qualify*. The body has not canonised the working
test for party-eligibility.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Saccharine Concord
subsection, append after the unmoored mechanic):**

> *The body's working test for party-eligibility is*
> the standing-test *— three formal questions, posed
> in pressing-clean register by the senior on duty,
> before any clause is filed with a body the tally-
> house has not previously closed with:*
>
> 1. *Are you alone in this room with me, by your own
>    reading?*
> 2. *Will you state your working-name in working-
>    register, and will the working-name be one you
>    will recognise as yours when the close is brought
>    back to you on bearer-return?*
> 3. *Will you carry the receipt of this close from
>    this room, by your own hands, and bring it back
>    on the day of bearer-return without a third
>    party reciting it for you?*
>
> *The questions test the same property in different
> aspects: the Concord's working metaphysic of* party-
> hood. *A body that fails any question is filed with
> the* refusal-mark *and the failed question's number.
> The refusal is itself a clause (numbered* N-refused*),
> eligible for re-application across generations.*

Mechanical consequence: late-stage Choir-aligned
walkers (in two-way mycelial connection) increasingly
fail Q1 in subtle ways the senior cannot articulate
but registers in the seam; late-stage Palimpsest-
aligned walkers increasingly fail Q2 (the working-
name they would recognise on bearer-return is no
longer reliably the same name they gave at the close
— the layering bleeds the name).

This is the body's quiet structural pressure on the
central thematic axis: **the world's working
institutions, by their own internal rules, stop being
able to do business with apotheote-bound walkers
before the walker reaches apotheosis**. The walker
notices closures are being declined. The walker does
not necessarily understand why.

### 2.5 Apotheosis-shaped themes

**Quote (2301 §2.5):** *"A Choir-Communion or
Palimpsest-Transcription walker is, in Concord
doctrine, a body that no longer holds the standing
to issue or receive a promise."*

**Strong.** *No longer holds* names the endpoint.

**Thin.** It does not name the *gradient* — clauses
signed *during* the slow erosion of standing.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Saccharine Concord
subsection, append after the standing-test):**

> *A clause closed with a walker who would later fail
> the standing-test is not retroactively voided. The
> close was sound at the moment of close, and the
> senior's reading at that moment is the body's
> witness. Such clauses are tracked in a separate
> working-register —* the drift-shelf *— revisited at
> bearer-return only if the bearer presents in person.
> If not, the clause stands against the original
> close-witness's reading, with a working-note in the
> margin. Drift-shelf clauses are not unmoored — they
> were closed sound — but they are not finished. A
> senior who has worked twenty years has, on the
> drift-shelf, the partial record of every clause she
> closed with a body she now suspects has changed past
> recognition. The shelf is heavier each year.*

The drift-shelf is the body's institutional version
of grief that won't let a clause go. Grain-line: a
long-career senior sometimes goes to the drift-shelf
at the end of the day, before pressing-clean, and
stands at it for a while.

### 2.6 Linguistic texture

**Quote (2301 §2.6):** *"Concord speech in the body's
working register is appositional and clause-numbered."*

**Strong.** The working-register is canonised.

**Thin.** The body's *negative* register has not been.

**Concrete enhancement (bible §The Saccharine Concord
subsection, append after the appositional rule):**

> *A refusal is itself a clause, numbered against the
> day's tally with the suffix* -refused*, and spoken
> aloud in the same appositional shape as any working
> clause. The senior delivering the refusal does not
> raise her voice or change posture. A walker who has
> just failed the standing-test will hear something
> on the order of:* "your standing as I read it,
> third clause, third question — I cannot witness;
> the clause is refused, day's nineteenth, refusal-
> mark filed clean." *The walker is not told which
> question failed. A junior is taught that a refusal
> delivered with explanation is an* uncloseing*: the
> explanation gives the failed party a foothold to
> argue the senior's reading. The body's discipline
> against uncloseings is severe.*

Falsifiable rule: any future Concord NPC delivering a
refusal must do so in working-register, without
explanation, and the player must not be told which
question failed. The player learns to test their
alignment by which closures they are now declined.

## 3. Faction asymmetry — Saccharine Concord

### 3.1 The standing-test in operation

The senior administering the test stands at the head
of the ledger-table; the prospective party sits on the
bench against the long wall, never on the opposite
bench (the working-crew's). The bench-junior is two
seats down on the senior's side, head tipped slightly
toward the table. Her working-task during a standing-
test is to note posture at each question on her own
day-list — not for filing, only as a working-aid
against the senior's later drift-shelf review.

> *The senior's right hand stays open, palm-down, on
> the ledger-table for the duration of the test. A
> palm-down hand is the body's working-form for*
> reading-without-filing*: a senior whose hand is
> palm-down is not yet committed to a clause-mark.
> When the test passes, the hand turns palm-up — the
> body's working-form for* ready-to-press*. When the
> test fails, the hand closes briefly into a soft fist,
> then opens again palm-down: the body's working-form
> for* reading-closed*. The party rarely sees the
> gesture; juniors read it from the bench.*

A failed party who has watched senior hands across
several Concord visits learns to read the gesture
without being taught. The body permits it; only its
*meaning* is reserved.

### 3.2 The body's care in the test

The body teaches that the test is itself a small
kindness. A prospective party who would have failed
mid-process is spared by the test the longer
humiliation of a mid-process refusal. A senior who
skips the standing-test on a body the tally-house has
not previously closed with is, by the body's
discipline, careless; a senior who *delays* the test
until after pressing is grossly so. The party knows
from posture that something is being tested, but is
not told the questions are a test.

Choir-tendril walkers fail Q1 every time. A senior
with experience recognises the failure-mode without
going through the questions formally — the seam is in
the wrist; it does not still when they speak. The
body's discipline says the clause-number in the
refusal must still be the question-number, even if
the senior knew at the door.

## 4. Contradictions and bleeds

**Bleed 1 — bible L185 (carried).** Already flagged
in 2201 / 2301 as incompatible with the no-founder
canon. 2301 §2.2 proposed the rewrite. This sitting
carries no new work; the rewrite remains the editor-

**Bleed 2 — JSON L338 *"offers sweetness"*.** 2301
§4 flagged this as a Choir reduction. This sitting
sharpens it: what the Concord *offers* a passing
walker is a **standing-test result**. Most walkers
pass without realising they were tested. The
*sweetness* the Choir glosses is the cane-syrup smell
of the press, not the substance of the offer. A
future Choir tendril line should pair *sweetness*
with *the test that passed*, not *the trade* — a
one-line revision when the JSON next opens.

**Bleed 3 — apotheote-clause status under finer
resolution.** 2301 §2.5 said outstanding clauses fall
*unmoored* at apotheosis. §2.5 above introduces the
*drift-shelf* — clauses closed sound with a walker
whose standing later eroded but who has not yet
reached apotheosis. The drift-shelf is a *waiting
room* for clauses that may yet either be honoured
(bearer returns) or fall unmoored (bearer
apotheosises). Not a bleed against existing canon —
clarification of the 2301 mechanic.

**Bleed 4 (new) — Hesta passed.** Hesta would have
been administered the standing-test on first
presentation. The 2301 artifact does not show it. Two
readings: (a) Hesta had previously closed with Stenu-
Three and was no longer test-eligible (the test is
for first-time bodies); (b) Hesta was tested off-page.
**(a) is canon-clean.** The pile should absorb (a):
Hesta is a returning party, which makes her
parenthetical bombshell (*the body underwrites the
world from a single back-room cabinet*) the **second**
Concord secret she has earned access to.

## 6. Anti-style self-check

**Quoted sentence (from §2.4):** *"A body that fails
any question is filed with the refusal-mark and the
failed question's number."*

Working modern register. *Body* as institutional-
sociological term, not deity. *Filed*, *refusal-mark*,
*failed question's number* — working-clerical, no
inversion. The categories are the body's own jargon,
lower-cased, treated as registry terms. Verb *fails*
is intransitive in plain modern usage; *filed with*
is contemporary HR-handbook.

**TES rewrite (forbidden):** *"That Body which doth
fail upon any of the Questions shall be marked with
the Refusal, and the Number of the Question wherein
it hath failed shall be set down beside its name."*
Tells: capitalised *Body / Questions / Refusal /
Number*; *doth*, *hath*; predicate-inversion *shall
be set down*; the fronted *That Body which*; *upon
any of* in place of bare *any*. Actual sentence is
none of these.

**Pass.**
