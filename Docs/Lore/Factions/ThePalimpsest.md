# The Palimpsest

> *The Palimpsest is the writing under the writing — and it remembers
> what you don't.*

---

## What it is

Not a faction. A *condition*. The Palimpsest is what remains of the old
world, pressed against the underside of the present. The cataclysm
overwrote reality imperfectly — like a manuscript scraped clean and
written over, with the older words still faintly visible — and the
Palimpsest *is the layering*. A few places, a few objects, and a few
people exist in two times at once.

The Palimpsest has no centralised consciousness. There is no one to
talk to. It is more like a library that reads itself, the accumulated
memory of the world pressing up against the present, occasionally
breaking through. People who learn to read the layering — through long
practice, or through being born partial-Glimpsing on what the Curation
calls a *bleed-line* — become *readers*. A few stabilise mid-change as
**doubled readers** (formal name: Re-pressed) — both the *now* and the
*was* legible in the same body.

---

## What it believes

If "believes" is even the right word: the Palimpsest does not arbitrate
which layer of a place is *true*. To it, *all of them are, at the
pressure the writing took.* A reader who insists on a single layer is
*flattening*, the gentlest reproof in the Palimpsest's vocabulary.

It does not seek to seal the cracks in the substrate. It does not seek
to reverse the cataclysm. The Choir believes it does (and the Choir
treats the Palimpsest as a competitor) — but the Palimpsest, asked
directly, has answered: *we do not know how to seal what we did not
break. We only read what is leaking through.*

The Palimpsest grammar refuses closure. Past, present, and a softer
future are spoken simultaneously: *I lifted-and-am-lifting-and-will-
have-lifted the cup.* Re-pressed readers can hold all three. To them,
*closing* a sentence — picking one tense — is a courtesy paid to the
listener's grammar, not the honest form.

## A starter NPC

You will likely meet **a first-time Echo-Reader** — a young walker who
has just discovered the Glimpse, who has just realised that the empty
field she is standing in has *layers*. She is sitting on a stone with
both hands flat on it, eyes closed, listening to the stone's history
the way you would listen to a low note. After a while she will look
up. Her eyes will not focus quite the same way they did an hour ago.

If you ask her what the stone said, she will tell you. She will tell
you accurately. She will not understand, yet, that she has just begun
to remember a thing that did not happen to her.

## Where it lives

In the layering of every place that was sufficiently inhabited before
the breaking. Old roads. Half-collapsed cities. Wells. Sleeping rooms.
Battlefields. The thicker the historical use, the thicker the
Palimpsest at that location. Some surfaces are *thick* (three or more
layers); some are *thin* (under-text faded with disuse); some are
*flat* — the layers have equalised, and even a gifted reader can no
longer tell which was first.

There is no Palimpsest *city*. There is no Palimpsest *temple*. There
is the layering of every other place. There is also the **Cosmic Wood**,
where psionic readers gather — the tree-city of Chavvah, the taproot of
Eyn Roj — but these are sites, not faction-headquarters.

## Going deeper

If you want to know more:

- **Echo Reading.** Touch an object and feel its history. The Glimpse
  is the toggle, Echo Reading is the touch, the **Manuscript** is
  writing on the layering yourself, and the **Revision** is briefly
  restoring a place to a previous state. Each costs the reader
  something; each is real.
- **The Re-pressed (the doubled).** The Palimpsest's stable mid-state.
  Both layers legible in the same body. Their mornings are odd: the
  over-text body sits up while the under-text body still dreams.
- **Transcription.** The Palimpsest's apotheosis. The walker becomes a
  memory written into the page — every layer of history at once,
  forever, witness rather than participant. Requires a single living
  witness to *remember* the walker after.
- **The unread page.** A surface no one is reading is not blank — it is
  *between readings*. Layers drift toward equilibrium over time. The
  Curation has a technique for *re-pressuring* a flat surface back into
  legibility. Five known re-pressurings have been deemed *successful*;
  five more have not, and are filed only by their absences.
- **Forced flattening.** What happens when the Choir digests a
  Palimpsest-readable surface — the layers equalise live, in real time.
  A reader who Glimpses such a surface in its last moments gets one
  bilingual reading: a single sentence true in both grammars at once.
