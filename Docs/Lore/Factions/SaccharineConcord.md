# The Saccharine Concord

> *The Concord believes every debt can be balanced. They are quietly
> afraid this is wrong.*

---

## What they are

The traders. The market-makers, the assayers, the keepers of the
ledger. The Concord runs every market in the world — including some it
does not own, because no one else has the protocols.

Their currency is the **sweet-mark,** a compressed honey-grain disc you
can eat after spending. Their language is mercantile through and
through. Even casual conversations carry small priced clauses (*I will
meet you at Salt-Hinge, ¼*); the fraction is the *opening cost* of the
meeting. Concord children learn this before they learn full sentences.

They look genial — sweet things, candied bark, glazed grain, friendly
greetings — and they are. They are also the most metaphysically
ambitious faction. Quietly.

---

## What they believe

That value is a layer of the world the cataclysm did not reach. The
breaking cracked the material world (the Choir's stratum) and the
temporal world (the Palimpsest's stratum). It did not crack *agreement*
between people. The Concord considers consensus its own substrate, and
their entire economic order rests on this claim.

They believe every debt can be balanced. Their actual practice — three
centuries deep — is **the half-redeemed debt**: a debt repeatedly
halved until the remainder is too small to denominate. The debt is
never truly closed, only reduced past speech. They have institutionalised
this as the standard. Their senior thinkers know that *true closure* is
in fact dangerous — it leaves no record, and Concord metaphysics requires
records to persist. There is one fully-closed debt in living memory.
The Reckoner who closed it carries it in her pocket because the
institution structurally cannot file it.

Their secret problem is harder still. Their **coins are not minted —
they are gathered.** Every coin in circulation pre-dates the cataclysm.
The coins are running out. Concord assayers can no longer reliably
identify their own alloys. Their senior assayer estimates that
Foretime stock will be exhausted in eleven years.

## A starter NPC

You will likely meet **Brem the apprentice** — sitting in the
**Reckoner's chair** at a Salt-Hinge stall, listening to a trade he is
not party to. He is tallying *priced clause tails* on a slate: every
fractional unit that passes between buyer and seller, including the
ones tucked into idle conversation. At the end of the trade he reads
his tally aloud. If he is within a quarter-credit of the actual price,
he has *heard the trade.* If he isn't, he sits the chair another day.

He is patient and bright and entirely unaware that the Concord he is
training to serve is metaphysically running out of time. If you tell
him, he won't believe you.

---

## Where they live

Everywhere there is trade — and Concord trade reaches everywhere. Their
spiritual centre is **Salt-Hinge**, a market at the cross-faction
boundary, where all six factions converge four mornings a week. The
Concord runs it. The half-redemption convention — any market trade can
be re-opened next market-day at half its original price, no reason
given — is their innovation, fifty years old.

Beyond the market they have **chapter-houses** in three regional
flavours: Northern, Southern, and Coastal. Each chapter-house teaches
a different one of the three founder-stories. The chapter-houses do
not agree on which story is true, and the disagreement is a doctrinal
discipline.

## Going deeper

If you want to know more:

- **The Founder's Own.** A small thirty-one-page register kept in the
  Northern chapter-house. Three pages filled. Twenty-eight blank. The
  fourth diner — un-named in the entries — was the founder eating
  their own loaf. The Concord cannot close it, because the
  counterparty record exists in the same hand.
- **Tossan's slip.** The only fully-closed Concord debt in living
  memory. Folded once. In Reckoner Tossan's outer pocket. The
  institution structurally cannot accommodate it.
- **The priorless cabinet.** The Concord's most valuable cabinet,
  officially empty (Veshen refuses to register what she suspects is
  in it). The Curation reads it once a year in a tense the Concord
  cannot register.
- **Mer Hin's day-book.** A small wax-sealed coin in its binding
  margin, six chisel-marks on the rim — the same coin re-classified
  *downward* through five generations of assayers. Veshen signed her
  name to *not knowing.*
- **Senior Assayer Tobir Veshen, Reckoner Tossan, Halen-Iss, Mer Hin.**
  The senior figures whose private worries are the faction's quiet
  weather.
- **Closure** (the Concord apotheosis) and **the Balanced Account**
  (the rumoured limit case) — surrendering the price of being
  recorded, in exchange for being *settled.*
