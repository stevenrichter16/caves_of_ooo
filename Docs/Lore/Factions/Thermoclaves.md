# The Thermoclaves

> *The fire-keepers tend nine fires. The tenth tends itself.*

---

## What they are

Fire-keepers. The Thermoclaves — *the fire-keepers* on first encounter
— live in cohorts that maintain nine numbered fires continuously. Each
fire has a name, a tally, a temperament, and a keeper. Each cohort has
a slate-soot register that is wiped at dawn, and a senior keeper who
carries a small slate of strokes — one per day-of-keeping — forward
each morning.

Their grammar is fire-aspect. Every important verb takes a particle
indicating the speaker's relationship to fire: *-keep* (banked,
ongoing, requires attention), *-quench* (deliberately ended), *-cold*
(failed, the gravest particle), *-watch* (witnessing another's
keeping). There is no particle for *light*; lighting is a moment, not
a duration. Thermoclaves *kindle once* — the founding fire — and
**carry** every fire afterward, ember to ember.

The most important of their nine fires is the **tenth**, which is not
counted, not kept, and older than the camp.

---

## What they believe

The world has no foundation, only kept fires and the cold between them.
A foundation, in Thermoclave grammar, would be *a fire that does not
need keeping*; no such fire has ever been observed. The cataclysm was
*the long quench, then the relighting* — read as a maintenance lapse,
not as a break.

Pressed on what keeps the world between fires, they give one of three
doctrinally co-equal answers: *the next keeper*; *the hand that has
not yet reached the fuel*; *nothing — we accept this.* Apprentices
choose one at first-quench and may change it once, at ninth fire. The
choice is not asked again.

Their substrate-claim is the **ember-floor / kept ember**: the
stratum at which heat ceases to radiate outward and begins to radiate
*downward*, into the substrate itself. A fire kept long enough warms
its own ground. The senior keepers can read this — hold a hand above a
hearth and tell within a minute whether the ember is *kept* or the
warmth is *decay*.

Banking a fire — reducing it to its kept ember without extinguishing —
is the hardest verb. **Banking requires a witness.** A Thermoclave
banking unwitnessed is, grammatically, *not banking but extinguishing*.
The grammar enforces it.

## A starter NPC

You will likely meet **a smoke-born apprentice** — a member of the
eleventh-fire cohort, the bridge generation born in the first twenty
years after the breaking. She keeps a hearth-log in pencil margins,
recording what the senior keepers will not write down: gap-burns, the
private griefs of keepers who have lost too many fires, the long-quiet
elders.

Her hearth-log is not authorised. She knows it is not authorised. She
also knows that a Choir tendril came to the soil-side of her hearth
and asked whether her hearth's *kept ember* and the Brine's *held
depth* were the same thing sampled differently — and the senior
hearth-keeper said *yes, immediately*.

She is sixteen. She knows.

---

## Where they live

In **camps** organised around their nine fires. Each camp's geography
is the count: nine numbered hearths, an unkept *cold-fire* on the
camp's edge for spoiled food, a *quench-fire* for the dead, and the
**untended tenth** — usually marked off, never approached. A camp
without a tenth is not a Thermoclave camp; it is a settlement of
keepers who have lost the count.

The most senior camps cluster near old terrain features the
hearth-keepers describe as *warm rock that is alive on the inside*.
These are probably substrate-leaks the Choir would call *patient soil*
— the same phenomenon read in two grammars.

## Going deeper

If you want to know more:

- **The tenth fire.** Older than the camp, older than the chain. The
  Apprentice's Catechism asks whether it is a *keep, a quench, or a
  cold* and accepts the apprentice's silence as the answer. Probably a
  pre-cataclysm substrate-leak.
- **Sixth-Vell-Keeping, Hearth-Keeper Tossin of the Eleventh, Keeper
  Suul of the Ninth.** The senior figures whose lineages thread the
  faction's quiet hierarchy.
- **Erro.** Keeper of the tenth fire at the cataclysm-quench. Did not
  survive. Keeper Suul was apprenticed to him at fourteen, which makes
  her the only living keeper *lineage-orphaned* to a fire no longer in
  the chain.
- **The long quench / long warming.** The Thermoclave apotheosis —
  walk away from a fire as it cools. The walker becomes a *floor-
  keeper*, tending the substrate's warmth rather than a fire.
- **The fire-tally.** A Thermoclave document signed by every keeper who
  ever held that fire, illegibly. *Asked who wrote a tally, a
  Thermoclave answers: the fire did.*
- **The kept ash.** The pouch of ash a properly-quenched fire produces
  retains heat below ambient. A pouch of kept ash carried near another
  hearth registers to that hearth's keeper as a small rise in the
  ember-floor's reading. It is how some letters travel.
