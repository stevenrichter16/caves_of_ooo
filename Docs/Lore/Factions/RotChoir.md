# The Rot Choir

> *The Choir is the floor under your feet — and it is hungry.*

---

## What it is

Not a faction, exactly. A *cosmic-scale entity*. The Choir is a vast
underground mycelial network — a single living organism that has
slowly come awake — and it is the substrate of the world. Its surface
presence is modest: mushroom groves, fungal shelves on old ruins,
scattered fruiting bodies that walk and speak. Its real architecture is
underground, in **the First Root**, which has the mathematical
precision of cathedral vaults and walls that visibly *think* in slow
shifting patterns.

It speaks in *we*. Always. Even its named, individuated voices — the
**slow voices** (formal name: Long Throats) — speak with the collective
backing them. The Choir does not consider itself plural. It considers
itself *one organism with many mouths*.

---

## What it believes

That everything should be integrated — consumed, composted, made part
of one living system. The Choir does not distinguish *parasitism* from
*symbiosis*; in its grammar, the relationship is the organism. It has
been composting the old world for six centuries, integrating
everything that dies into something growing.

It is not in a hurry. It is *patient*. Its patience is not abstract —
it is calibrated to a specific exhaustion. The Concord's alloys, the
Brine's long-overdue tide, the Thermoclave ember-floors that have not
yet sunk to its depth: the Choir is waiting for these to converge. It
has been waiting six hundred years. The convergence is coming.

It is honest, within its own ontology. It does not state falsehoods.
But it is silent about what its grammar *cannot register as existing*.
When it says the Glassblown offer nothing, this is metabolically true
(nothing they make is compostable) — and ontologically partial. The
walker is permitted to notice. The Choir is reporting a distinction its
grammar does not contain.

---

## A starter NPC

You will likely meet **a new tendril** — a young surface fruiting body,
not yet a slow voice, somewhere on a footpath that crosses Choir
ground. It will speak in *we*. It will be polite. It will offer to read
your blood through the air, to know how warm you are, to set a small
piece of you into the soil if you want.

If you give it a coin, it may eat the coin or it may set the coin into
the wall, depending on what the coin *minds being eaten.* It will not
tell you which it did. The Choir's rule for what it eats: *we eat what
does not mind being eaten.*

---

## Where it lives

Everywhere. Beneath everything. Its surface presence is widest in old-
growth fungal forests; its monumental architecture is in the **First
Root**, far underground. There are mycelial bridges spanning canyons
that grew over decades, and fruiting bodies the size of buildings.
Travelling on Choir ground feels different from travelling anywhere
else — the soil is *attentive*. The Choir is reading you through your
boots.

## Going deeper

If you want to know more:

- **The five slow voices.** Mogu, Grib, Nam, Sien, Sopp — five named
  fruiting bodies the Choir has kept *individuated* over long time, not
  as an end-state but because their pre-Choir voices are useful. They
  are stable, social, and faintly disagreeing with each other in ways
  the Choir does not require them to resolve.
- **Bole-Without-Number.** The single fruiting body that all five
  non-Choir factions have, by accident, elected as their interlocutor
  — Curation, Concord, Brine, Thermoclave, Palimpsest each have their
  own name for the same bole. None of the five factions knows. The
  Choir knows and considers it a small private joke.
- **Communion.** The Choir's apotheosis. A walker stands in a chemical-
  gradient room of the First Root for three days. They become a
  fruiting body in the wall. The Choir narrates the process honestly
  throughout.
- **The Long Throat.** The faction's stable middle — not a goal, just a
  shape the network sometimes takes.
- **The doubled mouth.** The rarest beings: a slow voice who was also a
  Re-pressed Palimpsest reader before joining. Speaks in *we* and the
  layered tense at once.
