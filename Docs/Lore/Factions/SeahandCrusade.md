# The Sealhand Crusade

> *The Overwriting was incomplete. We finish it.*

---

## What they are

The newest faction. The only one that did not inherit a pre-Overwriting
trade. The Sealhand Crusade is forty years old — formed inside living
memory — and is the only faction in the world built around a doctrine
rather than a craft.

Their founding claim: *the world was only half-broken. Both halves now
bleed into each other. The Choir feeds on what bleeds in; the Palimpsest
reads it. Both are sicknesses. The cracks must be sealed shut.*

In practice, this means: burning libraries (the Pale Curation's
chapter-houses especially), dismantling Choir tendrils where they can
be dismantled, hunting Palimpsest readers as *infected*, and salting
the soil in any place where the bleeding-through is strongest. They
are organised, well-equipped, and growing. They recruit from every
other faction. Their converts are usually fervent in proportion to
the depth of the loss the converter survived.

---

## What they believe

The doctrine is clean and sincere. The Sealhand does not consider
itself cruel. It considers itself *surgical*. The world's two cosmic
forces — the Choir and the Palimpsest — are, in Sealhand terms,
*an open wound* and *an infection of that wound*. Sealing means
removing both. When the last tendril is cut and the last reader
stops reading, the world will be, in their grammar, *finished*.

Their apotheosis is **the Sealing**. A Crusader who has sealed enough
cracks — by salt, by fire, by the destruction of layered artifacts —
is anointed with salt and ash and given the title **Sealmaster**.
There are two Sealmasters in the world today.

The Sealhand interprets every other faction's crisis as evidence that
they are right. The Concord's alloys are running out? *The world's
natural healing.* The Brine's long-overdue tide has not returned? *The
sea withdrawing from a wound.* The Curation's twenty-year end-date?
*The old world finally releasing its hold.* They are not faking this
reading. They believe it. They are accelerating.

---

## What distinguishes them

Three things that separate the Sealhand from every other faction:

**They do not negotiate.** Every other faction has conventions for
shared markets, border rituals, hand-off protocols. The Sealhand
considers these conventions *maintaining the wound open*. When they
arrive at a chapter-house or a market-stall, they explain. They do not
bargain.

**They are certain.** The Thermoclaves have three doctrinal answers to
the question of what lies between fires, and accept all three as co-
equal. The Concord has priced ambiguity into its grammar. The Sealhand
does not. Their certainty is not a pose. It is the deepest thing in
their doctrine — certainty is what the sealed world has, once the
bleeding stops.

**They recruit from loss.** The Sealhand's most effective recruiting
tool is proximity to grief. A village burned by Choir fruiting bodies.
A reader who became Re-pressed and was not glad of it. A keeper whose
tenth fire was sealed shut by an early Sealhand column and replaced
with a stone floor. The Crusade finds these people, and it offers
them a story in which their loss is *meaningful* — a data point in
the doctrine of sealing.

## A starter NPC

The Sealhand does not have a starter NPC in the conventional sense.
They do not place themselves in starting villages for recruitment.
They show up at the gates.

The player's first encounter is most likely a **Sealhand column** —
perhaps thirty Crusaders, organised in a way the rest of the world is
not, carrying torches and salt-tools where the rest of the world
carries food and bedding. The column is visible at distance. It is on
the road. It is heading somewhere specific, and it will arrive.

If the player tries to speak with a column member, they will be heard
and then asked a series of questions: *Are you a reader? Have you
touched Choir ground in the last seven days? Do you carry any layered
artifact?* The questioning is not hostile — yet. The Sealhand does
not attack walkers who do not appear infected. But their manner with
the unaffiliated is the manner of a physician assessing a patient: not
cruel, not personal, and not interested in conversation for its own
sake.

## How they sit relative to the other factions

Every other faction now has a position toward the Sealhand:

- The **Pale Curation** files them — at tier-3 only, and in a sleeve
  marked *do not intake unless re-opened*. The Curation is the first
  faction in living memory to formally refuse to witness for another.
  (The Sealhand is the party refused.)
- The **Saccharine Concord** refuses them credit. Veshen's reason, on
  record: *one cannot price a thing that aspires to be unfileable.*
- The **Brine Communion** watches them from offshore. The Sealhand
  destroys salt-tides, which the Brine considers a foundational
  violence. The Brine is not at war. They are watching.
- The **Ember Order** will not light a fire for them. Their doctrine:
  the Sealhand is *cold-coming* in a way no carrying can warm. The
  grammar does not have a further explanation.
- The **Glassblown Remnant** declines their letters by folding them
  back. The Crusade has burned three Glassblown workshops; each time,
  the Glassblown rebuilt without speaking. The Sealhand does not know
  what to do with this.
- **The Choir**, asked once, said: *we taste them. they taste of fire
  that does not yet know what it is burning.* The Choir has twice
  opened the soil to swallow Sealhand columns attempting to seal
  substrate-cracks. It is patient.

The world was not at war forty years ago. It is slowly becoming one.

## Going deeper

If you want to know more:

- **Iron-Hand Anselm.** The fortieth Crusader and the first born after
  the Sealhand's founding. Forty-one years old. Lost his left hand to
  a Palimpsest reading at fifteen — the iron prosthesis is intentional;
  he could afford better. He believes the world will be saved when the
  last reader stops reading. He has never been insincere about this for
  a single day of his life.
- **Sealmaster Iro.** The second Sealmaster. Was a Pale Curation
  surveyor for twenty years before her conversion. Runs the Sealhand's
  book-fires personally. She knows the Curation chapter-houses from
  the inside, which is why the Curation fears her more than Anselm.
- **The Burnt Houses.** Three Sealhand strongholds in the long northern
  flats, built on Choir-grown soil salted dead. Visible at night from
  miles. The Choir cannot approach within three hundred metres.
- **The Sealing.** The Sealhand's apotheosis — salt, ash, the title
  Sealmaster, and the specific conviction that the world is now, in
  this one person, one crack closer to shut.
