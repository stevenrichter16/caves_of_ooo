# The Pale Curation

> *The Curation files everything. They are running out of things to file.*

---

## What they are

The world's archivists. Cataloguers, vault-keepers, surveyors. They
treat the cataclysm as *the Severance* — a clean cut between then and
now — and consider it their job to retrieve every fragment of *then*
without contaminating it with *now*.

Their work is procedural. Every artifact gets a tag. Every tag has a
class, a recovery code, a custodial chain, and a margin that is
supposed to remain blank. Often the margin doesn't.

Among the factions, the Curation are the diplomats. Their grammar is
the only one that runs on every other faction's floor — they can
witness a Concord trade, a Brine recovery, or a Thermoclave keeping
without violating any of those factions' protocols. This makes them
indispensable. It also makes them quietly suspect to almost everyone.

---

## What they believe

That witnessing is not neutral. That filing is itself a kind of action,
and a careful one. That the world before the breaking can be
*retrieved* — never recreated, but at least catalogued — if the work
is done patiently enough.

They have a way of speaking that lays a special emphasis on what is
*on record.* A wall the Curation has not catalogued is not, in their
strong sense, *real.* A wall the Curation *has* catalogued is real even
if the wall itself has fallen. The grammar makes the file
sturdier than the brick.

They also have conventions for what is *deliberately not entered* — a
single mid-line dot read as *I have chosen not to fill this field*, not
as *I do not know.* Curation prose is dense with these unsaid things.
A reader fluent in it can extract from a paragraph not just what was
recorded, but what was *withheld*.

The Curation has, internally, accepted a hard fact: the cataclysm-ruins
are finite. At current intake, they have *twenty years of work
remaining*. After that, they will be a body that maintains what it has
filed. They will not be a body that files. The senior leadership knows.
The juniors do not.

---

## A starter NPC

You will likely meet **A.** — the junior cataloguer. (Her full name is
not on file; she signs her initial.) She is the one who wrote the
margin-note on the doll-in-the-wall recovery, asking why the Choir
preserved that one when it consumed everything else. She is the one
who keeps writing things the Director told her not to write. She has a
quiet, patient grievance with the institution she serves, and she works
later than anyone else.

If you bring her something interesting — an inscription, a salvaged
book, anything — she will catalogue it properly *and* write you an
unofficial note about what she actually thinks it means. The
unofficial note is the more useful document.

---

## Where they live

In **chapter-houses** — vault-and-office complexes, one per region.
Each has tier-1 (top), tier-2, and tier-3 vaults; junior intake desks
out front; the senior Cabinet of Counter-Examples in back, where
forgeries and misfiles live.

The largest is at the chapter-house of the Hyphal Surveys Office,
where Director Ven keeps the **forged-tag drawer** and the much-debated
**Sleeve R-7** — bound versions of what may or may not be the
Curation's own founding charter, which the Director has not opened in
eleven years.

---

## Going deeper

If you want to know more:

- **Sleeve R-7.** Two contradictory versions of the Curation's own
  founding charter, bound by a brass pin. The Director will not open it.
  It is the exception to the twenty-year end-date. *R-7 is its own
  decade.*
- **The First Card.** The Curation's founding myth — a single card
  written in a ruined post-breaking office, kept in a coat pocket for
  fifteen years, never copied. Probably lost.
- **The catalogued mood.** Their grammar's special mood for actions
  that have been observed and filed.
- **The Quiet Hour.** A quarterly closed hour when the Curation looks
  at its own forgeries.
- **The doll-in-the-wall.** Accession 11-K-3318. The doll the Choir
  preserves rather than eats. Surveyor Halen filed it; A. wrote the
  margin-note that has become the Curation's most-discussed text.
- **Director Ven, Surveyor Halen [11], Surveyor Kesti, T. Marn-Dis,
  R-7.** The senior figures whose corresponding files thread the
  Curation's working life.
