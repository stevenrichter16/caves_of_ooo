# The Glassblown Remnant

> *The Glassblown were burned. Now they refuse to make anything permanent.*

---

## What they are

A small craft-people who work with glass paste — the wet, malleable
material that becomes glass when fired. They rarely *fire* it. Most of
what they make is deliberately reversible: shapes that get folded back
into the next batch unfired, agreements that aren't yet sealed,
sentences that aren't yet finished. They speak less than any other
faction. The silences are not empty. They are part of the answer.

They are the survivors of the cataclysm-fire — the same fire that
rendered their ancestors into glass. Of all the factions, the Glassblown
remember the breaking most personally. They were *the fuel of it*.

---

## What they believe

The world is in a *state,* not on a *foundation*.

That state is **unfired** — paste before commitment, soft and
reversible. The cataclysm was the world's first irreversible firing.
The four hundred years since are the world *trying to fold back the
firing,* knowing it cannot. The Glassblown work as though it still
could be folded back, because the discipline of folding is what keeps
something else from being fired. They do not believe the unfiring will
succeed. The doctrine specifically forbids the hope.

They have a way of speaking that other factions find disorienting.
Their grammar has a fourth verb mood for sentences they choose *not* to
finish — and the choosing-not-to is the content. To press a Glassblown
for clarification is impolite. The reader is expected to *let the
unsaid stand as said.*

---

## A starter NPC

You will likely meet your first Glassblown not as a senior shaper but
as **an unfired** — the Glassblown word for a child who has not yet
been given their adult shape-name. They are sitting in the doorway of a
workshop, with a small ball of paste in their hands.

If you ask the unfired a question, they will form a shape between their
palms while you talk, hold it for the length of your question, then
fold it back into the batch beside them. The shape rises and dissolves.
You may not realise that this *was* the answer.

Most travellers don't.

---

## Where they live

In the southern shaperies — small workshops clustered near the **Cool
Kiln**, where the senior paste-mothers tend long-running batches. The
Cool Kiln is the centre of Glassblown life, but most workshops are
modest: a kiln, a sand-bed, a high shelf.

The high shelves of older workshops carry glass-shapes of the dead. The
Glassblown bury their dead by *partially firing* them into vessel-form
and placing the vessel on the shelf of the workshop where they lived.
A Glassblown master may continue working for decades beneath a dozen of
their dead. The weight is part of the discipline.

## Going deeper

If you want to know more:

- **The Six-Almost.** A single kept paste, two centuries old, refused
  six times by six different paste-mothers and never fired. Currently
  in the keeping of Tulen Niemensa-Sopellen at the Cool Kiln.
- **Tulen Niemensa-Sopellen.** Paste-mother of the Cool Kiln. Her name
  means *the-shape-that-is-not-yet* + *the-not-yet-naming-of-it.* She
  has fired only eleven shapes in sixty-eight years.
- **The grief-marker.** A palm-sized cured-glass disc with six numbered
  fields. Most fields blank — the blanks are the testimony.
- **The unfired letter.** Glassblown long-distance correspondence:
  glass-paste sent in wet cloth, fired by the recipient (agreement) or
  folded back (refusal).
- **Unfiring.** The Glassblown apotheosis — a drop of the petitioner's
  blood mixed into a batch, the batch shaped into ordinary vessels by
  others, fired and distributed. *The petitioner is what those vessels
  are, by a fraction.*
- **The unsaid mood.** Their grammar's fourth verb mood — *I went-han
  to the well* means *I considered going and did not, and that is the
  content.*
- **The four first-shapes.** No first-shape exists in any catalogue,
  anywhere — but four warm vessels marked *first-shape* sit in the Pale
  Curation's vault, contradicting both the Glassblown doctrine and the
  Curation's own record. Nobody can explain.
