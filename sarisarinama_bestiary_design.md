# Sarisariñama Bestiary Design Notes

> Companion document to `sarisarinama_herpetofauna_findings.md` (Barrio-Amorós & Brewer-Carías 2008). Translates the real-world Sarisariñama herpetofauna into **Caves of Ooo** bestiary content — proposed in-game names, habitat mappings, combat behaviour, and lore significance — organized by elevation to align with the existing tepui-thread in `IDEAS.md` (Root of the World, Sinkholes, Catacombs, Gin Frogs, Urqu, Catacomb-villagers).
>
> Per the running **Cultural Sources & Credits ledger** in `IDEAS.md`, every borrow is documented; every Ye'kwana name from the source is acknowledged in design notes but **not lifted as an in-game term.**

---

## Methodology and source acknowledgment

**Primary source.** Barrio-Amorós, C. L., & Brewer-Carías, C. (2008). *Herpetological results of the 2002 expedition to Sarisariñama, a tepui in Venezuelan Guayana, with the description of five new species*. Zootaxa, 1942, 1-68. Summarized in the project's `sarisarinama_herpetofauna_findings.md`. This document derives CoO design content from those findings.

**Cultural context.** Sarisariñama lies in **Ye'kwana** (also *Ye'kuana*, *Yekuana*) ancestral territory. The Ye'kwana maintain rich oral cosmology around the mountain, its sinkholes, its fauna, and associated spirits. Several species in the source carry sacred or culturally-weighted Ye'kwana names:

- ***Dendrobates leucomelas*** — Ye'kwana **Wanadi Jiñamohidi**. **Wanadi is the Ye'kwana creator deity.** This is sacred living religion, not folklore.
- ***Bothrops atrox*** — Ye'kwana **Kinoto**. Associated with evil. This is the same Ye'kwana cosmology that gives `IDEAS.md` its **Urqu** entry (the *sari... sari...* spirit of Sarisariñama).
- ***Harpia harpyja*** — Ye'kwana **Dimoshi**. A giant evil bird spirit directly associated with Sarisariñama.
- ***Pseustes poecilonotus*** — Ye'kwana **Wasaihiudei**. Respected as a *protector* against venomous serpents.

**Authoring stance** (mirrors the IDEAS.md Cultural Sources & Credits principle):

1. **Do NOT lift Ye'kwana terms** — *Wanadi*, *Kinoto*, *Dimoshi*, *Wasaihiudei*, *Müdü Wënäwësiye*, *Tamene-mü*, etc. — into in-game text. CoO's own factions provide player-visible vocabulary.
2. Ye'kwana names are recorded here in design notes for transparency and credit, per the running-ledger principle. Add a single **Sarisariñama Bestiary** ledger entry when this content is implemented (recommended additions to `IDEAS.md`'s Cultural Sources & Credits at end of this document).
3. **The Wanadi connection on *Dendrobates leucomelas* is the most sensitive thread** in the entire source. Recommend **not borrowing the divine framing at all** — design CoO's equivalent without any creator-deity association. The frog can be culturally weighted in CoO (rare, valued, mythologized by some in-game faction) without that weight being a borrow from sacred Ye'kwana religion.
4. The Ye'kwana cosmology around **evil-snake** (*Kinoto*) and **giant evil bird** (*Dimoshi*) is *already* the structural source of CoO's **Urqu** entry. New content from those threads should integrate with Urqu rather than parallel it. *Bothrops atrox* and *Harpia harpyja* in CoO become Urqu-affiliated wild fauna — *Urqu's signs in flesh*.
5. The **Pseustes protector-snake** insight is design gold and, mechanically, the natural source of a player-friendly serpent companion or zone-warden NPC. Do not lift the Ye'kwana name; do build on the structural concept.

**Connections to existing IDEAS.md content.**

- **Sarisariñama** is already canonical in CoO via the **Urqu** entry (which derives the *sari... sari...* sonic signature and the cosmic-evil concept from Ye'kwana cave-spirit legend on this exact mountain).
- **Gin Frogs** are *Stefania ginesi*-inspired (a different species from the same genus). Sarisariñama hosts ***Stefania riae*** — an *even more endemic* species — which becomes a canonical **Sarisariñama-specific Gin Frog variant** in CoO.
- The **Sinkholes** entry's **Drowned Sima** and **Choir Cathedral** archetypes map directly onto Sima Mayor's biology (humid mossy walls, accidental fauna entering via crevices, summit-light filtered through bromeliads).
- **Catacomb-villagers**' light-cultivation economy intersects with multiple species below (predator wardens against venomous fauna; tomb-fauna; light-creature breeding stock).

## Habitat zones — mapping the real tepui to CoO topology

The 2008 paper organizes findings by elevation. The mapping is clean:

| Real elevation | Source camp | CoO equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| Lowlands 400-600m | Camp V, Locality VI | Surface villages and adjacent forest/savanna around a tepui |
| Foothill creeks 450-1100m | Camp IV, Locality VI | Lower slopes of a tepui (per **Root of the World** ascent stages) |
| Inside Sima Mayor 1100-1375m | Camp II, Camp I | Canonical **Drowned-Sima / Choir-Cathedral** hybrid archetype |
| Summit 1328m+ | Camp III | Tepui summit zone (per **Root of the World**) |

This gives CoO an immediate biome-by-elevation generator drawing on real Sarisariñama biology — and the elevation gradient itself becomes a player-visible reading of *how deep into the tepui's grammar* the player has gone.

# I. Lowland fauna (tepui base — surface villages, savanna, blackwater streams)

These species map onto **surface villages adjacent to a tepui** in CoO. Player encounters them in the first elevation tier of the ascent, and many are encountered well outside the tepui-thread proper.

## *Bothrops atrox* — the Fer-de-lance / Kinoto / **CoO: the *Sari-Snake***

**Real species.** Large pit viper (commonly 1.2-1.6m, source female 960+160mm), Amazonian-Guianan, hemotoxic venom causing necrosis, tissue damage, hemorrhage. Sit-and-wait ambush predator on small mammals and frogs; the source specimen had a rodent in its stomach. *Ye'kwana **Kinoto**, "associated with evil" — directly the cosmological source of the Urqu entry's "evil spirit on Sarisariñama" thread.*

**CoO in-game name candidates.** *Sari-Snake* (echoing the *sari... sari...* sonic signature from Urqu, claiming the etymological thread); *Stoneback Viper* (descriptive); *Kinothread* (carrying the Ye'kwana association as a borrowed *concept* without lifting the word). **Recommended:** *Sari-Snake* — it integrates with the existing Urqu lore without importing Ye'kwana cosmology.

**Habitat in CoO.** Wherever Urqu is locally active. Lower slopes of any tepui, especially near sinkhole rims (the source specimen was found at the edge of Sima Mayor). Increased spawning frequency during Urqu's manifest periods — they are *Urqu's signs in flesh* and the player learns to read their presence as a manifestation indicator.

**Combat behaviour.**
- Sit-and-wait ambush from concealed cells (under leaf-litter glyphs, in stream-cells).
- Single-strike attack with **Hemotoxic Venom** status: bleeding damage over 10 turns + AV reduction (necrosis).
- Slow movement; will not pursue a fleeing player far.
- **Not aggressive when handled** in the source — design hook: a player with high Apatheia affinity can pass a Sari-Snake without triggering its strike. Bloodlust-aligned players trigger it on sight.

**Lore significance.** The strongest Urqu-thread fauna. A high-population Sari-Snake zone is a manifestation indicator before any other diag clue. Their venom is also the basis for an Urqu-aligned bargain — **Sari-Snake venom mixed with bone-ink** produces a substance the Driving Bloom can feed on.

**Cross-links:** **Urqu**, **Spirit of Apatheia** (handling without provoking), **Driving Bloom** (venom as feedstock).

## *Pseustes poecilonotus polylepis* — the Bird Snake / Wasaihiudei / **CoO: the *Wardline***

**Real species.** Large olive-brown colubrid (~2m). Arboreal, semi-diurnal, eats nestling birds. *Ye'kwana **Wasaihiudei**: respected as **a protector against venomous snakes** — believed to keep viperids away from the tribe.*

**CoO in-game name candidates.** *Wardline* (it walks the boundary, it wards), *Branch-Watcher*, *Olive Sentinel*. **Recommended:** *Wardline* — captures the protective and territorial behaviour, no Ye'kwana borrow.

**Habitat in CoO.** Branch-overhang cells above creeks and trails on lower tepui slopes. Tree-cell dwellers; player sees them as branch-glyphs above their movement path.

**Combat behaviour.**
- **Passive toward the player by default.** Will not strike unless attacked or its territory is invaded.
- Aggressively hunts venomous snakes in the same zone. **A Wardline's presence reduces local Sari-Snake spawn rate.**
- If attacked, fast strike; non-venomous bite damage; will retreat after one or two hits.

**Lore significance.** The structural opposite of the Sari-Snake. Where Sari-Snakes are Urqu's signs, Wardlines are *Urqu-opposed wild fauna* — a free, distributed defence against the manifestation indicator population. Players who learn to recognize a Wardline's branch-cell can use the species *as a tool*: attracting Wardlines to a settlement (with bait or simple co-presence) reduces local Sari-Snake threat.

**Cross-links:** **Urqu** (opposed wild fauna), **Catacomb-villagers** (some catacomb cultures revere Wardlines as living wards — a structural inheritance from the Wasaihiudei concept without lifting the name), **Spirit of Apatheia** (Wardlines exemplify non-aggressive sufficiency).

## *Dendrobates leucomelas* — the Yellow-Banded Poison Frog / Wanadi Jiñamohidi / **CoO: the *Bandfrog***

**Real species.** Small (~3-4cm), brilliant yellow-and-black aposematic skin, batrachotoxin-grade alkaloids, diurnal, active after rain. *Ye'kwana **Wanadi Jiñamohidi** — appears in mythology and basket motifs.* **Wanadi is the Ye'kwana creator deity.**

**Cultural-responsibility note.** This is the **most sensitive species in the source.** The Wanadi association is sacred living religion. **Strong recommendation: do not borrow the divine framing at all.** Design CoO's equivalent without any creator-deity connection.

**CoO in-game name candidates.** *Bandfrog* (visual-descriptive), *Sigil-Frog* (carries the basket-iconography insight without the divinity), *Yellow-Skin*. **Recommended:** *Bandfrog* — terse, visually-descriptive, no cosmological borrow.

**Habitat in CoO.** Forest floors and creek margins on lower tepui slopes; village outskirts. Diurnal — players actually *see* them, unlike most cave fauna.

**Combat behaviour.**
- Passive; does not engage. The frog itself is no threat.
- **Skin-contact damage**: bumping a Bandfrog's cell deals contact poison damage over time. Picking one up bare-handed is briefly lethal at low level.
- The Bandfrog is primarily a **resource species**: harvested skin secretions are the basis for one of the most potent in-game poisons (applied to weapons or thrown items).

**Lore significance.** A *resource-grade* species that is also strikingly beautiful. The **Bower-Folk** (`IDEAS.md`) collect Bandfrogs as living aesthetic objects (kept in glass enclosures lit by bio-light); poison-crafters harvest them with care. A village's relationship with its local Bandfrog population is a quiet political indicator — extracting from them = utilitarian; protecting them = aesthetic/reverent.

**Cross-links:** **Bower-Folk** (collected as aesthetic objects), **Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy** (Bandfrogs displayed in glass enclosures lit by rare-spectrum bio-light strains command absurd prices among the Bower-Folk), **Tinkering** (poison-craft recipes).

## *Rhaebo guttatus* — the Smooth-Sided Toad / Kawau / **CoO: the *Cropside Toad***

**Real species.** Large, plump (~17cm), Amazonian-Guianan, common along riverbanks. Ye'kwana food source.

**CoO in-game name.** *Cropside Toad* (rivers, food).

**Habitat in CoO.** Riverbanks and pond margins. Common, abundant, slow.

**Combat behaviour.**
- Non-aggressive. Hops away when approached.
- Food source — yields meat for surface cultures.
- Mild parotoid-gland toxin makes raw consumption risky; cooked meat is fine.

**Lore significance.** A *commodity* species. Surface villages around a tepui rely on Cropside Toads for protein; Tent-Right hospitality codes (`IDEAS.md`) often feature Cropside meals; the **Saccharine Concord** trades dried Cropside in volume.

**Cross-links:** **Tent-Right**, **Saccharine Concord**.

## *Chaunus marinus* — the Cane Toad / Tururu / **CoO: the *Maw-Toad***

**Real species.** Massive (source females 220mm+), invasive globally. Highly toxic parotoid secretion. Will eat anything that fits in its mouth.

**CoO in-game name.** *Maw-Toad* (size + appetite).

**Habitat in CoO.** Human-disturbed lowlands; village edges; refuse heaps; settlements where humans have created opportunity.

**Combat behaviour.**
- Slow, ground-level, but **dangerous to small companions or pets** — Maw-Toads will eat small rented lantern-beetle colonies, glow-worm jars, and anything in the bio-light economy that fits in a mouth.
- Highly toxic if attacked — splash damage on death (parotoid burst).

**Lore significance.** **Pest species.** Catacomb-villagers consider them an existential threat to their light economy. A Maw-Toad incursion into a catacomb-village's lantern-beetle hatchery is a quest-tier event. Surface and underground cultures broadly agree they should be killed.

**Cross-links:** **Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy** (existential threat to the light supply), **Catacomb-villagers** (pest hunters as a profession).

## *Hypsiboas boans* — the Gladiator Tree Frog / Kï-tó / **CoO: the *Sparring Frog***

**Real species.** Large (~10cm), lowland Guianan, males combat for nesting territory using bony forearm spines. Ye'kwana dry-season protein source.

**CoO in-game name.** *Sparring Frog*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Lowland riverbanks, large tree branches over still water.

**Combat behaviour.**
- **Males are aggressive in breeding season.** Will leap to attack the player's cell with bony-spike forearm strikes — a unique melee attack profile (for an amphibian) doing puncture damage.
- Females ignore the player.
- Outside breeding season, all individuals ignore the player.

**Lore significance.** **Wild fauna with seasonal aggression.** Pairs with a calendar mechanic — certain months produce hostile Sparring Frog encounters; others produce passive ones. Surface villages plan around the cycle. The **Felling-Counter** (`IDEAS.md`) artifact can predict the breeding-season activations alongside larger cosmic cycles.

**Cross-links:** **Felling-Counter** (cycle prediction), **Tent-Right** (Sparring Frog cuisine is dry-season hospitality).

## *Lithobates palmipes* — the Neotropical Green Frog / Murunashi / **CoO: the *Reedfrog***

**Real species.** Large, aquatic, abundant in Canaracuni Creek. Tadpoles algae-eaters. Highly appreciated Ye'kwana food.

**CoO in-game name.** *Reedfrog*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Creek edges, foothill streams.

**Combat behaviour.** Passive. Flees into water on approach. **Resource species** — meat, eggs, and tadpoles are all harvestable.

**Lore significance.** **Algae-grazing tadpoles** are a small but real piece of the bio-economy: they keep stream-algae populations in check, which keeps water clear, which keeps **bioluminescent slime pools** clean. A Reedfrog die-off (overharvesting, pollution) leads to algae blooms that **crash the slime supply** for a region. Subtle ecology.

**Cross-links:** **Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy** (slime-pool clarity).

## *Chelonoidis denticulata* — the Yellow-Footed Tortoise / Wayamo / **CoO: the *Yellowfoot Wayfarer***

**Real species.** Large slow tortoise (~50cm), lowland rainforest, Ye'kwana food.

**CoO in-game name.** *Yellowfoot Wayfarer* (the slow-traveling pace gives the name its second word).

**Habitat in CoO.** Lowland rainforest. Wanders.

**Combat behaviour.** Effectively non-combat. Cannot be threatened by small attacks (high natural AV from shell). **Excellent companion candidate** — slow but durable; carries cargo; cannot be lost.

**Lore significance.** **Companion species** for low-stakes carrying. A tortoise carrying the player's bio-light supply through a long catacomb expedition is an evocative image. Their lifespan exceeds the player's run.

**Cross-links:** **Catacomb-villagers** (some villages keep generations-old Wayfarers as living archives — the *tortoise has seen the village's whole history*), **Lineage** (a tortoise that survives multiple player runs becomes a continuity-thread between descendant playthroughs).

## *Paleosuchus* sp. — the Black Dwarf Caiman / Yadibe / **CoO: the *Riverwarden***

**Real species.** Small (~1.5m) crocodilian, riverbanks; Ye'kwana hunted for food.

**CoO in-game name.** *Riverwarden*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Riverbanks of large tepui-base waterways.

**Combat behaviour.**
- Aggressive when approached on land; will lunge from water into shore-cells.
- Strong bite + grapple — drags player into water for damage-over-time (drowning + bite).
- Difficult to kill due to natural AV.

**Lore significance.** **Apex-tier riverbank predator**, distinct from the catacomb dead-zone apex predators (`Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy`). Surface dangers, not catacomb dangers — gives the surface a real combat threat that isn't already covered by the existing tepui-thread.

**Cross-links:** none required; it's its own threat.

## *Helicops angulatus* — the Brown-Banded Water Snake / Mokóroho / **CoO: the *Shallow-Strike***

**Real species.** Common aquatic colubrid, juveniles with red venters. Ye'kwana believe it venomous (the source notes this is *believed* but not necessarily true biologically).

**CoO in-game name.** *Shallow-Strike*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Stream cells, shallow water in lowland rainforest.

**Combat behaviour.**
- Strikes from water cells. Mild venom (status effect: weakened muscles, reduced movement speed for 5 turns).
- Common spawn, low individual threat, dangerous in numbers.

**Lore significance.** Filler-tier antagonist. The Ye'kwana belief-vs-reality gap (people think it's deadly venomous, biology says no) gives a small lore hook: in CoO, surface NPCs **fear** Shallow-Strikes more than the actual threat warrants, which is a useful dramatic asymmetry — the player can demonstrate competence by killing one casually in front of villagers.

## *Hypsiboas* cf. *crepitans* — the Emerald-Eyed Tree Frog / Kuhaka / **CoO: the *Veil-Eye***

**Real species.** Variable lowland Guianan, day color bluish tan, night color brown.

**CoO in-game name.** *Veil-Eye*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Lowland riverbank trees. Color-shifts between day and night.

**Combat behaviour.** Passive. Notable for **day/night appearance shift** — the same individual displays differently at different times. Mechanically useful for the player as a **timekeeping fauna** (their color tells you time of day in catacombs and other timezone-ambiguous contexts).

**Lore significance.** **Practical fauna**: the player who studies Veil-Eyes can read time-of-day in lightless environments by watching for the Veil-Eye color-shift in pets/companion versions of the species.

## *Ameiva ameiva* — the Amazon Race Runner / Makasana / **CoO: the *Sun-Striker***

**Real species.** "The most striking and dominant lizard in the lowlands around the massif" (source). Fast diurnal teiid.

**CoO in-game name.** *Sun-Striker*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Sunny lowland clearings, savanna, village edges.

**Combat behaviour.** Aggressive territorial display. Fast melee. Bites hard but no venom.

**Lore significance.** Dominant lowland lizard — a status indicator. A village's Sun-Striker population is high in healthy ecosystems; absent populations suggest something is wrong (Choir colonization, Driving Bloom infestation, or Urqu activity).

## Other lowland species (concise)

- ***Rhinella margaritifer*** complex (crested forest toad, Cro-cro/Wa-sa-wa) → **CoO: *Crestmark Toad*.** Sex-dimorphic crested heads. Forest ground spawn.
- ***Leptodactylus*** spp. (*andreae*, *diedrus*, *riveroi*) → **CoO: *Litterfrog*** (collective, three local variants). Forest-floor spawn; calls fill the soundscape.
- ***Kentropyx calcarata*** (striped forest whiptail) → **CoO: *Stripe-Whip*.** Forest-edge cells; fast.
- ***Mabuya*** sp. (brown-tailed forest skink) → **CoO: *Sun-Skink*.** Sunny clearings; harmless.
- ***Imantodes cenchoa*** (blunt-headed tree snake, Kan-mwa-atotijiri) → **CoO: *Lean-Branch Serpent*.** Arboreal, slim, harmless.
- ***Liophis typhlus*** (velvety swamp snake, Mansé) → **CoO: *Backwater Hunter*.** Searches creek backwaters for frogs; small, harmless to player.

# II. Foothill / lower-slope fauna (450-1100m, waterfall spray, cascade zones)

These map onto the **lower slopes of a tepui** in the Root of the World ascent.

## *Anomaloglossus moffetti* — the Sarisariñama Rocket Frog / **ENDEMIC** / *sapito niñera de Sarisariñama* / **CoO: the *Cascade-Father***

**Real species.** Sarisariñama-endemic. ~27mm, smooth skin, pale lateral stripe, yellow-orange belly. Cascading streams and waterfall spray zones. *No calls heard.* The Spanish common name *"sapito niñera"* (little babysitter toad) hints at parental care behaviour typical of *Anomaloglossus*.

**CoO in-game name.** *Cascade-Father* — captures the parental-care implication and the waterfall habitat. Other candidates: *Spray-Skin*, *Quietfoot*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Waterfall cells and cascade-spray cells specifically. Will not appear in standing water or dry cells. **Highly localized** — gives players a reason to seek out specific waterfall zones.

**Combat behaviour.**
- **Passive throughout combat.** Cannot be aggressed effectively (always near water; uses spray as cover; jumps to next cell on approach).
- The frog itself is *not* the encounter — Cascade-Fathers are **indicator species** for high-quality cascade-spray zones, which themselves are biodiversity hotspots in the source paper.

**Lore significance.** **Indicator fauna.** A village whose nearby cascade no longer hosts Cascade-Fathers is a village in ecological trouble. Catacomb-villagers cultivating waterfalls for slime extraction monitor Cascade-Father populations as a quality signal.

**Cross-links:** **Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy** (cascade quality affects slime-pool yield), **Catacomb-villagers** (indicator-species knowledge).

## *Hyalinobatrachium mesai* — the Sarisariñama Glass Frog / **ENDEMIC** / *ranita de cristal* / **CoO: the *Glasspane Frog***

**Real species.** Sarisariñama-endemic. **Transparent skin**, pale green bones visible in life, lime-green body, transparent pericardium (you can see its heart beating). Calls from *Heliconia* leaves above streams. Single-note 4700 Hz call.

**CoO in-game name.** *Glasspane Frog* — captures the transparency directly.

**Habitat in CoO.** Specifically on broad-leaf cells (Heliconia analogs) above streams in foothill rainforest. Heard before seen — its high-pitched call carries.

**Combat behaviour.**
- **Effectively non-combat.** A breath misses it; a strike at it usually misses by virtue of its translucency.
- **Resource species:** harvested glass-frog skin yields a *transparent skin-resin* used in CoO crafting — applied to weapons or armor it grants brief invisibility-flicker (camouflage tier, not full invisibility).

**Lore significance.** **Aesthetic / mystical species.** The Bower-Folk prize Glasspane Frogs as living display objects. The transparent-pericardium imagery (heart visible while alive) gives the species ritual significance — *you can see its heart and it cannot hide.* Surface mystics use it as a metaphor for honesty; one possible Spirit-of-Inquiry sub-cult treats Glasspane observation as a meditative practice.

**Cross-links:** **Bower-Folk** (aesthetic display), **Spirit of Inquiry** (Glasspane meditation), **Tinkering** (transparent-resin crafting).

## *Hypsiboas tepuianus* — the Tepui Tree Frog / **NEW SPECIES** / *rana tepuyana* / **CoO: the *Tepui Bronze***

**Real species.** Newly described in the source paper (separated from *H. benitezi*). Reddish-orange fingers, toes, webbing. Calls from bushes 0.5-1.5m above streams. 2-5 note call ~2500 Hz. Known 400-1600m.

**CoO in-game name.** *Tepui Bronze* — captures the orange-bronze coloration and the tepui-locality.

**Habitat in CoO.** Mid-elevation tepui slopes, specifically near streams and waterfalls.

**Combat behaviour.** Passive. Calls heavily — useful as **soundscape fauna** (a player navigating an unmapped tepui slope can locate streams by Tepui Bronze calls).

**Lore significance.** **Newly-described** in real life — gives CoO an aesthetic-narrative hook: Tepui Bronzes are "still being discovered" by surface-faction biologists. The Palimpsest pays a small premium for new specimens (every catalog entry is precious). Inkbound (if introduced) treats new species as new sentences in the world's text.

**Cross-links:** **Palimpsest**, **Inkbound** (new sentences in the text).

## *Neusticurus tatei* — the Water Teiid / Maskani / **CoO: the *Cascade-Lacer***

**Real species.** Highly aquatic teiid. Common in Canaracuni Creek. Adults rest at night in waterfall-spray crevices. Tail regeneration common (suggesting heavy predation). Microsympatric with *N. racenisi* (also in the source).

**CoO in-game name.** *Cascade-Lacer* (the lacework pattern of their skin + cascade habitat). Sister species *Neusticurus rudis* and *N. racenisi* become regional variants.

**Habitat in CoO.** Stream cells and waterfall-spray cells. Day-active swimmers; night-resters in spray crevices.

**Combat behaviour.**
- Passive but **fights when cornered.** Sharp bite, no venom.
- **Tail-drop** as escape — the player who catches the tail finds themselves holding only the regenerating limb while the lizard swims away. Mechanic: a low chance to drop tail-resource on a kill instead of full corpse.

**Lore significance.** **Companion candidate** for water-traveling players. A tame Cascade-Lacer can scout submerged stream cells the player cannot enter, returning information about depth and current.

**Cross-links:** **Catacomb-villagers** (some catacomb cultures keep tame Cascade-Lacers as scouts).

# III. Sima Mayor fauna (1100-1375m — sinkhole interior)

These species map onto **CoO's canonical Drowned-Sima / Choir-Cathedral hybrid** — the inside-of-the-sinkhole zone. The 2008 paper gives Sima Mayor real biological character: it's not a barren sinkhole, it has its own fauna.

## *Stefania riae* — the Sarisariñama Carrying Frog / **ENDEMIC** / Müdü Wënäwësiye / **CoO: the *Sima Gin Frog***

**Real species.** Sarisariñama-endemic *Stefania*, the same genus as *S. ginesi* that inspired CoO's **Gin Frogs**. Found mostly **inside Sima Mayor** on humid mossy walls, sandstone cave/crevice walls, and bushes 0.3-1.5m above ground. Females carry developing froglets on dorsal skin. Four color-pattern morphs observed in source. **No advertisement calls** (release calls only).

**CoO in-game name.** *Sima Gin Frog* — explicitly a Gin Frog variant tied to a specific named sinkhole. Reads as a regional sub-species of the canonical Gin Frog.

**Habitat in CoO.** Specifically inside named sinkholes — Sima Mayor and equivalents. Mossy-wall cells, sinkhole-crevice cells, bromeliad cells.

**Combat behaviour.** Identical to base Gin Frog (per `IDEAS.md` Gin Frogs entry): brood-armor mechanic with `BabyCount * AvPerBaby` AV, hits that don't penetrate strip a baby instead of damaging the parent. **Sima Gin Frog distinctions:**
- **Larger brood** (12-16 froglets vs. base 6-12) due to sinkhole humidity.
- **Color-morph variation** (four observed) maps to four AV-pattern variants — players learn to read the morph for tactical info on resistance type.
- **Silent** (no advertisement call) — they cannot be located by sound, only by sight, making them a stealth-encounter.

**Lore significance.** Confirms the Gin Frog species' **deepest-tepui form.** Sima Gin Frogs are *the* canonical Gin Frog of the central tepui; their abundance inside Sima Mayor is a quiet validation that the Gin Frog concept maps to real Sarisariñama biology. The four color morphs become four lore variants: *Plain*, *Striped*, *Chevroned*, *Gold-Dusted* — each with a distinct catacomb-villager cultural reading.

**Cross-links:** **Gin Frogs** (this is the elite tepui-endemic variant), **Sinkholes Drowned-Sima archetype** (canonical fauna), **Catacomb-villagers** (cultural readings of color morphs).

## *Gonatodes superciliaris* — the Sarisariñama Forest Gecko / **ENDEMIC** / Tamene-mü / **CoO: the *Sima Pricklebrow***

**Real species.** Sarisariñama-endemic gecko described in source. **Distinct elongate supraciliary spine** ("eyebrow" spine). Strong sexual dimorphism: males blackish-blue with orange/yellow throat; females dark green with blue vertebral stripe and yellow spots. Forest-floor microhabitats inside Sima Mayor — leaf litter, under rocks, crevices, never more than 30cm from litter. Diurnal. Communal nesting (16-egg communal clutch in source).

**CoO in-game name.** *Sima Pricklebrow* — captures the supraciliary spine + sinkhole habitat. Sexual dimorphism gets named distinctions: *Indigo Pricklebrow* (male) and *Lacescale Pricklebrow* (female).

**Habitat in CoO.** Sinkhole-floor cells, leaf-litter, under rock-glyphs, in crevices. Diurnal — visible during in-game daytime if any light reaches the floor.

**Combat behaviour.**
- **Predatory** — small enough to be more nuisance than threat, but in groups they're a real encounter.
- Cricket-tier diet (one source specimen ate a cricket): they hunt small fauna in the sinkhole.
- **Swarm behaviour** at communal nest sites — disturbing a nest cell triggers 16-egg-worth-of-defenders cascading from nearby cells.

**Lore significance.** **Sinkhole-endemic apex of the small-fauna tier.** Catacomb-villagers may keep Pricklebrow colonies as low-tier guard fauna — the swarm response makes them cheap living perimeter alarms. Their distinctive supraciliary spine is harvestable as a tier-1 weapon component.

**Cross-links:** **Catacomb-villagers** (perimeter-guard colonies), **Sinkholes** (canonical Drowned-Sima fauna).

## *Norops ortonii* — Orton's Beta Anole / **CoO: the *Lichenback Anole***

**Real species.** Found inside Sima Mayor on lichen-covered rocks in the driest sinkhole zone. Second confirmed Venezuelan record. Small dewlap.

**CoO in-game name.** *Lichenback Anole*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Dry-zone cells inside sinkholes — the parts not soaked by groundwater or wall-runoff. Lichen-rock cells.

**Combat behaviour.** Passive, fast. Display-attacks (dewlap-flash) on perceived rivals; can be triggered by mirror-glyphs.

**Lore significance.** **Dry-zone indicator.** Their presence marks sinkhole sub-zones that are *not* Drowned-Sima — useful navigational fauna. A sinkhole with lots of Lichenback Anoles is a dry sinkhole, with different content tables than a wet one.

## *Osteocephalus taurinus* — the Casque-Headed Frog / Ko-maka / **CoO: the *Helmwood Frog***

**Real species.** Generally a primary/secondary rainforest frog. The source recorded a female **inside Sima Mayor** unexpectedly — interpreted as accidental, possibly arrived via crevices/subterranean water channels seeking water in dry season.

**CoO in-game name.** *Helmwood Frog*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Standard surface forest. **Rare accidental Sima Mayor encounter** is the interesting hook: occasionally a Helmwood appears inside a Drowned-Sima, having traveled through subterranean water routes, *implying that hidden water passages connect surface and sinkhole zones.*

**Lore significance.** **Hidden-passage indicator.** A Helmwood Frog inside a sinkhole is canonical evidence that an unexplored water-passage connects this sinkhole to a surface stream. Players who track Helmwood encounters can map the **Door-archetype** subset of sinkholes (per `IDEAS.md` Sinkholes — the rare wormhole archetype).

**Cross-links:** **Sinkholes** (Door archetype indicator).

# IV. Summit fauna (1300m+ — tepui top, bromeliad scrub)

These map onto **CoO's tepui-summit zone** (per Root of the World).

## *Pristimantis sarisarinama* — **ENDEMIC** / **CoO: the *Summit Singer***

**Real species.** Sarisariñama-endemic. New species from source. Summit/sima/upper-slope, 1000-1400m. Calls from tree trunks 1-3m above ground at twilight (1600-1900 hours). 1-2-note calls every 2-6 seconds, ~2500 Hz. W-shaped dorsal mark.

**CoO in-game name.** *Summit Singer*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Tepui summit forest cells, especially mossy tree-trunks. Twilight-active.

**Combat behaviour.** Passive. **Soundscape signature** — the player learns to recognize the Summit Singer's W-mark and 1-2-note call as the *sound of being on a tepui summit.* It's an audio-lore element.

**Lore significance.** **Atmospheric anchor.** When the player crosses the elevation threshold into summit zones, the Summit Singer's call begins. Conversely, its silence signals something has changed about the summit (Choir colonization muting wildlife; Driving Bloom infestation; Urqu activity).

**Cross-links:** **Root of the World** (summit elevation indicator), **Urqu** (silence as indicator of activity).

## *Pristimantis* cf. *cavernibardus* — **CoO: the *Cave-Singer***

**Real species.** Tentative ID. Diurnal trill call. Green lichen-like cryptic coloration. Abundant on Sarisariñama summit; calls during rainy or cloudy days.

**CoO in-game name.** *Cave-Singer*.

**Habitat in CoO.** Summit and upper-slope humid cells. **Rain-active** — calls increase during in-game rain.

**Combat behaviour.** Passive, cryptic. **Camouflage tier-1** — the player who is not paying attention will miss them entirely.

**Lore significance.** **Weather-soundscape coupling.** Like Summit Singers, but rain-coupled. A summit zone where Cave-Singers refuse to call during rain is a zone in trouble.

## *Tropiduridae* sp. — Granite Dome Lizard / **CoO: the *Brocchinia-Sentinel***

**Real species.** Tentative ID; possibly a tepui-endemic tropidurid not yet described. Found on **granitic domes covered by *Brocchinia hechtioides* bromeliads** at the summit. The *only* lizard observed at the Sarisariñama summit.

**CoO in-game name.** *Brocchinia-Sentinel* — names it for its bromeliad-cell habitat.

**Habitat in CoO.** Summit granite-dome cells with bromeliad cover.

**Combat behaviour.** Cryptic; flees into bromeliad cells when approached.

**Lore significance.** **Possible new endemic species.** In CoO, treated as a *Sarisariñama-summit-only* species — encountered nowhere else in the world. Catching one is a unique-event Palimpsest payment.

**Cross-links:** **Palimpsest** (bounty for new-species evidence).

# V. Flora callouts

## *Brocchinia hechtioides* — Carnivorous Tepui Bromeliad / **CoO: the *Tank-Brocchinia***

**Real species.** Carnivorous bromeliad endemic to tepui summits. Pitcher-shaped leaf rosette holds rainwater that traps and digests insects. Provides **microhabitat** for a wide range of summit fauna — frog tadpoles, insects, microbiomes. Source paper notes summit Sima Mayor area is **"bromeliad-rich"** with *Brocchinia* concentrations.

**CoO in-game name.** *Tank-Brocchinia* (or simply *the Brocchinia*).

**Function in CoO.** A **summit-zone interactive flora-cell.** Each Tank-Brocchinia holds:
- Drinkable rainwater (resource).
- A small frog or insect inhabitant (pickup encounter).
- A tiny ecosystem the player can study (Inquiry-affinity yield).
- **Light-cultivation potential**: bioluminescent fungi can be inoculated into Brocchinia tanks for portable summit bio-light.

**Lore significance.** **Summit terrain feature** — the *defining* terrain feature of a tepui summit, equivalent to fungal patches in catacombs. Bromeliad-rich summit zones are hosting grounds for the highest-tier summit fauna; bromeliad-poor zones are barren.

**Cross-links:** **Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy** (Brocchinia-inoculated bio-light is a summit-portable variant), **Bower-Folk** (Brocchinia gardens as aesthetic installations), **Summit Singer** / **Cave-Singer** (their habitat depends on bromeliad density).

## *Heliconia* — Habitat Plant / **CoO: the *Spear-Leaf***

**Real species.** Broad-leafed forest plant; *Hyalinobatrachium mesai* (Glasspane Frog) calls from *Heliconia* leaves above streams.

**CoO in-game name.** *Spear-Leaf*.

**Function in CoO.** **Foothill-rainforest terrain feature.** Spear-Leaf cells are habitat for Glasspane Frogs (above-stream broad-leaf cells specifically). A broad-leaf canopy that Glasspane Frogs use for calling. Players who learn to find Spear-Leaf cells find Glasspane populations.

# VI. Non-herpetofauna with cultural / lore weight

These are not the focus of the source paper but are mentioned for ecological/cultural context. Each has direct CoO design-relevance.

## *Steatornis caripensis* — the Oilbird / **CoO: the *Sima-Steward Bird***

**Real species.** The world's only **nocturnal flying frugivore**, **echolocating** in caves. Cave/sima-dwelling; lives in colonies in dark caverns, navigates by clicks. Source paper: oilbirds **transport seeds into Sima Mayor**, creating a "huge seed accumulation" below their nesting crevices — the foundation of the sinkhole's plant ecosystem.

**CoO in-game name.** *Sima-Steward* — captures their ecological role as the seed-bringer that *creates* sinkhole vegetation.

**Habitat in CoO.** Sinkhole-rim cells (nesting crevices). Active at night; navigates Sima interiors by sound.

**Combat behaviour.**
- **Passive toward player** by default.
- **Defensive group response** if their nesting cell is attacked: full colony swarms the attacker, echolocation-disrupting sound effect, mid-tier damage.
- **Cannot be hunted in dead zones** (they don't go there) — their absence is itself a dead-zone indicator.

**Lore significance.** **Foundational ecology species.** Without Sima-Steward seed dispersal, sinkhole vegetation would not exist — they are the *origin of the Drowned-Sima archetype's biological character.* Catacomb-villagers revere them; killing them is a deep taboo. Some catacomb cultures consider Sima-Steward attacks the cosmological worst-case scenario (more taboo than Choir integration). The bird's **echolocation-by-clicks** is mechanically interesting: in dark catacombs, a Sima-Steward colony's click-soundscape is a navigational aid the player can learn to read.

**Cross-links:** **Sinkholes** (foundational seed disperser), **Bioluminescent Catacomb Economy** (alternative non-light navigation: echo-by-bird vs. light-by-fungi), **Catacomb-villagers** (sacred fauna; killing them is taboo).

## *Harpia harpyja* — the Harpy Eagle / Dimoshi / **CoO: the *Sky-Sari***

**Real species.** Largest eagle in the Americas. Apex aerial predator of Amazonian rainforest. *Ye'kwana **Dimoshi**: a giant evil bird spirit directly associated with Sarisariñama.* This is the same Ye'kwana cosmological thread that gives CoO its **Urqu** entry.

**Cultural-responsibility note.** **Dimoshi is sacred Ye'kwana mythology.** Strong recommendation: do not lift the Dimoshi name. The structural concept (a giant, evil aerial predator linked to Sarisariñama) is what the CoO design borrows; the naming is CoO-original.

**CoO in-game name.** *Sky-Sari* — echoes Urqu's *sari... sari...* signature, claiming this aerial threat as the *aerial counterpart* to Urqu's underground evil. *Dimothread*, *Mountain-Talon*, *the Stoop* are alternates.

**Habitat in CoO.** Tepui-summit airspace and high-slope canopy cells. Active during Urqu's manifest periods; rare or absent otherwise.

**Combat behaviour.**
- **Apex aerial predator.** Stoops from summit cells onto unaware prey for massive single-strike damage.
- Avoidable by tracking sky-cells; punishing for players who don't.
- **Coordinates with Sari-Snakes** when Urqu is locally active — the player who sees a Sky-Sari and a Sari-Snake population in the same zone can be certain Urqu is manifesting.

**Lore significance.** **The aerial Urqu-thread.** Where the Sari-Snake is Urqu in the slither, the Sky-Sari is Urqu in the stoop. Together they're the visible signs of an Urqu manifest period: snakes below, eagles above. Catacomb-villagers (who never see the sky directly) tell stories of what their surface ancestors saw — Sky-Saris are part of the surface mythology that the catacomb cultures inherited from generations ago and have transformed into something stranger.

**Cross-links:** **Urqu** (aerial complement to Sari-Snake), **Catacomb-villagers** (inherited surface myth, transformed by isolation).

## *Ara chloroptera* — the Scarlet Macaw / **CoO: the *Bower-Macaw***

**Real species.** Frugivorous parrot; source paper notes they **transport seeds to simas** alongside oilbirds.

**CoO in-game name.** *Bower-Macaw*.

**Lore significance.** **Bower-Folk-aligned wild fauna.** Their colorful plumage and seed-transport behaviour both serve the **Bower-Folk** (`IDEAS.md`). Wild Bower-Macaw flocks are *the visual ancestor* of the Bower-Folk's aesthetic culture — the parrots got to bower-building first, biologically.

**Cross-links:** **Bower-Folk** (aesthetic-culture ancestor species).
